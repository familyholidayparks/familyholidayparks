LOCAL KNOWLEDGE:
# Visiting Pemberton, WA with Kids

**Best time to visit is September through May** – avoid the school holidays in December/January when everything's packed and prices spike. Spring (September-November) is brilliant for wildflowers and comfortable temps, while autumn (March-May) gives you warm days without the summer heat. Summer gets hot and dry, which is fine if you're heading to Karri Valley Vineyard for a swim or tackling the hiking trails early in the morning. Winter's mild compared to the east, but you'll cop some rain, so pack layers and waterproofs.

**Stick to the main spots: Gloucester National Park for easy family walks through the massive karri trees, Big Brook Dam for a casual picnic and a splash, and the Pemberton Tramway if the kids like trains.** Get fuel in Pemberton town before heading out – the nearest servo is ages away once you venture into the forests. Book accommodation early even in shoulder season; there's not heaps of choice and the good spots fill up. Bring insect repellent year-round because the mozzies and march flies can be brutal near water. Watch out for narrow forest roads with oncoming traffic around blind corners – drive slower than you think you need to and keep right.

**Here's the insider bit: grab fresh produce and proper coffee from the locals in town before heading to the parks – the food options near attractions are pretty limited.** The karri trees are genuinely jaw-dropping, so don't rush through the walks; let the kids climb on fallen logs and just soak it in. Summer bushfire season means checking fire bans before you visit – some parks close without warning, so ring ahead if you're planning between December and March.
