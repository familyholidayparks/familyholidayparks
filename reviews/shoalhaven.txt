HERO INTRO:
# Shoalhaven: A Family Holiday Destination

Shoalhaven sits on the NSW South Coast about two hours south of Sydney, offering families a genuine seaside escape without the crowds. The region boasts stunning beaches at Shoalhaven Heads, Culburra Beach, and Currarong, where kids can swim, build sandcastles, and explore rock pools. Behind the beaches lie the Shoalhaven River and surrounding bushland, perfect for spotting local wildlife like kookaburras, goannas, and if you're lucky, dolphins. The area's relaxed vibe means families can move at their own pace, whether that's a beach day or a walk through the national parks.

For accommodation, you've got solid options with Holiday Haven parks at Culburra Beach, Shoalhaven Heads, and Currarong offering cabins and powered sites, while Treehaven Tourist Park and Nowra Riverside Tourist Park provide alternatives nearby. Beyond the beaches, families can kayak on the river, visit the local shops and cafes in charming towns, or tackle walking tracks that suit all fitness levels. It's the kind of place where kids genuinely unwind, parents actually relax, and you leave feeling like you've had a proper break rather than just a change of scenery.

LOCAL KNOWLEDGE:
# Shoalhaven Family Visit Guide

The best time to visit Shoalhaven is September through November (spring) or April through May (autumn) when the weather's lovely and you're not fighting school holiday crowds. December to February gets properly hot and packed, so if you're avoiding that chaos, aim for shoulder seasons. Summer's great for water activities but expect bigger accommodation prices and packed beaches. Winter's mild enough for families—you won't freeze—but water's chilly for swimming. Book accommodation early during school holidays or you'll be looking at dodgy options inland.

Locals will tell you to skip the main Nowra beaches on weekends if you've got young kids—go to Currarong or Hyams Beach instead, they're cleaner and way less hectic. The Shoalhaven River is perfect for families: hire a kayak or just walk along the pathway, and the kids will burn energy without you losing your mind. Hit up the Sunday markets at Nowra if you're here on weekends—proper local vibe and decent coffee. Watch out for rips at ocean beaches; even experienced swimmers respect the southern NSW coast. And honestly, the inland towns like Kangaroo Valley are absolute gems if you want to escape the crowds—rainforest walks, cute cafes, and half the tourists.

The real insider move is visiting Jervis Bay in mid-May when the southern right whales come through; it's absolutely magical and genuinely one of Australia's best family experiences. Book a boat tour early, bring layers, and don't expect guarantees, but when you see them it's life-changing. Finally, download the Shoalhaven visitor info before you arrive because phone reception drops out in pockets, especially if you venture inland. Bring reef-safe sunscreen—the marine parks here are precious and worth protecting.

DESTINATION SUMMARY:
# Shoalhaven Holiday Parks

The Shoalhaven region has carved out a reputation as one of the South Coast's most reliable holiday park destinations, largely because the park operators here actually understand what regulars want. Holiday Haven dominates the scene with three strategically positioned properties—Culburra Beach sits right on the sand with direct beach access, Shoalhaven Heads occupies prime real estate where the Shoalhaven River meets the ocean, and Currarong offers a quieter alternative across the water. What sets these parks apart isn't flashy resort amenities but rather their grip on the fundamentals: solid powered sites, genuine waterfront positioning, and management that's been fine-tuning operations for decades. The supporting parks—Nowra Riverside Tourist Park and Treehaven Tourist Park—fill gaps for those wanting riverside or bush-surrounded camping, giving visitors real choice depending on whether they're chasing beach time or something more sheltered.

You'll find every variety of visitor here, from grey nomads who've been parking in the same spot for 20 years to young families discovering the coast for the first time. Caravanners and motorhomers make up the bulk of the crowd, particularly the dedicated repeat visitors who book school holiday periods a year in advance. The Culburra and Shoalhaven Heads parks fill to capacity during July and December-January, with Easter and September school holidays running a close second. Many families return annually because the parks sit within striking distance of proper beaches—Culburra's patrolled and suitable for kids, while Shoalhaven Heads offers river swimming and rock pools that younger children can explore safely. It's the accessibility that keeps them coming back: you're not fighting crowds, the parks maintain their grounds properly, and you can actually get your caravan in and out without a logistics operation.

The real advantage the Shoalhaven parks offer is their position between serious beach access and the quieter Shoalhaven River system. Culburra Beach gives you proper coastal experiences—patrolled swimming, fishing, and that open water feel—while the riverside parks cater to a different tempo entirely, with kayaking, birdwatching, and calmer water activities. From Shoalhaven Heads, you're in a transitional zone where families can split time between river and ocean without relocating. Nowra sits far enough upriver to be genuinely peaceful but close enough to town for supplies and services. What brings families back year after year isn't marketing; it's the simple fact that these parks work logistically, the facilities don't disappoint, and there's enough water-based activity within arm's reach to keep everyone occupied. The South Coast genuinely gets quieter than the Central Coast once you're south of Wollongong, and Shoalhaven captures that sweet spot between accessibility and actual coastal peace.

FAQ:
Q: What are the best family holiday parks in Shoalhaven, NSW?
A: Holiday Haven Culburra Beach, Holiday Haven Shoalhaven Heads, Holiday Haven Currarong, and Nowra Riverside Tourist Park are top-rated family options. Each offers different advantages—beachfront locations, river access, or quieter settings. All provide family-friendly facilities including playgrounds, pools, and on-site activities. Choose based on your preference for beach versus river experiences and desired amenities.

Q: Which caravan park in Shoalhaven is best for kids?
A: Holiday Haven Culburra Beach and Holiday Haven Shoalhaven Heads excel for families with children. Both feature swimming pools, kids' clubs during peak seasons, playground equipment, and shallow beach access. Nowra Riverside Tourist Park offers river activities and spacious grounds for exploration. All three provide safe, supervised environments specifically designed for family holidays.

Q: Are there holiday parks with pools near Shoalhaven?
A: Yes, Holiday Haven Culburra Beach and Holiday Haven Shoalhaven Heads both have heated pools ideal for families. These facilities operate year-round, with extended hours during school holidays. Pools are monitored and complemented by other water activities like beach swimming and river access, ensuring multiple cooling-off options for children.

Q: What holiday parks are near the beach in Shoalhaven?
A: Holiday Haven Culburra Beach and Holiday Haven Shoalhaven Heads offer direct or immediate beach access. Culburra provides patrolled swimming beaches perfect for families, while Shoalhaven Heads has scenic coastal walks and sheltered swimming spots. Both locations combine beach convenience with park facilities, eliminating the need for separate accommodation and beach trips.

Q: Which Shoalhaven parks offer powered sites?
A: All four parks—Holiday Haven Culburra Beach, Holiday Haven Shoalhaven Heads, Holiday Haven Currarong, and Nowra Riverside Tourist Park—provide powered caravan sites. Powered options typically include 15-amp or 10-amp connections suitable for caravans and motorhomes. Book ahead during school holidays and peak seasons as powered sites fill quickly.

Q: What cabin options are available in Shoalhaven holiday parks?
A: Holiday Haven locations offer various cabin styles from basic self-catering to fully equipped units with ensuites. Options range from budget-friendly cabins to premium family units sleeping 4-8 people. Nowra Riverside Tourist Park also provides cabin accommodation near the river. Most include heating, kitchenettes, and bedding—ideal for families preferring established accommodation over caravans.

Q: Are there pet-friendly holiday parks in Shoalhaven?
