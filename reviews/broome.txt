HERO INTRO:
# Broome, Western Australia: A Family Holiday Destination

Broome is a relaxed tropical town on Western Australia's far north coast that delivers genuine appeal for families. The beaches here are spectacular – Cable Beach stretches for 22 kilometres of golden sand where kids can swim, build sandcastles, and spot the famous camel trains. Beyond the shoreline, you'll find natural wonders like Roebuck Bay's tidal flats and nearby gorges perfect for exploring. The town's pearling heritage is real history, not just tourist talk, and attractions like the Pearl Broome Museum and outdoor pools keep children entertained without breaking the bank.

What makes Broome work for families is the straightforward combination of space, good facilities, and year-round warm weather. Several caravan parks offer solid accommodation options – Cable Beach Caravan Park, RAC Cable Beach Holiday Park, Broome Caravan Park, Tarangau Caravan Park, and Discovery Parks – Broome all provide safe bases with playgrounds and swimming pools for the kids. The town's compact enough that you're not constantly driving between attractions, yet there's enough room to move without feeling crowded. Between the beaches, nature walks, accessible local attractions, and reliable facilities, Broome gives families the ingredients for a proper holiday without the fuss.

LOCAL KNOWLEDGE:
# Visiting Broome with Kids: Local Parent's Guide

The sweet spot for visiting Broome is April to September – it's hot but manageable, and you'll dodge the wet season cyclones that can shut things down. May through August is pure gold if you can swing it: brilliant sunny days, comfortable temps around 25-28°C, and the famous staircase to the moon phenomenon happens at Cable Beach (time it right during full moon and it's genuinely magical). Whatever you do, avoid November to March unless you're heat-proof – it's absolutely scorching and humidity will do your head in. Pro tip: book accommodation six months out if you're coming during school holidays; this place fills up fast and prices go silly.

Families absolutely lose it over Cable Beach – it's the main drawcard and for good reason, though keep an eye on tide times and swim between the flags during stinger season (October to May, use the netted areas). The pearl farms are worth a visit, the Japanese cemetery is quietly special, and Gantheaume Point has dinosaur footprints the kids can actually see. Hit the local cafes around Roebuck Street rather than tourist traps, grab fresh barramundi from the fish markets, and hire a 4WD if you want to explore properly – some beaches need it. Watch out for saltwater crocs in mangrove areas – locals aren't being dramatic about this one. Also, mobile reception is dodgy outside town, and fuel prices are brutal, so plan your drive routes carefully.

The real secret Broome experience is getting out to the small beaches and pindan cliffs at sunset, hiring bikes to ride around town, and chatting to locals – they're genuinely friendly and full of good stories. Book a sunset camel ride if your kids are into it (they're well-treated and it's iconic), but honestly, the free stuff beats the touristy gear. Most importantly, build in lazy days – Broome's vibe is deliberately slow, and if you're constantly rushing, you'll miss what makes it special. Bring good sunscreen and a hat you don't mind losing to the wind.

DESTINATION SUMMARY:
Broome's holiday parks sit in one of WA's most distinctive coastal settings, where the Indian Ocean meets red pindan soil and tidal ranges that hit some of the biggest in the world. The five parks in the area each have their own character—Cable Beach Caravan Park and RAC Cable Beach Holiday Park sit right on the famous sweep of sand that defines Broome, while Tarangau and Broome Caravan Park are closer to town but still within easy reach of water. Discovery Parks - Broome rounds out the options. What makes this pocket different from southern caravan parks is the sheer number of repeat visitors who've been coming for decades—grey nomads who time their migration north to hit Broome in the cooler months, families who've booked the same dates every school holiday for years, and couples who've made it their annual reset button. The parks fill predictably hard during WA school holidays and the May-September shoulder season when southern Australia's winter drives people north.

The beachfront parks give you direct access to Cable Beach's 22 kilometres of sand, and timing matters here—when the tide goes out, you can walk for hours on hard-packed beach. Tarangau and Broome Caravan Park offer a quieter setup slightly inland but with Roebuck Bay nearby, plus you're closer to the town's proper infrastructure, the markets, and the cafes. Beyond the water, there's the nature side: Broome's surrounded by spinifex country, bird-watching spots around Cygnet Bay, and the Dampier Peninsula's remote beaches if you're willing to drive further. Most parks have reasonable facilities—powered sites are standard at the top-ranked parks, and the beachfront ones have that particular appeal of waking to ocean views and the smell of salt. Families with young kids tend to stay longer here than they would down south, partly because the weather works for them during winter and partly because there's enough to do across a week or two without getting bored.

What keeps families coming back isn't one thing—it's the combination. Cable Beach Caravan Park and RAC Cable Beach Holiday Park offer that direct beach access, while the town-closer options like Broome and Tarangau give families more flexibility for daily activities. School holidays see occupancy spike hard; booking six to twelve months ahead isn't uncommon for families wanting to lock in their preferred park and dates. The parks themselves have regular visitors who've watched their kids grow up coming here, and that creates a genuine community feel that seasonal parks often lack. Broome's remoteness—it's still five hours from Kununurra, hours from anywhere else—actually works in its favour; once you're here, you stay put and settle in, which is exactly what families want. The combination of reliable winter weather, accessible beaches with safe swimming conditions, and a town that's relaxed enough to absorb families without feeling overwhelmed keeps the parks full and keeps people returning.
