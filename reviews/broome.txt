HERO INTRO:
# Broome, Western Australia: A Family Holiday Destination

Broome is a relaxed coastal town in Western Australia's Kimberley region that offers families a genuine outback experience without the stress. The town's main drawcard is Cable Beach, a stunning 22-kilometre stretch of red sand and turquoise water perfect for swimming, paddling and building sandcastles. Beyond the beach, families can explore natural attractions like Roebuck Bay's tidal flats, where the dramatic 11-metre tidal range creates unique landscapes, and nearby nature reserves that showcase the region's diverse wildlife. The town also has kid-friendly attractions including the Broome Crocodile Park, pearl industry heritage sites, and regular markets that give families a genuine taste of outback life.

For accommodation, several caravan parks cater well to families seeking a relaxed stay. Options like Cable Beach Caravan Park, RAC Cable Beach Holiday Park, Discovery Parks – Broome, Tarangau Caravan Park and Broome Caravan Park offer powered sites, swimming pools and easy access to the beach. Broome suits families who prefer a slower pace and don't need theme parks or elaborate entertainment—the real appeal is outdoor adventures, beach days and learning about the natural environment. With warm weather most of the year, particularly April to September, it's an ideal destination for families wanting to escape the southern winter.

LOCAL KNOWLEDGE:
# Visiting Broome with the Family

Dry season (May to October) is your sweet spot – it's hot enough to enjoy the beach without melting, and the cyclone risk is basically zero. April and November are transitional months that can work, but come December through March you're looking at stinking humidity, frequent storms, and the real possibility of a cyclone shutting everything down. School holidays are manic here; book accommodation months ahead or expect to pay through the nose. The locals will tell you the Wet season sunsets are incredible, but they're not wrong – if you time it right between storms, the light is unreal. Just don't bank on it.

Stick to Cable Beach and Town Beach during the day – they're patrolled and safe. The mudflats around Roebuck Bay look tempting but are genuinely dangerous; people get bogged and the tide comes in fast. Stingers are a real thing from November onwards, so check before you let the kids loose in the water. Bring serious sunscreen – the sun here is no joke, even for us tanned Aussies. The town itself is pretty compact, so you don't need a massive car, but do fill up on fuel before heading out to Cape Leveque or the inland spots; there's bugger all out there. Book restaurants ahead, especially dinner – places fill up quick and some close randomly.

Here's the insider move: skip the main tourist traps and hit the actual local beaches early morning. The pearl farms offer decent tours if your kids are into that sort of thing, but honestly, the Broome Crocodile Park and the dinosaur footprints near Gantheaume Point keep most families happiest. Grab coffee at a local spot rather than the chains, and don't miss the markets if you're here on a weekend – proper local vibe. The cable beach camel rides are touristy as they come, but if your family's into it, do it at sunset when it's cooler and actually pleasant.

DESTINATION SUMMARY:
Broome's caravan parks sit in a unique pocket of Western Australia where the Indian Ocean meets red pindan soil, and this geography shapes everything about staying here. Cable Beach Caravan Park and RAC Cable Beach Holiday Park put you right on the sand—literally metres from the water where you can swim, fish, or just watch the famous Broome sunsets that turn the beach orange. Tarangau and Broome Caravan Park offer a different experience, set back slightly with bush surrounds that feel more sheltered and quieter, though you're still close enough to reach the beach and river access within minutes. Discovery Parks - Broome fills the middle ground, giving families resort-style facilities without the beachfront intensity. What makes Broome different to other coastal parks down south is the raw feel of it—this is the Kimberley, not some sanitised holiday zone. The tides here are massive, the light is harsh, and the landscape doesn't apologise for itself.

You'll find everything from retirees in permanent vans who've essentially moved to Broome, to families packing a caravan for the winter school holidays when the weather's perfect, to motorhomers doing lap of Australia routes. The parks have evolved to handle this mix—Cable Beach caters heavily to tourists wanting beachfront convenience, while Tarangau gets repeat visitors who book the same site year after year. School holidays, particularly June through August, are when these parks fill to capacity; families know that Broome's dry season is the only realistic window to visit comfortably. You'll see the same caravans return regularly, their owners having worked out which park suits their style and staking their claim for the cooler months.

What keeps people coming back isn't just the beach access, though wading through shallow turquoise water at Cable Beach or spotting crocodiles from a distance at Roebuck Bay matters. It's the combination—mornings at the markets, afternoon swims, evening walks along the shore, and nights where the Milky Way is so bright it casts a shadow. Families appreciate that kids can safely explore rock pools, ride bikes through the park, and wind down in the evening without needing constant entertainment. The parks themselves are low-key; they're bases for exploring Broome rather than destinations in their own right, which is exactly what appeals to people who come back. Once you've spent a winter season here, you understand why caravanners queue up for bookings months in advance.

FAQ:
Q: What are the best family holiday parks in Broome?
A: Cable Beach Caravan Park, RAC Cable Beach Holiday Park, Tarangau Caravan Park, and Broome Caravan Park all offer family-friendly facilities. Cable Beach parks are closest to swimming and water activities. Tarangau and Broome Caravan Park offer quieter settings. Compare amenities like pools, playgrounds, and proximity to beaches before booking based on your family's priorities.

Q: Which caravan park in Broome is best for kids?
