HERO INTRO:
# Apollo Bay, Victoria

Apollo Bay is a laid-back coastal town on Victoria's famous Great Ocean Road, offering families the perfect mix of beach time and nature exploration. The sweeping sandy beach is ideal for swimming and building sandcastles, while the rock pools at nearby Marengo are great for spotting marine life without venturing too far from shore. The town itself has good amenities without being touristy, and plenty of local fish and chip spots to grab dinner after a day outdoors. Kids can burn energy at the Apollo Bay Recreation Reserve or explore the walking trails through the surrounding national park without needing to drive far.

The real drawcard for families is the natural playground on your doorstep. Walks range from short coastal strolls to more substantial hikes through rainforest, where you might spot native wildlife. Several well-maintained caravan parks operate in the area, including BIG4 Apollo Bay Pisces Holiday Park, Apollo Bay Holiday Park, and Marengo Family Caravan Park, all offering family-friendly facilities and easy beach access. If you're after a holiday where kids can actually get outside and tire themselves out, Apollo Bay delivers without the stress of crowded tourist traps.

LOCAL KNOWLEDGE:
# Apollo Bay Family Guide

Best time to visit is December through February if you want proper beach weather and the kids actually want to get in the water – it's warm enough then. December school holidays get rammed with tourists, so if you can swing January or early February, you'll have better luck finding parking and the beaches won't be completely packed. Winter's beautiful and way quieter, but the water's bloody cold and the wind can be fierce, so it's really only for the hardy types. Spring and autumn are goldmines – good weather, fewer crowds, and the Great Ocean Road drive isn't a nightmare.

Get to the main beaches early, especially Marengo Beach – parking fills up quick on weekends and school holidays. The rock pools at Moggs Creek are magic for younger kids during low tide, but check the tides before you go or you'll waste time looking at wet rocks. Buy your fish and chips from Catch or grab takeaway from the general store rather than the tourist traps closer to the water – saves money and the quality's better. Fair warning: the rips can be serious around here, so keep an eye on the kids in the water and stick to the patrolled beaches. The wind picks up in the afternoon, which is actually great if you've got older kids into watersports, but brutal if you're trying to have a quiet picnic.

Skip the pricey holiday park accommodation if you can and book one of the holiday homes – you'll get more space, a kitchen to actually cook in, and better value. The Great Ocean Road walk to the Twelve Apostles is worth doing, but do it early in the day before it gets chocker with tour buses. Bring good rain gear even when it looks fine – weather changes fast down here. Kids absolutely love exploring the tidepools and the rockier bits of beach, so let them loose there rather than trying to keep them entertained on the sand all day.

FAQ:
Q: What are the best family holiday parks in Apollo Bay?
A: BIG4 Apollo Bay Pisces, Apollo Bay Holiday Park, Marengo Family Caravan Park, and Apollo Bay Recreation Reserve are the top family options. BIG4 Pisces offers extensive facilities including pools and entertainment. Apollo Bay Holiday Park provides beachfront access. Marengo suits families seeking quieter settings. Recreation Reserve offers budget-friendly powered sites near the beach.

Q: Which caravan park in Apollo Bay is best for kids?
A: BIG4 Apollo Bay Pisces Holiday Park is ideal for children with dedicated kids' clubs during peak seasons, playgrounds, heated pools, and organized activities. Apollo Bay Holiday Park also features family-friendly amenities and direct beach access, making it excellent for keeping children entertained throughout your stay.

Q: Are there holiday parks with pools near Apollo Bay?
A: Yes, BIG4 Apollo Bay Pisces Holiday Park features heated indoor and outdoor pools, making it perfect for year-round swimming. Apollo Bay Holiday Park also provides pool facilities. These parks are essential for families wanting water activities regardless of weather conditions during your Apollo Bay visit.

Q: What holiday parks in Apollo Bay are near the beach?
A: Apollo Bay Holiday Park offers direct beachfront positioning with immediate sand access. Marengo Family Caravan Park and Apollo Bay Recreation Reserve are also very close to the beach, within walking distance. BIG4 Pisces is slightly inland but still within 10 minutes' walk, providing excellent beach proximity for families.

Q: Does Apollo Bay have holiday parks with powered sites?
A: All four parks—BIG4 Apollo Bay Pisces, Apollo Bay Holiday Park, Marengo Family Caravan Park, and Apollo Bay Recreation Reserve—offer powered caravan sites. Availability varies seasonally, so book ahead during school holidays. Powered sites typically include water, electricity, and sewerage connections for modern caravan conveniences.

Q: What cabin options are available in Apollo Bay holiday parks?
A: BIG4 Apollo Bay Pisces offers spacious cabins with modern facilities, from budget-friendly to premium options. Apollo Bay Holiday Park provides comfortable cabin accommodation. Marengo Family Caravan Park features cabin availability for families. Apollo Bay Recreation Reserve offers more basic cabin options at lower prices, suiting budget-conscious families.

Q: Are there pet-friendly holiday parks in Apollo Bay?
A: BIG4 Apollo Bay Pisces and Apollo Bay Holiday Park accept well-behaved pets in designated areas, though restrictions apply during peak periods. Confirm pet policies when booking, as size and breed limitations exist. Many families appreciate having their pets alongside them during Apollo Bay family holidays.

Q: What's the best value holiday park in Apollo Bay?
A: Apollo Bay Recreation Reserve offers the most affordable nightly rates with powered sites and basic cabins. Marengo Family Caravan Park provides good value for families seeking quieter settings. BIG4 Pisces costs more but justifies it with facilities. Compare rates seasonally—off-peak visits offer significant savings across all parks.

Q: What's the best area to stay in Apollo Bay for families?
A: The beachfront precinct near Apollo Bay Holiday Park suits families wanting beach access and town proximity. BIG4 Pisces offers self-contained resort facilities appealing to families preferring on-site entertainment. Marengo suits families seeking quieter, more relaxed environments. Choose based on whether you prefer activity-focused or peaceful holiday styles.

Q: What are top rainy day activities in Apollo Bay for kids?
A: Indoor pools at BIG4 Pisces and Apollo Bay Holiday Park provide swimming options. Visit the Apollo Bay Museum, explore local shops, or enjoy the Great Ocean Road Discovery Centre. Kids enjoy visiting nearby Maits Rest Rainforest walks under shelter. Most parks offer indoor entertainment facilities during wet weather periods.
