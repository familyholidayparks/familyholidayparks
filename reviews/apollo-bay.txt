HERO INTRO:
Apollo Bay is a relaxed coastal town on Victoria's famous Great Ocean Road, offering families a genuine seaside escape without the crowds. The main beach is perfect for kids – a gentle sandy stretch backed by grassy reserves where you can set up for the day, with rock pools to explore at low tide and reliable waves for those learning to surf. The town itself is compact and walkable, with a working fishing fleet, decent fish and chips, and enough local character to feel authentic rather than theme-park-like.

What really sets Apollo Bay apart for families is the surrounding natural playground. You've got the Otway Ranges right on the doorstep with excellent walks ranging from short rainforest strolls to longer hikes, plus Cape Otway Lighthouse is just half an hour away. The area has solid caravan park options including BIG4 Apollo Bay Pisces, Apollo Bay Holiday Park, and Marengo Family Caravan Park – all well-equipped with amenities and kids' facilities. The Apollo Bay Recreation Reserve adds another option for those wanting a simpler setup. Between beach days, forest walks, and lighthouse visits, there's enough variety to keep families occupied for a solid week without feeling like you're constantly chasing activities.

LOCAL KNOWLEDGE:
# Apollo Bay for Families: Local Insider Tips

The sweet spot for visiting Apollo Bay with kids is September through November (spring) or March through May (autumn) – you'll dodge the school holidays chaos and actually find a carpark. Summer holidays are absolutely rammed, prices spike, and the beaches get crowded with tourists who don't know the rips. If you've got school-age kids, mid-week visits during term time are gold. Winter's quieter but can be gloomy and the water's bloody freezing, though it's brilliant for spotting whales (June-November). Pack layers no matter when you visit – the wind whips in off Bass Strait and the weather changes faster than kids' moods.

Hit the rock pools at Marengo Reef or Fairhaven when the tide's out – kids lose hours here, and it costs nothing. Grab fish and chips from one of the spots near the beach and picnic rather than paying through the nose at cafes. The Great Ocean Road walk sections are manageable with littlies if you pick short stretches (Marriner's Lookout is perfect), and Otway Fly Treetop Walk is brilliant but book ahead. Watch out for the currents and rips – Apollo Bay's gorgeous but the ocean's serious; stick to patrolled beaches and read the flags. Also, the campground fills up fast during holidays, so book well ahead or expect to stay in Lorne or Geelong and day-trip in.

Don't miss the Colac-Otways Visitor Centre or grab a local paper for what's actually happening – heaps of seasonal stuff that doesn't make it online. Stock up on groceries in town before heading out to the cape or rockpools because there's bugger all shops out there. And pro tip: if rain sets in, the coastal drive is still spectacular and moody – Australians tend to think rain means staying home, but it's when you see the real coast at its best.

Check school term dates and book accommodation early for holiday periods when availability is limited.
