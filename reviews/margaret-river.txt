HERO INTRO:
Margaret River is a brilliant spot for a family getaway, sitting right on the WA coast between Perth and Busselton. The area's got stunning beaches that suit everyone – think calm sheltered bays perfect for little ones to splash about, and bigger breaks for the older kids to explore rock pools. The surrounding forest is genuinely impressive, with tall karri trees and native wildlife that'll keep the kids entertained without needing a theme park. You can do as much or as little as you fancy, from easy walks to spotting kangaroos and birdlife, plus there's enough going on that boredom isn't really an issue.

The area's got solid family appeal without being overcrowded or pretentious. There's wineries for the adults while kids run around the grounds, a few decent cafes and restaurants, and some good natural attractions like the Margaret River caves and seaside lookouts. For accommodation, you've got proper options in Discovery Parks – Margaret River, RAC Margaret River Nature Park, Margaret River Tourist Park, and Riverview Tourist Park, all offering caravan and cabin setups that make family trips easier. The main town's got everything you need – supermarkets, takeaway spots, petrol – so you're not caught out when someone gets hungry or cranky.

LOCAL KNOWLEDGE:
# Margaret River Family Guide

The sweet spot for visiting Margaret River is September to November (spring) or March to May (autumn) – you'll avoid the brutal summer heat and school holiday crowds that clog up the wineries and make accommodation pricey. Winter's mild but can get rainy, so pack layers either way. December to February is absolute chaos with families and tourists, and it's scorching hot if you've got little kids in tow. Book accommodation well ahead if you're coming during school holidays; everything gets snapped up fast. The beaches are stunning year-round, but spring is when you'll actually want to spend time there without melting.

Here's what families actually do here: hit the beaches at Prevelly and Surfers Point (great for teaching kids to swim in the calmer spots), explore the caves at Lake Cave or Jewel Cave (kids find them magic, though toddlers might struggle with stairs), and do the wineries – grab a picnic, plonk yourself on the lawns while you sip wine and the kids run around. The Margaret River Chocolate Company and local farm gates are brilliant for keeping everyone happy. Watch out for rips at the beaches and always check conditions; the Indian Ocean doesn't mess around, and tourists get into strife every summer ignoring the flags. Also, petrol and groceries are dearer out here, so stock up in town before heading out, and don't expect the same level of services you'd find in Perth – this is rural WA.

The real local tip: skip the touristy restaurant strips in peak times and grab takeaway from the good independent spots, or better yet, do a proper grocery shop and cook something yourself if you've got accommodation with a kitchen. Bring bikes if you can – there's a brilliant rail trail that's flat and safe for kids. And honestly, don't feel like you need to do the full wine-tasting circuit; half the magic is just being outdoors with the family, hitting the beaches and the farmers markets on weekends. The region's chilled – embrace that instead of trying to tick boxes.

DESTINATION SUMMARY:
Margaret River's holiday parks tap into what makes this corner of WA genuinely special – you've got the Southern Ocean minutes away, the Margaret River itself winding through native forest, and some of the best wine country in Australia all within a short drive. What sets these parks apart from your standard caravan stop is that they're positioned as bases for a proper region rather than just overnight spots. Discovery Parks – Margaret River sits closest to town and the river, giving you easy access to cellar doors and local cafes, while RAC Margaret River Nature Park leans into the bush experience with more space between sites and walking trails through jarrah and marri forest. Riverview Tourist Park and Margaret River Tourist Park fill different niches – one's more compact and family-focused, the other's got that mix of caravanners, motorhomes and a handful of cabins. You'll see serious caravan people who've been coming for decades, families who've worked Margaret River school holidays into their annual rhythm, and increasingly motorhomers doing longer loops through WA who've heard the reputation and stick around longer than planned.

Booking patterns here are pretty predictable – Easter and winter school holidays see the parks absolutely rammed, and you need to be in early. Summer's quieter than you'd expect given the proximity to beach and river, mainly because most families hit the coast further north. The real returning visitors are the ones who've figured out that Margaret River works year-round; September through November is honestly the sweet spot with fewer crowds and the forest at its best. Families come back because there's genuinely something for everyone – you've got river swimming and fishing spots, forest walks that don't require epic fitness, and the older kids can explore the cave systems or go mountain biking. The parks themselves aren't fancy, but that's not the point; people are here for what's around them, not resort facilities.

What keeps people coming back year after year is that Margaret River hasn't turned into a theme park version of itself. You can stay in a caravan park and spend your days doing anything from serious wine tasting to mucking about with fishing rods in the river to walking through old-growth forest where you'll actually see wildlife. The combination of river access, proximity to the ocean at Prevelly and Gnarabup, and the wine country creates something you don't get in other holiday park destinations – there's enough variety that different family members with different interests actually get what they want. And once people settle into a favourite park and discover their spots around the region, they tend to book the same weeks, same sites, year after year. It becomes less about the holiday and more about the ritual of returning somewhere that works.

Compare options based on your priorities—some excel with pools, others with beach proximity or cabin quality.

Q: Which caravan park in Margaret River is best for kids?
A: Discovery Parks - Margaret River stands out for families with its dedicated kids' activities, playground facilities, and family-oriented amenities. Margaret River Tourist Park also caters well to children with recreational spaces. Both parks understand family needs and provide entertainment options beyond typical caravan facilities, making them top choices for parent travelers.

Q: Do Margaret River holiday parks have swimming pools?
A: Yes, Discovery Parks - Margaret River features a swimming pool, making it excellent for families wanting water play without traveling to the beach. This is particularly valuable during cooler months or when you need a break from ocean conditions. Check current facilities before booking, as amenities can vary seasonally.

Q: Which holiday parks near Margaret River are closest to the beach?
A: Margaret River Tourist Park and Riverview Tourist Park offer proximity to local beaches and waterfront areas. Discovery Parks - Margaret River also provides reasonable beach access. Most Margaret River parks sit within 10-15 minutes of coastal spots, making beach visits convenient for families. Confirm exact distances when booking for your preferred beach activities.

Contact Discovery Parks, RAC Margaret River Nature Park, Margaret River Tourist Park, and Riverview Tourist Park directly about their current pet policies. Q: What rainy day activities work near Margaret River holiday parks?
A: Margaret River offers excellent rainy-day options: visit local wineries (many kid-friendly), explore the Margaret River Discovery Centre, try indoor adventure parks, or visit the Mammoth Cave. Many parks have covered recreation facilities. Beach walks remain possible in light rain. Plan indoor activities for guaranteed bad weather days during winter visits (June-August).
