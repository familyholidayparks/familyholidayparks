HEADING:
Best Family Holiday Parks in Lakes Entrance, Victoria

HERO INTRO:
Lakes Entrance is a laid-back coastal town on Victoria's Gippsland coast that ticks the boxes for a proper family getaway. You've got excellent beaches like Eastern Beach and Echo Beach that are patrolled by lifeguards during summer, making them safe spots for kids to swim and play. The natural appeal is genuine—there are walking trails through the lakes and wetlands, birdwatching opportunities, and the chance to spot local wildlife without needing to trek far from town. The main street has fish and chips shops, ice cream spots, and enough low-key attractions to keep everyone happy without breaking the bank.

For families wanting a home base, there's a good range of caravan parks and holiday parks dotted around the area. NRMA Eastern Beach Holiday Park and Koonwarra Family Holiday Park are solid options with facilities geared toward kids, while Lakes Entrance Recreation Reserve and Camping Ground offers affordable powered and unpowered sites. The real drawcard is the space and freedom—kids can run around, you can take a boat out on the lakes, do a spot of fishing, or just spend the day on the beach without feeling rushed. It's the kind of place where a week or even a fortnight feels like proper time out, not a rushed tick-off-the-bucket-list holiday.

WHY FAMILIES LOVE:
- Pristine beaches perfect for swimming and safe family fun
- Famous Lakes Entrance pelican feeding at the historic pier
- Gippsland Lakes offer calm waters for boating and fishing
- Native wildlife spotting including dolphins and brolga cranes
- Affordable coastal town with fish and chips culture

LOCAL KNOWLEDGE:
# Lakes Entrance Family Visit Guide

Best time to visit is December through February for proper beach weather, though it gets packed and accommodation prices spike – if you can swing school holidays in autumn (April-May), you'll get beautiful weather without the crowds and the water's still swimmable. Winter's crisp and quiet but the water's bloody cold, so unless your kids are keen swimmers, stick to the warmer months. Avoid Easter and school holidays if you want to actually park near the beach or grab a table at a café without a 45-minute wait.

The locals' trick is hitting the beaches early – head out before 10am and you'll have your pick of spots. Lakes Entrance is genuinely great for families: the beach is patrolled, the Gippsland Lakes are calmer than the ocean if you've got nervous swimmers, and the Kalimna Terrace walk is dead easy with little kids and has killer views. Grab fish and chips from one of the spots near the bridge, not the touristy joints on the main strip. Do watch out for the rips if you're heading to the ocean beaches, keep an eye on the swell reports, and remember the sun here is no joke – we're talking sunburn in 20 minutes if you're not careful.

Pro tip from us locals: skip the main tourist attractions if you can and instead explore Wyanga Park for a proper explore, or take the kids to the Lakes Entrance Aquarium early in the morning when it's quieter. The rock pools near the eastern beach are gold for rock pool hunting – proper little ecosystem stuff that blows kids' minds. Bring bathers, a hat, proper sunscreen, and honestly, just wander – some of our best family memories have been from stumbling onto quiet spots while exploring.

DESTINATION SUMMARY:
Lakes Entrance has carved out a genuine reputation as one of Victoria's best-kept caravan destinations, and it's not hard to see why once you've spent time here. The town's unique geography—sitting at the junction where the Gippsland Lakes meet the ocean through a narrow entrance—means the holiday parks genuinely offer something different from other coastal spots. You've got beachfront parks like NRMA Eastern Beach where you can literally walk onto the sand, riverside options where the Cunninghame Arm laps against your van, and bush-set alternatives further back that appeal to those wanting quieter surroundings without the sound of waves. The Recreation Reserve sits right in the middle of town action, while Koonwarra over on the western side offers a bit more seclusion. What makes it work is that all these parks are within spitting distance of each other, so you can choose your vibe without leaving the area entirely.

The demographic across these parks tells you something about Lakes Entrance's staying power. You see plenty of grey nomads who've been coming here for decades, many booking the same spot year after year—they know the rhythm of the tides and exactly which carpark gets sun in winter. Families with caravans and motorhomes dominate the school holidays, particularly Christmas and the autumn break, and the parks fill fast during these windows. There's good reason: kids can alternate between safe beach swimming at Eastern Beach and exploring the calm, shallow lakes for a completely different water experience. The river access means proper kayaking and fishing without massive swells, and the hinterland walks through the national park aren't far if you want to stretch legs without crowds.

What keeps people returning isn't just the scenery, though that helps. It's the practical stuff that works here. The fishing is genuinely good, the boat ramp infrastructure is solid, and the town has real services without feeling overdeveloped. Parents know their kids will be occupied between water activities, the adventure playground, and the jetty precinct without needing theme parks or entertainment complexes. The mix of park types also means repeat visitors can try different spots as their circumstances change—upgrading from a tent site to van accommodation, or downsizing from Eastern Beach's busier vibe to the quieter Recreation Reserve once they've settled into the rhythm of the place. It's this combination of accessible lake and ocean, workable park facilities, and genuine town character that explains why you'll see the same caravans and motorhomes rolling back in season after season.
