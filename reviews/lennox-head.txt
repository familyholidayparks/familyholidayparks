HERO INTRO:
# Lennox Head, NSW

Lennox Head is a laid-back coastal village on the far north coast of NSW that ticks all the boxes for a family getaway. The area boasts some genuinely great beaches – Seven Mile Beach and Lennox Head Beach itself are perfect for swimming, with patrolled lifeguard areas and reliable waves that suit everyone from timid paddlers to keen surfers. The town sits right next to the Minyon Falls hinterland, so you've got rainforest walks, swimming holes, and proper nature experiences within an hour's drive. The local parks and reserves are well-maintained and family-friendly, including Reflections Lennox Head Holiday Park, which offers comfortable accommodation options if you want the convenience of staying put.

What makes Lennox Head work for families is the no-fuss vibe and the range of activities on offer. Kids can spend hours at the beach or exploring the rock pools, while you've got decent fish and chip shops, a few cafes, and enough going on to keep everyone entertained without the chaos of busier tourist spots. The hinterland offers proper adventure – think rainforest walks at Minyon Falls or kayaking on the Richmond River – so you can mix beach days with exploring. It's the kind of place where families actually relax rather than spend the whole time chasing attractions, which is half the battle when you're travelling with kids.

LOCAL KNOWLEDGE:
# Lennox Head Family Guide

**Best times to visit are September to November and March to May** – you'll dodge the summer crowds and the cyclone risk that can kick up between December and February. School holidays get rammed, so if you can swing visiting outside those weeks, you'll find better parking at the beach and shorter queues at cafes. Winter's mild here, so don't write off June to August; the water's cooler but still swimmable for most kids, and you get crisp mornings perfect for walking.

**The beaches are your main drawcard, but know the rips.** Lennox Head Beach is patrolled and great for families, but that easterly swell can surprise you – keep kids close and always swim between the flags. Seven Mile Beach down south is wider and gentler if you've got littlies. Pro tip: grab fish and chips from the local shops rather than the tourist traps; check out what's actually fresh that day. The headland walk is stunning and doable with kids – takes about 45 minutes return, mostly flat, and you'll spot dolphins if you're lucky.

**Watch out for school holidays and long weekends** – the town fills up fast and accommodation books out months ahead. The roads aren't great when it's busy, so leave early if you're heading anywhere. Bring good sunscreen and a hat; the UV here is brutal. Book your accommodation and restaurant spots before you arrive, don't assume you'll just rock up. If you're keen on a quieter vibe, avoid Easter and July school breaks entirely – that's when half of Brisbane descends on us.

DESTINATION SUMMARY:
# Lennox Head Holiday Parks

Reflections Lennox Head stands as the main accommodation option for caravan and motorhome visitors to this far-north coast town, positioned right where the Lennox Head peninsula meets the ocean. What makes Lennox Head different from the sprawling parks down the coast is its compact geography—you're working with a small headland, which means parks here operate tight and purposeful rather than sprawling across hundreds of acres. The town's holiday park scene caters heavily to self-contained travellers, with full-amenity sites designed for longer stays rather than drive-through tourism. This isn't Coffs Harbour with multiple competing parks; it's a single focal point that absorbs most of the caravan traffic heading to this part of the coast.

The visitor mix at Lennox Head parks tells you something important about the destination's appeal. You'll find multigenerational families arriving in school holidays—particularly June and December—when the parks fill completely within days of bookings opening. But the real backbone is repeat visitors: retirees in motorhomes and permanent-placement caravans who've claimed their favourite spots and return seasonally, families who've been coming for fifteen years and book the same site month after month, and couples escaping winter from inland NSW and southern Queensland. This loyalty exists because Lennox Head offers something specific: the beachfront itself is immediately accessible from the park, the Lennox Head lagoon sits protected on the other side of the headland for gentler water activities, and the surrounding hinterland—Byron Bay is thirty minutes away—provides enough variety that people don't feel trapped. Parents know their kids can walk straight onto patrolled beaches, fish the rocks, or explore the headland walking tracks without leaving the immediate area.

The booking pattern at Lennox Head runs counter to what happens elsewhere. Yes, school holidays are absolutely mad—Easter, winter break, and summer holidays will have you locked out months ahead—but the shoulder seasons and term-time windows show why families keep returning. The water stays swimmable year-round, the town itself stays quieter than the hype zones of Byron, and there's genuine maritime character here rather than holiday park sterility. Parents appreciate that their kids learn to navigate rockpools and coastal terrain rather than just sitting poolside, and the older demographic values the no-nonsense practicality of the park setup. Once you've spent a week here and realised the combination of beach access, fishing spots, and proximity to decent cafes without the crowding, you book again. That's why Reflections Lennox Head works as hard as it does and why spaces disappear so quickly—locals and returning visitors know exactly what they're getting.
