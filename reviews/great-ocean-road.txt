HERO INTRO:
# Great Ocean Road: A Family Holiday Destination

The Great Ocean Road stretches along Victoria's stunning coastline, offering families an incredible mix of natural beauty and outdoor adventure. Kids love exploring the famous Twelve Apostles limestone formations, spotting wildlife at Otway National Park, and playing on the region's pristine beaches. Towns like Apollo Bay and Lorne provide safe swimming spots and rock pools perfect for younger children, while the quieter beaches around Wye River and Anglesea suit families after a more relaxed pace. The drive itself is spectacular, with countless pull-off points and short walks that keep children entertained without requiring serious hiking skills.

For accommodation, you've got solid caravan park options that cater specifically to families. BIG4 parks at Apollo Bay, Anglesea, Wye River, and Aireys Inlet all offer playground facilities, camp kitchens, and holiday park amenities that make extended stays practical. The Anglesea Family Caravan Park rounds out your choices if you want something more intimate. Most parks are close enough to beaches and attractions that you can fill your days without endless driving, and they provide a good base for exploring coastal walks, visiting local wildlife sanctuaries, and enjoying fresh fish and chips by the ocean. It's the kind of place where families can genuinely relax while still having plenty to do.

WHY FAMILIES LOVE:
- Stunning coastal cliffs and the famous Twelve Apostles rock formations
- Safe, family-friendly beaches perfect for swimming and surfing lessons
- Wildlife encounters with koalas, kangaroos, and little penguins
- Scenic driving route with countless pullouts and photo stops
- Charming seaside towns offering fish and chips, ice cream

LOCAL KNOWLEDGE:
# Great Ocean Road: Local Parent's Guide

The best time to visit is December to February if you want warm weather and calm seas, but honestly, you'll be fighting crowds and paying premium prices. If you've got school holidays flexibility, aim for September to November – spring's brilliant, the weather's mild, and it's not packed. Winter's actually underrated; sure it's cooler, but the swell's incredible if you've got older kids into surfing, and you'll have the place to yourself. Whatever you do, avoid school holidays unless you've booked accommodation months ahead. Pro tip: start early in the day – we're talking before 9am – to beat the tourist buses at the Twelve Apostles and snag decent parking.

Here's what families genuinely love that the brochures don't bang on about: Bells Beach for a proper swim (the rips can be sketchy, so watch the flags), the rock pools at Fairhaven when the tide's out, and grabbing fish and chips in Lorne or Apollo Bay rather than eating overpriced cafe food at every lookout. The drive itself is the thing – don't try to smash it in a day, you'll miss everything and exhaust the kids. We usually base ourselves in one spot for 2-3 nights and explore from there. Watch out for: dodgy mobile reception in patches, roads that get genuinely dangerous in heavy rain, and seagulls that'll nick your lunch the second you're not looking. Keep your distance from the cliffs – wind gusts are no joke, people get complacent and it's tragic.

The real insider move? skip the main Twelve Apostles carpark and do the Glenample Homestead walk instead – fewer crowds, better views, and you learn something about the area's history. Drive the road at a leisurely pace, pull over at random lookouts, and let the kids splash in creeks. Bring layers – coastal weather changes fast – and pack way more snacks than you think you'll need because service stations are sparse and expensive. If you're visiting in warmer months, book water activities (kayaking, whale watching) in advance, but don't stress if you can't – half the magic is just wandering the beaches and letting the kids explore the rock pools. Seriously, a good day here costs next to nothing if you're not chasing every paid attraction.

DESTINATION SUMMARY:
# Great Ocean Road Holiday Parks

The Great Ocean Road corridor between Anglesea and Apollo Bay has carved out a unique position in Victoria's holiday park landscape, largely because the parks here sit at the intersection of two distinct environments—the dramatic coastline and the Otway hinterland. Unlike parks further inland, these BIG4 properties and independents have direct access to both pristine beaches and temperate rainforest, which shapes everything from what guests pack to how they spend their days. The Wye River and Aireys Inlet parks offer genuine riverside settings where you can walk straight from your van to the water, while Apollo Bay Pisces and Anglesea properties command beachfront positions that catch the swell and give you that immediate sense of being somewhere genuinely coastal. This isn't manufactured holiday park atmosphere—it's built into the geography, and returning visitors notice the difference immediately when they pull in.

The demographic here is remarkably mixed, which speaks to how the parks service different travel styles. You'll find serious grey nomads who've been returning to the same site at Apollo Bay for fifteen years running, families who book the school holidays eighteen months out because competition for spots is fierce, and motorhome travellers treating it as a base to explore the Great Ocean Road proper. The BIG4 parks pull in families because the facilities are reliable and the beach access is straightforward—kids can burn energy on sand and rocks without parents losing sight of them. Repeat visitors come back because they've learned where the good fishing spots are, which beaches handle which weather patterns, and which local suppliers deliver real value. The Anglesea parks attract visitors who want slightly more shelter and suburban convenience, while Wye River and Aireys Inlet draw people specifically seeking quieter, more immersive coastal-forest experiences.

Booking patterns here are completely dominated by school holidays—Easter, winter break, and especially the summer stretch are locked in months ahead—but what distinguishes Great Ocean Road is the shoulder-season traffic of people who understand the region deeply. Summer school holidays see parks at absolute capacity with families, but May through August brings different visitors: retirees who've timed their travel to avoid crowds, couples exploring the region's hiking, and locals from Melbourne who know the weather and conditions well enough to take calculated risks. The roads and facilities here can handle volume better than smaller coastal destinations, and the parks themselves have invested seriously in maintaining amenities without losing the character that made people return in the first place. Families specifically come back because their kids know the beach rotation, they've got favourite fish and chips shops, and the combination of outdoor activity and genuinely good park infrastructure means both parents and children leave satisfied rather than just exhausted.

FAQ:
Q: What are the best family holiday parks on Great Ocean Road?
A: BIG4 Apollo Bay Pisces, BIG4 Anglesea, and BIG4 Wye River Holiday Parks offer excellent family facilities along Great Ocean Road. Each provides powered sites, cabins, and pools. Anglesea Family Caravan Park is another solid option. These parks combine beach access with on-site amenities, making them ideal bases for exploring the region's attractions like the Twelve Apostles and local beaches.

Q: Which caravan park on Great Ocean Road is best for kids?
A: BIG4 Anglesea Holiday Park stands out for families with its dedicated kids' facilities, playground, and heated pool. The park's beachfront location means children can safely explore rock pools and sandy shores. BIG4 Apollo Bay Pisces also excels with family-friendly activities and proximity to both beach and town attractions.

Q: Which Great Ocean Road holiday parks have pools?
A: All major parks—BIG4 Apollo Bay Pisces, BIG4 Anglesea, and BIG4 Wye River—feature heated pools ideal for year-round swimming. Many heated pools operate during cooler months, making them essential facilities for rainy days or when ocean swims aren't appealing. Check seasonal hours when booking.

Q: What holiday parks are closest to the beach on Great Ocean Road?
A: BIG4 Anglesea Holiday Park offers direct beachfront access, perfect for families wanting beach time before breakfast. BIG4 Apollo Bay Pisces sits near Apollo Bay's patrolled beach, excellent for swimming. BIG4 Wye River Holiday Park provides beach proximity in a quieter location. All three eliminate the need for car travel to reach quality swimming spots.

Q: Where can I find powered caravan sites on Great Ocean Road?
A: All four parks—BIG4 Apollo Bay Pisces, BIG4 Anglesea, BIG4 Wye River, and Anglesea Family Caravan Park—offer powered sites suitable for caravans and motorhomes. BIG4 parks typically have premium powered options with full hookups. Book early during school holidays as powered sites fill quickly along Great Ocean Road.

Q: What cabin options exist for families at Great Ocean Road parks?
A: BIG4 parks offer various cabin styles from budget-friendly options to luxury family units with ensuite bathrooms. Most sleep 4-6 people and include kitchenettes. Anglesea Family Caravan Park also provides cabin accommodation. Cabins are ideal for families wanting comfort without caravan setup, particularly during winter or rainy periods.

Q: Are there pet-friendly holiday parks on Great Ocean Road?
A: Most parks welcome pets, though policies vary. BIG4 Apollo Bay Pisces and BIG4 Anglesea typically allow dogs in designated areas. Always confirm pet fees and restrictions before booking. Great Ocean Road's beaches have dog-friendly zones and walking trails, making pet-friendly parks practical for families with animals.

Q: Which Great Ocean Road holiday park offers best value for families?
A: Anglesea Family Caravan Park typically provides competitive pricing for families without sacrificing quality facilities. During shoulder seasons (April-May, September), all parks offer better rates than school holidays. BIG4 parks offer discounted family packages and memberships, worth investigating if planning multiple Great Ocean Road trips.

Q: What's the best area on Great Ocean Road to stay with kids?
A: Anglesea balances beach access, family facilities, and proximity to attractions like Split Point Lighthouse and local pools. Apollo Bay works well for families wanting more town amenities and adventure activities. Wye River suits families seeking quieter surroundings. Consider your priorities: activity level, beach access, or peaceful relaxation when choosing.

Q: What can kids do on rainy days at Great Ocean Road parks?
A: Heated pools at all major parks become rainy-day hubs. Many parks offer indoor entertainment spaces. Nearby, families visit the Surf World Museum, local libraries, or shopping centers. Apollo Bay's town offers cafes and indoor attractions. Wye River's nature provides sheltered walks. Pack indoor entertainment; rainy days occur frequently during cooler months.
