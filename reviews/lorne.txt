HERO INTRO:
# Lorne, Victoria – A Family Holiday Worth the Drive

Lorne is a proper seaside town on Victoria's famous Great Ocean Road, and it ticks the boxes for families looking to mix beach time with bush walking. The main beach is patrolled by lifeguards during summer, with gentle waves that suit younger swimmers, while Lorikeet Beach offers rockpools perfect for kids to explore. Beyond the sand, the hinterland is packed with walking tracks through tall timber and fern gullies – some short enough for little legs, others better for parents needing a proper stretch. Popular spots include Sheoak Falls and Erskine Falls, where you can swim in freshwater pools on warmer days.

The town itself is manageable for families without being too touristy, with fish and chips shops, cafes, and a decent range of accommodation including the Lorne Foreshore Caravan Park for those after budget-friendly beachfront stays. You'll find enough to keep kids entertained – rock hopping, spotting local wildlife like kookaburras and wallabies, and having a hit at mini golf – without the crowds you'd get at more famous destinations. It's the kind of place where you can actually relax while the kids burn off energy, and you're positioned perfectly for a day trip along the Great Ocean Road if you fancy it.

LOCAL KNOWLEDGE:
# Visiting Lorne with Kids: Local Knowledge

The best time to visit is outside school holidays if you can swing it – Christmas and Easter get absolutely rammed, and you'll pay premium prices for accommodation and restaurants. Late autumn (March-April) and spring (September-October) are golden: the weather's mild, crowds are manageable, and you can actually find a park. Summer's ripper if you don't mind the chaos, but bring plenty of sunscreen and get to the beach early. Winter can be gloomy and rainy, so unless your kids are keen hikers, save it for the warmer months. Book accommodation well ahead – everything fills up fast, especially anything close to the beach.

Here's what actually matters: the rock pools at Fairhaven Beach are incredible for kids and worth timing with low tide; download a tide chart before you go. Erskine Falls is a must-do walk but it's muddy as hell and gets slippery – proper shoes are non-negotiable. The main beach gets patrolled by lifeguards in summer, but the rips are real, so watch your kids carefully and swim between the flags. Lorne can feel pricey for families – the cafes are good but not cheap – so grab supplies from the IGA or visit the fish and chip shop on the main drag instead. The Great Ocean Road can get gridlocked on weekends; if you're driving through, go early morning or late afternoon.

The real gem locals know about is hitting the local playground and reserve areas away from the main tourist drag – they're genuinely nice and usually quiet. Allenvale Reserve has good walking tracks that aren't as crowded as Erskine Falls. Watch out for the Apollo Bay road in fog – it pops up without warning and visibility drops to nothing. Finally, bring wet gear even in summer; the Southern Ocean's cold, and if your kids want to actually swim rather than paddle, they'll need it. The town itself is friendly and family-oriented, which is why we all stay.

DESTINATION SUMMARY:
Lorne's holiday park scene is built almost entirely around its position on the Otway Coast, and the Lorne Foreshore Caravan Park dominates for good reason—it's essentially the only game in town if you want powered sites and the kind of facilities families rely on. What makes Lorne different from other Victorian coastal parks is the sheer pull of the location: you've got the beach literally metres away, the Erskine River running through town, and the Great Otway National Park pressing in from the back. Most parks around here lean heavily into that beachfront positioning because frankly, that's what people come for. You won't find massive resort-style parks with pools and entertainment venues like you get further east; this is a more authentic, rougher-edged coastal experience. The Foreshore park itself runs right along the sand, so caravan sites and cabins have genuine beach access rather than being a ten-minute walk away.

The repeat visitor pattern here is genuinely strong—you'll notice the same faces every school holiday, particularly Victorian families who've been coming for decades. Caravanners make up the bulk of the crowd, everything from grey nomads spending a few days to families with vans who book the same spot for the Christmas school holidays year after year. Motorhome visitors pass through regularly, and increasingly you're seeing people who skip the powered sites and use the river access for their own purposes. The park fills hard during the main school holiday breaks—Christmas, Easter, and winter holidays can be fully booked months in advance—but it stays reasonably busy through the quieter months too because people know they can reliably get close to the beach without dealing with the tourist sprawl of Lorne's town centre.

What keeps families returning is partly habit and partly because Lorne delivers consistently on its basics: safe beach swimming, a functional caravan park with proper amenities, and genuinely good walks into the Otways that you can do in a few hours without needing a full day's expedition. The river's there if the ocean swells get too rough, there's a reasonable pub and café culture without it feeling overrun, and you can actually park a van and walk to the beach rather than driving everywhere. Kids who grew up coming here as families now come back with their own kids. It's not fancy, but it works, and for people who've spent years holiday-parking along the Victorian coast, that reliability is worth more than themed pools and activity schedules.

FAQ:
Q: What are the best family holiday parks in Lorne?
A: Lorne Foreshore Caravan Park stands out as a top choice for families, offering direct beach access on the Great Ocean Road. Its central location puts you steps from the main beach, local shops, and restaurants, making it ideal for families wanting convenience without sacrificing the seaside experience. The park's proximity to Lorne's attractions means less driving with restless kids.

Q: Is Lorne Foreshore Caravan Park good for kids?
A: Yes, Lorne Foreshore is excellent for children. The beachfront location allows kids to safely explore rock pools and sandy shores within sight of your accommodation. The park's proximity to playgrounds, the Lorne Pier, and family-friendly cafes makes it easy to keep children entertained throughout the day.

Q: Are there holiday parks with pools near Lorne?
A: While Lorne Foreshore Caravan Park doesn't have an on-site pool, its beachfront position means families have direct access to swimming at Lorne's patrolled beaches during summer months. For heated pool facilities, check nearby parks further along the coast or consider the natural swimming at the beach as your primary water activity.

Q: Which holiday parks in Lorne are near the beach?
A: Lorne Foreshore Caravan Park is literally beachfront, making it the closest park option to Lorne's main swimming beaches. You'll have direct beach access from your site, perfect for families wanting immediate access to the sand and water without loading kids into the car.

Q: Does Lorne have powered caravan sites?
A: Lorne Foreshore Caravan Park offers powered sites suitable for caravans and motorhomes, with electrical hookups available. This allows families to run appliances, charge devices, and enjoy comfortable camping. Book ahead during school holidays, as powered sites fill quickly.

Q: What cabin options are available in Lorne?
A: Lorne Foreshore Caravan Park provides cabin accommodation options alongside caravan sites, offering families a middle ground between camping and hotels. These are ideal if you want easier setup than a tent but prefer the holiday park atmosphere and beach proximity over standard accommodation.

Q: Are there pet-friendly holiday parks in Lorne?
A: Lorne Foreshore Caravan Park welcomes pets, making it perfect for families wanting to bring dogs or cats on holiday. The beachfront location offers great opportunities for pet walks along the shore, though check current pet policies regarding beach access during peak seasons before booking.

Q: What's the best value holiday park in Lorne?
A: Lorne Foreshore Caravan Park offers excellent value considering its beachfront position. Compared to nearby resorts or hotels, caravan park accommodation costs significantly less while providing direct beach access, on-site facilities, and the flexibility to self-cater. Powered sites are more affordable than cabins.

Q: Where should families stay in Lorne?
A: Lorne's foreshore area is the best location for families. Lorne Foreshore Caravan Park puts you in this prime spot, offering beach access, walkable distance to shops and cafes, and close proximity to attractions like the Lorne Pier, playgrounds, and coastal walks without needing constant car trips.

Q: What rainy day activities are available in Lorne?
A: Lorne offers several indoor options for wet weather: the Lorne Community House hosts family activities, local cafes and fish and chip shops provide quick meals, and nearby Erskine Falls features scenic walks through forested areas. The Lorne Pier is accessible regardless of weather, and shops along the main street offer browsing opportunities.
