# Kiama, NSW – A Family Holiday Destination

Kiama on the South Coast of New South Wales offers families a genuine beach holiday without the crowds and chaos of busier coastal towns. The region boasts a string of beautiful beaches perfect for swimming, surfing lessons, and long afternoon walks – Werri Beach and Seven Mile Beach are particular favourites for families wanting safe swimming spots and great facilities. Beyond the sand, the hinterland reveals native bushland ideal for exploring, with walking tracks suitable for kids and plenty of spots to spot local wildlife. The nearby towns have good cafes, fish and chip shops, and local produce markets, so you can eat well without leaving the area.

What makes Kiama genuinely family-friendly is the range of quality holiday parks designed specifically for people travelling with children. Options like BIG4 Easts Beach Holiday Park, Surf Beach Holiday Park, and Kendalls On The Beach offer everything from ensuite cabins to powered sites, plus playgrounds, swimming pools, and organised activities to keep kids entertained. Most parks sit right on or steps from the beach, which means less faffing about with packed cars and more time actually enjoying the ocean. School holidays here feel manageable – you get proper outdoor space, affordable accommodation, and the kind of relaxed pace that lets families actually switch off.

# Visiting Kiama, NSW – Local Family Tips

Kiama's a ripper spot for families, especially if you're after a quieter escape without the crowds. Best time to visit is September through November (spring) or March through May (autumn) – the weather's perfect for getting outdoors and the kids won't be melting in summer heat or dealing with winter dampness. School holidays get busy, so if you can swing it outside those times, you'll have a much better experience. Summer can get scorching, and February's when the bushfire risk kicks up, so plan accordingly.

Here's what you need to know: bring plenty of water and sun protection – the sun's no joke out here, even when it doesn't feel that hot. Stock up on supplies before you leave town because options are limited once you're out this way. The local families love hitting up the quieter spots for picnics, exploring the surrounding bushland, and letting the kids run around without worrying about traffic. Watch out for snakes in warmer months (just keep your eyes open, don't panic), and always let someone know where you're headed. The roads are quiet but can be tricky, so take your time driving.

My insider tip: grab a good local map or download offline maps before you arrive because phone reception can be dodgy. Talk to locals at the general store – they're genuinely helpful and know all the best swimming holes and walking spots that don't make it into guidebooks. Bring bikes if you've got them; the area's perfect for family cycling. And don't expect fancy cafes or restaurants – pack a esky with proper food or plan your meals before you arrive. It's about getting back to basics out here, and families who embrace that have an absolute ball.

DESTINATION SUMMARY:
Kiama's holiday park scene sits in that sweet spot on the South Coast where you've got the beach practically on your doorstep but you're close enough to the Shoalhaven River system that you can shift your focus inland if the ocean swells are pumping or the weather turns. What makes this stretch different from other NSW coastal clusters is the variety packed into a small radius—you can stay beachfront at places like BIG4 Easts and Surf Beach, where the sand's literally metres away, or grab a riverside spot at Werri Beach or Seven Mile Beach if you want calmer water and better shelter. The bush-backed parks like Kendalls give you that semi-rural feel while still being walkable to the beach, so you're not choosing between nature access and coastal views. Most of these aren't flashy resort setups with water features and activities programs; they're solid, unpretentious parks built by people who understand that caravanners and motorhome crews want decent facilities, good sites, and proximity to what matters—which here means the water.

The parks fill a real niche for repeat visitors and families who've worked out that Kiama delivers consistent conditions without the chaos of peak holiday destinations. You'll find plenty of grey nomads who've basically got standing bookings, families who return Easter and winter school holidays like clockwork, and a steady stream of caravaners doing the South Coast loop who know which parks have the best infrastructure and the fewest headaches. School holidays get tight—especially winter and summer breaks when the parks fill weeks in advance—but the demand is predictable enough that regulars have learned to book early. The mix means parks stay busy enough to maintain standards but not so overcrowded that you're packed in like the holiday parks up near Coffs Harbour.

What keeps families and regular visitors coming back is the combination of reliable beach access, river activities, and enough space to actually relax without feeling like you're on top of your neighbours. The kids can walk to Easts Beach or Surf Beach from the top-ranked parks, you've got rock pools and protected swimming spots along the coast, and if you want something quieter, the river options give you kayaking, fishing, and natural bushland without fighting crowds. There's no pretence to it—no themed attractions or forced entertainment—just decent caravan sites, the ocean or river within walking distance, and enough local knowledge floating around the parks that newcomers quickly figure out where to find the best fish and chips, where the swell's pumping, or which beach is best for families with young kids. That straightforward approach is exactly why people come back year after year.
