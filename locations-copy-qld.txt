HEADING:
Best Family Holiday & Caravan Parks in Airlie Beach

HERO_INTRO:
Planning a family trip to Airlie Beach? We compared the best family holiday and caravan parks in Airlie Beach so you can quickly find the right park for your kids, budget and travel style.
Airlie is the launchpad for the Whitsundays, and the smart family move is a park with a proper pool and splash pad, because between boat trips the lagoon and the pool carry the holiday.

LOCAL_KNOWLEDGE:
The free lagoon on the Airlie foreshore is the town's family centrepiece, patrolled, shaded and surrounded by playgrounds and BBQs. It matters because ocean swimming here runs into stinger season from roughly November to May. Discovery Parks is the strongest family setup with its splash pad waterpark and jumping pillow, and both Discovery and Tasman run daily bird feeding that younger kids build their mornings around. Whitehaven Beach and reef day trips leave from Port of Airlie and Coral Sea Marina; the half-day Whitehaven runs suit younger kids better than full-day reef trips. Book parks and boats well ahead for the June to September dry season.

IF_WE_WERE_BOOKING:
* On-site water play: We'd choose Discovery Parks Airlie Beach for the splash pad waterpark, jumping pillow and bird feeding, and treat the shuttle into town as part of the fun.
* Walk to town: We'd choose NRMA Airlie Beach for the lagoon-style pools within reach of the marina, shops and foreshore playgrounds.
* Our tip: Do the lagoon in the morning before the heat, book a half-day Whitehaven trip rather than a full-day reef trip with kids under 8, and keep one full day for nothing but the park pool. Airlie punishes overplanning.

FAQ:
Q: Is Airlie Beach good for families?
A: Yes. The free patrolled lagoon, foreshore playgrounds and Whitsundays day trips make it one of Queensland's best family bases, especially in the dry season.
Q: Can you swim in the ocean at Airlie Beach?
A: Stinger season runs roughly November to May, so most families swim at the free foreshore lagoon or the park pools instead. The lagoon is patrolled and shaded.
Q: Which Airlie Beach park is best for kids?
A: Discovery Parks Airlie Beach, with a splash pad waterpark, jumping pillow, playground and daily bird feeding. It's a short drive or shuttle from town.
Q: Are Whitehaven Beach trips worth it with kids?
A: Yes, it's the trip they'll remember. Half-day options suit younger children better than full-day reef trips, and the calmer morning runs are easier on small stomachs.
Q: When is the best time to visit Airlie Beach?
A: June to September brings dry, warm days and calm water. It's also peak season, so book parks and boat trips months ahead.
Q: What are the best free family activities?
A: The lagoon, the foreshore playgrounds, the Bicentennial Walkway along the water and watching the boats come and go at the marina.
Q: Do the parks have pools?
A: All three main parks do. NRMA runs two pools including a lagoon-style pool, Discovery adds the splash pad waterpark, and Tasman's pool is warm and family-friendly.
Q: How far are the parks from town?
A: NRMA is closest to the centre. Discovery and Tasman sit a few minutes drive out in bush settings, which buys quieter nights and more space.
Q: What should families do when it rains?
A: Tropical rain usually passes quickly. The games rooms cover an hour, and the lagoon is fine in drizzle. If it sets in, the Proserpine cinema is 25 minutes away.
Q: Is Airlie Beach expensive?
A: Peak dry season prices reflect demand, for parks and especially for boat trips. May and October offer the same weather odds for noticeably less.

HEADING:
Best Family Holiday & Caravan Parks in the Whitsundays

HERO_INTRO:
Planning a family trip to the Whitsundays? We compared the best family holiday and caravan parks across the Whitsundays so you can quickly find the right park for your kids, budget and travel style.
The Whitsundays region runs from Bowen's quiet beaches down through Airlie to Proserpine, and where you base yourself changes the holiday: resort-style parks near the boats, or sleepy beachfront parks with the sand to yourselves.

LOCAL_KNOWLEDGE:
Most families default to Airlie Beach for the boat trips, but the region rewards looking wider. Bowen, 40 minutes north, has some of Queensland's most underrated beaches; Horseshoe Bay is a sheltered, rock-framed cove that feels like a secret, and Rose Bay Beach Caravan Park's magnesium pool is a genuine one-off. Lake Proserpine's park sits beside a free Olympic pool and waterpark, which is outrageous value. Stinger season, roughly November to May, pushes swimming to pools, lagoons and netted areas region-wide. The Airlie lagoon is free and patrolled, and Whitehaven day trips run from Airlie's marinas year-round.

IF_WE_WERE_BOOKING:
* Boat trip base: We'd choose Discovery Parks Airlie Beach for the splash pad, toddler pool and jumping pillow, minutes from the Whitehaven and reef departures.
* Quiet beaches: We'd choose Rose Bay Beach Caravan Park or NRMA Bowen Beachfront for Bowen's sheltered coves, the magnesium pool at Rose Bay and beaches without the Airlie crowds.
* Our tip: Split the stay. A few nights at Airlie for the boats, then a few at Bowen where Horseshoe Bay does the entertaining for free. The two halves feel like different holidays 40 minutes apart.

FAQ:
Q: Where should families stay in the Whitsundays?
A: Airlie Beach for boat trips and facilities, Bowen for quiet beaches and value. Many families split their stay between the two, 40 minutes apart.
Q: Is Bowen worth visiting with kids?
A: Genuinely. Horseshoe Bay is one of Queensland's best small beaches, sheltered and rock-framed, and the parks there are quieter and cheaper than Airlie.
Q: When is stinger season in the Whitsundays?
A: Roughly November to May. Swim at the Airlie lagoon, park pools and netted or sheltered spots during those months, and pack stinger suits for boat trips.
Q: Which park has the best value facilities?
A: Whitsunday Holiday Parks Lake Proserpine sits next to a free Olympic pool and waterpark, which is hard to beat anywhere in Queensland.
Q: Can you see Whitehaven Beach without an overnight boat?
A: Yes, day trips and half-day trips run from Airlie's marinas year-round. Half-day options suit younger kids.
Q: When is the best time to visit?
A: June to September for dry warm days and safe swimming everywhere. Book well ahead, the grey nomad migration and school holidays collide here.
Q: What are the best free family activities?
A: The Airlie lagoon, Horseshoe Bay at Bowen, the Bicentennial Walkway and Bowen's big mango photo stop, which costs nothing and delights kids anyway.
Q: Do the parks take pets?
A: Most in the region do, including Discovery, Tasman, Rose Bay and NRMA Bowen. Peak season rules vary, so confirm when booking.
Q: What should families do when it rains?
A: Tropical showers pass fast. The park games rooms, Proserpine's cinema and a rainy-day run to Bowen's Summergarden Theatre cover the rare wet day.
Q: How far is the Whitsundays from Brisbane or Cairns?
A: Airlie Beach is about 11 hours drive from Brisbane and 7 from Cairns. Proserpine airport, 25 minutes from Airlie, takes the driving out of it.

Best Family Holiday & Caravan Parks in Cairns

HERO_INTRO:
Planning a family trip to Cairns? We compared the best family holiday and caravan parks in Cairns so you can quickly find the right park for your kids, budget and travel style.
Cairns pairs one of Australia's best resort-style family parks with rainforest creek swimming twenty minutes from town, and the free Esplanade lagoon fills every gap in between.

LOCAL_KNOWLEDGE:
BIG4 Cairns Coconut is the park families cross the country for, with a waterpark, multiple pools, mini golf and enough on-site entertainment that some kids never want to leave for the reef. In town, the Esplanade lagoon is free, patrolled and beside Muddy's playground, the best free play space in the north. Ocean swimming is off the table most of the year between stingers and crocs, so the lagoon and park pools do the work. The Kuranda Scenic Railway up and Skyrail back is the classic family day, and reef pontoon trips give kids a stable base for first-time snorkelling. Crystal Cascades and the freshwater creeks handle the hot afternoons.

IF_WE_WERE_BOOKING:
* The full resort: We'd choose BIG4 Ingenia Cairns Coconut, the 88-scored standout, for the waterpark, slides, mini golf and pedal karts. Plan reef and Kuranda days around it, not instead of it.
* Rainforest and creeks: We'd choose Crystal Cascades Holiday Park or Lake Placid Rainforest Retreat for creek swimming, a quieter pace and shaded tropical sites.
* Our tip: Do Kuranda as a loop, train up in the morning, Skyrail back over the canopy in the afternoon. Book a reef pontoon rather than a snorkel-off-the-boat trip for first-timers, the platform changes everything for nervous kids.

FAQ:
Q: Is Cairns good for families?
A: One of the best in the country. A standout resort park, the free Esplanade lagoon, the Kuranda rail and Skyrail loop and reef pontoon trips built for kids.
Q: Which Cairns holiday park is best for kids?
A: BIG4 Ingenia Cairns Coconut, scoring 88/100, with a waterpark, multiple pools, waterslides, splash park, mini golf and pedal karts about 10 to 15 minutes from the centre.
Q: Can you swim in the ocean at Cairns?
A: No, between stinger season and crocodiles, ocean swimming is out. The free Esplanade lagoon, park pools and freshwater spots like Crystal Cascades are the swimming.
Q: What is the Esplanade lagoon?
A: A free, patrolled saltwater lagoon on the Cairns waterfront, next to Muddy's playground. It's the city's family hub and costs nothing.
Q: Are reef trips suitable for young kids?
A: Pontoon-based trips are. The fixed platform offers shade, easy water entry, underwater viewing and a place to retreat, which suits first-time snorkellers far better than boat-only trips.
Q: Is the Kuranda train worth it?
A: Yes, as a loop: scenic railway up, Skyrail cableway back over the rainforest canopy. Kuranda's markets and wildlife parks fill the middle of the day.
Q: Where can kids swim in fresh water?
A: Crystal Cascades, 20 minutes from town, and the creeks near the southern parks. Always obey croc signage and swim where locals swim.
Q: When is the best time to visit Cairns?
A: May to September for dry warm days and lower humidity. The wet season is steamy with afternoon storms, though the lagoon and pools still deliver.
Q: What should families do when it rains?
A: The Cairns Aquarium, Muddy's covered sections, the park games rooms and the Botanic Gardens boardwalks between showers. Tropical rain rarely cancels a whole day.
Q: How far ahead should you book?
A: Coconut books out months ahead for school holidays and the dry season. The rainforest parks are easier but still fill for July.

Port Douglas puts the reef and the rainforest in the same holiday: waterslides at Glengarry, Four Mile Beach a few hundred metres from Tropic Breeze, and the Daintree an hour up the road.

LOCAL_KNOWLEDGE:
Glengarry is the family engine room here, with a waterpark, heated pool and pedal carts a short drive from town, while Tropic Breeze trades facilities for position, 300 metres from Four Mile Beach with a new splash park out the back gate. Four Mile's netted swimming enclosure runs through stinger season, roughly November to May, and the patrolled section sits at the town end. The Wildlife Habitat's breakfast with the birds is the best animal experience for younger kids in the north. Mossman Gorge, 20 minutes away, offers cool freshwater swimming, and Daintree river croc cruises land the safety message better than any lecture. Sunday's Anzac Park markets are made for families.

IF_WE_WERE_BOOKING:
* Waterpark and space: We'd choose BIG4 Port Douglas Glengarry for the waterslides, heated pool and spacious tropical sites, and drive the five minutes to the beach.
* Walk to the beach: We'd choose Tropic Breeze Caravan Park, 300 metres from Four Mile Beach with the splash park out the back gate, and accept the cosier sites.
* Our tip: Swim Four Mile early morning at the patrolled town end, do Mossman Gorge in the heat of the day for the cold fresh water, and book the Wildlife Habitat's breakfast with the birds once. It's the photo that ends up framed.

FAQ:
Q: Is Port Douglas good for families?
A: Yes, it's the reef-meets-rainforest base done comfortably. A waterpark park, a beachfront park, Mossman Gorge 20 minutes away and reef trips from the marina.
Q: Which Port Douglas park is best for kids?

Planning a family trip to Mission Beach? We compared the best family holiday and caravan parks in Mission Beach so you can quickly find the right park for your kids, budget and travel style.

This is cassowary country, the best place in Australia to see one wild, so drive slowly and let the kids keep watch; Tropical Hibiscus has wallabies through the park and the odd cassowary nearby. BIG4 South Mission Beach sits 100 metres from the sand with a resort pool and waterslide, the strongest facilities in town. Swimming runs through the netted enclosures during stinger season, roughly November to May, and the beaches are patrolled at the main sections in season. Dunk Island sits 4 kilometres offshore, reachable by water taxi for a day of snorkelling and the Muggy Muggy walk. The Wednesday and Saturday markets at Porter Promenade are small and good.

* Wildlife and quiet: We'd choose Tropical Hibiscus for the wallabies, spotless amenities and easy walk to Wongaling Beach, or Mission Beach Camping for long beach walks straight off the site.
* Our tip: Take the water taxi to Dunk Island for a day. The sheltered side snorkels well for beginners, the Muggy Muggy track is short enough for young legs, and being on an island beats almost anything on the mainland for a seven-year-old.

* Our tip: If you're here between July and October, book a morning whale cruise. The bay is a humpback nursery and calves breach close to the boats. It's the single best whale watching in the country and the kids will never forget it.

HEADING:
Best Family Holiday & Camping on K'gari (Fraser Island)

HERO_INTRO:
Planning a family trip to K'gari? We put together what families need to know about visiting and camping on the world's largest sand island, from barge access to dingo-safe camping.
K'gari is a 4WD-only island of freshwater lakes, rainforest growing out of sand and beach highways. It rewards preparation more than any other Queensland family trip, and repays it double.

LOCAL_KNOWLEDGE:
There are no conventional holiday parks with powered sites in the usual sense on K'gari; families either camp at QPWS campgrounds, some with dingo-deterrent fencing, stay at the island's resorts, or base themselves at Hervey Bay or Rainbow Beach and visit by day tour. Barges run from River Heads near Hervey Bay and from Inskip Point near Rainbow Beach; both need a 4WD and permits booked ahead. Lake McKenzie's white sand and clear water is the family centrepiece, Eli Creek is a lazy float that kids repeat for hours, and the Maheno wreck breaks up the 75 Mile Beach drive. Take dingo safety seriously: keep kids within arm's reach, never carry food openly, and use the fenced campgrounds with young children.

IF_WE_WERE_BOOKING:
* First K'gari trip: We'd base at Hervey Bay or Rainbow Beach and take a guided day tour. The operators handle the sand driving, tides and permits while you handle the swimming at Lake McKenzie.
* Camping with kids: We'd book a dingo-fenced QPWS campground and treat the fence as non-negotiable with children under 12, planning drives around the tide chart.
* Our tip: Eli Creek is the moment. Walk the boardwalk upstream, drop into the clear fresh water and float back to the beach. Kids do it on repeat until you physically remove them, so plan lunch there and surrender.

FAQ:
Q: Can families visit K'gari without a 4WD?
A: Yes, guided day tours and multi-day tours run from Hervey Bay and Rainbow Beach and handle all the sand driving and permits. It's the easiest first visit.
Q: Is K'gari safe for kids with dingoes around?
A: With the rules followed, yes. Keep children within arm's reach, never leave food out, walk in groups and camp behind the dingo-deterrent fences with young kids.
Q: Where can kids swim on K'gari?
A: The freshwater lakes, especially Lake McKenzie and Lake Birrabeen, and Eli Creek. The ocean beach is not for swimming, between rips, sharks and no patrols.
Q: How do you get a vehicle to the island?
A: Barges from River Heads near Hervey Bay and Inskip Point near Rainbow Beach. You need a genuine 4WD, tyre deflation gear and vehicle and camping permits booked ahead.
Q: What is Eli Creek?
A: A fast-flowing freshwater creek on the eastern beach where you walk up a boardwalk and float back down. It's the island's most repeated family activity.
Q: When is the best time to visit?
A: April to October for mild weather and firmer beach driving. Summer brings heat and heavier rain.
Q: Do you need to plan around tides?
A: Yes, the eastern beach is the island's highway and sections close around high tide. Every driving day starts with the tide chart.
Q: How long should a family spend on K'gari?
A: A day tour covers the highlights. Camping families usually stay two to four nights, which allows the lakes, Eli Creek and Champagne Pools without rushing.
Q: What should you pack that people forget?
A: A tide chart, cash-free permit confirmations downloaded offline, insect repellent, and more drinking water than seems reasonable. Phone coverage is patchy.
Q: Can you buy supplies on the island?
A: Limited and expensive supplies exist at island stores. Stock up fully at Hervey Bay or Rainbow Beach before the barge.

Best Family Holiday & Caravan Parks in Bundaberg

Planning a family trip to Bundaberg? We compared the best family holiday and caravan parks around Bundaberg so you can quickly find the right park for your kids, budget and travel style.
Bundaberg's family headline isn't the rum, it's the turtles: Mon Repos beach hosts Australia's most accessible sea turtle nesting and hatching season, and the beach suburbs of Bargara and Moore Park do the swimming.

LOCAL_KNOWLEDGE:
From November to late March, Mon Repos runs ranger-guided night encounters where families watch loggerhead turtles nest or hatchlings dash for the water, book tickets the moment they release because it is the single best wildlife experience on the coast for kids. The beach suburbs beat town for a family base: Moore Park Beach has patrolled swimming in season and a quiet holiday park, and Burnett Heads suits fishing and cycling families near the marina. The Bundaberg Barrel's ginger beer tour is the kid-appropriate version of the drink tourism, and lady Musgrave reef day trips run from the port for families with confident swimmers.

IF_WE_WERE_BOOKING:
* Beach base: We'd choose Moore Park Beach Holiday Park for patrolled seasonal swimming, a quiet foreshore and the small pool for the hot afternoons.
* Fishing and cycling: We'd choose Burnett Heads Holiday Park near the marina and river mouth, where the entertainment is rods, bikes and wildlife rather than waterslides.
* Our tip: Build the whole trip around a Mon Repos turtle encounter between November and March. Book online the day tickets open, bring warm layers for the wait, and let the kids stay up late. Nothing else in Bundaberg comes close.

FAQ:
Q: What is Mon Repos and why does it matter?
