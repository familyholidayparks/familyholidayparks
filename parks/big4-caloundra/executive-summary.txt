PARK: BIG4 Caloundra Waterfront Holiday Park
SCORE: 88/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-28
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 440

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (19/20) — Reviews consistently highlight the "spectacular" heated pool complex with water slides that keep kids entertained for hours, complemented by jumping pillow, pedal karts, daily activities coordinated by "amazing" staff member Nick, kids discos, bingo nights, and poolside restaurant service. Families describe it as "resort-style" with "everything you need" on-site.

Nature (16/20) — The waterfront location provides direct access to coastal walking paths and Bulcock Beach via a back gate, with reviews praising "stunning walks along the coast" and "beautiful water views." The park features well-maintained gardens with native vegetation and bird-watching opportunities, though it's more suburban than bushland with some road noise.

Cleanliness (15/15) — Overwhelmingly described as "the cleanest campground" with "spotless" and "immaculate" amenities that are "always sparkling clean." The camp kitchen is called "five-star restaurant quality" and multiple cleaning staff are named personally for their "exceptional" work. Reviews repeatedly state "best ever" and "cleanest park in 20 years."

Value (11/15) — Pricing is described as "premium" and "expensive" at $100+ per night for powered sites and $350+ for cabins, with several reviews noting it's "50% more expensive" than comparable parks. However, families who stay repeatedly say "you get what you pay for" and the resort-quality facilities, location, and service justify the cost for many.

Site Size (7/10) — Premium sites with artificial grass are "spacious" and "generous," but tent/camper sites are described as "TINY" and "cramped" with some vans "hanging over onto the road." Drive-through sites exist but can be "very tight" for larger rigs. Cabins are well-sized for families.

Sentiment (10/10) — Exceptional repeat visitation with families returning annually for 3-12+ years. Reviews use phrases like "can't wait to come back," "best park we've stayed in," "already rebooked," and "this is our go-to destination." Net Promoter Score appears extremely high with families actively recommending to friends.

Location (10/10) — Perfect urban waterfront position with Coles/Woolworths "literally across the road," beaches and coastal walks "right outside the back gate," and Caloundra CBD within "easy walking distance." Families praise being able to "walk everywhere" without needing a car, close to Australia Zoo, markets, and Sunshine Coast attractions.

WHY NOT 100:
Premium pricing puts the park out of reach for budget-conscious families, with multiple reviews noting the cost has increased significantly year-over-year and some loyal customers stating they "can't afford to stay here next time." Tent and camper sites along the back fence are small and experience constant traffic noise from the busy intersection, with reviews specifically warning against these sites. Limited dedicated kids playground equipment beyond the jumping pillow, with families noting the lack of traditional play structures for younger children who find the pillow too rough when older kids are present.
