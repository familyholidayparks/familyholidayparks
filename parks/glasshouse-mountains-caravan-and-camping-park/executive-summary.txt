PARK: Glasshouse Mountains Caravan and Camping Park
SCORE: 52/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-24
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 34

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (6/20) — Reviews indicate no structured kids entertainment, activities, or playground facilities. One family mentioned "kids loved running around on the grass" during rain, and communal fire pits provide evening gathering spots, but there's no waterpark, playground equipment, or organized activities for children.

Nature (18/20) — Exceptional marks for natural environment with "stunning views" of Mount Ngungun and Mt Tibrogargan, described as "beautiful environment around" and "surrounded by nature." Located in rainforest setting with tree-lined creek, dam with lily pads and ducks, excellent birdlife with "beautiful sunrise chorus," and "amazing wildlife at night." Ideal base for Glasshouse Mountains hiking trails.

Cleanliness (13/15) — Consistently praised with "spotlessly clean," "extremely clean," and "meticulously clean" facilities. Multiple reviews note "clean toilets, hot showers" with "good water pressure." Recent reviews confirm "very clean" amenities though described as "older" and "basic." Minor concerns about ground maintenance with "holes and dirt mounds" noted.

Value (8/15) — Mixed feedback with some finding it "very reasonably priced" while others complain about pricing. Multiple reviews cite "$20 per person per night is a bit over," "$70 a night for 2 people" described as "appalling," and complaints about "hidden fees" and "expensive considering it's basically someone's back yard." One review praised it as "very reasonably priced" but value concerns are prominent.

Site Size (8/10) — Reviews describe "grands espaces" (large spaces), "lots of space," "plenty of space," and "concrete pads to pull up next to." Sites characterized as having "green grass" with room for kids to run. However, described as "very basic" with limited site amenities and some ground quality issues noted.

Sentiment (7/10) — Generally positive with many "highly recommend" and "will be back" comments. Hosts Ian and Steve receive exceptional praise as "absolute legend" and "accommodating." However, sentiment impacted by value concerns, basic facilities, and some operational issues. Strong repeat intention from nature-focused visitors but mixed overall.

Location (9/10) — Excellent location "in the middle of the glasshouse mountains" with "close to local attractions and hiking trails." Easy access from Brisbane (1.5 hours). Proximity to all Glasshouse Mountains walks praised. Only negative is weekday quarry noise "which can be quite noisy during daylight hours."

WHY NOT 100:
1. Complete absence of family entertainment infrastructure - no playground, no kids' activities, no pool or water features, making it unsuitable for families seeking structured holiday park amenities rather than basic camping.
2. Significant value concerns with multiple reviews citing high per-person pricing ($20-70/night), hidden fees, and poor value compared to other parks, particularly for families with more than 2 people.
3. Very basic facilities limited to bathroom amenities only - no camp kitchen, dump points, bins on-site, washing machine often broken, non-potable water at sites, and only 2 toilets/showers serving the entire park.
