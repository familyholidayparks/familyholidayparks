PARK: Capital Country Holiday Park
SCORE: 66/100
DATE SCORED: 2025-06-24
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 144

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (14/20) — Reviews consistently praised mini golf, pool, playground, soccer pitch, basketball court, and volleyball, with families noting "kids had a wonderful time playing putt putt, soccer and baskets ball" and "lots of kid's activities." However, some reviews from 2023 noted "tennis court was closed up, jumping pillow gone, mini golf in poor conditions," and pool water quality raised concerns with one child getting an ear infection.

Nature (14/20) — The park offers a peaceful rural setting with "plenty of trees," a lake with cabins overlooking it, and wildlife including "rabbits, ducks, kangaroos" visible around the property. Guests appreciated the "lovely estate with duck ponds and pathways" and "quiet leafy location," though it's more rural retreat than natural bushland, with "lots of bird poop" from the many trees mentioned as a minor drawback.

Cleanliness (9/15) — Cleanliness reviews are deeply polarized. Recent positive reviews note "spotlessly clean," "very clean, neat and tidy," and "brand new camp kitchen was spotless." However, significant concerns appear repeatedly: "Filthy public bathroom amenities and laundries with mould in the tile grouting," "mold all on the bathroom floor and walls," "cabin needs proper cleaning," and "dust anywhere" with dead ants in cupboards. Amenities are consistently described as "old but clean" to "very old & dirty/mouldy."

Value (12/15) — Most reviews indicate reasonable value with "very reasonably priced," "good value for money," and cabins at "$110" mentioned positively. Several guests noted it's affordable compared to other Canberra options. However, some felt pricing didn't match quality: "Not Worth it for the price," "a bit pricey," and complaints about facilities being dated for the cost charged.

Site Size (8/10) — Sites generally received positive feedback as "ample sized sites," "great sites heaps of room," "roomy and comfortable," and "spacious parking." The drive-through sites were particularly praised. One negative noted sites were "too close to the sites either side," but overall space was considered adequate to generous for caravans and motorhomes.

Sentiment (6/10) — Sentiment is mixed with passionate advocates ("This will be where we stay from now on," "always come back to this park") balanced against harsh critics ("DO NOT WASTE YOUR MONEY staying in this dump," "wouldn't stay here again"). Many repeat visitors love it, especially dog owners, but first-time guests were often disappointed by dated facilities. The polarization suggests inconsistent experiences across different accommodation types and timeframes.

Location (3/10) — Location is the park's weakest aspect, consistently noted as "20 minute drive from town," "a fair way out of Canberra," and "a long way out of town." While some appreciated being "away from the bussel of Canberra," families wanting to tour attractions found the distance inconvenient. One review mentioned airport noise and highway noise, though others found it "quiet." It's positioned for those using Canberra as a day-trip base rather than central convenience.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Amenities are significantly dated with repeated serious mould issues reported in bathrooms and laundries, with management response described as dismissive ("they tried to say it was just old mould stains").
2. The location 20 minutes from central Canberra makes it inconvenient for families wanting easy access to tourist attractions, museums, and city amenities.
3. Cleanliness standards are highly inconsistent across accommodation types, with school cabins particularly criticized for dirty conditions, old furnishings, and poor maintenance that disappoints many families.
