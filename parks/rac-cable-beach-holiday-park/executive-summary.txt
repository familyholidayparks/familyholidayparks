PARK: RAC Cable Beach Holiday Park
SCORE: 68/100
DATE SCORED: 2025-06-10
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 164

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (8/20) — Reviews consistently highlight the complete absence of children's entertainment with multiple guests requesting a playground. One review states "just needs something for kids IE play ground / pillow / adventure play" and another notes "Es hat kein Spielplatz, obwohl in der Umgebung keiner zu finden ist." The pool offers some entertainment but closes at 6pm limiting evening use, and there's a small putt-putt golf facility next door but no on-site kids activities or adventure play equipment.

Nature (17/20) — Exceptional beach access with Cable Beach directly across the road, described as "2 minute drive" or "400m walk to the patrolled area." Multiple reviews praise walking to the beach for sunsets, with one stating "we were a 2 minute drive from the beach where we would meet mates, swim, have some drinks and dinner and watch the beautiful sunset which never got old." The park features established shady trees throughout and guests mention seeing "wildlife blue tounge, tata and frilled neck lizards." The natural sand dune environment and proximity to iconic Cable Beach delivers strong nature credentials.

Cleanliness (11/15) — Mixed feedback with newer amenities praised as "stunning" and "very clean" but older facilities criticized. Recent reviews note "the cleanest and best showers/toilets so far on our roadtrip" and "amenities are always very clean" for the main block. However, cabin cleanliness receives multiple complaints: "cabin was dirty with strands of long black hair," "kitchen was very greasy," "fridge was extremely dirty." One review states the amenities "need a good scrub to keep it looking new, it is getting dated and stained very quickly." Pool maintenance also criticized with hair left for days and chlorination issues.

Value (9/15) — Significant concerns about pricing with multiple reviews calling it "very expensive" and "overpriced." One family paid "$2,000 for 7 nights" in a cabin, another "$1400 for 4 nights" expecting better quality. Powered sites at "$80 per night" and "$65 for a sandy dusty campsite" viewed as excessive. Several reviews note better value available at competing parks nearby. The location premium is acknowledged but many feel the price doesn't match the experience, particularly given tight sites, lack of kids facilities, and inconsistent cabin quality. RAC membership benefits appear limited with members not receiving preferential treatment.

Site Size (5/10) — Overwhelmingly negative feedback about site size and access. Reviews repeatedly describe sites as "tight," "packed in," "squished in like sardines," and "the tightest sites we've seen in our 5 and a half years of travelling around Australia." Multiple guests struggled with access: "roads are barely wider than a van," "near impossible to reverse in," "took 20 minutes of manoeuvring to even get out." Smaller campers and rooftop vehicles allocated the worst sites despite same pricing. One review notes "our neighbour just for eg had to get the neighbours across from him to move their car out so he could move his van over." Trees create additional obstacles. Only positive mentions are for select larger sites when park is quiet.

Sentiment (8/10) — Polarized sentiment with passionate supporters and disappointed critics. Positive reviews show strong loyalty: "10/10 from us," "we have booked again for next year," "second time staying here and we will return." Multiple guests extended their stays. However, significant negative sentiment from families: "would never recommend it to anyone," "won't be coming back," "honestly it was not relaxing at all." Customer service issues with specific staff members (Maurice/Monica) generate strong negative reactions. The park maintains high occupancy and repeat bookings from grey nomads, but families express frustration with lack of child facilities and tight sites.

Location (10/10) — Outstanding location scores with universal praise for Cable Beach proximity. Reviews consistently highlight "amazing location right across the street from Cable Beach," "500 metre walk to Cable Beach," "just across the road from the famous cabel beach." Convenient access to Divers Tavern pub, general store with cafe, and bus stop directly outside. Walk to restaurants and amenities. "Everything is only a 10min drive away." The beachside position is the park's strongest asset with families able to easily access the patrolled beach, sunset viewing, and camel rides. Location alone drives many bookings despite other shortcomings.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Complete absence of children's entertainment facilities — Multiple reviews from families specifically request a playground, adventure play, or any kids entertainment, with one noting "Es hat kein Spielplatz, obwohl in der Umgebung keiner zu finden ist und es auf dem Campingplatz genug Möglichkeiten da wären um einen zu erstellen," meaning there's no playground despite space being available and none in the surrounding area, which is a critical gap for a family holiday park.

2. Extremely tight and poorly designed site access — Reviews consistently describe the sites as "the tightest we've seen in 5 and a half years," with families taking "20 minutes of manoeuvring to even get out," roads "barely wider than a van," and neighbors unable to open awnings or needing others to move cars to maneuver, creating significant stress for families with larger rigs that contradicts the relaxation expected at a holiday park.

3. Inconsistent cleanliness and maintenance, particularly cabins — While the main amenities block receives praise, multiple cabin reviews detail "dirty kitchen utensils," "extremely dirty" fridges with sand, "strands of long black hair clinging to the bathroom floor," "pink mould all over" showers, and "filthy" rangehoods, indicating quality control issues that undermine the premium pricing and RAC brand standards families expect.
