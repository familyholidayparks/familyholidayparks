PARK: Kendalls On The Beach Holiday Park
SCORE: 72/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-03-25
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 87

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (10/20) — Reviews indicate no on-site kids entertainment facilities or activities program. Families mention a "public children's play park that has a slide and swings not too far from the beach" and describe it as "great for young families" with the beach itself being the main entertainment. Multiple reviews note "perfect for all aged kids with walks, beach and playground" but entertainment is entirely nature-based rather than organized activities.

Nature (19/20) — Exceptional natural environment scores with consistent praise for "right on the beautiful Kendall's beach," "absolute beach frontage," "stunning quiet beach," and "gorgeous beach, great location, lots of walks and places to see." The park is described as "beautifully leafy and incredibly relaxing" with "lovely beach and great walks around the foreshore." Only minor deduction for one review noting "Beach is nice although not good for young kids with waves crashing right in beach."

Cleanliness (14/15) — Strong cleanliness ratings with frequent mentions of "amenities are spotless," "immaculately clean," "5 star amenities," and "always clean and well maintained." Recent reviews consistently praise "clean amenities" and "immaculate" facilities. Minor points lost for a Feb 2026 review noting "amenities were only moderately clean" and Feb 2026 comment that "toilets and bathrooms could do with a spruce up."

Value (11/15) — Mixed value perception with some noting "$45 for 2 of us to stay on an unpowered site, which is great considering the location" while others comment "a little bit expensive" and "$60pn for a powered site which is up there." Several reviews mention pricing concerns, with one noting "all sites are the same price ($60) regardless of whether your site has torn matting, is uneven and your view is the row of 12 bins." The beachfront location justifies pricing for most but not universally perceived as excellent value.

Site Size (7/10) — Moderate site size scores with mixed feedback. Positive mentions include "bigger than usual pitches," "big sites with heaps of room," and "good size." However, multiple concerns noted: "camp sites are a little crowded," "everyone too close to each other," "spot 41, which was so close to number 40 that our slide-out was under their awning," "only small, very narrow to get in," and "camp has lots of cabins and only 15 camp-sites." Space adequate but not exceptional.

Sentiment (9/10) — Very positive overall sentiment with exceptional loyalty shown by "21st year at Kendall's," "past 4 years" annual visits, and "rebooked for 2026." Frequent mentions of "will definitely stay again," "we'll be back," and "would definitely return." Strong praise for staff being "friendly and welcoming" and "management team went out of their way." Points lost due to several negative experiences with COVID policy, neighbour noise complaints, and management described as "a bit of a dragon."

Location (10/10) — Perfect location scores with "prime position," "excellent location," "walking distance into town" (15-20 minute walk), "easy walk into Kiama," and "close drive to Blow hole and main shops." Described as "immaculately located within the city centre of Kiama" with "walking track that takes you straight to Kiama shops." Direct beach access is the standout feature mentioned in virtually every review.

WHY NOT 100:
1. No on-site kids entertainment or activity programs - families rely entirely on natural environment and must create their own entertainment, with no pool, waterpark, jumping pillow, games room, or organized activities that Gold-rated parks typically offer.

2. Significant noise disturbance from neighbouring properties - multiple recent reviews (2026, 2025) detail serious issues with "extremely loud music across the entire campground all day long" from adjacent residents, with one reviewer stating "this completely ruined what could have been a peaceful weekend" and management appearing unable to resolve the issue.

3. Cramped site spacing and inconsistent site quality - several reviews note sites are "a little crowded," "too close to each other," with specific complaints about sites 41, 49, 60, and 61 being substandard, plus the park charging uniform pricing despite significant quality differences between sites.
