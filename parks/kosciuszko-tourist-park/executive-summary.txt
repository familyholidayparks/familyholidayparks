PARK: Kosciuszko Tourist Park
SCORE: 78/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-24
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 185

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (10/20) — Limited structured kids entertainment, though families appreciated the playground, basketball court, recreation room with pool table, and natural wildlife viewing opportunities with kangaroos, wallabies, deer, and possums providing daily entertainment.

Nature (20/20) — Exceptional natural environment set within Kosciuszko National Park among snow gums, with daily wildlife encounters (kangaroos, wallabies, possums, deer), beautiful bushland setting, walking trails on-site and nearby, and stunning views of surrounding mountains and native bush.

Cleanliness (14/15) — Consistently praised for spotlessly clean, modern amenities with heated bathroom blocks, great shower pressure, well-maintained toilets described as "brand new" and "immaculate," though a few reviews mentioned camp kitchen needing updates and occasional maintenance issues.

Value (13/15) — Generally considered good value given the unique national park location and quality facilities, though some felt pricing high for camping sites ($70/night mentioned), extra costs for wifi and national park entry fees ($17/day) noted, with cabin accommodation rated excellent value compared to hotels.

Site Size (9/10) — Sites described as spacious with good privacy in bush setting, adequate room for caravans and trailers, individual fire pits at most sites, though a couple of reviews mentioned needing to ensure correct site selection for larger rigs.

Sentiment (9/10) — Overwhelmingly positive with high repeat visitation rate, families returning yearly, strong recommendations, exceptional praise for manager Rakesh and staff helpfulness, though a small number of negative reviews regarding customer service issues and booking/payment disputes.

Location (10/10) — Perfect location with convenient access to Perisher (15-20 mins), Thredbo (30-40 mins), Charlotte Pass (20-25 mins), Jindabyne (10 mins), numerous hiking trails, and positioned right within the national park for easy exploration.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Very limited on-site kids entertainment and activities beyond the basic playground - no waterpark, kids club, or organized family programs that Australian holiday parks typically offer.
2. Aggressive possum population creates significant issues with food security, requiring families to lock everything in vehicles, with multiple reports of possums climbing on cars, getting into food, and leaving droppings, impacting outdoor dining experiences.
3. Several serious customer service complaints regarding inflexible cancellation policies (no refunds for family emergencies), booking disputes, payment issues with early card charges, and inconsistent staff availability with reports of unwelcoming or rude interactions.

NOTABLE REVIEW PHRASES:
"kangaroos roaming around freely," "spotlessly clean amenities," "perfect location to ski parks during winter and hiking tracks during summer," "beautiful bush setting," "cabins perfect for a family," "heated modern amenities blocks with great shower pressure," "possums extremely friendly," "excellent value for money"
