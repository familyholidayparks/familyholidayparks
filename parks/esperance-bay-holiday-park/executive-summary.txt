PARK: Esperance Bay Holiday Park
SCORE: 68/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-26
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 187

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (8/20) — Limited on-site entertainment with a small children's playground and TV lounge mentioned; families praised the nearby foreshore attractions including "play parks, mini train, and mini golf" within 2 minutes walk, but the park itself offers minimal kids' activities or dedicated entertainment facilities.

Nature (16/20) — Excellent beach and coastal access with the park "opposite the foreshore," "close to the waterfront," and walking distance to Esperance's beautiful beaches and coastline; families enjoyed the natural surroundings and ocean proximity, though the park itself is small with limited green space and some sites lack shade or trees.

Cleanliness (13/15) — Consistently praised as "spotlessly clean," "immaculate bathrooms," and "very well maintained" with facilities cleaned daily; a few recent reviews (2025-2026) mentioned "run down and filthy" camp kitchens and maintenance issues including water damage and mold, suggesting some inconsistency.

Value (12/15) — Mixed feedback with winter specials at $25/night praised as "great value," but peak season pricing of $65 described as "a bit salty" and some feeling it was "overpriced" for what's offered; families appreciated well-equipped cabins and kitchens providing good self-catering value.

Site Size (6/10) — Frequently described as "small sites," "tight," "very close together," with unpowered sites "not much wider than a supermarket parking bay"; some powered sites were "good size" with "plenty of room," but maneuvering larger vans was challenging in this compact park.

Sentiment (8/10) — Predominantly positive with many "would stay again" and "highly recommend" comments, plus families returning multiple times; sentiment was dampened by several 1-star reviews citing poor customer service, booking issues, double-booking incidents, and staff described as "rude" or "unwelcoming" in isolated cases.

Location (9/10) — Exceptional location "close to everything," walking distance to town center, shops, restaurants, and foreshore attractions; "conveniently located" for exploring Esperance and beaches, though proximity to highway and port created noise issues for some sites.

WHY NOT 100:
The park offers minimal on-site entertainment for children beyond a small playground, relying entirely on nearby foreshore attractions rather than providing dedicated kids' activities, waterpark, or organized entertainment that family parks typically feature. Site sizes are genuinely small and tight with many reviews noting difficulty maneuvering larger rigs, sites "very close together," and unpowered areas basically on the roadside verge. Recent reviews reveal concerning inconsistencies in cleanliness standards and customer service, including reports of "filthy" facilities, double-bookings, unwelcoming staff interactions, and unresolved maintenance issues that contradict the historically strong cleanliness reputation.
