PARK: Riverside Tourist Park
SCORE: 52/100
DATE SCORED: 2024-01-15
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 110

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (4/20) — Reviews consistently report no organized activities or kids entertainment, with the games room described as "creepy" with "magazines from 2017" and "smells like something not cleaned in a long time." Families noted "no activities planned for people staying" and the overall entertainment offering is essentially non-existent for young children.

Nature (13/20) — The park sits directly on the Fitzroy River with "plenty of trees" providing shade and a "tropical feeling garden" atmosphere. Families appreciated the riverside location for sunsets and the natural bush setting, though this comes with significant mosquito and sandfly infestations that multiple reviews warned about. River access for fishing was noted as limited.

Cleanliness (7/15) — Reviews are deeply divided: some found amenities "always clean and tidy" with "lovely hot showers," while many others reported "terrible amenities block. So outdated and filthy," "bathrooms were filthy," and "camp kitchen had no plugs for washing up and was dirty and uncared for." The consensus suggests inconsistent cleaning standards in aging facilities.

Value (10/15) — At $38-42 per night, families acknowledged "great value for money" and "cheap accommodation" for the central location. However, multiple reviews questioned value, stating "for $41/night, no value for money" and "It's cheap - for a reason," with cabin stays deemed overpriced at $160 for the condition provided.

Site Size (4/10) — This was a major complaint across reviews: "tight campsites right on top of each other," "super tight space for the van. Could not put awning out," "sites are small," and "some sites extremely narrow and tight." Families with larger rigs consistently struggled with inadequate space.

Sentiment (6/10) — Mixed family sentiment with some repeat visitors appreciating the no-frills approach, but many families stated they "wouldn't go again," "would not visit again," and "won't be back." Service issues were common with reports of unprofessional staff, unanswered phones, and inflexible policies that frustrated families.

Location (8/10) — The park's strongest asset is its proximity to Rockhampton CBD with "walking distance to city centre," "15 minute walk to pubs and restaurants," and "close to all shopping centres." However, this urban location brings significant traffic noise from the nearby Fitzroy bridge that disrupted sleep for many families.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Complete absence of kids entertainment or activities with a neglected games room that families found unusable, making this unsuitable as a destination for families with young children seeking engagement.
2. Severely compromised site sizes that prevent awning deployment and create cramped conditions, with families reporting feeling "squashed" by neighboring vans and inadequate space for typical family camping setups.
3. Deeply inconsistent cleanliness and maintenance standards in aging facilities, with multiple families reporting filthy bathrooms, broken fixtures, deteriorating cabins with rot, and an overall impression that "owners have lost interest" in upkeep.

NOTABLE REVIEW PHRASES:
"Terrible amenities block. So outdated and filthy!", "tight campsites right on top of each other", "infested with sandflies", "No activities planned for people staying", "games room was creepy", "owners have lost interest", "Walking distance to city centre", "Cheap accommodation great value"
