PARK: Aspen Holidays Highway1
SCORE: 62/100
DATE SCORED: 2024-01-15
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 108

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (10/20) — Limited on-site kids entertainment with playgrounds on both sides of the park and a recreational room with arcade machines (though some reportedly malfunction). One standout review praised "fantastic holiday activities for kids over the Xmas and New Years period" including wildlife shows, but most reviews made no mention of structured activities. The pools appear to be the main attraction, with kids not wanting to leave.

Nature (14/20) — The park features "beautifully manicured gardens" with palm trees creating a "tropical rain forest" setting around one pool area. Multiple reviews describe it as "like being in our own little paradise" and "a little oasis." However, the nearby Bolivar sewerage treatment plant creates occasional "pungent smell" issues that significantly detract from the natural environment experience, particularly affecting certain cabin areas.

Cleanliness (10/15) — Highly inconsistent cleanliness standards across different accommodation types. Recent reviews show polarized experiences: many praise "spotless" cabins and "clean amenities," while others report "dirty walls covered in dirt," "mouldy roller blinds," "ripped flooring," and even lice incidents. Amenities blocks receive generally positive feedback for cleanliness, though busy periods strain maintenance schedules. The park appears to be improving under new management since 2023.

Value (9/15) — Mixed value perceptions with several families finding it "affordable and not too over priced" and "greatly priced," while others complained it was "extremely expensive" at $600 for two nights in a dog-friendly cabin. International visitors found it the "most expensive campsite" on their Australian journey with experience not matching price. Positive value mentions often tied to location convenience rather than amenities quality.

Site Size (8/10) — Caravan sites consistently praised as "large and level," "massive sites," "big sites," and "drive through with ample area to park car." One negative review mentioned Pierre's sites being "very small." Most families with caravans found adequate space, though cabin parking varies. Sites are gravel-based which prevents mud but some prefer grass.

Sentiment (6/10) — Sentiment heavily weighted by recency shows improvement trajectory. Loyal repeat visitors spanning 4+ years contrast with recent first-time disappointments. Staff universally praised as "amazing," "friendly," and "accommodating." Major detractors include permanent resident dominance making it feel less like a tourist park, inconsistent cabin quality, sewage odours, and wifi connectivity issues. Roughly 60% would return, 30% disappointed, 10% neutral.

Location (5/10) — Highway-adjacent location is double-edged: convenient for transit and "40 mins for Central Adelaide" but brings constant traffic noise despite some saying "you do not hear much noise." Proximity to shops and fuel (Ampol) praised. The Bolivar sewerage plant location significantly impacts appeal. Not near beach, bush walks, or natural attractions. Functions well as overnight stop or Adelaide base rather than destination location.

WHY NOT 100:
The park suffers from significant location challenges with the nearby Bolivar sewerage treatment plant creating persistent odour issues that multiple international and domestic reviewers cited as deal-breakers, fundamentally compromising the holiday experience. Secondly, cabin quality and cleanliness are wildly inconsistent—while some guests found "spotless" accommodation, others encountered dirty walls, mould, maintenance issues, lice, and structural problems, suggesting inadequate quality control systems. Finally, the park has transitioned heavily toward permanent residents rather than maintaining tourist park standards, with multiple reviews noting it's "not really a tourist park these days" and permanent sites looking "trashy," which reduces the family holiday atmosphere.
