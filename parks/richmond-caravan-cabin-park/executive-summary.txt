PARK: Richmond Caravan & Cabin Park
SCORE: 58/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-15
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 66

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (8/20) — Limited kids facilities with an indoor heated pool that receives mixed feedback ("pool is ok but not more"), a games room with "aged games that need replacement", outdoor play equipment mentioned briefly, and a half basketball court. One family noted "the pool was great for the grandkids" but overall entertainment offerings are minimal for young families.

Nature (14/20) — Pleasant rural setting "in the middle of winefield and goat farm", with guests enjoying views of "sheep and lambs in a field", close proximity to Coal Valley wineries and scenic areas. The location provides a peaceful country atmosphere near Richmond's historic village, though the park itself was described as a "dust bowl" with limited grass in some reviews.

Cleanliness (13/15) — Consistently praised with phrases like "immaculate bathroom and toilets", "facilities are spotless", "amenities highly clean", and "very clean and tidy". However, one notable complaint stated "camp kitchen were disgusting could do with a good clean", preventing a higher score.

Value (8/15) — Mixed feedback on pricing with rates of $45-55 per night for powered sites described as "a bit expensive", "way over priced", and "just not worth it" by several reviewers. Some found it reasonable value, but the inconsistent service quality diminishes perceived value for families.

Site Size (7/10) — Sites described as "good roomy sites", "spacious", "plenty of room for big rigs", and "large caravan spaces". However, some guests were placed in less desirable locations "way down the back behind the pool shed 80 mtrs from toilet block", affecting the overall site allocation experience.

Sentiment (4/10) — Severely impacted by multiple reports of poor customer service. Reviews describe staff as "extremely rude and aggressive", "horrible people", "not very welcoming or helpful", with families with young children specifically stating it's "not a nice place for a young family". While some had positive experiences, the pattern of negative service interactions significantly damages overall sentiment.

Location (4/10) — Well-positioned near Richmond (14-minute walk) and 20 minutes to Hobart, close to wineries, zoo, and golf course. However, accessibility concerns noted including "not safe" wheelchair access on roadside paths, and the location is more suited as a touring base rather than a destination itself.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Significant and repeated customer service failures with multiple families reporting aggressive, rude, and unwelcoming treatment from management, particularly impacting families with young children and creating an uncomfortable atmosphere that deters recommendations.

2. Minimal on-site entertainment and activities for children with outdated games room equipment, basic pool facilities, and limited organized activities, making it unsuitable for families wanting kids to be entertained at the park itself.

3. Value proposition undermined by pricing that reviewers found expensive relative to service quality and amenities offered, with inconsistent site allocation and booking management issues including confirmed reservations not being honored.
