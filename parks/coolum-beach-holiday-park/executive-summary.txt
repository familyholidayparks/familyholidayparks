PARK: Coolum Beach Holiday Park
SCORE: 68/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-20
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 173

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (6/20) — Reviews consistently note there is "no pool or kids playground" and "absolutely none of that here - just the site & bathrooms and camp kitchen." Multiple families stated "it's not very kid friendly" with no evening activities, games rooms, or organized entertainment. The park offers only a camp kitchen and basic facilities, with one reviewer noting "no extra touches to make the park special."

Nature (18/20) — The park scores exceptionally well for beach access, with reviews praising "best beach and easy access," "right on the beach," "short stroll over the dune to the patrolled beach," and "2 minutes to the beach from anywhere in the park." Dog-friendly beach access is highlighted repeatedly. A nearby skate park adds minor value. The only limitation is lack of bushland or creek environments.

Cleanliness (13/15) — Amenities receive strong praise: "super clean," "spotless," "very clean and well maintained," "amenities always clean and serviced," and "clean as a whistle." However, some reports of "slab was filthy on arrival," occasional toilet light failures, and "amenities nice but could be cleaner" prevent a perfect score.

Value (8/15) — Pricing is a major complaint throughout reviews: "daylight robbery," "overpriced," "way overpriced," "most expensive park we have stayed at," with costs ranging $74-$155+ per night. Additional fees frustrate guests: "$10 to park your van with tractor," "$5 per dog per night," "$6 washing machine." Many note "nothing special about the caravan park other than location" and compare unfavorably to parks with pools and activities at lower prices.

Site Size (6/10) — Site size generates significant criticism: "sites are too small for large vans," "very tight," "cramped in like sardines," "sites a bit cramped," "some almost impossible to get into." Many require "tractor to park vans" with associated fees. However, some premium sites are described as "enormous" and "more than big enough," indicating variability.

Sentiment (8/10) — Mixed sentiment with loyal returners ("27 years," "10 years," "30 years") balanced by those who "won't be going back" due to price increases and service issues. Positive phrases include "would happily stay again," "will definitely be returning," but significant negative experiences with staff ("extremely rude," "abusive and intolerant") and value concerns dampen overall satisfaction.

Location (9/10) — Location is the park's standout feature: "best location," "perfect location," "excellent location," "handy to everything." Reviews praise proximity to "shops across the road," "Coles, Dan Murphys, pub, restaurants," "surf club," and "walking distance to everything." The only minor limitation is road noise on some boundary sites.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Complete absence of on-site kids' entertainment, pools, playgrounds, or organized activities that families expect at premium-priced parks, with multiple reviews stating it's "not very kid friendly" and offers no facilities beyond basic amenities.
2. Site sizing and accessibility issues requiring paid tractor assistance ($10 fee) for many sites, with widespread complaints about cramped, difficult-to-access spaces that are "too small for large vans" and feel like "sardines in a tin can."
3. Persistent customer service problems with reception staff described as "very rude," "unfriendly," "abusive," "seriously intense," and "not interested in any customer service," combined with poor communication (unanswered calls, unreturned messages) and inflexible booking policies that frustrate guests during health emergencies and special circumstances.
