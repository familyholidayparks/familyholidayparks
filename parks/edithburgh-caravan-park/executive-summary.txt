PARK: Edithburgh Caravan Park
SCORE: 73/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-20
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 140

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (12/20) — Reviews consistently mention a jumping pillow, small playground, and giant chess board for kids, with families praising Easter activities organized by management including free entertainment and pancakes. However, multiple reviews note equipment issues ("chess set was broken and bouncy pillow was deflated") and several families specifically requested "a heated pool would be great for an extra activity" indicating limited water-based entertainment for young children compared to competitor parks.

Nature (16/20) — Strong coastal location with "beautiful sea views" and "great location by water with easy walks north and south on paths and beach." Families enjoyed spotting dolphins, pelicans, and wildlife, with access to two jetties for fishing and crabbing. However, multiple reviews note "there is no beach to speak of" and the focus is more on jetty/foreshore access than natural swimming beaches or bushland exploration that young families typically seek.

Cleanliness (14/15) — Exceptional performance with overwhelming praise: "spotlessly clean park," "amenities were extremely well maintained every single day," "one of the cleanest caravan parks I have ever stayed in," and "immaculate amenities, so accolades to the cleaners." Minor points deducted for isolated incidents including sanitary bins not emptied for 24+ hours and one review noting facilities "not cleaned sufficiently or frequently enough" during school holidays, plus older reviews mentioning "toilets and showers are completely old."

Value (11/15) — Mixed feedback with some praising "great and priced well" and "great value with GDay membership," but notable complaints about pricing: "$190 later... very small I mean tiny superior cabin," "$175! We paid less than this for a 4-star hotel room in Adelaide CBD," and "$195 per night for 3 nights it was a very very small room. Not worth it at all!" Several reviews suggest cabin value doesn't match pricing, though powered site rates appear more competitive.

Site Size (9/10) — Consistently positive with "large sites," "spacious grassy powered site," "big sites," "nice big level sites," and "the park itself is lovely and well spread out, so you don't feel cramped." Only minor deduction for one review mentioning "campsites were cramped and covered in gravel" in a specific area, and a few noting some sites are gravel rather than grass.

Sentiment (9/10) — Very strong positive sentiment with high repeat visitation: "been our go to for the past couple of years," "we will be back," "definitely stay again," and "10/10 could not recommend highly enough." Management Sarah and Damien receive exceptional praise for hospitality and community-building. Points lost due to several strongly negative 1-star reviews about poor customer service interactions, rigid cancellation policies, and one reviewer warning the office staff was "extremely rude."

Location (9/10) — Excellent coastal positioning "right on the water" with "walking distance to town," jetty, pubs, cafes, and IGA. Families appreciated using it as a "base camp for day trips" around Yorke Peninsula. However, the remote location means "the town has very poor signal" requiring WiFi reliance, "everything closes early," and "not much action anywhere on Yorke Peninsula early in the week" which can limit convenience for families with young children needing supplies or entertainment options.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Limited active entertainment for young children — no pool/waterpark facilities, basic playground equipment with recurring maintenance issues (deflated jumping pillow, broken chess set), significantly reducing appeal for families seeking daily structured kids' activities compared to resort-style parks.
2. Cabin value concerns with multiple families reporting very small, dated cabins at premium prices ($175-$195/night) that don't justify the cost, particularly when compared to hotel alternatives with more amenities.
3. Several concerning customer service incidents including rigid cancellation policies during genuine emergencies and reports of rude office interactions, creating risk of poor experiences despite generally excellent management by Sarah and Damien.
