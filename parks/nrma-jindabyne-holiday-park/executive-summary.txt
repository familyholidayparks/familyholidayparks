PARK: NRMA Jindabyne Holiday Park
SCORE: 76/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-22
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 186

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (14/20) — Reviews mention modest but valued activities including Easter activities for kids, pizza nights with live music, outdoor movie screenings, reading room, TV room, games room with pool table, and playground. Staff member Naomi specifically praised for organizing kids activities. However, multiple families noted limited facilities for older children compared to other NRMA parks, and entertainment offerings are seasonal rather than extensive year-round.

Nature (18/20) — Exceptional lakeside setting consistently praised with "beautiful location right beside the lake," "stunning views," "perfect for kids to explore nature safely," peaceful river nearby, and walking/bike tracks around Lake Jindabyne. Easy access to Kosciuszko National Park, Snowy Mountains, and off-leash dog areas. Minor deductions for mosquitos in summer and bee/wasp issues reported during Easter period.

Cleanliness (12/15) — Mixed feedback with many praising "spotlessly clean amenities," "very clean showers and toilets," and bath mats provided in showers (frequently mentioned positive). However, significant concerns raised about cabin cleanliness including dust, cobwebs, dirt on floors, unmade beds, dirty kitchen equipment, and stained mattresses. Some reviews noted amenities need more frequent cleaning and facilities showing age.

Value (11/15) — Pricing concerns evident with "$50 p/night for unpowered site is totally unreasonable," "definitely more on the expensive side," "overpriced" cabin accommodation, and "paying $550 a night in peak season" with maintenance issues. Positive mentions of NRMA member discounts and reasonable rates for the location, but overall sentiment suggests pricing doesn't always match facility quality or site size.

Site Size (6/10) — Consistent criticism that "sites are too small for today's bigger vans," "cramped inverted site," "very little room for anything else," sites "narrow and shallow," "tight for caravans," and "50% slope" on some sites. Multiple reviews mention difficulty reversing vans and needing levelling equipment. Unpowered lakefront sites praised for views but lack shade and picnic tables.

Sentiment (8/10) — Generally positive with many "would definitely come back," "highly recommend," and "best site I've stayed at" comments. However, significant volume of 1-star reviews citing rude reception staff, poor customer service, and disappointing experiences. Strong praise for specific staff (Naomi, Julie, Craig) but also complaints about dismissive and unhelpful management. Repeat visitors evident but some long-term guests disappointed by changes.

Location (9/10) — Outstanding marks for "unbeatable location," "right on the lake," "walking distance to shops, restaurants, woolies," "only 33km from Perisher," "30 min drive from Mt Kosciuszko," and central to Snowy Mountains attractions. Minor safety concern about crossing main road to shops. Some sites near main road suffer from truck noise from 5am.

WHY NOT 100:
Site size is a consistent pain point with multiple families reporting sites too small for modern caravans and uneven ground requiring levelling equipment, directly impacting family comfort. Cleanliness standards are inconsistent, with several recent reviews detailing unacceptable cabin conditions including dust, cobwebs, dirty floors, stained mattresses, and poor kitchen cleanliness that fall well short of expectations for the price point. Customer service issues are notably problematic with recurring complaints about rude, dismissive, and unhelpful reception staff (specific names mentioned), poor communication about bookings, and inflexible responses to reasonable guest concerns, significantly diminishing the family-friendly experience despite the beautiful location.
