PARK: Kookaburra Park
SCORE: 88/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-24
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 142

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (19/20) — Outstanding daily animal interactions including guinea pig cuddles, deer feeding, goats, pigs, and chickens are consistently described as magical and the highlight for children. Movie nights, kids discos, Jurassic Trail walks, fairy garden, river swims, basketball, and volleyball provide diverse activities. Ranger Steve and his family receive exceptional praise for their knowledge and engaging animal education sessions.

Nature (18/20) — Beautiful hinterland setting with mountain views, spacious grounds with native wildlife including wallabies and kookaburras. Easy access to Mary River for swimming and platypus spotting, nearby Booloumba Creek with crystal clear waters, walking trails through the property, and stunning valley scenery. Peaceful natural environment perfect for outdoor exploration and disconnecting from technology.

Cleanliness (14/15) — Facilities consistently praised as spotless, modern, and very well maintained with hot showers, clean toilets, and regular upkeep. Camp kitchen, barn amenities, and communal areas described as excellent. One older toilet block noted as needing renovation (reportedly being demolished). Overall maintenance standards are high with grounds well kept and mowed.

Value (12/15) — Mixed feedback on pricing with some noting it's on the expensive side, though most feel the experience justifies the cost. Included animal activities, quality facilities, spacious sites, and exceptional service are seen as good value. Day visitor pricing ($35 for 3 people) received criticism as too high for limited animal viewing.

Site Size (9/10) — Sites consistently described as extremely generous, well-spaced, and not cramped. Families appreciate not feeling on top of other campers even when fully booked. Various site options from powered to off-grid locations, each with fire pits. Some sites are distant from amenities but offer more privacy and stunning views.

Sentiment (10/10) — Overwhelmingly positive sentiment with exceptional repeat visitation. Multiple families report this as their favourite camping spot with annual return visits. Consistently described as magical, heavenly, and perfect for families. High recommendation rate with guests already booking return trips before leaving. Staff particularly praised for warmth and genuine care.

Location (6/10) — Located near Kenilworth (8-12 minutes drive) for bakery, dairy, and supplies. Access to natural attractions like Booloumba Creek (10 min), Little Yabba Creek (5 min), and Mary River. Remote enough for peaceful escape but close enough to town amenities. Limited mobile reception on site (Vodafone mentioned). About 1 hour from Noosa beaches.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Location is quite remote from major attractions and beaches with limited mobile reception on site, requiring drives for supplies and connectivity.
2. Pricing is considered on the expensive side by multiple reviewers, particularly for day visitors, though most camping families feel it offers reasonable value.
3. One toilet/shower block is outdated and had maintenance issues (blocked toilet, flooding laundry, broken shower), though management is reportedly addressing this with demolition planned.
