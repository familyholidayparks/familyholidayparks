PARK: BIG4 Launceston Holiday Park
SCORE: 71/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-26
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 324

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (13/20) — Reviews consistently mention jumping pillow as the main entertainment feature with kids enjoying it, plus playground equipment; however limited on-site activities beyond this with families relying on nearby Launceston attractions like Cataract Gorge, no pool or waterpark facilities mentioned
Nature (14/20) — Families delighted by regular wildlife sightings including wallabies, pademelons and kangaroos visiting at dusk; park situated on hillside with city views; however location is urban/industrial rather than bushland setting with freeway proximity dominating the environment
Cleanliness (14/15) — Overwhelmingly positive feedback about cleanliness with phrases like "spotlessly clean," "immaculate," "exceptionally clean" appearing repeatedly; modern amenities described as "the best showers" with excellent water pressure; housekeeping receives specific praise
Value (10/15) — Mixed feedback on value with many describing it as "expensive" or "overpriced" particularly for powered sites ($55-$84 per night); tent campers felt particularly price-gouged; cabin guests more satisfied feeling they got what they paid for; several reviews mention it as "dearest park" in Tasmania
Site Size (8/10) — Powered sites described as "spacious," "generous space," and "large enough for our van" with good levelling; some complaints about sloping sites and tight spaces; sites accommodate large rigs up to 22 feet; varied terrain with concrete slabs and gravel options
Sentiment (8/10) — Strong positive sentiment from cabin guests with many saying "will definitely stay again" and "highly recommend"; however campers and tent users significantly less satisfied; staff service consistently praised as "amazing," "friendly," "helpful"; repeat customers mentioned; some very disappointed guests who left early
Location (4/10) — Close proximity to Launceston CBD (walking distance), Cataract Gorge, airport and shops is positive; however major deduction for location directly beside freeway causing "incredible noise all day and night," "constant truck noise," trucks using "exhaust brakes going downhill," plus flight path noise; described as "noisiest campsite" by some; industrial surroundings rather than scenic

WHY NOT 100:
1. Severe highway noise issue — described as "incredible," "unbearable," "constant and horrendous" truck noise from adjacent freeway with air brakes, affecting sleep and enjoyment particularly for campers, with multiple guests leaving early or warning others to avoid; some calling it "the noisiest campsite we've ever booked"
2. Camp kitchen facilities inadequate — consistently criticized as "tiny and basic," "barely usable," "one of the least equipped," "dreadful," currently under construction but described as open to elements, freezing, with minimal equipment, wrong sink plug, and inconveniently located at bottom of steep hill away from most sites
3. Poor value for tent/camper users — tent campers paying $40-65 for minimal facilities with no unpowered option, same price as caravans despite not needing power, hard ground unsuitable for pegs, exposed windy sites, with reviews calling it "pure price gouging," "ridiculously expensive," and "worst camping experience in years at ridiculous price"
