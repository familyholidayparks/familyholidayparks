PARK: Holiday Haven Burrill Lake
SCORE: 78/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-07
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 103

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (14/20) — Reviews mention movie nights, kids activities, playground, games room with air hockey and table tennis, and teen hangout room. While families appreciate these offerings, entertainment is described as modest rather than extensive, with the natural environment providing most of the activity opportunities.

Nature (19/20) — Exceptional natural setting with direct lakefront access for swimming, kayaking, and paddleboarding in crystal-clear water. Short walk to beautiful Burrill Beach. Families consistently praise the safe lake swimming, fishing opportunities, stunning sunsets, and peaceful natural surroundings with birdlife and occasional dolphin sightings.

Cleanliness (14/15) — Overwhelmingly positive feedback about "spotless facilities," "always clean" amenities, and well-maintained park. Minor criticism from 2-3 reviews about slightly dated amenities and one instance of ant infestation in shared kitchen, but vast majority praise cleanliness standards.

Value (14/15) — Described as "budget-friendly," "great value," and "amazing value for money." Families appreciate reasonable pricing ($20 unpowered sites mentioned, $40 powered). Good value proposition with location and facilities, though some note it lacks fancy features of larger parks.

Site Size (7/10) — Multiple reviews mention sites are "not large," "a little smaller than some places," though also described as "spacious" and "generous" by others. One specific complaint about site 71 having a slope. Generally adequate but not a standout feature.

Sentiment (10/10) — Exceptional repeat visitation with families returning for 6, 7, 12+ years consecutively. Overwhelmingly positive sentiment with phrases like "favourite spot," "will definitely be back," and "perfect for all ages." Manager Kirra frequently praised by name.

Location (10/10) — Perfect score for location directly on Burrill Lake with beach walking distance. Close to cafes, bakery, fish shop, IGA all within walking distance. Five minutes to Ulladulla shops. Easy access to local attractions like Cupitt's Winery, breweries, and coastal bike tracks.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Limited structured kids entertainment compared to larger holiday parks - while there are movie nights and basic activities, this is not a resort-style entertainment program that keeps kids occupied all day.
2. Site size inconsistency with some sites described as small or with leveling issues (particularly site 71 mentioned specifically), and the park feeling "a bit tired" according to some guests.
3. Amenities described as "older" and "slightly dated" by multiple reviewers, with camp kitchen needing investment according to regular guests, preventing it from achieving premium cleanliness/facilities scores.
