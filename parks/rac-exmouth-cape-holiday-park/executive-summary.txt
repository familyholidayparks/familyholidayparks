PARK: RAC Exmouth Cape Holiday Park
SCORE: 72/100
DATE SCORED: 2025-06-15
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 244

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (13/20) — Reviews highlight two swimming pools with families noting "kids loved it and played for hours," a games room (coin-operated), nightly outdoor movies at 7pm, and cart hire. However, multiple families noted the games room requires payment which felt like "a money grab," and the playground was frequently mentioned as closed or lacking. "Amazing new facilities but lacking a decent children playground" was a common sentiment.

Nature (12/20) — The park itself is described as "dry dusty car park not trees or shade" with "hardly any trees" and "limited shade." However, families appreciated its proximity to natural attractions with "walking distance to beach" and being "30-45 minutes drive to the Cape Range NP hiking areas and Ningaloo coastline beaches." The visiting emus were a unique natural highlight. Location compensates for lack of on-site nature.

Cleanliness (11/15) — Reviews are mixed with many praising "spotlessly clean ablution blocks" and facilities "checked regularly throughout the day." However, during peak times families noted "bathrooms during this period were disgusting 1st thing in the morning" with "faeces on a toilet seat" and some older amenities "disgustingly old and well and truly badly need upgraded." Cleanliness appears inconsistent and dependent on timing and which block you use.

Value (10/15) — Value scores are moderate with many families noting it's "expensive" at $58-75+ per night, with comments like "pretty over-priced" and "tourism tax." However, others found it "excellent value for money" and "budget friendly" especially compared to expectations for Exmouth. The requirement to pay for games room activities on top of site fees frustrated families. Overall, expensive but reasonable for the region.

Site Size (9/10) — Strong scores here with consistent praise: "generous sites," "spacious sites," "large site for our 20ft caravan," "big sites," and "good sized powered sites." Families with large rigs specifically noted "if you tow a large caravan with a large tug then you will be happy here." The main negative was sites being hard-packed gravel requiring drilling pegs.

Sentiment (8/10) — Overall sentiment is positive with many families extending stays: "booked originally for 2 nights but ended up staying for 7." Staff received consistent praise as "friendly and welcoming" and "incredibly helpful." However, notable negative experiences included complaints about "nasty customer service," strict check-in policies, and a "really negative vibe" mentioned by some guests. Repeat visitors noted the park has "gone downhill since 2018."

Location (9/10) — Excellent location scores with families noting "central to everything," "walking distance to cafes," "close to Town Beach," and "across the road from the Information Centre." The park provides easy access to Ningaloo Reef attractions and Cape Range National Park. Only downsides were noise from the main road and nearby pub keeping some families awake until late.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Significant cleanliness inconsistency during peak periods with families reporting disgusting toilet conditions, old amenities desperately needing upgrades, and dusty red dirt covering everything despite high prices.
2. Limited on-site entertainment value with games requiring additional payment, playground often closed, and lack of natural shade/greenery creating a "car park" atmosphere rather than a natural holiday park setting.
3. Service and policy issues including inflexible check-in times (2pm being "too late"), inconsistent staff attitudes from "beyond helpful" to "nasty customer service," and frustrating security code requirements throughout the park.
