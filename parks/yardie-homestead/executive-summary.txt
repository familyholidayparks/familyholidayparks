PARK: Yardie Homestead
SCORE: 58/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-21
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 208

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (10/20) — Limited on-site entertainment with a small pool that closes early (5:15-5:30pm), occasional live music mentioned positively, playground described as "dangerous and rusty" and needing replacement, tennis court with no net and cracked surface, and a basic cafe offering food but no structured kids activities or programs.

Nature (18/20) — Exceptional natural setting and access, described as "oasis in the desert," positioned perfectly for Cape Range National Park with "pristine beaches" 5-15 minutes away, world-class snorkelling at Turquoise Bay, Oyster Stacks and Lakeside, excellent stargazing with "Milky Way perfectly visible," close to Yardie Creek boat tours, and described as "nature-oriented" wilderness experience.

Cleanliness (8/15) — Highly inconsistent cleanliness with many complaints about "dirty" and "disgusting" facilities, bathrooms described as "very dreckig und uralt" (very dirty and ancient), "rubbish bins smelly and overful" not emptied for days, fish cleaning station creating odours, blocked grey water causing smells, though some reviews mention facilities were "clean" or "kept spotless" suggesting variability depending on location and timing.

Value (6/15) — Poor value perception with frequent complaints of being "extremely overpriced" at $50-$90 per night for limited services, "powered sites" that can't run appliances or air conditioning, strict 35-day cancellation policy with no flexibility for emergencies or cyclones, extra charges for late checkout, expensive laundry, and overall feeling of being "gouged for money" with one reviewer stating "insulting to campers that they would charge anything really for the conditions they offer."

Site Size (8/10) — Generally positive feedback on "spacious sites" and "big powered sites," "large raked clean caravan sites with concrete pad," "enough to fit a camper, tent, jetski and ute," though some complained about cramped conditions when full, sites sharing power/water access points, and lack of shade on some sites.

Sentiment (5/10) — Deeply polarized sentiment with loyal repeat visitors praising location but majority expressing frustration, many one-star reviews citing "terrible rude staff," "worst customer service I have ever witnessed," several vowing "will never return," significant complaints about management attitude, though some families report positive experiences and state "will definitely be back."

Location (10/10) — Universally praised as the park's strongest asset, "great location excellent facilities," "5 minute drive to boat ramp," "close to all the best snorkelling and beaches," "perfect location for Ningaloo Reef," "15 mins to Exmouth town," ideal positioning between Cape Range National Park and town, only fully-equipped campground within the national park area.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Severe and misleading power limitations where "powered sites" cannot run air conditioning, kettles, toasters, microwaves, or heating appliances despite families paying premium rates, with power outlets glued shut in bathrooms and no charging stations for unpowered sites.

2. Consistently reported poor customer service and rude management, particularly one staff member repeatedly mentioned, with rigid enforcement of policies, no empathy for vehicle breakdowns or emergencies, threatening behaviour toward guests, and refusal of reasonable requests like filling water containers after checkout.

3. Aging, poorly maintained facilities including "dilapidated" amenities blocks, "rusty playground," "disgusting" kitchen areas with no refrigeration or lighting, inconsistent cleaning schedules, blocked grey water systems, and overall infrastructure described as "outdated" and "runtergekommen" (run down) requiring urgent replacement.
