PARK: NRMA Treasure Island Holiday Resort, Gold Coast
SCORE: 78/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-20
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 360

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (19/20) — Exceptional on-site entertainment with multiple pools including waterslides, pirate-themed splash park, jumping pillow, mini golf, tennis/basketball courts, go-karts for hire, and daily kids activities run by enthusiastic staff. Reviews consistently praise the variety of activities: "so much to do for the kids", "kids loved the waterslide and jumping pillow", "didn't have to leave the park". Weekend activities include movie nights on big screen, trivia, bingo, and kids club with crafts. Only minor deductions for some activities not running during non-peak periods.

Nature (11/20) — The park is surrounded by retail outlets in an industrial estate setting with limited natural environment. Reviews note it's "surrounded by large retail outlets", "in the middle of an industrial retail estate", and "far from the beach" requiring a 5-minute drive or 30-minute bus/tram ride to reach the coast. While the park has lovely gardens and tropical plantings with bush turkeys and possums visible, there's no direct beach access or significant bushland. The nearby Biggera Creek provides some natural setting for ensuite sites at the rear.

Cleanliness (12/15) — Mixed feedback on cleanliness with many praising "great clean facilities", "spotless rooms", "amenities were clean", and "cleanest facilities of any holiday park". However, numerous recent reviews note declining standards: "toilets and showers were constantly dirty during our 3 night stay", "rubbish through the splash pad", "undies in the spas", "German cockroaches", "thick dust on ceiling fans", "black mould in air conditioning units", and "filthy floors in safari tent". Maintenance issues mentioned include broken locks, dated facilities, and outdoor furniture in poor condition.

Value (11/15) — Reviews show mixed value perception. Many find it reasonable: "great value", "$68 a night for powered site", "affordable meals", "reasonably priced". However, numerous complaints about pricing: "$100 per night... absolutely ridiculous", "nearly $400 a night I expected more", "ridiculously overpriced" food ($6 ice cream, $5 soft drink with 15% surcharge, $24 cocktails, $17 for 2 coffees). Several note the park is "expensive for what was offered" and facilities need upgrading for the prices charged. Peak season pricing particularly criticized.

Site Size (6/10) — Consistent complaints about tight caravan sites: "sites are tight", "a little tight", "very tight to back into", "postage stamp size caravan site", "site was too small for our van", "small site with each site crammed in", "couldn't put the van annex out", "difficult to reverse van in". Powered sites noted as barely big enough for tent and car. Premium sites described as adequate but ensuite sites vary significantly in size. Cabins and villas received better space reviews as "spacious" and "roomy".

Sentiment (9/10) — Overwhelmingly positive family sentiment with high repeat visitation: "will definitely be back", "stayed multiple times", "our families favourite place in the world", "best caravan park we have been in", "kids didn't want to leave", "3rd Halloween in a row", "coming here for YEARS". Strong recommendation rate evident. However, some families stating "this will be our last" due to declining standards, and a few very negative experiences with customer service and cleanliness issues prevent a perfect score.

Location (10/10) — Excellent location scores with reviews praising proximity to everything: "walking distance to Harbour Town", "Coles just over the road", "close to all theme parks" (15-20 minutes to SeaWorld, Movie World, Wet'n'Wild), "central to everything", "short drive to Surfers Paradise" (10-20 minutes). Bus stop at front gate, easy freeway access. Only negative is distance from beach, but convenience to shopping and attractions compensates significantly.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Site sizes are consistently too small for larger caravans with multiple reviews describing difficult reversing, inability to fit awnings, and cramped conditions that detract significantly from the camping experience.
2. Cleanliness and maintenance standards have declined with recent reviews reporting dirty amenities, pest issues (cockroaches), mould in air conditioning, worn furniture, and general dated facilities that need urgent attention and investment.
3. Value concerns are significant with overpriced food/beverages on-site, high accommodation costs for dated facilities, and families feeling the park doesn't justify premium pricing without facility upgrades, particularly during peak seasons.
