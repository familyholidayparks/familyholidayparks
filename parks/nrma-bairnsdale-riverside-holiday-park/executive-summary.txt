PARK: NRMA Bairnsdale Riverside Holiday Park
SCORE: 78/100
DATE SCORED: 2025
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 184

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (16/20) — Reviewers consistently highlighted organized activities including outdoor cinema with big screen TV and popcorn, live music events, food trucks, karaoke, and kids' activities. The wood-fired pizza oven (pre-heated on request), communal fire pit with fairy lights, games on the lawn, and playground equipment created a family-friendly atmosphere. Families mentioned "thought of everything for the kids" and "lots of stuff out for the kids." The park across the road features an all-abilities playground and skate park. Regular entertainment during holiday periods and long weekends was frequently praised.

Nature (16/20) — The park sits directly on the Mitchell River banks with excellent access to a 5-6km riverside walk and cycle track. Families enjoyed the fishing jetty within the park, abundant shade from mature established trees, and described it as having a "bush-like setting." The location serves as a base to explore nearby natural attractions including Lakes Entrance beaches (10-35 minutes), Raymond Island koala trail, Paynesville fishing, and the Bairnsdale to Orbost rail trail. However, some noted the river isn't always visible from sites and cockatoos (100+) create significant noise and mess.

Cleanliness (14/15) — Reviews overwhelmingly praised cleanliness with phrases like "spotlessly clean," "very clean amenities," "cleanest place I have stayed in," and "best kept amenities." The provision of fresh bath mats daily in showers was mentioned in numerous reviews as an exceptional touch. However, several recent reviews (2025-2026) noted declining standards during peak periods with bathrooms running out of supplies, messy conditions after long weekends, and poorly maintained camp kitchens with permanent residents leaving dishes and creating smelly conditions. One March 2026 review described serious cleanliness issues including cigarette smoke smell, dead cockroach, mouldy bread in cabin, and blocked shower drains.

Value (13/15) — Most families found the park offered "great value" and "really great value" with pricing around $29-32 for unpowered sites. Multiple reviews mentioned the park could "easily charge more" given the facilities and service. However, powered sites and cabins received mixed value feedback, with some noting cabins "could have done with a makeover" and basic budget cabins having uncomfortable beds and noisy air conditioning that some felt weren't worth even budget pricing.

Site Size (7/10) — Reviews consistently described the park as "small" and "smallish" but well-utilized. Multiple guests noted sites were "a bit tight," "small but ample," and "tightly packed in" especially during peak seasons. Some mentioned "confined" spaces under trees and limited grassy areas for tents on caravan-designated sites. However, reviewers appreciated the park's compact nature made facilities easily accessible, and some praised "good sized sites" and "generous space for each van" without a "carpark vibe."

Sentiment (9/10) — Guest sentiment was overwhelmingly positive with numerous return visitors ("third stay," "sixth time," "always book here," "second time stay and many more to come"). Reviewers frequently stated "will definitely stay again" and "can't wait to return." Many called it their "favourite park," "one if not the best caravan park," and "10/10." Staff members Pete, Tracey, Cathy, Ben, and Adam received exceptional praise for friendliness and going above and beyond. However, a few negative experiences with rude staff encounters, late check-in issues, and declining maintenance standards prevented a perfect score.

Location (9/10) — The park's location received consistent praise for being "walking distance to town" (5-10 minutes to Coles, Main Street, shops, cafes, and restaurants). Families appreciated proximity to attractions while remaining peaceful. The riverside position and access to walking/cycling trails was valued. However, the location directly on Princes Highway was frequently mentioned as causing traffic noise from trucks and semi-trailers, particularly for sites near the entrance, though many noted it "wasn't a problem" or "quietened down at night."

WHY NOT 100:
1. Traffic noise from the Princes Highway location is mentioned in numerous reviews as a significant drawback, with trucks using compression brakes and constant traffic noise affecting sleep quality, particularly for sites near the road.
2. Site sizes are consistently described as tight and compact, especially during peak holiday periods, with some families noting limited space for tents and feeling "tightly packed in" compared to expectations.
3. Recent reviews (2025-2026) show declining cleanliness standards during busy periods, with camp kitchens becoming disgusting from permanent residents, bathrooms running out of supplies after long weekends, and one serious cabin cleanliness failure including cigarette smoke, pests, and mouldy food.
