PARK: Blackheath Glen Tourist Park
SCORE: 68/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-04-24
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 125

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (8/20) — Reviews consistently note "no real offerings for kids" on-site, with families relying entirely on the public playground and pool complex across the road; no waterpark, jumping pillow, or organized activities mentioned, limiting appeal for young families seeking on-site entertainment.

Nature (18/20) — Exceptional natural setting with "beautiful bush setting," direct access to Blue Mountains walking tracks including Popes Glen and Grand Canyon trails within minutes, surrounded by native forest with cockatoos; families praise it as "perfect for sightseeing and bush walking in the Blackheath area."

Cleanliness (11/15) — Mixed feedback with many praising "exceptionally clean" facilities and "spotless" amenities, but recent reviews note "bathrooms need a good scrub," "grime inside showers," "amenities block was only cleaned once in 5 nights," and inconsistent maintenance standards affecting family comfort.

Value (10/15) — Pricing concerns dominate with "$70 for an unpowered site felt overpriced," "$80 for a powered site for 2a2c" deemed excessive, and comparisons to cheaper council parks; some find it "well priced" but value perception hurt by dated facilities and limited on-site amenities for families.

Site Size (7/10) — Sites described as "adequate" and "good sized" for caravans, though "unpowered spots are small and close together," "access roads inside the park are narrow making for tight site access for vans larger than 18'," and some families found spacing insufficient for larger family setups.

Sentiment (8/10) — Generally positive with families appreciating the location and helpful staff, though repeat visitation mixed; phrases like "we'd happily stay again" balanced by disappointment over cleanliness lapses, pricing concerns, and limited facilities for young children reducing overall family enthusiasm.

Location (6/10) — Strong for Blue Mountains access with walking tracks nearby and "short walk into Blackheath" village, but families note it's "20 min walk to the main street/town," requiring driving for most amenities, and positioned away from main tourist hub of Katoomba, reducing convenience for families.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Zero on-site entertainment for children with no playground, waterpark, games room, or activities, forcing families to rely entirely on external facilities across the road, significantly limiting appeal for families with young children seeking engaging holiday experiences.
2. Cleanliness standards inconsistent with multiple recent reviews noting dirty showers with grime, infrequent amenities cleaning during peak periods, cold showers reported on multiple occasions, and maintenance issues like mouldy glamping tents and worn cabin furnishings disappointing family guests.
3. Value perception poor with pricing $70-80 per night considered steep compared to better-equipped alternatives, particularly given dated facilities, minimal family amenities, and lack of on-site pool or entertainment justifying premium rates for family holidays.
