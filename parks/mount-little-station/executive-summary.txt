PARK: Mount Little Station
SCORE: 83/100
DATE SCORED: 2025
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 131

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (18/20) — Exceptional kids' entertainment with daily animal feeding sessions (bottle-feeding lambs, calves, interacting with camels, donkeys, goats), beloved pirate cave treasure hunt with coins, animal maze, 4WD tracks, Mayo Gorge walks with swimming holes, shearing demonstrations, arts and crafts activities, and the famous twice-weekly pizza nights at the tavern that families describe as "unforgettable" and "to die for."

Nature (20/20) — Absolutely spectacular 27,000-acre working station in the heart of the Flinders Ranges with breathtaking views of the Elder Range from every campsite, access to famous Heysen and Mawson trails, Mayo Gorge with rock pools, historical ruins, sunset lookouts, and abundant wildlife (kangaroos, emus, wallabies, parrots, owls) visiting campsites daily. Reviews consistently describe it as "magical," "jaw-dropping," and "pure magic."

Cleanliness (13/15) — Facilities described as "spotless," "clean and tidy," "well presented and maintained" by the majority, with hot showers and flushing toilets scattered throughout. A few reviews mention camp kitchen is "very basic and rough" and one mentioned amenities "not very clean," but overwhelmingly positive feedback about cleanliness standards for a station stay.

Value (11/15) — Pricing noted as "on the high side" by several reviewers at $15 per person per night for unpowered sites, with some feeling it's overpriced for basic facilities. However, many families counter that the experience, space, and activities justify the cost, with multiple stating they'd "pay even more for the experience we had" and describing it as "wallet-friendly escape" considering the unique station access.

Site Size (10/10) — Universally praised as "enormous," "massive," "extra large," with families reporting they "could fit 10 vans in our area with room to spare" and feeling "completely alone" despite full bookings. Sites consistently described as the biggest families have stayed on in extensive travels, providing genuine privacy and seclusion.

Sentiment (10/10) — Extraordinary positive sentiment with families on their 9th visit, multiple returning annually for 4+ years, countless "will definitely be back" statements, children asking "when are we going back," and emphatic recommendations. One family called it "one of our most favourite spots after 3 years travelling Australia," another said "one of the best places on earth."

Location (9/10) — Strategically located just outside Hawker (20-minute drive for supplies) in the heart of the Flinders Ranges, providing easy access to Ikara-Flinders Ranges National Park, Brachina Gorge, Wilpena Pound, and other major attractions. Remote enough for authentic outback experience but convenient as a base for regional exploration. Some reviews note limited mobile reception and rough access roads requiring 4WD.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Facilities are genuinely rustic station-style with solar showers that only provide hot water during sunlight hours, very basic camp kitchen with non-functioning BBQs reported, and limited shelter, which disappointed some guests expecting standard caravan park amenities despite the station stay classification.
2. Access roads described as "alternating from gravel to very rocky" with multiple reports of vehicle damage (tyres punctured from barbed wire, near car damage from rocks), with 4WD strongly recommended but not always clearly communicated at booking.
3. Value perception divided with pricing around $15pp/night considered steep by experienced campers for unpowered sites with basic facilities, though many counter that the unique experience and space justify the premium over standard campgrounds.
