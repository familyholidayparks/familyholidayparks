WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (8/20) — No on-site entertainment or pool facilities; reviews mention "no pool" and families rely on the adjacent public "skate park and playground next door" for children's activities, though one review notes proximity to "sport installations nearby."

Nature (14/20) — Strong beachfront location "across the road from the beach" with views of Moreton Island and Bribie Passage; reviews praise "beautiful walks," "birdsong in the morning," beach walks with "thousands of light blue soldier crabs," though the tidal beach "not a beach you can sunbathe on or walk along."

Cleanliness (15/15) — Exceptional scores with reviews repeatedly calling amenities "spotless," "the cleanest I have ever seen in fact," "immaculate," and "sparkling clean" throughout 2023-2026; management praised for maintaining pristine facilities.

Value (12/15) — Moderate value with pricing at "$36.50-46 per night for 2 people" described as "reasonably priced" and "you could do a lot worse," though lacking premium facilities; good proximity to shops and cafes adds value.

Site Size (8/10) — Reviews describe "sites are huge," "generous sites," "spacious," and "decent space for rv's," though it's a "small park around 30 vans" with some grass sites lacking slabs.

Sentiment (9/10) — Overwhelmingly positive with many repeat visitors saying "we'll be back," "would definitely return," "always full," though two 1-star reviews and limited family-specific enthusiasm from young families.

Location (10/10) — Excellent location praised in nearly every review: "walking distance to IGA, cafes, shops, pub," "close to Brisbane," beach access, and convenient as a "perfect stop along the route to Brisbane."

WHY NOT 100:
1. No pool or dedicated children's entertainment facilities for families with young kids—reviews specifically note "no pool" and reliance on adjacent public facilities rather than on-site amenities.
2. Limited family-oriented activities and resort-style features—this is a small, quiet park (30 sites) designed more for peaceful stopovers than as an extended family holiday destination with kids' clubs or playgrounds.
3. Tidal beach conditions limit water play opportunities—reviews note the beach is tidal and "not a beach you can sunbathe on or walk along," reducing its appeal for families wanting beach-based activities.
