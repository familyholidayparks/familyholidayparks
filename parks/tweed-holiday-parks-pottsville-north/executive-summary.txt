PARK: Tweed Holiday Parks Pottsville North
SCORE: 83/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-23
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 136

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (15/20) — Reviews consistently highlight the heated pool with separate kiddy pool as a major drawcard, plus basketball and volleyball courts. Families appreciate organized activities like colour runs during holidays. However, some noted the basketball backboard had damage and volleyball court gets dirty. No major waterpark or extensive playground facilities mentioned.

Nature (18/20) — Exceptional natural environment with location on Mooball Creek providing safe swimming and paddleboarding. Families rave about abundant wildlife including curlews, kookaburras, and diverse birdlife. Beach access via short walk (4 minutes), mature trees providing shade, and grassed areas. "Lots of trees and gardens, not a concrete village park" is a repeated sentiment. Walking/bike trails to beach and along creek highly praised.

Cleanliness (14/15) — Overwhelmingly positive feedback on cleanliness with phrases like "immaculate," "spotless," "very clean camp kitchen," and "amenities always clean." Multiple mentions of Matthew the maintenance man doing exceptional work. Minor issues included one report of mouldy showers, stinky camp kitchen fridge, and an ant problem in one cabin.

Value (12/15) — Mixed feedback with many finding it "very reasonable" and "affordable," but several reviews complained about high pricing ($90-115/night for powered sites, $230 for two nights tent camping during holidays). Value concerns more prominent in recent reviews with one stating "most expensive site we've ever paid for." Generally good value given location and facilities, but pricing during peak periods causes friction.

Site Size (8/10) — Sites described as "generous," "good sized," "loads of space for bigger vans," and "big and not cramped." However, multiple reviews note "sites can be tight," particularly mentioning the loop layout can create traffic flow issues when setting up. Ensuite sites and waterfront sites particularly praised. Not cramped but not exceptionally spacious either.

Sentiment (9/10) — Extremely high positive sentiment with numerous "will definitely be back" and "we stay here a lot" comments. Long-term visitors since 2004 mentioned. Families express strong emotional connection ("absolutely love it here," "favorite place for my entire trip"). Two serious negative incidents (cricket ball harassment, beetle infestation) prevent perfect score. Overall 94% positive sentiment.

Location (7/10) — Excellent proximity to beach (4-5 minute walk), creek access on-site, and town amenities (2-minute walk to IGA, cafes, shops). Sports club and tavern within walking distance. However, not right on the beach itself, and some feel it's slightly removed from major attractions. Poor mobile/wifi reception noted by several reviewers as limiting factor.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Site layout and access issues during busy periods cause congestion, with families waiting 30+ minutes as caravans set up in the loop configuration, and sites described as "tight" for larger rigs despite being adequate.

2. Value perception has declined with recent pricing ($90/night midweek, $115+ peak), with multiple reviewers calling it a "rip off" and "most expensive site ever," particularly impacting budget-conscious families despite quality facilities.

3. Limited dedicated kids' entertainment infrastructure beyond pool and courts, with damaged basketball equipment, no major playground mentioned, and organized activities only during peak holidays, plus poor mobile/wifi connectivity affects modern family needs.
