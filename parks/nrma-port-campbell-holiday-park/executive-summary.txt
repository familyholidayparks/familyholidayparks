PARK: NRMA Port Campbell Holiday Park
SCORE: 62/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-24
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 168

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (4/20) — Reviews consistently note "no activities for the kids," "no playground," and "nothing for kids." The park offers no organized entertainment, kids club, or recreational facilities beyond the natural environment. No pool, waterpark, or structured activities mentioned.

Nature (18/20) — Exceptional natural setting scores highly with "great location right on the beach," "beautiful creek with suspension bridge," "lots of cute rabbits everywhere," walking trails, and "sky full of stars." Direct beach access within 3-5 minutes walk, river frontage, and proximity to Great Ocean Road attractions. The natural environment is the park's strongest asset.

Cleanliness (8/15) — Mixed reviews reveal inconsistent standards: "toilets are constantly dirty," "amenities incredibly dirty and they smell," "dirty pink loofa sitting for three days," contrasted with some noting "clean amenities" and "heated amenities." Budget cabins described as "sterk verouderd," with mould, spider webs, and dust. Maintenance appears sporadic.

Value (11/15) — Generally considered reasonable for location with "affordable," "very reasonable priced," "cost only $41 with power" mentioned positively. However, some felt overcharged: "83 Dollar für eine Person absolut Abzocke," and complaints about being charged for sites not suitable as booked. Value perceived as fair if expectations are managed.

Site Size (6/10) — Significant concerns raised: "sites are tiny," "terribly uneven and dusty," "parking was a bit tight," "sites smallish and quite close with no screening." Premium sites with synthetic grass received criticism: "fake and HARD grass," "full of gravel underneath." Caravan maneuverability challenging in places.

Sentiment (7/10) — Divided loyalty: loyal returners "coming here for over 30 yrs" alongside first-timers vowing "never coming back here again." Many appreciate location despite shortcomings, but recent management changes show mixed results. Repeat visitation driven by location rather than park quality.

Location (10/10) — Perfect score for location excellence: "best location on the Great Ocean Rd," "walking distance to town, cafes, restaurants," "short drive to the apostles," "perfect base for visiting Great Ocean Road attractions." Central Port Campbell position with beach, shops, and attractions all accessible on foot.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Complete absence of children's entertainment facilities—no playground, no kids activities, no pool, no organized programs—leaving families entirely dependent on external attractions.
2. Inconsistent and often poor cleanliness standards with multiple reports of dirty amenities, inadequate maintenance, blocked urinals, broken dryers, missing flush buttons, and facilities cleaned inadequately or not at all.
3. Site quality and space issues with cramped, uneven, dusty sites that don't match descriptions ("grass" sites that are dirt/gravel), challenging access for larger rigs, and premium sites that fail to deliver premium quality.

NOTABLE REVIEW PHRASES:
"Perfect location but that is all it has going for it," "no playground for the kids," "toilets are constantly dirty," "sites are tiny, ours was terribly uneven," "walking distance to the beach, cafes, restaurants," "lots of cute rabbits on site," "in urgent need of renovation," "great base for sightseeing around the area"
