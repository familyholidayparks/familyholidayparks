PARK: Sydney Tourist Park
SCORE: 42/100
DATE SCORED: 2024-01-17
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 43

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (2/20) — Multiple reviews explicitly state "nothing for children", "no playground or even a area for the kids", with one reviewer warning "Do not take children there as this is not a park you can let them wander and play on their own". No kids entertainment, activities, or recreational facilities mentioned across any reviews.

Nature (8/20) — Limited natural environment with one positive mention of "abundant bird life" near road. Described as having "little to no grass", "tiny spots", primarily concrete slabs. Located near beaches (Cronulla, Brighton le Sands mentioned) but the park itself has minimal natural features. One reviewer noted "One grass patch with a hills hoist on it".

Cleanliness (11/15) — Mixed feedback with recent reviews generally positive: "amenities are always clean", "toilets and showers very clean", "cleaned multiple times throughout the day". However, critical reviews cite "dirty water taps", "floors were always wet from the showers", "kitchen dirty", and facilities described as "old shipping container". Amenities are dated but mostly maintained.

Value (8/15) — At $56 per night, reviews show divided opinions. Positive mentions include "fair rates for location", "reasonable rates", "low cost". However, harsh criticism includes "$56 A NIGHT FOR THIS IS CRAZZYYYY", "$15 to use dump point is excessive", and gas refill reportedly only 60% filled for $30. Value primarily driven by Sydney location rather than facilities.

Site Size (6/10) — Reviews describe sites as "tiny spots", "very compact", though some mention "good size spaces" and "double slab very convenient" for larger rigs. Mixed feedback with 8m vans able to fit on some sites, but overall described as compact for an urban park.

Sentiment (4/10) — Heavily polarized with loyal repeat visitors alongside strongly negative family experiences. 90% long-term/permanent residents creates questionable atmosphere. Security concerns raised ("no boom gate", "felt like we were going to be robbed"). Some families explicitly warn others to avoid. Positive sentiment mainly from couples/solo travellers using it as transit stop.

Location (9/10) — Strongest feature with consistent praise: "Great location", "close to train station", "Westfield Shopping Centre close by", "transport to city just at your front door", "Perfect spot for exploring/shopping in the Sydney area". Easy access to Sydney CBD, airport, and southern beaches.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Complete absence of children's facilities with multiple reviews explicitly warning families away, stating there's no playground, no kids' area, and children cannot safely wander the park due to permanent resident population and lack of security.
2. Park is 90% permanent/long-term residents with "questionable and grumpy tenants", creating an atmosphere more akin to a residential caravan park than a holiday destination, with visible homeless people using facilities and compromised security (no gates).
3. Facilities are dated shipping containers with persistent wet floors, limited toilet/shower capacity (only 3 male and 3 female), minimal grass, and an overall "dreary, cold, depressing concrete block" appearance that lacks appeal for family holidays.

NOTABLE REVIEW PHRASES:
"Nothing for children", "no playground or even a area for the kids", "Do not take children there", "90% of the park is long term or permanent residents", "dreary, cold, depressing concrete block", "AVOID AT ALL COSTS", "Great location", "close to train station", "amenities are always clean", "no boom gate or gates at all", "suitable for everyone" (contradicted by family reviews), "recommend highly for all the family" (minority view)
