PARK: Tidal River Campground
SCORE: 72/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-21
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 227

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (10/20) — Limited organized activities but natural entertainment abounds with wombat visits, birdlife, outdoor cinema mentioned occasionally, general store, and cafe; reviewers noted "80% school camps" at times and emphasis on self-directed nature activities rather than structured kids' programs or waterpark facilities.

Nature (20/20) — Absolutely spectacular natural environment with perfect scores across reviews: "one of the most beautiful places in Australia," direct access to Tidal River and Norman Beach, "spectacular surroundings," abundant wildlife including wombats, wallabies, kangaroos and birds, stunning walks including Mount Oberon, Squeaky Beach nearby, and families consistently praised the "nature in all its beauty."

Cleanliness (11/15) — Mixed feedback with many noting facilities are "dated" and "run down," some reporting "filthy disgrace" toilets and "dirty from lots of people using them," though others found amenities "clean" and "well maintained"; shower blocks cleaned daily (twice on busy days) but overall maintenance described as needing work with "shelves largely bare" in store.

Value (13/15) — Generally considered good value with prices around $32-37 per night for camping, described as "reasonable rates," "very cheap," and sometimes free during off-season; families returning for 15+ years indicates strong perceived value despite basic facilities, though some felt "not worth the money" given dusty conditions.

Site Size (6/10) — Significant complaints about cramped conditions: "sites are small and tight," "on top of each other," "super cramped together," some resembling "carpark" or "gravel parking lot," though unpowered bush sites received better reviews than powered sites; "spaces can be really tight for full-size caravans."

Sentiment (8/10) — Strong repeat visitation with families coming for 15-40 years and multi-generational loyalty ("go every year"), but tempered by frustrations with booking systems, crowding, weather dependency, and facility maintenance; many 5-star reviews balanced by significant 1-3 star complaints about dust, wind, safety, and overcrowding.

Location (10/10) — Perfect location score with "best location in Victoria" within Wilsons Promontory National Park, walking distance to beach, river and hiking trails, though remote (3-hour drive from Melbourne); visitors consistently praised "stunning location," "perfect location," and proximity to nature attractions.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Facilities are dated, run-down and inconsistently clean with toilets described as "filthy disgrace" at times, shower blocks needing renovation, and general maintenance falling short of visitor expectations for such a popular destination.

2. Sites are cramped, dusty and weather-exposed with many resembling "gravel carparks," extreme dust problems in wind, inadequate shade, and poor site differentiation making the camping experience uncomfortable, especially for tent campers.

3. Minimal organized kids entertainment or activities beyond the natural environment, no pool, limited on-site recreation facilities, and overwhelming reliance on self-directed nature activities which doesn't suit all family types.

NOTABLE REVIEW PHRASES:
"one of the most beautiful places to visit and stay in Australia", "spectacular surroundings", "wombats walking around at night", "extremely dusty when the wind picks up", "sites are small and tight", "facilities are dated", "perfect place to stay with young kids", "camp here at least once in your life"
