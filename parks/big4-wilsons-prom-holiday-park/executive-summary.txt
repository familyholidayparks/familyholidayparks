PARK: BIG4 Wilsons Prom Holiday Park
SCORE: 78/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-25
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 405

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (12/20) — Limited on-site kids entertainment with only a small playground and basketball hoop; no pool, jumping pillow, or organized activities, though the park occasionally offers movie nights and visits from food trucks/coffee vans during peak periods.
Nature (19/20) — Exceptional natural environment with waterfront sites on Corner Inlet offering stunning sunrise views, direct beach access, established bushland providing privacy between sites, abundant wildlife including black swans and wombats, and proximity to Wilsons Promontory National Park with Duck Point walking track adjacent to the park.
Cleanliness (13/15) — Generally clean and well-maintained facilities with regular cleaning of amenities blocks twice daily, though some reviews note the older rear toilet block needs updating with cobwebs, mould, and tired fixtures, while the newer front amenities receive praise for being modern and spotless.
Value (11/15) — Mixed feedback on value with some guests finding it reasonable while others complain about price increases ($70-149 per night for powered sites) and lack of water connections on many sites, dump points, and limited facilities for the cost, though location near the Prom partially justifies pricing.
Site Size (10/10) — Outstanding marks for site size with reviewers consistently describing sites as "huge," "enormous," "spacious," and "very generous," with room for large caravans, cars, and tents on a single site, plus excellent privacy through bush screening between sites.
Sentiment (9/10) — Very positive overall sentiment with high repeat visitation rates, many guests rebooked for following years, strong recommendation rates, and praise for friendly helpful staff, though some negative experiences with booking issues, noise control, and management flexibility reduced the perfect score.
Location (9/10) — Excellent location right on Corner Inlet waterfront with beautiful views, 20-30 minute drive to Wilsons Prom National Park entrance and Tidal River, close to Duck Point walk, though remote from towns with limited mobile reception and 40+ minute drive to amenities in Foster or Fish Creek.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Minimal on-site entertainment facilities for children - no pool, jumping pillow, mini golf, or organized kids activities that families expect from a BIG4 park, with only a small playground and basketball court.
2. Inconsistent facility quality with the older rear amenities block described as tired, dated, needing renovation with poor shower hooks/benches, and some sites lacking water connections despite being a BIG4 premium park.
3. Management and service issues including poor noise control after 10pm, inflexible cancellation policies, booking errors with double bookings and site changes, no dump point on-site, and limited WiFi/mobile reception throughout the park.
