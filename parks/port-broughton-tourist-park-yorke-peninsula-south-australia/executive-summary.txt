PARK: Port Broughton Tourist Park, Yorke Peninsula, South Australia
SCORE: 76/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-24
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 145

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (18/20) — Consistently praised for "water park, shaded swimming pool, jumping pillow, mini golf and play equipment", "skate park", pump track, and "so much for the kids to do". Families describe it as having "ALL the kids facilities and activities" with movies during school holidays. The waterpark receives particular acclaim as "fun for all ages" and a major drawcard.

Nature (16/20) — Beachfront Spencer Gulf location with "beautiful beach view", "stunning sunsets and sunrises", direct beach access for fishing and crabbing, and peaceful coastal atmosphere. Reviews mention "lovely walks along Spencer Gulf", native birdlife (wattle birds, honeyeaters, magpies), and dog-friendly beach areas. However, limited bushland and natural shade noted.

Cleanliness (10/15) — Highly polarized feedback creates significant concern. Many recent reviews praise "exceptionally clean and well maintained" facilities and "spotlessly clean" amenities. However, multiple detailed complaints describe "cabin was so dirty, floors hadn't been swept, mopped or vacuumed", "amenities block has not had a good clean for years, scum & mould build up", "toilets disgusting", and "camp kitchen putrid, floors look like someone tapdanced while cleaning 100 live squid". Staff response appears inconsistent.

Value (11/15) — Mixed perceptions with "definitely get your value for money" from repeat visitors, but pricing concerns emerge: "$60 per night powered sites" considered expensive by some, "not a bargain at $50 for a powered site", and "very expensive" for cabin quality received. Several guests felt premium pricing didn't match service/cleanliness standards delivered.

Site Size (9/10) — Strong performance with "caravan sites were huge", "sites are spacious plenty of room for the awning and car", "very large sites", and "good size sites" mentioned frequently. Both powered and beachfront sites described as generous, though some are synthetic grass/gravel rather than natural grass.

Sentiment (7/10) — Divided sentiment from families. Many 5-star "5th visit" regulars who "absolutely love going here for family holidays" and "will definitely be back". However, significant number of 1-star reviews stating "will not be going back", "wouldn't recommend at all", and complaints about poor customer service, particularly around cancellation policies and front desk interactions creating negative experiences.

Location (5/10) — "Easy walking distance to the Main Street and pub", "1km walk to town, pub and jetty", close to IGA supermarket and local amenities. However, Port Broughton itself is a small town with limited attractions beyond fishing/beach activities. Remote Yorke Peninsula location means considerable drive from Adelaide (approximately 2.5 hours) with limited nearby tourism infrastructure.

WHY NOT 100:
1. **Inconsistent cleanliness standards severely impact guest experience** — Multiple detailed reviews from 2024-2026 describe filthy cabins with dog fur, dirty sheets, mould in bathrooms, unswept floors, and poorly maintained amenities. One April 2026 review details "cabin was so dirty... dog fur everywhere, hair through sheets, walls hadn't had a scrub, dishes incredibly dirty" while a January 2025 review states "amenities block has not had a good clean for years, scum & mould build up in showers". This inconsistency creates an unacceptable risk for families.

2. **Dust and mosquito issues create environmental challenges** — Numerous reviews describe "dirt/gravel site that blows up so much dust, you'll be cleaning your gear for eternity", "dust bowl", "lots of dirt everywhere constantly", and "nothing can prepare you for the onslaught of mosquitoes at dusk". The coastal location attracts "tons of mozzies, flies and wind gust" making outdoor enjoyment difficult during peak season.

3. **Customer service and management concerns damage reputation** — Multiple reviews describe "very poor attitude towards people" from front desk staff, "unfriendly and unhelpful" service, guests being "evicted from the park" after complaints, "very rude manner" interactions, inflexible cancellation policies even for family deaths, and pricing discrepancies between online and counter rates. Management appears defensive rather than solution-focused.
