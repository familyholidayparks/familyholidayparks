PARK: Kalbarri Red Bluff Tourist Park
SCORE: 82/100
DATE SCORED: 2025-05-24
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 240

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (12/20) — No playground, jumpy pillow, or structured kids activities mentioned; families appreciated the natural entertainment of beach access, rock pools for crab spotting, kangaroos visiting at night, and whale watching; one reviewer specifically praised the absence of kids entertainment saying "there is no jumpy pillow or other kids entertainment and we liked this, please dont get one!!"

Nature (18/20) — Exceptional coastal location directly next to Red Bluff Beach and cliffs with spectacular ocean views; 2-minute walk to beach; kangaroos regularly visit the park at night; whale watching from cabins and sites; adjacent to Kalbarri National Park; 4km coastal walking path to town; families consistently mention stunning sunsets and natural beauty as major highlights

Cleanliness (14/15) — Overwhelmingly praised as "immaculate," "spotlessly clean," and "the cleanest caravan park" by numerous families; ensuites described as "spotless" with strong hot showers; camp kitchen and laundry facilities consistently noted as very clean and well-maintained; staff observed constantly tidying and maintaining grounds; one point lost for isolated mentions of mould/rust in some ensuites and pool needing cleaning in one review

Value (11/15) — Described as expensive and "on the pricey side" with sites over $80/night; however, most families felt the quality justified the cost with comments like "well worth it" and "you pay a little more but well worth it"; unique ensuite feature for every site adds value; some confusion noted about variable daily rates; points lost for being among the most expensive parks on travellers' routes

Site Size (9/10) — Sites consistently described as "large," "spacious," "generous," and "wide" with ample room for big rigs, caravans with awnings, and 4x4s; all sites fully grassed; plenty of privacy between sites; one family with "big rig" specifically praised the ample space; sites noted as level and flat

Sentiment (10/10) — Exceptionally high sentiment with families repeatedly calling it "the best park" they've stayed at; numerous mentions of extending stays, rebooking for following years, and "will definitely be back"; many reviews state they would "highly recommend"; families expressed genuine enthusiasm and satisfaction; minimal negative sentiment aside from price concerns

Location (8/10) — Beautiful coastal location next to Red Bluff with beach access; about 5km (5-minute drive) from Kalbarri town center, not walking distance; convenient for Kalbarri National Park access (30 minutes); some road noise noted from highway proximity; stunning views and natural setting praised; points lost for distance from town amenities and some traffic noise

WHY NOT 100:
1. No dedicated kids entertainment facilities (playground, jumpy pillow, or structured activities) which limits appeal for families seeking on-site entertainment beyond natural surroundings.
2. Pool area lacks adequate shade with "only one single umbrella and 6 chairs" leading to families unable to use the pool comfortably in 30+ degree heat; multiple reviews specifically mentioned this issue.
3. Premium pricing ($80+ per night for powered sites) and location 5km from town (requiring driving for shops, restaurants) means less convenience and higher costs than some families prefer, despite the quality justifying it for most.
