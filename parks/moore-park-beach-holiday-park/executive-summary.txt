PARK: Moore Park Beach Holiday Park
SCORE: 68/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-18
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 116

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (10/20) — Reviews consistently note this is a "quiet park" with "not usually too many kids around" and no mention of organized entertainment, kids clubs, or waterparks; one family mentioned Santa visit and sausage sizzle events; nearby Bob Neilsen Park, skate park, and three playgrounds provide off-site options for children.

Nature (19/20) — Outstanding natural setting with "right on the beach" location mentioned in almost every review; families report "sound of the waves," turtle sightings, dolphins on beach walks, patrolled swimming beach, and extensive bike paths and boardwalks along the waterfront for nature-based activities.

Cleanliness (13/15) — Mixed evidence with majority praising "spotless amenities," "always clean," and "very clean" facilities, but several reviews from 2023-2024 reported amenities "not cleaned once" over multiple days, bins overflowing, and pool incidents; recent 2025-2026 reviews show improvement under current managers.

Value (13/15) — Reviews describe pricing as "fair priced," "reasonably priced," and "great price for such a lovely area," with good value for beachfront location; families appreciate the "old school" affordability, though strict cancellation policy (lose deposit with 10 days notice) disappointed some guests.

Site Size (9/10) — Consistent praise for "huge sites," "good sized sites," "decent size," "spacious and not cramped in," and "well spaced" throughout reviews; sites suitable for large vans and caravans with both powered and unpowered options available right on beachfront.

Sentiment (9/10) — Strong repeat visitation with families stating "we go every year," "third time here," "came back after 6 years," and "will definitely be back"; highly recommended by most guests; negative sentiment focused on one poor management period in late 2023 and occasional cleanliness lapses.

Location (9/10) — Excellent beachfront position 16-20km from Bundaberg with walking distance to bowls club, tavern, IGA (5-10 min drive), and local shops; "away from hustle and bustle" yet accessible; patrolled beach and surf club adjacent; some feel the distance from major attractions limits convenience.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Zero organized children's entertainment, kids clubs, or structured activities on-site—this is explicitly noted as a "quiet park" without kid-focused programming, making it less suitable for families needing organized entertainment for young children.
2. Inconsistent cleaning standards with multiple reviews reporting amenities not cleaned for days at a time, overflowing bins with flies, and pool hygiene incidents during peak periods, though recent management appears to have improved this.
3. Aging and basic facilities throughout—amenities described as "old," "tired," "aging," "not modern" with maintenance issues in cabins (broken stove, door problems, minimal supplies) and limited camp kitchen facilities (no stove mentioned as needed).

NOTABLE REVIEW PHRASES:
"right on the beach," "spotless amenities," "not usually too many kids around," "old but very clean," "peaceful and quiet," "sound of the waves," "friendly and helpful staff," "decent size sites"
