PARK: Pine Country Caravan Park | Mt Gambier
SCORE: 78/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-22
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 215

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (16/20) — Families consistently praise the playground and jumping pillow that keep kids entertained, with flat land perfect for bike riding. Weekend food trucks and coffee vans add value. Pop-up events like wine tasting and waffle vans mentioned. Glamping tents with fire pits provide unique experiences. Alpacas on-site delight children. However, no water park or pool.

Nature (16/20) — Park features beautiful lush green grass, mature trees providing shade, and a farm-like atmosphere with alpacas and views of cows in adjacent paddocks. Located near Mount Gambier's natural wonders including the Blue Lake, sink holes, and caves. Reviews describe "gorgeous surrounds," "breathtaking" scenery, and "relaxing country vibes." However, it's a suburban park rather than bushland setting.

Cleanliness (14/15) — Overwhelming consensus on exceptional cleanliness. Reviews repeatedly use "spotless," "immaculate," "super clean," and "cleanest I've ever been to." Dedicated groundsman maintains grounds daily. Amenities described as "constantly cleaned" with music playing in bathrooms. A few isolated complaints about dated facilities and one incident during Easter with temporary water outage.

Value (14/15) — Consistently praised as excellent value with prices around $30-35/night for powered sites. Multiple reviews state "very reasonable," "great price," and compare favourably to more expensive Big4 competitor. Weekend food trucks and events add value. Some guests felt ensuite sites slightly pricey, and cabin pricing for summer heat conditions questioned.

Site Size (9/10) — Drive-through sites repeatedly described as "spacious," "large and lush," with "plenty of room for annex and car." Unpowered sites accommodate large tents plus vehicles. A few reviews mention sites felt "tight" or "uncomfortably narrow" when neighbouring large caravans, affecting privacy.

Sentiment (9/10) — Strong repeat visitation and recommendations with phrases like "will definitely be back," "our first choice always," and "one of our favourite caravan parks." Staff praised for being "lovely," "helpful," and going above and beyond (helping with lost phone, emergency support). Several 1-star reviews cite poor customer service, racism allegations, generator noise, and facility issues that prevent full marks.

Location (10/10) — Perfect location just outside Mount Gambier (3-5 minutes drive) providing quiet, peaceful setting while maintaining easy access to town, Blue Lake (2km), sinkholes, caves, and Port MacDonnell. Described as "close to everything" with shops 400m away including Scroll Queen bakery. Some road noise from truck bypass noted but mostly bearable.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Aging facilities - Multiple reviews note showers and cabins are dated, cramped, or need renovation despite being clean and functional. One glamping tent review described dangerous heat levels in summer without adequate cooling.
2. Limited on-site entertainment - No pool, water park, or extensive activities beyond playground and jumping pillow. Jumping pillow was out of order for 3 months during one family's visit, still being advertised.
3. Inconsistent customer service - While most praise staff highly, several serious complaints about rude service, poor site allocation, racism allegations, and lack of flexibility detract from otherwise excellent reputation.
