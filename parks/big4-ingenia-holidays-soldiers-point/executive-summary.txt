PARK: BIG4 Ingenia Holidays Soldiers Point
SCORE: 83/100
DATE SCORED: 2025-01-10
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 181

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (19/20) — Exceptional facilities praised by families: indoor heated pool with toddler area, outdoor pool with slide, bowling alley, jumping pillow, pedal carts, under-5s Jungle Bungle indoor play area, bike track, tennis courts, daily kids club activities with crafts and entertainment, coffee vans and food trucks. Families consistently report children "entertained from dawn to dusk" with "so much to do" across all age groups, particularly outstanding for under-5s.

Nature (15/20) — Described as having a "tropical resort feel" with palm trees and lush landscaping creating privacy between sites. Amazing bird life with friendly lorikeets that eat from hands. Beach access directly across the road, fishing jetty at Soldiers Point, and bay location provide natural attractions. However, not close to major beaches and some reviews note it's "near the bay" rather than ocean beaches, limiting the natural environment score.

Cleanliness (13/15) — Strong majority praise facilities as "impeccable," "spotless," and "the best we have ever seen," with amenities cleaned 4 times daily. Modern ensuite bathrooms with heated floors consistently highlighted. However, significant concerning reviews describe older cabins with serious cleanliness issues: ants on beds, carpet not cleaned "in 10 years," cockroaches, dirty kettles, greasy rangehoods, dusty fans, and blood-stained sheets. Clear quality gap between new and old accommodation.

Value (11/15) — Mixed feedback creates tension. Many families call it "fantastic value for money" and "worth every cent," appreciating the extensive facilities that keep families on-site. However, strong criticism of pricing: $165/night for powered sites called "ridiculous and disgusting," $350/night cabins, described as "way too expensive," "most expensive caravan park we have ever stayed in," and "taking advantage of being the only caravan park in the area." Cancellation policies also criticized as inflexible.

Site Size (8/10) — Positive feedback on caravan sites being "very spacious," "large," and "perfectly sized for a large van, annex and car in a row" with privacy screens and tropical plants creating separation. Ensuite sites particularly praised. However, some sites described as "tight" for vans, park "built with max land usage so you are very close to your neighbours," and tent sites "scattered just filling up empty lots" with limited space. Site 19 specifically noted as having no room for car with caravan.

Sentiment (9/10) — Overwhelmingly positive with families declaring it "the best park we have ever stayed at," "one of the best BIG4 parks," and stating they "never want to leave." High repeat visitation with many families visiting multiple times and "can't wait to come back." Strong recommendations across reviews. However, sentiment damaged by several 1-star reviews citing poor customer service, inflexible policies, construction noise during stays, disruptive guests, and disappointing older cabin conditions. Staff receive mostly high praise but some describe reception as "not welcoming" and "not friendly."

Location (8/10) — Excellent convenience factors: bowling club next door for meals, pub across the road, beach access opposite, walking distance to cafes and eateries, short drive (5-10 mins) to shops and IGA. Close to Sydney (2.5 hours) making it accessible for families. However, described as "out of town," "way out" requiring driving to main shopping areas, and "out of the way if you want close to the Bay." Not near major beaches, which limits the location appeal for some families.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Significant cleanliness concerns in older cabins with multiple detailed reports of ants, cockroaches, filthy carpets, blood-stained sheets, and greasy appliances creating serious quality inconsistency across accommodation types.
2. Premium pricing ($165+ for powered sites, $350+ for cabins) generates strong value concerns with guests feeling it's "taking advantage" and comparing unfavourably to nearby alternatives, while inflexible cancellation policies cause frustration.
3. Management issues including inadequate response to disruptive guests, construction/maintenance occurring during peak holiday periods without guest notification, inconsistent customer service, and some staff described as unwelcoming or dismissive of concerns.
