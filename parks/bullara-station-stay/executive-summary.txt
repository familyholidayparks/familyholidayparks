PARK: Bullara Station Stay
SCORE: 86/100
DATE SCORED: 2025-05-28
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 339

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (17/20) — Reviews consistently praise the station's unique entertainment: famous Friday 'Burger Night' with live music serving 400-500 guests, Thursday pizza nights, 'The Table' restaurant with communal dining, camp host activities, and roaming farm animals (cattle, sheep, goats, horses). Children love seeing Billy the Goat, feeding animals, and the quirky outdoor 'lava tree' showers. Walking trails include Sunset Hill, Windmill Walk, and Tin Can Wall. Limited traditional playground equipment noted by some families.

Nature (18/20) — Authentic working cattle station set in WA's red earth outback with vast open spaces and natural bush setting. 4WD track to Exmouth Gulf mangroves and mudflats praised by adventurous families. Central location provides access to Ningaloo Coast (60km to Coral Bay, 100km to Exmouth) for whale sharks, snorkelling, and pristine beaches. Walking trails showcase native landscape. Some reviewers note dust and heat, lacking water features for cooling off.

Cleanliness (14/15) — Reviews overwhelmingly praise cleanliness: "immaculately clean", "facilities spotless", "never felt cleaner camping", "not even a cobweb in sight". The rustic outdoor showers and toilet blocks (some with trees inside) are quirky yet consistently described as very clean with hot water. Camp kitchen and amenities well-maintained by staff who "clearly love their jobs".

Value (12/15) — Mixed feedback on pricing. Many families find it "worth every penny" for the unique experience, quality food, and excellent facilities. However, some reviews note it's "fairly pricey" with examples: $78pp for dinner (one rib), $36 for firewood bag, $76pp for restaurant, $500/night safari tents with maintenance issues. Several reviews call it "overpriced" compared to alternatives, while others say "reasonably priced" and better value than staying in Exmouth.

Site Size (9/10) — Reviews consistently praise "spacious sites", "generous sized sites", "exceptionally large (almost twice the size of normal cp sites)", "very large site", and "huge sites which is nice for a change". 160+ campsites across powered and unpowered areas. Some families placed in tight spots near toilets with noise complaints, and a few note being "camped in the scrub out the back".

Sentiment (10/10) — Exceptional family sentiment with reviews declaring it "the highlight of our road trip", "our favourite place to stay in Australia", "must-do", "bucket list worthy", "we will definitely be back", and "extended our stay because we loved it so much". Staff happiness noted repeatedly: "you can tell they love their jobs", "happiest staff in all of Australia". High recommendation rate with families planning return visits and telling friends.

Location (6/10) — While the station setting itself is praised, the location is "in the middle of nowhere" - 60km from Coral Bay and 100km from Exmouth, requiring significant driving for beach access. Reviewers appreciate it as a "great base" for day trips and "central to most area attractions", but note it's "not really practical for the reef" and "quite a return drive to swim". The 1km+ dirt access road has "lots of holes and washboarding".

WHY NOT 100:
1. No traditional water play facilities (pool, splash pad) for cooling off in the hot, dusty outback environment - several families noted extreme heat with nowhere to swim nearby.
2. Limited food flexibility and kitchen capacity - restaurant books out months ahead (must book before arrival), cafe closes mid-day, camp kitchen has only one small stove/oven causing queues, creating challenges for families with dietary requirements or those unable to pre-book meals.
3. Remote location requires 1+ hour driving to reach beaches/swimming at Coral Bay or Exmouth, and the dirt access road is rough with washboarding - not ideal for families wanting daily beach access or those with smaller vehicles.
