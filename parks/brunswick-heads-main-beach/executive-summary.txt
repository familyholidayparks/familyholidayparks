WHY THIS SCORE:

There are no waterparks, jumping pillows, or kids clubs.

Cleanliness (11/15) — Generally positive comments about beach cleanliness with "Always clear water and clean sand" mentioned multiple times, though one review noted "homeless people living behind the beach in the nature reserve" which impacted perception of the area.

Value (4/15) — Significant concerns raised about parking costs: "Paid $7.50 for 1.5 hours parking," "parking beyond our budget," "we shouldn't have to pay for parking," and "food is over priced." No accommodation value to assess as this is not a holiday park.

Site Size (2/10) — Not applicable as a holiday park metric. Reviews mention "Wide open spacious beach" and "plenty of space on the beach" but no camping or caravan sites exist at this public beach location.

Sentiment (8/10) — Very positive emotional responses with "paradise," "I love this place. Always have. Always will," "can't wait to go back," though tempered by frustration over parking challenges and lack of facilities for some visitors.

Location (7/10) — Good accessibility with "really easy to get around and park" (when not busy), walking distance to Byron Bay, near Brunswick Heads township, though parking difficulties during peak times were frequently mentioned.

WHY NOT 100:
[1] This is a public beach, not a holiday park - there are zero accommodation facilities, no powered sites, no cabins, and no camping infrastructure whatsoever for families seeking multi-day stays.
[2] Minimal amenities for families - multiple reviews specifically noted "no amenities like showering place after swimming," "Not really any facilities so bring a picnic," and lack of conveniences that family holiday parks typically provide.
[3] Significant parking challenges and costs undermine family value - recurring complaints about expensive metered parking ($7.50 for 1.5 hours), "Hard to find parking," "Get there really really early or it's hopeless," making it impractical for families with young children and beach gear.
