PARK: Gladstone Showground RV Campsite
SCORE: 38/100
DATE SCORED: 2025
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 79

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (2/20) — Reviews indicate absolutely no on-site entertainment or activities for children at this basic showground. One reviewer mentioned "my kids loved it" but provided no detail about activities. The only family amenity mentioned is a public pool and water park next door to the showground, not part of the facility itself.

Nature (4/20) — This is an urban showground with minimal natural environment. Reviews describe "not much shade or green grass and grounds are run down" and "freie Fläche" (open area). Multiple mentions of grass sites but no references to bushland, nature, or scenic surroundings. Located in town centre, not a nature-based setting.

Cleanliness (9/15) — Reviews consistently mention clean amenities with phrases like "Clean facilities", "very clean toilets", "amenities are clean" appearing frequently. However, facilities are repeatedly described as "very old", "dated", "toilet blocks usable but not pretty", and one review mentioned toilets smell "like a stock yard". Clean but very basic and aging.

Value (11/15) — Pricing at $30 per night for power and water is frequently described as "reasonable", "good price", "great value", and "a lot cheaper than Caravan Parks". Some reviews note it's expensive for the basic facilities provided. No dump point on site is mentioned as a negative. Generally considered fair value for an overnight stop.

Site Size (7/10) — Reviews mention "plenty of room", "heaps of sites", "big area", "Large sites", and "drive through ones for longer rigs". However, multiple reviews warn sites are "very un level", "sloping ground", "be prepared to level up quite a bit", and "Level sites are a premium". One review noted being "squeezed in very close to other vans".

Sentiment (6/10) — Mixed sentiment with many positive comments about caretakers being helpful, but also concerning negative reviews about "rudest caretaker", service inconsistency, and discriminatory policies (no tents, no car camping, no swags). Some repeat visitors evident, but significant complaints about management and restrictions dampen overall sentiment.

Location (8/10) — Consistently praised for central location with "close to town", "walking distance" to Woolworths, shops, cafes, brewery, and "5 minute walk" to amenities. Pool next door mentioned multiple times. Good base for exploring Gladstone and accessing Heron Island. Urban setting rather than scenic.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Zero on-site entertainment or activities for families with young children - this is a basic overnight campsite, not a holiday park with kids' facilities or programs.
2. Extremely dated and aging infrastructure with old toilet blocks, uneven sloping sites requiring significant levelling, no dump point, and minimal natural environment or shade.
3. Inconsistent service quality with multiple reviews citing rude caretakers, discriminatory policies excluding tent campers and car sleepers, and poor communication about events (rodeo night) that impact availability.
