PARK: NRMA Airlie Beach Holiday Park
SCORE: 58/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-24
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 312

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (9/20) — Reviews highlight two swimming pools including a "resort-style lagoon pool" that kids love, with occasional live music and happy hours at the on-site cafe, but families noted very limited organized children's activities, with one review mentioning only "one activity for the kids during our 4 day stay," and the entertainment facilities are significantly outweighed by overcrowding issues.

Nature (12/20) — The park offers tropical surroundings with palm trees, native birds including cockatoos, and access to a scenic coastal boardwalk leading to the marina, but the immediate park environment is described as "not much shade, no grass, just rocks, leaves & dirt" in some areas, with limited natural bush or beach access directly from the park itself.

Cleanliness (7/15) — Reviews consistently report major cleanliness concerns including "dirty amenities," "camp kitchen is bedlam with dirty pans and sinks full of table scraps," "bathroom floors were slippery, many door handles were broken," showers with "mould in the tile grout," and facilities that are "not cleaned or maintained often enough," though some families found certain blocks "spotless" and "well maintained."

Value (7/15) — Families repeatedly described the park as "very expensive for what it is," "totally overpriced," and "not worth the money," with specific complaints like "$60 for a campsite" with inadequate facilities, "$88 in July" for small uneven sites, and "over $1000 for two weeks," though some budget travelers found unpowered sites at "$20-44 per night" reasonable for the location.

Site Size (5/10) — Reviews consistently describe sites as "packed in like sardines," "tiny sites," "ridiculously small and uneven," "very small, with most people having issues parking their vans," and unpowered areas as "a big patch of grass which anyone could park anywhere" on sloped, uneven ground with "no demarcation," making this a major concern for families with larger rigs.

Sentiment (6/10) — Guest sentiment is deeply divided, with many families stating "won't be back," "worst caravan park we have stayed in," "DO NOT STAY HERE," and "worst experience ever," while others praised staff as "amazing" and facilities as "excellent," resulting in mixed recommendations and low repeat visitation intent particularly for tent/unpowered campers.

Location (12/10) — The park scores highly for location with reviews praising it as "next door to Coles," "5 minute walk to shops," "close to everything," "great location with easy walk to supermarket," and "perfect base for exploring the Whitsundays," with convenient access to marina tours and a scenic boardwalk to Airlie Beach town center.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Severe overcrowding and insufficient amenities create poor family experiences, with multiple reviews citing "only 6 showers per gender for hundreds of people camping," "only 3 washing machines and 2 dryers for nearly 150 sites," long wait times, and unpowered areas "jam packed with backpackers" creating noise and privacy issues for families.

2. Persistent cleanliness and maintenance failures significantly impact family comfort, with widespread reports of dirty camp kitchens, mouldy shower blocks, broken facilities, "bathroom floors were slippery, many door handles were broken, some doors were rotting," and inadequate daily cleaning given the volume of guests.

3. Very poor site quality and space, particularly for unpowered camping, with families reporting "sloping, lumpy bit of grass," "uneven paddock at the back of the park with poor drainage," sites so small they're "jammed together" for caravans, and inadequate infrastructure causing families to walk through "mud and slush" during wet weather, making this unsuitable for many family camping styles.
