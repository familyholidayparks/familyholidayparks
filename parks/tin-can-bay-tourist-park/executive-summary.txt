PARK: Tin Can Bay Tourist Park
SCORE: 72/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-21
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 154

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (11/20) — Reviews consistently note the park lacks traditional kids entertainment like playgrounds or bouncy pillows, with only a basketball court available; families appreciated the heated pool and mentioned "kids busy all the time" but primarily through nature activities like dolphin feeding and bay walks rather than on-site entertainment infrastructure.

Nature (16/20) — Strong natural setting with families praising proximity to dolphin feeding ("hand feed the local dolphins", "dolphin feeding was awesome to watch"), 4km walking/bike paths along the bay, bird watching opportunities ("so many different types of birds"), views of wetlands, and easy access to Fraser Island/K'gari; described as having "nice garden/bush setting" with "lots of shady trees".

Cleanliness (15/15) — Exceptional and consistent praise across virtually all reviews: "cleanest toilets, showers, and eating area I have ever come across", "amenities block is one of the cleanest you could encounter", "spotless", "immaculate", "super clean well set out", with amenities described as modern and regularly cleaned throughout stays.

Value (11/15) — Mixed feedback with some praising "reasonable price" and "fair for what you get", but several reviews mentioning "overpriced", "a little expensive", and "very expensive" particularly for shorter stays; one reviewer noted $50 for one night as overpriced, though G'day members found "pricing is very good".

Site Size (9/10) — Consistently positive with reviews noting "huge sites", "spacious sites", "great size", "big sites for the vans", "not all squeezed together", "plenty of space so you aren't rubbing shoulders with the neighbour's", and "good roomy sites down the back" with level ground and modern slabs.

Sentiment (10/10) — Overwhelmingly positive sentiment with extremely high repeat visitation intent: multiple families booked for following year, many stating "will definitely be back", "would stay again", "adding this to our list of favourite spots", with one family staying 2 weeks one year then 3 weeks the next and already booked for a third year.

Location (10/10) — Well-positioned on edge of quiet town with short walk to waterfront (200m), close to dolphin feeding marina (3km), easy access to shops/IGA/pubs, ideal base for Fraser Island/Rainbow Beach trips, with walking paths to playgrounds and esplanade; families appreciated the quiet location off main road while remaining accessible.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Limited on-site kids entertainment infrastructure with no playground facilities, bouncy pillows, or gaming rooms that modern family parks typically offer - multiple reviews specifically noted "no playground" and the park is "well suited to the family who enjoys being in nature and doesn't need a lot of entertainment".
2. Value perception issues with several families finding pricing high, particularly for short stays, with specific complaints about $50 for one night and general comments that it's "a little expensive" or "very expensive" compared to expectations.
3. Minor amenity gaps including limited WiFi ("extremely limited and lasted half an hour for a family of 4"), poor internet connectivity noted by multiple guests, one washing machine not working, and low water pressure in showers mentioned as the "only small downside" by several reviewers.
