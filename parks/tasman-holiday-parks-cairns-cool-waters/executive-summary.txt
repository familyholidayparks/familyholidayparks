PARK: Tasman Holiday Parks - Cairns Cool Waters
SCORE: 68/100
DATE SCORED: 2025-05-23
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 176

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (11/20) — Limited structured entertainment with no dedicated playground mentioned; main attraction is the unique turtle and fish feeding experience in the creek which families consistently praised, plus a pool and spa area. Food trucks on Fridays and occasional live music events provide some activity.

Nature (17/20) — Outstanding natural setting with a freshwater creek running through the property featuring turtles, fish, eels, and occasional goannas. Lush tropical vegetation with "triple size" plants, shaded sites, nearby rainforest walks, and access to Goomoora Park. Creek is croc-free and swimmable. Highway noise noted by some reviewers.

Cleanliness (10/15) — Highly inconsistent reports; some amenity blocks described as "spotless" and "super clean" while others were "filthy most of the time" with overflowing bins, no soap, and dirty showers. Multiple cockroach and ant complaints in cabins. Pool area consistently praised as clean.

Value (10/15) — Mixed value perception; some found it "great value for money" at $123-180/night while others felt it was "very very poor" value and overpriced for cramped sites. Budget accommodation options available. Pricing varies significantly by season.

Site Size (5/10) — Consistently criticized as "packed in like sardines," "tight sites," "very cramped" with narrow roads making navigation difficult for larger rigs. Multiple reports of damage to vehicles trying to maneuver. Creek-front sites praised but limited availability.

Sentiment (8/10) — Divided sentiment with passionate advocates calling it "the best camp site" alongside strongly negative experiences citing flooding, security issues, and poor management response. Many repeat visitors and recommendations balanced against "would never stay here again" reviews.

Location (7/10) — 15-minute drive to Cairns CBD, close to shopping centers, Skyrail, and Kuranda. Bus stop opposite (hourly service until 6pm). Convenient for Cairns attractions but highway noise disturbs some guests. Not within walking distance of city center.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Serious safety and maintenance failures including documented flooding incidents that damaged guest property ($24,000 claim), security breaches with drug-affected individuals entering vans, cockroach infestations in cabins, and management's poor response to these critical issues.
2. Extremely cramped site layout causing multiple reports of vehicle damage during maneuvering, with sites described as too small for larger rigs and roads so narrow that two-way traffic is unsafe.
3. Highly inconsistent cleanliness standards with one amenity block "filthy most of the time" with broken facilities for days, mouldy cabin walls, oily floors, and variable cabin quality ranging from modern/clean to "run down" and "nothing like pictured" despite identical pricing.
