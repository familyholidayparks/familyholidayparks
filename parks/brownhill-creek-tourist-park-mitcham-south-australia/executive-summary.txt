PARK: Brownhill Creek Tourist Park - Mitcham, South Australia
SCORE: 58/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-13
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 166

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (8/20) — Reviews consistently note "limited children entertainment" with a basic playground and small pool. Multiple families mentioned "nothing for teenagers" and lack of kids activities. The park has a cafe and occasional pizza nights but no organized entertainment or kids clubs. One reviewer specifically stated it's a "great spot for adult campers" rather than families seeking entertainment.

Nature (18/20) — The park excels here with overwhelmingly positive comments about the "beautiful bush setting," "thousands of birds," and wildlife including "koalas, kookaburras, magpies, kangaroos." Located in a hidden valley with a creek, guests described it as "nestled away between mountains" with "gorgeous flora and fauna" and "breath taking" walking trails. Many noted you "feel like you are really in the bush" despite being 15 minutes from Adelaide CBD.

Cleanliness (8/15) — This is a major weakness with persistent complaints across recent reviews. Multiple families reported "cabins aren't clean," "mould on the roof of the shower," "dirty sheets, towels," "sticky and filthy floors," "dust and cobwebs," and "food residue." The unpowered section was described as "Very 3rd class" with amenities that "never looked clean." While powered site amenities received better marks, cabin cleanliness issues dominate negative feedback.

Value (10/15) — Mixed sentiment with some finding it reasonable ($51-60/night) but many felt "not value for money" especially for cabins given cleanliness issues. Several mentioned price gouging during events (up to $90/night). Positive comments about location justify pricing for some, but families in cabins consistently felt disappointed given the condition versus cost.

Site Size (8/10) — Powered sites received positive feedback with "large sites," "drive through," and "artificial grass" noted as improvements. However, unpowered sites had issues with some being "sloping," "dusty," and poorly maintained. Families with caravans generally found sites adequate with "good shade" and space.

Sentiment (6/10) — Highly polarized reviews. While many caravanners loved the natural setting and would return, cabin guests were frequently disappointed. Recent reviews show declining satisfaction with persistent cleanliness complaints despite management being "told." Safety concerns and poor staff interactions mentioned by multiple guests. Repeat visitors often specifically for caravan stays, not cabins.

Location (9/10) — Strong performance with "only 15 min drive from the city but you feel like you are really in the bush" being a common theme. Close to Mitcham shopping centre, Adelaide Zoo, and public transport. The bush valley setting provides seclusion while maintaining accessibility. Only downside is very poor mobile reception in the valley location.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Severe and persistent cleanliness issues in cabins with mould, dirty linens, sticky floors, and poor maintenance despite multiple guests reporting these problems to management over extended periods.
2. Extremely poor WiFi and mobile phone reception throughout the park creates safety concerns and frustration for families, with many reviews describing it as a "complete black hole" despite being in a capital city.
3. Minimal kids entertainment facilities with only a basic playground and small pool, making it unsuitable for families seeking engaging activities for children, particularly teenagers.
