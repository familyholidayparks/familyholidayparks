PARK: Bananacoast Caravan Park
SCORE: 58/100
DATE SCORED: 2024-01-13
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 82

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (8/20) — Reviews mention a pool and play area for children, with one family noting "swimming pool suited small children which was wonderful." However, a critical review from a family with children under 3 reported the playground was "closed due getting some maintenance" with no prior notification, causing disappointment. Another review noted "rails surrounding the childrens playground is no longer connected to the ground supports as the wooden support beams have rotted." Limited on-site entertainment activities were mentioned beyond basic facilities.

Nature (12/20) — The park is located near Coffs Harbour beaches with reviews noting it's "not far from shops, beaches not far." One review highlighted the "convenient location makes it easy to explore the surrounding area like Byron Bay." The park itself is described as "beautifully maintained" in grounds, though the natural environment is limited by its highway-side location. Access to beaches requires crossing "an extremely busy 4 lane highway" according to one detailed review.

Cleanliness (8/15) — Cleanliness reviews are highly polarized. Recent positive reviews praise "very very clean and tidy" cabins and "spotlessly clean" service. However, multiple concerning reviews describe serious issues: two separate 1-star reviews detail "faeces all over the amenities" in shared facilities with rodent problems, another found "bed bugs" in cabin furniture, and several mention "mould on the ceiling in the bathroom," "urine smell coming from the lounge," and "filthy" conditions in budget cabins. The inconsistency suggests variable cleaning standards.

Value (11/15) — Many reviews praise affordability with phrases like "great price," "very affordable prices," and "value for money." Families appreciate the budget accommodation with one noting "$180 in total for a one night stay for 4 ppl" though calling it "what you pay for." However, some felt it overpriced, with one stating "most expensive cabin we stayed in on our holidays but also the most run-down" and another mentioning pricing "on the higher side" during peak season. Pet-friendly policies add value for many travellers.

Site Size (5/10) — Multiple reviews indicate tight spacing with "parking was a bit tight," "cabins are tightly packed in," and "car parking space was very tight." One review noted permanent residents' sites are "too close to other cabins." Several mentioned cramped conditions with "very small" cabins and limited space. No specific praise for spacious sites was found in family traveller reviews.

Sentiment (7/10) — Family sentiment is mixed. One glowing review from a family of 8 stated their son insists "we HAVE to always stay at this place" and they "booked rooms as soon as we got back home." Multiple families say "would stay again" or "will be back." However, several families with young children report they "would never stay again," with one family leaving early due to safety concerns and another due to bed bugs. Staff receive consistent praise ("Mel was amazing," "staff were very welcoming") which partially offsets facility concerns.

Location (7/10) — The park benefits from proximity to the Big Banana ("1 minute from the big banana") and shopping ("2 minute drive from the shopping centre"). However, the Pacific Highway location is a significant drawback with numerous reviews citing "noisy highway," "loud trucks going past all night," and "road noise from trucks and cars very loud." Roadworks complicated access during several stays. The location works well as a stopover point but limits appeal for extended family holidays.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Serious and recurring cleanliness issues in shared amenities including multiple reports of faecal contamination, bed bugs in cabins, and mould problems that pose health risks to families with young children.
2. Constant Pacific Highway noise disrupts sleep for families, with cabins positioned directly against the highway experiencing truck traffic throughout the night, making it unsuitable for restful family holidays.
3. Critical safety concerns with children's playground equipment deteriorating (wooden supports rotted, rails disconnected), playground closures without guest notification, and doors "off their hinges" creating hazards for young children.
