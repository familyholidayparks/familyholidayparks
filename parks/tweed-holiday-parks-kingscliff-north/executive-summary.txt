PARK: Tweed Holiday Parks Kingscliff North
SCORE: 72/100
DATE SCORED: 2024-01-15
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 89

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (8/20) — No dedicated kids club, playground, or waterpark mentioned. Families note "kids can ride around on the concrete paths" and had "an absolute ball" at the beach itself, but no organized activities or entertainment facilities are evident in reviews. Camp kitchen serves as social hub.

Nature (18/20) — Outstanding natural environment with beach "directly in front of the park," "less than 20 steps away." Families watched whales from cabins, walked to Fingal Head, and enjoyed "crystal clear water." Warning about brown snakes on beach noted. Beautiful coastal walks along pathway.

Cleanliness (14/15) — Consistently praised as "spotlessly clean," "immaculate," with "best camp kitchen ever." Amenities described as "always clean," "modern," and "well maintained." Minor mentions of aging ensuites and tent wear-and-tear in older accommodation.

Value (8/15) — Significant concerns about pricing with multiple reviews calling it "very expensive," "way overpriced," and "$131 for powered site...outrageous." However, some felt it was "fair given prime location" and "fantastic value" for cabins. Council criticized for "doubling nightly rate."

Site Size (9/10) — Sites consistently described as "generous," "large," "roomy," with "double concrete slabs." Easy access for vans and RVs. One review mentioned "bit tight for our rig" but generally very spacious. Artificial grass noted as helpful feature.

Sentiment (9/10) — Very high repeat visitation with families returning annually for 51 years in one case. Strong recommendations despite price concerns. Multiple theft incidents dampen sentiment. Families "love this place" and "can't wait to return."

Location (10/10) — Perfect score for location: "right on the beach," "across the road from shops," bowling and surf clubs adjacent, Woolworths 5-minute walk. Easy access to Tweed Coast attractions. Only negative: need to drive to main Kingscliff township.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Complete absence of on-site kids entertainment, playground equipment, or pool facilities means families must rely solely on beach activities for children's entertainment
2. Significant security vulnerabilities with multiple theft incidents reported, unlocked perimeter gates, no cameras on beachfront sites, and low fencing allowing unauthorized access
3. High pricing concerns repeatedly mentioned across recent reviews, with families feeling "fleeced" by council rate increases that impact affordability for Australian families
