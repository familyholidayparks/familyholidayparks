PARK: Fishery Falls Cairns Holiday Park
SCORE: 72/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-21
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 91

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (16/20) — Standout Saturday night Thai food dinners ($20pp) with live music and dancing consistently praised; creek swimming popular with kids; pool available but limited structured kids activities or playground mentioned; more adult-oriented entertainment than dedicated children's facilities.

Nature (18/20) — Exceptional tropical setting with lush plants described as "like a botanic garden"; creek access for swimming highly valued; "surrounded by tropical rainforest" atmosphere; beautiful bird sounds and flowing water create relaxing environment; close to Babinda Boulders and national parks.

Cleanliness (14/15) — Overwhelming praise for "spotless" and "immaculately clean" amenities; "cleanest facilities anywhere" mentioned repeatedly; daily bin collection; well-maintained grounds; some concerns about mould in dongas during wet season and boggy conditions when raining.

Value (10/15) — Mixed feedback with prices ranging $48-$189 per night; many found it "fair" and "lower than Cairns parks"; several complaints about being "overpriced" at $52-$56; Saturday Thai meals considered excellent value at $20pp; laundry noted as expensive at $5 per load.

Site Size (8/10) — Large, grassy sites consistently mentioned; "big spots" and "huge sites" praised; some complaints about trees encroaching and "not much space for van and car"; sites described as fairly private with tropical plants between them.

Sentiment (8/10) — Strong positive sentiment from majority with many return visitors; extremely helpful owners Ann and Dave frequently praised; significant negative reviews about permanent residents, barking dogs, and management issues from long-term stayers; "best park we've been to in all our travels" vs "wouldn't recommend" polarization.

Location (8/10) — 30-40km south of Cairns on Bruce Highway; "quiet" and "away from hustle" appreciated; 10 mins to Gordonvale, 15 mins to Babinda; close to supermarkets and national parks; distance from Cairns attractions a drawback for some; "bit far from anything if you want to tour around".

WHY NOT 100:
1. High proportion of permanent residents creates "shanty town" feel for some visitors with ongoing noise complaints about barking dogs that detract from the family holiday experience.
2. Limited dedicated children's entertainment and play facilities beyond the creek and pool — no playground, jumping pillow, or structured kids activities mentioned for young families.
3. Distance from Cairns (30-40km) and tourist attractions makes it less convenient for families wanting to visit theme parks, aquarium, and main Cairns attractions daily.
