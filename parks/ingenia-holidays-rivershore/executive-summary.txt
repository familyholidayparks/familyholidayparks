PARK: Ingenia Holidays Rivershore
SCORE: 85/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-23
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 302

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (18/20) — Reviews consistently praise the heated pool with two "ridiculously fun" waterslides, organized daily activities including archery, animal encounters, kids discos, movie nights, scavenger hunts, and facilities like mini golf, pedal karts, jumping pillow, and playground. Families report children being entertained all day with "so much to do" and many declare it "one of the best holiday parks ever."

Nature (16/20) — Located directly on Maroochy River with beautiful waterfront sites, fishing access from pontoon and jetty, and "stunning views of sunsets and sunrise." Reviewers appreciate the "peaceful natural setting," bird life, and proximity to beaches (15 mins) and attractions like Australia Zoo. Large grassy areas and "spectacular well manicured grounds" create a resort-in-nature feel.

Cleanliness (13/15) — Facilities described as "spotless," "immaculate," and "very clean" with modern amenities throughout. Multiple camp kitchens, clean showers with "great water pressure," and regular maintenance noted. Some reviews mention occasional lapses during peak periods with amenities needing more frequent cleaning, and a few specific maintenance issues like gravel on grass sites and water drainage from pool showers.

Value (12/15) — Pricing generates mixed feedback with powered sites ranging $87-250+ per night and glamping tents $580+ per night. Many find it "well worth it" for the resort-style experience and facilities, while others feel it's "expensive" or "over priced" compared to what's offered. Positive value mentions include reasonable food/drink prices, happy hour specials, and "great value for money" on glamping accommodation.

Site Size (9/10) — Sites consistently described as "huge," "massive," "extremely spacious," and "the biggest site I've ever stated at." Unpowered riverfront sites are "HUGE" and powered sites fit large rigs easily. Reviewers note "plenty of room" even with 30ft caravans and that glamping tents feel very private with gardens/trees between them.

Sentiment (9/10) — Overwhelmingly positive with families declaring "we will definitely be back," "already booked for next year," and "can't wait to come back." Staff repeatedly praised as "amazing," "friendly," and "going out of their way." Return visitors common with some visiting 3-4 times. Minor detractors include occasional rude staff encounters, road noise complaints, and crowding during peak periods with day visitors.

Location (8/10) — Central Sunshine Coast position with "good location for day trips." Approximately 15 mins to beaches, Big Pineapple, and SEA LIFE Aquarium; 7km from Maroochydore; 4km to Bli Bli shopping. Riverfront setting praised but positioned on busy David Low Way creating road noise. Described as "blissful" being slightly out of town yet convenient to everything.

WHY NOT 100:
Road noise from David Low Way is a significant complaint with multiple reviews mentioning traffic noise "all night" that disrupted sleep and "destroyed all the beauty" for some tent campers. Peak period overcrowding impacts experience with day visitors (paying $20 entry) filling the pool, cabanas, and restaurant to capacity while overnight guests paying $200-580+ per night struggle to find seating or pool space. Cleanliness and maintenance standards show inconsistency with some reviews noting facilities not cleaned daily during busy periods, gravel spread over grass sites, drainage issues creating muddy areas after rain, and specific maintenance lapses like blood on toilet doors and cobwebs remaining for days.
