PARK: Shipwreck Bay Holiday Park
SCORE: 58/100
DATE SCORED: 2024-01-15
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 15

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (10/20) — No on-site kids entertainment facilities mentioned; families rely entirely on the excellent playground across the road at Lake Pertobe and beach activities; one reviewer noted "kids were having a great time in the outdoors" but no organized activities, kids club, or entertainment programs evident.

Nature (18/20) — Outstanding natural environment with "awesome beach access and lovely scenery," direct coastal location with "great walking trails and fishing," Lake Pertobe across the road, and "beachside with loads of parks"; multiple reviewers gave location "10 out of 10" for beach access.

Cleanliness (9/15) — Inconsistent maintenance with mixed reviews; one family praised "cleanliness in the toilet block 10/10" while recent reviews cited "toilet paper stuck walls and not cleaned off," "bathrooms/showers haven't been cleaned for a few days," and sites with "a lot of dirt and barely any grass."

Value (8/15) — Multiple complaints about pricing with "expensive," "over priced over rated" mentioned; families felt the cramped sites and basic facilities didn't justify costs, though location partially compensates.

Site Size (4/10) — Significant criticism about "sites were very small and crowded," "7 point turn just to get in and out of my site," "caravan sites and laneways are a bit small and close together for a larger van," and concerns about having to "share my site."

Sentiment (6/10) — Mixed family sentiment; while some said "fantastic for families" and "would definitely go again," others stated "would not stay again" due to noise issues, cramped conditions, and security concerns including attempted theft; noise management repeatedly criticized.

Location (10/10) — Perfect beachside location with "beach access and Lake Pertobe" within walking distance, "right on the coast," and proximity to Warrnambool attractions; universally praised as the park's strongest feature.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Severely cramped and poorly designed sites make maneuvering larger vans extremely difficult, with multiple families reporting multi-point turns and sites that feel shared rather than private.
2. Inadequate noise management and security with teenagers partying until 3am, attempted theft incidents, and "management and security inactive" when families needed intervention.
3. Inconsistent cleanliness standards and basic facilities with no camp kitchen found, difficult power access requiring long extension cords, and maintenance issues ranging from dirty amenities to sites with excessive dirt and minimal grass.
