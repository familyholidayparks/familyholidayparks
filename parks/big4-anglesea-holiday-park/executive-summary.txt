PARK: BIG4 Anglesea Holiday Park
SCORE: 83/100
DATE SCORED: 2025
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 121

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (18/20) — Reviews consistently highlight the "very large indoor heated pool" with waterslides, splash park, baby pool and toddler areas as exceptional. Families praised jumping pillows, adventure playgrounds, games rooms with ping pong and arcade games, dedicated toddler activity rooms, and special holiday programming including science shows, magic shows, and regular food truck visits. Multiple families noted kids "couldn't get enough" and spent 2.5+ hours in facilities daily.

Nature (16/20) — The park delivers authentic Australian wildlife experiences with resident kangaroos appearing at cabin doors, cockatoos, galahs, and "wildlife right at the door." Located on the Great Ocean Road with beach access via 10-15 minute walk or short drive. Beautiful well-kept gardens and landscaping throughout. However, not directly beachfront which cost some points for families wanting immediate beach access.

Cleanliness (14/15) — Exceptional praise for cleanliness: "cleanest Big 4 I've ever stayed in," "immaculately clean," "spotlessly clean," "facilities always in perfect condition." Individual bathroom/shower rooms described as "best I've seen" with layout feeling like private facilities. Grounds "well manicured" and "clean all the time." Minor points lost for occasional mentions of bathrooms getting dirty quickly during peak periods.

Value (11/15) — Mixed feedback on value. Positive mentions of "cheaper than next two Big 4 sites" and reasonable off-season rates ($55/night caravan site). However, multiple complaints about peak pricing ($460/night for 3-bedroom unit, $220/night safari tent) feeling expensive, especially with limited supplies (coffee, dishwashing tablets) requiring reception visits. Some felt entertained for 3 days maximum before activities became repetitive, making longer stays less valuable.

Site Size (6/10) — Significant weakness with multiple complaints: "very cramped campsite," "couldn't put up awning," "like rabbits in cages," "sites are a tight fit." Caravan sites on artificial turf are compact, requiring good reversing skills. Cabins well-spaced but powered sites clearly undersized for larger rigs. Some sites next to main road suffered noise issues.

Sentiment (9/10) — Overwhelmingly positive with families stating "best holiday park we've ever stayed at," "already planning our return," "immediately booked again for next year," and "10+ years as our go-to park." High repeat visitation and strong recommendations. Points lost for several serious complaints about inflexible booking policies during emergencies, staff rudeness in isolated incidents, and disappointment from families with older children (8-12) finding limited age-appropriate activities.

Location (9/10) — Convenient location "5 minute walk to town," close to shops, cafes, and IGA. Excellent Great Ocean Road base for touring. Beach access via 10-minute walk or short drive. Some families with young kids found walk "not really within walking distance" when carrying beach gear. Close to Torquay (20 minutes) for major shopping.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Site size is the major weakness—cramped caravan/RV sites with insufficient space for awnings and outdoor living, making families feel "like rabbits in cages" and requiring expert reversing skills for larger rigs.
2. Value concerns during peak periods with high nightly rates ($460 for 3-bedroom units) combined with limited in-unit supplies and entertainment options that become repetitive for stays beyond 3-4 days, particularly for families with children aged 8+.
3. Beach access requires a 10-15 minute walk or drive rather than being beachfront, which disappointed families expecting easier access, and some serious customer service failures around booking flexibility during emergencies and isolated incidents of staff rudeness.
