PARK: Anchor Belle Holiday Park
SCORE: 82/100
DATE SCORED: 2025
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 151

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (17/20) — Reviews consistently highlight exceptional kids entertainment with heated indoor pool maintaining perfect temperature year-round, large jumping pillow, fully equipped games room with pool tables and arcade games, basketball court, playground, and organised activities including scavenger hunts and seasonal events. Families note "jumping pillow, indoor heated pool and usual jumping pillow games room to keep kids entertained" and "indoor pool is GENUINELY WARM, which was delightful and draws the kids in." Staff organise special events for Easter, Australia Day, and New Year's Eve.

Nature (17/20) — Excellent natural setting with easy 5-minute walk to beach via bush path, daily wildlife sightings including "little rabbits happily running around mainly at sunset," wallabies, kangaroos, and native birds. Direct beach access through back gate for fishing and walks. Located on Phillip Island with penguin parade 10 minutes away and close to all island attractions. Reviews note "short walk to the beach" and "peaceful surroundings, and visits from wildlife including kangaroos, rabbits, and even rare birds."

Cleanliness (14/15) — Outstanding cleanliness standards with reviews stating "extremely clean; we are traveling with a rent van and we stayed in few camper sites and I found anchor bell park the cleanest," "amenities are spotless," "ensuites are immaculate," and "staff seen constantly maintaining gardens." Camp kitchens described as "very very clean" and "immaculate condition." New amenities block particularly praised.

Value (12/15) — Generally good value with positive comments on "reasonable price," "great value for every dollar spent," and "fantastic value for money sites." Some concerns raised about "$15 per child is a joke" and costs during peak times. G'Day Parks membership offers savings.

Site Size (7/10) — Mixed feedback on site size. Positive: "large sites," "good size," "beautifully grassed," "spacious and grassy." Negative: "sites are tight to get into and may catch a novice caranner off guard," "can be a little tight to get onto site when park is busy," "sites were tiny," and "very cramped...barely enough space to park a car and set up a tent together."

Sentiment (9/10) — Exceptional loyalty with multiple families returning for 15-22+ years, describing it as "second home," "home away from home," and "genuinely feels like a second home." Staff (Amy, Brad, Kelli, Janine) receive universal praise. Very high recommendation rate. A few negative experiences relate to customer service incidents with tent campers and site allocation issues.

Location (6/10) — Good location but not premium beachfront. Short walk to beach (5 minutes), but 30-minute walk or 2km drive to main Cowes town centre. Close to penguin parade (10-15 minutes) and island attractions. Reviews note "away from the hustle and bustle but walking distance to the shops" and "not too far from down town Cowes."

WHY NOT 100:
1. Site access can be challenging with tight turns and narrow internal roads, particularly difficult for larger caravans and causing concern for novice caravanners, with some sites described as cramped especially for tent camping.
2. Location requires driving or 30-minute walk to Cowes main street rather than being in prime town centre position, and while beach access is good it's still a walk rather than absolute beachfront.
3. Some facilities showing age with pool and games room described as "a little outdated" and "old" despite being functional and clean, plus a few serious customer service incidents with tent campers regarding checkout flexibility and site allocation damaged otherwise stellar reputation.
