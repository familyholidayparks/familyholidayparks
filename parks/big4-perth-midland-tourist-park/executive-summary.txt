PARK: BIG4 Perth Midland Tourist Park
SCORE: 58/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-06-14
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 191

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (8/20) — Limited on-site entertainment with reviews mentioning a games room with air hockey, table tennis, and DVD library, but equipment often damaged or outdated. Pool and spa frequently reported as unheated or freezing cold despite heated pool claims. Activity facilities minimal for families with young children, with one family noting damaged air hockey table and poor table tennis bats.

Nature (6/20) — Urban caravan park setting with limited natural environment. Some cabins overlook bushland or trees providing pleasant views, but overall described as "very much a city caravan park" with minimal nature access. Close to Swan Valley wine region but not immersed in natural surroundings. Blue gum trees mentioned as dropping debris on caravans.

Cleanliness (11/15) — Amenities consistently praised as "spotlessly clean," "very clean and tidy," and "super clean." Camp kitchen, laundry, and bathroom blocks maintained to high standards. However, multiple reviews report dirty rooms with food on dishes, dusty floors, mouldy cabins, and general rundown appearance of permanent resident sites creating untidy park presentation.

Value (10/15) — Mixed feedback with some praising affordability and reasonable pricing ($118/night school holidays noted as good value), while others felt overpriced for condition. Budget accommodation adequate for stopovers. Washing machines expensive at $7 per load plus card fees. Several families felt value didn't match aging facilities and cramped sites.

Site Size (5/10) — Consistently criticized as "very small," "too small for van," and "jammed in like sardines." Multiple reviews report sites so tight visitors couldn't put out awnings or needed multiple-point turns. Concrete pads often unlevel requiring ramps. Space between sites minimal with no privacy. Permanent residents encroaching on visitor sites with trailers and quad bikes.

Sentiment (7/10) — Polarized experiences with loyal repeat visitors praising staff and location, but numerous families reporting disappointing stays and vowing not to return. Many describe feeling unsafe, uncomfortable around permanent residents, and frustrated by poor customer service. Staff generally praised as friendly and helpful, though management received criticism for lack of compassion and communication failures.

Location (11/10) — Strongest scoring category with excellent access to Midland Gate shopping centre (10-15 minute walk), Swan Valley (8 minutes drive), Perth CBD (20-30 minutes), and Perth Airport. Convenient public transport with bus stops nearby and train station walkable. Central to Perth metro activities making it ideal for event attendance and day trips.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Significant presence of permanent residents creates an untidy park appearance with rundown sites, junk, and visitors feeling uncomfortable or stared at, detracting from holiday park atmosphere particularly for families with children.
2. Minimal kids entertainment and recreational facilities with broken equipment, unheated freezing pool despite advertising, and very limited play areas that don't meet family holiday park expectations.
3. Very small, cramped caravan sites that are difficult to access, often unlevel, with permanent residents blocking access and encroaching on visitor spaces, plus major cleanliness issues in some cabins with dirty dishes, floors, and maintenance failures like flooding showers and broken appliances.
