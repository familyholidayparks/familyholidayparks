PARK: Discovery Parks - Margaret River
SCORE: 78/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-15
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 142

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (18/20) — Exceptional family entertainment with daily animal feeding at 3:30pm featuring kangaroos, camels, pigs, cows and ducks that children absolutely loved; huge jumping pillow consistently praised; FREE school holiday activities including crafts, teddy-making, face painting, sausage sizzles, and treasure hunts; pedal carts and go-karts; occasional live music and movie nights; "heaps of things for the kids to do" throughout stays.

Nature (18/20) — Authentic working farm setting with animals roaming freely; beautiful farm backdrop with grassy paddocks and cattle grazing; surrounded by trees providing shade and bush camping feel; views over farmland and dam with birdlife; close proximity to wineries, breweries and Margaret River attractions; peaceful rural atmosphere; "you can really feel the beauty of farm life" with kangaroos and farm animals visible throughout the day.

Cleanliness (11/15) — Mixed reviews with significant decline noted since Discovery takeover; many recent reviews praise "super clean bathrooms" and "spotless" facilities with individual ensuite-style amenities; however, January 2024 period showed multiple complaints of inadequate cleaning, dead mice in kitchen, locked amenities, insufficient amenities for guest numbers, and "messy all the time" during peak periods; generally well-maintained between busy periods but struggles under capacity.

Value (12/15) — Reasonable pricing at $107 for 2 nights powered site for family; "fair price" for comprehensive facilities; free animal feeding and school holiday activities add significant value; some reviewers felt it "not sure it's worth the $$$" due to sandy sites and distance from town; cabins described as "enormous" and well-equipped; value perception varies with site condition (grass vs dust/sand).

Site Size (9/10) — Consistently praised for "sites are large and drive thru" with "very generous sites"; "huge" powered sites providing ample room; families noted "still ample of room left" after setup; "you don't feel like you're ontop of each other"; some variation with a few "sites very small" mentioned; power and water connections sometimes "a few sites over" requiring long leads.

Sentiment (8/10) — Strong multi-generational loyalty with "30 years strong" and "coming for over 20 years" from long-term visitors; many "will be back" and "definitely return" statements; however, significant negative sentiment from recent management changes: "gone downhill fast" since Discovery takeover; "used to be good" sentiments; noise management issues with unsupervised children and late-night parties affecting family atmosphere; booking system frustrations.

Location (2/10) — Major weakness: "20 km out of Margaret River in the middle of nowhere"; "not in Margaret River" disappointed many; 15-17km from Margaret River town center; closer to Cowaramup (5 minutes); "not within walking distance to anywhere"; however well-positioned for wineries and northern Margaret River region attractions; "max drive time 30mins" to area sights; highway noise from 110km/h road noted.

WHY NOT 100:
Location is the primary issue—positioned 17-20km north of Margaret River township, many families felt misled by the "Margaret River" name and found it inconvenient for accessing beaches and town amenities. Management and cleanliness standards have declined significantly since Discovery Parks takeover with multiple recent reviews citing inconsistent policies, inadequate amenities during peak periods, poor maintenance response, and facilities that become overwhelmed with large groups. Site conditions vary dramatically from advertised—many families arrived expecting grassy sites shown in photos but found "total DUST bowl" or sandy sites requiring extensive cleanup, with drainage issues causing flooding during rain and inadequate facilities like missing clotheslines and single kitchen sinks for large guest numbers.
