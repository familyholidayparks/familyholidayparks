PARK: BIG4 Shepparton Park Lane Holiday Park
SCORE: 68/100
DATE SCORED: 2024-01-15
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 79

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (15/20) — Reviews consistently praised the jumping pillow as "a huge hit with the kids," with families mentioning the rec room with wifi and games, tyre maze, and pool. However, several families noted facilities were "a bit run down" compared to other BIG4 parks, pedal cars were advertised but unavailable, and the jumping pillow was sometimes deflated. The games room received mixed feedback as "quite average."

Nature (10/20) — The park scored low on natural environment with reviews describing it as a small park on a main road with minimal natural features. Multiple reviews mentioned the majority of the park is residential with limited areas for kids to explore. Some families used it as a base to visit nearby Goulburn River and noted it's "just out of town," but there's no bush setting, creek access, or significant natural amenities on-site.

Cleanliness (11/15) — Cleanliness scores were highly inconsistent across reviews. Recent positive reviews praised "sparkling clean pool," "spotless" amenities, and "immaculate" facilities. However, multiple family reviews from 2024-2025 reported serious issues: "cabin was very dusty and floors had hair all over," "cat urine smell was very strong," "bugs on chopping board," "beds had fleas," and "pool was filthy." The inconsistency suggests significant variation between accommodation units.

Value (12/15) — Families found the park offered "great value for money" with "cheap powered sites" and reasonable pricing. The well-equipped camp kitchen with free BBQs was frequently praised. However, value was diminished by the poor condition of some cabins relative to cost, with one family stating "for what they charge, the condition of this place is unacceptable" after experiencing Cabin 9's deteriorated state.

Site Size (8/10) — Powered sites received excellent feedback for size, with families noting "huge sites," "site was large enough for caravan, swags and 2 cars," and "spacious sites." Ensuite sites were particularly praised. However, cabin space was limited with reviews mentioning "not a lot of space" in cabins, and the residential nature of much of the park restricted roaming areas for children.

Sentiment (7/10) — Overall sentiment was mixed with passionate advocates and disappointed families. Positive reviews emphasized exceptional staff warmth and return visits ("2nd year we have stayed there"). However, recent negative reviews showed strong dissatisfaction with several families stating they would "NEVER again" stay or wanted to "leave early." The residential park atmosphere divided opinions, with some finding it welcoming and others feeling it prioritized residents over short-term guests.

Location (5/10) — Location reviews were mixed for family purposes. Families appreciated being "close to shops and petrol station" and "easily accessible to shops and restaurants." However, the park is near an airfield with some noise, on a main road, and Shepparton itself "isn't too much to do" for extended family holidays. Most families used it as a convenient stopover rather than a destination, noting it's good "for a few nights" but "you wouldn't want to stay too long."

WHY NOT 100:
1. Severe inconsistency in cabin cleanliness and maintenance, with multiple families reporting uninhabitable conditions including strong pet urine odors, flea infestations, broken fixtures, and dirty facilities in specific cabins (particularly Cabins 6 and 9), indicating poor quality control.
2. Limited on-site entertainment and aging facilities compared to other BIG4 parks, with the jumping pillow sometimes deflated, no pedal cars despite advertising, small pool size, and overall facilities described as "run down" and "mediocre" by families expecting BIG4 standards.
3. Predominantly residential park layout restricts guest movement with areas off-limits to children, limited space for bike riding, and a general feeling among some families that "this is a park for residents, not campers," diminishing the holiday park experience for short-term family visitors.
