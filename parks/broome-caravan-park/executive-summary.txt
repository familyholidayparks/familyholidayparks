PARK: Broome Caravan Park
SCORE: 68/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-26
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 293

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (8/20) — Reviews indicate minimal on-site kids entertainment with occasional food trucks and live music, but no dedicated playground or structured activities. Families appreciated the nearby speedway for occasional events, but most entertainment requires driving into town or to beaches.

Nature (12/20) — The park features lovely mature shady trees throughout and guests can see wallabies roaming the grounds. Located about 10 minutes from Cable Beach and Gantheaume Point, families have good access to Broome's natural attractions, though the park itself sits on a main road without direct beach or bushland access.

Cleanliness (10/15) — Reviews consistently mention that facilities are kept clean with daily maintenance, though multiple guests note amenities are "tired," "dated," and "showing their age." Some reviews mention cockroaches, crickets, and sewerage smells, while others praise spotless amenities, indicating variable experiences across different areas of the large park.

Value (11/15) — Off-season rates around $25-40/night receive praise as "fantastic value" and "cheapest in Broome," but peak season pricing ($60-80/night) draws criticism as expensive for the older facilities provided. Families appreciate the value compared to Cable Beach alternatives, though some felt amenities don't match the price point.

Site Size (8/10) — Reviews frequently mention "spacious," "large," and "huge" sites with good room for big rigs, though experiences vary significantly. Some guests received generous grassy sites with shade, while others were allocated cramped dirt sites close to roads or neighbors, suggesting inconsistent site allocation.

Sentiment (8/10) — Overall sentiment is positive with many return visitors and recommendations, though management changes appear to impact consistency. Recent reviews show mixed experiences with some guests extending stays multiple times while others left early. The pool is the consistent highlight across virtually all reviews.

Location (7/10) — Situated 5-10 minutes drive from Broome town center and beaches, the location receives mixed feedback. Many appreciate being "out of town so security is not the worry" and away from crowds, while others note it's too far to walk anywhere and taxi costs add up. Proximity to main road creates noise issues for some sites.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Amenities are consistently described as old, tired, and dated throughout the park, with bathrooms, camp kitchens, and cabins all requiring modernization to meet current caravan park standards.
2. Pool closes at 5:20-5:30pm daily, which reviewers find frustratingly early during Broome's hot summer months when families most want to cool off in the evening.
3. Minimal on-site entertainment or activities for children, with no playground equipment, kids' club, or structured activities mentioned, requiring families to drive elsewhere for entertainment.
