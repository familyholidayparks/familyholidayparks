PARK: Reflections Shaws Bay - Holiday Park
SCORE: 68/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-08
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 89

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (8/20) — No dedicated facilities mentioned; reviews note absence of pool, playground, or games room. One review mentions "putt-putt" but this wasn't confirmed by other guests. Families rely on natural environment and nearby pub/cafes for activities.

Nature (18/20) — Outstanding natural setting with direct bay frontage, beach access perfect for children, excellent walking and cycling paths, safe swimming areas, and beautiful water views. Multiple reviews praise "stunning location", "magnificent" bay sites, and easy access to beaches and nature trails.

Cleanliness (13/15) — Consistently praised as "clean and spotless", "exceptionally clean", "immaculate facilities". Older amenities but "kept very clean". Minor issues with sensor lights turning off in amenity blocks and some cabins showing wear.

Value (10/15) — Mixed feedback; described as "expensive" by several guests ($70+ per night), with comments like "cost is too high for a park that doesn't have things like a pool". However, others felt it was "worth it" for the location. Premium waterfront sites command higher prices.

Site Size (6/10) — Significant concern with multiple reviews noting "sites are so tiny", "crammed in like sardines", "spaces are a bit too small", difficulties parking vehicles alongside vans, and one guest allocated 7m site for 7.5m van. Drive-through sites praised but overall space is limited.

Sentiment (8/10) — Generally positive with many repeat visitors and bookings, excellent staff reviews ("Carren", "Dee" mentioned by name), but marred by noise complaints from adjacent hotel, rule enforcement issues (unruly guests, e-bikes, barking dogs), and site allocation problems affecting family relaxation.

Location (9/10) — Prime waterfront position on Shaws Bay, walking distance to town, Ballina attractions, beaches, and amenities. Pub, cafe, pizza shop adjacent. Easy access to everything needed. Only drawback is hotel noise on weekends.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Complete absence of on-site entertainment infrastructure (no pool, playground, or kids' activity facilities) means families must rely solely on natural environment and leave the park for structured activities.
2. Site size constraints create significant challenges with cramped spaces, difficult vehicle parking, and guests "almost backing into someone's awning" - unsuitable for larger family rigs.
3. Noise pollution from adjacent Shaw's Bay Hotel with "music until midnight" on weekends and complaints of "continuous loud music" disrupting family relaxation and sleep, plus inconsistent rule enforcement around guest behaviour.
