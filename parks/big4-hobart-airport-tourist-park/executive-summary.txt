PARK: BIG4 Hobart Airport Tourist Park
SCORE: 78/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-15
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 152

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (16/20) — Families consistently praise the "jumping pillow as an absolute highlight," well-maintained playground with swings, basketball court, and picture hunt activities. Multiple reviews describe it as "perfect for kids" with "plenty of activities for the kids in the complex." The camp kitchen provides indoor entertainment space with games and reading corners, though no water park or major structured programs mentioned.

Nature (10/20) — Limited natural environment as the park is in an industrial airport precinct. Reviews note "no shade trees" and "little shelter," with "well kept grass" but minimal bushland character. Seven Mile Beach is nearby (5-10 min drive), and the park serves as a base for accessing Mount Wellington, Richmond, and Port Arthur (20-60 minutes away), but the immediate surrounds lack natural appeal.

Cleanliness (15/15) — Outstanding scores with repeated phrases like "absolutely immaculate," "one of the cleanest caravan parks in the world," "spotless amenities," and "the best camp kitchen." Every review mentioning cleanliness gives top marks, noting "very, very clean" facilities throughout including bathrooms, laundry, and communal areas maintained to exceptional standards.

Value (13/15) — Generally viewed as good value with reviews stating "great value for money," "price was reasonable," and "great prices." Some note it's "not cheap" for what it is given the airport location, and one reviewer felt cabins were cramped for the price paid. Most families find it fair for the quality and convenience provided.

Site Size (9/10) — Powered sites described as "good size," "large," "spacious and level" with concrete slabs and adjacent grass areas. Some mention sites near cabins are "a little tight to get in" and one reviewer noted their motorhome site near entrance was "small" and "poorly maintained," though most report generous space for large vans and caravans.

Sentiment (9/10) — Overwhelmingly positive with families stating "will definitely stay again," "always feels like a second home," and "couldn't ask for more helpful staff." High repeat visitation noted with many families returning multiple times. Manager Emma receives particular praise. A few complaints about aircraft noise, cramped cabin size, and noise management drop the perfect score.

Location (6/10) — Excellent for airport convenience (2-5 min drive, free shuttle) but isolated from Hobart city (15-20 min drive) with poor public transport access. "20 minutes walk to hourly bus which ends at 6 pm" and "35 minutes walk" to groceries without a car. Great highway access for exploring Tasmania but the immediate airport/industrial location lacks appeal and walkability for families.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Airport/industrial location lacks natural environment and bushland character that Australian families typically seek, with persistent aircraft noise mentioned by multiple reviewers despite most saying it's manageable.
2. Limited accessibility without a car, with poor public transport connections and long walks to basic amenities like groceries, making it car-dependent for families.
3. Some cabin accommodation criticized as "WOEFULLY small and cramped" for families, with inadequate storage, limited cooking facilities, and tight spaces that don't suit larger family groups or extended stays.
