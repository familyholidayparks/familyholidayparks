PARK: Yorketown Caravan Park
SCORE: 58/100
DATE SCORED: 2025-12-02
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 30

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (2/20) — No on-site kids entertainment, playground, or activities mentioned across any reviews. This is purely a base camp park with no facilities to keep children occupied on-site.

Nature (16/20) — Strong coastal access with reviews highlighting it's "central to beautiful beaches," "close to a lot of great beaches," and ideal for "exploring the whole lower part of the YP" with "beautiful coast" nearby. Not beachfront but well-positioned for nature exploration.

Cleanliness (12/15) — Mixed results: amenities consistently described as "spotless," "immaculate," and "best showers ever," but significant June 2024 complaint about cabin with "cutlery all dirty with insect droppings," "disgustingly dirty fridge," and "coffee cups with lip marks." Camp kitchen needs upgrading with no hot water.

Value (11/15) — Caravan sites praised as "VERY affordable" at $25/night, but cabins heavily criticized at $180/night as "ridiculous" and "very small" with poor value. Pricing disparity creates mixed value perception for families.

Site Size (7/10) — Described as "small park" with "spacious sites" for caravans but "sites are a little compact for larger vans." Grass sites well-maintained with "rose gardens, plenty of trees."

Sentiment (8/10) — Strong repeat visitation (9th visit mentioned, rebookings), but recent negative reviews (Sept 2025, June 2024) from previously loyal guests show concerning decline. Overwhelmingly positive about hosts Gary, Shane, and Crystal.

Location (2/10) — Not near beaches despite being a base for beach exploration. "Walking distance to shops" in small town but this offers minimal appeal for young families seeking holiday attractions or activities.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Zero kids entertainment infrastructure (no playground, pool, waterpark, or activities) makes this unsuitable for families wanting on-site holiday entertainment for young children.
2. Significant cleanliness inconsistency, particularly in cabins, with a loyal repeat guest noting "total turnaround" from clean to "disgustingly dirty" with insect droppings and unwashed items.
3. Poor cabin value proposition at $180/night for "very small" accommodation with communication issues and dated facilities, particularly problematic for families who can't bring caravans.
