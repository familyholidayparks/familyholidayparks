PARK: Discovery Parks - Rockhampton
SCORE: 78/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-20
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 216

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (18/20) — Reviews consistently praise the waterpark as the star attraction with families saying "water park was a hit", "kids loved the waterpark", and describing it as "like a mini Wet 'N' Wild". Multiple pools including heated options, splash park, basketball courts, tennis courts, and games room provide extensive entertainment. Some reviewers noted the waterpark "could use some love" and equipment showing age, preventing a perfect score.

Nature (12/20) — The park is in an urban/industrial location with reviews noting "en pleine zone industrielle" and being "on the Bruce Highway". Positive mentions include "bird life was amazing" and "quiet enough to hear the birds chirp". However, this is clearly a city park with proximity to shopping centres and service stations rather than natural bushland, beaches, or creek access.

Cleanliness (13/15) — Strong consensus on cleanliness with "spotless amenities", "super clean ensuite", "very clean amenities", and "camp kitchen very clean and tidy" appearing frequently. However, multiple concerning reviews mention "mould in bathroom drain", "black mould all over the ceiling", "mould in shower", and "old and shabby" cabins, indicating inconsistency across accommodation types.

Value (13/15) — Generally positive value perception with "price was very good", "$32 a night powered sites good value", "super affordable", and "reasonable pricing". However, several reviews highlight overpricing: "$380 a night" for run-down cabin described as "extremely poor", "$260 a night" without working air conditioning, and "$51 powered site" difficulties, showing significant price variations.

Site Size (8/10) — Positive feedback includes "spacious site", "good size sites", "generous sized van sites", "huge site", and "tons of room for our 29 foot motorhome". Negatives include "sites very tight for van", "hard to get our van on the two strips of concrete", "slightly crowded sites", and some caravans finding it "very unlevel, on a hill".

Sentiment (9/10) — Strong repeat visitation with "stayed numerous times", "second stay", "will definitely be back", and "favourite place to stop". Staff praised as "wonderful", "friendly", "went above and beyond". However, serious complaints about customer service ("refused to refund", "management never called back"), theft incidents ("2 bikes stolen", "lock up your bikes"), and poor complaint handling lower the perfect sentiment score.

Location (5/10) — Mixed location feedback: praised for convenience with "close to everything", "walking distance from supermarkets", "20 minute walk to Stockland Mall", "Repco and Battery World across the road". However, urban setting means "heavy traffic", "street noise", "adjacent to a school", and "nothing nearby" for those seeking nature. Not a destination location but excellent for city access.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Serious and recurring maintenance issues with mould in multiple cabins and bathrooms, broken air conditioning units, and aging facilities that don't match promotional photos, indicating inconsistent property standards across accommodation types.
2. Urban industrial location on the Bruce Highway lacks natural environment, beach access, or bushland setting that many families seek, making it purely a convenient city stopover rather than a nature-based holiday destination.
3. Security and operational concerns including multiple theft incidents, local day visitors overcrowding facilities that paying guests booked for, inconsistent customer service, and management's poor handling of legitimate complaints about health hazards.
