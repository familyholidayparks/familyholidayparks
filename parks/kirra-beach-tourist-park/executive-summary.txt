PARK: Kirra Beach Tourist Park
SCORE: 73/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-19
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 260

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (12/20) — Limited onsite entertainment with a jumping pillow and small playground mentioned, plus pool table and table tennis in rec room; food trucks on various nights are a highlight; basic facilities compared to resort-style parks.
Nature (16/20) — Excellent proximity to Kirra Beach (10-minute walk), beautiful paperbarks and shady trees throughout park, coastal walking tracks nearby, beach access is a major drawcard though dogs not allowed on main beach.
Cleanliness (13/15) — Generally clean amenities with regular cleaning noted, though some reviews mention dated facilities, occasional maintenance issues, inconsistent mowing, and shower blocks needing refurbishment.
Value (10/15) — Mixed feedback on pricing with many noting peak season rates of $70-80/night feel expensive for aging facilities; some cite good value while others say overpriced compared to alternatives.
Site Size (9/10) — Consistently praised for spacious, large powered sites with room for big rigs and vehicles; good spacing between sites; easy access for caravans.
Sentiment (8/10) — Strong repeat visitation and many 5-star reviews, though sentiment divided on staff friendliness, some security concerns, and frustration with inflexible policies; overall positive but not unanimous.
Location (9/10) — Prime beachside position walking distance to Kirra Beach, cafes, shops; excellent highway access; proximity to airport is double-edged (convenient but noisy from 6am).

WHY NOT 100:
Aircraft noise from Gold Coast Airport significantly impacts guest experience with planes taking off from 6am, mentioned repeatedly as disturbing sleep and ambiance. Aging infrastructure throughout with dated amenities, showers needing upgrades, poor drainage, and maintenance issues noted by long-term repeat visitors. Safety concerns raised including bike thefts, car theft incident, broken glass in play areas, and visible homeless encampment behind park affecting perceived security.
