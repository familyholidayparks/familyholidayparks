PARK: Robe Holiday Park
SCORE: 78/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-24
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 183

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (12/20) — Reviews consistently mention a jumping pillow as the main dedicated kids' facility, with families noting "the jumping pillow is always a favourite with the kids" but emphasizing that "children need to make their own fun here" through bikes, cricket, and exploring natural surroundings like bush tracks to beach and creeks. Multiple reviews state explicitly "if you want pools, spas, waterslides, outdoor cinema, bike track, then don't come here" as this is not a resort-style park. Families appreciated the nearby lake and beach access for water-based activities.

Nature (19/20) — Exceptional natural environment with reviews praising "beautiful bushland setting," "peaceful bush environment amongst gums," and "feels like camping in a national park with all the luxuries." Families loved "wombat visits at night," "surrounded by native bush land," "bush tracks to the beach and creeks," and being "nestled amongst bushland." Multiple reviews mention "beach and lake right at your door step" and "short walk to Long Beach." The natural setting is consistently rated as the park's standout feature.

Cleanliness (14/15) — Overwhelmingly positive feedback with phrases like "immaculately clean," "spotless amenities," "cleanest by far" compared to other Australian parks, and "facilities kept remarkably clean." Individual ensuite-style bathrooms described as "modern," "like hotel level," and "better than our one at home." A few isolated reviews mentioned dirty floors in cabins and one complaint about smelly bathrooms during peak season, preventing a perfect score.

Value (13/15) — Generally positive with reviews noting "great value," "$42 per night for powered sites," and appreciation for the quality of facilities relative to price. Several families mentioned it being "cheaper than other parks" in Robe. However, a couple of reviews felt peak season pricing was high for limited entertainment facilities, and one family complained about "a lot of bucks for a little piece of grass."

Site Size (10/10) — Universally praised with consistent comments about "massive sites," "huge grassy sites," "spacious sites," "big sites that can accommodate the biggest vans easily," "massive room to park a caravan and still have so much room," and "not packed in like sardines." Families with large rigs consistently noted excellent maneuverability and space.

Sentiment (10/10) — Exceptionally high repeat visitation and recommendation rates with phrases like "will definitely be back," "5th visit," "our happy place," "best caravan park," "can't wait to return," and "2027 is already booked." Multiple reviews from experienced travelers stated this was their favorite park in Australia. Very high proportion of 5-star reviews.

Location (10/10) — Excellent location feedback with reviews noting "1.5km from town," "walking distance to town and beaches," "5 minute drive to main street," "great coastal walk into town," and "perfectly located" with beach, lake, and town access. Protected from coastal winds while still being close to all attractions. Consistently praised as ideal positioning.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Limited dedicated kids' entertainment beyond jumping pillow—no pool, waterslides, playground equipment, or organized activities that families with young children often expect at holiday parks
2. Several cleanliness complaints during peak periods including dirty cabin floors, smelly bathrooms, and one serious issue with sewage odor near site 161 that management allegedly ignored
3. One highly concerning review about aggressive staff behavior toward a family with toddler at night, plus complaints about poor customer service response to facility issues and medical emergencies

NOTABLE REVIEW PHRASES:
