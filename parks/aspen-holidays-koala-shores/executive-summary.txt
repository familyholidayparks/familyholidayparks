PARK: Aspen Holidays Koala Shores
SCORE: 58/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-03-27
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 100

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (11/20) — Reviews mention a jumping pillow, putt putt golf, tennis court, basketball, ping pong, and pool as available activities. However, multiple families reported facilities were closed, locked, or "badly worn and cracked," with one noting "the kids zone was all but closed with the jumping pillow locked up." Pool closures without advance notice disappointed several families who specifically chose the park for pool access.

Nature (16/20) — Strong praise for the natural setting with families loving the "waterfront location, boat ramp, wildlife" including kookaburras, rainbow lorikeets, ducks, rabbits, and possums. Reviews highlight "beautiful water views," "stunning spot" along the waterfront, and a "nice bush walk track." The location on the water's edge with inlet access for swimming, kayaking, and fishing is consistently praised as a highlight.

Cleanliness (7/15) — Significant recurring concerns about cleanliness across multiple reviews. Families reported "dust and crumbs everywhere," "floors were dusty, corners had built-up dirt," "never cleaned the windows," and cabins that "doesn't smell fresh." One family with three young children noted cutlery and utensils "still had food on them" and recommended bringing "your own cleaning supplies." Multiple reviews mention the park relies on guests cleaning before departure.

Value (7/15) — Mixed feedback with several families questioning value at $76-$110 per night for ensuite sites with one comparing unfavorably to cheaper alternatives. One family paying $2,680 for five nights felt "extremely disappointed" given the quality. Another paid $1,500 and received "no wifi, no mobile reception, no tv signal." However, some found it reasonable for the waterfront location when facilities worked properly.

Site Size (7/10) — Reviews indicate "sites are large" and "plenty of space" with both concrete slabs and grass matting options. Waterfront sites praised for views and space. However, comments note "sites fairly close together" and some permanent resident areas described as "messy and rundown looking" with "long grass and random things lying around."

Sentiment (6/10) — Highly polarized reviews from loyal repeat visitors giving 5 stars to families who "will never be returning." Recent 2026 reviews show deteriorating sentiment with multiple 1-star reviews from previously loyal customers citing "completely changed how I view this place." Positive reviews often from off-season visitors or those with lower expectations, while peak season family visitors expressed significant disappointment.

Location (9/10) — Consistently praised as "right on the water," "beautiful location," with "close to a number of attractions as well as shops and amenities." Proximity to Tanilba Bay, Nelson Bay area, and having its own boat ramp valued highly. Boardwalk to town and courtesy buses to clubs mentioned positively. Only minor deduction for limited nearby shops without a car.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Chronic cleanliness and maintenance issues across cabins and facilities, with multiple families reporting they had to clean accommodations themselves or found food residue on utensils, dust throughout, and unmaintained bathrooms that significantly detracted from family holidays.
2. Poor operational management including wrong cabin allocations, forcing families to move multiple times during stays, failure to notify guests of pool closures, unresolved safety hazards (frayed wire causing child injuries), and staff describing elderly guests as "winging old moles" on two-way radio.
3. Deteriorating facilities and amenities with outdated cabins showing rotting floorboards, missing appliance parts, broken locks, very thin kids beds described as uncomfortable, limited toilet facilities (only 2 for entire park), and kids entertainment areas locked or in poor repair during peak family periods.
