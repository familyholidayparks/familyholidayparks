PARK: Lakeside Tourist Caravan Park
SCORE: 73/100
DATE SCORED: 2024-01-15
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 37

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (12/20) — Reviews mention a kids playground and live music entertainment during Easter and Christmas holidays with performers like Wayne Cox. However, entertainment is occasional rather than regular, with most families focused on wildlife watching and nature-based activities like platypus viewing at the adjacent arboretum.

Nature (19/20) — Outstanding natural setting with the Don River flowing through the park, platypus families viewable morning and afternoon, pademelons roaming, beautiful bush setting, and direct access to Tasmania Arboretum. Multiple reviews highlight wildlife encounters as a key highlight, with families extending stays specifically for the nature experience.

Cleanliness (15/15) — Consistently praised as "immaculate," "the cleanest of anywhere we have stayed," with facilities described as "very clean," "smell fresh," and "in perfect conditions." Recent reviews from 2025-2026 confirm ongoing high cleanliness standards under current management.

Value (11/15) — Good value with flexible late checkout options for Spirit of Tasmania travellers and spacious sites, though specific pricing rarely mentioned. The park offers modern cabins and quality amenities, but the remote location and limited on-site entertainment may not suit families seeking resort-style value.

Site Size (9/10) — Sites described as "generously sized," "spacious," "plenty of space," and "grassy, spacious and level sites." Multiple reviews specifically praise the site sizes with room for large rigs and camper trailers.

Sentiment (9/10) — Overwhelmingly positive with multiple repeat visitors, one family returning six times in a year. Recent 2025-2026 reviews maintain strong enthusiasm. One guest extended from 2 weeks to over 4 weeks. Called "the best caravan park in Tasmania" and "a model for caravan parks."

Location (8/10) — Convenient 15 minutes to Devonport and Spirit of Tasmania, 5 minutes to IGA supermarket, close to Latrobe and attractions. However, described as "a bit in the middle of nowhere" and "remote" by some, which suits those seeking peace but may not suit families wanting immediate town access.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Limited structured kids entertainment - playground provided but entertainment mainly limited to occasional holiday live music rather than daily activities or programs that would engage young children throughout their stay.
2. Remote rural location approximately 15 minutes from major towns means families must drive for shopping, dining and main attractions rather than having walkable access to amenities.
3. No swimming pool or water play facilities - only river swimming available in summer, which limits water-based entertainment options for young children compared to parks with pools or waterparks.
