PARK: Lorne Foreshore Caravan Park
SCORE: 48/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-15
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 138

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (4/20) — Minimal on-site entertainment for families with children. Reviews mention a "small playground" that was "dirty" with "dog poo right at the base of the slide." Multiple families noted "not much for kids" and "no room to play," with virtually no organised activities or kids' facilities. Not suitable for families seeking active children's entertainment.

Nature (15/20) — Excellent natural setting beside the Erskine River with "lots of birds and ducks," parrots, and wildlife. Direct access to the beach and river, described as "beautiful" and "peaceful." Families praised the riverside sites for nature immersion, though some sections (Top Bank, Ocean Road) lack natural appeal and are industrial/roadside settings. Close to waterfalls and coastal walks.

Cleanliness (7/15) — Highly inconsistent across the park. Many reviews cite "bird poo all over the womens showers," "amenity blocks are dirty and uncared for," "facilities were filthy," and cabins "not being properly cleaned." Some guests found amenities "exceptionally clean" and "cleaned daily," but negative cleanliness reports dominate, especially regarding bird infestations and general maintenance neglect.

Value (5/15) — Overwhelmingly poor value perception. Repeatedly described as "expensive for what you get," "most expensive park on our travels," "overpriced," and "extortionate pricing" ($70-$139/night for basic/cramped sites). Families felt the high cost didn't match the cramped, poorly maintained facilities. "Rip off" appears multiple times. Prices "nearly double on busy periods."

Site Size (3/10) — Major weakness for families. Sites consistently described as "tiny," "cramped," "laughable," "packed like sardines," with "barely enough room for tent and car." Caravans over 20ft struggled to fit. "No room to play" for children. Some sites literally on public roads with cars driving past tents. Poor for families needing space for kids or annexes.

Sentiment (5/10) — Deeply polarised sentiment. Location lovers return repeatedly, but the majority express disappointment and state they "would not stay here again" or "will not be returning." Safety concerns for children and pets, "horrific experience," "worst camping experience of my life." Negative reviews far outweigh positive ones, especially from families.

Location (9/10) — The park's standout strength. "Unbeatable" position in central Lorne with "walking distance to all the shops and restaurants," beach access within minutes, supermarket across the road, and easy access to Great Ocean Road attractions. "Can't get much better location." However, this proximity means noise from traffic and deliveries.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Safety and site quality issues severely impact families—multiple reviews cite dangerous public road access through campsites with speeding cars near children's tents, jack jumper ant infestations, and cramped sites making the park unsuitable and unsafe for young families.
2. Extremely poor value for money with consistently high prices ($70-139/night) for substandard, cramped sites with minimal facilities, no camp kitchen in many sections, and maintenance neglect that doesn't justify premium coastal pricing.
3. Inconsistent and inadequate amenities across different park sections—'Top Bank' and 'Ocean Road' areas are essentially dirt roads with industrial backdrops, bird-infested bathrooms, no kitchen access, and poor lighting, creating a two-tier park where families risk being allocated to severely substandard areas.
