PARK: Riverlander Caravan Park
SCORE: 48/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-02
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 22

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (6/20) — Reviews mention only a pool and BBQ facilities with one reference to "child friendly has all Amminates and a nice pool." No evidence of playground, kids club, waterpark, or organized activities for children. One review notes "events" but provides no detail.

Nature (16/20) — Strong natural setting "Right on the banks of the Murray River" with "lots of bird life," proximity to fishing, boat ramp access, and nearby Turrumbary weir. Described as "unspoilt quiet location" though primarily accessed for peaceful relaxation rather than active nature exploration.

Cleanliness (9/15) — Mixed feedback with recent reviews noting "very clean" and older amenities "clean and well kept for their age," but multiple complaints about "holes in the walls of the showers full of mould," "very dirty" floors, and facilities needing "a lot of work." Infrastructure appears tired.

Value (9/15) — "Reasonable rates" mentioned positively, but one 2024 review calls it "Very Expensive at $45 a night" given the limited facilities. Mixed perception of value with infrastructure issues impacting perceived worth for families.

Site Size (6/10) — Described as "small and bespoke" park with limited specific commentary on site sizes. No detailed feedback on caravan site spaciousness or suitability for large rigs, suggesting average to modest site dimensions.

Sentiment (6/10) — Polarized reviews with multiple 5-star ratings from repeat visitors seeking peace, but several 1-star reviews citing booking issues, cleanliness problems, and warnings it's "a permanents park not a caravan park." Not recommended by several reviewers for families.

Location (8/10) — "Only 5 minutes from Echuca" with good accessibility and "good distance from Highway makes it child friendly." Near Murray River attractions and walking/cycling tracks, though "out of town" for those wanting walkable amenities.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Severe infrastructure deficiencies for families including no potable water points, no grey/blackwater dumps, and aging amenities with mould issues that compromise the family holiday experience.
2. Virtually no kids entertainment or activities beyond a basic pool - no playground, kids club, waterpark, or organized entertainment that Australian families with young children expect.
3. Operates primarily as a permanent residents park with complaints about barking dogs, late-night parties, and lack of rules enforcement creating an unsuitable environment for family holidays.
