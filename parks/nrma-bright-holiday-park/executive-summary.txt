PARK: NRMA Bright Holiday Park
SCORE: 68/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-24
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 120

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (12/20) — Reviews mention pool and spa, playground, mini golf, and the Ovens River as the primary entertainment. Families praised "heaps for the kids to do" and river activities like swimming and tubing, though no organized kids' club or structured programs were mentioned. The natural river environment provides much of the entertainment value.

Nature (18/20) — Exceptional natural setting consistently praised across reviews. Families loved the "magical forest" atmosphere, "beautiful river running thru the caravan park," "majestic tree canopy," and "ducks and birds." The creek running through creates a "peaceful, scenic" environment. Multiple long-term visitors (15-40 years) cite the natural beauty as the primary reason for returning.

Cleanliness (9/15) — Significant inconsistency evident in reviews. While some praised "ultra clean amenities" and "very clean," many recent reviews cited serious issues: "toilets were filthy," "faeces smeared on toilet lid for days," "most toilets missing flush buttons," "mould on tiles," and amenities "not cleaned for whole Easter week." Maintenance appears reactive rather than proactive.

Value (10/15) — Mixed feedback on pricing. Some found it "reasonably priced" and "perfect value for money," but multiple families felt it was "too expensive compared to service offered" and "extremely overpriced." Specific complaints included "$62 for dirt site" and "$1600 for 2 nights" with poor amenities. Value perception closely tied to site and amenity condition received.

Site Size (6/10) — Consistent criticism about site sizing. Multiple reviews noted "sites too small for size of vans," "tiny sites with nowhere to park car," "very tight spaces for reversing," and difficulties with "8m van" fitting allocated sites. Riverside sites described as "smallest." Many sites on steep slopes making leveling difficult. Positive notes for ensuite sites being more spacious.

Sentiment (7/10) — Divided sentiment with loyal returners balanced by frustrated recent visitors. Strong positive from long-term guests ("20 years," "40 years," "15 years") but concerning trend of recent reviews showing decline. Many families vowed to return, but others stated "will be a different park" next time and "never come here." Staff received mixed reviews from "fantastic" to "unhelpful."

Location (9/10) — Universally praised as exceptional. Reviews consistently highlighted "2 minute walk into town," "few minutes walk to town centre," "walking distance to shops and cafes," and proximity to cycling trails and attractions. The central Bright location with easy access to amenities while maintaining natural setting was a standout feature.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Chronic maintenance neglect particularly with laundry facilities (multiple reviews across years citing "5 of 6 washing machines broken," "only 1 working") and toilet amenities (missing flush buttons, broken fixtures, inadequate cleaning during peak periods) significantly impacts family convenience and hygiene standards.

2. Site allocation system appears poorly managed with consistent complaints about sites unsuitable for van sizes provided at booking, steep unlevel sites requiring extensive chocking, and families having to inspect multiple sites before finding suitable options, wasting holiday time.

3. Cleanliness standards have declined noticeably in recent years according to long-term visitors, with specific hygiene concerns (faeces left for days, overflowing bins, mouldy showers) and infrequent cleaning schedules (once per 24 hours during peak) falling well below family holiday park expectations.

NOTABLE REVIEW PHRASES:
