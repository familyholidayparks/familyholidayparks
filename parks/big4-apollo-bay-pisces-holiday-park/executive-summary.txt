PARK: BIG4 Apollo Bay Pisces Holiday Park
SCORE: 93/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-23
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 428

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (19/20) — Reviews consistently highlight the spectacular nightly light show as "next level" and "not expected from a caravan park." The park offers undercover jumping pillow, heated pool, water play area, pump track, playground with flying fox, games room, fire pit with complimentary marshmallows, scavenger hunts, kids club during school holidays, face painting, live music, reptile shows, and daily coffee van. Families report children being "entertained for days" and the park having "everything you could possibly need to entertain the kids."

Nature (18/20) — The park delivers "million dollar ocean views from every site" with its elevated tiered design. Located directly opposite the beach with the sound of waves audible from sites, guests report watching dolphins, whales, and koalas on property. The setting is described as "nestled between scrub and sea" with "views of the ocean on one side and the Otways on the other." Easy access to Great Ocean Road attractions, Otway rainforest walks, and beach walks along the foreshore.

Cleanliness (15/15) — Reviews are unanimous: "spotlessly clean," "immaculate," "pristine in every way," and "the cleanest Big4 we have ever stayed at." Amenities are described as "impeccable" with heated bathrooms, "lovely strong hot showers," and "constantly cleaned." Staff maintain the park to resort standards with daily cleaning, manicured lawns, and attention to every detail including regular restocking of playground equipment and rebuilding the communal Jenga set.

Value (12/15) — Pricing is premium at $80-$500+ per night depending on season and accommodation type, with several reviews noting it as "expensive" or "the most expensive we have ever paid." However, most guests conclude "you get what you pay for" and "worth every cent" given the facilities and location. Some international visitors and off-season guests questioned the value, though families with children overwhelmingly felt the experience justified the cost.

Site Size (9/10) — Caravan sites are consistently described as "huge," "spacious," "large level sites," and "very large and very neatly set out." Sites feature synthetic grass or combination synthetic/natural grass, with excellent drainage, easy access, and room for van, annex, and car. A few reviews mentioned some sites being "difficult to get into" but praised staff assistance with parking. Cabins are well-appointed though some noted them as "compact" for larger families.

Sentiment (10/10) — Exceptional repeat visitation and recommendation rate with phrases like "will definitely be back," "already booked again next year," "can't wait to return," and "we will be back for sure" appearing throughout reviews. Many describe it as their "favorite Big4" and "best caravan park" they've stayed at. Staff are described as "angels," "amazing," and genuinely caring. Even negative experiences were typically resolved quickly and professionally.

Location (10/10) — Perfect positioning at the entrance to Apollo Bay with beach access directly across the road, 1-2km walk to town center, and easy access to Great Ocean Road attractions. Reviews note "perfect central location," "ideal location," and "supreme location." The elevated hillside position provides views while being close enough to walk or short drive to shops, restaurants, breweries, and the famous scallop pie bakery.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Premium pricing (especially peak season/public holidays at $200-$500/night) makes it cost-prohibitive for some families, with several reviews citing it as significantly more expensive than comparable parks and one stating "this Greed is very disgusting" for $229 one-night powered site fee.

2. Some site allocation issues with guests not receiving their pre-booked specific sites (one motorhome owner booked 12 months in advance but was moved, with staff allegedly lying about the reason), and a few cabin booking disputes where guests claim they received older cabins than advertised on the website.

3. Minor capacity constraints during peak times including small pool that's "always full," limited recycling bins near playground, some noise from construction/maintenance work, and distance from town (1.5-2km walk) may not suit all families, particularly those with very young children or mobility limitations.
