PARK: Blue Lake Holiday Park
SCORE: 86/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-21
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 306

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (18/20) — Reviews consistently praise the "indoor heated massive concrete swimming pool with 2 slides" as outstanding, with families spending hours there across all seasons. The park offers jumping pillow, new modular pump track, basketball court, pickleball courts, playground, and organized activities including wildlife shows, face painting, hair braiding, and kids' bingo during holidays. One family noted "there was always something happening for them" and activities covered "both kids and adults."

Nature (18/20) — Located "directly opposite Blue Lake" with "30 second walk to the blue lake crater areas," featuring a "5km path loop around the crater edge with stunning views and gorgeous sunrises." Reviews mention "kookaburras laughing in the trees," beautiful landscaped gardens with palm trees, and access to Valley Lake and nature reserves. The natural volcanic lake setting provides "serene and picturesque backdrop" with excellent walking tracks.

Cleanliness (14/15) — Overwhelmingly positive feedback: "immaculately maintained," "very well maintained facilities," "amenities very clean," and "this is one of the best maintained parks I have seen." Multiple reviews note "spotless amenities," "clean facilities," and grounds "always looking absolutely amazing." Minor points lost for isolated incidents including one review about cabin bed bugs and another about toilet flooding during peak period.

Value (11/15) — Reviews acknowledge premium pricing with comments like "expensive, but worth it when you use all the park facilities" and "a bit pricey" but "great value for your money" when facilities are utilized. Several families note it "rivals many other more expensive big 4 parks" and is "affordable" relative to what's offered. Criticism focused on pricing for basic cabins ($250-300/night) that felt "overpriced" and minimum stay requirements during holidays.

Site Size (9/10) — Consistently praised as "nice big sites," "spacious," "large enough for a few swags and a rooftop tent," and "big grassy sites." Ensuite sites described as "so spacious" and "very spacious site for our large van." Reviews note "plenty of space between sites" and sites are "well spread out" so "you didn't feel crammed on top of your neighbours." Minor points lost for some unpowered tent sites on slopes and one comment about sites being "too close."

Sentiment (9/10) — Exceptional repeat visitation and recommendations: "perfect stay as always, which is why we keep coming back," "our sixth stay at the park," "we will definitely be back," "already booked for next year," and "one of the best parks we have stayed in." Strong recommendation rate with phrases like "highly recommend," "would definitely recommend," and "will definitely recommend this place to everyone." Points lost for several negative experiences regarding customer service, booking errors, and theft incident.

Location (7/10) — Strong proximity to Blue Lake (across the road) but mixed accessibility feedback. "3 min drive from town centre," "5 min drive to town proper," and "short drive or walk into town." Excellent for natural attractions but some reviews note "it is a drive into town" and road noise mentioned. Perfect for exploring Mount Gambier region with caves, sinkholes, and coastal areas within easy reach.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Customer service inconsistencies during peak periods with multiple reviews citing "rude and unaccommodating" reception staff, booking errors not properly resolved, and one detailed complaint about discriminatory treatment and unprofessional management response that resulted in booking cancellation.
2. Safety and security concerns with a detailed theft incident (mountain bikes stolen) revealing no CCTV coverage except main gate, park backing onto public golf course allowing unrestricted access, and police confirming regular occurrence over 3 months with inadequate guest warnings.
3. Variable accommodation quality with older basic cabins described as "very dated," "cramped, cold," "outdated," smelling "bad," and one family leaving early from $250/night cabin, while basic cabins lack Smart TVs and some had maintenance issues (ants, bugs, insufficient heating, shower temperature problems) creating inconsistent guest experiences across accommodation types.
