PARK: Townsville Lakes Holiday Park
SCORE: 52/100
DATE SCORED: 2024-01-15
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 84

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (8/20) — Reviews mention a swimming pool with waterslide that is sometimes clean but other times described as dirty with monopolised spaces. Camp kitchen with TV and BBQ facilities available, but very limited on-site kids entertainment or activities. No playground or kids club mentioned by families.

Nature (10/20) — Park located beside an artificial lake with fenced perimeter and barbed wire preventing access. Reviews note "the lakes add no value to the stay" and "can't walk around the lake." Some shaded sites with trees mentioned, but overall described as a "concrete jungle" with little natural environment or bushland access.

Cleanliness (8/15) — Highly mixed reviews with recent feedback showing significant concerns. Multiple families report "ants all around outdoor shower," "marks on bedding where kids sleep," "old cigarette butts in ashtray," "hair in bathroom sink," and "half-eaten sandwich under bed." Ensuites described as clean by some but "old and dirty" by others. Rubbish overflowing from bins noted.

Value (6/15) — Strong negative sentiment on value with families stating "quite pricey for what's offered," "highly overpriced," and "highway robbery at $60 a night." Multiple reviews compare unfavourably to other parks offering more facilities for same price. Several families mention $50-$169 rates depending on accommodation type, with consensus that pricing doesn't match condition.

Site Size (7/10) — Positive feedback on "large sites" and "great double slab provided for van and annex." Sites described as having "plenty of space for caravans" with sealed concrete pads. However, tight roads and difficult access noted, with parking alongside roads making navigation challenging for larger rigs.

Sentiment (5/10) — Extremely polarized reviews with several families "left after 20 mins" or stating "would not stay there again." Multiple 1-star reviews from families with young children citing poor conditions. However, some repeat visitors and long-term guests praising staff helpfulness. Overall trend shows declining satisfaction in recent reviews.

Location (8/10) — Consistently praised for convenience with "walking distance to food shops," "close to Castletown shopping centre," "McDonald's, Pizza Hut, Subway just outside." Easy access to Townsville attractions including The Strand and Magnetic Island ferry. However, proximity to highway and emergency services creates significant noise issues.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Severe infrastructure deterioration with "potholes everywhere," "roads more potholes than road," broken concrete edging creating tripping hazards, and ongoing maintenance issues from cyclone damage that remain unrepaired months later, making the park unsuitable for families with young children navigating the grounds.

2. Critical cleanliness failures specifically affecting families with children including dirty bedding, ant infestations in sleeping areas and bathrooms, leftover food under beds, and inconsistent cleaning standards that multiple families found unacceptable enough to demand refunds or leave immediately upon arrival.

3. Virtually no on-site entertainment or nature-based activities for children, with an inaccessible fenced-off lake, no playground equipment mentioned, limited pool appeal due to cleanliness concerns, and heavy reliance on off-site fast food outlets rather than family-friendly park amenities or natural environment engagement.
