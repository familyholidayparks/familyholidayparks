PARK: Tasman Holiday Parks - Airlie Beach
SCORE: 72/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-24
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 255

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (15/20) — Reviews consistently highlight the popular daily bird feeding at 4pm with rainbow lorikeets as a major drawcard for families. Regular food trucks (Thai, pizza, Korean) visit nightly and there's a morning coffee van. Kids enjoy the playground, jumping pillow/bouncy castle, and pool area. However, some felt entertainment options were limited compared to larger parks.

Nature (14/20) — The park features beautiful tropical gardens with "lush" landscaping and abundant birdlife including cockatoos, lorikeets, and bush stone curlews. Multiple reviews mention the "tropical atmosphere" and "leafy privacy screens." Location provides access to Whitsundays reef and island tours. However, the park is near a stagnant creek causing mosquito/midge issues, and positioned close to the main road rather than beachfront.

Cleanliness (10/15) — Recent reviews show significant decline from earlier high standards. Multiple 2025-2026 reports cite "ant infestation," "cockroaches in dishwasher," cabins that were "old and dirty," and facilities needing "deep clean." Earlier reviews praised "spotless" amenities and "always clean" bathrooms. Cleaning frequency appears inconsistent with some noting "bathrooms cleaned multiple times daily" while others found them "not clean." Pest control is a recurring concern in tropical climate.

Value (11/15) — Mixed feedback on value with prices ranging from affordable ($35 unpowered sites) to expensive ($270-300/night cabins). Several reviews mention "very reasonable price," "good value for money," and "affordable." However, recent guests felt cabins were overpriced for condition received, with one stating they'd expect to pay "$180 tops" not $300. Dynamic pricing system caused confusion with guests paying "almost double" neighboring sites. Glamping tents received positive value comments.

Site Size (7/10) — Reviews indicate "spacious sites," "roomy sites," and "big spacious sites" for some areas, particularly ensuite and premium sites. However, multiple reviews note "sites are very small," "tiny site," "not much room," and people parking on neighboring sites. Some unpowered sites described as cramped. Powered sites with ensuites received better ratings for space. Inconsistency across different site types.

Sentiment (8/10) — Overall positive sentiment with many "highly recommend" and "will be back" comments. Staff (especially Sarah, Rachel, Kaz) frequently praised as "lovely," "helpful," "amazing." However, sentiment dropped significantly in recent reviews due to cleanliness issues, pest problems, and maintenance failures. Some guests left early or stated they "will not stay there again." Repeat visitors noted the park "used to be immaculate" but standards have fallen. Family-friendly atmosphere appreciated.

Location (7/10) — Located about 2km from Airlie Beach center, requiring 20-25 minute walk or 5-minute/50-cent bus ride. Reviews note "great location," "convenient," and proximity to Jubilee Tavern across the road. Good base for Whitsundays tours with pickup service. However, some felt it was "too far from city," "not close to downtown," and "beside the road" causing noise. Not beachfront location. Bus access helps but still requires transport.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Cleanliness standards have significantly declined with multiple recent reports (2024-2026) of pest infestations including ants, cockroaches, and flies, plus inadequate cleaning between guests with one guest finding an unflushed toilet and having to clean the glamping tent themselves.

2. Chronic maintenance issues including all 8 washing machines out of order for extended periods (reported by multiple guests over months), broken showers with only lukewarm water, corroded shower heads, non-functioning dryers, and facilities with "out of order" signs throughout the park.

3. Inconsistent site allocation and quality with guests receiving sites much smaller than advertised, sites next to laundry facilities instead of quiet areas, muddy/wet sites without proper drainage, and significant price discrepancies using "dynamic pricing" where guests paid double compared to neighbors for similar sites.
