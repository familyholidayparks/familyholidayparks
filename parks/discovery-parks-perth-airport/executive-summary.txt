PARK: Discovery Parks - Perth Airport
SCORE: 58/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-24
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 133

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (12/20) — Reviews frequently mention pool, playground, kids games room, and theatre room. Families appreciated Halloween activities including "haunted villa for the kids," activity packs for travelling children, and "free icy pole and kit kat for kids." However, entertainment offerings are basic compared to dedicated holiday parks.

Nature (8/20) — Described as having "lovely trees and greenery" with "leafy sites" and located near a waterway. Guests noted "lots of birds in the trees" but this is primarily an urban park near the airport, not a nature-based destination. Limited natural environment beyond landscaping.

Cleanliness (13/15) — Consistently praised with "spotless" amenities, "immaculate" cabins, and "best amenities blocks in WAs van parks." Multiple reviews highlight "very clean," "modern and clean" facilities. Some concerns about mould in bathrooms and occasional maintenance lapses with "grounds very dirty and lots of sand."

Value (9/15) — Mixed feedback with "good value for money" and "competitive price" alongside complaints of "EXPENSIVE" at "$260 per night" and "Pritty average for the price." Families felt pricing was reasonable for short stays but questioned value for extended periods.

Site Size (8/10) — Reviews mention "great size for van, annex & car," "spacious sites," and "very generous size" drive-through sites. Some guests struggled with tighter sites noting difficulty getting caravans into certain spots, requiring site changes.

Sentiment (4/10) — Severely impacted by multiple theft incidents. Reviews detail "all my valuables stolen," "LOTS OF CRIMES," "stuff stolen of the front porch," and "robbery seems far too common." Management response criticized as showing "zero responsibility." Despite positive staff interactions, security concerns dominate recent family reviews.

Location (4/10) — Convenient for airport access ("stone throw away") and shopping (DFO, Costco, Woolworths nearby). However, urban location lacks holiday appeal, with airport proximity not ideal for family holidays. "Close to city" but not near beaches or natural attractions families seek.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Rampant security issues with multiple documented thefts from vehicles and sites throughout the park, with management showing poor accountability and no meaningful security measures despite repeated incidents.
2. Urban airport location lacks the natural environment, beach access, and holiday atmosphere that families with young children prioritize for memorable holiday experiences.
3. Limited on-site entertainment beyond basic pool and games room—no waterpark, organized activities, or dedicated kids club that defines top-tier family holiday parks.
