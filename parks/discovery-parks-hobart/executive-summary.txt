PARK: Discovery Parks - Hobart
SCORE: 58/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-21
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 121

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (8/20) — Reviews reveal inconsistent kids facilities with multiple mentions of "kids area needing work", "jumping pillow out of action", and "kids facilities are very over grown". One family noted they paid $700 for 3 nights and were "very let down". While some reviews mention a "playground area was fantastic" and "huge playground for the kids", the more recent evidence (2024-2025) suggests deterioration. One review specifically requested "it would be even better if there were a playground", indicating absent or inadequate facilities. The park offers BBQ areas and a fire pit, but lacks structured entertainment programming.

Nature (10/20) — The park is situated in a suburban/highway location with limited natural environment appeal. Multiple reviews mention views of the prison across the road and proximity to the "busiest freeway". There's no mention of beach access, bushland, creeks, or natural play areas. The grounds are described as "well maintained" with "beautiful gardens" but this reflects landscaping rather than natural environment. Location is practical for accessing Hobart attractions but offers no inherent nature-based experiences for families.

Cleanliness (10/15) — Reviews consistently praise cleanliness with phrases like "really clean", "very clean", "exceptionally clean", and "cabins are clean and comfortable". However, several concerning reports emerge: one cabin had "pee all over the toilet floor", another smelled "heavily of mold", one family found "chicken bones and dirty cutlery", and another noted "cockroaches egg" on bedding. One reviewer stated "this place is an absolute dive" with the "worst" cabin. A builder noted poor waterproofing leading to flooding. While many stays were clean, the severe lapses prevent a higher score.

Value (12/15) — Strong value proposition with "budget friendly option", "great value for money", "affordable price", and "well appointed at a budget price" appearing frequently. Families appreciate the "incredible value" and self-contained cabins with full kitchens, washers/dryers, and even dishwashers in deluxe units. However, some felt pricing didn't match quality: "great but pricey for cabin", one family paid "almost $700 for 3 nights" with broken appliances, and economy cabins felt cramped for the price. The $188.50 cancellation fee for 5-days notice was deemed "totally unreasonable".

Site Size (5/10) — Consistent complaints about cramped sites: "sites were very small, no room to open our awning", "barely enough room to swing a cat", "parking spaces are barely big enough for a small car", and "ensuite sites are very small". Powered sites particularly criticized with "no space to put an awning out" and awkward layouts. Caravan sites described as generous by some but the ensuite powered sites (except #4) were problematic. Cabins noted as "smallish but adequate" with "tiny" bathrooms.

Sentiment (6/10) — Mixed sentiment with passionate advocates and detractors. Positive: "will definitely stay next time", "would give 10 starts if I could", "didn't want to leave", "100% staying here every single time we come back". Negative: "run as fast as you can", "never stay at another discovery park again", "wouldn't recommend staying here at all", "never ever going back", "absolute dive". Recent reviews (2025-2026) show service improvements but facilities concerns remain. Many families return despite limitations, suggesting acceptable baseline experience.

Location (7/10) — Excellent proximity to Hobart CBD (7-15 minutes), airport (10 minutes), and serves well as a base for exploring southern Tasmania including Port Arthur, Richmond, and Royal Botanical Gardens. Convenient highway access and close to shopping facilities. However, the freeway location creates persistent noise issues with "traffic noise doesn't stop till 100am and starts back at 5.00 am" and "constant alarms" from nearby emergency services. The prison view also detracts from the setting.

WHY NOT 100:
1. **Deteriorating Kids Entertainment Infrastructure** — Multiple recent reviews (2024-2025) document overgrown kids areas, non-operational jumping pillow, and facilities needing significant work, with one family explicitly stating the park "would be even better if there were a playground", indicating inadequate provision for the core family demographic.

2. **Severe Highway Noise Pollution** — Persistent complaints about traffic noise from the adjacent "busiest freeway" creating sleep disturbance ("traffic noise doesn't stop till 100am and starts back at 5.00 am", "most diabolical nights sleep"), with noise audible despite being described as "beside" rather than buffered from the highway.

3. **Cramped Site Sizes Incompatible with Family Needs** — Consistent evidence that caravan sites are "very small" with "no room to open our awning and sit outside", economy cabins have "barely enough room to swing a cat", and families with larger rigs or multiple children find spaces inadequate, with one family's motorhome repeatedly "bumped the side of our cabin".
