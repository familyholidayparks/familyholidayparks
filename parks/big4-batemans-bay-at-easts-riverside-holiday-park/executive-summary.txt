PARK: BIG4 Batemans Bay at Easts Riverside Holiday Park
SCORE: 72/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-24
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 101

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (12/20) — Limited on-site facilities with a "small pool", "tiny freezing pool" mentioned negatively, basic playground that "definitely needs an upgrade", mini golf course that was "a hit with the kids", giant chess set with "broken pieces", and games room with table tennis. One concerning review mentioned finding "an old man slumped over with a needle" in a play tunnel. Not much structured entertainment but natural play opportunities by the river.

Nature (18/20) — Outstanding waterfront location "right on the water", "metres from the water", positioned on Clyde River with "beautiful views over the Clyde River, Bay and islands". Direct beach/river access for swimming, fishing ("catch them of the shore flathead, bream, trevally, whiting"), dolphin watching from cabins, sunrise viewing, and walks along the shore. "Great location for paddling", mangrove walks nearby, and "untouched" natural areas. Consistently praised natural environment.

Cleanliness (14/15) — Overwhelmingly positive: "spotlessly clean", "very clean park", "absolutely spotless", "cleanest I've ever seen", "amenities were always clean", "camp kitchen was spotless", "very clean and in good condition". A few older reviews mentioned "tired and run down amenities" and one bad cabin experience with mould and leaking fridge, but recent reviews consistently praise cleanliness standards.

Value (10/15) — Mixed feedback with pricing concerns: "most expensive caravan park we stayed at ~$70/night", "nearly $300 per night", "almost $140 per site" in peak with complaints about limited facilities for the price. However, some note "great value for money", "fair pricing", and competitive rates. General consensus is clean and functional but overpriced compared to facilities offered.

Site Size (7/10) — Generally adequate but tight: "spacious" sites mentioned by some, "level" concrete/gravel sites, but multiple reviews warn "extremely tight to get around with a 20ft van", "bit tight to park larger caravans", "internal roads are a bit narrow especially if you have a bigger caravan". Sites described as "big enough for a caravan and car to park but feels so close to other campers". Staff assist with parking which helps.

Sentiment (9/10) — Very positive overall with strong repeat visitation: "3rd time we've stayed", "will now be regulars", "can't wait to return", "will definitely be back", "would happily recommend it to friends and family". Consistent praise for staff helpfulness, peaceful atmosphere, and family-friendly environment. A few negative experiences with management attitude and site allocation, but majority highly satisfied.

Location (10/10) — Excellent positioning: "short walk over the bridge" to Batemans Bay town centre (10 minutes), "close to cafes, restaurants and supermarkets", "walking distance to the centre of town", great cycle paths, proximity to beaches (10 minutes drive to patrolled beach). Waterfront position praised universally while maintaining convenient access to all amenities.

WHY NOT 100:
Limited entertainment infrastructure with a notably small, unheated pool and basic playground facilities that reviewers consistently described as needing upgrades, making it unsuitable for families prioritizing resort-style activities. Value perception is significantly impacted by peak pricing ($140-$300/night) that multiple reviewers felt didn't match the modest facilities offered compared to other holiday parks. Site access and internal roads are challenging for larger caravans (20ft+) with tight turns and narrow roadways, limiting appeal for families with bigger rigs.
