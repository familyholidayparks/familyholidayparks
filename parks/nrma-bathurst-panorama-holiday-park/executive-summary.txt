PARK: NRMA Bathurst Panorama Holiday Park
SCORE: 62/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-17
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 164

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (13/20) — Reviews consistently praise the mini golf as a highlight, with families calling it "the best thing about the park" and "very cool." The pool with water slide receives positive mentions for families, though some noted it was occasionally out of service. However, the games room is a major disappointment with multiple families reporting "broken and filthy" equipment, ripped pool table, and broken arcade games. The advertised jumping pillow has been removed for over 6 years despite still appearing in marketing materials. No organized activities or daily programs mentioned.

Nature (10/20) — The park is described as "picturesque" with hedges providing privacy between sites and "lots of birds around." Located on the outskirts of Bathurst near industrial shops, it offers "lovely sunsets" and some "great views of the countryside" from certain areas. However, this is primarily a caravan park setting rather than a natural environment, with most sites on gravel or concrete rather than grass. Proximity to Mount Panorama and Blue Mountains for day trips mentioned positively. Not a nature-focused destination.

Cleanliness (9/15) — The renovated amenities block receives strong praise as "modern, fresh and clean," "top notch," and "best toilet block in four thousand kilometres." However, multiple recent reviews note inconsistent cleaning with "wasn't cleaned once during our stay," "floors not washed," "toilets had faeces on doors and walls," and "no soap in dispensers." Cabins vary widely - some "spotlessly clean" while others show "mould on ceiling," "ripped fabric," and maintenance issues. The playground and sandpit described as "really dirty."

Value (10/15) — Pricing receives mixed reviews. Some found it "very reasonable" and "cheaper than showgrounds" at approximately $36-37 per night with NRMA discount. Others felt it "overpriced" especially during events when unpowered sites reached $61/night. Several long-term NRMA members expressed disappointment given the park's condition. Laundry at $4 per wash considered good value. Overall, families felt the facilities didn't match expectations for the price paid.

Site Size (5/10) — This is a consistent pain point. Sites repeatedly described as "very tight," "poky," "tiny," "hard to back trailer into," and requiring unhitching even for one-night stays. Narrow internal roads make maneuvering difficult, especially when neighboring vans are present. Ensuite sites particularly cramped with "no room for your car." Some sites have "huge muddy holes" or uneven ground. A few reviewers found adequate space, but the majority of families with caravans struggled.

Sentiment (7/10) — Sentiment is polarized. Positive reviews praise friendly staff, good location for Bathurst exploration, and clean modern amenities. Many families enjoyed their stay and said they'd return. However, significant negative sentiment around broken/outdated facilities, misleading advertising (jumping pillow, board games, broken pool table), inconsistent cleaning, and tight sites. Multiple reviews from disappointed long-term NRMA members saying "never coming here again" and "worst we've ever stayed in." Staff receive consistent praise even when facilities don't.

Location (8/10) — Located on the western outskirts of Bathurst, approximately 9 minutes drive from town center. Easy highway access with minimal road noise for most sites (though some report truck noise). Close to Mount Panorama race track and retail shops like Bunnings and Anaconda. Not walkable to restaurants or cafes. Good base for exploring Bathurst region, Hill End, Mudgee, and Blue Mountains. Convenient for travelers but not a destination location itself.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Entertainment facilities are outdated and broken - the games room has ripped pool tables, broken arcade games, and is described as "filthy," while the advertised jumping pillow hasn't existed for over 6 years, creating significant disappointment for families who chose the park based on these amenities.

2. Caravan sites are consistently too tight for modern rigs - multiple families report extreme difficulty maneuvering into sites, having to unhitch for one-night stays, driving over neighboring sites, and cramped spaces with no room for vehicles, making the caravan experience stressful rather than relaxing.

3. Cleanliness is inconsistent despite modern facilities - while the amenities block is renovated and modern, recent reviews show it's often not cleaned during multi-day stays, with toilets unflushed, faeces on walls, no soap, and floors unwashed, plus some cabins showing mould, broken fixtures, and poor maintenance.
