PARK: NRMA South West Rocks Holiday Park
SCORE: 82/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-23
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 182

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (19/20) — Reviews consistently praise the "sensational" and "amazing" pirate-themed waterpark as the standout feature, with families describing it as worth traveling for alone. The park offers extensive activities including mini golf, jumping pillow, pedal carts, tennis court, games room with arcade games, Bear Grylls adventure course, and daily kids club activities with movie nights and discos. Multiple reviews state "so much for the kids to do" and "best park for kids I've been to yet."

Nature (17/20) — The park is set in a beautiful bushland location with kangaroos freely roaming the grounds, described as "peaceful" and "secluded" with "lots of wildlife." Families enjoy seeing goannas, ducks, and bird life throughout their stay. However, the park is not beachfront and requires a 5-minute drive to beaches and town, with some reviews noting it's "too far to walk" to beach or town.

Cleanliness (13/15) — Amenities are consistently described as "immaculate," "spotlessly clean," and "pristine" with staff constantly maintaining the grounds. Camp kitchens and bathrooms receive particular praise, though some reviews mention certain older cabins had cleanliness issues with floors not properly mopped and some dated facilities needing deep cleaning in specific areas.

Value (11/15) — Reviews are mixed on value, with many saying "great value" and praising the extensive facilities, while others describe it as "expensive" and "overpriced" with pricing like $470-580 per night for some accommodations and $500+ for unpowered sites over holidays. The consensus is that while expensive, you get extensive facilities and activities that justify costs for families prioritizing entertainment.

Site Size (7/10) — Multiple reviews mention "sites were super tight," "on top of each other," and "tiniest sites we have ever seen." Powered sites are described as having artificial turf with concrete slabs which families appreciate, but spacing is noted as compact. Some unpowered areas are described as poorly developed. Larger sites are available but the overall consensus is that site sizing is a compromise.

Sentiment (9/10) — Extremely positive family sentiment with numerous reviews stating "will definitely be back," "already planning our next trip," and families returning annually. Many describe it as "the best park we've stayed at" and "one of my favorite NRMA parks." Some negative experiences with specific staff members and a few security/noise incidents prevent a perfect score.

Location (6/10) — The park is well-located near Trial Bay Gaol, lighthouse, and beaches, described as central to attractions. However, it's not beachfront or walkable to town for most, requiring a 5-minute drive. Some reviews note the "remote location outside of town" and distance from beaches as drawbacks, though families with kids found staying in the park sufficient entertainment.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Site spacing is consistently criticized as too tight and compact, with multiple reviews describing sites as "on top of each other," "tiniest sites we have ever seen," and caravans requiring neighbors to move vehicles to maneuver, which impacts privacy and comfort.

2. Premium pricing divides opinion with several reviews calling it "expensive," "overpriced," and noting costs like $580 for ensuite sites outside school holidays and $470/night for units with faulty water heaters, which some felt didn't match the value delivered.

3. Several cleanliness and maintenance issues in specific accommodations including dated facilities needing renovation, floors not properly cleaned, moldy porta-cots, smelly ensuites, faulty water heaters not fixed before guest arrival, and some amenities described as "worn out and dated" needing upgrading to NRMA standards.
