PARK: Thredbo Diggings campground
SCORE: 62/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-04-15
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 34

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (8/20) — No structured kids entertainment or playground facilities; families create their own fun through bike riding on trails, fishing in the river, and interacting with wildlife. Reviews mention "kids learning to fish" and bike tracks, but this is self-directed nature play rather than organized activities.

Nature (20/20) — Exceptional natural setting right on the pristine Thredbo River with stunning mountain views, abundant wildlife including platypus, wombats, kangaroos and deer. Reviews consistently praise the "amazing scenery," "breathtaking" starry nights, clean river with swimming holes, and wilderness feel within Kosciuszko National Park.

Cleanliness (13/15) — Long drop toilets consistently described as "clean" and "neat and tidy" with good maintenance by National Parks. Lost points for basic facilities and occasional mentions of bugs, plus sites where people walk through others' campsites affecting privacy.

Value (12/15) — Very affordable at approximately $6 per night camping plus $29 parking, representing excellent value for the location. However, the lack of facilities (no water, power, showers) and tight spacing means families don't get premium amenities despite the beautiful setting.

Site Size (6/10) — Multiple reviews note "camping spots are tight" and "we just fit our entire set up." Sites accommodate 22ft caravans but are described as spacious enough yet "a push for larger caravans." Layout requires walking through other sites, reducing privacy and usable space.

Sentiment (8/10) — Strong positive sentiment with families loving the experience ("place is heaven," "will definitely be back"), but two 1-star reviews about generator noise and booking system issues, plus warnings about extreme weather preparation needed, prevent a higher score.

Location (9/10) — Excellent access to Thredbo Village for supplies, 20 minutes to Jindabyne, within Kosciuszko National Park with hiking and mountain biking trails. Only concern is the steep, narrow access road challenging for towing caravans.

WHY NOT 100:
No structured entertainment, playground equipment, or organized kids activities—families must create all their own entertainment through nature-based play. Zero facilities for modern camping expectations: no showers, no power hookups, no potable water on site, and no camp kitchen or communal amenities. Tight, cramped campsites with poor layout forcing guests to walk through others' sites, plus extreme weather exposure requiring significant preparation and experience.
