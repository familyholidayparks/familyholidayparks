HEADING:
Best Family Holiday & Caravan Parks in Noosa

HERO_INTRO:
Planning a family trip to Noosa? We compared the best family holiday and caravan parks in Noosa so you can quickly find the right park for your kids, budget and travel style.
Noosa's parks split into two camps: entertainment-focused parks a short drive from the beach, and river or north shore parks where nature does the entertaining. Knowing which camp suits your family saves a lot of second-guessing.

LOCAL_KNOWLEDGE:
Here's the thing most families don't realise about Noosa: none of the holiday parks sit on Main Beach. The Ingenia parks trade beachfront position for heated pools, jumping pillows and outdoor movie nights, which works well for younger kids. The north shore parks sit across the Noosa River car ferry at Tewantin, so factor the crossing into your plans, especially with a van on. Main Beach is patrolled year-round and faces north, so the surf stays gentle for little ones. Book the national park walk to the fairy pools for low tide, and get to Hastings Street before 9am or expect to circle for parking.

IF_WE_WERE_BOOKING:
* On-site entertainment: We'd choose Ingenia Holidays Noosa or Ingenia Holidays Noosa North for the heated pools, jumping pillows and movie nights, and treat the short drive to the beach as part of the routine.
* Nature and adventure: We'd choose BIG4 Park Lane or the Noosa North Shore Beach Campground if our kids were happier with laser tag, bush and beach than polished facilities, and we didn't mind the car ferry crossing.
* Our tip: Noosa books out fast in school holidays and the parks are smaller than Gold Coast equivalents. If you strike out on dates, look at Sunshine Coast parks 30 to 40 minutes south and day-trip in.

FAQ:
Q: Is Noosa good for families?
A: Yes. Main Beach is patrolled year-round with gentle north-facing surf, the national park walks suit school-aged kids, and the river is calm enough for kayaking and paddleboarding with younger children.
Q: Are any Noosa holiday parks on the beach?
A: Not on Main Beach. The closest thing to beachfront is the north shore, across the river by car ferry. Most families stay at parks a short drive from the beach and treat the pool as home base.
Q: What is the best Noosa holiday park for young kids?
A: Ingenia Holidays Noosa and Ingenia Holidays Noosa North both have heated pools, kids wading pools, jumping pillows and outdoor movie nights, which is the strongest on-site entertainment in the area.
Q: How does the Noosa North Shore ferry work?
A: The car ferry crosses the Noosa River at Tewantin and runs continuously through the day. It takes a few minutes, carries caravans, and you pay per vehicle on board.
Q: Where should young children swim in Noosa?
A: The river foreshore near Noosaville is calm and shallow, and Main Beach is patrolled with gentle surf. Both work well for kids under 10.
Q: What are the best free family activities in Noosa?
A: The national park coastal walk, swimming at Main Beach, the river foreshore parks at Noosaville and the Noosa Farmers Market on Sundays are all free or close to it.
Q: How far in advance should you book a Noosa holiday park?
A: School holidays and long weekends book out months ahead. Noosa has fewer parks than most Queensland destinations, so book as soon as your dates are set.
Q: Can you take a caravan to Noosa North Shore?
A: Yes, the car ferry carries caravans and motorhomes. Check your rig's length against the ferry's limits and allow extra time on changeover days.
Q: What should families do in Noosa when it rains?
A: The Ingenia parks run indoor games rooms, Noosa Cinema is in Noosa Junction, and Eumundi Markets run under cover on Wednesdays and Saturdays about 20 minutes away.
Q: Is Noosa expensive for holiday parks?
A: Generally yes, sites cost more than comparable parks on the Sunshine Coast. Travelling outside school holidays makes a noticeable difference.

Best Family Holiday & Caravan Parks on the Sunshine Coast

HERO_INTRO:
Planning a family trip to the Sunshine Coast? We compared the best family holiday and caravan parks on the Sunshine Coast so you can quickly find the right park for your kids, budget and travel style.
From waterslide resorts at Caloundra to classic beachfront parks at Dicky Beach, the Sunshine Coast has one of the strongest park line-ups in the country, and the calm water at Golden Beach makes it a standout for younger kids.

LOCAL_KNOWLEDGE:
The Sunshine Coast rewards picking your patch. Caloundra suits families with young kids: Golden Beach is calm, protected water and BIG4 Caloundra sits 300 metres from it with waterslides and a kids coordinator running daily activities. Mooloolaba and Alexandra Headland are busier but walkable to cafes and the Sea Life aquarium. The Maroochy River parks trade surf for still water and space. Kings Beach has a free saltwater pool on the rocks that kids love. Australia Zoo is 20 minutes inland from Caloundra and worth a full day. School holiday bookings for the beachfront parks open early and go quickly.

IF_WE_WERE_BOOKING:
* Waterslides and activities: We'd choose BIG4 Caloundra or Ingenia Holidays Rivershore for the heated pools, waterslides and daily kids programs. Caloundra also puts you 300 metres from calm swimming at Golden Beach.
* Classic beachfront: We'd choose Dicky Beach Family Holiday Park or Alex Beach Cabins and Tourist Park if we wanted to fall out of the van and onto the sand, with the skatepark out the front of Dicky Beach a bonus for older kids.
* Our tip: Golden Beach at Caloundra is the best young-kid swimming on the coast. Calm, shallow and protected. If your kids are under 8, base yourself at that end and day-trip north.

FAQ:
Q: Is the Sunshine Coast good for families?
A: Yes, it's one of Australia's most reliable family destinations. Calm swimming at Golden Beach, patrolled surf beaches, Australia Zoo, Sea Life Mooloolaba and a strong line-up of holiday parks with pools and waterslides.
Q: Which Sunshine Coast holiday park has the best waterslides?
A: BIG4 Caloundra has a heated pool complex with slides suitable for all ages, and Ingenia Holidays Rivershore runs two waterslides and a splash pool.
Q: What is the best area of the Sunshine Coast for young children?
A: Caloundra. Golden Beach is calm and shallow, the foreshore has playgrounds and BBQs, and BIG4 Caloundra is a short walk away.
Q: Are there beachfront caravan parks on the Sunshine Coast?
A: Yes. Dicky Beach Family Holiday Park sits 200 metres from the sand and Alex Beach Cabins is 400 metres from Alexandra Headland Beach.
Q: How far is Australia Zoo from the holiday parks?
A: About 20 minutes drive from Caloundra and 30 to 40 minutes from Mooloolaba and Maroochydore. Allow a full day.
Q: What are the best free family activities on the Sunshine Coast?
A: The Kings Beach saltwater pool, Golden Beach foreshore playgrounds, the Coastal Pathway for bikes and scooters, and Mooloolaba's beachfront parks are all free.
Q: How far in advance should you book?
A: Beachfront parks book out months ahead for school holidays, especially Christmas. Book as early as your plans allow.
Q: Which parks suit caravans with big rigs?
A: BIG4 Maroochy River has spacious riverside sites and most of the larger parks handle big rigs. Call ahead for slab sites over 10 metres.
Q: What should families do when it rains?
A: Sea Life Mooloolaba, the cinema at Maroochydore, indoor play centres at Warana and Kawana, and the games rooms at the bigger parks cover most wet days.
Q: Is the Sunshine Coast cheaper than Noosa?
A: Generally yes. Comparable powered sites and cabins run noticeably cheaper, and there are more parks to choose from, which helps in peak periods.

HEADING:
Best Family Holiday & Caravan Parks in Port Stephens

HERO_INTRO:
Planning a family trip to Port Stephens? We compared the best family holiday and caravan parks in Port Stephens so you can quickly find the right park for your kids, budget and travel style.
Port Stephens is calm bay swimming, dolphin cruises and the biggest sand dunes in the southern hemisphere, with parks ranging from all-weather resorts to simple beachfront setups.

LOCAL_KNOWLEDGE:
Port Stephens works because the bay stays calm. Shoal Bay, Nelson Bay and Little Beach are flat-water swimming that suits toddlers, while One Mile and Fingal Bay bring gentle surf for older kids. BIG4 Ingenia Soldiers Point is the wet-weather insurance policy with its indoor heated pool and bowling alley. The Stockton sand dunes at Worimi are 30 minutes away and sandboarding there is usually the trip highlight. Dolphin cruises run year-round from Nelson Bay marina, and whales pass through from May to November. The Tomaree Head summit walk is steep but short, and school-aged kids manage it fine.

IF_WE_WERE_BOOKING:
* All-weather facilities: We'd choose BIG4 Ingenia Holidays Soldiers Point for the indoor heated pool, bowling alley and under-5s play area. It's the park that saves a rainy week.
* Beach-first holiday: We'd choose Halifax Holiday Park, Fingal Bay Holiday Park or Shoal Bay Holiday Park if calm bay swimming across the road mattered more than on-site entertainment.
* Our tip: Book a sandboarding tour on the Stockton dunes early in your stay. If the kids love it, and they will, you can go back. Quad and camel tours on the dunes book out in school holidays.

FAQ:
Q: Is Port Stephens good for families?
A: Yes. The bay beaches are calm enough for toddlers, dolphin cruises run year-round, and the Stockton sand dunes give older kids a genuine adventure day.
Q: Which Port Stephens holiday park is best for young children?
A: BIG4 Ingenia Soldiers Point has an indoor heated pool, a bowling alley and a dedicated under-5s play area, making it the strongest all-weather pick for young families.
Q: Which beaches are calmest for little kids?
A: Shoal Bay, Nelson Bay foreshore and Little Beach are flat-water bay swimming. Fingal Bay is gentle surf and patrolled in season.
Q: Are the parks close to the dolphin cruises?
A: Yes, cruises leave from Nelson Bay marina, about 5 to 15 minutes drive from most parks. Morning cruises usually have the calmest water.
Q: When can you see whales in Port Stephens?
A: May to November, with the peak from June to October. Several bay cruises switch to whale watching in season.
Q: What are the Stockton sand dunes and are they worth it?
A: The Worimi Conservation Lands hold the largest moving sand dunes in the southern hemisphere, about 30 minutes from Nelson Bay. Sandboarding tours are usually the highlight of a family trip.
Q: What are the best free family activities?
A: Swimming at the bay beaches, the Tomaree Head summit walk, Fly Point snorkelling and the foreshore playgrounds at Nelson Bay and Shoal Bay.
Q: Do the parks have pools?
A: Soldiers Point and Fingal Bay do. Halifax and Shoal Bay skip the pool and rely on their beach positions instead, so pick based on how your family swims.
Q: How far in advance should you book?
A: School holidays book out months ahead, especially at the beachfront parks. Summer weekends fill fast too because Port Stephens is an easy drive from Sydney and Newcastle.
Q: What should families do when it rains?
A: The indoor pool and bowling at Soldiers Point, Irukandji Shark and Ray Encounters at Anna Bay, and the cinema at Nelson Bay cover wet days.

HEADING:
Best Family Holiday & Caravan Parks in Byron Bay

HERO_INTRO:
Planning a family trip to Byron Bay? We compared the best family holiday and caravan parks in Byron Bay so you can quickly find the right park for your kids, budget and travel style.
Byron rewards families who pick their base carefully. Stay walkable to town and you skip the parking nightmare entirely, or head to Suffolk Park and Broken Head for the quiet beachfront version of Byron most visitors never see.

LOCAL_KNOWLEDGE:
Parking is Byron's real challenge, so the smartest move a family can make is staying somewhere you don't need the car. First Sun puts you 500 metres from Main Beach and the town centre. Discovery Parks is the entertainment option with the only waterpark in the area, a short walk from Belongil Beach. Suffolk Park and Broken Head, ten minutes south, are the quiet alternative with patrolled swimming at Broken Head in season and a fraction of the crowds. The lighthouse walk is doable with school-aged kids if you start early. Main Beach is patrolled year-round and stays gentle most days.

IF_WE_WERE_BOOKING:
* Walk to town and beach: We'd choose First Sun Holiday Park or Reflections Byron Bay to skip the parking battle entirely and walk to Main Beach, the playground and dinner.
* Pools and quiet beaches: We'd choose Discovery Parks Byron Bay for the waterpark and heated pool, or Suffolk Beachfront and Broken Head if a quieter patrolled beach beats on-site facilities.
* Our tip: Do the Cape Byron lighthouse walk before 8am. You'll beat the heat and the crowds, often spot dolphins from the headland, and be back at the park pool before the day gets busy.

FAQ:
Q: Is Byron Bay good for families?
A: Yes, with the right base. Main Beach is patrolled year-round and gentle, the lighthouse walk suits school-aged kids, and staying walkable to town removes the parking stress that frustrates most visitors.
Q: Which Byron Bay holiday park has a pool?
A: Discovery Parks Byron Bay is the standout, with a waterpark, heated pool and splash pad. The other parks rely on beach access instead.
Q: Where should families stay to walk to town?
A: First Sun Holiday Park sits between Main Beach and the town centre, and Reflections Byron Bay is a short walk from both. Neither requires a car for daily life.
Q: Is Suffolk Park a good alternative to staying in Byron?
A: Yes, especially for families wanting quiet. It's ten minutes south, has its own shops and cafes, and Broken Head's beach is patrolled in season with far fewer people.
Q: Where is the safest swimming for young kids?
A: Main Beach in front of the surf club is patrolled year-round, and Clarkes Beach next to it is usually the calmest stretch. At Suffolk, swim at patrolled Broken Head in season.
Q: What are the best free family activities in Byron?
A: The lighthouse walk, swimming at Main Beach, the beachfront playground near the surf club and watching the sunset from the headland cost nothing.
Q: How bad is parking really?
A: In holiday periods, genuinely difficult from mid-morning. Families staying at walkable parks save an hour a day. If you drive in, arrive before 9am.
Q: How far in advance should you book?
A: Byron's parks are among the most in-demand in the country. School holidays and festival weekends book out many months ahead.
Q: What should families do when it rains?
A: The cinema in town, the games room at Discovery Parks, and the Macadamia Castle at Knockrow, 15 minutes away, cover most wet days.
Q: Is Byron Bay expensive for holiday parks?
A: Yes, sites and cabins price above nearly everywhere else in NSW. Travelling outside school holidays or staying at Suffolk and Broken Head softens it.

HEADING:
Best Family Holiday & Caravan Parks in Jervis Bay

HERO_INTRO:
Planning a family trip to Jervis Bay? We compared the best family holiday and caravan parks in Jervis Bay so you can quickly find the right park for your kids, budget and travel style.
Jervis Bay is white sand, clear calm water and dolphins on demand. The parks range from a beachfront spot 100 metres from Huskisson Beach to simple camping inside Booderee National Park.

LOCAL_KNOWLEDGE:
The bay is the whole point here. The water is calm, clear and shallow for a long way out, which makes it some of the best young-kid swimming in NSW. Huskisson is the hub, with the wharf, cafes and dolphin cruises, and Holiday Haven Huskisson Beach sits 100 metres from the sand. Tasman Holiday Parks trades beach proximity for the area's best pool setup. Hyams Beach is famous and correspondingly impossible to park at in summer; go early or pick another white-sand beach, there are plenty. Green Patch and Murrays Beach inside Booderee are worth the park entry fee. Watch for stingrays cruising the shallows at the Huskisson wharf around fish-cleaning time.

IF_WE_WERE_BOOKING:
* Beachfront position: We'd choose Holiday Haven Huskisson Beach to wake up 100 metres from the sand and walk to town, or Holiday Haven White Sands one street back.
* Pool and facilities: We'd choose Tasman Holiday Parks Jervis Bay for the pool with slide and spa, pedal carts and playground, accepting a short drive to the main beaches.
* Our tip: Skip the Hyams Beach parking scramble. The sand at Murrays Beach, Green Patch and Huskisson is the same blinding white, and you'll actually get a park. Book Booderee campsites and cruises ahead in summer.

FAQ:
Q: Is Jervis Bay good for young children?
A: Exceptionally. The bay's water is calm, clear and shallow, more like a giant swimming pool than open ocean, and Huskisson Beach and Callala Bay suit toddlers well.
Q: Which Jervis Bay holiday park is closest to the beach?
A: Holiday Haven Huskisson Beach is about 100 metres from the sand, with Holiday Haven White Sands a few minutes walk from Huskisson Beach.
Q: Which park has the best pool?
A: Tasman Holiday Parks Jervis Bay, with a pool, waterslide and spa plus a playground beside it. It's the pick if your kids want a pool as well as the beach.
Q: Is Hyams Beach worth visiting?
A: The sand is genuinely white, but parking is extremely limited in summer. Green Patch, Murrays Beach and Huskisson offer the same white sand with easier access.
Q: When can you see dolphins and whales?
A: Dolphins live in the bay year-round and cruises from Huskisson see them most days. Whales pass through from roughly May to November.
Q: What is Booderee National Park and is it worth the entry fee?
A: It's the national park covering the southern side of the bay, home to Green Patch, Murrays Beach and Cave Beach. For a family it's easily worth a day or two.
Q: What are the best free family activities?
A: Swimming at Huskisson and Callala, the White Sands walk between Huskisson and Vincentia, spotting stingrays at the wharf, and the playgrounds along the Huskisson foreshore.
Q: How far in advance should you book?
A: Summer school holidays book out many months ahead, and Booderee's campgrounds run a ballot for peak season. Shoulder seasons are far easier and the water is still swimmable into autumn.
Q: What should families do when it rains?
A: The Jervis Bay Maritime Museum at Huskisson, the games rooms at the bigger parks, and Nowra's indoor play centres 25 minutes away cover wet days.
Q: Is Jervis Bay suitable for caravans?
A: Yes, the Holiday Haven and Tasman parks all take vans. Sites at the beachfront parks are tighter, so ask for a larger site when booking if you're over 8 metres.

Planning a family trip to Phillip Island? We compared the best family holiday and caravan parks on Phillip Island so you can quickly find the right park for your kids, budget and travel style.

LOCAL_KNOWLEDGE:
Phillip Island's weather turns quickly, which is why Anchor Belle's genuinely warm indoor heated pool earns its reputation; families rebook it year after year. The north-facing beaches around Cowes, including Chicken Beach and Childrens Beach, are calm and shallow, while the ocean side at Woolamai is strictly for strong swimmers. Book Penguin Parade tickets ahead in school holidays and take warm layers even in January, the viewing stands get cold after sunset. The Koala Conservation Reserve's boardwalks put you at eye level with koalas, and the Nobbies boardwalk has seals on the rocks below through summer.

IF_WE_WERE_BOOKING:
* Kids entertained on-site: We'd choose Anchor Belle Holiday Park for the heated indoor pool, games room and scavenger hunts. It's the park families return to for 15 years straight.
* Beach and nature: We'd choose NRMA Phillip Island Beachfront or BIG4 Ingenia Phillip Island for beach access, kids clubs in holidays and a quieter pace between penguin and koala outings.
* Our tip: Book the Penguin Parade for your first night, not your last. If weather turns it into a washout, you've got spare nights to rebook, and the kids will want to go twice anyway.

FAQ:
Q: Is Phillip Island good for families?
A: Yes, it's one of Victoria's best family destinations. Penguins every night of the year, koalas at the conservation reserve, calm northern beaches and parks built around kids.
Q: Which Phillip Island holiday park is best for young kids?
A: Anchor Belle Holiday Park. The indoor heated pool is genuinely warm, there's a toddler pool, and the games room and organised activities keep under-12s busy whatever the weather.
Q: How do Penguin Parade tickets work?
A: Book online ahead, especially for school holidays and weekends. Penguins arrive after sunset year-round, so the start time shifts with the season.
Q: Which beaches are safe for young children?
A: The north-facing beaches near Cowes, including Childrens Beach and Chicken Beach, are calm and shallow. Avoid the ocean beaches like Woolamai with young kids, the rips are serious.
Q: What else is there beyond the penguins?
A: The Koala Conservation Reserve, the Nobbies boardwalk with summer seal watching, Churchill Island's heritage farm with daily animal demonstrations, and A Maze'N Things for a rainy day.
Q: How far is Phillip Island from Melbourne?
A: About 90 minutes to two hours drive from Melbourne via the bridge at San Remo. No ferry required.
Q: What should families pack that they usually forget?
A: Warm layers, even in summer. The Penguin Parade stands are exposed and cold after dark, and island weather changes fast.
Q: What are the best free family activities?
A: The beaches at Cowes, the Nobbies boardwalk, watching the pelican feeding at San Remo at noon, and the rhyll wetlands walks are all free.
Q: How far in advance should you book a park?
A: Summer school holidays book out months ahead, and Anchor Belle's returning families snap up peak weeks early. Winter is quiet and easy.
Q: What should families do when it rains?
A: A Maze'N Things, the games rooms at Anchor Belle and BIG4 Ingenia, and the Phillip Island Chocolate Factory cover most wet days.

HEADING:
Best Family Holiday & Caravan Parks on the Great Ocean Road

HERO_INTRO:
Planning a family trip along the Great Ocean Road? We compared the best family holiday and caravan parks on the Great Ocean Road so you can quickly find the right park for your kids, budget and travel style.
This is Australia's best family road trip, and the parks along it are genuinely strong: a 92-scored beachfront resort at Apollo Bay, an indoor waterslide complex at Anglesea and koalas wandering through camp at Wye River.

LOCAL_KNOWLEDGE:
The smart family move is basing yourself in one or two spots rather than moving every night. Apollo Bay sits mid-route and BIG4 Pisces is the standout park on the entire road, with nightly light shows, a pump track and a heated pool across from the beach. Anglesea suits the Melbourne end, and BIG4 Anglesea's indoor heated pool with waterslides is the region's wet-weather trump card. Kennett River has the most reliable wild koala spotting on the road; look up on the walk behind the caravan park. The Twelve Apostles are 90 minutes past Apollo Bay, so do them as a day trip and time it for late afternoon light. The Otway treetop walk and Erskine Falls at Lorne break up the drive nicely.

IF_WE_WERE_BOOKING:
* The full resort experience: We'd choose BIG4 Apollo Bay Pisces, the highest-scored park on the road, for the heated pool, pump track, nightly light shows and beachfront position mid-route.
* Wet-weather insurance: We'd choose BIG4 Anglesea for the big indoor heated pool with waterslides, splash park and toddler rooms. In Victorian coastal weather, that indoor pool earns its keep.
* Our tip: Stay two or three nights in one base rather than hopping nightly. Apollo Bay puts the Twelve Apostles, the Otways and Kennett River's koalas all within day-trip range, and the kids get to actually enjoy the park.

FAQ:
Q: Is the Great Ocean Road good for kids?
A: Yes, if you pace it. Base yourself at one or two parks, keep drive days under two hours and mix beach time with short walks. The winding road can make backseat passengers queasy if you rush it.
Q: What is the best family holiday park on the Great Ocean Road?
A: BIG4 Apollo Bay Pisces scores 92/100, the highest on the road, with a heated pool, water play area, pump track, jumping pillow and nightly light shows across from the beach.
Q: Where can kids see wild koalas?
A: Kennett River, between Lorne and Apollo Bay, is the most reliable spot. Walk up Grey River Road behind the caravan park and look up. Early morning and late afternoon are best.
Q: How far are the Twelve Apostles from Apollo Bay?
A: About 90 minutes drive. Go late afternoon for the best light and fewer coaches, and add Loch Ard Gorge next door, kids usually prefer it.
Q: Which park is best in bad weather?
A: BIG4 Anglesea. The large indoor heated pool with waterslides, splash park and toddler areas keeps a family busy through a full day of rain.
Q: Are the beaches safe for young children?
A: Swim between the flags at patrolled beaches like Lorne, Apollo Bay and Anglesea in season. The ocean beaches carry strong rips, so keep young kids to the patrolled stretches.
Q: How many days do you need?
A: Three to five days works well for families. One or two nights at the Anglesea or Lorne end, then two or three at Apollo Bay covers the road without rushing.
Q: Is the drive hard with a caravan?
A: It's winding but sealed and well-maintained. Take it slow between Lorne and Apollo Bay, use the pull-over bays, and avoid towing it as a day-trip road, park the van and drive light.
Q: What are the best free family activities?
A: Koala spotting at Kennett River, Erskine Falls at Lorne, the beaches, and the lookouts the whole way along cost nothing.
Q: How far in advance should you book?
A: Apollo Bay and Anglesea book out well ahead for summer school holidays. Shoulder season, March to May and September to November, is quieter and the road is at its best.
