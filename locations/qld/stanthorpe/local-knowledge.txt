G'day! Stanthorpe and the Granite Belt sit pretty at 900 metres above sea level, which means you'll actually need a jumper in winter – a rare treat in Queensland – and the kids can run around without melting in summer. September through May is your sweet spot for visiting, when the apple orchards are either in blossom or laden with fruit you can pick yourselves, and the local strawberry farms are in full swing. Here's a tip from someone who's done the drive more times than I can count: fill up with fuel before you leave Warwick, because servo prices up the mountain will make your eyes water, and you'll want that extra cash for the amazing Italian gelato at the Granite Belt Maze.
