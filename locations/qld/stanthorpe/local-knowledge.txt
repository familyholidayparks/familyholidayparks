# Stanthorpe Family Visit Guide

Stanthorpe's best between March and May, and September to November – you'll dodge the scorching summer heat and the winter cold snaps that can catch you off-guard. Spring's brilliant for the wineries and farmers markets, while autumn's perfect for hiking without melting. Avoid school holidays if you can; the carpark at Main Street gets absolutely rammed and accommodation prices spike like you wouldn't believe. Winter's not terrible, but some roads to the lookouts can get dodgy after rain, and a few spots shut down. Bring layers – the temperature swings up here are no joke, especially if you're heading to the higher lookouts.

What families actually love here is how easy it is to get kids outdoors without it being complicated. Sunridge Lookout and Girraween National Park are must-dos – the walks are manageable and the views shut people up pretty quick. The markets on weekends are decent for grabbing local produce and letting the kids run around. Hit up one of the cellar doors for a family lunch; most have good food and don't mind kids, and honestly, you'll need the break. The local shops are friendly and you can actually park without stress on quiet days. One solid tip: grab a coffee at one of the local cafes and ask the staff where they take their own kids – locals know the spots tourists miss.

Watch out for the fruit fly restrictions if you're heading home – you can't take fresh fruit across the border without a permit, which catches everyone out. The roads up here get tight and twisty, so if you're not comfortable driving through hills, take it slow and don't stress about the cars behind you. Summer storms roll through fast and hard, so check the forecast before planning outdoor stuff. And here's the real local knowledge: skip the generic tourist shops on Main Street and head to the small galleries and the farmers market instead – better quality, better prices, and you're actually supporting locals. Book accommodation well ahead, especially for school holidays, because it genuinely does fill up.
