PARK: Top of the Town Tourist Park
SCORE: 68/100
DATE SCORED: 2024-01-15
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 107

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (10/20) — Limited on-site entertainment with one reviewer noting "nothing much to amuse the kids" requiring "a quick trip into town to the playground." The pool receives mixed feedback with families reporting "kids loved the pool" but several noting teenagers jumping from rocks made it unsafe, with "families with young children had to get out." One review mentioned "surprisingly good facilities for the kids" but most family reviews focus on using the park as a base rather than destination entertainment.

Nature (16/20) — Strong natural environment with multiple reviews praising the bushland setting. Families noted "beautiful bush up the back of the park," "like a mini Girraween on the edge of town," "stunning views," and "felt like camping in the bush." The park backs onto a hill with terraced camping areas among granite rocks. Easy access to Girraween National Park mentioned by families. One reviewer with kids specifically praised "lovely park, lots to explore, our kids had a great time" in the natural setting.

Cleanliness (12/15) — Generally positive with most reviews praising "very clean amenities," "spotless amenities," and modern facilities. However, consistency is an issue with several families reporting problems: "amenities could of been better cleaned," "shower block could do with more attention on weekends," and two scathing 1-star reviews describing cabins as "extremely dirty," "filthy bathroom," and "mould in the shower." Recent reviews more positive about amenity blocks being kept clean during busy periods.

Value (12/15) — Mixed feedback on value. Positive reviews note "prices are very reasonable," "good value country camp ground," and "reasonable price with modern amenities." However, families staying in cabins reported concerns: "pretty hefty price for what we got," "barely had the bare essentials," and confusion over inconsistent pricing with some paying $27-42 for similar sites. One family felt they "paid $20 to just not have to stay another night." The customer service issues around refunds for medical emergencies damaged value perception.

Site Size (7/10) — Site sizes vary considerably. Positive mentions include "good sized sites," "nice roomie sites," and "big sites, some drive through." However, families with caravans noted challenges: "some are rather small, 55-60 m2," "quite a few with no slabs," "extremely hard to navigate and uneven," "quite unlevel," and "difficult to level the caravan on some sites." The terraced layout on the hillside creates access challenges. Ensuite sites described as "large" with good spacing.

Sentiment (7/10) — Sentiment is inconsistent across family reviews. Strong positive reviews state "absolutely loved it and cant wait to return" and "kids had a great time." However, several families had negative experiences, particularly those in cabins during peak periods. One family with young children placed near a Christmas party until 10:15pm and woken at 4:50am called it "vile" and "derelict." Another family cut their holiday short. The rigid no-refund policy during medical emergencies upset families. Pool incidents with unsupervised teenagers also frustrated families with young children.

Location (9/10) — Location consistently praised as "close to town," "5 minute easy walk," "15 min walk to main hub," and "great base to visit attractions of Stanthorpe." Families appreciated proximity to shops, cafes, restaurants, and "easy drive to Girraween National Park." Located beside showgrounds with markets next door. Access to wineries, truffle farms, and other attractions convenient. Only minor criticism: "a little far out of the town centre" for walking with small children.

WHY NOT 100:
The park lacks dedicated kids' entertainment and playground facilities, requiring families to drive into town for children's activities, significantly limiting its appeal for families with young children seeking on-site entertainment. Site quality is inconsistent with multiple families reporting unlevel pitches, poor road conditions, and difficulty accessing sites, particularly problematic for families with larger rigs and young children. Cleanliness and maintenance show significant variability, especially in cabins which received several scathing reviews from families describing filthy conditions, with "mould in shower," "ants in beds," and outdated facilities that don't meet family standards, while customer service issues around inflexible refund policies and poor handling of noise complaints from families with young children damaged the family-friendly reputation.
