# Stanthorpe Family Holiday Guide

Stanthorpe sits in Queensland's Granite Belt region, about two hours inland from the Gold Coast, making it an easy drive for families wanting to escape the crowds. The town itself doesn't have beaches, but what it does offer is something different – cool mountain air, local wineries where parents can relax while kids explore, and genuine small-town charm. The surrounding national parks and granite peaks provide brilliant opportunities for hiking, picnicking, and getting kids outdoors without the heat of coastal Queensland. For families, this means a quieter holiday where everyone actually gets along better.

The area's real strength is its mix of nature and practical family amenities. You'll find quality caravan parks like Country Style Holiday Park, Top of the Town Tourist Park, and Tiny Away Escape @ Granite Belt that cater well to families with kids, offering playgrounds and space to move around. Local attractions include the Stanthorpe Heritage Museum, various fruit farms where you can pick your own produce, and plenty of short walks suitable for younger children. It's the kind of place where you can genuinely relax without spending a fortune, and your kids will actually tire themselves out doing real stuff rather than staring at screens.
