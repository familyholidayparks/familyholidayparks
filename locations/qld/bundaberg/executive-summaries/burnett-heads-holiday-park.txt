PARK: Burnett Heads Holiday Park
SCORE: 68/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-26
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 162

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (10/20) — Limited permanent facilities with one review noting "NOTHING to do for kids in the park," though families praised Easter 2026 events including "face painting and movie night for the kids," "colouring competition," "BBQ, live music," and visits from the Easter bunny. Activities appear seasonal/event-based rather than daily offerings.

Nature (16/20) — Strong natural setting with "large leafy sites, lots of gardens and trees," "Kangaroos and birds around for the nature enthusiasts," waterfront views across the Burnett River, and "lovely walks around town and over to the water." Close proximity to Mon Repos Turtle Centre and coastal bike paths, though "not really close to a beach but close enough if you don't mind a trudge."

Cleanliness (14/15) — Overwhelmingly positive with "spotless amenities" mentioned repeatedly, "very clean amenities" across most 2025-2026 reviews, and "amenities were very clean and bathroom always smelt lovely." New management (Jess & Ross) credited with improvements, though a handful of older reviews mentioned mould issues and dated facilities that "desperately needs to be replaced."

Value (13/15) — Generally regarded as "good value," "very well priced," and "cheap rates." Multiple reviews praised reasonable pricing for what's offered, though one review questioned "$40 a night" value without upgrades like a pool, and another mentioned "$69 for a caravan site I expected more."

Site Size (9/10) — Positive feedback on "large powered slab site," "roomy sites, shaded," "very spacious with concrete slab," and "spacious tent sites." Some criticism that "sites are small and congested" and "non powered sites are very small," indicating variability across different site types.

Sentiment (9/10) — Strong repeat visitation signals with "will definitely be back" appearing frequently, "our 3rd visit," "stayed for 7 weeks," and "being staying here for years." However, several negative experiences with previous management involving rude behaviour and confrontations lowered overall sentiment, despite improvements under new management from late 2025 onwards.

Location (9/10) — Excellent convenience with "walking distance to shops, cafes," "short 200 metre walk to shops," IGA, pub, bakery, and fish & chips all nearby. "Close to the port for our Lady Musgrave trip" and proximity to Bundaberg and Bargara attractions. Only 15 minutes to Bundaberg CBD, though the waterfront location is a marina rather than swimming beach.

WHY NOT 100:
1. No swimming pool or water play facilities, which multiple reviews noted would make it worthy of 5 stars: "It would have a 5-star review with a pool" and requests for "putting in a pool would make this place worth $40 a night."
2. Minimal dedicated kids' entertainment infrastructure beyond seasonal events, with clear statement "NOTHING to do for kids in the park" and "not much around or to do for young families" for extended stays.
3. Management inconsistency created negative experiences in 2023-2024 period with reports of staff being "very rude," "yelled and swore at my husband in front of my 3 year old," and "aggressive" behaviour, though this appears resolved with new management from late 2025.
