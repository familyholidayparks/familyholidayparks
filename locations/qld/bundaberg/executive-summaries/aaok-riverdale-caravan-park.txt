PARK: AAOK Riverdale Caravan Park
SCORE: 62/100
DATE SCORED: 2025
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 126

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (4/20) — Minimal on-site entertainment with only a "small playground ideal for kids to burn off some energy" and a camp kitchen with TV and occasional Friday campfires; no waterpark, pool, or structured activities for children.

Nature (13/20) — Located "right on the Burnett River" with river views and walking access, though "no view due to mangroves" and the river is "brown and unswimable"; pleasant riverside setting but limited natural play areas for children.

Cleanliness (12/15) — Consistently described as "extremely clean and well kept" with "spotless" bathrooms and "clean amenities," though facilities are "old but clean" and "dated but always clean"; some complaints about "amenities need regular cleaning" and "long grass & bindis."

Value (13/15) — Strong value proposition with "very reasonably priced" rates around "$35 per night for powered site" and "great value compared to motels"; families appreciate the "excellent price" though some felt it "expensive as far as value for money" compared to alternatives.

Site Size (7/10) — Sites described as "generous and level" with "spacious sites" and "loads of room," though "not really made for larger vans" and some sites "a bit hard to get into"; "nice green grassy areas" but "no trees around caravans" for shade.

Sentiment (7/10) — Mixed sentiment with many positive experiences ("will definitely come back," "highly recommend") balanced by serious concerns including a dog attack incident, factory fumes, and occasional service complaints; repeat visitation evident among budget travelers.

Location (6/10) — Excellent proximity to Bundaberg CBD with "10-15 min walk from central Bundaberg" and "just cross the bridge and you are in town," but no beach access and concerns about adjacent factory; convenient for town access but not a destination location for families.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Virtually no on-site kids entertainment or activities beyond a small basic playground, making it unsuitable for families seeking engaging holiday experiences for children.
2. Significant environmental concerns with adjacent factory producing "toxic fumes like burning rubber" that negatively impacted multiple guests' experiences.
3. Park infrastructure is dated and requires upgrading with "older facilities," "grounds need a lot of work," and "amenities need regular cleaning," plus serious safety incident with dangerous dog attack poorly managed by staff.
