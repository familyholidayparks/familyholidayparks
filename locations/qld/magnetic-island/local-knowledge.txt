# Visiting Magnetic Island with Kids

October to April is school holiday sweet spot if you've got time, but honestly, May to September is when locals actually enjoy the island—fewer crowds, perfect weather, and you won't melt. Avoid the stinger season (November to May) unless you're confident with vinegar and stinger suits; Picnic Bay has netted areas but it's a real consideration. Book accommodation early if you're coming during school holidays, but midweek visits will save you a fortune and your sanity. Pro tip: catch the ferry from Townsville in the morning when the water's calm—afternoon runs can get rough, and kids get cranky on choppy seas.

The must-dos with kids are pretty straightforward: Horseshoe Bay for the obvious reason (best beach on the island), the Magnetic Island Forts walk which takes about an hour and has actual history without being boring, and honestly, just let them run around Picnic Bay's rockpools for hours—it's free and they'll be knackered after. Skip the touristy koala parks and head to Geoffrey Bay early morning instead to spot wallabies and birdlife. Grab your groceries at Nelly Bay shops before you settle in—prices are steep but nowhere near as bad as you'd think for an island.

Watch out for rocky entry points at most beaches, bring decent reef shoes, and don't underestimate the sun—it'll knock you flat. The island's got plenty of hiking but check weather conditions; trails get slippery fast. Also, there's not much nightlife, so if you're after a party weekend, you're in the wrong spot—but if you want the family to actually switch off and play together, this is gold. Insider move: visit West Point or the northern beaches on the quiet side for stunning scenery without the tourist crowds.
