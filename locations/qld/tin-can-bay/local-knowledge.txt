# Tin Can Bay Local Knowledge for Families

The best time to visit is May through September when it's warm but not scorching, and the water's actually swimmable without worrying about stingers. Winter school holidays (late June/early July) gets packed, so if you can swing it, go in May or early September instead. December to February is proper hot and humid – save that for the beach if you must, but honestly, the bay's nicer earlier in the year. Book accommodation well ahead no matter what, as family-friendly spots fill up fast. Don't underestimate the tides here – they're significant, and timing your beach time around low tide means you've got heaps more sand to work with and better swimming conditions.

What makes Tin Can Bay special for families is the dolphins – the wild ones actually come into the shallows to feed, and you can watch them from the shore for free, which beats paying for a tour every time. The patrolled beach is small but perfect for littlies, and Coondapoi Creek is great for exploring. Here's the insider bit: grab fish and chips from the local spots (Tin Can Bay Tavern does decent family meals too) and eat on the foreshore at sunset – it's genuinely lovely and costs way less than the tourist cafes. Bring good sunscreen and reapply constantly because that water reflects like crazy, and pack bathers you don't mind getting sandy because you'll be in and out constantly.

Watch out for the heat in summer – seriously, it'll sneak up on you. The bay's shallow so the water warms up quickly, which is great for little ones but means sea lice can be an issue December through March (check before you swim). Roads around here aren't flash, so drive carefully, especially if you're towing a caravan. And genuinely, leave the stinger suit at home outside summer months – locals will clock you as a tourist immediately. Bring bikes or borrow some – it's a fantastic spot for cycling around, especially the quieter back roads.
