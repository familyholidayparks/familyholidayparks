G'day! Mission Beach is where the rainforest literally tumbles onto the sand, and your kids can spot cassowaries wandering through the caravan parks at dawn – I've seen it happen at Beachcomber myself. The shoulder seasons of April-May and September-October are magic here: the stinger nets are still up, the humidity drops off, and you'll actually get a campsite without booking six months ahead. Here's something most visitors miss: grab breakfast at Bingil Bay café early, then head straight to the rockpools at the northern end during low tide – the local families know this spot stays calm even when Mission Beach proper gets choppy, and the kids can snorkel safely while you keep one eye out for those prehistoric birds.
