PARK: Tasman Holiday Parks - Hervey Bay
SCORE: 58/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-22
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 103

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (8/20) — Reviews consistently note there is NO playground for young children, which was "a let down for us with younger kids." A games room and BBQ area exist, with mentions of "games area and common room" and "Pool, kid's area, BBQ" but the games area was "usually occupied by older kids with attitudes." No waterpark, jumping pillow, or organized kids activities mentioned.

Nature (10/20) — The park is centrally located "between shops and beach" with the "beautiful esplanade a short 5 minutes walk away" and "1km walk to beach." However, the park itself offers limited natural environment, with no mention of bushland, creeks, or significant green space. The location is "20 minutes from the beach" by some accounts, suggesting distance varies by cabin/site placement.

Cleanliness (10/15) — Mixed evidence with strong recent positives but concerning negatives. Many reviews praise "exceptionally clean," "spotless," "one of the cleanest campsite bathrooms I've ever seen," and "clean amenities" maintained throughout stays. However, multiple family reviews report serious issues: "Deluxe Cabin was Filthy," "hair all over the shower walls, mould in the shower, food scraps between cushions," "dirty, tiles chipped cracked, stains on floor," "ant infestation," and "wasn't cleaned very well as there were bits of dirt on the floor."

Value (9/15) — Generally positive for budget accommodation with "outstanding value for money," "good price," "reasonable price," and "very good value for money" mentioned frequently. However, families paid premium prices during peak with poor experiences: "$362 per night" described as "overpriced tiny cabin," "almost $200 a night" was "an absolute disappointment," and one reviewer felt it was "the least value for money park during our 6 week trip. Expensive, small site."

Site Size (5/10) — Significant concerns for caravans and families. Multiple reviews report "sites are tiny," "small site," "very tight row," "sites are small and access is pretty tight," "about 1.5 metres to a tent next door and about 1 metre to a camper trailer on the other side," and "just a little tight trying to park van." Cabins described as "spacious" and "very spacious" but caravan sites clearly cramped.

Sentiment (7/10) — Polarized between loyal repeaters and disappointed families. Strong positive: "home away from home," "we will be back," "would gladly stay here again," "already booked again for next Christmas." However, recent family reviews show frustration: "staff couldn't care less," "we won't be back," "would not recommend this park," "I will probably look for somewhere else to stay," and serious billing disputes with "still scammers!!!"

Location (9/10) — Consistently praised as "central location," "close to everything," "centrally located between shops and beach," "great location for the esplanade - only 15 minute walk away," "walking distance to the beachfront and shops," and "10 minutes walk to Stocklands shopping centre." Main negative is road noise: "backed onto a main road which was very noisy," "road noise is terrible," and "road noise is pretty full on too and runs through the night."

WHY NOT 100:
1. **No playground or structured kids entertainment** — Multiple family reviews explicitly state "no playground for kids which was a let down for us with younger kids" and the games room was "usually occupied by older kids," leaving young families with limited on-site activities beyond the pool.

2. **Inconsistent cleanliness with serious cabin issues** — While amenities are praised, multiple families reported filthy cabins with "black mould in bathroom," "hair all over the shower walls," "food scraps between cushions," "ant infestation," and stains, suggesting cleaning standards vary significantly between accommodation types.

3. **Very tight sites unsuitable for larger rigs** — Caravan sites are repeatedly described as "tiny," with "about 1.5 metres" clearance, making it "nearly impossible" to reverse without hitting something, and "lots of permanents" occupying the better spaces, making this challenging for family travellers with caravans.
