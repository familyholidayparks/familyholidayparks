PARK: BIG4 Hervey Bay Holiday Park
SCORE: 58/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-15
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 270

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (10/20) — Reviews consistently mention limited on-site entertainment with a basic games room that was frequently "dirty", "not functional", or closed and relocated to a "tin shed" during renovations. The pool table was often out of order, and families reported "nothing for the kids to do" on site, requiring day trips for activities. A few positive mentions of staff-organized sausage sizzles and Easter egg hunts, but insufficient for typical BIG4 family expectations.

Nature (12/20) — The park offers walking distance beach access ("2 minutes away", "5 minute walk") but reviewers note it's rocky with "not a lot of sand" and "not great for swimming". Located in a residential area away from main Hervey Bay attractions, with some tree coverage and bird life mentioned positively. The natural environment is pleasant but not outstanding, with the bay and esplanade walks being the primary nature drawcard.

Cleanliness (9/15) — This scored poorly with deeply divided opinions. Many recent reviews cite "dirty pool", "cabin floor was disgusting", "toilets and showers not very clean with grout and tiles looking very dirty and mouldy", "amenities old, inadequate and dirty", and "expired milk" in cabins. However, some guests found facilities "spotless", "always clean", and "very clean amenities". The inconsistency and high volume of negative cleanliness feedback, particularly regarding amenities and cabins, significantly impacts this score.

Value (8/15) — Families felt the park was overpriced for what's delivered, with reviews stating "bit pricey", "very expensive for what it is", "$80 for a tiny unpowered", and "price you pay is the same like you are staying at a normal big 4" despite being "not a modern Big 4". Dynamic pricing was criticized ("charged $85 despite website saying $63"). Some found it "really cheap rates" and "excellent value" but majority of family reviewers felt disappointed given BIG4 expectations versus delivery.

Site Size (6/10) — Significant criticism about site size with reviews stating "sites are small is an understatement", "too close to each other", "very small with very narrow roads", "tight parking", "site was a bit tight", and "extremely tight parking our ute". Multiple reviews mention needing staff assistance to reverse into sites. Some praised "spacious sites" and concrete slabs, but the overwhelming evidence points to cramped conditions for larger modern rigs.

Sentiment (6/10) — Mixed sentiment with passionate advocates (repeat visitors praising staff like Craig) versus families who "left after 1 night", "left after 2 and booked somewhere else", "left straight away", or "wouldn't book again". Many questioned "how they got the big 4 name" and called it "worst BIG4 we've stayed at". The high number of permanent residents created a "trailer park vibe" that detracted from the holiday feel families seek. Staff received consistent praise but facilities disappointed.

Location (7/10) — Located "a little bit out of town" in Point Vernon, "at the wrong end of town", "12 minutes from centre", making it "too far to walk to main shops" but offering a "quiet location" away from hustle. Beach access within walking distance is positive, but families noted it's "far away from beach" (rocky beach, not main swimming beach) and "not close to shops or cafes". The quieter residential setting suited some but disappointed families expecting proximity to Hervey Bay's main attractions.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Severely dated and inadequately maintained amenities with persistent cleanliness issues - multiple reviews cite dirty showers, mouldy tiles, scummy toilets, broken washers/dryers, and cabins requiring "desperate scrub or new flooring", failing to meet basic BIG4 standards that families expect and pay premium prices for.

2. Minimal on-site entertainment and kid-friendly facilities - the games room was frequently closed, dirty, or non-functional, with "nothing for the kids to do" forcing families into constant day trips, contradicting the BIG4 family holiday park promise and leaving children disappointed.

3. Very tight, cramped sites unsuitable for modern family rigs with high permanent resident population creating a "trailer park vibe" rather than a holiday atmosphere - sites described as "too close", "small is an understatement", with difficult access requiring staff parking assistance, and the permanent resident presence (some described as "rude" and "grumpy") detracted significantly from the family holiday experience.
