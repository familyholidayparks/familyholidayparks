Hervey Bay's holiday park scene punches well above its weight because the town sits at the sweet spot between serious beach culture and genuine wildlife access. Unlike the Gold Coast's theme park crowds or the Sunshine Coast's boutique pretension, Hervey Bay parks cater to people who want to actually *use* the outdoors rather than just photograph it. The top-ranked parks like Discovery Parks and Ingenia Holidays have nailed the formula: solid infrastructure without the resort markup, positioned within cooee of both the beach and the launching point for Fraser Island day trips. You'll find everything from sprawling beachfront setups like Fraser Coast Beachfront Tourist Parks at Scarness—where you can literally walk onto the sand—to riverside options that give you water access without the noise and swell conditions. The park options range from traditional caravan parks with full hookups to resort-style accommodation that's crept in over the last decade, so whether you're rolling up in a beat-up Jayco or after a cabin with aircon, there's something that fits.

The demographic here tells you everything about why these parks work. You've got the hardcore grey nomads who spend half the year moving between parks and know exactly which sites have good water pressure and afternoon shade. Families come for the school holidays in droves—particularly the Brisbane mob who treat it as their second home for a week or two—and many of them are repeat visitors who book the same park, sometimes the same site, year after year. Motorhome owners love it because Hervey Bay doesn't have the snobby attitudes you get in some coastal towns; the parks welcome you whether you're in a $200k rig or a van held together with optimism. You'll see blended families, retirees, young couples, and multi-generational groups all sharing the same parks, which creates that particular Australian holiday park vibe where everyone's civil but nobody's in each other's business.

What keeps people coming back is the access combined with the pace. You've got proper beaches for swimming and walking, the river for quieter days, and Fraser Island is genuinely life-changing—most visitors do at least one day trip and many do multiple. The parks themselves have added activities like mini golf, pools, and kids' programs, but honestly, that's not really why you're there. School holidays absolutely pack the parks to capacity and you need to book months ahead, but outside those periods you'll find spots available and a more relaxed atmosphere. The difference between a Hervey Bay park stay and others is that you're genuinely positioned to do something, not just exist in a park. Families come back because the kids remember jumping off the pier, spotting whales (in season), or that perfect day on Fraser Island—and the parks provide a comfortable base for those memories rather than being the attraction itself.
