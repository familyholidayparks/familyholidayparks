PARK: Ocean View Tourist Park
SCORE: 52/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-03-23
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 34

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (3/20) — No mention of any kids club, playground, jumping pillow, or entertainment facilities. Reviews focus on the park being quiet and peaceful with no organized activities or family entertainment options.
Nature (16/20) — Strong nature element with views of the Glass Mountains/hinterland, wildlife sightings including kookaburras and yellow-tailed black cockatoos from the deck area. Located in a peaceful natural setting with bushland surroundings, though ocean views are minimal despite the name.
Cleanliness (11/15) — Mixed feedback with many recent reviews praising "spotlessly clean" and "superbly clean" amenities, but offset by consistent concerns about outdated facilities, blocked drains, poor lighting in ladies toilets, and general maintenance issues from aging infrastructure.
Value (6/15) — Significant negative feedback about pricing with multiple reviews calling it "overpriced," "ludicrous," and "very expensive for basic camp ground." Rates of $50/night for powered sites and $225/night for cabins considered poor value given dated facilities.
Site Size (6/10) — Reviews mention "bit tight for parking," "small park," and limited space. One motorhome user found it "easy to park" but other reviews suggest cramped conditions with guests placed close together.
Sentiment (5/10) — Polarized reviews ranging from enthusiastic 5-stars to strong 1-star warnings. Recent reviews show declining satisfaction with facility age. Some repeat interest but many state they'd seek other accommodation.
Location (5/10) — Convenient proximity to Australia Zoo (main attraction mentioned), 15 minutes to Maleny, 25 minutes to Montville, but situated on a busy road with traffic noise. Misleading "ocean view" name as it's 20km from ocean with minimal water views.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Complete absence of family entertainment facilities - no playground, pool, waterpark, kids club, or organized activities that families with young children expect from a holiday park.
2. Severely outdated facilities described as a "time capsule" from the 1980s with blocked drains, poor lighting, old amenities, and maintenance issues that detract from the guest experience despite cleanliness efforts.
3. Poor value proposition with pricing ($50-225/night) that doesn't match the basic, aging facilities, leading multiple recent reviewers to explicitly warn others away or recommend alternative parks.

NOTABLE REVIEW PHRASES:
