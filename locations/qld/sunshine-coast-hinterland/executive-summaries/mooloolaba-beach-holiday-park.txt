PARK: Mooloolaba Beach Holiday Park
SCORE: 78/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-25
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 500

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (14/20) — Reviews consistently note minimal on-site entertainment with a small playground and games room, but families appreciate the heated pool and mention the beach is the main attraction. Food trucks visit regularly (pizza, Greek, Mexican) which adds convenience. No waterpark or extensive kids club facilities.

Nature (18/20) — Outstanding beach access with private gates leading directly to patrolled swimming beaches, beautiful coastal walks, and adjacent parkland with duck pond. Families praised the "3-5 minute walk to beach," "dog-friendly beach nearby," and "lovely parkland" next door for walks and cycling.

Cleanliness (14/15) — Overwhelmingly positive feedback on cleanliness with phrases like "spotlessly clean," "immaculately clean amenities," "excellent water pressure," and "well maintained." A few reviews mentioned amenities not cleaned on some days and facilities being "fairly poor and dirty" in isolated cases, preventing a perfect score.

Value (10/15) — Mixed reviews on value with many calling it "expensive," "very expensive for what you get," "too expensive for pensioners," and noting council greed. Powered sites $68-95/night considered steep for a basic park. However, location proximity saves on transport costs and some found it "good value" given the beach access.

Site Size (8/10) — Sites described as "spacious," "excellent sized," "good concrete slabs," with privacy hedges between most sites. However, multiple reviews noted "small sites," "extremely tight and squishy," "compact," and challenges for larger rigs. En-suite facilities particularly criticized as "extremely small."

Sentiment (9/10) — Very high repeat visitation with many families returning annually for 10-15+ years. Phrases like "love this place," "will definitely be back," "highly recommend," and "can't wait to return" dominate. Friendly staff consistently praised. Some frustration with customer service inflexibility and pricing dampened perfect sentiment.

Location (10/10) — Perfect score for location with universal praise: "walking distance to everything," "close to beach, cafes, shops," "central to everything on Sunshine Coast," "can't beat the location," and "ideal location." Proximity to Mooloolaba esplanade, surf clubs, and patrolled beaches is the park's standout feature.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Limited on-site entertainment and facilities for children beyond a small playground and basic pool - families noted "nothing kid friendly" and minimal activities compared to larger resort parks.
2. Pricing considered high relative to basic amenities offered, with numerous reviews citing expensive rates ($80-95/night) for a council-run park with dated facilities and tight sites.
3. Site size and spacing issues, particularly for larger rigs and families, with multiple mentions of cramped conditions, tight maneuvering, and very small en-suite bathroom facilities that felt inadequate.
