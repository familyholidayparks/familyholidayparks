PARK: Landsborough Holiday Park (Ingenia Rivershore)
SCORE: 84/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-24
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 500

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (18/20) — Reviews consistently praise the heated resort-style pool with two waterslides as 'ridiculously fun' and a major highlight. Daily activities include archery, animal encounters, kids discos, movie nights, scavenger hunts, mini golf, pedal karts, and jumping pillow—all included at no extra cost. Families describe children spending 'all day having fun' with activities keeping them entertained throughout their stay. Some reviews note kids activities during school holidays could have more staff enthusiasm.

Nature (17/20) — The park sits alongside a beautiful river with fishing access, a pontoon for crabbing, and peaceful riverside views. Families love the natural setting with 'stunning location right next to the river' and opportunities for kayaking and bird watching. Safari glamping tents overlook the river creating a nature-immersed experience. Minor deduction for being in hinterland rather than beachfront, though beaches are 15 minutes away.

Cleanliness (14/15) — Facilities described as 'spotless,' 'immaculate,' and 'always clean and well maintained' across hundreds of reviews. Amenities blocks receive consistent praise for cleanliness with showers 'delightfully hot and free.' Some reviews from school holiday periods note pool-area bathrooms for day visitors needed more frequent cleaning, and one review mentioned female toilets without hand soap for 3 days.

Value (11/15) — Pricing is premium at $200+ per night for powered sites and $580 for glamping tents during peak periods. Many families note 'expensive' or 'overpriced' but consistently add that facilities and activities justify the cost. Food and drinks at restaurant are 'reasonably priced' and happy hour appreciated. Some families feel glamping tents offer excellent value while others find tent pricing too high for what's provided.

Site Size (9/10) — Powered sites consistently described as 'huge,' 'very generous,' 'spacious' and 'biggest site I've ever stayed at' with room for large caravans and extra vehicles. One review noted a 9m caravan site 'could have fit another caravan and car on it.' Unpowered riverside sites are 'HUGE' according to multiple families. Minor point loss for some deluxe sites near playground being noisier than expected.

Sentiment (9/10) — Overwhelming majority of families express desire to return with phrases like 'will definitely be back,' 'our regular family camping spot,' 'already booked for next year,' and 'couldn't wait to come back.' Staff receive exceptional praise for being 'friendly,' 'helpful,' and going 'above and beyond.' Some negative sentiment around staff rudeness (particularly one reception staff member named Helen), and permanent residents in some reviews expressing frustration with families.

Location (6/10) — Park is centrally located 15 minutes to beaches, 5 minutes to Australia Zoo, and accessible to Sunshine Coast attractions. Families appreciate the convenient location as a 'base camp' for exploring. Major drawback is significant road noise from main highway that 'woke us up on several occasions' and is 'pretty bad' according to multiple reviews. Park is not beachfront and requires driving to coast.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Road noise from the main highway significantly impacts the experience with multiple families reporting being woken at night by traffic and describing it as 'really bad' and an ongoing issue throughout their stay.
2. Premium pricing at $200+ per night for powered sites and up to $580 for glamping makes it one of Queensland's most expensive parks, with value concerns expressed even by families who enjoyed their stay.
3. Staff service inconsistency with several detailed complaints about rude reception staff (particularly during school holidays), lack of enthusiasm in kids activity delivery, and poor handling of guest concerns, contrasting with many positive staff reviews.
