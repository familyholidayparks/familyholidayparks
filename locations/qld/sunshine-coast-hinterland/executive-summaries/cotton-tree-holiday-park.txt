PARK: Cotton Tree Holiday Park
SCORE: 71/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-14
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 256

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (8/20) — Reviews consistently note no pool, no playground, no structured kids entertainment or activities. Families mention "no pool or other entertainment" and "not much to offer except a beach." Occasional food trucks and live music events mentioned positively. Location provides natural entertainment through beach and river access.

Nature (18/20) — Outstanding natural setting praised extensively: "between the river and the beach," "right on the water," "calm waters great for kids," patrolled surf beach on one side and peaceful Maroochy River inlet on the other. Families love the "easy swim for the kids" in calm river waters. Bush turkeys mentioned frequently (sometimes as nuisance). "One of the best on this coastline" for sunsets and views.

Cleanliness (12/15) — Mixed feedback with recent improvements noted. Many praise "very clean," "spotless," "impeccably clean" amenities and "cleanest caravan park toilet I ever been." However, multiple reviews mention dirty amenities during peak times, blocked urinals, toilets running out of paper, and inconsistent cleaning schedules. Dump points reportedly overflowing during busy periods. Recent management improving standards.

Value (9/15) — Significant criticism of pricing: "$80 a night is outrageous," "very expensive," "most expensive nightly rate," "overpriced & disgusting." Families question value with no pool or entertainment at $76-84 per night. Some accept premium pricing for location: "expensive for power, water and a parking spot but very clean." Perception of poor value compared to Big4 parks with more facilities.

Site Size (5/10) — Major pain point in reviews. Overwhelming feedback: "sites are really small & close together," "crammed in like sardines," "packed in tight," "squeezed in," "tiny space," sites "too small." Many reports of difficulty reversing caravans in, cars needing to be moved, 20+ minute setup struggles. Some corner and waterfront sites noted as better. Uneven sites mentioned with tree roots and sloping ground.

Sentiment (8/10) — Polarized sentiment. Loyal long-term visitors (15-25+ years) express disappointment with new management approach. Many "will not be back" or "won't be returning" due to poor customer service, rigid policies, lack of flexibility. However, equal number praise location and plan to return: "already booked for next year," "can't wait to come back," "definitely returning." Family atmosphere and safety noted positively when not crowded.

Location (10/10) — Near-perfect scores for location. "Perfect location," "best located place," "ideal Sunny Coast spot," "prime location," "location you can't beat." Walking distance to Cotton Tree village cafes, shops, butcher, Sunshine Plaza, surf club. Direct beach and river access from sites. Close to Mooloolaba and Maroochydore CBD. Easy access without needing to drive during stays.

WHY NOT 100:
No dedicated kids entertainment or facilities (no pool, playground, jumping pillow, games room) loses significant points for family appeal - families paying premium prices expect more than just location. Site size and crowding issues with caravans "crammed in like sardines" and many reports of sites too small for modern rigs creates stressful arrival experiences. Customer service and management flexibility concerns appear frequently with reports of rude staff, rigid cancellation policies, poor communication, and lack of compassion for genuine emergencies reducing overall guest satisfaction and likelihood to recommend.

NOTABLE REVIEW PHRASES:
"right next to the river and the beach," "crammed in like sardines," "perfect location," "sites are really small and close together," "very expensive," "no pool or other entertainment," "staff are rude," "clean amenities," "walking distance to cafes and shops," "beautiful sunsets," "won't be back," "best located place to stay"
