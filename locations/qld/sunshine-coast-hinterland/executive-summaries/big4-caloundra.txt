PARK: BIG4 Caloundra Waterfront Holiday Park
SCORE: 88/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-26
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 440

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (18/20) — Reviews consistently highlight the "resort-quality" heated pool with two waterslides that kids and adults love, jumping pillow, pedal karts, go-karts, and organized daily activities including discos, bingo, and kids entertainment coordinated by staff member Nick who is "amazing with kids." Families report children "didn't want to leave" and spent hours entertained.

Nature (17/20) — The park sits on Pumicestone Passage waterfront with "stunning views" and direct gate access to coastal walking/cycling paths, Bulcock Beach "just around the corner," and beautiful morning beach walks. Gardens are "beautifully maintained" with local wildlife, though it's more waterfront urban than bushland setting.

Cleanliness (15/15) — Overwhelmingly praised as "the cleanest campground I have stayed at" with amenities "always spotless," "immaculate," and "sparkling clean no matter the time of day." Camp kitchen described as "like a five-star restaurant" and villas "exceptionally clean." Staff dedication to maintenance is evident throughout reviews.

Value (11/15) — Premium pricing at $100+ per powered site and $350+ per night for cabins is noted as "expensive," "pricey," and "out of touch with caravan affordability" by some guests. However, many feel "you get what you pay for" given the resort-style facilities, location, and quality. Some feel pricing doesn't match value compared to other parks.

Site Size (8/10) — Sites described as "adequate," "reasonable size," and "spacious" on premium sites with artificial grass. Some areas have "tight" sites where "caravans hang over onto the road" and "neighbours congregate on the road because sites aren't big enough." Drive-through sites and greenery between sites are appreciated.

Sentiment (10/10) — Exceptional repeat visitation with families returning annually for 3-12 years. Reviews express "absolute 10/10," "can't wait to come back," and "already re-booked for next year." Staff praised as "friendly," "professional," and "going above and beyond." Strong recommendation rate from families.

Location (9/10) — Perfect urban convenience with Woolworths/Coles "across the road," walking distance to Caloundra CBD, beaches, restaurants, and cafes. "Everything at your fingertips" and "literally outside the gate." Central Sunshine Coast location for attractions like Australia Zoo. Some road noise from busy intersection affects sites near boundary.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Premium pricing ($100+ powered sites, $350+ cabins) is considered expensive and "out of reach" by some long-term guests, with one reviewer noting an $800 increase year-over-year that feels "out of touch with caravan affordability."
2. Site sizes vary significantly across the park, with some areas having tight, cramped sites where vans overhang roads and cars must park in overflow areas, while only premium sites offer generous space.
3. Traffic noise from the busy Bulcock/Bowman intersection affects sites along the back boundary, with constant road noise until midnight requiring air conditioning to block it out, and single-key access to amenities creates inconvenience for families.
