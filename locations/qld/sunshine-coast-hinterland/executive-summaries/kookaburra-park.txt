PARK: Kookaburra Park
SCORE: 88/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-24
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 142

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (19/20) — Reviews consistently highlight exceptional animal experiences as the star attraction: daily guinea pig cuddles and feeding, deer interactions, goats, pigs, geese, and chickens. Ranger Steve and his children receive extraordinary praise for making animal encounters educational and magical. Additional activities include movie nights in the barn, kids disco events, the Jurassic Trail bushwalk, fairy garden with cubby house, basketball, volleyball, and organized Saturday night activities including food trucks. Families report children never running out of things to do across multi-day stays.

Nature (18/20) — Set in stunning Sunshine Coast Hinterland with valley views and mountain surrounds. Easy access to Mary River for swimming and kayaking, proximity to Booloumba Creek (10 minutes) with crystal clear waters, Little Yabba Creek, and multiple nature walks including the 1.2km Jurassic Trail with panoramic lookout. Abundant native wildlife including wallabies, kookaburras, and diverse birdlife. Reviews describe the setting as peaceful, green, and perfect for disconnecting from busy life while remaining close to Kenilworth town and attractions.

Cleanliness (14/15) — Overwhelmingly positive feedback describes amenities as exceptionally clean with hot showers, well-maintained toilets, and facilities kept spotless throughout stays. The Barn amenities block receives particular praise. One older shower block at Pine Tree Hill was noted as run down with maintenance issues (blocked toilet, leaking pipes) but reviews indicate it's scheduled for demolition and replacement. Park grounds are well-mowed and maintained.

Value (12/15) — Mixed feedback on pricing: some reviews mention it being "on the expensive side" particularly for day visits ($35 for non-campers), while camping guests generally find it reasonable for the facilities and experiences provided. Free animal feeding for campers is valued highly. Coffee van on-site, shop with supplies. Several reviews from repeat visitors suggest perceived value improves with longer stays and full engagement with activities.

Site Size (9/10) — Sites consistently described as spacious, generous, well-spaced, and not feeling crowded even when fully booked. Multiple camping areas (Pine Tree Hill, Flat Hill, The Paddock, Damside, Gum Tree Valley, etc.) offer variety. Campers don't feel "on top of each other." Sites accommodate large rigs with power and water available. Some sites offer private, tucked-away locations with stunning views.

Sentiment (10/10) — Exceptional repeat visitation rate with numerous reviews from families returning annually or multiple times. Phrases like "favourite camping spot," "can't wait to come back," "booking our next trip already," and "will definitely be back" dominate. Strong emotional connection evident with descriptions of "heaven," "magical," and "cures burnout." Very high recommendation rate to friends and family. Staff (especially Ranger Steve) create genuine connections that drive loyalty.

Location (6/10) — While the Kenilworth hinterland location offers natural beauty and proximity to swimming holes, it's relatively remote (10 minutes to Kenilworth town, 1 hour to Noosa). No mobile reception with Vodafone on-site. Location is intentionally secluded which appeals to target market but limits convenience scores. Close to Kenilworth Bakery, Kenilworth Dairies, and natural attractions but requires driving for supplies and activities outside the park.

WHY NOT 100:
1. The park's remote hinterland location means limited mobile reception and genuine isolation from conveniences, requiring 10+ minute drives to Kenilworth for supplies or bakery runs, which may challenge families needing connectivity or easy access to amenities.

2. Value perception issues exist with day-visitor pricing ($35 for animal viewing) receiving harsh criticism as a "scam" from non-camping guests, and some camping families noting costs are "on the expensive side" compared to alternatives, suggesting pricing could be more competitive.

3. Maintenance inconsistencies appeared in 2025-2026 with the Pine Tree Hill amenities block described as run down with blocked toilets, broken showers, and flooding laundry facilities, and some reviews noting overgrown grounds creating tick/snake concerns, though improvements are reportedly underway.
