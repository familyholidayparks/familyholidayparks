PARK: Ingenia Holidays Rivershore
SCORE: 87/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-22
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 296

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (19/20) — Consistently praised for exceptional kids entertainment including heated pool with waterslides, jumping pillow, go-karts, mini golf, pedal carts, archery, animal encounters, kids discos, movie nights, and scavenger hunts. Families describe it as "cruise-ship" atmosphere with daily activity planners during school holidays. Pool is the standout feature mentioned in majority of reviews.

Nature (16/20) — Beautiful riverfront location with pontoon access for fishing and crabbing, grassy sites with river views, boat ramp, and peaceful natural setting. However, proximity to main road diminishes the natural tranquility. Described as "paradise" by multiple families with stunning sunrise/sunset views over the water.

Cleanliness (14/15) — Overwhelmingly positive comments about clean, modern amenities with multiple toilet/shower blocks kept "spotless" and "immaculate." Some reviews noted inconsistent bathroom cleaning frequency (once daily rather than continuous), occasional maintenance lapses, and declining standards over time according to repeat visitors.

Value (12/15) — Mixed feedback on pricing. Powered sites $87-$200+ per night and glamping tents $400-$580 per night described as "expensive" and "over the top" by several families. However, many felt the extensive included activities, resort facilities, and staff service justified premium pricing. Restaurant food reasonably priced with happy hour specials appreciated.

Site Size (9/10) — Repeatedly described as "huge," "massive," and "extremely spacious" sites that could fit multiple caravans. Even 30ft caravans fit easily. Unpowered riverfront sites particularly praised for size. Some deluxe sites near playground noted as noisier locations.

Sentiment (9/10) — Very high satisfaction with phrases like "absolute dream come true," "heaven on earth," "best park ever," and "couldn't fault a single thing" appearing frequently. Strong repeat visitation with families booking return stays immediately. However, scattered complaints about rude reception staff (specifically named), poor pool slide supervision, and declining standards reduced perfect sentiment score.

Location (8/10) — Centrally located 15 minutes from Big Pineapple, Sea Life aquarium, and beaches. Close to Bli Bli shops (4km) and Maroochydore (7km). Easy highway access for day trips. However, direct road noise is a significant negative mentioned by multiple reviewers. Not walkable to attractions but convenient base camp for exploring Sunshine Coast hinterland.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Road noise from David Low Way is a recurring complaint, with several families reporting traffic noise disrupting sleep and describing it as "extreme" and "destroyed all the beauty" of the riverside location—some even cancelled their stay early due to this issue.

2. Value concerns are significant, with families paying $200-$250 per night for powered sites and $400-$580 for glamping tents questioning whether the experience justifies these premium prices, particularly when compared to other Queensland parks. Weekend surcharges of 15% on food also mentioned negatively.

3. Service inconsistency appears in multiple reviews, with specific staff members described as "extremely rude," "abrupt," and "unwilling to help" at reception. Pool slide supervision noted as inadequate with safety concerns, and bathroom cleaning frequency dropped from continuous to once daily, suggesting operational standards have slipped.
