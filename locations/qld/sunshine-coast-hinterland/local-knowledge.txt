# Sunshine Coast Hinterland: Local Family Know-How

Best time to visit is April to September – you'll dodge the summer heat and humidity that makes the hinterland pretty uncomfortable, plus school holidays get rammed. The wet season (November to March) brings downpours that can close roads and make hiking dodgy, though it's stunning if you don't mind getting wet. If you're hitting the big attractions like Australia Zoo or Dreamworld, go midweek if possible – weekends are absolutely chokas. Pack layers because it genuinely gets cool up here, especially in the morning, even when it's warm down on the coast. The elevation plays tricks on the weather, so bring a light jacket even in summer.

What families actually love doing up here is way beyond the theme parks – the waterfalls at Kondalilla and Mapleton are brilliant for a splash about, the hinterland markets (especially the weekend ones) are genuinely good for locals and tourists alike, and the quiet rainforest walks beat the crowds you get elsewhere. The Eumundi Markets are iconic but can be mental – go early or give them a miss. Real talk: watch out for leeches on the wet rainforest walks (salt or insect repellent kills them instantly), don't underestimate how hilly the drives are if you've got a car-sick kid, and service stations aren't thick on the ground, so fill up in town. Cyclones in the wet season are rare but possible, so check the forecast. Our best local tip? Skip the obvious tourist traps and head to smaller towns like Mapleton or Montville for genuine cafes and views – you'll spend less and the kids won't be miserable in queues.
