PARK: 1770 Camping Ground
SCORE: 68/100
DATE SCORED: 2025-05-17
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 185

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (8/20) — No dedicated kids entertainment, playground, or pool facilities; the onsite Rusty Pelican cafe offers live music some evenings but entertainment is limited to beach activities, fishing, crabbing, and natural play; families noted the absence of typical holiday park amenities like pools and playgrounds.

Nature (20/20) — Exceptional beachfront location with direct beach access, stunning east coast sunsets over calm water, beautiful headland walking tracks with whale watching opportunities, shady trees throughout, clean sandy beach perfect for swimming and water play; multiple reviews praised the "spectacular location," "calm water," and natural setting as world-class.

Cleanliness (10/15) — Mixed feedback with recent reviews noting amenities are "old but clean" while others described facilities as "dirty," "run-down," and "disgraceful"; cleaning occurs daily but infrastructure is dated from the 1960s; some reviewers praised cleanliness while others were disappointed; general consensus is basic but acceptable maintenance.

Value (10/15) — Pricing around $51-78 per night seen as high for the dated facilities offered; many reviews mentioned "poor value," "expensive for what's provided," and better options available; grey water disposal issues and lack of modern infrastructure noted as not matching price point; location saves the value score.

Site Size (5/10) — Consistently criticized as "very small," "cramped," "tight," "packed in," with caravans difficult to maneuver; beachfront sites slightly larger but still challenging; trees and poles create tight turning spaces; multiple reviews warned about compact sites feeling like a "car park" during peak times.

Sentiment (8/10) — Strong repeat visitor base with families returning annually for the location despite limitations; many passionate advocates balanced by disappointed guests; recent management changes noted; families love the beachfront experience but acknowledge compromises; overall positive for location-focused campers.

Location (7/10) — Prime beachfront position is the park's greatest asset with direct beach access and stunning water views; located in 1770 QLD with cafes, restaurants, and marina within walking distance; boat ramp nearby; accessing town amenities requires short drives; some found it remote but most praised the waterfront positioning.

WHY NOT 100:
The park has no children's playground, pool, or organized entertainment facilities that Australian families with young children expect from modern holiday parks. Site sizes are severely constrained with most reviewers describing them as cramped, tight, and difficult to navigate, particularly for larger modern caravans. Amenities and infrastructure are outdated from the 1960s with inconsistent grey water disposal, no dump point onsite, broken fire pits, and facilities that multiple reviewers described as needing urgent upgrades despite daily cleaning efforts.
