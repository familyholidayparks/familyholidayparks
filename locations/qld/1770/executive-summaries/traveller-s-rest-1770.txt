PARK: Traveller's Rest 1770
SCORE: 72/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-20
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 133

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (10/20) — No traditional kids entertainment facilities like playgrounds or waterparks; entertainment comes entirely from nature-based activities including owner Mike's highly-praised bush tucker talks, guided boardwalk tours, evening slide show presentations about local history and wildlife, and self-guided nature walks through the property's extensive bushland trails.

Nature (20/20) — Exceptional natural environment set within bushland with native vegetation; reviews consistently highlight the stunning boardwalk through pristine bush, over 100 bird species sighted on property, kangaroos grazing in mornings, beautiful pond with lilies and lotus flowers, fire trails, and immersive connection with nature that guests describe as "breathtaking" and "one of the most peaceful places we've visited."

Cleanliness (15/15) — Amenities receive universal praise as "spotless," "very clean," "exceptional conditions," and "always lovely and hot showers"; multiple reviews specifically mention facilities are new or fairly new and maintained to an exceptionally high standard with hot water consistently available.

Value (13/15) — Generally described as affordable and good value with pricing around $30-50 per night; reviews note "surprisingly affordable," "great deal," "only $15 per person" for unpowered sites, and pay-3-get-4th-free deals; minor points lost due to two complaints about refund policy during natural disaster and one review calling it expensive at $40 for basic facilities.

Site Size (10/10) — Consistently praised for "very spacious sites," "plenty of room," "lots of space between sites," "big camping areas away from others," with guests noting ability to choose location on large grassed areas and easily fit multiple camper trailers with room to spare.

Sentiment (10/10) — Overwhelmingly positive sentiment with guests describing it as "favorite stop," "highlight of our trip," "felt like home," "little piece of paradise"; extremely high repeat visitation intention with numerous "will definitely be back" and "can't wait to return" statements; family hosts Mike and Michi consistently praised as welcoming, knowledgeable, and passionate.

Location (8/10) — Well-positioned 5-10 minutes drive to Agnes Water and 1770 beaches and town facilities; guests appreciate being close enough for convenience yet "far enough away to feel like you're in the middle of nowhere"; points deducted for road noise from nearby highway mentioned in multiple reviews and limited mobile phone reception.

WHY NOT 100:
[1] No dedicated children's entertainment facilities—no playground equipment, jumping pillow, games room, or pool means families with young children seeking structured activities may find limited options beyond nature-based experiences.
[2] Road noise from the nearby main highway is mentioned repeatedly as a drawback, with several reviews noting traffic noise during the day detracts from the otherwise peaceful bush setting.
[3] Limited infrastructure in some areas—reviews mention no kitchen facilities or BBQ (still being built at time of some visits), no caravan water hookup requiring full tanks on arrival, limited powered sites, and difficult site navigation in the dark with signage issues.
