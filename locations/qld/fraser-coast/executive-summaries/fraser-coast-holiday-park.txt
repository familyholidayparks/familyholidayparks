PARK: Fraser Coast Holiday Park
SCORE: 56/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-04-30
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 58

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (10/20) — Limited on-site kids entertainment with a rec room featuring pool table, arcade machine, board games and TV mentioned positively, but no dedicated playground or structured activities for young children evident in reviews.

Nature (14/20) — Excellent beach proximity at just 50m from the esplanade with multiple reviews praising beach access for walks and fishing, though limited natural bush or creek environment on-site beyond grassy sites with some nice trees.

Cleanliness (8/15) — Highly inconsistent with some praising "very spacious and clean" cabins and "spotless" amenities, while numerous recent reviews report "dog hair all over blankets," "filthy couch," "bed bugs," "gritty floors," safety hazards, and sites "strewn with rubbish."

Value (9/15) — Mixed feedback with some finding it "great value" and "cheap and not nasty," but others reporting $160/night for dog-friendly cabins, "huge variations in prices" ($25 vs $59 for similar sites), and concerns about pricing inconsistencies between quote and actual charge.

Site Size (4/10) — Consistently criticized as "little tight for big rigs," "parking very tight," "extremely small and tight," "small sites" suitable only for small vans, with congestion and difficult access frequently mentioned.

Sentiment (5/10) — Polarized with loyal repeat visitors praising management ("Natalie is amazing"), but significant negative experiences including management disputes, noise complaints, medical episodes from guest conflicts, and strong warnings to "avoid this park."

Location (9/10) — Outstanding location praised universally as "walking distance to beach," "close to shops and cafes," "100m walk to esplanade," "close to everything," and "centrally located" in Hervey Bay.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Severe cleanliness inconsistencies with multiple reports of filthy cabins, bed bugs, dog hair on bedding, broken fixtures, and poorly maintained caravan sites with rubbish and safety hazards.
2. Site sizes are critically tight and unsuitable for larger family rigs, with difficult access, congestion, and multiple warnings about space limitations for caravans over 26ft.
3. Limited kids entertainment infrastructure with only a basic rec room and no dedicated playground, waterpark features, or structured activities for young families.
