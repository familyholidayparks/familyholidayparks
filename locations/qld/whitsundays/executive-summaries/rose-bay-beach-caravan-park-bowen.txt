WHY THIS SCORE:

The entertainment comes from natural activities like beach play, snorkelling, and the pool rather than structured programs.

Nature (19/20) — Outstanding natural environment with "beaches are so close and divine," "Huge coral gardens just off the beach," "great snorkeling just out the front with beautiful reefs," "beach is 2 minutes walk across the road," and access to the "5 km Walk connecting all the famous Beaches." Families praise "stunning views," "panoramic views of the Whitsundays," dog-friendly beaches, and bushwalks to Murray Bay and Horseshoe Bay.

Cleanliness (15/15) — Exceptional cleanliness throughout reviews: "clean upgraded amenities," "amenities are spotless," "always spotlessly clean," "very clean facilities," "Amenities so so clean," "spotless refurbed bathrooms," and "Amenities are really clean." New owners Tony and Bernie consistently praised for maintaining immaculate facilities.

Value (13/15) — Generally positive value sentiment with "good value," "incredible value," "reasonable," and "so affordable." The glamping tent experience described as exceptional value. One review mentions "Expensive" and another notes tight spacing, suggesting some families felt the premium pricing wasn't fully justified by site size.

Positive notes that owners help with placement and "Big vans no problem they will park it for you," but space remains a concern for families.

Sentiment (10/10) — Overwhelmingly positive family sentiment: "We will definitely be back," "Can’t wait to come back," "will happily come back," "We can’t wait to go back," multiple families extended their stays, and numerous 5-star reviews praising the "welcoming, safe space for families." Repeat visitation intentions extremely high.

Location (10/10) — Perfect location scores with "A1 location," "perfect location," "Terrific location," "great location," beach access "2 minutes walk across the road," "stone's throw from the beach," close to town, walking tracks, and "between 2 beautiful bays." Families love the beachside position in the Whitsundays.

WHY NOT 100:
1. No dedicated children's playground or structured kids entertainment facilities—families rely entirely on natural beach/pool activities rather than purpose-built play equipment or programs that young children typically enjoy at holiday parks.
2. Site sizes are significantly cramped with multiple families reporting being "crammed in like sardines," difficulty parking cars, and tight maneuvering for larger vans, which impacts the family camping experience despite helpful owners.
3. Limited on-site organized activities or kids club programming—while the cafe and social gatherings are mentioned, there's no evidence of scheduled family entertainment, kids activities, or holiday programs during peak periods.
