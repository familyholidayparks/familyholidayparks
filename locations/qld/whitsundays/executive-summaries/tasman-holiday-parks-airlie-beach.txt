PARK: Tasman Holiday Parks - Airlie Beach
SCORE: 73/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-24
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 255

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (16/20) — Reviews consistently praised the daily bird feeding at 4pm with rainbow lorikeets as a major highlight for families, plus playground, jumping pillow, and pool facilities. Food trucks visit nightly offering variety. Some noted lack of waterpark or larger entertainment facilities.

Nature (14/20) — The park features tropical gardens with "lush and tropical" landscaping and abundant birdlife including cockatoos and curlews throughout the grounds. Located 2km from Airlie Beach lagoon, requiring bus/car access. Near Whitsundays gateway but not beachfront. Some sites near stagnant creek with mosquitoes.

Cleanliness (10/15) — Mixed reviews with recent decline noted. Multiple 2024-2026 reviews cited "ALL 8 washing machines out of order," ant infestations, cockroaches in cabins, and maintenance issues. Amenities described as "spotless" by some but "untidy" and needing "deep clean" by others. Inconsistent standards across accommodation types.

Value (11/15) — Pricing criticism frequent with $87-300/night rates feeling high given maintenance issues. Dynamic pricing complaints noted. Some found "great value" while others cited "extremely expensive" for quality delivered. Glamping tents rated better value than older cabins.

Site Size (7/10) — Reviews described sites as "spacious" and "big spacious sites" but also "quite small" and "tiny very close to neighbours." Ensuite sites praised for size. Some campers couldn't fit car on site. Variable depending on site type and location within park.

Sentiment (8/10) — Strong positive sentiment from families loving bird feeding and atmosphere, with many stating "will definitely be back." However, significant negative reviews about cleanliness and maintenance issues, with some declaring "will not stay there again." Staff consistently praised as "friendly and helpful."

Location (7/10) — Located 2km from Airlie Beach center, described as "20 minute walk" or "5 minute bus ride for 50 cents." Across from Jubilee Tavern pub. Good for Whitsundays access but not central. Convenient bus stop outside, though some found it "too far from city."

WHY NOT 100:
1. Severe and recurring maintenance issues with washing machines (multiple reviews citing ALL machines out of order for extended periods), broken showers with lukewarm water only, and equipment failures significantly impacting guest experience.

2. Documented pest control problems including ant infestations ("50 ants roaming around counters"), cockroaches in cabins and dishwashers, and flies requiring guests to buy their own insecticides, indicating inadequate cleaning protocols and tropical pest management.

3. Inconsistent cleanliness standards with recent decline noted across multiple 2025-2026 reviews citing dirty cabins, unclean amenities, bins not emptied, and general neglect despite high nightly rates, suggesting management/staffing issues.
