PARK: Discovery Parks - Airlie Beach
SCORE: 78/100
DATE SCORED: 2025
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 182

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (17/20) — Families consistently praised the "fabulous pool/waterpark area" with new splash pad being "the real winner," jumping pillow receiving multiple mentions, bird feeding at 8am daily described as a highlight, games room, playground, and pedal carts. A few reviews noted the old waterpark was "tired" or "rickety" before recent upgrades, and one mentioned lack of shade over baby pool.

Nature (16/20) — Reviews highlighted the "tropical garden" setting with guests feeling "like we were in the rainforest" surrounded by "lush and beautiful" grounds. Bird life including lorikeets, cockatoos, and kookaburras received enthusiastic mentions with morning feeding experiences. Some guests saw water monitors. Nature connection praised though not beachfront location.

Cleanliness (13/15) — Amenities described as "spotless," "very clean," and "clean as a whistle" with "large shower space and bright lighting." Camp kitchens noted as well-maintained. However, several negative reviews mentioned ants in cabins and rooms, cockroaches in drawers, bed bugs in one case, mould smell, and coffee-stained sheets in specific rooms.

Value (12/15) — Mixed feedback with some calling it "best value for money" and "cheap," while others felt it was "quite expensive for what you get" or had "poor price-performance ratio." One guest noted paying only $33/night with 20% discount, while another complained about paying over $200 AUD for smallest cabin. Generally considered reasonable but not exceptional value.

Site Size (7/10) — Multiple complaints about tight sites particularly for larger vans with one reviewer noting their 23' van barely fit a site meant for 19' vans. Some sites described as "very tight" with overgrown hedges reducing space. Grass sites mentioned as uneven. However, many reviews praised "spacious sites" and "plenty of room," suggesting inconsistency across the park.

Sentiment (8/10) — Strong repeat visitation with families saying "will definitely be back," "booked for Easter 2026 while we were there," and "stayed multiple times." Many "highly recommend" comments. However, sentiment damaged by service issues (office closing at 4pm, poor communication during cyclone, rude staff interactions), water supply problems, and several "won't be returning" reviews.

Location (5/10) — Park located 2.6-3.5km from Airlie Beach town centre, requiring bus (50 cents) or car. Bus stop directly at park entrance praised for convenience to tours and town. Close to Jubilee Tavern (short walk). However, multiple reviews noted it's "far away from everything," "not within walking distance," and "a distance to the hub of Airlie Beach" making it less convenient for those without transport.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Location requires bus/car access being 3km from town centre, reducing convenience for families wanting to walk to beach and attractions compared to parks with direct beach access or in-town locations.
2. Site sizing inconsistency with multiple complaints about tight sites for larger vans (19-23'), overgrown hedges, and only one amenities block causing long walks from back sites, particularly problematic for families with young children needing toilet access.
3. Cleanliness issues in specific accommodation units including reported ants, cockroaches, bed bugs, mould smell, and maintenance problems (broken shower doors, poor hot water, WiFi failures) alongside service gaps (office hours, communication failures, water supply disruptions) that detracted from the otherwise clean facilities.
