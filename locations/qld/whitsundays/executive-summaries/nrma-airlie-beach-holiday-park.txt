PARK: NRMA Airlie Beach Holiday Park
SCORE: 62/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-27
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 300

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (11/20) — Reviews mention two swimming pools with the larger lagoon-style pool being a highlight for families, but very limited kids entertainment or activities beyond the pools. No playground, waterpark features, or organized kids activities mentioned consistently. Several reviews note the pool gets families visiting but amenities like water features not working.

Nature (14/20) — Park is located across from a coastal boardwalk with beautiful water views and walking paths to the marina. Reviews praise the "wonderful Arlie beach walkway, board walk" and tropical gardens with native birds including cockatoos. However, it's an urban park next to a shopping centre rather than a bushland setting, and some sites have no shade.

Cleanliness (8/15) — This is a major weakness with numerous reviews citing "dirty amenities," "camp kitchen is bedlam," "toilets and showers were just constantly being used and dirty," "bathroom floors slippery, door handles broken, facilities very run down." Amenities described as needing "more regular cleaning" and "old and dilapidated."

Value (9/15) — Mixed feedback with many calling it "overpriced," "very expensive for what it is," and "way to expensive for that quality." Prices cited at $60-88 per night with complaints about cramped sites and poor facilities not justifying the cost. Some found it reasonable for the location, but overall poor value sentiment dominates.

Site Size (5/10) — Consistently criticized as "packed in like sardines," "tiny sites," "absolutely jammed together," and "ridiculously small and uneven." Unpowered sites described as "a sloped, lumpy bit of grass" and "uneven paddock." Many reviews mention difficulty maneuvering large vans and cars with "very very small sites."

Sentiment (6/10) — Heavily mixed with many stating "won't be back," "worst experience ever," "wouldn't stay here again," versus some saying "will definitely be back" and "highly recommend." Significant number of 1-2 star reviews alongside 5-star reviews. Repeat visitation appears low among disappointed guests.

Location (9/10) — Strong point with reviews praising "next door to Coles," "perfect location," "great location," "5 minute walk to shops," and easy access to Airlie Beach town, marina, and boardwalk. Bus stop outside and 20-30 minute walk to town center. Close to Whitsunday island tour departures.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Severe overcrowding and inadequate amenities for park capacity — only 6-7 showers per gender and 3 washing machines for 150+ sites causing long waits and consistently dirty facilities that cannot be maintained with the volume of guests.

2. Extremely cramped sites especially for caravans — reviews repeatedly describe sites as "tiny," "packed like sardines," "ridiculously small and uneven," making it difficult to maneuver large rigs and park vehicles, with many unpowered sites on sloped, uneven ground.

3. Poor cleanliness standards throughout — camp kitchen described as "filthy," "grotty," and "bedlam," bathrooms "dirty and unmaintained," facilities "run down" with broken equipment, mould, and insufficient cleaning frequency for the number of guests using them.
