PARK: Gunna Go Holiday Park
SCORE: 58/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-04-12
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 50

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (8/20) — Reviews consistently note "not much for kids to do other than swimming pool" with one family highlight being "kids playing in the background" at a special bonfire event. The park offers Friday night sausage sizzles and occasional live entertainment, but lacks playground equipment or structured children's activities. The onsite bar provides adult entertainment but minimal family-focused programming.

Nature (14/20) — Multiple reviewers praise the "nice bushland setting amongst the cane fields" and "beautiful" natural surroundings. Guests enjoyed watching "cane trains in action and even watched cane being harvested." The park backs onto cane fields providing a peaceful rural atmosphere, though it's not near beaches or significant natural attractions beyond the agricultural landscape.

Cleanliness (11/15) — Reviews are mixed, with many praising "spotless amenities" and "very clean and tidy" facilities, while recent 2025 reviews report serious concerns: "toilets to be cleaned everyday but they weren't. No toilet paper for 4 days and bins over flowing" and "amenities were not cleaned daily." Older reviews consistently mention "clean" facilities, suggesting recent deterioration.

Value (10/15) — Guests appreciate "great price" and "great value" with specific mentions of avoiding "high prices in Airlie Beach." However, one guest paying "$140 a night for 2 nights in a cabin was not so impressed" with small room size and maintenance issues. Generally considered good value for basic stopover accommodation.

Site Size (9/10) — Consistently praised as "spacious, much bigger than most parks" with "large" and "level" sites. Multiple reviews mention "sites are a good size" and one guest's "large van fits perfectly." However, one negative review about being given muddy sites instead of concrete pad sites impacts the score.

Sentiment (4/10) — While many 5-star reviews exist, recent negative experiences significantly impact sentiment. Two 1-star reviews from 2025-2026 cite cleanliness failures and poor management decisions. One long-term caravanner stated "last straw we won't be back" after repeated site allocation issues. Positive reviews note extending stays, but concerning recent trends lower overall sentiment.

Location (2/10) — The park's highway location is a significant drawback for families: "on the Bruce Hwy & can get noisy," "can be a bit noisy with highway traffic." It's "about 10 minutes from town Center" and requires an "easy drive to Airlie beach" rather than being at the destination. "Carefull when exiting or entering park" due to highway access concerns.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Severely limited children's entertainment with only a swimming pool and no playground facilities, making it unsuitable for families with young children seeking active holiday experiences.
2. Recent significant cleanliness deterioration with reports of daily cleaning not occurring, no toilet paper for 4 days, overflowing bins, and inconsistent maintenance standards that contradict earlier positive reviews.
3. Poor location for family holidays being on a busy highway (noisy traffic and safety concerns entering/exiting) and requiring drives to reach actual Whitsundays attractions rather than being at a destination families want to be.
