PARK: Proserpine Holiday Park
SCORE: 72/100
DATE SCORED: 2024-01-12
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 104

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (16/20) — Reviews consistently highlight the FREE Olympic-sized heated pool and kids' water park next door as a major drawcard, with families specifically mentioning "waterpark next door was a huge hit" and "amazing pool for the kids." However, no on-site playground or organized kids' activities are mentioned, with entertainment limited to the external pool facility and golf course walking distance away.

Nature (12/20) — The park features "plenty of shady trees," grassed sites, and a "lush and well kept" environment, though multiple reviews note it's in a residential area rather than natural bushland. Access to the Whitsunday Coast and nearby attractions is convenient, but the park itself is not in a natural setting and requires driving to beaches and nature experiences.

Cleanliness (15/15) — Exceptional performance with reviews repeatedly stating "cleanest amenities I have ever seen," "immaculate facilities," "spotless," and "the cleanest bathrooms in all our travels around Australia." This was the most consistently praised aspect across recent reviews.

Value (14/15) — Excellent value with specific pricing mentioned: "$34-46 per night," "super cheap," "not overpriced," and "great value for money." Families specifically note it as a budget alternative to expensive Airlie Beach parks while maintaining high standards.

Site Size (9/10) — Very positive feedback on "big sites," "plenty of space," "large level sites," and "spacious" powered and unpowered areas. Sites described as "reasonably sized and separate from other sites" with hedges providing privacy.

Sentiment (9/10) — Overwhelmingly positive sentiment with numerous mentions of returning, extended stays beyond original bookings, and high recommendations. A few isolated negative experiences mentioned regarding management interactions and long-term residents impacting aesthetics.

Location (7/10) — Located in quiet Proserpine, "950m walk to town," "20 minutes to Airlie Beach," and "away from the highway." While peaceful and convenient for Whitsunday access, families note "need to drive to shops" and it's not a beachfront or resort location.

WHY NOT 100:
The park lacks any on-site children's entertainment facilities beyond the adjacent public pool - no playground, jumping pillow, or organized kids' activities are mentioned. The location requires driving to beaches, major shops, and Whitsunday attractions, making it more of a stopover base than a destination park. Some reviews mention dated facilities, permanent residents impacting aesthetics, and occasional management conflicts that detract from the otherwise excellent family experience.

NOTABLE REVIEW PHRASES:
