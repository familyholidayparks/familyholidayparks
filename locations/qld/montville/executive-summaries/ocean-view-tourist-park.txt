PARK: Ocean View Tourist Park
SCORE: 48/100
DATE SCORED: 2024-01-15
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 34

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (2/20) — No kids entertainment, playground, or recreational facilities mentioned in any reviews. Park appears to have only basic amenities with no organized activities or family entertainment options.
Nature (14/20) — Guests enjoyed views of kookaburras and yellow tail black cockatoos, with mentions of "amazing views" and peaceful surroundings in the Glass House Mountains area. However, the "ocean view" name is misleading ("if you squint you can see water") as it's 20km from the coast. Natural environment is pleasant but limited.
Cleanliness (11/15) — Mixed feedback with many praising "spotlessly clean" amenities and "excellent amenities very clean," but recent reviews note "drains are blocked," "facilities clean, basic and old," and "only a few lights in ladies toilets that work." Maintenance appears inconsistent.
Value (6/15) — Major concern across reviews: "very expensive for basic camp ground," "$156 for 3nights is ludicrous," "$225pn overpriced," "$50 dollar; langs een drukke weg," and multiple complaints that pricing doesn't match the dated facilities offered.
Site Size (6/10) — Several mentions of "bit tight for parking up," "ground small," and guests placed closely together ("put us next to the only three other caravans"). Limited privacy and space for families with multiple vehicles or larger rigs.
Sentiment (5/10) — Divided reviews with positive comments from short-stay guests but significant complaints about value, facilities, and suitability. Several strongly negative reviews ("DOE HET NIET!", "Don't camp here") balanced by those finding it adequate for overnight stays. Limited repeat visitation signals for families.
Location (4/10) — Convenient for Australia Zoo visits ("very close by") and Glass House Mountains exploration, but located "by the busy road" with road noise, 20km from actual ocean, and reception closes at 1pm. Limited nearby family amenities.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Complete absence of family entertainment facilities - no playground, pool, kids club, or activities for children, making it unsuitable as a family holiday destination.
2. Significantly overpriced for what's offered - multiple reviews cite expensive rates ($50-75/night for powered sites, $225/night cabins) that don't reflect the dated, basic facilities and lack of amenities.
3. Dated infrastructure requiring major updates - facilities described as "time capsule from the 80s," with blocked drains, poor lighting, locked camp kitchen, inadequate shower facilities, and maintenance issues throughout.
