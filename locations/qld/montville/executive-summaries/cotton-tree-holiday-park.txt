PARK: Cotton Tree Holiday Park
SCORE: 71/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-14
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 246

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (8/20) — Reviews consistently note "no pool or other entertainment" and describe the park as having "not much to offer except a beach" with "no play grounds" or organized kids activities, though food trucks occasionally visit on weekends.

Nature (19/20) — Families rave about the "best of both worlds, access to the Maroochydore river and the ocean" with "calm inlet on one side, surf patrolled beach on the other" providing "crystal clear calm waterways great for kids" and "easy swim for the kids" with "beautiful beach sunrises."

Cleanliness (13/15) — Recent reviews frequently mention "amenities spotless," "very clean," "impeccably clean," and "cleanest caravan park toilet I ever been," though some peak period reports note toilets "beyond filth" and amenities "gross" when overcrowded with insufficient cleaning frequency.

Value (8/15) — Families repeatedly complain about pricing: "$76-$84 per night very expensive with no pool or other entertainment," "$80 a night is outrageous," and "overpriced" is a common theme, with many noting other parks offer pools and facilities for less money.

Site Size (5/10) — The most consistent complaint across reviews: "sites are really small & close together," "packed in like sardines," "crammed in tight," "way too small," "very tight sites," with numerous families struggling to fit vans and experiencing difficulty maneuvering into cramped spaces.

Sentiment (8/10) — Reviews are polarized: loyal repeat visitors praise it as "our favorite park" and "home away from home," while many recent visitors state "will never return" due to rigid management, site issues, and poor customer service, particularly since ownership changes.

Location (10/10) — Universal praise for location: "perfect location," "prime location," "amazing location right by the inlet," "walking distance to shops, cafes, surf club," "everything is close and handy," positioned directly between river and ocean with Cotton Tree village across the road.

WHY NOT 100:
Sites are severely undersized for modern caravans with families reporting 20+ minute struggles to squeeze into allocated spaces, cars unable to fit on sites, and vans extending into neighboring areas, creating safety concerns and stress. Customer service has deteriorated significantly under current management with multiple families reporting "incredibly rude, abusive, condescending" staff, inflexible policies even for medical emergencies or extreme weather, unanswered phones and emails, and unauthorized credit card charges. The park commands premium waterfront pricing ($76-84/night) but provides no pool, playground, or entertainment facilities that families expect at this price point, especially compared to similarly-priced Big4 parks with comprehensive amenities.
