PARK: Kookaburra Park
SCORE: 87/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-25
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 136

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (19/20) — Exceptional animal experiences dominate reviews with daily guinea pig cuddles and deer feeding described as 'magical' and 'phenomenal'; Ranger Steve and family praised extensively for creating engaging, educational interactions; regular movie nights, disco events, Jurassic Trail hike, fairy garden, and basketball/volleyball areas provide diverse activities; multiple families report children 'never wanted to leave' and made friendships with ranger's children.

Nature (17/20) — Beautiful hinterland setting with 'stunning scenery everywhere' and mountain views; Mary River access for kayaking and platypus spotting; proximity to Booloumba Creek (10 min) for swimming; river walk and Jurassic Trail with spectacular lookout views; abundant wildlife including wallabies, kookaburras, and birdlife; described as 'peaceful valley surrounded by mountains' with fresh country air and dark skies for stargazing.

Cleanliness (14/15) — Facilities consistently praised as 'some of the cleanest camping grounds we've stayed at' with hot showers and clean toilets; camp kitchen and amenities 'very well kept' and 'impeccable'; grounds well-maintained with mowed sites and tidy trails; one older amenities block noted as needing replacement (scheduled for demolition); occasional mention of overgrown areas but overall property described as 'incredibly well maintained'.

Value (12/15) — Reviews note pricing is 'on the expensive side' with day visit costing $35 for family causing one negative review; most families feel facilities and experiences justify costs with 'reasonable and fair' fees reflecting service quality; free animal feeding appreciated; coffee van and shop on-site convenience valued; no major value complaints from overnight guests but not described as budget-friendly.

Site Size (9/10) — Sites consistently described as 'extremely generous', 'large', and 'well spaced out' so families 'don't feel on top of other campers'; multiple site options from powered to off-grid; specific sites like Pine Tree Hill, Flat Hill, and Damside praised for space and views; campers note 'plenty of space' even when fully booked; sites accommodate families comfortably with room for gazebos and outdoor activities.

Sentiment (9/10) — Overwhelmingly positive with families using superlatives like 'best camping experience ever', 'magical', and 'heaven'; extremely high repeat visitation with many annual returnees and multi-visit families; strong recommendations throughout with 'highly recommend' appearing in majority of reviews; staff particularly Steve/George/Oakley receive exceptional praise; handful of negative reviews regarding group bookings noise incident in 2023 and one day-visitor complaint prevent perfect score.

Location (7/10) — Kenilworth township 8-10 minutes away with famous bakery, dairy, and playground; Booloumba Creek 10 minutes for swimming; Little Yabba Creek 3-5 minutes; Noosa Beach approximately one hour away; Montville hinterland location provides peaceful setting but requires driving for most attractions; no mobile reception on-site (Vodafone) but available in town; not directly beach-accessible but well-positioned for hinterland exploration.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Value perception issues with pricing noted as 'expensive side' and one very negative day-visitor review regarding $35 fee for limited animal viewing without clear communication about feeding times
2. Maintenance inconsistencies with Jurassic Trail occasionally 'badly overgrown' and impassable, one older amenities block with blocked toilets/flooding issues (though scheduled for replacement), and some grounds noted as 'alittle over grown' with concerns about ticks
3. July 2023 group booking incident resulted in multiple 1-star reviews citing poor communication, immature management response via social media posts, and issues with noise complaint handling that damaged long-term customer relationships
