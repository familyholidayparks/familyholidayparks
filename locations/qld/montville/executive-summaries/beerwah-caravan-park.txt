PARK: Beerwah Caravan Park
SCORE: 62/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-24
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 108

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (4/20) — Reviews explicitly note "no special offers for kids" and confirm absence of pool, playground, jumping pillow, or any on-site entertainment. One German review specifically warned there are no facilities for children. Multiple reviews describe it as quiet and relaxing but with zero kids' activities.
Nature (14/20) — Park situated amongst working strawberry fields with views of Glass House Mountains nearby. Reviews mention rural agricultural setting with birds, herb gardens, and peaceful natural surroundings. Close proximity to hiking in Glass House Mountains and natural attractions, though not directly bushland-adjacent.
Cleanliness (15/15) — Overwhelming consensus across 100+ reviews: "cleanest amenities in any caravan park," "spotlessly clean," "immaculate," cleaned daily at 7am. Facilities described as "impeccable" with consistent praise for toilet, shower, kitchen, and laundry cleanliness standards.
Value (11/15) — Mixed feedback on value. Most paid $50/night for powered sites with some noting "good value" and free night per week for longer stays. However, several reviews called it "overpriced" for the location and lack of facilities, with one comparing unfavorably to nearby Ingenia at $36/night.
Site Size (9/10) — Reviews consistently mention "spacious sites," "large sites" (10m x 13m), concrete pads, adequate space between vans, and easy accommodation of 31ft motorhomes. Power and water readily accessible without needing extension cords.
Sentiment (7/10) — Highly polarized. Majority love the cleanliness and hosts (Fay and Peter praised extensively), with many saying "will definitely stay again." However, concerning number of 1-star reviews citing rude management, confrontational interactions, and issues with permanent residents. Family travellers less enthusiastic.
Location (2/10) — Repeatedly described as "far from everything," "middle of nowhere," "a bit out of the way," 7km off highway. Main attraction is proximity to Australia Zoo (8-10 minutes drive). Town of Beerwah 5 minutes away for shops. Not near beaches or major family attractions beyond the zoo.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Complete absence of family entertainment infrastructure (no pool, no playground, no kids' activities) makes it unsuitable for families with young children seeking holiday entertainment, losing all entertainment points.
2. Remote location "far from everything" with limited nearby family attractions beyond Australia Zoo, and 30+ minutes from beaches, significantly limiting location appeal for family holidaymakers.
3. Multiple concerning reviews about management being "rude," "bossy," and confrontational (shower time restrictions enforced abruptly, defensive behavior), plus issues with permanent residents speaking poorly to guests, creating inconsistent hospitality experience despite many positive reviews about Fay and Peter.
