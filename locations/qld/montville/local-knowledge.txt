# Visiting Montville with Kids

Best time to visit is April to October when it's cooler and you avoid the summer humidity and school holidays madness. The village itself is pretty quiet midweek, which is gold if you've got little ones – you can actually move through the galleries and shops without fighting crowds. Spring (September-October) is stunning with wildflowers everywhere. Skip December-January unless you don't mind queues and paying premium prices; it's packed with Brisbane families escaping the heat. Fair warning: the mountain weather's unpredictable, so bring layers – you can be in sunshine one minute and fog the next.

Families absolutely love the walking tracks around here, especially the Kondalilla Falls walk, but start early because it gets packed by mid-morning. The local playground near the village is solid, and kids genuinely enjoy wandering through the quirky shops and grabbing homemade ice cream. Most cafés are pretty family-friendly, though some of the fancier spots get precious about noise levels. Pro tip: park in the main car park and explore on foot rather than driving around looking for spots – saves heaps of stress. Bring cash to some places; not everywhere takes cards.

Watch out for the steep, winding roads getting here – they're not fun in heavy rain or fog, and if your kids get carsick, take the drive slowly. The village can feel a bit pricey for families, so budget accordingly; a basic lunch for two adults and two kids will easily hit $80-100. Also, some of the "family-friendly" attractions listed online are actually pretty basic – do your homework before committing. The real magic here is the natural beauty and village vibe, not structured attractions. Come for the walks, the fresh mountain air, and a bit of a slow weekend, not expecting theme park thrills.
