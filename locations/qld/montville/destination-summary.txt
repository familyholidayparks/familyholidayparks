Montville's holiday parks occupy a genuinely unique position on the Sunshine Coast—they're perched in the hinterland where you get proper mountain scenery without sacrificing beach access. What sets this area apart is the mix of park types: you've got your bush settings like Kookaburra Park tucked into native vegetation, the beachfront options at Alex Beach and Cotton Tree that sit right on the water, and the more resort-style facilities at BIG4 Caloundra. Most visitors don't realise Montville itself is actually inland, but the parks in this broader area pull in both the hinterland crowd wanting rainforest walks and river time, and the beach-focused families who'd rather not be stuck in the holiday crowds at Mooloolaba. The variety means repeat visitors can try different parks depending on what they're after—whether that's lazy river afternoons or direct beach access.

The parks here genuinely split into two visitor camps: you've got caravan and motorhome families who've worked out the logistics of the area and keep coming back, and holiday-home seekers who book cabins at places like Alex Beach for school holidays. The caravan crowd gravitates toward Kookaburra and BIG4 because the facilities are solid and the setup suits longer stays or seasonal migration patterns. Families with kids tend to book school holidays well in advance—Easter, July, and Christmas fill up fast—and they return because they've found parks where their kids actually settle into a routine rather than needing constant entertainment. The river access at Cotton Tree and Coolum Beach is genuinely useful for families, and the proximity to walks, swimming holes, and less-crowded beaches than the main tourist strips keeps people loyal.

What drives the year-round appeal is that Montville area parks sit in a sweet spot for accessibility. You're 20 minutes from proper beachfront without being trapped in the tourist infrastructure of the main strips, there's genuine bushland for walks, and the river systems actually offer something beyond just looking pretty. The local knowledge repeaters develop matters more here than at mega-parks—knowing which parks suit your van size, which have the quietest spots, when the schools empty out and when you can actually get peace and quiet. BIG4 Caloundra pulls the resort-facility crowd who want water features and activities on-site, while the smaller parks attract people who've decided they want less noise and more actual nature access. That combination of repeat-visitor loyalty and the genuine variety on offer keeps these parks consistently booked, especially when families realise they can do beach days, hinterland drives, and river swims without moving parks.
