# Visiting Townsville with Kids: Local Parent Wisdom

**Best time to visit is May to September** – you'll dodge the brutal heat and humidity that makes December to February absolutely unbearable. Winter's when locals actually leave their houses, and the beaches are properly swimmable without worrying about stingers (box jellyfish). School holidays get packed, so if you can avoid July, do it. The Townsville Show (August) is worth timing your trip around if the kids are into that sort of thing. Water temp sits at a perfect 24-26°C during these months – ideal for Castle Hill swimming holes and Strand beach without wetsuits.

**The must-dos that actually live up to the hype:** Reef HQ is genuinely brilliant and eats up a solid half-day – the kids will lose their minds at the touch pools. Get there early to beat crowds. Billabong Sanctuary is ace for seeing crocs and native wildlife up close, and the Strand has a proper free playground that keeps them occupied for hours. Pro tip – grab barramundi and chips from one of the local spots near the water and eat right there; saves time and cash. Skip the tourist traps in the CBD and head to The Strand precinct instead – better food, better vibes, and locals actually hang out there.

**Watch out for:** Stinger suits are non-negotiable October to May; your kids need them even in the "safe" season. The sun here is absolutely ruthless – SPF 50+ and reapply constantly. Traffic during school drop-off (7:45-8:15am) is a nightmare, and parking at popular spots fills up quick on weekends. Don't underestimate the heat inland – stick to water activities midday. One last thing: crocs are real, saltwater crocs exist here, so don't let kids dangle hands off jetties or get too brave near waterways. Freshwater swimming holes are your friend – they're croc-free and genuinely lovely.
