PARK: Bluewater Caravan Park
SCORE: 42/100
DATE SCORED: 2024-01-15
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 43

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (2/20) — No playground, jumping pillow, or organized kids activities mentioned across any reviews. One September 2023 review noted "there is a small pool" but no other family entertainment infrastructure was identified. This is clearly a transit park rather than a destination for families with young children.

Nature (8/20) — Reviews describe the park as a "large flat paddock area" and "55 acres" of grassed sites, though recent reviews note the park is "overgrown and neglected." Limited natural environment features with no mention of beach access, bush walks, or nature-based activities. Highway location reduces natural appeal.

Cleanliness (8/15) — Mixed feedback with recent reviews polarized. Positive comments include "Clean amenities", "very clean facilities", and "Super clean amenities" from 2023-2025. However, concerning recent reviews from February 2026 cite "camp kitchen was filthy and bbq unusable" and "overgrown" sites. Amenities described as "older but clean" consistently.

Value (9/15) — Strong value proposition with prices from $37.80-$42 per night noted as "Great value", "Inexpensive", and "reasonable price." However, some recent reviews question value due to maintenance issues, with one stating "not good value for the money" and another saying owners "need to upgrade park to justify $42.00 per night."

Site Size (9/10) — Consistently praised across all review periods: "huge sites", "So much room", "large pitches", "spacious camping bays", "sites are big enough for buses", and "Plenty of space." This is the park's strongest feature with drive-through concrete pad sites.

Sentiment (3/10) — Overall sentiment is mixed to negative for families. Only one review explicitly mentioned children: "Kids and I enjoyed our time" (May 2025). Multiple reviews recommend it only as an "overnight stop" rather than destination. Recent reviews show declining satisfaction with management issues and maintenance concerns. Police incident mentioned in one review.

Location (3/10) — Located "right on the highway" north of Townsville with "fair bit of highway traffic noise" consistently noted. Positive aspects include being "close to Townsville" (15-20 minutes), "easy to get in and out of", and near medical centre and shops. However, described as transit location rather than tourist destination with no attraction proximity mentioned.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Zero family entertainment infrastructure - no playground, kids club, jumping pillow, or organized activities for children across 43 reviews spanning 3 years, making it unsuitable as a family holiday destination.
2. Severe maintenance deterioration with recent reviews citing "roads are a disgrace, potholes and washed away", "overgrown and neglected", "filthy camp kitchen", and "ankle deep water" access issues during rain.
3. Park functions as permanent resident facility with transit sites rather than family holiday park - multiple reviews note it's "geared around permanent tenants" with "ramshackle shacks" and "semi-permanent resident sites" dominating the landscape.
