PARK: BIG4 Tweed Billabong Holiday Park
SCORE: 87/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-26
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 285

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (19/20) — Reviews overwhelmingly praise the "massive waterpark," "multiple waterslides," heated pool, jumping pillow, kids club with cooking/craft activities, go-karts, kayaks, paddle boards, mini golf, pickleball court, and fishing in the billabong. Families report "endless activities," children "never had a dull moment," and "you don't need to leave the park." The waterpark alone is described as "incredible" and "resort-style" with families spending entire days there. Daily kids activities, food trucks, live music, and movie nights create constant entertainment options.

Nature (16/20) — The natural billabong is the standout feature where families swim, fish, kayak, spot stingrays and barracudas, and camp on waterfront sites with "dreamiest north-facing views." The park is described as "beautifully kept," "green," "secluded with all the trees," and "parklike tropical grounds." However, it's not beachside (12 minutes to Coolangatta Beach) and is positioned between highway and airport, limiting the purely natural environment feel despite the lovely billabong setting.

Cleanliness (14/15) — Reviews consistently describe facilities as "spotless," "immaculate," "very clean," "well maintained," with "modern amenities" and "always clean" bathrooms featuring "rain shower heads" and "fans and music." The "award-winning camp kitchen" is repeatedly praised as the "best we've ever seen." Minor points lost for occasional mentions of toilets not being cleaned frequently enough during peak periods and one negative review about an older cabin condition.

Value (13/15) — Activity pricing is praised as "excellent value" with equipment hire at just $10/hour for kayaks, go-karts, and paddle boards. Cabin accommodation receives mixed feedback - many note "fantastic value" and "budget friendly" while others cite "extremely overpriced" at $150-330/night during peak periods. The "stay 4 pay 3" deals are appreciated. Overall, families feel the extensive facilities justify costs, though some feel pricing is steep for powered sites in school holidays.

Site Size (8/10) — Powered caravan sites are described as "standard size," "generously sized," "decent size," and "plenty of room" with some "not packed in like sardines." Billabong sites are particularly spacious. However, multiple reviews note sites are "on the smaller side" and some found them "tight to get into" with 8.5m rigs. Cabins are consistently praised as "spacious," "large," and "well appointed" with 2-3 bedrooms perfect for families.

Sentiment (10/10) — Sentiment is overwhelmingly positive with families stating "can't wait to come back," "booked return visit immediately," "return every year," "kids cried leaving," and "best park we've ever stayed at." Staff receive exceptional praise as "the friendliest," "incredible," "warm and welcoming," going "above and beyond." The repeat visitation rate mentioned in reviews is extremely high, with families making this their regular holiday destination.

Location (7/10) — The park is "12 minutes from Coolangatta Beach," "10 minutes to shops," "close to Gold Coast," and "central location to all activities" making day trips convenient. However, being directly beside the M1 freeway and under the airport flight path (planes 6am-11pm) significantly impacts the location score. Reviews repeatedly mention "highway noise," "plane noise," and "noisey" as the primary location drawback, though many note "you get used to it."

WHY NOT 100:
1. Constant noise pollution from M1 freeway and Gold Coast Airport flight path (planes every 30 minutes, 6am-11pm) is mentioned in approximately 30% of reviews as a significant detractor, with some families unable to sleep and choosing not to return specifically due to noise levels.

2. Not beachside location - the park is 12 minutes drive from beaches, which disappointed families expecting coastal access, and the surrounding area has limited walking distance amenities, requiring vehicle transport for most attractions.

3. Inconsistent cabin quality with older units (Kirra cabins) showing wear including ripped sheets, smelly blankets, cockroaches, poor condition furniture, and uncomfortable beds, while newer cabins (Duranbah, Fingal) are excellent - creating an uneven guest experience depending on accommodation type allocated.
