PARK: Noosa River Holiday Park
SCORE: 68/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-25
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 244

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (8/20) — Reviews consistently note extremely limited on-site children's entertainment, with multiple families reporting "broken playground access" and "nothing offered for kids." Families seeking activities were disappointed, with one PE teacher describing management as unwelcoming to children playing outdoors. The park appears to cater to an older demographic with minimal facilities for active families.

Nature (18/20) — Exceptional natural environment praised throughout reviews. "Beautiful white sandy river," "stunning views of sunrises and sunsets," and "right on the river where we swam" feature prominently. Direct waterfront access, pelicans, safe swimming areas, and proximity to Noosa National Park create outstanding natural experiences. Location beside Noosa River consistently described as "breathtaking" and "one of the best located caravan parks in Queensland."

Cleanliness (10/15) — Significant and consistent concerns emerge in recent reviews. "Bathrooms were not cleaned," "camp kitchen was filthy," "human waste left in toilets," and "tables disgustingly sticky" appear repeatedly. Multiple long-term visitors note dramatic decline "since new management has taken over." Some positive mentions of "super clean" and "clean amenities" exist, but negative trend dominates recent 2024-2026 feedback.

Value (11/15) — Mixed feedback with pricing concerns. "$60/night for unpowered, which I thought was quite pricey" and "$67 a night for an unpowered tent site" compared unfavorably to alternatives. Several reviews mention "not worth it" or "overpriced for what you get." However, some note "reasonably priced" and "well priced so close to hasting st." Value perception heavily dependent on site allocation and experience quality.

Site Size (8/10) — Generally positive for caravans with "large sites," "double slab sites," and "oversized well maintained caravan sites with plenty of room." However, tent sites receive criticism: "camping tent spots are too small," "no bigger than a disabled car spot," and waterfront tent sites described as cramped. Powered sites have adequate space, but unpowered tent areas significantly compromise the score.

Sentiment (8/10) — Deeply polarized feedback creates moderate sentiment score. Loyal repeat visitors express love for the location: "been coming for 15 years," "stay there a couple of times a year," "we love it and return and return." However, recent reviews show concerning dissatisfaction: "will never return," "ruined our stay," "worst park we've ever stayed at." Management change appears to have impacted long-term guest relationships significantly.

Location (10/10) — Universally praised location scores maximum points. "Perfect location," "fantastic location right on the river," "close to shops, water, restaurants," and "walking distance to Noosa Heads" appear consistently. Easy access to Hastings Street, Noosaville cafes, ferry connections, and 50c bus service create exceptional convenience. Location repeatedly described as the park's strongest attribute, compensating for other shortcomings.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Cleanliness and maintenance have deteriorated significantly under new management, with multiple families reporting human waste in toilets, filthy camp kitchens with sticky surfaces, broken equipment left unrepaired, and insufficient cleaning frequency during peak periods—creating hygiene concerns that are unacceptable for families with young children.

2. The park demonstrates a strongly unwelcoming attitude toward families with children, with multiple reviews describing threats of expulsion for normal childhood play, staff getting "mad at our kids for riding bikes," management creating a "silent retirement village" atmosphere rather than family-friendly environment, and complete absence of children's entertainment or play facilities.

3. Customer service receives consistently poor reviews with "rude," "dismissive," and "officious" staff behavior reported across multiple years, particularly at reception where families describe being blamed for mistakes, refused refunds, treated with condescension, and receiving eye rolls—with management described as "extremely rude" and "entirely dismissive of concerns."
