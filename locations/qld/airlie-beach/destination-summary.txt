Airlie Beach is the gateway to the Whitsundays, and that's what sets the holiday parks here apart from anywhere else in Queensland. You've got Discovery Parks, Tasman, and NRMA all competing for the same patch of paradise, and they know it—the standard of facilities here is genuinely high because these parks understand what people are after. Most visitors are either serious caravaners who've towed up the coast and want a solid base for a week or two, or families flying in and renting a cabin for school holidays. What keeps them coming back is the access. From these parks, you're minutes from the patrolled swimming beach, the esplanade is walkable from most sites, and the river foreshore is right there if you want to dodge the crowds. The Whitsunday Islands day trips depart from here, and that proximity matters—you're not driving an hour to get to what you came for.

The parks themselves offer different flavours depending on what you need. Discovery Parks sits on the riverside with good shaded sites and solid amenities, Tasman is beachfront-adjacent with a more resort-style feel and better for families wanting a bit of pampering, and NRMA takes the bush approach with more spacious sites if you're after breathing room. All three have mix of powered caravan and motorhome sites plus family cabins, so whether you're rolling up in a van or looking for a self-contained unit, you'll find what suits. The riverside and beachfront locations are the big drawcard—you get that tropical feel without being hemmed in by trees, and the cooling breeze matters when you're here in summer.

School holidays are absolute carnage here, and if you're planning to visit then, you need to book months ahead. Easter, July, and especially the December-January stretch fill up completely. But the shoulder seasons—May, September, October—are when the smart repeaters come. The families who book year after year have worked out that Airlie delivers what they need: good facilities, real beach and water access, and proximity to activities that actually engage kids. Parents appreciate the safe swimming and the fact they can walk to the esplanade for dinner. It's become a destination where three generations of the same family have their favourite park and their favourite sites within it, and they book the same time every year without even thinking about it.
