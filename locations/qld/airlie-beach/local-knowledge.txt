# Visiting Airlie Beach with Kids: Local Knowledge

**Best times and what to expect:** October to April is stinking hot and humid with cyclone season looming, so avoid November to March if you can – it's not just the heat, it's the stingers in the water that'll ruin your beach days. April to September is your sweet spot: warm enough for swimming, manageable humidity, and stinger season is done. School holidays get absolutely packed, especially the winter break, so accommodation fills up fast and prices spike. If you can dodge those weeks, you'll have a much better time and actually find parking.

**What families actually enjoy and what catches people out:** The Lagoon is brilliant for little kids – it's patrolled, shallow, and stinger-free year-round, so forget the drama of trying to work out beach safety. The Whitsunday Islands are spectacular but book those cruises early; the cheaper operators get packed and the boat ride can be rough on queasy stomachs. Plenty of families think they'll just rock up and head out, then get caught by weather or rough seas. Here's the insider tip: grab fish and chips from Airlie Takeaway (locals rate it), pack a proper hat and sunscreen, and plan beach days early because the wind picks up by arvo. Watch out for the drop-off on the main beach – it catches families out. Most importantly, check the stinger forecast even outside season; we've had random box jellyfish pop up when they shouldn't, and nobody wants that drama on their holiday.
