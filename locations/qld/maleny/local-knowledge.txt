# Visiting Maleny with the Family

**Best time to visit is May through September** – you'll dodge the summer heat and humidity that makes the Hinterland pretty uncomfortable with kids in tow. School holidays are absolutely rammed, so if you can swing it outside those periods, you'll have a much better time at the attractions. Watch out for winter fog on the Blackall Range Road; it rolls in thick and fast, so take it slow if you're driving in the early mornings. The waterfalls are best after decent rainfall, so check conditions before heading out to Kondalilla or Baroon Pocket – they can be pretty dry mid-summer.

**Locals know to grab coffee and brekkie at the main street cafes early** – places like Tiny Wheels or the local spots fill up quick once the day-trippers arrive. The Maleny Botanic Gardens and Bird World are genuinely good for families, but book ahead during peak times. Hit the farmers market on Saturday mornings if you're around – it's where locals actually hang out, not just tourists. Keep an eye on your kids around the lookout areas; the views are stunning but the drops are real, and the wind can come up suddenly.

**Bring bug spray and don't underestimate the sun** – the elevation tricks people into thinking it's cooler than it is, and you'll fry if you're not careful. The roads around here are winding and narrow, so don't speed and watch for other vehicles taking corners wide. Petrol up in town before heading out to the national parks; there's no service stations beyond the main area. Finally, embrace the slower pace – this place is about wandering gardens, spotting wildlife, and enjoying fresh air, not rushing between attractions.
