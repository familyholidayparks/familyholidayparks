# Rainbow Beach Local Tips for Families

Look, the best time to visit is April to May or September to October – you'll dodge the school holidays madness and the summer heat's at its worst. Winter's fine if you don't mind it being a bit cooler, but the water's chilly and stingers aren't an issue, so that's a plus. Avoid December through February unless you've got a high tolerance for crowds and humidity that'll make you sweat just walking to the shops. Pro tip: come mid-week if you can – the weekends get rammed and parking near the beach becomes a nightmare.

Kids absolutely lose their minds for the coloured sand cliffs – fair dinkum, they'll want to climb them for hours, and honestly, it's a free afternoon sorted. The rock pools at low tide are brilliant for spotting fish and crabs, way better than paying for aquariums. But here's what'll catch you out: the currents here are no joke, especially near the rocks, so stick to the patrolled areas and watch the kids like a hawk in the water. The sand flies can be absolutely feral at dawn and dusk, so slap on the insect repellent or you'll regret it. Also, bring plenty of water and sunscreen – the sun bounces off those sand cliffs something fierce.

One insider thing most families miss: hit up Double Island Point at low tide if the kids are keen hikers – it's stunning and way quieter than the main beach. The local fish and chip shop near the tavern does decent brekkie too, saves you cooking. And don't bother trying to park right by the beach during peak times; use the carpark further back and enjoy the walk. Check the tide times before you head out – low tide's when the beach is at its best and safest for little ones.
