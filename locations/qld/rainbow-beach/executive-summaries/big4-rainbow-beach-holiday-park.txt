PARK: BIG4 Rainbow Beach Holiday Park
SCORE: 68/100
DATE SCORED: 2025-05-24
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 259

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (12/20) — Reviews frequently mention jumping pillow and playground for toddlers/young kids, but games room described as "run down," "outdated," with "lots of machines not working." No pool is major negative repeatedly cited. Go-carts available for hire, Thai food trucks visit regularly. Limited organized activities for kids noted.

Nature (16/20) — Park sits directly on waterfront with "gorgeous sunsets over Tin Can Bay strait" and "beautiful views." Spacious, bushy landscape with trees providing shade. Major drawback: no beach access from park, must drive 3-4km to actual Rainbow Beach. Water access is via boat ramp/jetty only, described as "mangrove swampy area not suitable for swimming."

Cleanliness (10/15) — Highly inconsistent across reviews. Many praise "spotless" and "immaculate" facilities kept "100% clean & tidy all day," but significant number cite "filthy amenities," "disgusting" bathrooms, mould in shower blocks, and "dirty floors." Standards appear to have declined with management changes. Daily cleaning mentioned but execution varies.

Value (10/15) — Mixed sentiment with many calling it "overpriced" ($220-245/night cabins, $54 unpowered sites). Phrases like "not worth the money," "way over priced," and "expensive for facilities offered" common. Positive reviews note "good value" and "fair price" for spacious sites. Inflexible cancellation policies criticized heavily, even for compassionate circumstances.

Site Size (9/10) — Consistently praised as "huge," "massive," "enormous," with families noting "could put 2 vans on our site." Reviewers love that "you don't want to be camped on top of each other" and sites are "not squished in." Unpowered areas particularly spacious with tree coverage. Some powered sites noted as sandy rather than grassy.

Sentiment (6/10) — Polarized reviews with devoted repeat visitors loving it as "family holiday spot" and "home away from home," but substantial negative sentiment from families leaving early, never returning, and actively warning others. Management described as "rude, aggressive, dismissive" in multiple recent reviews. Midges cause many departures. Strong criticism of customer service recovery.

Location (5/10) — Park is 3-4km from Rainbow Beach town and actual beach, requiring vehicle access. Described as "out of town," "not close to town, beach etc," "isolated." Positive: direct water access for fishing, boat ramp adjacent, close to Fraser Island ferry. Negative: "walking distance to shops" claims disputed, dangerous road entrance on blind corner noted.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Midges and sandflies are a severe, recurring problem causing many families to leave early despite using repellent, with guests reporting "200-300 bites," "allergic reactions," and describing conditions as "unbearable" - this environmental issue fundamentally impacts the family camping experience during warmer months.

2. Cleanliness standards have become inconsistent with recent reviews citing "filthy amenities," "disgusting" conditions, and facilities cleaned only once daily during peak periods, contrasting sharply with earlier positive reviews and suggesting declining maintenance standards under current management.

3. Management practices receive harsh criticism for inflexibility (no refunds even for bereavement/serious illness), staff described as "rude," "aggressive," and "dismissive," with poor complaint handling and zero service recovery, creating negative experiences that overshadow the physical park qualities and drive families away permanently.
