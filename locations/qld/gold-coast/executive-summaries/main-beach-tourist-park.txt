PARK: Main Beach Tourist Park
SCORE: 76/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-19
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 103

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (13/20) — Reviews highlight a well-equipped games room, pool with shallow areas for young children, and special events like Easter activities, food trucks, coffee vans, and live music organized by management. One recent review noted disappointment that the small playground was removed after closure, leaving only the pool for on-site kids entertainment beyond the games room.

Nature (18/20) — Exceptional beach access directly across the road from Main Beach with patrolled swimming and surf club access. Multiple families praised the "green grassy oasis park" surrounded by high-rises, easy walking/cycling access along the foreshore to Surfers Paradise and towards Sea World. Limited bushland but the beachfront location is the standout natural feature.

Cleanliness (14/15) — Overwhelmingly positive feedback about "spotless," "immaculate," and "near new" amenities with "best bathrooms on the east coast." Recent reviews consistently mention modern, clean facilities, excellent laundry, and well-manicured grounds. One family with infant noted some cigarette butts on tent site, and another mentioned kitchen/showers needing more cleaning, preventing a perfect score.

Value (12/15) — Reviews are mixed on value with many stating "very reasonably priced compared to other parks on the Gold Coast" and "good value for money" considering the prime location. However, several noted "pricey especially over Xmas," rates are "up there," and one review mentioned tight sites don't justify premium pricing in this location.

Site Size (6/10) — Consistent feedback that sites are "tight," "packed," "close together," with "little room to move around" even for smaller vans. Multiple reviews mention difficulty backing in larger vans and sites being "sardines." Grass sites with astroturf at doors are noted, but the compact nature is a clear compromise for the urban beachfront location.

Sentiment (9/10) — Strong positive sentiment with families returning multiple times, booking repeat visits, and stating "best caravan park experience on the Gold Coast." Praise for exceptional staff (Nicky, Alan, Jo) going above and beyond. A few negative reviews about cabin quality, noise from construction, and management being "over the job" regarding e-scooter restrictions prevent a perfect score.

Location (10/10) — Perfect score for location with universal praise for being "right across from the beach," walking distance to Tedder Avenue cafes/restaurants, 10-minute walk to tram, bus stop at entrance, and central to Surfers Paradise and Sea World. Families love not needing to drive anywhere with "everything within your reach."

WHY NOT 100:
1. Site sizes are consistently described as tight, cramped, and challenging for larger rigs, with limited space between caravans creating a "sardines" feeling that compromises comfort for families wanting outdoor space.

2. Limited on-site kids entertainment infrastructure after playground removal, with only pool and games room mentioned, and no water slides, jumping pillow, or structured kids club that families expect from top-tier holiday parks.

3. Cabin accommodation received poor reviews citing old uncomfortable beds, broken air conditioning, maintenance issues, and poor staff reception, suggesting inconsistent quality across accommodation types and management responsiveness to cabin guests.

NOTABLE REVIEW PHRASES:
"perfect for families," "spotless amenities," "right across from the beach," "well-equipped games room," "Easter activities for the kids," "smoke-free, incredibly quiet," "sites are tight," "best caravan park experience on the Gold Coast"
