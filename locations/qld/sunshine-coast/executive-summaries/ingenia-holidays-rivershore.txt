PARK: Ingenia Holidays Rivershore
SCORE: 86/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-22
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 302

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (18/20) — Reviews consistently praised the heated pool with water slides as "ridiculously fun," the jumping pillow, mini golf, pedal karts, and daily organized activities including kids' discos, movie nights, archery, wildlife shows, and scavenger hunts. Families noted "so much to do with the kids" and children "never stopped smiling." The pool area is described as "resort style" with cabanas available for booking and poolside food service.

Nature (17/20) — The park sits on the Maroochy River with "stunning location right next to the river" and "beautiful big grassy area" for kids to play. Guests enjoyed fishing from the pontoon, kayaking, crabbing, and "beautiful views of sunsets and sunrise." The natural riverfront setting is described as "magical" though some noted it's not directly on a beach and mosquitoes can be an issue near the water.

Cleanliness (13/15) — Most reviews praised "spotless" facilities with "amenities always clean and well maintained" and bathrooms "kept clean." Several families noted daily cleaning of pool areas and regular bin emptying. However, some recent reviews mentioned cleanliness standards had "lapsed" with issues like cobwebs, weeds near cabanas, and bathrooms needing more frequent attention during busy periods. A few glamping tents needed "DEEP clean" with the floor feeling "grimey."

Value (12/15) — Pricing received mixed feedback. Many felt it was "great value for money" with "reasonably priced" food and drinks, particularly appreciating included activities. However, several reviews noted it as "very expensive" at nearly $200-250 per night for powered sites and $580 per night for glamping tents. Some felt "ripped off" given the pricing, though many still felt facilities justified the cost. Food described as "slightly on the higher price range" but acceptable.

Site Size (9/10) — Powered sites consistently praised as "huge," "extremely spacious," and "the biggest site I've ever stated at." Families noted sites could "fit another caravan and car" and "even with huge caravans on either side we didn't feel cramped." Unpowered riverfront sites described as "HUGE and grass beautifully kept." Minor deductions for some roads being "tight to maneuver through" and glamping tents built "very close on either sides."

Sentiment (9/10) — Overwhelmingly positive with phrases like "we will definitely be back," "absolute dream come true," "couldn't have been more pleased," and "one of the best parks we've stayed at." Many families book return visits and describe it as their "favourite caravan park." Some noted it as a "yearly visit for us." Staff praised as "amazing," "friendly," and "go above and beyond." Minor issues with occasional rude reception staff and inconsistent service quality.

Location (8/10) — Positioned centrally on the Sunshine Coast, "15 mins from tourist activities such as the big pineapple and sealife aquarium" and close to beaches, shops (IGA 4km), and attractions. Easy highway access for day trips to Noosa. However, several reviews mentioned significant road noise from the adjacent busy David Low Way, particularly at night with traffic described as "very busy road" and "extreme traffic noise." Not within walking distance to beaches or town.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Road noise from the adjacent David Low Way significantly impacted sleep quality for multiple guests, with some describing "extreme traffic noise all night" that "destroyed all the beauty" and caused them to cancel stays early.

2. Cleanliness standards have become inconsistent, particularly during peak periods, with recent reviews noting bathrooms not cleaned daily, showers needing "good scrub," glamping tents requiring deep cleaning, and maintenance issues like weeds, cobwebs, and blood on toilet doors remaining for days.

3. Overcrowding during school holidays and weekends severely impacts guest experience, with day visitors (paying $20 entry) taking over pool cabanas and facilities, making it "too busy and dangerous" for young children, forcing paying guests to leave pool areas despite paying $250-580 per night.
