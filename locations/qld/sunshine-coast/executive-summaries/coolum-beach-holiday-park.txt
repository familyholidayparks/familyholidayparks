PARK: Coolum Beach Holiday Park
SCORE: 68/100
DATE SCORED: 2025-05-29
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 173

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (6/20) — Reviews consistently note there is no pool, no kids playground, no organized activities, and no entertainment facilities on-site. One reviewer stated "no pool or kids playground you have to go out of the park for that" and another noted "doesn't offer any extra touches to make the park special, like evening activities." The park relies entirely on the beach and external attractions for entertainment.

Nature (18/20) — Exceptional natural environment with direct beach access being the standout feature. Reviews repeatedly praise "right on the beach," "perfect spot right across from the beach," "dog friendly beach access," and "beautiful long sandy beach." Multiple families mention the patrolled beach, surf areas, and dog-friendly sections. The coastal location with creek access for dogs and beachfront sites receives consistent praise.

Cleanliness (13/15) — Generally excellent with reviews frequently mentioning "super clean," "amenities very clean," "spotless amenities," and "facilities are excellent & clean as a whistle." However, some reviews mention declining standards with "slab was filthy on arrival," "amenities could be cleaner," and occasional maintenance issues like broken lights and security doors.

Value (8/15) — Significant concerns about pricing throughout reviews. Common phrases include "overpriced," "daylight robbery," "way too expensive," "$76-90 per night," "most expensive park we have stayed at," and complaints about additional fees like "$10 tractor fee," "$5 per dog per night," and "$10 charge to place van on site." Many note "no Pool and no wifi" doesn't justify the premium pricing.

Site Size (6/10) — Major recurring issue with "sites are too small for large vans," "very tight to get a caravan into the site," "sites are a bit cramped," "impossible to manoeuvre a caravan," and frequent need for "tractor to park vans." Some sites described as "about the width of a footpath" with "narrow roadways" and "high gutters." However, some premium beachfront sites received praise for generous space.

Sentiment (8/10) — Mixed sentiment with many loyal repeat visitors loving the location, but increasing dissatisfaction with pricing, staff, and facilities. Positive: "been coming here for years," "one of our favourite," "will definitely be returning." Negative: "won't be going back," "very disappointed," recurring complaints about rude staff, particularly reception, and feeling the park has "turned quite commercial."

Location (9/10) — Outstanding location consistently praised as the park's strongest feature. Reviews emphasize "walking distance to shops," "Coles, cafes and restaurants across the road," "close to beach, surf club," "handy to everything," and "perfect location right between beach and shopping strip." Accessibility to amenities without driving is highly valued.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Complete absence of family entertainment facilities (no pool, no playground, no kids' club, no activities) - a critical gap for family holiday parks that significantly impacts the entertainment score and overall appeal for families with young children.
2. Serious site accessibility and size issues with many sites too tight for caravans, requiring paid tractor assistance, narrow laneways, and reports of damaged vans during parking - creating stress and additional costs for families.
3. Widespread and recurring complaints about staff attitude, particularly reception being "rude," "unhelpful," "abusive," and "not interested in customer service," combined with poor communication (phones not answered, no call-backs) which severely impacts the family experience.

NOTABLE REVIEW PHRASES:
"best beach and walking distance to everything," "super clean amenities," "sites are too small for large vans," "overpriced," "staff are very rude," "no pool or kids playground," "right on the beach," "most expensive park we have stayed at"
