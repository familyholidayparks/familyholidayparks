PARK: BIG4 Maroochy River
SCORE: 78/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-21
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 416

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (14/20) — Reviews consistently mention jumping pillow, mini golf, small pool, and playground as core attractions. School holiday activities including movie nights, disco, reptile shows, science shows, daily kids programs, and visiting food trucks received strong praise. Wednesday pancake breakfasts and evening campfires with marshmallows created memorable experiences. However, multiple families noted limited entertainment variety for older children/teens, with several calling the pool "very small" and facilities "not much for big kids to do" compared to other BIG4 parks.

Nature (16/20) — The park sits directly across from the Maroochy River with excellent walking/cycling paths, fishing access, and sunset viewing opportunities. Reviewers loved the "beautiful river walks," herb gardens throughout the park, and tropical garden atmosphere with "manicured lawns, flowers and fresh herbs." However, the park itself is described as "small" and the natural setting is significantly compromised by being situated between the Sunshine Coast Motorway and busy arterial roads, with constant traffic noise diminishing the nature experience.

Cleanliness (15/15) — Exceptional scores across hundreds of reviews. Described as "cleanest park we've ever been to," "immaculately maintained," "spotless amenities," and "amenities cleaned twice daily." Staff named individually (Lisa, Emily, Savannah) for outstanding cleaning work. Grounds, toilets, showers, camp kitchens, and sites all received consistent 5-star cleanliness ratings with maintenance staff visibly working throughout the day.

Value (11/15) — Mixed feedback on pricing. Many found it "reasonable" and "good value" with quality facilities. However, several reviews complained about high prices: "$350 for a cabin ridiculous," "expensive for what it was," "definitely not worth the money we paid," and "way too expensive for what you actually get." The $10 per night pet fee drew specific criticism as a "money gouging rip off." Peak season pricing and powered site costs were frequent pain points.

Site Size (8/10) — Sites received mixed reviews. Many praised "nice roomy sites," "good size caravan sites," "spacious" with artificial turf. However, multiple reviews noted "very tight getting in with larger vans," "sites are very close together," "squeezed in that close," and "vans like 1.5 meters either side of you." The tractor "marriage saver" service exists specifically because sites are tight for reversing, though this service received overwhelming praise.

Sentiment (9/10) — Very high repeat visitation with phrases like "we keep coming back," "this is our favourite caravan park," "will definitely be back," and "7 years enjoying one of the best managed parks." Staff consistently praised for friendliness and going "above and beyond." However, some negative experiences included complaints about management, safety concerns, ant problems in cabins, and several guests leaving early due to noise issues.

Location (5/10) — The park's location receives the most polarized feedback. Positives include being "central to everything," "close to shops, beaches and holiday attractions," with IGA, pub, and Flume cafe within walking distance, and "great location for getting around the Sunshine Coast." However, the proximity to the Sunshine Coast Motorway is a major detractor: "very very loud traffic noise," "right by the motorway SO noisy," "road noise is like staying in a breakdown lane," "trucks vibrated the van," and "traffic noise was LOUD until 10pm and started again around 4:30am." Multiple families left early specifically due to noise.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Significant motorway and arterial road noise impacts many sites, with numerous families reporting poor sleep and some leaving early due to constant traffic sounds from 4:30am onwards.
2. Limited entertainment variety for older children and teens, with a notably small pool that becomes overcrowded during busy periods and insufficient activities compared to other BIG4 parks.
3. Premium pricing doesn't align with value expectations for some families, particularly given the small pool, limited facilities, tight sites, and additional pet fees that reviewers found excessive.
