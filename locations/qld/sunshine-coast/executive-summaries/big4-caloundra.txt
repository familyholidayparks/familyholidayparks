PARK: BIG4 Caloundra Waterfront Holiday Park
SCORE: 88/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-25
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 440

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (18/20) — Reviews consistently praise the "spectacular" heated pool complex with waterslides that keep kids entertained for hours, jumping pillow, pedal karts and go-karts, daily kids activities including discos and bingo, and the kids activities coordinator Nick who is "amazing with kids." The onsite Tripcony Traders restaurant with poolside service and Tom & Andy's coffee van add to the resort-style entertainment experience.

Nature (17/20) — The waterfront location provides "stunning walks along the coast," direct access to beaches and coastal pathways through back gates, water views from premium cabins, and a salt-water creek running through the property. Families appreciate the proximity to beaches, the esplanade walk linking multiple beaches, and the "beautiful scenic view of the ocean by the pool."

Cleanliness (15/15) — Overwhelmingly described as "spotless," "immaculate," and "the cleanest park we have stayed at." Reviewers specifically mention showers, toilets, and camp kitchen are "always spotless," amenities "sparkling clean no matter the time of day," and cleaning staff are called out by name for exceptional work. The park is "beautifully maintained" with manicured grounds and gardens.

Value (11/15) — While facilities are described as "worth every cent" and meals "reasonably priced," many reviewers note the park is "expensive," "pricey," and "not for the budget conscience" at $100+ per night for powered sites and $350+ for cabins during peak times. Some felt pricing was inconsistent or increased significantly year-over-year, though most concluded "you get what you pay for."

Site Size (8/10) — Sites described as "adequate but not huge," "spacious," and "well maintained" with artificial grass. Premium sites offer "the right size of large to spread out," though some standard sites are "tight" for larger vans, with caravans hanging over roads. Drive-through sites praised, but size varies significantly across the park.

Sentiment (10/10) — Exceptional loyalty with families returning annually for 3-12+ years, many rebook before leaving, and consistently recommend to others. Described as "favourite place to stay," "best holiday park," and one family has made it a yearly birthday tradition. Very high recommendation rate despite premium pricing.

Location (9/10) — Perfect for families: 2-minute walk to Coles and Woolworths, walking distance to Caloundra town centre, beaches, cafes and restaurants, and RSL. Easy access to Australia Zoo, Aussie World, Eumundi Markets. Only drawback is road noise on sites backing the highway, though proximity to amenities typically compensates.

WHY NOT 100:
Pricing is significantly higher than comparable parks ($100-350+ per night), with some guests feeling pricing inconsistencies and year-over-year increases ($800 increase for one regular) make it "out of touch with caravan affordability." While facilities justify premium rates for many, value-conscious families may find better options elsewhere.

Site size variability creates inconsistent experiences, with some sites described as "TINY" with "mud after rain," vans overhanging roads, and insufficient space between neighbours, while premium sites are spacious. Traffic noise affects sites along the main road significantly, requiring air conditioning to block sound at night.

Kids entertainment infrastructure has gaps including no proper playground equipment (only jumping pillow), pool closes at 6:30pm which families found limiting, and some reviewers noted lack of activities during non-peak periods. The jumping pillow was described as "terrible" by one family, and larger unsupervised children can dominate shared play spaces.
