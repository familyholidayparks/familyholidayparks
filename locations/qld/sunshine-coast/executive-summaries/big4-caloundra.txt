PARK: BIG4 Caloundra Waterfront Holiday Park
SCORE: 87/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-24
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 440

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (18/20) — Exceptional heated pool complex with water slides described as "amazing" and suitable for all ages, plus jumping pillow, pedal karts, go-karts, daily kids activities with coordinator Nick praised by name, movie nights, discos, bingo, trivia, and live music events at the restaurant. Families report kids "having the time of their lives" and "never a dull moment."

Nature (16/20) — Prime waterfront location with "stunning walks along the coast just a step out of the park," direct beach access through back gate to coastal walking paths, views over Pumicestone Passage, beautiful landscaped gardens with native birds, and close proximity to beaches. Some reviewers note it's not directly beachfront and requires short walk to quality swimming beaches.

Cleanliness (15/15) — Outstanding cleanliness across all facilities with reviews consistently using terms like "spotless," "immaculate," "sparkling clean," and "the cleanest campground I have stayed at." Amenities, camp kitchen, pool area, and grounds maintained to "consistently high standard" with housekeeping staff (Louise, Sienna, Nikki, Jane, Bree) mentioned by name for exceptional work.

Value (11/15) — Mixed value perception; many families find it "worth every cent" for the resort-style experience, excellent facilities, and location. Restaurant described as "reasonably priced" with "great value." However, significant concerns about pricing with multiple reviews noting it's "the most expensive park we have stayed in" at $100+ per powered site, cabin pricing $350-400/night seen as excessive by some, and recent price increases ($800 jump year-on-year noted) causing affordability concerns for repeat visitors.

Site Size (7/10) — Sites described as "adequate" and "reasonable size, not large but very adequate" with "plenty of space." Premium sites on artificial grass are "spacious" and "well maintained," but standard sites vary significantly—some cramped with "vans hanging over onto the road," tight maneuvering, and cars parked on roadways. Drive-through sites praised but some tent/camper sites described as "TINY" with space concerns.

Sentiment (10/10) — Exceptional guest sentiment with overwhelming majority of 5-star reviews, high recommendation rate, and numerous repeat visitors ("3rd year in a row," "4th time here," "12 years coming back"). Guests express strong emotional connection: "feels like coming home," "can't wait to come back," "already rebooked for next year." Staff culture praised with guests noting staff "clearly enjoyed working there."

Location (10/10) — Perfect location "right on the water" with "everything within walking distance"—Coles and Woolworths "2 minute walk," cafes, restaurants, RSL, shops, and beaches all accessible on foot. Coastal boardwalk directly outside back gate, Caloundra town center easily reached, and convenient base for regional attractions (Australia Zoo, Aussie World, Eumundi Markets) all within easy drive.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Value concerns are significant: pricing at $100+ per site and $350+ per cabin is substantially higher than comparable parks, with recent sharp price increases causing affordability issues for families and loyal repeat customers who report the park is "getting out of reach."

2. Site size and placement inconsistency: while premium sites are excellent, many standard sites are cramped with tight access, road noise issues (particularly along Bulcock Street with traffic noise "constant until midnight"), and significant variation in quality between different areas of the park.

3. Minor facility gaps: only one key provided for amenity access (multiple families note this as problematic), pool closes at 6:30pm (earlier than desired), limited kids playground equipment (jumping pillow only, no traditional playground mentioned), no dedicated dog facilities despite $10/night pet fee, and some maintenance issues noted (decking with protruding nails, BBQ hire equipment underperforming).
