PARK: Dicky Beach Family Holiday Park
SCORE: 78/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-15
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 133

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (14/20) — Reviews mention pool, tennis courts, games room, rec room, and playground positioned next to BBQ area for supervision. Pizza nights and food vans occasionally visit. However, multiple reviews note entertainment is modest compared to other parks, with comments like "not really a kids park as caravan parks go these days" and requests for "a bigger pool or second pool." School holiday activities mentioned positively by some families.

Nature (19/20) — Exceptional beach access is the standout feature with "direct beach access," "patrolled beach," "right on the beach," and "only a carpark away" appearing repeatedly. Dicky Beach Surf Club adjacent. Coastal walking tracks north and south. Many mature trees provide shade over sites. SS Dicky shipwreck remnants on beach. Minor deduction for urban setting rather than bushland.

Cleanliness (13/15) — Majority praise cleanliness with "immaculately clean," "amenities always clean," "spotless," and "very well maintained" appearing frequently. However, two serious incidents reported: gastro outbreak with "poo smeared in toilet bowls for half the day" and management's poor response, plus complaints about insufficient toilet facilities ("only 3 cubicles for 75 sites") causing queues. Generally excellent but inconsistencies noted.

Value (11/15) — Mixed feedback on value. Positive comments include "excellent value for money," "reasonable prices," and "good value for money." However, multiple recent reviews cite significant price increases: cabin costs rose from "$760 to $1200 per week," "prices increase every time," and "$131 for 2 nights for powered site was expensive." One long-term guest stated they "will never be coming back because of new pricing."

Site Size (8/10) — Generally positive with "spacious sites," "large caravan sites," "plenty of room to move," and "big sites with plenty of room." Some reviews mention sites are "little tight getting van in" and powered sites described as "sehr klein" (very small) and close to neighbors. Unpowered sites noted as more spacious under trees.

Sentiment (9/10) — Strong positive sentiment overall with numerous "will definitely be back," "highly recommend," and repeat visitors including one stating "10th year at this park." Some families visit annually. However, negative experiences regarding staff attitude, poor customer service responses, a concerning child safety incident, and pricing concerns temper the perfect score.

Location (10/10) — Perfect location score with universal praise. "Prime location," "perfect location," "location location location" repeated throughout. Direct beach access, shops/IGA/cafes/restaurants across the road, surf club next door, bus stops nearby, skatepark out front, and close to Glass House Mountains for day trips. "Everything needed within walking distance."

WHY NOT 100:
1. Entertainment facilities are adequate but modest for a family holiday park, with several reviews noting it's "not really a kids park" compared to modern standards and requests for more/bigger pool facilities reducing the entertainment score.

2. Two serious cleanliness incidents (gastro outbreak with poor management response, and persistent toilet facility inadequacy with long queues) alongside staffing/customer service complaints prevent a perfect cleanliness and operations score despite generally excellent standards.

3. Significant and repeated price increases have impacted value perception, with cabin prices jumping 58% and multiple guests stating they won't return due to costs, particularly when compared to renting houses nearby for less money.
