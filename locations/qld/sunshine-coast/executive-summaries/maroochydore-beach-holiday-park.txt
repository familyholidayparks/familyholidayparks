PARK: Maroochydore Beach Holiday Park
SCORE: 52/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-04-11
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 53

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (3/20) — Multiple families explicitly stated there is "nothing kid friendly or anything for kids to do except walk to the beach" with one review rating it "0/10" for families with children. No playground, pool, or organized activities mentioned across all reviews. One older reviewer noted "very little for children, which makes it great for oldies."

Nature (18/20) — Exceptional beach access with "private access gate" reaching beach "in 1 minute" consistently praised. Direct beachfront location with patrolled surf club nearby. Some sites feature tree coverage, though many described as open air. Walking distance to Cotton Tree and natural coastal environment highly valued.

Cleanliness (8/15) — Highly inconsistent across timeframes. Recent 2024-2026 reviews frequently cite "disgusting" amenities, "dirty" facilities, and one guest reported amenities not cleaned on four occasions over 13 nights with "showers a disgusting mess." One cabin had cockroaches and ants. However, some 2023-2025 reviews praised "spotless amenities" and "extraordinarily clean" facilities, suggesting staff turnover issues.

Value (6/15) — Overwhelming criticism of pricing: "Very expensive for what you get," "NOT worth 80$ per night," "very overpriced" appear repeatedly. Rates of $68.50-$80 per night cited as excessive given minimal facilities. Multiple reviews recommend competing Big 4 and Ingenia parks as better value.

Site Size (5/10) — Significant criticism of "EXTREAMLY tight and squishy" sites. Multiple reviews mention cramped conditions, though some guests with larger rigs reported adequate space. Sites described as variable in quality with some exposed to wind and road noise.

Sentiment (6/10) — Deeply polarized. Recent 2026 reviews heavily negative (multiple 1-star ratings) while older 2023-2024 reviews more positive. Several families stated they would "never recommend" or "won't be back." Some repeat guests booked for 2026, but overall trajectory declining. Emergency response failure and poor customer service damaged reputation.

Location (10/10) — Universally praised as "prime location," "central to everything," walking distance to shops, restaurants, Mooloolaba, and Cotton Tree. Bus stop outside park. Beach access the standout feature with unanimous agreement this is the park's greatest strength.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Complete absence of children's entertainment facilities (playground, pool, activities) makes this unsuitable for families seeking on-site kid activities, losing nearly all entertainment points.
2. Severe inconsistency in cleanliness and maintenance with multiple recent reports of disgusting amenities, uncleaned facilities for days, cockroach infestations in cabins, and hot water failures indicating operational breakdown.
3. Poor value proposition with prices of $68.50-$80 per night considered excessive for minimal facilities, tight sites, and inconsistent service, with multiple reviewers specifically recommending competitors instead.

NOTABLE REVIEW PHRASES:
