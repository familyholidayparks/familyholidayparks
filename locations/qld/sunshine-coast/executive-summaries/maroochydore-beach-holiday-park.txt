PARK: Maroochydore Beach Holiday Park
SCORE: 52/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-04-10
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 53

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (2/20) — Multiple families explicitly stated there is "nothing kid friendly or anything for kids to do" and "Very little for children," with the only activity being beach access. This is a critical deficiency for family holiday parks.

Nature (18/20) — The park's strongest asset is its beachfront location with "private access to the beach," described as "literally on the beach through an access gate in 1 minute." The natural coastal environment and proximity to patrolled beaches earned consistent praise.

Cleanliness (8/15) — Reviews show inconsistent standards, with some praising "extra ordinarily clean" facilities while recent reviews report "amenities were disgusting," "showers a disgusting mess," amenities not cleaned for days, and cabins "infested with cockroaches and ants."

Value (6/15) — Overwhelmingly negative feedback on pricing: "Very expensive for what you get," "NOT worth 80$ per night," "Very overpriced," with costs at $68-$80 per night for basic, aging facilities that don't match the premium charged.

Site Size (5/10) — Consistent complaints about "EXTREAMLY tight and squishy" sites with poor site allocation practices, though some beach-side sites received positive mentions for adequate space.

Sentiment (6/10) — Mixed sentiment with polarised reviews; some long-term guests loved it and rebooked, but many families with children were deeply disappointed and explicitly warned others not to visit.

Location (10/10) — Universal praise for "prime location" right on the beach, walking distance to Cotton Tree, Mooloolaba, surf clubs, shops, restaurants, and cafes, with bus access directly outside.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Complete absence of kids entertainment facilities makes this unsuitable for families with young children, resulting in explicit warnings from family reviewers
2. Inconsistent and declining cleanliness standards with multiple reports of disgusting amenities, missed cleaning schedules, pest infestations in cabins, and poor maintenance
3. Extremely poor value proposition charging premium beachfront rates ($70-80/night) for run-down, aging facilities with minimal amenities that don't justify the cost

NOTABLE REVIEW PHRASES:
