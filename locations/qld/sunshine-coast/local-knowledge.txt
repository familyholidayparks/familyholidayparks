# Visiting Sunshine Coast with Kids: Local Knowledge

**Best times to visit are school holidays and May-September** when the weather's perfect – warm but not scorching, and the water's still swimmable. December to February gets absolutely rammed with tourists and the heat's brutal, so avoid unless you're after crowds. The shoulder seasons (March-April and September-October) are gold if you can swing it – fewer people, still great weather. Book accommodation early no matter when you come; this place fills up fast. Pro tip: visit the patrolled beaches during lifeguard hours, check the Surf Life Saving Club websites, and don't assume all beaches are safe for kids – Coolum and Noosa Main Beach are usually your safest bets for families.

**What families genuinely love here is the variety without needing to drive far.** You've got the Sunshine Coast Hinterland (rainforest walks, cute towns, ice cream stops) only 20-30 minutes inland, beautiful beaches that actually have amenities, and attractions like the Wildlife HQ Zoo and Aussie World that'll keep kids entertained. The Noosa Heads coastal walk is fantastic for families – it's easy, scenic, and there are cafes at both ends. Here's what catches people out though: the sun is intense, so pack proper sunscreen and reapply constantly; the roads inland get congested during school holidays; and some "hidden gem" beaches advertised online are actually sketchy or have strong currents. Stick to the main patrolled beaches unless a local tells you otherwise.

**Local insider advice: skip the tourist traps along the main strip and eat where families actually go** – the cafes in Noosa Junction and Coolum have better coffee, food, and prices. Download the Beachsafe app before you arrive to check conditions and hazards. If you want to avoid the worst crowds, hit the beach before 10am or after 3pm. The hinterland towns like Montville and Mapleton are worth a half-day trip, especially if it's too hot for the beach. Finally, bring a rash shirt even if your kids don't usually wear one – the sun here will burn them in minutes, and the extra protection saves a miserable holiday.
