PARK: Bribie Island Caravan Park
SCORE: 62/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-24
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 119

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (14/20) — Good range of activities including pool, mini golf, tennis court, arcade, air hockey, basketball, putt putt and 10-pin bowling mentioned positively by families. Pool closes early (6pm in summer) which disappointed guests. Kids entertainment facilities described as dated but functional, with "heaps for kids to do" and "great facilities to entertain children" noted repeatedly.

Nature (13/20) — Excellent beach proximity (100-300m walk to Woorim surf beach) consistently praised. Park described as "surrounded by trees" with "lovely bird sounds" creating a natural feel. No surf beach, calm waters. Beach access to bunkers and 6km walks available. Some concerns about bull sharks mentioned. Natural environment is pleasant but not exceptional.

Cleanliness (10/15) — Mixed reviews with significant concerns. Recent reviews mention bird lice infestation in community room, mouldy curtains, rust staining, dirty blankets, cockroaches, and "disgusting shower" in cabins. Amenities described as both "very clean" and "aged & disgusting" depending on timing. Grounds and camp kitchen generally well-maintained but cabin cleanliness inconsistent.

Value (10/15) — Polarizing feedback on value. Many found it affordable and reasonable ($42-49 per night for powered sites, under $100 for 2 nights camping with kids). However, numerous complaints about being "overpriced for what it is," "too high priced for the size of the tiny cabins," unexpected price increases for events ($224 for 2 nights), and extra charges not honoured in quotes. Dated facilities don't match pricing for some guests.

Site Size (6/10) — Sites described as "spacious older style" by some but "a little small and cramped in" by others. Parking confusion around cabins with unclear designated spaces. Sites "really close together" noted. Not ideal for large rigs, though one reviewer appreciated accommodation after being turned away elsewhere for being "too big."

Sentiment (6/10) — Mixed sentiment with passionate advocates and disappointed critics. Many families return repeatedly ("3rd time here," "always amazing, which is why we keep coming back," "been coming since late 70s") but recent reviews show declining satisfaction. Serious complaints about theft, permanent residents causing issues, poor refund policies, and pest problems. Approximately 60% positive vs 40% negative/neutral in recent reviews.

Location (9/10) — Consistently praised as the park's strongest feature. Walking distance (5 minutes) to beach, shops, IGA, cafes, restaurants, bars, surf club, post office. Close to Brisbane. "Perfect location" and "great location" mentioned in majority of reviews. Easy access to everything needed without requiring a car.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Accommodation desperately outdated - Multiple recent reviews describe cabins as "very outdated and looking shabby," with rust, mould, broken fittings, uncomfortable furniture, tiny bathrooms, and facilities "from the 1980s." One reviewer specifically called out bird lice infestation as a known, ongoing problem.

2. Significant cleanliness and maintenance concerns - Reports of cockroaches, dirty cabins, rust staining bathmats, mouldy curtains, disgusting showers, broken toilet roll holders with packing tape, and inconsistent cleaning standards undermine family confidence, especially for young children.

3. Problematic permanent resident situation and theft issues - Multiple reviews mention permanent residents causing nuisance (drinking, noise, foul language until late), known theft problems in camp kitchen with cameras not being used to help, and generally creating an uncomfortable environment that detracts from the family holiday experience.

NOTABLE REVIEW PHRASES:
"kids loved it," "cabins are in very desperate need of renovating," "great location, ammenities, kids entertainment," "bird lice infestation," "facilities are old, but clean," "walking distance to the beach," "outdated, but location and services, and price beats all of that," "permanent residents here are a nuisance"
