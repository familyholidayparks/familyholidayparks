PARK: Bongaree Caravan Park
SCORE: 62/100
DATE SCORED: 2024-01-11
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 89

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (8/20) — Multiple families explicitly stated the park is "definitely not for young kids" with "no where for children to play safely" and "not alot at the actual park for kids." One review mentions a playground and wildlife, but the consensus is minimal on-site entertainment. No pool is consistently noted as a drawback, with families needing to plan off-site activities daily.

Nature (18/20) — Exceptional natural setting directly opposite Pumicestone Passage with "spectacular sunsets," abundant wildlife including lorikeets, cockatoos, dolphins, dugongs, and wild kangaroos. Beautiful beach access across the road with walking tracks along the bay. Only minor deductions for some traffic noise and flying fox issues on specific sites.

Cleanliness (14/15) — Outstanding consistency with "super clean," "spotlessly clean," "very clean amenities," and "amenities are spotless" appearing throughout recent reviews. Modern facilities with clean camp kitchen, showers, and toilets maintained to high standards. Minor point lost for older cabin condition in some reviews.

Value (13/15) — Generally positive with "great value for money," "cheap rates," and specific pricing ($37-$425 for 3 nights) noted as reasonable. However, several reviews mention "prices are higher" than previous years, and strict deposit policies frustrate some families, preventing a higher score.

Site Size (8/10) — Good-sized concrete slab sites with "heaps of room between caravans" and "lots of space between sites." Some reviews note difficulty maneuvering into corner sites with trees, and one family felt sites were "very close to each other," preventing a perfect score.

Sentiment (8/10) — Strong repeat visitation with "come every year" appearing multiple times, but balanced by critical reviews about lack of facilities for young families and inflexible booking policies. Overall positive sentiment with families loving the location but acknowledging limitations for certain age groups.

Location (10/10) — Perfect scores for location with "fantastic location," "perfect location," and "amazing location" consistently mentioned. Walking distance to shops, bowling club, RSL, IGA, restaurants, and beach. Direct access to Pumicestone Passage for water activities. Central to everything on Bribie Island.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Minimal on-site entertainment for children with no pool, limited playground equipment, and explicit warnings from families that it's "not for young kids" unless planning daily off-site adventures, resulting in significant entertainment score loss.

2. Safety concerns raised by families with young children about busy road corners, no safe play areas within the park, and requirement to walk around gates to exit, making supervision stressful for parents of toddlers and preschoolers.

3. Limited family-specific amenities including no WiFi (explicitly stated as unavailable), no dogs allowed (disappointing to pet-owning families), inflexible deposit policies that frustrated families with sick children, and dated cabins in some areas requiring improvement.
