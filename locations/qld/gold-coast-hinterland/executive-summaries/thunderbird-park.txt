PARK: Thunderbird Park
SCORE: 73/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-15
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 317

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (18/20) — Reviews consistently praise the exceptional range of activities including Dig It excavators ("2 hours of fun", "kids absolutely loved it"), Junior and Adult TreeTop Challenges, Canyon Flyer ziplines, thunder egg fossicking, crystal panning, mini golf, laser tag, and bird feeding. Staff receive outstanding reviews for being "amazing with kids", "friendly and enthusiastic", and creating safe, fun experiences. Multiple families note "so much to do" and "could easily spend the whole day there".

Nature (18/20) — The Gold Coast Hinterland location delivers "stunning" natural surroundings with Cedar Creek running through the property offering "crystal clear" rock pools for swimming. Families love the "beautiful bush camping" setting "amongst massive gum trees" with native wildlife including kookaburras and bush turkeys. The creek access and natural environment are frequently highlighted as major drawcards, though not beach access.

Cleanliness (10/15) — This is the park's weakest area with consistent complaints about amenities. Reviews describe toilets as "in desperate need of care", "smell like someone's died in there", with "dribble water" pressure and aging facilities. While some note amenities are "cleaned multiple times during the day", the infrastructure itself is "well past their use by date". New amenities have been built and are described as "absolutely stunning", but older blocks remain problematic. Some glamping tents had "mould covered" issues.

Value (10/15) — Value receives mixed feedback. While base camping rates and some activities are seen as reasonable ("fairly priced", "great value for money"), the accumulation of paid activities creates concern: "everything else was just too expensive", "charge for everything", "had our hands in our pockets for activities all the time". Specific complaints include $15 for adults to watch kids fossick, $4.50 cans of soft drink, and cafe prices. Families with multiple children find costs "add up" quickly, though many still feel experiences justify prices.

Site Size (7/10) — Camping sites receive varied feedback. Some describe "nice big sites" and "good size camp site" with "large powered sites", but others note sites "can be pretty small", "cramped onto camp sites", and hard ground making tent pegging difficult. Several reviews mention sites changed from bookings made months in advance. Caravan access is challenging with "difficult manoeuvres due to a ditch running alongside the road".

Sentiment (8/10) — Overall sentiment is positive with many families saying "will definitely be back", "one of our favourite places", and "highly recommend". Recent reviews show strong appreciation for staff and activities. However, repeat visitation is tempered by value concerns and amenity issues, with some stating they "won't be back" due to costs or camping conditions. The park handles feedback professionally but some guests won't return due to pricing or facility conditions.

Location (2/10) — Located in Gold Coast Hinterland on Mt Tamborine, approximately 50 minutes from Logan. While the mountain setting is beautiful, it's not beachside and requires mountain driving. Close to Mt Tamborine attractions (waterfalls, Gallery Walk, Skywalk) but isolated from coastal attractions. The location scores lower as it's not convenient for typical family holiday destinations and activities mostly require staying on-site.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Amenities infrastructure is significantly aged and inadequate for park size - older toilet blocks have poor water pressure, mould, smell issues, and insufficient shower/toilet numbers for camper volume, despite new blocks being built recently.

2. Value concerns are prominent with every activity requiring separate payment beyond camping fees, making family visits expensive especially with multiple children, and resort pricing ("$4.50 for a can of Pepsi") throughout.

3. Camping facilities don't match the premium pricing - no concrete slabs, hard ground difficult for tent pegs, sites changed after booking, and basic infrastructure (one washing machine for 500+ sites, limited BBQ areas, no dump point) falls short of expectations at $70-100+ per night.
