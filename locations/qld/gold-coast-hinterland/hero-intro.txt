# Gold Coast Hinterland Family Holiday Guide

The Gold Coast Hinterland is where rainforest meets the beach—a genuinely brilliant setup for families wanting variety without the drive. You've got world-class beaches at Kirra, Tallebudgera, and Ocean Beach right there, perfect for kids who want to swim and build sandcastles, while the hinterland proper sits just inland with national parks, hiking trails, and cool mountain towns that give you a proper break from the sun. It's easy enough for younger kids to manage, and there's enough here to keep older ones interested without everyone feeling like they're in the same theme park queue all day.

For practical family holidays, the parks here do the job well. BIG4 Gold Coast Holiday Park, Broadwater Tourist Park, Tallebudgera Creek Tourist Park, Ocean Beach Tourist Park, and Kirra Beach Tourist Park all offer the basics you need—cabins, powered sites, and decent facilities—so you're not hunting around stressed on arrival day. The real drawcard is having nature on your doorstep: walking trails through rainforest, waterfall swims, wildlife spotting, and genuine fresh air without feeling like you've left civilisation behind completely. It's the sort of place where kids actually want to be outside, and parents can actually relax.
