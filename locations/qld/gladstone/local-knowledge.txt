# Visiting Gladstone with the Family

Honestly, the best time to visit Gladstone is May through September when it's warm but not stupidly hot – you won't be melting in 40-degree heat or dealing with summer cyclone season. October to April gets pretty humid and the thunderstorms can be fierce, so keep that in mind. School holidays are packed, especially the winter break, so if you can avoid those times you'll actually find parking and won't queue forever at the shops. The winter months are absolutely perfect for getting out and exploring without the kids complaining they're roasting.

Here's the real talk: bring reef shoes or proper water shoes for the beach and rock pools – there are sharp shells and sea urchins about, and it saves heaps of drama. The local beaches are good but nothing compared to the southern Gold Coast, so manage expectations there. What families actually love is the Gladstone Marina precinct – the kids can run around, there's decent food options, and you can grab fresh fish from the local suppliers. The Barrage is worth a visit for walking and birdwatching, and if your crew likes nature, the Curtis Island tours and kayaking are brilliant. Just book ahead because spots fill up quick, especially in peak season.

Watch out for the stingers (box jellyfish) from October onwards – check the beaches before you go in, and stick to the patrolled areas. The tidal range here is huge, so if you're planning rock pool exploring or beach walks, time it around low tide or you'll be disappointed. Local advice: grab a copy of the Gladstone Visitor Guide from the information centre, chat to the staff there, and ask about what's actually on during your visit – heaps of community events happen but they don't always get big publicity. The locals are helpful, so just ask around; we generally know what's good.
