# Moreton Bay Family Visit Guide

The sweet spot for visiting is April to October when the weather's perfect and the sea wasps bugger off. November through March gets hot and sticky, plus stinger season means the beaches get netted and kids get cranky. School holidays are packed with families, so book accommodation early if you're coming then. Pro tip: hit the quieter shoulder months like April or September for better crowds and still-brilliant weather. The bay's calm most of the time, but keep an eye on the forecast – sudden storms can kick up rough water fast, and getting to the islands becomes dodgy.

What families absolutely love here is the islands – Stradbroke, Moreton, and North Stradbroke are magic for kids. You've got calm beaches, snorkelling that doesn't require diving skills, and tonnes of wildlife if you're patient. Tangalooma on Moreton Island has hand-fed dolphins at sunset, which the kids will bang on about for years. The mainland beaches at Cleveland and Wynnum are solid backups with rock pools, playgrounds, and normal suburbs nearby. Book island ferries well ahead – they fill up fast – and bring loads of water because the islands don't have much shade and sunburn happens quick.

Watch out for the obvious: rips, stingers in summer, and sun that'll absolutely roast you. Less obvious? The tidal flats can trap you if you're not paying attention, and the sand gets scorching on bare feet by midday. Bring proper water shoes. Hire a car if you can – public transport's patchy – and don't assume restaurants on the islands are open year-round. Pack snacks, sunscreen like your life depends on it, and a good attitude about sand in the car. Honestly, the bay's forgiving if you're sensible, but it demands respect.
