PARK: BIG4 Port Douglas Glengarry Holiday Park
SCORE: 82/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-04
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 100

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (18/20) — Reviews consistently praise the waterpark with multiple slides, heated swimming pool, jumping pillow, and pedal carts as major highlights. Families report children spending hours enjoying water slides and play equipment, with the waterpark described as "awesome for kids and family" and providing "tons of fun for the kids." Easter hunts and day pass options add value.

Nature (16/20) — The park features beautiful tropical gardens with palms, mountain views described as "scenic," and abundant wildlife with morning bird concerts mentioned positively. The lush, peaceful environment surrounded by plants and trees creates a tranquil tropical atmosphere, though the park lacks direct beach or creek access.

Cleanliness (13/15) — Amenities consistently described as "spotlessly clean," "very clean," and cleaned daily. Camp kitchen praised as "huge" and "absolutely spotless." However, some reviews mention mould in cabin bathrooms, wet shower floors, and occasional issues with dusty villas and facilities needing refreshment.

Value (12/15) — Mixed feedback on pricing, with some noting $90+ for powered sites seems "a little pricey" compared to beach alternatives at $40. However, many families feel the waterpark and extensive facilities justify the cost, with BIG4 members praising "amazing rates" and value for the entertainment provided.

Site Size (8/10) — Sites described as "massive" compared to other BIG4 parks, "generous," and "good size with palms in between each site" providing privacy. However, multiple reviews complain about being "crammed in like sardines" during peak periods, sites being "pretty tight," and "not much room to move on most sites."

Sentiment (9/10) — Strong positive sentiment with repeat visitors (23 years, multiple visits), "10/10" ratings, and "will definitely be back" comments. Staff consistently praised as friendly and helpful. Some negative experiences around crowding during peak season, noise issues, poor phone/wifi reception, and maintenance concerns detract slightly.

Location (6/10) — Located 10-15 minutes drive from Port Douglas town centre, described as "convenient for exploring" but requiring a vehicle. Close to petrol station (3 minutes) and restaurants (10 minutes), with good access to Great Barrier Reef tours and Cape Tribulation. However, distance from town noted as "a little inconvenient" by several families.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Poor connectivity throughout park with "nil internet," wifi only near reception despite codes being given, and "very poor" phone reception frustrating families trying to plan activities.
2. Cleanliness inconsistencies with mould in cabin bathrooms, wet/dangerous shower floors, dusty villas, and some facilities "in need of maintenance" despite generally clean amenities.
3. Significant site crowding during peak periods with families "crammed in like sardines," noise issues from unsupervised children and social gatherings, and some sites receiving water runoff creating muddy conditions unsuitable for young children.
