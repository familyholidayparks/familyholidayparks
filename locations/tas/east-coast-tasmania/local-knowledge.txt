# East Coast Tasmania Travel Tips

The best time to visit East Coast Tassie with kids is late December through early January or the Easter holidays – the water's actually swimmable then, and you won't be fighting the summer crowds that hit places like Freycinet. Spring (September-October) is brilliant too if your kids can handle cooler water. Avoid winter unless you're after dramatic weather and don't mind grey skies; it gets proper cold and dark down here. Book accommodation early during school holidays – there's bugger all around, and prices double. Pro tip: the shoulder seasons (April-May and September-October) give you the best bang for your buck with fewer tourists and decent weather.

What families genuinely love is the beach-to-hike combination – you can literally do a proper walk at Wineglass Bay or along the Freycinet Peninsula, then hit a sheltered beach with the kids after. The coastal towns like Coles Bay and Swansea have solid fish and chips spots, and the local produce is actually quality. Keep an eye out for sea eagles and wallabies – kids go mental for them. Watch out for the notorious winds (seriously, they're no joke), rocky beaches that'll shred your feet if you're not careful, and mobile coverage that's patchy as hell inland. Don't rely on Google Maps out here; download offline maps before you leave civilisation.

Here's what most visitors miss: the smaller towns between Hobart and Freycinet are goldmines. Triabunna has a proper wharf where you can grab fresh crayfish, and there's actual parking. The East Coast Natureworld near Bicheno is better than you'd expect for young kids. Fill up your fuel tank whenever you see a servo – distances are deceptive, and you don't want to be stranded. Lastly, bring layers; the weather changes like a moody teenager, and the sun burns different down here because of the ozone layer.
