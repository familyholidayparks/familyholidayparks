PARK: Greens Beach Caravan & Holiday Park
SCORE: 58/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-03-19
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 23

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (8/20) — Very limited structured entertainment. One review mentions "lots for children to enjoy" and references a pool, with wildlife viewing and beach activities being the primary entertainment. No playground or organized kids' programs mentioned. Described as "very family friendly" but relies entirely on natural environment for entertainment.

Nature (18/20) — Exceptional natural setting with "50m walk to the beach," "massive beach," "plenty of wildlife for the kids," and "West Head Coastal walk" access. Multiple reviews praise hearing "the ocean when you sleep" and "beautiful spot." Strong nature score driven by coastal location and wildlife opportunities, though limited shade noted.

Cleanliness (12/15) — Mixed feedback on cleanliness. Multiple recent reviews praise "facilities sparkling clean," "immaculately clean," and "very clean and tidy." However, concerns raised about "old amenities" and facilities being "well away from caravan & camp sites." Generally clean but aging infrastructure.

Value (9/15) — Significant value concerns in recent reviews. "Paying $50 per night with facilities lacking is way too steep," "un peu cher pour la qualité," paid showers at "$1 per douche," no camp kitchen, bore water unsuitable for drinking, and no dump point. One older review mentions "cost is minimal" and "affordable," but recent sentiment is negative.

Site Size (8/10) — Generally positive with "sites were large," "plenty of space," though one review notes "markings for powered caravan sites need to be set out clearer" and "neighbouring caravan was half on our site." Some issues with site definition but adequate space overall.

Sentiment (7/10) — Mixed sentiment weighted toward recent reviews. Many 5-star reviews from 2023-2024 praising the park, but recent 2025-2026 reviews show declining satisfaction with 2-3 star ratings citing missing facilities and value concerns. Positive comments about helpful manager Dean, but infrastructure issues dominate recent feedback.

Location (8/10) — Excellent beach proximity and access to coastal walks. "Great position, very close to beach," shop and golf course within walking distance. "Start line was just outside the caravan park" for running events. East Coast Tasmania location provides good base for day trips, though somewhat remote.

WHY NOT 100:
Critical missing facilities severely impact family experience - no camp kitchen, paid showers at $1 for 4 minutes, no dump point on-site, and bore water unsuitable for drinking create significant inconveniences for families with young children. The lack of structured entertainment, playground equipment, or organized kids' activities means children rely entirely on beach and wildlife for entertainment. Value proposition has deteriorated with recent reviews citing $50/night as too expensive for aging amenities, booking errors placing guests on wrong sites, and basic infrastructure that doesn't meet modern family camping expectations.
