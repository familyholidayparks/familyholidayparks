PARK: Triabunna Cabin & Caravan Park
SCORE: 52/100
DATE SCORED: 2025
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 91

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (2/20) — Multiple reviews explicitly state "nothing to keep kids entertained" and "no place for kids entertainment." The park offers no playground, no activities, and no structured entertainment of any kind for families with children.

Nature (14/20) — The park's primary draw is proximity to Maria Island National Park, with reviews praising it as "great spot to catch the ferry" and "perfect place to start and finish your Maria Island experience." However, the park itself offers minimal natural environment beyond "nice grassy campsites" and one mention of camping "under the willow."

Cleanliness (10/15) — Reviews are mixed but lean positive on cleanliness: "clean and tidy," "toilets very clean," "extremely well maintained despite their age." However, several reviews mention mould issues ("mould in the grout in the whole shower," "bathrooms are mouldy"), facilities needing "major overhaul," and one report of "live ants in cabins." The camp kitchen received both praise as "well stocked" and criticism as "open and dirty."

Value (6/15) — Strong negative sentiment dominates value ratings. Reviews repeatedly cite "very expensive," "overpriced for the quality," "rip off," and "most expensive we have come across since touring Tassie." Multiple guests express frustration about paying $49-$63 per night plus an additional $2-$4 for coin-operated showers. Several note free camping alternatives exist nearby with shower facilities.

Site Size (4/10) — Consistently negative feedback: "sites are very small," "packed in rather tightly," "small and squishy," "very cramp and not much space," "our 19ft van was difficult to put on site," and "not enough room to fully extend annexe." The small park size limits spacing between sites.

Sentiment (7/10) — Family traveller sentiment is cautiously neutral to negative. While some guests appreciated the "friendly hosts" and basic functionality, families specifically note it lacks entertainment value. Repeat visitation intentions are low among family reviewers, with most indicating they'd only return by necessity for Maria Island access rather than choosing the park as a destination.

Location (9/10) — The park's strongest attribute with consistent praise: "super close to the Maria Island Ferry," "5 min walk to an IGA," "walking distance to supermarket, marina," "3 minute walk to IGA," and "close to everything." The convenient location to ferry terminal, shops, and town amenities is mentioned positively in majority of reviews.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Complete absence of family entertainment facilities - no playground, no activities, no kids' amenities repeatedly cited as major limitation for families with young children.
2. Controversial pricing structure with expensive nightly rates ($49-$63) plus mandatory coin-operated showers ($2 per 5 minutes) creates poor value perception, especially when free camping alternatives exist nearby.
3. Severely limited site sizes with caravans "packed in rather tightly" and insufficient space for larger rigs to extend annexes or park vehicles alongside, making it unsuitable for family setups requiring room to spread out.

NOTABLE REVIEW PHRASES:
