PARK: Barilla Holiday Park
SCORE: 72/100
DATE SCORED: 2024-01-15
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 97

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (12/20) — Mini golf is consistently praised as excellent fun for families and "the best in Australia" by some reviewers. However, multiple families note the playground is very basic with "two tiny slides off some rocks" and "90% of park is gravel." The pool and advertised hot spa are no longer available due to insurance costs, which disappointed families. The on-site Thai restaurant provides dining entertainment but limited structured kids' activities beyond mini golf.

Nature (16/20) — Strong wildlife experiences with daily visits from bandicoots, pademelons, native hens, rabbits, and magpies that families loved. Reviews describe a "peaceful, semi-rural escape" with "a touch of bush" and "beautiful hillside views." The hedge-bordered sites create a country atmosphere "15 minutes to the city but feels like you're in the bush." While not pristine wilderness, the semi-rural setting with abundant wildlife provides genuine nature connection for children.

Cleanliness (14/15) — Amenities consistently described as "very clean," "cleanest across Tasmania," and "the most clean amenities we have experienced." Innovative converted water tank bathrooms praised as "amazingly simple and clean." Minor issues noted with shower drainage and shower time limits (5-7 minutes), plus some comments about tired gardens with weeds. Overall maintenance standard is high with grounds "perfectly sculpted."

Value (9/15) — Significant concern about pricing with multiple reviews stating "extremely expensive," "outrageous" ($60-70 per night), and "very expensive price for what the park offers." Several families specifically compared unfavorably to other Tasmanian parks. Positive value mentions note "reasonable" $59 powered site and 10% discount for phone bookings. The removal of pool without price adjustment affects perceived value. Laundry ($5) and BBQ ($1 for 17min) add extra costs.

Site Size (9/10) — Powered sites consistently praised as "spacious," "plenty of room for van, awning & car," and "could easily fit a van and car." Hedge separation between sites provides excellent privacy and clear parking boundaries. Sites described as "flat & well maintained" with synthetic grass. Some unpowered sites criticized as small for the price. Overall, caravan/motorhome sites well-sized but not exceptional.

Sentiment (8/10) — Strongly positive sentiment with many "definitely be back" and "highly recommend" statements. Staff praised as "incredibly welcoming," "LOVELY," and "went out of their way." However, recent reviews show declining sentiment regarding value and facilities. Some families specifically advise to "avoid if travelling with kids." Repeat visitors note consistency in quality. Strong word-of-mouth from most families balanced by pricing concerns.

Location (4/10) — Close to airport (10-15 minutes) praised for arrival/departure convenience. However, "middle of nowhere" comments and "not centrally located" concerns from families. 15-20 minutes to Hobart CBD requires driving. Bus services described as "limited" and infrequent. Richmond and wineries nearby but requires transport. While safe and quiet, the location requires a car and doesn't offer walk-to-attractions convenience families prefer.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Limited on-site kids' entertainment with basic playground facilities (just two small slides) and no pool/water play options, which significantly impacts family appeal compared to other holiday parks.
2. Pricing concerns consistently mentioned as "extremely expensive" relative to other Tasmanian parks, with additional costs for laundry, BBQ use, and mini golf affecting overall value perception for family budgets.
3. Location requires driving to most attractions with limited public transport options, and the "middle of nowhere" setting doesn't suit families seeking walkable access to beaches, town centers, or tourist activities.
