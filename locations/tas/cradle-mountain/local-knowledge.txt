# Visiting Cradle Mountain with the Kids

Timing is everything at Cradle—come in late spring (November) or early autumn (March to April) and you'll have manageable crowds, decent weather, and kids who aren't melting or freezing. Summer's packed and muddy, winter's seriously cold and the high walks close. The visitor centre opens early, so get there before 9am to snag parking and beat the tour buses. Pro tip: do the main walks early in the day—the light's better, kids have more energy, and you'll miss the afternoon crush. Pack layers no matter what the forecast says; the weather changes fast up here and a sunny morning can turn nasty quick.

The Dove Lake circuit is your go-to for families—it's stunning, mostly flat, and doable in under two hours if you keep the pace steady. Kids love spotting wombats and wallabies (especially early morning or late afternoon), and there's something about the mountain that just grabs them. Skip the Cradle Summit walk unless your kids are genuinely keen hikers; it's longer and steeper than people think. Bring proper water and snacks, not just what you think you'll need—the walks are more exposed than you'd expect and there's bugger all shade. The visitor centre has good info but can be touristy; grab a map and head straight out.

Watch out for the leeches after rain and wear closed shoes on wet days—they're not dangerous but kids freak out about them. Roads can ice over in winter even when it's not freezing at sea level, and mobile service is patchy. Book accommodation well ahead if you're going school holidays; everything fills up. Stay longer than a day if you can—it's worth settling in, and you'll actually see the place instead of just ticking a box.
