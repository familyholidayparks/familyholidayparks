# Blue Mountains Family Visit Guide

Best time to visit is September to November (spring) or March to May (autumn) – you'll dodge the summer crowds and crazy heat, plus the wildflowers are brilliant. Winter's beautiful but bitingly cold, especially at night, and some lookouts get icy. School holidays are absolute mayhem everywhere, so plan around them if you can. Pro tip: weekdays are your mate here. Hit the major spots like Echo Point or Scenic World early, before 9am, and you'll have way more breathing room. Bring layers no matter the season – the mountains are seriously unpredictable, and it's always cooler than you think.

Kids absolutely lose their minds over the Scenic World railway, cableway, and walkway – it's touristy but genuinely fun, and younger ones get properly tired out. Norman Lindsay Gallery's quirky enough to hold their attention, and the various waterfalls and short walks around Leura are perfect for families who don't want to trek for hours. But here's what catches people out: the bushwalks are way harder than they look on the map, trails can be slippery year-round, and kids underestimate distance badly. Always check conditions before heading out, bring proper water and snacks, and be realistic about what your crew can manage – nothing kills a day faster than a cranky, exhausted kid. Watch out for drop-offs too; they're not always fenced, and toddlers don't understand consequences.

Stay in Katoomba or Leura rather than spreading yourself thin – they're the proper hubs with decent food, shops, and accommodation options. Leura's prettier and quieter, Katoomba's more practical and has more bang-for-buck eating spots. Buy the Echo Point combo ticket if you're doing multiple attractions, eat early (places get rammed), and genuinely leave your phone out of your pocket near lookouts – the distractions aren't worth the risk. The locals know these mountains can turn on you quick, so respect them and you'll have a ripper of a family trip.
