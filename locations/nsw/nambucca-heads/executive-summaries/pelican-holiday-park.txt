PARK: Pelican Holiday Park
SCORE: 58/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-07
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 77

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (8/20) — Limited structured entertainment with families mentioning an Easter egg hunt, buggies for kids to ride, and a pool as the main activities; no evidence of kids' clubs, playgrounds, or organized programs, with most entertainment coming from self-directed fishing and river exploration.

Nature (16/20) — Exceptional riverside location on the Nambucca River with beautiful water views, abundant birdlife including pelicans and dolphins, excellent fishing access from bank/boat/jetty, peaceful natural setting with shady spots, though 6km from town and beach access.

Cleanliness (7/15) — Highly inconsistent standards with many families praising cabin cleanliness as "spotless" and "immaculate" while multiple recent reviews describe amenities blocks as "dirty," "gross," "not fit for human use" with bugs, hair, mould, and insufficient toilet paper; clear evidence of renovation in progress.

Value (8/15) — Mixed value perception with some families finding it "very affordable" and "low prices" while others describe $69-84/night for powered sites as "overpriced" and "ridiculous," particularly given the poor amenity conditions; cabin value rated more positively.

Site Size (7/10) — Family reviews mention "plenty of space for everyone to relax," "spacious" cabins suitable for families of 5-6, "good size" rooms, and riverside powered sites, though some mention hilly layout and muddy conditions after rain with limited unpowered options.

Sentiment (7/10) — Polarized sentiment with enthusiastic repeat visitors praising staff friendliness and location beauty, but significant recent negative reviews from families describing "horrible experience," "absolute worst," and leaving early; many positive family reviews from 2024-2026 balanced by serious cleanliness complaints.

Location (5/10) — Located 6km from Nambucca Heads town center and beaches, families note it's "a little outside" town with "no shops around" requiring drives for dining/shopping, though positioned conveniently between Macksville and Nambucca Heads on the Pacific Highway for stopover families.

WHY NOT 100:
The park has severely inadequate amenities facilities with only one toilet and one shower per gender in the older block, described by multiple families as "dirty," "rotting," "rusty," and "pitch black" with sensor lights that turn off, creating significant issues for families without ensuite facilities. Cleanliness standards are inconsistent across the park, with numerous families reporting uncleaned cabins containing food remnants, hair, used soap, mould, cockroaches, and overwhelming pest control chemical smells that required hours of guest cleaning. The park offers minimal organized children's entertainment or play facilities beyond a pool and occasional events, lacking the playgrounds, kids' clubs, waterslides, or structured activities that families with young children specifically seek.
