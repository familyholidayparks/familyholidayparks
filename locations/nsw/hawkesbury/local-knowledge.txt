# Visiting Hawkesbury with the Family

The best time to visit is April to May or September to October when the weather's perfect—not too hot, not too wet. Avoid summer if you can (December to February gets absolutely scorching and the river can be dodgy after heavy rain), and winter's fine but some riverside spots close or get quieter. School holidays bring absolute chaos to the main spots like Penrith Whitewater Stadium and the lookouts, so if you're flexible, travel midweek. Pro tip: the Hawkesbury River floods regularly, so always check conditions before heading to riverside activities—locals know to ring ahead rather than rock up and find access blocked.

What families genuinely love here is getting on the water, whether that's a guided canoe trip, a riverboat cruise, or just mucking about at the beaches at Wisemans Ferry and Webbs Creek. The Grose River loop walk is brilliant for kids with decent fitness, and don't skip the Blue Mountains edge stuff like the Bathurst Lookout—the views are actually mad. Local markets at Windsor are worth a Saturday visit, and the historical sites like Old Government House give you something educational between the play time. Pack plenty of water and sun cream; locals aren't shy about it because the sun here is no joke.

Watch out for the river currents and always wear life jackets—sounds obvious but tourists underestimate how serious the Hawkesbury is. Ticks are a genuine thing in the bushland, so check yourselves after walks and wear light-coloured clothes. The local roads have blind corners, especially heading into the valleys, so don't speed. And honestly, bring bug spray; the sandflies at dusk are feral. One last thing: fuel up in Windsor before heading into the back roads—petrol stations are sparse once you're out there, and mobile coverage can be patchy too.
