PARK: Tweed Holiday Parks Kingscliff North
SCORE: 73/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-04-18
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 80

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (10/20) — Reviews consistently note the park has no playground, kids club, or dedicated entertainment facilities. Families mention "kids can ride around on the concrete paths" and children "had an absolute ball" at the beach, but all entertainment relies on the beach location rather than onsite facilities.

Nature (18/20) — Outstanding natural setting with direct beach access receiving universal praise: "beach less than 20 steps away", "right on the beach", "crystal clear water", and "whales sighted from our site". Easy access to Tweed Coast natural attractions and "a short walk to the pub and shops".

Cleanliness (14/15) — Exceptional cleanliness standards with phrases like "spotlessly clean", "immaculate park", "everything was spotlessly clean", and "one of the cleanest parks we've stayed at". Modern renovated facilities consistently praised, though minor wear noted in some glamping tents.

Value (10/15) — Major concern across reviews: "very expensive", "$131 for a powered caravan site", "expensive now as the council have gotten greedy", "way overpriced", and "touch on the expensive side". Some families felt pricing fair given location and facilities, but value is a consistent complaint.

Site Size (9/10) — Generally positive feedback: "generous sized sites", "large caravan sites", "sites were very generous with their size", and "plenty of space". One review noted "a bit tight for our rig" and the park "could do with some trees" as it's somewhat open.

Sentiment (9/10) — Strong repeat visitation with "51 years" of stays, "will definitely be back", and "Every year my extended family all book sites". However, security concerns during school holidays, theft incidents, and customer service complaints ("rude", "completely unreasonable") reduced the perfect score.

Location (10/10) — Perfect beachfront positioning: "across from main street", "walking distance to shops, hotel", "right next door to the bowling club", "central to anything around the Tweed Coast area", and "easy drive to the Gold Coast".

WHY NOT 100:
[1] No onsite entertainment facilities - zero playground, pool, kids club or organized activities forcing families to rely entirely on beach access for entertainment.
[2] Premium pricing concerns - multiple families cite excessive costs ($131/night powered sites) with poor value perception, described as "outrageous" and "greedy" by long-term visitors.
[3] Security vulnerabilities - documented theft issues with low perimeter fencing, unlocked gates, no security cameras, and recurring problems with local teens accessing the park and stealing from campsites.
