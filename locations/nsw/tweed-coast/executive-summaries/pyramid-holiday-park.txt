PARK: Pyramid Holiday Park
SCORE: 62/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-24
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 285

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (8/20) — Reviewers consistently noted the park removed its playground with "sad that play area gone" and "no playground" being common complaints. Families reported limited activities beyond a pool and basic recreation room with pool table, books and DVDs, with one family stating "there wasn't really anything for our daughter to do" and signs prohibiting ball games frustrated families wanting outdoor play.

Nature (10/20) — The park is located close to beaches ("5-10 minute drive to Kirra and Coolangatta", "close to beautiful beaches") but reviewers noted it lacks natural environment within the park itself. The grounds are well-maintained with "manicured gardens" and "lush gardens" mentioned, plus lorikeet feeding as a highlight, but the park is primarily a residential caravan park under a flight path rather than a nature-focused destination.

Cleanliness (13/15) — This was a consistent strength with "spotlessly clean", "exceptionally clean", "very clean ensuite", and "cleanest cabin/accommodation I've ever stayed in" appearing frequently. Multiple reviews praised "well maintained" facilities, clean amenities blocks, and "impeccably tidy" presentation, though a few isolated complaints about bed bugs and ants in one cabin, and mould in bathroom windows.

Value (12/15) — Reviews highlighted "great value for money", "well priced", "very affordable", and competitive rates especially for budget cabins. Families appreciated the practical accommodation close to attractions, though some felt it was "very expensive for what you get" given the lack of facilities, and washing/drying at $6 per load was noted as expensive.

Site Size (6/10) — Multiple reviewers reported "very tight narrow roads", "tight site for large caravans", sites being "squishy and zero privacy", and "parking was a bit tight". Ensuite sites were adequately sized but the park caters primarily to permanent residents, leaving limited space for holidaymakers with larger rigs.

Sentiment (7/10) — Mixed sentiment with many repeat visitors praising staff friendliness and cleanliness, but significant concerns about the flight path dominated reviews. Positive: "we love it so much we won't stay anywhere else", "will definitely stay again". Negative: "wouldn't be rushing back", "would not recommend", "not that kid friendly". The park polarized guests between those unbothered by planes and those who found it unacceptable.

Location (6/10) — Excellent highway access ("seconds from the motorway") and proximity to airport ("5 minutes to airport"), shops ("IGA 2 minutes down the road"), and beaches, but the location directly under the flight path was the single most mentioned negative: "planes every 5-10 minutes from 6am til 11pm", "so low you can read the writing on them", "literally a few meters above the roof line". Many felt inadequately warned about this pre-booking.

WHY NOT 100:
1. The park is positioned directly under the Gold Coast Airport flight path with extremely low-flying aircraft every 5-10 minutes from 6am-10pm, creating noise that reviewers consistently described as disruptive, with many stating they were not adequately informed before booking and that this is "unethical" for noise-sensitive guests.

2. The playground has been removed and there are minimal on-site entertainment facilities for children, with families reporting "nothing for our daughter to do", signs prohibiting ball activities, and the park feeling more like a permanent residence park ("80% permanent residents") than a family holiday destination.

3. Multiple safety and management concerns were raised including reported bed bugs, insects in cabins, incidents of theft from camp kitchen, reports of domestic violence and unsafe atmosphere, inflexible refund policies even for medical emergencies and natural disasters, and some guests feeling the park prioritizes permanent residents over holidaymakers.
