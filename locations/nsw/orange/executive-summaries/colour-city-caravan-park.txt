PARK: Colour City Caravan Park
SCORE: 58/100
DATE SCORED: 2025
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 43

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (6/20) — Minimal on-site entertainment with only basic swings mentioned by one family reviewer; no waterpark, jumping pillow, or organised activities. Adjacent showground provides some outdoor space but the park itself offers little to keep children engaged on-site.

Nature (12/20) — Located on showground perimeter with some wildlife sightings noted and grassy sites. One reviewer mentioned "gorgeous sunrise" views. However, it's a suburban setting near roads and train lines rather than bushland or natural environment. A drive required to reach Orange's nature attractions.

Cleanliness (11/15) — Generally clean amenities with most reviews praising "spotless" showers and "very clean bathrooms." However, two significant negative reports about mouldy cabin bathrooms with grime buildup, plus one Christmas review noting "toilets not so clean" and non-functioning showers, preventing a higher score.

Value (11/15) — Excellent pricing at $41 per night for powered sites. Reviews mention "reasonable prices" and "camping pas cher" (cheap camping). However, one cabin guest paid $120/night for unsanitary conditions. Good value for caravan sites but mixed for cabin accommodation.

Site Size (9/10) — Consistently praised as "spacious," "large roomy powered sites," and "plenty of space for caravan." Concrete pads with grass. Minor deduction for site gutters mentioned as "a little annoying."

Sentiment (6/10) — Mixed sentiment with very friendly staff repeatedly mentioned but concerns about noise issues, after-hours management, and facilities needing upgrades. Several repeat visitors but also reviewers who "won't be going back." Described as functional rather than exceptional.

Location (3/10) — Significant drawbacks for families: "A drive to town still," located beside noisy train line with freight trains at night, and near busy roads. Convenient Aldi across the road and walking distance to some amenities, but not an attractive or peaceful location for a family holiday.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Virtually no children's entertainment infrastructure beyond basic swings—no waterpark, playground equipment, games room, or activities that would engage young families for extended stays.
2. Problematic location beside active freight train line causing sleep disruption, with multiple reviews citing "extremely noisy diesel freight trains," horn-blowing at night, and cabins rattling from passing trains.
3. Inconsistent cleanliness standards with serious cabin bathroom hygiene issues (mould, grime buildup) and Christmas period complaints about non-functioning showers and unruly guests with no after-hours management at this council-run facility.
