PARK: Reflections Brunswick Heads - Holiday Park
SCORE: 63/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-04-16
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 62

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (8/20) — Very limited on-site entertainment for kids; one review mentions pedal cart hire and references to a playground at the entrance possibly being upgraded for safety reasons. No pool, waterpark, or structured kids' activities mentioned. Families rely on natural play opportunities in river and at beach rather than park-provided entertainment.

Nature (18/20) — Excellent natural setting praised consistently across reviews. Park sits directly on the river/creek with beautiful sunrise views, abundant birdlife including bush turkeys, shallow river access at both tides, and 5-minute walk to beach. Multiple reviews highlight "beautiful turquoise estuary," wildlife viewing, and the option of both river and ocean swimming as major drawcards.

Cleanliness (13/15) — Generally very positive feedback on cleanliness with many reviews praising "spotless" cabins, "immaculate" facilities, and amenities "cleaned or checked several times a day." However, some reviews mention bird droppings on concrete pads, older amenities blocks that weren't as well maintained, and issues with gravel sites being dirty.

Value (7/15) — Significant concerns about pricing throughout reviews. Multiple families cite "very pricey," "way overpriced," "shameful pricing," and "price gouging" with rates of $72-$90 per night mentioned. Several reviews note this is expensive for a government-owned park with limited facilities, and pricing concerns appear consistently across 2023-2026 reviews.

Site Size (5/10) — Major weakness identified across multiple reviews. Sites described as "tiny," "on the smallish side," "crammed in like sardines," and "right on top of each other." One detailed review mentioned sites being smaller than quoted and some concrete slabs being unsuitable for camper trailers. This is consistent across older and recent reviews.

Sentiment (7/10) — Mixed sentiment overall. Many repeat visitors and positive comments about returning, but significant negative experiences around theft (multiple recent reports of stolen eskies and belongings), poor refund policies during emergencies/flooding, security concerns, and management responsiveness. Highway noise from trucks also repeatedly mentioned as sleep-disrupting issue.

Location (9/10) — Consistently praised as a top feature. Walking distance to shops, cafes, bakery, pub, IGA, BWS, footbridge, and beach. Reviews emphasize "perfect location," "everything in walking distance," and "no need to drive anywhere." Easy access to both river and ocean activities.

WHY NOT 100:
Serious and recurring theft issues from campsites with multiple families in 2025 reporting stolen eskies, drinks, and equipment overnight, with management not warning guests or taking adequate preventative measures despite acknowledging it as a known problem. High pricing ($72-$90/night) not justified by facilities offered, with consistent feedback about poor value for families especially given government ownership and lack of entertainment amenities. Cramped site sizes described as "tiny" and "crammed like sardines" make the park unsuitable for larger rigs and diminish the camping experience for families needing space.
