PARK: Reflections Ferry Reserve - Holiday Park
SCORE: 62/100
DATE SCORED: 2024-01-14
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 75

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (5/20) — No on-site kids entertainment, playground, or pool facilities mentioned across any reviews; families noted the park lacks organized activities, with one reviewer specifically citing "no pool" as a negative; recreation limited to river access and nearby town walks.

Nature (18/20) — Exceptional riverside setting with "stunning river+mountain views," "beautiful sunsets over distant hills," river swimming spots with clear water, bush walking tracks along the river, easy access to beaches (25-30 min walk), abundant birdlife, and fishing opportunities consistently praised.

Cleanliness (14/15) — Overwhelmingly positive feedback: "amenities was one of the cleanest I have seen," "spotless clean," "very clean facilities," "modern amenities," "cleaned regularly"; one negative review noted maintenance issues in tiny home with cockroaches and poor cleaning during peak season.

Value (9/15) — Mixed reviews with significant concerns: multiple guests cited "mega overpriced $75 per night," "$81 per night was a bit rich," "one of the most expensive caravan park I have stayed in" for a park without pool or beach proximity; "value for money at peak holiday rates was not there"; positive comments on pricing were rare.

Site Size (9/10) — Consistently praised "spacious" sites with "double slab" concrete pads, "lots of room between vans," sites large enough for multiple setups; family noted "big enough site" for grandparents' camper van plus tent setup.

Sentiment (8/10) — Strong repeat visitor base with many "will definitely be back" comments and "always stay here when passing"; however, several families left early or stated "will not be returning" due to highway noise; positive staff interactions and dog-friendly policy drove loyalty.

Location (7/10) — Close to Pacific Highway (major negative), "easy flat walk into town" (20-30 mins to Brunswick Heads), "short drive" to Byron Bay and beaches, but "out of town, not close to the beach" with no pool; convenient motorway access praised by travelers.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Complete absence of on-site kids entertainment, playground, or pool facilities makes this unsuitable for families seeking organized activities or water play beyond the river.
2. Pacific Highway noise is a significant and recurring complaint, with multiple families leaving early, describing it as "relentless," "war zone," and "impossible to sleep" especially for tent campers.
3. Value concerns are substantial with multiple reviewers citing high prices ($75-81/night) relative to limited facilities and distance from beaches, noting it as "one of the most expensive" parks they'd experienced without commensurate amenities.
