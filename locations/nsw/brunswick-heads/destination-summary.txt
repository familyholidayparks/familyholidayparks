Brunswick Heads has carved out a solid reputation as a holiday park destination because of its location at the convergence of the Brunswick River and the Pacific Ocean. The Reflections parks dominate the area—you've got riverside options at Ferry Reserve and Massy Greene that put you right on the water with boat ramp access, while Brunswick Heads and Brunswick Head sit closer to the beachfront precinct. What sets this place apart from other NSW coastal towns is the genuine mix of water-based recreation without the overdevelopment. You can launch a tinny from your park, walk to Main Beach within minutes, or paddle up the river into quieter reaches. The parks themselves range from powered caravan sites to self-contained cabins, with varying levels of facilities—some are straightforward caravan parks, others lean more resort-style with pools and amenities blocks that actually get maintained properly.

The crowd here is genuinely mixed, which is part of the appeal. You'll see long-term caravanners who've booked the same site for years, families who treat it as their annual pilgrimage during winter school holidays, and motorhome tourists working their way up the coast. School holidays fill the parks fast—you need to book months ahead if you want January or July—but shoulder seasons are perfectly pleasant and considerably quieter. The repeat visitor rate is noticeably high; people come back because the parks aren't trying to be Gold Coast glitz, they're functional bases for actually doing things. You get river fishing, beach swimming, reasonable cafes and takeaway joints within walking distance, and enough space that you're not packed in like a carpark.

Brunswick Heads works particularly well for families because there's genuine separation between water activities and accommodation without needing to drive between them. The river is calm for kids learning to fish or paddleboard, Main Beach suits swimmers and surfers depending on conditions, and the hinterland's fifteen minutes away if you want to escape onto rainforest walks. The parks have kept their character—they're not franchise operations trying to look identical to parks in five other towns. Local knowledge matters here; the difference between Ferry Reserve's river frontage and the beachside parks isn't cosmetic, it changes what your week actually looks like. People return because they know exactly what they're getting, the parks are reliably maintained, and Brunswick Heads itself hasn't been chewed up by poker machine joints and chicken shops the way some holiday towns have.
