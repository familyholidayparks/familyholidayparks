# Visiting Merimbula with Kids: Local Intel

Best time to visit is late September through October or April to May – you'll dodge the school holiday madness and still get decent weather. December to January gets absolutely rammed with families, prices spike, and the beaches get crowded fast. Winter's mild enough for a visit, though the water's bloody cold. Pro tip: book accommodation well ahead if you're coming during school holidays; everything decent fills up months out. The weather's pretty reliable most of the year, but we do get some wild southerlies that can close the beaches, so check conditions before you head down.

What families actually love here is the combination of safe beaches, rock pools teeming with sea life, and the Merimbula Aquarium – kids go nuts for it. Lake Merimbula's calm waters are perfect for younger swimmers, while Pambula Beach has better waves for the older kids. The playground at the foreshore is solid, and walking the headland trails is free entertainment that burns off heaps of energy. The local fish and chips from spots around the marina are genuinely good; skip the tourist traps and ask locals where they eat. Grab fresh produce from the farmers market on weekends if you're self-catering.

Watch out for rips at the ocean beaches – seriously, always swim between the flags and keep an eye on conditions. The rock pools are brilliant but slippery as hell, so proper water shoes are non-negotiable. Parking can be tight during peak times, so arrive early or use the foreshore car park rather than fighting for spots near the beach. One insider move: head to Tura Beach just south – it's less crowded than Merimbula proper but equally good, and the locals know it as the better-kept secret. Also, bring decent sunscreen and reapply constantly; the UV here is no joke.
