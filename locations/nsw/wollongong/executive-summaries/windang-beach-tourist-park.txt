PARK: Windang Beach Tourist Park
SCORE: 71/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-23
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 121

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (8/20) — Very limited on-site entertainment with only a basic children's playground mentioned; families primarily rely on natural activities like beach play, fishing, kayaking, and visits to nearby Windang Bowls Club. No waterpark, jumping pillow, or organized kids activities noted.

Nature (20/20) — Exceptional natural setting with direct access to both Lake Illawarra and Windang Beach, creating a "true escape" between calm lake waters and surf. Reviews consistently praise the unique waterfront location, fishing opportunities, wildlife including pelicans and seals, beautiful sunsets, and proximity to waterfalls.

Cleanliness (13/15) — Generally clean facilities with many reviews praising "spotlessly clean" amenities and "very clean" cabins, though several recent reviews note inconsistent maintenance including blocked urinals, sites with mostly dirt, rundown permanents, and occasional cleanliness issues like hairs on sheets.

Value (12/15) — Reasonable pricing noted by many families with cabins around $159/night, though some felt $100/night for a family of four off-peak was overpriced for what's offered. Generally considered good value given the location, but not exceptional compared to facilities provided.

Site Size (9/10) — Consistently praised for "nice big flat sites," "massive" camp kitchen, "big drive thru sites," and "spacious" cabins. Sites accommodate large caravans with awnings out and car parking alongside, though some mention cramped cabin layouts for larger families.

Sentiment (7/10) — Strong positive sentiment from long-term returners (35 years, 4 years) and families extending stays, but tempered by several negative experiences with booking cancellations, unsuitable accommodations for infants, uncomfortable beds, and neighbor issues that caused early departures.

Location (10/10) — Perfect location score with universal praise for being nestled between lake and beach, walking distance to shops, bowling club, cafes, and boat ramp. Easy access to Wollongong (15 mins) and Kiama (20 mins), plus bike paths and recreation areas.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Complete absence of on-site kids entertainment facilities beyond a basic playground - no waterpark, pool, games room, jumping pillow, or organized activities that families with young children typically seek at holiday parks.
2. Inconsistent cleanliness and maintenance standards with recent reports of blocked amenities, dirty sites next to rundown permanents, grass needing mowing, and some cabins showing significant age with musty smells, hard beds, and dated furnishings.
3. No wifi availability throughout the park and weak mobile reception creates connectivity challenges for modern families, with multiple reviews specifically noting this as a significant downside.

NOTABLE REVIEW PHRASES:
"children's playground," "stayed here over the school holidays with my 2 sons," "beach every day," "kids friendly play area right outside," "Great for the kids plenty to do," "amenities weren't cleaned well or often," "no wifi and very weak mobile networks," "rows and rows of onsite cabins and caravans... looks like something out the 1990's"
