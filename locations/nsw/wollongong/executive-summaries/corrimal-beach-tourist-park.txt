PARK: Corrimal Beach Tourist Park
SCORE: 68/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-20
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 205

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (8/20) — Reviews consistently note "no entertainment" and "not much for kids to do" beyond the beach itself. The playground is described as "needing maintenance" and "aimed at the little ones" with multiple mentions that families must self-entertain. No pool, waterpark, or organised activities were mentioned, with one reviewer stating "no entertainment, just wash and toilet block."

Nature (18/20) — The park's standout feature with "steps from the beach," "direct access to Corrimal Beach," and "right on the beach" appearing throughout reviews. Families praise the "patrolled beach," "ocean pool nearby," "beautiful beach," and "great bike paths north and south" along the coast. Multiple reviews highlight hearing the waves at night and the natural beachfront setting as major drawcards.

Cleanliness (11/15) — Mixed feedback with newer Block 4 amenities described as "spotless," "exceptionally clean," and "really modern, clean with great shower pressure." However, significant concerns include one family finding "8 cockroaches," "bathrooms were always dirty," "broken tiles," and reviews noting amenities "need major repairs" and are "generally run down with dirty ceilings." Inconsistent cleaning standards across different accommodation types.

Value (13/15) — Strong value proposition with reviews praising "$35 unpowered site," "very reasonably priced," "great value for money," and "council run caravan park, so cheaper rates." However, cabin guests felt cabins were "absolutely not worth the VERY high price tag" and "too expensive for what they offer," with one stating they "could have easily stayed in a very nice hotel for the same price."

Site Size (9/10) — Consistently praised with "big sites," "huge campsites," "generous size site," "very large generous spots," "large area with room all around us," and "really big sites" mentioned repeatedly. Families with large rigs appreciated the "spacious" layout and ability to fit "van, annexe and car."

Sentiment (7/10) — Polarised feedback with many loyal repeat visitors ("been here multiple times for the last 20 years," "we come every year for the past 3 yrs") but significant concerns about security dominate recent reviews. Multiple theft incidents ("kids' bikes were stolen," "mountain bikes stolen worth $6,000," "people going through their stuff at 2am") severely impact family sentiment, with several stating they "would never stay again" due to crime.

Location (10/10) — Perfect scores for location with "perfect location 100m walk to the beach," "great location," "walking distance to local shops," "close to Wollongong," "Woolies a 7 min drive away," and "on the great walking path" consistently mentioned. Families appreciate proximity to cafes, bowling club, and coastal attractions.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Security and theft is a major recurring problem with multiple families reporting stolen bikes, scooters, and belongings being rifled through, creating significant anxiety for families with children and valuable equipment.
2. No pool or water play facilities beyond the ocean, and the playground is basic and "needing maintenance," leaving families entirely dependent on beach activities with no wet-weather alternatives.
3. Cleanliness is highly inconsistent, with some cabins described as "atrocious" with cockroaches and broken fixtures, while camping amenities vary from "spotless" to "run down," creating an unreliable experience for families.
