# Visiting Wollongong with the Kids

Best time to visit is late September through November (spring) or March to May (autumn) – you'll dodge the brutal summer heat and winter rain while getting perfect beach weather. School holidays are packed, so if you can avoid them, do. December to February gets scorching and the beaches cop dangerous rips, plus accommodation prices go mental. The shoulder seasons give you sunshine without the crowds and the ocean's actually swimmable without needing to check conditions constantly.

Stick to the obvious spots like Austinvilla Estate and Illawong Beach for families – they're patrolled by lifeguards and have proper facilities. Skip the sketchy car parks at night and don't leave valuables visible in your car, it's just common sense anywhere these days. The walk along the seafront from the city centre to Belmore Basin is brilliant for kids, flat and safe with cafes dotted along it. Grab lunch at one of the harbourside spots rather than the overpriced tourist traps – you'll eat better and cheaper. Pro tip: bring a wetsuit even in autumn; the water's colder than you'd expect coming down from Sydney, and the wind can kick up without warning.

What families absolutely love is how unpretentious it all is – no crowds like the Central Coast, proper local beaches, and heaps of free stuff to do. The escarpment hikes are genuinely spectacular if your kids can handle them, and Wollongong Botanic Garden is free entry. Watch out for the summer school holidays (mid-December to early February) when it's rammed, and be aware the weather can turn on you quickly with storms rolling in from the ocean. Honestly, treat it like a real coastal town rather than a theme park, and you'll have a ripper of a time.
