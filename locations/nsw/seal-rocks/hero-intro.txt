# Seal Rocks, NSW

Seal Rocks is a laid-back coastal village about three hours north of Sydney that ticks the boxes for families after a proper beach holiday. The area's got excellent swimming beaches with rock pools perfect for kids to explore, plus quieter spots if you want to escape the crowds. The village itself is small and manageable, so you won't be spending half your time driving between attractions, and there's a genuine community feel that makes families feel welcome rather than just tolerated.

The real draw here is the mix of beach time and easy nature access. You can spend mornings at the beach and afternoons walking through coastal bushland where you might spot native birds and wildlife. Reflections Seal Rocks Holiday Park and Treachery Camp offer solid accommodation options for families, with the parks themselves providing that base camp feel kids enjoy. The local fishing village character means decent fish and chips, a general store that actually stocks what you need, and enough space for kids to burn off energy without you needing to referee crowds.
