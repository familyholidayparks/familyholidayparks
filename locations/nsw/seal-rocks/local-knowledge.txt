# Seal Rocks Local Guide

Seal Rocks is best visited between September and April when the weather's mild and the water's actually swimmable – winter's pretty brutal here and the ocean gets absolutely freezing. Time your trip for low tide if you want to explore the rock pools properly; grab a tide table before you head out. The rock pools around the main beach are gold for families – heaps of fish, sea stars, and sometimes little octopuses if you're lucky. Just watch your kids closely on the rocks because they're slippery as hell even when they look dry, and the ocean can sneak up quick if the swell's running. Bring reef shoes or old sneakers, not thongs.

Pack more snacks and water than you think you'll need because there's bugger all up there – no shops, no cafes, just you and the rocks. The main beach is stunning but can get choppy, so stick to the protected pools for swimming with little ones. What locals love is how quiet it stays; it's not the crowded zoo that some NSW beaches become. Fair warning though: the road in is rough and winding, so leave the low-slung sports car at home and drive carefully. Also, the sun here is no joke – slap on sunscreen constantly because the reflection off the rocks will burn you crispy in no time. And keep an eye out for snakes in the warmer months, especially around the grass areas. Stay on the main paths and you'll be fine, but it's worth knowing.
