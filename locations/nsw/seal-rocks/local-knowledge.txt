G'day fellow adventurers! Seal Rocks is one of those magical pockets of the NSW Mid North Coast where your kids can spot dolphins from the breakfast table and spend their arvo beachcombing on beaches that still feel like a well-kept secret. We reckon April through October is your sweet spot for visiting – the humpback whales cruise past Sugarloaf Point Lighthouse, the summer holiday crowds have cleared out, and you'll actually snag a park at Treachery Beach without the early morning stress. Here's a local tip that'll make you look like a legend: grab fish and chips from the Blueys Beach Cafe on Friday night (they do a ripper grilled fish for the healthier crew), then head to Number One Beach for sunset – the rock pools on the southern end are brilliant for little explorers when the tide's going out.
