PARK: Treachery Camp Seal Rocks
SCORE: 58/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-18
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 40

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (8/20) — No organized kids activities, playground, or waterpark mentioned; entertainment is purely nature-based with wildlife spotting (dingoes, goannas, sea turtles), beach access, fishing, sand dunes, and campfires. One review noted "Great for families" but context suggests self-directed outdoor activities rather than structured entertainment. Tim's café provides food but no family entertainment programs.

Nature (19/20) — Exceptional natural environment consistently praised across reviews: "perfectly tucked away in the forest", "teeming with life", "pristine beaches", "gorgeous wild beach", "lots of wildlife" including dingoes, goannas, sea turtles, kookaburras, and whales (in season). Walking distance to beach via sand dunes, beautiful fig trees, lighthouse nearby. Only minor point lost for mosquitoes and midges mentioned in multiple reviews.

Cleanliness (10/15) — Mixed feedback: positive mentions of "very clean" amenities, "well maintained" facilities, "fantastic" showers with great pressure and hot water. However, multiple reviews noted "basic" amenities, one stated "camp isn't very tidy", another mentioned facilities "could use a little improvement". Cleanliness appears adequate but not exceptional, with maintenance concerns on access roads.

Value (7/15) — Significant value concerns in multiple reviews: "Very overpriced", "too expensive for what it is", "$88 for 2 weekend nights", "overpriced and overcrowded", "$17 for firewood", "charging a dollar for the bbq is almost insulting". Several reviewers explicitly stated better value available at nearby national parks. Pricing around $22-50 per night for basic unpowered camping on unmarked sites.

Site Size (4/10) — Major concern across reviews: "no designated camp sites", "free for all", "people tend to spread out too much", "struggled to find a level camping spot", "patch of sandy dirt", "sites are unmarked and rough". Multiple reviewers advised arriving early to secure flat spots. No allocated sites means inconsistent space and organization issues noted repeatedly.

Sentiment (6/10) — Divided sentiment: many 5-star reviews from surf/nature enthusiasts praising the location, but critical reviews from families noting noise issues ("kept awake until 2am", "party campground", "drunk and rowdy"), overbooking problems, and poor management ("Manager...is rude", "lax enforcement"). Recent reviews skew more positive but operational concerns persist. Several "would return" statements balanced by "would not recommend" reviews.

Location (4/10) — Mixed accessibility: beautiful coastal location near Seal Rocks beaches and lighthouse, but "last few ks...very badly maintained dirt" road, "road is unkept", "bumpy" access. No cell service/wifi noted by multiple reviewers. Beach is 10-minute walk over dunes. Close beaches not ideal for family swimming ("not a swimming beach", "too rough"), requiring 3-5 minute drive to better swimming spots. Remote feel appreciated by some, limiting for families needing amenities.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Complete absence of organized children's entertainment, playground equipment, or family activity programs—families must create their own entertainment through nature activities, limiting appeal for families with young children who need structured activities.

2. Unallocated camping sites create chaos and inequality, with reviewers consistently reporting difficulty finding level spots, people spreading out excessively, overbooking issues, and poor site management that particularly disadvantages families arriving with larger rigs or later in the day.

3. Value proposition is weak for families—basic facilities with no designated sites, coin-operated BBQs, expensive firewood ($17), minimal amenities, and pricing ($44-88 per night) that reviewers repeatedly stated was overpriced compared to better-equipped nearby alternatives, especially national park campgrounds.
