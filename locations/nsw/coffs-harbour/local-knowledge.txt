# Visiting Coffs Harbour with Kids

The best time to visit is September through November (spring) or April to May (autumn) – the weather's brilliant, the water's warm enough for swimming, and you'll dodge the school holidays chaos and summer humidity. Avoid December to February if you can, as it gets scorching, the beaches get rammed, and accommodation prices go mental. Winter's mild and fine for exploring, though the ocean can be a bit bracing. Pro tip: book accommodation early even for shoulder season – this place fills up quicker than you'd think, especially around Easter and the May school holidays.

Here's what actually matters when you're here with kids: the Big Banana is a bit kitschy but honestly, toddlers lose their minds over it, so don't skip it just to be cool. Hit Diggers Beach or Jetty Beach early in the day before families arrive; the rock pools are gold for exploring. Watch out for strong rips in summer – always swim between the flags and check conditions with the lifeguards. The Coffs Harbour Jetty is perfect for a walk and feeding the fish, but grab fish food from the shops nearby rather than bringing your own. Stock up on sunscreen and reef-safe stuff; the UV is no joke here, and the coral matters.

One honest thing: parking at popular beaches can be a nightmare during peak times, so arrive before 10am or after 3pm. The Solitary Islands Marine Park is spectacular for snorkeling if your kids are confident swimmers – it's genuinely world-class and worth the effort. Grab fresh fish and chips from any of the local spots near the wharf (way better than chain places), and don't miss the farmers markets on weekends. Finally, build in at least 2-3 days here; one day isn't enough to appreciate why locals rate it so highly.
