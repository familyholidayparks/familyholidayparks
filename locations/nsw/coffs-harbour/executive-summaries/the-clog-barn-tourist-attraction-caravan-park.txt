PARK: The Clog Barn Tourist Attraction & Caravan Park
SCORE: 58/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-21
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 332

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (14/20) — Families consistently praise the free miniature Dutch village as the standout attraction, with kids loving the detailed model buildings, clog-making demonstrations, and fish feeding at the pond. However, multiple recent reviews note interactive displays aren't working and the attraction needs maintenance. The cafe offers popular pancakes and Dutch treats but this is primarily a tourist stop rather than a full entertainment facility.

Nature (11/20) — Located on main road through Coffs Harbour with some bush outlook from cafe deck and wildlife (lizards, water dragons, birds) spotted around the miniature village gardens. Not a natural bushland or beachside setting, more of an urban stopover position. Some mention of creek alongside cafe but limited natural environment overall.

Cleanliness (9/15) — Very mixed feedback with amenities described as "spotless" and "very clean" by some, but multiple recent reviews cite ants in cabins, mould on shower curtains, dirty floors, unclean couches, and dog hair on blankets. Permanent residents' untidy sites also noted as detracting from presentation. Maintenance issues with buildings looking "run down" and needing refresh.

Value (10/15) — Generally considered affordable and good value at around $45 for powered sites, with free entry to miniature village. Some reviews mention cabin prices rising during holidays ($130 to $245) which disappointed guests. Food portions described as generous and reasonably priced. Budget-friendly option but accommodation quality doesn't always match expectations.

Site Size (6/10) — Sites described as "small" and "a bit wet" in rain, though "generous in size and fairly level" by others. One cabin review noted "extremely minimal" space for 3 adults and 4 children. Mixed evidence suggests adequate but not spacious sites, with some grass and concrete slab options.

Sentiment (5/10) — Strongly polarised reviews with many 5-star ratings for the attraction itself but significant 1-star accommodation complaints about cleanliness, ants, and maintenance. Repeat visitors love the cafe and miniature village, but several recent guests say they "won't be back" after disappointing cabin stays. Service quality also inconsistent with some staff described as rude or disinterested.

Location (3/10) — Right on main Pacific Highway through Coffs Harbour, convenient for stopover but not a destination location. About 20-minute walk from city centre, close to Park Beach Plaza and shops. Not near beach or in natural setting. Good for breaking up a coastal drive but noisy from road and not a holiday destination spot.

WHY NOT 100:
1. This is fundamentally a roadside tourist attraction with basic caravan facilities rather than a purpose-built family holiday park, lacking dedicated kids' entertainment areas, water features, or organized activities that families expect.
2. Cleanliness and maintenance issues dominate recent reviews, with multiple reports of ants in cabins, mould, dirty surfaces, broken interactive displays, and untidy permanent resident areas significantly impacting the family experience.
3. The location on a busy main road lacks the natural environment, beach access, or bushland setting that Australian families prioritize for holidays, functioning better as a quirky overnight stopover than a multi-day destination.
