PARK: Reflections Lennox Head - Holiday Park
SCORE: 62/100
DATE SCORED: 2025-05-28
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 166

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (8/20) — Limited on-site entertainment for kids; one review mentioned "fantastic range of Easter Holiday activities" during school holidays with pedal carts, paddleboards and kayaks for hire at $10/h, but multiple families noted "no playground or pool on site" and the park "feels aimed more at older travelers rather than families." Movie area mentioned but minimal permanent kids facilities.

Nature (18/20) — Outstanding natural environment with direct access to Lake Ainsworth (tea-tree lake) and surf beach across the road. Reviews consistently praised "right on Lake Ainsworth," "tea tree water," "paperbark trees," "gorgeous lake," and "dog friendly beach." Close to Byron Bay (20 minutes) and coastal walking tracks to Ballina. Natural setting is the park's strongest feature.

Cleanliness (8/15) — Highly polarized feedback; many praised "regularly cleaned" amenities and "spotless facilities," but significant number reported "cabin was filthy," "dirt under bed," "mouldy blinds," "dog hair on blankets," "kitchen cupboards rotting," and "floors bathroom toilet all appeared unclean." Caravan site amenities generally cleaner than cabin accommodation. Recent reviews show ongoing inconsistency.

Value (7/15) — Frequent complaints about pricing: "VERY expensive," "very overpriced for a caravan park," "$249 a night for standard cabin," "paid over $1000 for the stay," "could have stayed at a 5 star resort for same price." Many felt quality didn't match premium pricing, especially for cabin condition. Some found camping sites reasonably priced in off-peak.

Site Size (7/10) — Mixed feedback on site size; "large campsites," "decent sized sites," "nice big sites" mentioned by caravan users, but tent campers complained "small sites," "sites flooded," "very close to one another, so not much privacy," and "congested site." Powered site spacing adequate but not generous.

Sentiment (7/10) — Divided sentiment; many repeat visitors love the location and return annually, but significant number said "would not return," "won't be staying again," and "wouldn't recommend" primarily due to cabin condition and value concerns. Strong positive sentiment for location, negative for cabin maintenance and pricing. Dog owners particularly loyal.

Location (10/10) — Universally praised as the park's best feature: "location location location," "perfect location," "great location," "amazing location right next to the lake and beach," "walking distance to town," "close to all amenities restaurants pubs." 15-minute walk to village, short drive to Woolworths, close to Byron Bay and Ballina.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Significant cleanliness and maintenance issues in cabin accommodation with multiple reports of filthy conditions, mould, broken fixtures, worn furniture, and unacceptable hygiene standards that don't match the premium prices charged.
2. Minimal on-site entertainment facilities for families with young children—no permanent playground, no pool, limited activities outside school holiday periods, making it less suitable than other family holiday parks.
3. Poor infrastructure maintenance throughout the park with consistent complaints about "huge potholes," "roads in terrible shape," inadequate drainage causing flooding, broken amenity fixtures, and deferred maintenance issues affecting the overall guest experience.

NOTABLE REVIEW PHRASES:
"right on Lake Ainsworth," "cabin was filthy," "roads full of potholes," "no playground or pool for kids," "very overpriced," "location is just amazing," "wouldn't recommend the cabins," "dog friendly beach," "fantastic range of Easter activities," "tea-tree water paperbark trees," "below standard quality for hotel price," "walking distance to everything"
