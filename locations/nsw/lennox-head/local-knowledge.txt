# Lennox Head Family Guide

**Best times to visit are September to November and March to May** – you'll dodge the summer crowds and the cyclone risk that can kick up between December and February. School holidays get rammed, so if you can swing visiting outside those weeks, you'll find better parking at the beach and shorter queues at cafes. Winter's mild here, so don't write off June to August; the water's cooler but still swimmable for most kids, and you get crisp mornings perfect for walking.

**The beaches are your main drawcard, but know the rips.** Lennox Head Beach is patrolled and great for families, but that easterly swell can surprise you – keep kids close and always swim between the flags. Seven Mile Beach down south is wider and gentler if you've got littlies. Pro tip: grab fish and chips from the local shops rather than the tourist traps; check out what's actually fresh that day. The headland walk is stunning and doable with kids – takes about 45 minutes return, mostly flat, and you'll spot dolphins if you're lucky.

**Watch out for school holidays and long weekends** – the town fills up fast and accommodation books out months ahead. The roads aren't great when it's busy, so leave early if you're heading anywhere. Bring good sunscreen and a hat; the UV here is brutal. Book your accommodation and restaurant spots before you arrive, don't assume you'll just rock up. If you're keen on a quieter vibe, avoid Easter and July school breaks entirely – that's when half of Brisbane descends on us.
