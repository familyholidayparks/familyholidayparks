PARK: Wangi Point Holiday Park
SCORE: 58/100
DATE SCORED: 2024-01-15
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 60

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (8/20) — Very limited on-site entertainment with only a basic playground, BBQ areas, and fire pits mentioned; one family noted 'lots of thing for kids' but no structured activities, kids' clubs, or waterpark facilities are evident in reviews.

Nature (18/20) — Exceptional natural setting directly on Lake Macquarie with 'beautiful lake views', 'plentiful and friendly bird life', pontoon access, jetty, and proximity to bush reserves; families praised the scenic location, fishing opportunities, and wildlife encounters including ducks and possums.

Cleanliness (11/15) — Mixed feedback with many praising 'very clean toilets and shower block' and 'cleanest amenities in NSW', but several families reported issues including 'abit dirty', 'flooded and muddy campsites' during Easter, and 'scruffy residence vans/homes crap laying every where'.

Value (8/15) — Families expressed concerns about pricing with multiple reviews mentioning '$60 per night which is a bit pricey', '$54 per night powered for a slab & a piece of dirt', and '$21 plus for one night' ensuite fees being excessive compared to other parks.

Site Size (6/10) — Limited positive feedback on site size with mentions of 'tight spots to park a caravan', 'roads in the park are tight', and no specific praise for spaciousness from families; some sites described as basic slabs.

Sentiment (4/10) — Highly polarized reviews with strong negative experiences significantly impacting family sentiment; multiple 1-star reviews citing 'WORST customer service', 'extremely rude' management, 'not recommend for family camping people like us', safety concerns with 'drunken youth hang around', theft, and domestic violence incidents.

Location (3/10) — Remote location noted as 'not much is nearby, 15 mins away for most main shops', 'a bit remote', with limited immediate access to family attractions, though proximity to lake and bush reserves was appreciated.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Serious and recurring management issues with multiple families reporting 'extremely rude' customer service, intimidating behavior, and poor conflict resolution that significantly impacted family experiences, particularly during peak holiday periods.

2. Safety and security concerns raised by families including 'drunken youth hang around the park very late and noisy', theft of personal items, domestic violence incident at 4:30am with no management follow-up, and 'flooded and muddy' campsites creating unsafe conditions.

3. Minimal family-specific entertainment infrastructure with no waterpark, kids' club, organized activities, or modern play facilities that families with young children typically seek in holiday parks, relying solely on natural environment for entertainment.
