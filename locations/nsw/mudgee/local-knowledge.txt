# Visiting Mudgee with the Kids

**Best times to visit** are spring (September-November) and autumn (March-May) when the weather's cracking and the wineries aren't packed with tour groups. Summer gets hot and crowded, winter can be chilly but quiet if you don't mind layering up. School holidays will be mental – book accommodation early or you'll be stuck with overpriced dumps. The Mudgee Wine Festival in September is brilliant if you've got someone to watch the kids, but it's madness if you're trying to move around town.

**What families actually enjoy here** isn't the wine – it's the outdoors. The walk to Platypus Point near Cudgegong River is perfect for spotting wildlife, Perseverance Dam is great for a swim in warmer months, and the old Mudgee Pottery site has decent trails. Main Street's got decent cafes where you can get a proper flat white, and the local fruit and veg shops are quality. Stay at a farm stay if you can – the kids will be entertained for hours, and honestly, it's better value than overpriced B&Bs.

**Watch out for** the summer heat – it gets to 35+ degrees and the sun's brutal. Bring proper sunscreen and hats, not the fancy reef-safe stuff you've forgotten in Sydney. If you're heading bush walking, tell someone where you're going and bring plenty of water. The main roads around here get dodgy in rain, so don't be a hero driving to remote spots in bad weather. And here's the insider tip: skip the main tourist wineries and do a self-guided drive through the smaller family-friendly ones – better wine, nicer people, and the kids can run around.
