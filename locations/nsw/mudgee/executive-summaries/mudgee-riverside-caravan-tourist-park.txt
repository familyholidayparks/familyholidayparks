PARK: Mudgee Riverside Caravan & Tourist Park
SCORE: 58/100
DATE SCORED: 2024-01-14
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 115

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (2/20) — Multiple reviews explicitly state "no activities etc for children" and families noted difficulty finding the playground. No mention of kids' club, waterpark, or organized activities. One family review mentioned "children had trouble finding the playground in the caravan park."

Nature (16/20) — Strong riverside location with "surrounded by nature," "lovely riverside walk," "beautiful shady sites," and "Access to 6 Klms of level walking tracks at the back gate." Park described as "well treed" with "lots of shady trees" and "situated by the river with a lovely paved walking path."

Cleanliness (9/15) — Mixed feedback with many noting "amenities are old but super clean" and "kept spotless," but significant concerns about dated facilities. Multiple reviews cite "amenities block is outdated and should be replaced," "dry rot in the showers," and cabins with "mould on the ceiling." Generally clean but aging infrastructure.

Value (11/15) — Reviews split on value with prices ranging $42-$180/night. Some praised it as "very good value for money" and "reasonable price," while others felt "expensive" for dated facilities. Comments like "not worth $42.00 for a grass/gravel site" and "$165 a night" for poorly maintained cabins suggest inconsistent value.

Site Size (7/10) — Generally positive with "good sized van sites," "lovely, big spots," "sites were quite spacious," though some complained of "very overcrowded park, with campers sandwiched in" and sites that "didn't allow me to both park & put my awning out."

Sentiment (7/10) — Moderate positive sentiment with many repeat visitors saying "we always stay here" and "would stay again," but tempered by recent reviews noting decline and aging facilities. Strong location loyalty offset by facility frustrations.

Location (10/10) — Universally praised as "excellent location," "in the heart of Mudgee," "walking distance to town," "5-10 min walking distance," and "one street back from the main street." Consistent 5-star feedback on proximity to shops, pubs, wineries, and riverside trails.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Complete absence of children's entertainment facilities and activities - multiple families explicitly noted "no activities etc for children" and difficulty finding even a basic playground, making this unsuitable for families with young children seeking entertainment.
2. Severely dated amenities and cabins requiring urgent investment - consistent complaints about "amenities from the 70s," "dry rot in the showers," cabins "well past use by date," and facilities described as "a disgrace" that "should be replaced," significantly impacting the guest experience despite cleanliness efforts.
3. Inconsistent value proposition with pricing not matching facility quality - reviews cite $165-$180/night for poorly maintained cabins with maintenance issues, broken fixtures, and basic inventory, while powered sites at $42-$45 are considered expensive for aging amenities and overcrowded conditions.
