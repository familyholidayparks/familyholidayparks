# South West Rocks: What You Need to Know

**Best times to visit are September to November and February to April** – you'll dodge the summer holidays madness and the school holidays crowds. December to January gets rammed, accommodation prices shoot up, and the beaches get packed. Winter's mild down here, so it's actually perfect if your kids don't mind cooler water. Watch out for storms in autumn though; they can kick up quickly. The rock pools are best explored at low tide, so check the tide times before you head out – getting caught on the rocks when the tide comes in isn't fun. Trial Bay Gaol is genuinely worth a visit (kids find it spooky in a good way), and the Smoky Cape lighthouse walk is short enough for little legs but rewards you with killer views.

**Locals' real talk: the water's bloody cold even in summer**, so pack wetsuits or rashies if your kids are keen swimmers. There's a patrolled beach at Horseshoe Bay and Greystones, but rips can be strong – never assume it's safe just because it looks calm. Stock up on supplies in town before you settle in; the shops are decent but prices creep up during school holidays. The Arakoon area has great picnic spots and easier walks, but bring your own water because there's not much shade. Accommodation fills up fast during holidays, so book early. One solid local tip: the fish are biting at South West Rocks Wharf early mornings – grab some bait and let the kids try their hand at fishing; it's free entertainment that actually works.
