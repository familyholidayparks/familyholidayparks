# Visiting Kingscliff with the Family

**Best times to visit are September through November and February through April** – you'll dodge the brutal summer crowds and heat while still getting decent weather for the beach. School holidays are mental, especially Christmas and winter break, so unless you enjoy gridlocked traffic and packed beaches, plan around them. Summer (Dec-Feb) gets scorching and the water can have the occasional jellyfish, so if you're going then, check the conditions beforehand and stick to patrolled beaches. Winter is quieter and lovely, though the water's chilly for little ones.

**What families actually love here** is the laid-back vibe – Kingscliff Beach is perfect for kids with its calm, patrolled waters and rock pools that are endlessly entertaining. The beachfront has good facilities, decent playgrounds, and you can walk the coastal path to nearby Casuarina without drama. Hit the local IGA or farmers markets for decent produce, grab fish and chips from the beach shops, and don't miss the cafes along the strip for proper coffee. The rock pools at the southern end are goldmines for spotting crabs and fish – kids lose hours there.

**Watch out for**: parking can be tight during peak times, so arrive early or use the council car parks rather than street parking. The sun here is no joke – slip, slop, slap is non-negotiable, and the UV is intense even on cloudy days. Check the Beachsafe website for current conditions before you go, especially regarding marine stingers. Local tip: if you want a quieter experience, head to the beach early (before 9am) or late afternoon, and explore weekday mornings when it's just locals and a few tourists.
