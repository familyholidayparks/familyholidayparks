# Kingscliff, NSW: A Family Holiday Spot

Kingscliff is a relaxed beachside town on the far north coast that ticks the boxes for families wanting sun, sand and a bit of breathing room. The main beach is patrolled by lifeguards and backed by a solid rock pool perfect for younger kids, while the quieter northern beaches offer rock pools and rockhopping opportunities. You've got the Kingscliff Headland walk for easy coastal views, the Summergrove Historic House if anyone's keen on a bit of local history, and the Cudgen Estuary nearby for spotting birdlife. The town itself is compact enough that you're not spending half your holiday driving between attractions, yet has enough going on to keep everyone occupied without breaking the bank.

The caravan parks here are geared toward families, with Tweed Holiday Parks running two solid options at Kingscliff North and Kingscliff Beach, plus Ingenia Holidays Kingscliff offering well-maintained facilities. Most have playgrounds, pools, and direct beach access, which means you can actually let the kids burn energy without constant supervision. The beaches are protected from major swells, making them genuinely swimmable for younger children, and there's enough natural bushland around the headlands for exploring without venturing too far. It's the kind of place where you can slip into a rhythm pretty quickly, which is really what a family holiday is about.
