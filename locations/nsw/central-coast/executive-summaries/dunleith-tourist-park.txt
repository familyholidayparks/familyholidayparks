PARK: Dunleith Tourist Park
SCORE: 58/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-10
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 85

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (4/20) — Multiple reviews explicitly state "limited activities for kids no pool just the lake" and "no kids playground or anything." One reviewer noted "would have loved a small playground in the park for kids" with playground only "located right next to the park" outside the security gate. The games room was described as non-functional with "none of the machines worked and the pool table was missing a ball."

Nature (18/20) — Exceptional natural setting with "beautiful waterfront location" on Lake Macquarie with "waterfront sites" offering direct lake access. Reviews praise "excellent for fishing," "good for fishing and kayaking," and families enjoyed "catching fish the kids had a great time catching them right at the van." Beach access "within walking distance" and "close to marine wildlife reserve and oven beach."

Cleanliness (13/15) — Amenities consistently described as "very clean," "spotless," and "modern and clean." However, one family noted "children shower is dirty with a lot of insects" and another found "plastic rubbish and ciggie butts was on all the campsites." One review mentioned "slippery and dangerous" floor tiles. Overall maintenance praised with "staff busy keeping park up to scratch all day."

Value (8/15) — Significant concerns about pricing with "cabins are ridiculously small for the ridiculously expensive cost" and "expensive for camping consider the space and the amenities." One reviewer stated "$58 a night Dunleith and $40 at other parks some with ocean frontage. Sadly we can't afford to come back." Described as "on the expensive side" though some found "reasonable rates" off-season.

Site Size (5/10) — Major issues for families with larger rigs: "Do not attend this park if your caravan is over 25 feet," "sites can be tight," "small caravan sites and very close near neighbours." The cabins are "ridiculously small" where "bedroom door doesn't close as the bed is in the way" and "impossible for a family (2 adults & 3 kids) to stay." Tractor parking service exists because sites are so tight.

Sentiment (7/10) — Mixed sentiment with many repeat visitors loving the location but frustrated by limitations. Positive: "we are planning to bring the family back," "WE WILL BE BACK," "regularly visit." Negative: several 1-star reviews citing rude staff, and one family's experience was "not a great camping experience for family with young kids." Staff service inconsistent with some "rude" encounters noted.

Location (9/10) — Outstanding location praised as "perfect location," "fantastic location," "best location." Positioned "right on the water" with "short walk across the bridge to The Entrance" for "shops, cafe, playground." "Close to town" with easy access to "restaurants and the beach just a small walk." Some noise noted from "hooning cars over the bridge."

WHY NOT 100:
[1] Complete absence of on-site children's entertainment facilities - no pool, no playground within park grounds, non-functional games room - making it unsuitable for families with young children seeking activity-based holidays.
[2] Severely undersized sites and cabins creating practical difficulties for families, particularly those with caravans over 25 feet or families of 5+ people, with cabins so small that bedroom doors won't close and no space for basic furniture.
[3] Inconsistent value proposition with premium waterfront pricing that many reviewers found excessive relative to the limited facilities, small accommodation sizes, and amenities offered compared to similarly-priced alternatives.
