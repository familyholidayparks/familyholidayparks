PARK: BIG4 Easts Beach Holiday Park
SCORE: 88/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-25
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 288

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (19/20) — Reviews overwhelmingly praise the "incredible water park," heated pools with dedicated toddler area (70cm deep), jumping pillow, games room with arcade (Pac-Man, Space Invaders), kids club activities (making slime, rice bubble slices, crafts), 5:30pm movies, kids disco, and direct beach access. Families report kids "never wanted to leave" and facilities keep children entertained without leaving the park.

Nature (18/20) — The park sits directly on Easts Beach with a "protected & private inlet" for safe swimming. Reviews highlight the "breathtaking" coastal walks to little blowhole, lighthouse, and Gerringong, creek running through park with ducks, "stunning" ocean views from beachfront cabins, and proximity to Saddleback Mountain lookout. Some note limited natural shade and the developed/resort-style environment reduces bushland feel.

Cleanliness (14/15) — Described as "the cleanest park we have ever stayed in" with "spotlessly clean" amenities, "immaculate" cabins, and staff constantly maintaining grounds and emptying bins "many times a day." Child-sized bathrooms praised as brilliant. Two negative reviews from peak periods noted temporary cleanliness lapses and pool odour issues, preventing a perfect score.

Value (12/15) — Pricing runs $80-$169+ per night, which many call "expensive" or "not cheap," but most families conclude it's "worth every single penny" for the facilities provided. Several reviews note it's pricier than standard parks but "you see why when you get there." Some older guests felt poor value if not using kids facilities.

Site Size (9/10) — Powered sites described as "very generous in size" with room for "tent, gazebo and car" or "9m long caravan." Ensuite sites praised as spacious with large bathroom and storage cupboard. A few reviews note sites can be "a little tight for larger caravans" and some sites close together, particularly tent sites.

Sentiment (9/10) — Exceptionally high repeat visitation (3rd-4th stays common, one family visiting 20+ years, another 40 years). Families frequently report rebooking immediately and making it "an annual trip." Overwhelming recommendation rate with phrases like "can't wait to be back" and "best holiday park we have been to." Few negative experiences centered on booking errors, noise, or unwelcoming staff interactions.

Location (7/10) — Beachfront position is outstanding, but park is 5-10 minutes drive from Kiama town center (not walkable). Close to Jamberoo Action Park, blowholes, and coastal attractions. Train line proximity causes noise on some sites (coal trains at 3:50am, 4:40am, 5:50am noted). Reviewers appreciate the secluded feel while being close enough to town amenities.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Train line noise significantly impacts sites at the back of the park, with multiple reviews citing very loud coal trains at 3:50am, 4:40am, and 5:50am disturbing sleep.
2. Premium pricing ($80-$169+/night) reduces value score, with several reviews calling it "expensive" and poor value for guests not using kids facilities or traveling off-peak.
3. During peak periods (Christmas/holidays), cleanliness standards dropped with "disgusting" amenities reported, fire alarm incidents, and pool odour problems, plus booking/cancellation issues frustrated some long-term customers.
