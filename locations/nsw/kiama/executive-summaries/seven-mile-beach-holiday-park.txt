PARK: Seven Mile Beach Holiday Park
SCORE: 62/100
DATE SCORED: 2024-01-15
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 68

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (14/20) — Families consistently praise the jumping pillow, dual swimming pools, playgrounds, and the famous talking cockatoo that brings joy to children. Animal enclosures with chickens, ducks, and rabbits provide entertainment, and kayak hire is available for river exploration. However, one major review noted "only one holiday activity for the children" during a week-long school holiday stay, indicating limited organized programming compared to other parks.

Nature (18/20) — The park's standout feature is its exceptional natural setting with direct beach access ("1 minute walk to the beach"), positioning alongside Crooked River for kayaking, and beautiful coastal surroundings. Reviews highlight "listening to the waves crashing as you fall asleep" and "beautiful view of the river." The Kiama coastal walk is accessible by bus from the park entrance. Minor deductions for the riverside being described as "stagnant creek" in some reviews and dogs not being allowed on surrounding beach or bush areas.

Cleanliness (7/15) — This category shows the widest variation in reviews, indicating highly inconsistent standards. Recent positive reviews note "spotless amenities," "the cleanest it has ever been," and staff "constantly checking that all is well." However, multiple reviews across 2024-2025 describe facilities as "absolutely disgraceful," "filthiest I have experienced anywhere," with specific mentions of "mould in the shower, cobwebs everywhere," "toilets full," and "bins overflowing." The beachside appears better maintained than riverside.

Value (8/15) — Mixed value perception with some praising "fantastic prices" and "great value for money," while others found it "very pricey for the location and size" at "$50 a night" or "$795 for 2 nights" in premium cabins. Multiple reviews mention the park is "overpriced" given maintenance issues. The closed cafe/shop reduces convenience value. Council-run park pricing doesn't always match facility standards.

Site Size (6/10) — Consistent feedback that "powered sites are a bit on the small side" with "sites are very tight" for larger caravans. Reviews specifically mention difficulty maneuvering bigger rigs and recommend notifying reception about large vehicles. Ground is "really uneven with quite deep hollows" causing flooding issues. Some sites described as "dirt patch" that becomes muddy in rain. Concrete slabs with mesh are appreciated where available.

Sentiment (5/10) — Deeply polarized guest sentiment reflecting the park's inconsistency. Loyal returning families ("9 years," "27 years") show strong attachment, with some calling it "one of our favourite family holidays." However, numerous recent reviews express disappointment about decline: "has gone down hill," "we will never ever return," "can't see us going back." Management changes appear to have caused upheaval. Repeat visitation is split between devoted regulars and those who won't return.

Location (9/10) — Outstanding location scores with "perfect location," "great location by the ocean and river," and proximity to Gerroa, Berry, Kiama, and Wollongong. Beach access is the primary asset ("just across the road"). The main safety concern is road noise and danger with children needing to cross the road between park sections. Close to Fisherman's Club for dining. Well-positioned for South Coast exploration.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Cleanliness standards are severely inconsistent, with multiple 2024-2025 reviews describing "filthiest" and "disgraceful" amenities including mould, overflowing bins, and facilities not cleaned for days—this is the park's most critical failure for family safety and comfort.
2. Site sizes are inadequate for modern caravans with uneven ground causing flooding issues ("annexe floor filled up with water about 5cm deep"), and the riverside section is particularly run-down with "overgrown grass lots of dirt" and "zero internet."
3. Value proposition is undermined by high pricing relative to facility standards, with premium cabins showing poor maintenance (mould, broken appliances, insufficient inventory) and minimal organized activities during peak periods when families need them most.
