# Visiting Byron Bay with the Family

The best time to hit Byron Bay is May through September – you'll dodge the summer crowds and that sticky humidity that makes everyone miserable. October can be lovely too, but school holidays (especially summer) turn the place into an absolute zoo with prices to match. Winter's actually perfect for families: the water's still swimmable, the weather's beaut, and you can actually park without losing your mind. Skip December to January unless you enjoy queuing for everything and paying three times the normal price for accommodation.

Here's what locals actually do: forget the Main Beach tourist trap and take the kids to The Pass for proper swimming – it's patrolled, protected, and way less chaotic. The hinterland towns like Minyon Falls and the Crystal Castle are brilliant rainy-day options, but they're touristy as hell so go early or expect crowds. Tallow Beach is where we take our own kids – it's stunning, safe for swimmers, and you can walk to nearby cafes without fighting crowds. One thing nobody warns you about: the rips at some beaches are serious, so always swim between the flags and don't assume because it looks calm it's safe. Also, parking fills up ridiculously fast, so arrive at popular spots before 10am.

The insider tip? Skip the pricey beachfront restaurants and grab fish and chips from somewhere decent, then eat at one of the free picnic areas – you'll save a fortune and the kids will be happier. The Byron Bay Markets (first Sunday of each month) are worth the trip if you time it right, but go early before it turns into a shopping mall. Most importantly, the whole area's built on making you feel like you need to do everything – you don't. Pick a beach, grab good coffee, and just relax. That's actually the point.
