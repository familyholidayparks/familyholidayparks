Byron Bay is one of Australia's most family-friendly coastal towns, combining stunning beaches with easy access to rainforest hinterland. The main beach is patrolled by lifeguards and perfect for young swimmers, while quieter spots like Wategos Beach offer rockpools and calmer waters. Behind the sand, you've got everything from the iconic Byron Bay Lighthouse walk to the Crystal Castle and natural swimming holes in the nearby hills. It's the kind of place where kids can burn energy outdoors all day without you needing to plan much beyond breakfast.

For accommodation, several holiday parks cater specifically to families, including Suffolk Beachfront Holiday Park right on the sand, Discovery Parks and First Sun Holiday Park with good facilities, and Reflections Byron Bay for a bit more space. The town itself is walkable, has decent schools for school holidays programs, and the whole region feels relaxed without being chaotic. If you've got toddlers, the calm waters and short distances between attractions work well; older kids will enjoy the hiking, water sports, and the general vibe of exploring rock pools and coastal paths.
