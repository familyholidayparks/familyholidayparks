PARK: Suffolk Beachfront Holiday Park
SCORE: 78/100
DATE SCORED: 2025-05-15
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 91

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (8/20) — No on-site playground, kids club, or entertainment facilities mentioned; families rely entirely on beach access and natural surroundings for children's activities, with nearby shops and cafes providing additional options but no structured kids entertainment on-site.

Nature (20/20) — Exceptional natural environment with direct beach access "50 meters from the beach," beautiful tree-lined park setting, sounds of ocean waves, access to Tallow Beach for swimming and walks, proximity to Cape Byron lighthouse, and wildlife sightings including owls, dolphins, lorikeets and kookaburras creating an immersive natural experience.

Cleanliness (14/15) — Consistently praised as "immaculately clean," "spotlessly clean amenities," facilities "very clean and well maintained," though one negative review mentioned mould and poor condition (likely an isolated incident), and some noted amenities are older but still clean and functional.

Value (11/15) — Mixed feedback with many praising "very reasonable price" and good value, but complaints about "79 dollars for unpowered site," "way too expensive," credit card fees, and poor wifi quality detract from value perception, though location justifies pricing for most guests.

Site Size (6/10) — Frequent mentions that "sites fairly small," "small sites if you have a large caravan," "campsites are pretty small," though "perfect for campers in tents, camper vans, camper trailers, motor homes and small vans," with artificial turf and shaded areas compensating somewhat.

Sentiment (10/10) — Outstanding sentiment with numerous "will definitely return," "return every year," "single best campsite I've ever been to," extended stays beyond original bookings, strong recommendations, and glowing praise for staff (especially Craig) creating a welcoming, safe, relaxed atmosphere.

Location (9/10) — Excellent location "right on the beach," 10 minutes from Byron Bay, walking distance to shops/pub/bakery/restaurants in Suffolk Park, quiet setting away from crowds, easy beach walks to Byron or Broken Head, though some note it requires driving/cycling into Byron Bay town.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Complete absence of on-site kids entertainment infrastructure (no playground, pool, kids club, or structured activities) means families are entirely dependent on beach and natural surroundings for children's entertainment.
2. Site sizes are consistently described as small and not suitable for larger caravans or rigs, limiting accessibility for families with bigger setups despite the premium beachfront location.
3. Value concerns including high pricing for unpowered sites, inadequate wifi speeds for remote work despite advertising it, credit card surcharges, and dated (though clean) amenities reduce the overall value proposition for budget-conscious families.
