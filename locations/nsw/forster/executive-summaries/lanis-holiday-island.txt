PARK: Lanis Holiday Island
SCORE: 42/100
DATE SCORED: 2024-01-15
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 79

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (6/20) — Reviews consistently mention outdated facilities with an "old and out dated" games room "could definitely do with a revamp," limited activities for kids beyond basic pools and open space, and one reviewer noting "it would be great if there were more for families, entertainment or activities for kids would be ideal." Positive mentions include kids enjoying pools and open spaces for exploration.

Nature (12/20) — The park backs onto a lake/lagoon providing water views and fishing opportunities, with some sites offering waterfront positions. Multiple reviews mention the natural setting positively, though this is significantly offset by "loads and loads of mozzies" that "almost ruined the stay" and persistent midges near the water. No direct beach access mentioned.

Cleanliness (4/15) — Major recurring concern across recent reviews. Multiple families report "filthy" cabins with "dead cockroaches, ants and moths all over the floor," "black hair all over" linens and floors, "stained" sheets and mattresses, possible "bed bugs," toilets "still dirty" after cleaning, facilities "falling apart and filthy," and general reports of "very unclean" conditions. Even positive reviews acknowledge facilities are "starting to age."

Value (6/15) — Mixed feedback with budget positioning acknowledged ("budget friendly") but many feeling it's "not worth the $500 for 3 nights." Extra charges for linen ($15), towels, and limited inclusions frustrate families. Some appreciate "great value for money" but numerous complaints about paying for substandard, dirty accommodation undermine value perception.

Site Size (7/10) — Reviews mention "big sites," "decent sized van sights," "large" unpowered sites, and "plenty of room" for caravans. Some sites aren't flat making setup "challenging," and mix of grass and dirt sites noted. Generally adequate space but inconsistent quality.

Sentiment (3/10) — Heavily polarized with numerous 1-star reviews from families explicitly stating "would not stay here again," "wouldn't take kids there," and "definitely ruined our get away." Safety concerns raised about speeding, e-scooters endangering children, theft from clotheslines, noise violations, and police patrols. Some loyal long-term visitors exist, but recent family sentiment is predominantly negative.

Location (4/10) — Close to Stocklands shopping centre, McDonald's, and Forster mall ("short walk"), but no direct beach access and "a little way out of town" requiring driving. Convenient for supplies but not ideal for coastal holiday experience families typically seek.

WHY NOT 100:
Cleanliness and maintenance issues dominate recent family reviews with consistent reports of filthy cabins, bug infestations, dirty linens, and poorly maintained facilities creating unsafe and unpleasant conditions for families with young children. The outdated infrastructure desperately needs renovation with multiple families encountering broken fixtures, leaking taps, malfunctioning appliances, and inadequate furnishings that fail to meet basic accommodation standards. Safety and management concerns are significant with families reporting inadequate supervision leading to children endangered by speeding vehicles and e-scooters, noise violations going unenforced past quiet hours, theft, and an overall "free-for-all" atmosphere inappropriate for family holidays.

NOTABLE REVIEW PHRASES:
"good for young kids," "The cabin was filthy," "loads and loads of mozzies," "games room is old and out dated," "run down and not well kept," "wouldn't take kids there," "kids loved it," "budget friendly and close to town"
