PARK: NRMA Forster Tuncurry Holiday Park
SCORE: 82/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-20
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 193

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (18/20) — Exceptional facilities with three swimming pools including dedicated toddler pool and main pool with water slides, plus water park area. School holiday activities run daily with organized events, sausage sizzles, food truck visits, games room with arcade games and table tennis, volleyball court, and bike hire. Parents consistently report kids being fully entertained throughout stays.

Nature (16/20) — Beautiful waterfront location directly on Lake Wallis/Coolongolook River with boardwalk access, fishing from the bank, boat ramp, and fish cleaning facilities. Regular wildlife sightings including pelicans, dolphins, and ospreys. However, the park is 2km from ocean beaches requiring a drive, and some reviews note it's more resort-style than natural bushland camping.

Cleanliness (14/15) — Outstanding maintenance with amenities described as "spotless," "immaculate," and cleaned multiple times daily. Unique feature of artificial grass sites being regularly vacuumed. Park bins emptied twice daily. However, occasional reports during peak periods mention facilities struggling to keep up with capacity, and isolated incidents of mold in showers and broken amenities during busy times.

Value (11/15) — Mixed feedback with many praising affordability and good value, but significant concerns about pricing increases (sites reportedly doubling from $340 to $580-600/night for cabins, $85-160/night for powered sites). Several long-term guests stating they won't return due to price rises. NRMA member discounts available. Positive value noted for inclusions like tractor parking service, school holiday activities, and quality facilities.

Site Size (9/10) — Powered sites consistently described as "large," "generous," and "plenty of room" with 100% artificial grass coverage. Tractor "Marriage Saver" service handles van placement on tight spots. Some mention sites can be tight requiring tractor assistance, and roads described as narrow in places, but overall very positive about space for rigs and setup.

Sentiment (9/10) — Overwhelmingly positive with many declaring it "the best park" they've stayed at and booking return visits immediately. High repeat visitation rate with families returning annually. Staff praised as "amazing," "friendly," and "helpful." Some negative sentiment from noise issues during cultural celebrations, isolated theft incidents, and guests seeking quieter environments finding it too family-oriented.

Location (5/10) — Positioned on waterway with excellent water access but not on ocean beach. Requires car to reach town (2km), beaches, and main shopping. 15-minute walk to Woolworths, 25-minute walk to rockpool and breakwater. Close to golf courses, boat hire, and local attractions. Described as "tucked away" and quiet from road noise, but not ideal for those wanting beach-front or walk-to-everything convenience.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Location away from ocean beaches and town requires driving for most activities and shopping, limiting walkability and beach access that many families prioritize.

2. Significant pricing concerns with multiple recent reviews citing dramatic price increases (nearly doubling in some cases) that are pushing out budget-conscious families and long-term repeat guests who state they won't return due to cost.

3. Peak period challenges including noise management issues (multiple complaints about late-night noise, dogs barking, and lack of enforcement), amenities struggling with capacity during school holidays, isolated but recurring theft incidents, and some guests being evicted over noise complaints suggesting inconsistent policies.
