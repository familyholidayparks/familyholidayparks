G'day! The twin towns of Forster-Tuncurry sit right where Wallis Lake meets the Pacific, giving families that magic combo of calm lake paddling for little ones and proper ocean waves for older kids – all within a five-minute drive of each other. We've found late autumn (April-May) absolutely brilliant for visiting, when the water's still swimmable, school crowds have thinned, and accommodation prices drop nicely. Here's an insider tip: grab fish and chips from Wharf Street and head to the grassy area at the southern end of One Mile Beach for sunset – the kids can run around while you watch dolphins cruising past, and you'll dodge the sandflies that plague the northern picnic spots.
