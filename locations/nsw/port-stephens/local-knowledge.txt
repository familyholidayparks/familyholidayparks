# Port Stephens Family Guide

Look, if you're bringing the kids to Port Stephens, your sweet spot is September through November or February through April. December and January are rammed—school holidays, stinking hot, and everything costs a fortune. Winter's too cold for proper beach days, and the water's freezing. Spring and autumn give you perfect weather, fewer crowds, and you won't need to remortgage for a café coffee. Pro tip: avoid long weekends like the plague; that's when every family from Newcastle descends on the place.

Here's what actually matters: book your dolphin-watching tours early because they genuinely do fill up, and they're the real deal—you'll see dolphins, full stop. Shoal Bay and One Mile Beach are your mates for families; they're patrolled, safe, and have proper facilities. Skip the fancy resorts and grab a caravan or holiday unit instead—you'll save a fortune and have more flexibility with the kids. Grab fresh fish and chips from the local spots rather than tourist traps; locals know where to go. One thing that'll catch you out: the sun here is absolute murder. Slip, slop, slap isn't just a saying—apply sunscreen every two hours or your kids will be pink as prawns.

The water's generally safe, but watch the rips and the occasional stinger warnings in warmer months—check the local beaches website before you go. Bring more cash than you think; a lot of smaller spots are cash-only. And honestly, don't overplan it. Port Stephens is brilliant because you can just rock up, hit the beach, grab lunch, and let the kids run around. It's not fancy, but that's exactly why families love it.
