# Visiting Ulladulla with Kids

**Best time to visit is September through November and February through April** – school holidays are absolute carnage down here, especially during summer when every family from Sydney decides to hit the beach. The weather's perfect in spring and autumn anyway, warm enough for the beach but you won't be melting or dealing with stinger season. If you do come during school hols, book accommodation months ahead and avoid the first week of any holiday period. **Pro tip: hit Milton Farmers Market on Saturday mornings** – it's genuinely good, the kids can run around, and you'll find actual decent coffee and local produce. The beaches here are solid for families; Ulladulla Main Beach is patrolled and has great facilities, but **Narrawallee Inlet is our local secret** – calmer water, fewer crowds, and you can walk to the pub afterwards if you need a drink.

**Watch out for the rips at the ocean beaches and always swim between the flags** – locals take this seriously because we've had drownings. The hinterland roads get slick quickly in rain, so don't assume you can bomb down Milton Road in wet weather like you're in a video game. Fuel up in town before heading inland; petrol stations out that way are sparse. **The real gem most visitors miss is Pigeon House Mountain** – it's a proper bushwalk with amazing views, takes about 90 minutes return, and keeps the kids genuinely tired (which every parent needs). Book dinner ahead on weekends; good restaurants fill up fast. And honestly, just grab fish and chips from the wharf precinct – it's fresh, cheap, and you can sit by the water while the kids destress from the car ride.
