PARK: Yachting Point campground
SCORE: 42/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-04-20
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 15

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (2/20) — Reviews indicate zero organized entertainment or kids facilities; families create their own activities like firepits and wildlife spotting with kangaroos and emus the main attraction for children.
Nature (18/20) — Exceptional natural setting on Blowering Reservoir with "stunning sunsets," abundant wildlife including kangaroos and emus, water views from all sites, and bushland setting with shaded tree areas.
Cleanliness (9/15) — Drop toilets described as "clean and tidy" with active cleaning during stays, though facilities are "a bit tired" and reviewers noted significant dust issues and muddy conditions near water's edge.
Value (13/15) — Excellent value at $6 per night for a national park campsite, though limited facilities and self-sufficiency requirements mean families get basic amenities only.
Site Size (10/10) — Consistently praised for "wide open spaces," "heaps of space around us," sites spread over 1.2km, and campers "not on top of each other" even when fully booked.
Sentiment (7/10) — Mixed family sentiment with positive nature experiences offset by peak season chaos, noise issues, facility limitations, and challenging conditions for young families.
Location (7/10) — Accessible via Snowy Mountains Highway, 8-10 minutes to Talbingo for services, 25 minutes to Tumut, but remote with no on-site water or power requiring town visits for basic supplies.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Complete absence of family entertainment infrastructure, playgrounds, swimming areas, or organized kids activities making it unsuitable for families seeking a traditional holiday park experience with young children.
2. Basic facilities with only drop toilets, no water supply, no power, "tired" amenities needing renewal, and significant dust and mud issues creating challenging conditions for families with small children.
3. Peak season transforms from peaceful to chaotic with "hundreds of vans," "vehicles speeding," "non stop noise," late-night parties, and overcrowding that multiple recent reviewers specifically warned families to avoid.

NOTABLE REVIEW PHRASES:
"saw emus and lots of kangaroos," "wide open spaces," "cost us $6 a night," "highly avoid during peak season," "hundreds of vans everywhere, vehicles speeding, non stop noise," "quite dusty," "facilities a bit tired," "surrounded in kangaroos, right by the water"
