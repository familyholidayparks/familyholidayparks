PARK: Ngarigo Campground
SCORE: 42/100
DATE SCORED: 2024-01-15
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 25

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (2/20) — Reviews confirm zero on-site entertainment, no playground, no kids activities, no organized programs. This is a basic bush campground with "no facilities apart from bathrooms" as one reviewer stated. Wildlife viewing (deer, ducks, possums, kangaroos) is the only natural entertainment mentioned.

Nature (19/20) — Exceptional natural setting on the Thredbo River within Mt Kosciuszko National Park. Reviews consistently praise "beautiful" river location, abundant wildlife, stargazing opportunities, walking/biking tracks from campground, and proximity to Thredbo Village trails. Natural environment is clearly the park's strongest feature.

Cleanliness (11/15) — Mixed feedback on cleanliness. Multiple reviews mention "clean toilets" and "neat and tidy" facilities, but concerns raised about muddy sites, ticks in grass, and basic bush toilet standards. One site specifically noted "quite muddy" conditions.

Value (13/15) — Strong value proposition at "Only 6$" as noted by reviewers. Many describe it as excellent value for the location near Thredbo, though booking requirements introduced post-COVID frustrated some visitors. Price point praised repeatedly.

Site Size (6/10) — Reviews consistently mention "sites are smaller but suitable" and "lots of space around" with designated camp spots. Various site sizes accommodate vans and tents, but smaller than typical family parks. Some sites higher/drier than others.

Sentiment (6/10) — Polarized reviews with passionate repeat visitors offset by serious safety and noise complaints. Three 1-2 star reviews cite unsafe bike traffic through sites, noise disturbances, and frustration with booking. Noise from "loud mouth yobbo's" and 6am generators mentioned.

Location (8/10) — Excellent proximity "only 8 klms from Thredbo" and close to Mt Kosciuszko attractions. Accessible base for skiing, hiking, and mountain biking. However, remote Snowy Mountains location less accessible for families with young children than coastal parks.

WHY NOT 100:
[1] Zero family entertainment infrastructure - no playground, no kids club, no organized activities, no pool, no waterpark, making this completely unsuitable for families with young children seeking entertainment.
[2] Significant safety concerns raised by multiple reviewers including bikes riding through campsites "all day and night," ticks in grass, and camping essentially in "main traffic way" of cycle route.
[3] Basic facilities and amenities with only bush toilets available, noise management issues (generators allowed from 6am, late-night disturbances), and muddy/variable site conditions not suitable for young families.

NOTABLE REVIEW PHRASES:
"Unsafe camping area as bikes rode through it all day and night", "many many ticks in grass", "Sites are smaller but suitable", "Only 6$", "no facilities apart from bathrooms", "Loud mouth yobbo's up till all hours", "generators allowed", "Beautiful Campground on the Thredbo river"
