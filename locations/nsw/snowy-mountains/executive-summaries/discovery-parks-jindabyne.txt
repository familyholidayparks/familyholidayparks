PARK: Discovery Parks - Jindabyne
SCORE: 78/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-14
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 173

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (15/20) — Reviews consistently mention jumping pillow, playground, games room, tennis court, chess set, and spa/sauna facilities. Families note "Great playground and jumping pillow for our grandson" and "Kids love this place" with activities like "jumping pillow, chess set, and canoe/kayak hire onsite." However, multiple reviews state "our kids were disappointed and felt this could be improved" regarding playground equipment, and entertainment is described as "basic" with "not many options for things to do here at this park" when facilities are out of order.

Nature (18/20) — Outstanding natural setting with near-universal praise. Reviews highlight "stunning views," "beautiful location opposite the Jindabyne Lake," "right on the lake," and "breathtaking views and easy access to the surrounding natural beauty." Families love "sitting on the deck" with lake views, "bike riding and kayaking around the lake," and the "gorgeous views of Lake Jindabyne." The park sits in the Snowy Mountains with "easy distance to Thredbo and Perisher" and access to national park hiking.

Cleanliness (12/15) — Mixed feedback on cleanliness. Many reviews praise "super clean," "spotless," "extremely clean," and "very clean amenities." However, significant negative reports include "half of the beds were still dirty," "oven, microwave & kitchen exhaust were filthy," "shower stained with red mould," "several dishes in the cabin were filthy," "cabin really needed a good clean especially the lounge chairs," and "Floor found with chocolate wrappers when we entered." Standard rooms received particular criticism.

Value (13/15) — Generally positive value perception with reviews noting "excellent value for money," "great price," "fantastic prices," "reasonable prices," and "cheaper than nearby hotels." Families appreciate "only $7 a night unpowered, $22 powered" and that it's "most cost-effective way for day trip to the snowfields." However, some mention "very small & expensive," "expensive, but what isn't in Jindabyne," and concern about paying premium rates when facilities like spa/sauna were broken.

Site Size (7/10) — Consistent concerns about space. Reviews frequently mention "very tight," "cabins packed very tightly together," "rooms can be a bit tight," "very small bedrooms," "cramped," "powered sites are extremely small and poorly laid out," and "sites are tight." Families with 5 people found standard cabins challenging. However, deluxe/superior cabins received better ratings for space, and some families found adequate room in upgraded accommodations.

Sentiment (9/10) — Strong positive sentiment with high repeat visitation. Reviews include "definitely coming back," "we will always coming back," "can't wait to go back," "we definitely will be returning," and "this is our go to stay." Staff receive exceptional praise: "staff were incredible," "amazing staff," "super friendly and helpful staff." Many families report upgrades and feeling "so happy," though some serious complaints about refund policies and service inconsistency prevent a perfect score.

Location (4/10) — Major split in location assessment for family needs. While location is "perfect" for ski access (30 mins to Perisher/Thredbo) and "right on the lake," families note it's "a bit out of town," "not close to town," "about 4 km from the main road," and "would have to drive in to shops etc or walk 35 minutes." For families wanting walkable access to town amenities and entertainment, the location is inconvenient, though ideal for those prioritizing mountain/lake access over town proximity.

WHY NOT 100:
Limited on-site kids entertainment: Multiple families noted that beyond the jumping pillow, playground, and basic games room, there's limited structured entertainment compared to dedicated family resorts, with games room machines frequently broken and "not many options for things to do" when facilities are out of order.

Inconsistent cleanliness standards: While many cabins are praised as spotless, there are too many reports of dirty ovens, unwashed dishes, stained towels, uncleaned floors, and substandard rooms (particularly standard cabins) to achieve top-tier scores, indicating inconsistent cleaning between guest stays.

Tight site and cabin sizes: The park is described as "cramped," "over developed," with cabins "packed very tightly together" and sites that are "extremely small and poorly laid out," making it challenging for families with larger rigs or multiple children, particularly in standard accommodation levels.
