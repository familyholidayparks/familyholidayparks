# Snowy Mountains Family Guide

The best time to visit depends on what you're after, but school holidays are absolutely packed – expect crowds and higher prices if you go then. Winter (June to August) is brilliant if you've got confident skiers or want to see snow, though accommodation books out fast and the roads can be dodgy; spring and autumn are underrated for families who just want to hike and explore without battling crowds. Summer's perfect for walking, swimming holes, and mountain biking, though it can get busy around January. If you want my honest tip: aim for the shoulder seasons like April-May or September-October when the weather's mild, prices drop, and you'll actually get a car park at the trailheads.

What locals do that tourists miss: hit the smaller walking tracks instead of the Instagram-famous ones (Rawson Pass gets rammed; try the Cascades Track instead), pack extra layers even in summer because weather changes fast up here, and fill up on fuel and groceries in the bigger towns before you head into the villages – shops are limited and pricey once you're out there. Families rave about the adventure playgrounds, the outdoor pools in Jindabyne, and how good the local ice cream is, but honestly, the real magic is just being outside – kids love the creek walks and spotting wildlife. Fair warning though: leeches are a genuine nuisance in the warmer months, so wear shoes on wet walks and check yourselves after bushwalking. Also, the altitude catches some families off guard; take it easy the first day if your kids aren't used to heights, and bring decent insect repellent because the mosquitoes near water can be fierce.
