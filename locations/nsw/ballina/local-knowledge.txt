# Visiting Ballina with the Family

Best time to visit is September to November (spring) or March to May (autumn) – you'll dodge the ridiculous summer heat and the school holidays madness. December through February gets heaving with tourists and the humidity is brutal, plus it's peak stinger season in the ocean. Winter's mild enough for outdoor stuff, but the water's chilly and you might cop some rough seas. Book school holiday accommodation months ahead if you're coming then, prices go mental. The Ballina Shire website has a decent events calendar – time your trip around the Byron Bay Bluesfest (April) or Ballina Jacaranda Festival (November) if you reckon your kids can handle crowds.

Families absolutely love Kassandra Park by the Richmond River – it's got playgrounds, a skate park, and safe river swimming in the designated area, which beats fighting the ocean currents and rips. The Ballina Head lighthouse walk is short and the views are killer; take the path down to Sharman Beach for a swim after. The local beaches – Shelly, Lighthouse, and North Beach – are patrolled in summer, so stick to the flags. Grab fish and chips from the cafes along the riverfront; they're genuinely good and way cheaper than the touristy spots inland. One golden rule: always check conditions before ocean swimming. The water can turn rough quick, and the rips around here don't mess around – if you're not confident, use the patrolled beaches and ask the lifeguards about safe spots.

Pro tips from someone who's lived here: bring reef shoes or water shoes for the beaches, the rocks can be sharp. The mozzies are relentless in summer evenings, so pack proper insect repellent and don't plan outdoor activities for dusk. School holiday periods mean everything gets packed, so eat early or late to avoid restaurant queues. If you've got young kids, the Ballina library runs free programs during holidays – grab a schedule when you arrive. And honestly, don't skip Alstonville just inland; it's got a quiet charm, good cafes, and fewer tourists than the beachfront.
