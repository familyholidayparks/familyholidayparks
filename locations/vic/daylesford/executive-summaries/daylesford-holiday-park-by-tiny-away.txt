PARK: Daylesford Holiday Park by Tiny Away
SCORE: 58/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-04
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 104

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (6/20) — Limited on-site entertainment with one small, dated playground that kids still enjoyed; no swimming pool, kids' club, or organized activities; Easter 2025 brought special offerings like paella van, coffee van, face painting and Easter Bunny visit; TV room with fireplace available; primarily a passive relaxation destination rather than activity-focused.

Nature (16/20) — Stunning natural setting with "beautifully tree lined with huge old trees," "surrounded by spectacular old trees"; Lake Daylesford is a 10-minute walk away; adjacent to sports fields providing open space for dogs and children; "surrounded by nature yet still close to town centre"; beautiful trees and peaceful natural atmosphere consistently praised; located in scenic Daylesford district.

Cleanliness (9/15) — Highly inconsistent standards with recent reviews showing improvement under new management; some report "spotless" amenities and "very clean" cabins, while others cite "filthy" cabins with dirty dishes, spider webs, mould on curtains, dirty floors, uncleaned amenities for days; dated amenities with poor ventilation; maintenance issues including broken exhaust fans, non-working stoves, leaking showers.

Value (8/15) — Frequently described as "expensive," "very over priced," and "expensive for what's offered compared to similar parks"; cabins $160-$300+ per night; some found "reasonably priced" and "excellent value for money" but majority felt pricing didn't match facility quality; hidden costs with online booking discounts not honored; better value parks with pools and modern facilities available for less in region.

Site Size (6/10) — Powered sites described as "spacious," "generous in size," and "very large"; unpowered sites have "not a lot of flat area" with difficult terrain; many sites on slopes requiring extensive leveling; some cramped sites "stacked on top of each other"; adequate space for large rigs when proper sites allocated; uneven and hilly terrain throughout park.

Sentiment (7/10) — Mixed sentiment with passionate advocates and disappointed guests; strong repeat visitors love the park; many "will definitely be back" but others "wouldn't bother again"; staff (especially Kim) receive exceptional praise; recent management changes creating positive momentum; families appreciate the setting but frustrated by maintenance issues; one reviewer called it "worst park I've ever stayed at" while others say "love, love, love this park."

Location (9/10) — Excellent location "close to town," walking distance to Lake Daylesford, markets, cafes and shops; "perfect location" near Daylesford attractions and spas; easy 5-10 minute drive to town centre; confusing entrance through sports ground; peaceful setting while maintaining accessibility to everything Daylesford offers.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Severely inconsistent cleanliness with multiple reports of filthy cabins, dirty amenities, mould, and inadequate maintenance despite recent improvements under new management.
2. Minimal on-site entertainment for children with only one small dated playground and no swimming pool, water play, kids' club, or organized family activities.
3. Poor value proposition with premium pricing ($160-$300+ per night) that doesn't match facility quality, especially when compared to other regional parks offering pools and modern amenities for less.
