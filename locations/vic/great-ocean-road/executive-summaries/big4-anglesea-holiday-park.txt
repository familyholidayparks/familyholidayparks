PARK: BIG4 Anglesea Holiday Park
SCORE: 83/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-19
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 121

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (18/20) — Exceptional on-site facilities dominate reviews with the "surprisingly large" indoor heated pool and waterslide described as "unreal" and a clear standout. Families praise the jumping pillow, multiple playgrounds for different ages, toddler room with toys, games room with ping pong and arcade, giant chess, and special events including science shows, magic shows, reptile encounters, and food trucks during school holidays creating a "kids roam freely and safely" environment.

Nature (15/20) — While the park offers delightful wildlife encounters with resident kangaroos appearing at cabin doors and cockatoos throughout the grounds, creating "spectacular" moments for children, the natural environment scores moderately. The beach requires a 10-15 minute walk which multiple reviews note is "not really within walking distance with kids" and the park sits in town rather than bushland, though it provides good access to Great Ocean Road touring and a riverside bike trail.

Cleanliness (14/15) — Consistently rated "the cleanest Big 4 I've ever stayed in" with "immaculately maintained" facilities throughout. The individual bathroom rooms are praised as "best I've seen" and "spotlessly clean," cabins described as "immaculate" and "perfectly clean," and grounds "well manicured and clean all the time." Minor points deducted for occasional reports of bathrooms getting dirty quickly during peak periods and one report of filthy carpet and limited supplies in a unit.

Value (11/15) — Reviews show mixed value perception with several praising it as "cheaper than the next two sites" and appreciating included amenities, but criticism emerges for peak pricing ($460/night for 3-bedroom units) and additional costs (extra towels $5, ping pong balls $1, arcade games $2). One extended-stay family felt it "wasn't worth" the forced longer booking and costs, though most acknowledge "you get what you pay for" with the quality facilities.

Site Size (6/10) — Significant recurring complaint about "very cramped campsite" conditions where caravans "couldn't even put up our awning due to no space" and guests felt "like rabbits in cages." Multiple reviews warn sites are a "tight fit" requiring good reversing skills, though the compact layout is praised by cabin guests as family-friendly with everything close. Powered sites on astroturf help with mud management but reinforce the confined feeling.

Sentiment (10/10) — Overwhelmingly positive with extraordinary loyalty: "we immediately booked again next year," "our go-to park for 10+ years," "we will be staying here for years to come," and multiple "can't wait to come back" declarations. Families frequently state it's their favourite Big4 and best park experience, with children "haven't stopped asking to go back." Even most critical reviews acknowledge good aspects, and the passionate positive reviews far outweigh negatives.

Location (9/10) — Great Ocean Road location praised as "perfect for exploring" and "unbeatable" with Anglesea town shops, cafes and IGA within 5-10 minute walk. Close to Melbourne (1.5 hours) makes it ideal for weekend getaways. However, beach access requires 10-15 minute walk which isn't ideal with young children or beach gear, and one review notes driving 20 minutes to Torquay for major supermarkets. Overall considered "centrally located" and convenient but not beachfront.

WHY NOT 100:
[1] Site size and spacing issues create cramped conditions for caravans and RVs with insufficient room for awnings and outdoor living, making caravan guests feel confined despite the quality facilities. [2] Beach access requires a 10-15 minute walk that multiple families with young children found inconvenient when carrying beach gear, reducing the natural coastal experience despite the Great Ocean Road location. [3] Value perception suffers during peak periods with high nightly rates ($460 for 3-bedroom units), forced minimum stays, and additional costs for basics like extra towels, ping pong equipment, and short-duration arcade games that frustrated some families expecting better value from a premium park.
