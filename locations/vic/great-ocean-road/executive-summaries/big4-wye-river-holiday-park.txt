PARK: BIG4 Wye River Holiday Park
SCORE: 82/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-24
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 95

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (17/20) — Families consistently praised the pedal go-karts ($5 for 30 mins), jumping pillow described as "highlight for kids," games room with air hockey and car racing, playground, basketball court, movie nights, and new gym addition. Kids were "entertained from dawn till dusk" with "so much to do."

Nature (19/20) — Exceptional natural setting with river running through the park, kangaroos coming "right up to cabins," cockatoos, parrots, kookaburras, ducks, and koalas on-site. Beach access directly across the road, forest surrounds, and described as "stunning location where river & country meets ocean coast" with "wildlife galore."

Cleanliness (13/15) — Modern amenities blocks described as "spotless," "best ever I have seen at a park," with heated facilities and family bathrooms. Ensuite sites were "5 star clean." However, some reviews during peak periods noted amenities "never seemed clean" with "floors always dirty" and toilet paper running out, and one noted "bins spill in the open, grass above ankle high" in late 2024.

Value (10/15) — Mixed feedback on pricing with several families noting "very expensive even outside school holidays," unpowered grass sites at "$92 per night is highway robbery," and additional charges for fire pits ($50), go-karts, gym ($10/day), and doonas creating value concerns. However, many felt quality justified cost with "well worth it" for the experience.

Site Size (8/10) — Ensuite and concrete sites praised as "huge" and "spacious" with good grassed areas. River sites particularly popular. However, some grass sites were "literally dirt sites" and concerns about muddy areas in wet weather. Concrete slabs noted as "big positive."

Sentiment (9/10) — Overwhelmingly positive with families returning for 5-8 years, "only place we've ever repeatedly come to," "would give 6/5 stars," and "absolutely best place to spend Summer holidays." Strong recommendation rate despite some pricing concerns. One critical safety incident regarding 2026 flood communication.

Location (6/10) — Stunning Great Ocean Road setting with beach access, pub 50m away, general store and cafe nearby. However, "a bit isolated" with 20-minute drive to other shops, and between Lorne and Apollo Bay. Described as "small town" with limited dining beyond pub and general store.

WHY NOT 100:
Peak period pricing draws significant criticism with unpowered sites reaching $92/night and numerous add-on charges (fire pits $50, go-karts $5, gym $10/day) that families felt diminished value, particularly given BIG4's family market positioning. Cleanliness standards became inconsistent during busy school holiday periods with amenities not serviced frequently enough, floors dirty, and toilet paper running out, contrasting sharply with excellent off-peak maintenance. One serious safety incident regarding lack of flood communication in January 2026 "completely destroyed trust" for a long-term loyal guest, and some grass sites were essentially dirt patches that became muddy, not matching the premium pricing or photos.
