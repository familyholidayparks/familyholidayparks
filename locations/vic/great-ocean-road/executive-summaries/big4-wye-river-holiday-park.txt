PARK: BIG4 Wye River Holiday Park
SCORE: 82/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-24
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 95

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (18/20) — Exceptional on-site activities with pedal go-karts ($5 for 30 min), jumping pillow consistently praised as highlight, games room with air hockey and car racing, basketball court, movie nights, and new gym addition. Kids described as "entertained from dawn till dusk" with families noting they "never feel the need to leave the park."

Nature (19/20) — Outstanding natural environment with creek running through park, abundant wildlife including kangaroos visiting sites, koalas in trees, cockatoos, parrots, kookaburras, and ducks. Beach directly across road, river entrance and rock pools for kids. Location where "river & country meets ocean coast" on Great Ocean Road with whale spotting opportunities.

Cleanliness (13/15) — Modern amenities described as "spotless" and "5 star clean" with heated facilities, excellent water pressure. New cabins with stone benchtops and luxury finishes. However, during peak school holidays, amenities "never seemed clean," with "floors always dirty, toilets not clean and running out of toilet paper." Some reports of inadequate servicing during busy periods.

Value (11/15) — Pricing is premium with unpowered grass sites at $92/night and cabins $250+/night drawing criticism as "highway robbery" and "very expensive even outside school holidays." Multiple charges for extras (fire pits $50, gym $10/day, go-karts, doonas). Families feel "overcharged" though many still stayed acknowledging quality.

Site Size (9/10) — Ensuite sites praised as "huge" with concrete slabs and well-grassed areas. Powered sites described as spacious with room for large rigs. However, some grass sites "literally dirt" and muddy areas noted, with site selection important for best experience.

Sentiment (9/10) — Strong repeat visitation with families returning for 5-8 years annually. Described as "best big 4 in australia" and "place of dreams we stay every year." Waitlist to get in during peak times. However, one critical flood safety incident and several negative staff interactions with younger groups noted. Most families highly recommend despite pricing concerns.

Location (10/10) — Prime Great Ocean Road position with beach across road, pub 50m away with ocean views, general store and cafe adjacent. Perfect base for exploring Lorne to Apollo Bay, Otway rainforest walks, and TreeTop Adventures within 20 minutes.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Premium pricing significantly impacts value score, with families calling it "highway robbery" at $92+ per night for unpowered sites and $250+ for cabins, plus additional charges for fire pits, gym access, and other amenities that many feel should be included.

2. Cleanliness and maintenance standards drop noticeably during peak school holiday periods, with amenities "never seemed clean," floors dirty, toilets not maintained, running out of supplies, and inadequate servicing frequency for high guest numbers.

3. Critical safety incident during 2026 floods with "lack of any warning or communication" leaving long-term guests "uninformed in a dangerous situation," and some reports of inconsistent management with younger groups receiving unwelcoming treatment including extra bonds and stereotyping.
