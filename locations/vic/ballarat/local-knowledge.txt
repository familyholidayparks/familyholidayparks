# Visiting Ballarat with the Family

**Best times to visit are spring (September-November) and autumn (March-May)** – the weather's mild, the gardens are stunning, and you'll dodge the summer crowds and heat that can hit 35+ degrees. Winter's fine if your kids don't mind the cold, but some attractions have reduced hours. The Ballarat International Flower and Garden Show in March is brilliant if you're here then, though it gets packed. School holidays are chaos no matter when you go, so book accommodation early. **Pro tip: visit Sovereign Hill on a weekday if you can** – weekends mean queues for the gold panning and the old rides.

**What locals actually do**: forget spending all day at Sovereign Hill (it's good but pricey) and mix it with the free stuff. Lake Wendouree is your mate – walking trails, playgrounds, and the wildlife park. The Eureka Centre is genuinely interesting for older kids learning about the rebellion. Grab lunch at one of the cafes around Sturt Street rather than tourist traps. **Watch out for**: the traffic around the lake on weekends can be mental, and some of the "historic" shops are just gift stores. Also, book restaurants ahead during school hols or you'll be eating fast food.

**The real insider move**: most families miss the Ballarat Botanic Gardens completely – it's free, enormous, and the kids can run wild while you actually relax. Hit up the Ballarat Wildlife Park early morning when it's quieter and the animals are active. And don't bother with some of the smaller museums unless you're really into gold rush history – your time and money are better spent outdoors or at the main attractions. The town's genuinely friendly and easy to navigate, so don't stress about it.
