Echuca's holiday parks sit at the heart of what makes this town special—the Murray River. Unlike coastal parks chasing beach access or bush parks hidden inland, the parks here are genuinely built around the river. NRMA Echuca Holiday Park and Discovery Parks - Echuca both position themselves right on the water, which means you wake up to the Murray and can walk straight down to the boat ramps or riverside walks. The town itself is historic paddle-steamer country, so you're not just getting a caravan spot; you're getting proximity to that authentic river-town experience. Rich River Tourist Park and Riverlander Caravan Park offer similar river-adjacent positioning but with slightly different feels—some sites are right on the water, others set back a bit if you prefer quiet over views. This isn't about manufactured resort amenities. It's about location mattering enough that experienced caravanners book the same park year after year because the river access and town integration are genuine.

The parks draw a consistent mix of caravan couples doing the Murray circuit, families with boats or fishing gear, and motorhome travelers who've worked out that Echuca is a proper stop, not just a night between destinations. You'll see the same rigs returning in school holidays—Victoria's autumn break particularly fills these parks—because families know the kids can occupy themselves on the river, exploring the old wharves, or mucking around at Rotary Park if they want something more spacious and self-contained. Caravanners who've retired and travel for months at a time tend to stay longer here than typical holiday park guests, which tells you something about the appeal. The booking patterns show strong demand during winter school breaks and Easter, but unlike coastal parks that absolutely depend on summer holidays, these places stay reasonably busy year-round because the river is accessible and the town has substance beyond tourism.

What keeps families and regular visitors coming back is straightforward: the Murray River gives you something to do that doesn't require paid attractions. You can fish, kayak, walk the riverside, or just sit and watch the traffic. The paddle steamers run regular cruises if you want that, but it's optional. The towns services—decent pubs, decent takeaway, real grocers, not just convenience stores—mean you're not paying holiday park markup prices for basics. And the parks themselves have improved their facilities without losing the character that made them appealing in the first place. Discovery Parks and NRMA have both upgraded their amenities blocks and power options, so you get modern convenience without the resort park feeling. For repeat visitors, that combination—genuine river town, reliable parks with adequate facilities, and the ability to keep costs reasonable—is why they load up the van in the same season year after year and know exactly where they're headed.
