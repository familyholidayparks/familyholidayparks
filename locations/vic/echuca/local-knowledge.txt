# Echuca for Families: Local Knowledge

The best time to visit Echuca is April to May or September to October when the weather's pleasant and you're not battling school holiday crowds—though if you've got kids in school, you'll need to work around the holidays anyway. Summer gets scorching (often over 35°C), making the riverfront walks uncomfortable, and winter can be grey and chilly. Spring and autumn hit the sweet spot: mild temps, manageable crowds, and the Murray River's looking its best. Pro tip: avoid the long weekends if you can; the carpark at Port of Echuca fills up fast and prices spike. Book accommodation well ahead during school holidays because families snap it up quick.

What families genuinely love here is messing about on the water—the paddlewheel cruises are touristy but legitimately good fun, and the kids will talk about it for weeks. The Echuca Historical Society and Working Wharves are actually interesting, not just tedious museum stuff, and there's proper playground space along the riverfront. Stock up on supplies at the Coles or IGA before you get there; the local shops are pricey for groceries, and you'll want snacks on hand. Watch out for the Murray in summer—it gets warm and murky, so stick to designated swimming spots, and keep an eye on your kids around the water regardless; it looks calm but has currents.

Here's the insider move: skip the main tourist drag during peak times and wander the side streets and residential areas—you'll find quieter parks, better fish and chips spots, and locals will actually chat to you instead of rushing past. The camping grounds fill quickly, but there's decent caravan parking around town if you're flexible. Check the Murray River conditions before visiting if water activities matter to your trip; occasionally water levels drop and it changes what you can do. It's a genuinely solid family spot if you pace yourself right and don't expect theme-park levels of excitement.
