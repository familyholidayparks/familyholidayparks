PARK: Lorne Foreshore Caravan Park
SCORE: 52/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-13
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 138

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (6/20) — Reviews consistently note very limited on-site entertainment with only a small playground that multiple families described as dirty with "dog poo right at the base of the slide," no camp kitchen in several sections, and essentially no organized kids activities or facilities beyond the basic playground equipment.

Nature (16/20) — Strong positive feedback about the natural setting with sites "right on the Erskine River," abundant wildlife including parrots, ducks, and cockatoos, immediate beach access across the road, and beautiful river views, though the "Top Bank" section lacks these natural amenities entirely.

Cleanliness (7/15) — Highly inconsistent reviews with some praising "exceptionally clean" facilities while many others reported "bird poo all over the womens showers," "amenities were filthy," "nesting birds pooing over everything," poorly maintained sites with "mud and mulch everywhere," and general neglect across multiple sections of the park.

Value (5/15) — Overwhelmingly negative value perception with repeated complaints about being "the most expensive" park guests stayed at ($70-139 per night), described as "extortionate pricing," "incredibly overpriced," and "rip off" with families feeling the poor facilities, tiny sites, and lack of amenities did not justify premium Great Ocean Road pricing.

Site Size (4/10) — Severe criticism throughout reviews with families "crammed like sardines," "laughable" site sizes where a 6-person tent took up the entire site, caravans "barely fit in the space," inadequate parking requiring cars to block awnings, and multiple warnings that sites over 20ft should "go elsewhere."

Sentiment (5/10) — Deeply polarized sentiment with approximately 40% of families having terrible experiences stating "worst caravan park we have ever stayed at," "DO NOT STAY HERE," and "will not return," while another segment praised the location and riverside cabins, resulting in low overall recommendation rate and limited repeat visitation intentions.

Location (9/10) — The park's strongest attribute with universal praise for being "right in the heart of Lorne," "walking distance to all the shops and restaurants," "Foodworks just across the road," easy beach access, and proximity to Great Ocean Road attractions, waterfalls, and coastal walks.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Severe site size and crowding issues across most sections with families unable to set up annexes, caravans barely fitting, and dangerous proximity to public roads where children and pets were at risk from passing traffic.
2. Inconsistent and often poor cleanliness standards with bird droppings throughout amenities, dirty playground equipment, no camp kitchens in key sections, and general maintenance neglect that multiple families found unacceptable.
3. Extremely poor value proposition with premium pricing ($70-139/night) that families felt was unjustified given cramped dirt sites, minimal facilities, noise from supermarket loading docks, and lack of basic amenities like camp kitchens that are standard at lower-priced parks.
