# Visiting Lorne with Kids: Local Knowledge

The best time to visit is outside school holidays if you can swing it – Christmas and Easter get absolutely rammed, and you'll pay premium prices for accommodation and restaurants. Late autumn (March-April) and spring (September-October) are golden: the weather's mild, crowds are manageable, and you can actually find a park. Summer's ripper if you don't mind the chaos, but bring plenty of sunscreen and get to the beach early. Winter can be gloomy and rainy, so unless your kids are keen hikers, save it for the warmer months. Book accommodation well ahead – everything fills up fast, especially anything close to the beach.

Here's what actually matters: the rock pools at Fairhaven Beach are incredible for kids and worth timing with low tide; download a tide chart before you go. Erskine Falls is a must-do walk but it's muddy as hell and gets slippery – proper shoes are non-negotiable. The main beach gets patrolled by lifeguards in summer, but the rips are real, so watch your kids carefully and swim between the flags. Lorne can feel pricey for families – the cafes are good but not cheap – so grab supplies from the IGA or visit the fish and chip shop on the main drag instead. The Great Ocean Road can get gridlocked on weekends; if you're driving through, go early morning or late afternoon.

The real gem locals know about is hitting the local playground and reserve areas away from the main tourist drag – they're genuinely nice and usually quiet. Allenvale Reserve has good walking tracks that aren't as crowded as Erskine Falls. Watch out for the Apollo Bay road in fog – it pops up without warning and visibility drops to nothing. Finally, bring wet gear even in summer; the Southern Ocean's cold, and if your kids want to actually swim rather than paddle, they'll need it. The town itself is friendly and family-oriented, which is why we all stay.
