PARK: Apollo Bay Holiday Park
SCORE: 72/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-20
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 107

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (14/20) — Reviews consistently mention pool, playground, and games room with retro arcade games. Families note "kids have so much fun in pool and playground" and "play area for the kids" though some noted the "playground wasn't fenced" and was "very small" compared to other parks. Games room includes TV/Foxtel facilities but requires coins for use.

Nature (16/20) — Located on the Great Ocean Road with "3-minute stroll to the beach" and "not a long walk to the beach for a swim." Multiple reviews praise Apollo Bay Beach as "stunning" with magnificent ocean views. Park set back from highway so "no traffic noise" though lacks direct ocean views. Some mention resident koalas on site.

Cleanliness (13/15) — Overwhelming praise for cleanliness with phrases like "spotlessly clean," "immaculate," and "one of the tidiest caravan parks." Bathroom facilities particularly praised: "so much room in the shower area," "amenities were spotless." However, recent reviews note some maintenance issues: "dirty cutlery," "extremely soiled carpet," "amenities haven't been properly cleaned for a long time."

Value (10/15) — Significant concern about pricing with multiple reviews calling it "very expensive," "extremely overpriced," and "most expensive of 22 parks in our 2 months of travel." Extra charges for BBQ ($1 coin), heater ($1 for 30 minutes), and bedding frustrated families. Rates of $55-$240/night mentioned. Some found "great value for money" but majority felt pricing didn't match offerings.

Site Size (6/10) — Major weakness with consistent complaints: "sites a little cramped," "jammed in like sardines," "sites weren't wide enough to put out your awning," "extremely small" with "nowhere to park cars." Multiple reviews note park "set up for cabins, not vans" with difficult maneuvering for larger rigs.

Sentiment (7/10) — Mixed sentiment with passionate advocates ("4th time stay here," "been staying here since 2012") but significant negative experiences around management. Multiple complaints about "rude staff," "obnoxious, arrogant" owner Bruce, double bookings, and poor service recovery. Lady staff members (Jenny, Linda) praised but male manager criticized repeatedly.

Location (6/10) — Central Apollo Bay location praised as "5-minute walk to shops" and "10-minute walk into town." Easy beach access noted positively. However, park itself on side street with no ocean views, and some found it "down a side street" lacking the coastal setting expected.

WHY NOT 100:
Site size is a significant limitation with caravan sites consistently described as cramped, difficult to maneuver, and unsuitable for larger vans, with families unable to use awnings or having to move cars constantly. Customer service experiences are highly inconsistent, with multiple recent reviews citing rude management, poor problem resolution including double bookings handled unprofessionally, and dismissive attitudes that ruined family holidays. Value concerns are widespread with the park being among the most expensive visited by travelers yet charging extra for basic amenities like BBQ use and heating, while providing minimal inclusions compared to other G'day Parks in the network.

NOTABLE REVIEW PHRASES:
