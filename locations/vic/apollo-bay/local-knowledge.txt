# Apollo Bay Family Guide

Best time to visit is December through February if you want proper beach weather and the kids actually want to get in the water – it's warm enough then. December school holidays get rammed with tourists, so if you can swing January or early February, you'll have better luck finding parking and the beaches won't be completely packed. Winter's beautiful and way quieter, but the water's bloody cold and the wind can be fierce, so it's really only for the hardy types. Spring and autumn are goldmines – good weather, fewer crowds, and the Great Ocean Road drive isn't a nightmare.

Get to the main beaches early, especially Marengo Beach – parking fills up quick on weekends and school holidays. The rock pools at Moggs Creek are magic for younger kids during low tide, but check the tides before you go or you'll waste time looking at wet rocks. Buy your fish and chips from Catch or grab takeaway from the general store rather than the tourist traps closer to the water – saves money and the quality's better. Fair warning: the rips can be serious around here, so keep an eye on the kids in the water and stick to the patrolled beaches. The wind picks up in the afternoon, which is actually great if you've got older kids into watersports, but brutal if you're trying to have a quiet picnic.

Skip the pricey holiday park accommodation if you can and book one of the holiday homes – you'll get more space, a kitchen to actually cook in, and better value. The Great Ocean Road walk to the Twelve Apostles is worth doing, but do it early in the day before it gets chocker with tour buses. Bring good rain gear even when it looks fine – weather changes fast down here. Kids absolutely love exploring the tidepools and the rockier bits of beach, so let them loose there rather than trying to keep them entertained on the sand all day.
