# Apollo Bay Local Knowledge

The best time to bring the kids is late October through early November or March through April – you'll dodge the peak summer chaos and the water's actually swimmable without freezing your bits off. Winter's gorgeous if you don't mind wet weather, but the ocean's bloody cold and the Great Ocean Road gets packed with grey nomads. Summer school holidays (December-January) are absolute madness – accommodation's pricey, beaches are rammed, and parking's a nightmare. Skip the Easter and September school holiday periods if you value your sanity. The real gem is autumn (March-April) when the weather's still warm, the water's tolerable, and you can actually find a park at Main Beach.

Grab your fish and chips from Chris's Fish and Chips, not the touristy joints – it's legitimately good and won't destroy your wallet. Walk out to Marengo Reef at low tide if the kids are into rock pools; there's heaps of starfish and crabs. The Apollo Bay Marketplace on the main street is your go-to for fresh produce and picnic supplies. Watch out for rips at Main Beach – always swim between the flags, and don't assume it's safe just because other people are in the water. The roads around here get slick quickly when it rains, and tourists brake randomly for photo ops on the Great Ocean Road, so take it easy driving. Book accommodation well ahead even in shoulder season, and have a backup plan because accommodation gets tight fast.

The kids will lose their minds at the Maits Rest rainforest walk – it's short, easy, genuinely stunning, and they can burn energy running around. Grab a coffee at one of the beachfront cafes and just sit – the scenery does the heavy lifting. One insider tip: visit Otway Fly Treetop Walk on a weekday if possible, and go early before school groups arrive. The southern right whales (June-October) are spectacular from the shore if you've got patience, but you'll actually see them better from Cape Byron or Warrnambool if you're willing to drive a bit further.
