PARK: Silversands Tourist Park
SCORE: 56/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-25
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 55

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (8/20) — Reviews consistently note minimal on-site entertainment with a "small outdoor area for kids," a small pool that's only open November to April, and limited facilities. One review mentioned playground and table tennis, but families repeatedly cite lack of activities. One guest noted "pool only open from November to April" with "small outdoor area for kids" being the extent of entertainment options.

Nature (14/20) — The park benefits from proximity to Lakes Entrance's natural attractions with "walking distance to the beach" and "walks to the beach and the coastal walk was pleasant (especially the birdsong in the morning)." Reviews mention "near enough to bridge to the main beach" and beach access within 15 minutes via footbridge. However, the park itself offers minimal natural environment beyond being close to these attractions.

Cleanliness (11/15) — Mixed recent feedback shows declining standards. While older reviews praised "immaculate" amenities and "spotless" facilities, recent 2025-2026 reviews report "toilets cleaned about ones a week," "room was not clean at all. Was smelly and bedding was dirty," and "rotten skirting boards and dirty" bathrooms. Under previous management, cleanliness scored higher with "the cleanest Park I have ever stayed in," but new ownership has seen deterioration.

Value (7/15) — Family reviewers express significant value concerns: "paid $800 for 3 nights cabin was very small," "price has skyrocketed $55 a night for powered site off peak," "paid $185 a night, would have been better to buy a tent," and "very expensive for what it was." Multiple reviews note "price is not that cheap" relative to cramped accommodation and limited facilities. Some older reviews noted good value at $40-50 per night for sites.

Site Size (4/10) — This is a major weakness with consistent complaints: "sites very small," "very cramped cabin," "struggled to fit my family in," "maximum 33cm" to get out of bed, "not enough room in the lounge/main bedroom for a family of 5," "sites are small and the whole park very compact," and "some sites very small so ensure you tell manager size of van." Multiple families warn cabins are unsuitable for family accommodation.

Sentiment (6/10) — Sentiment has declined sharply with management change. Recent reviews show frustration: "never going back," "would not return any time," "we won't be back," "park has gone down hill big time," and "lost a 30 year customer." However, some positive sentiment remains from caravan users: "will definitely come back," "would 100% come again," though increasingly from non-family travellers. The ownership transition created significant negative sentiment among families.

Location (6/10) — Strong central location with "walking distance to town centre," "very close to IGA," "in town centre one street back from the Esplanade," and "prime location close to everything." However, this creates traffic noise issues with "some traffic noise from the road." Location is the park's strongest asset, though not particularly nature-focused for families seeking outdoor experiences.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Cabins are severely undersized for families with multiple reviews describing cramped conditions where "struggled to fit my family in," beds pushed against walls with only 33cm clearance, and "not enough room for a family of 5 to be comfortable" - making this accommodation genuinely unsuitable for family holidays.

2. Minimal on-site entertainment and activities for children, with only a small seasonal pool and limited outdoor play area mentioned, forcing families to rely entirely on external Lakes Entrance attractions rather than enjoying park-based family fun.

3. Significant decline in service standards and cleanliness under new 2025/26 overseas ownership with reports of infrequent cleaning ("toilets cleaned about ones a week"), dirty cabins, poor management responsiveness ("lost a 30 year customer" due to linen issues), and dramatic price increases without corresponding quality improvements.
