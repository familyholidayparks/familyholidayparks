PARK: NRMA Eastern Beach Holiday Park
SCORE: 73/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-15
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 125

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (15/20) — Reviews consistently praise the jumping pillow, playground, and heated pool as excellent for keeping kids entertained. Movie nights with free popcorn, food trucks, and pizza ovens in the camp kitchen are mentioned positively. However, recent reviews from January 2026 indicate children's activities and movie nights were cancelled due to budget cuts, and the kids room was closed, significantly impacting the entertainment offering during peak periods.

Nature (17/20) — The park's location next to Eastern Beach receives excellent ratings with families appreciating the "easy walk to eastern beach" and "5 minute walk to patrolled beach." The natural waterfront setting near the inlet, walking tracks into town, and proximity to wildlife areas like Raymond Island earn strong marks. Guests mention falling asleep to ocean waves and enjoying the peaceful bush setting, though it's not deeply immersed in bushland.

Cleanliness (10/15) — This is where the park loses significant points with stark differences between reviews. While many praise "very clean amenities" and "spotless" facilities, multiple recent reviews from January-April 2026 detail serious cleanliness failures including vomit in sinks for entire days, unfilled soap dispensers for days, filthy toilets and showers, and bins overflowing during school holidays. The inconsistency is concerning, with several reviews noting standards have declined compared to previous visits.

Value (12/15) — Families generally find the park reasonably priced with mentions of "great value," "value for money," and reasonable rates for both powered sites and cabins. The heated pool, spacious sites, and modern amenities contribute to perceived value. However, some guests note comparable or better options exist for less money, and one reviewer strongly objected to cleaning fees while still being required to do dishes and remove rubbish.

Site Size (8/10) — Reviews consistently mention "huge sites," "very generous size," and "roomy" powered sites that accommodate large rigs and awnings. Ensuite sites are particularly praised for space. Some criticism appears regarding sites being "packed in like sardines" in certain areas and varying sizes regardless of rig size, suggesting inconsistent spacing across different park sections.

Sentiment (7/10) — Family sentiment is mixed with many expressing strong loyalty ("Just keep going back because we can't get enough of it") and repeat bookings into 2025. However, significant negative sentiment appears in recent reviews with families explicitly stating they won't return due to declined standards. Noise issues with teenagers during peak periods and lack of quiet time enforcement also damaged experiences for families with young children.

Location (4/10) — This is a key weakness. While beach access is excellent (5-minute walk), the park is approximately 2.5km from Lakes Entrance town centre, requiring a vehicle or 30-minute walk. Multiple reviews note it's "a little further from town than some of the other parks" and "town centre is quite a ways away." Families without vehicles or who want easy walking access to attractions and dining will find this inconvenient.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Significant cleanliness failures during peak periods in 2026 with toilets remaining filthy, soap dispensers empty for days, and bins overflowing—representing a serious decline from previous standards that multiple families found unacceptable.
2. Location 2.5km from town centre makes it inconvenient for families wanting to easily access Lakes Entrance attractions, shops and restaurants on foot, particularly with young children.
3. Cancellation of children's activities, movie nights, and food vans in early 2026 due to budget cuts significantly reduced the entertainment value that families expect from a holiday park, with reception staff reportedly dismissive of complaints.

NOTABLE REVIEW PHRASES:
