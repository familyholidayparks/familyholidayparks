# Visiting Bendigo with the Kids

Best time to visit is September to November (spring) or March to May (autumn) – the weather's bloody perfect and you won't melt or freeze. Summer gets hot as, especially December-January, which is rough with kids in tow. Winter's mild compared to down south, but it can be grey and damp. School holidays get packed, so book accommodation early. Pro tip: hit up the Bendigo Easter Festival if you can swing it – the hot air balloons are magic for the little ones, and the whole town's buzzing. The Bendigo Festival in November is also worth timing your visit around.

What families actually love: the Bendigo Wildlife Park is a genuine winner (better than you'd expect for a regional zoo), Lake Weir's perfect for a casual walk and a swim in summer, and the Golden Dragon Museum gets kids excited with the whole Chinese history angle. The Bendigo Pottery works is surprisingly engaging, and you can grab decent coffee there too. Skip the overhyped spots and head to the gardens – Rosalind Park's free, huge, and genuinely lovely for a wander. The tram rides are a hit with younger kids, even if they seem a bit touristy. Eat at Kafrika or grab takeaway from the local Vietnamese spots – way better than the generic pub feed.

Watch out for: the town gets quiet on Sundays and Mondays, so check ahead if you're planning specific activities. Accommodation fills fast during school holidays and events, so don't leave it last minute. Summer can be scorching, so pack sunscreen like your life depends on it and plan indoor activities (museums, galleries) for the hottest parts of the day. The roads around Bendigo are fine, but if you're heading out to the goldfields or wineries, some are narrow and winding – not ideal if you've got car-sick kids. And honestly, it's not the Gold Coast – manage expectations. It's a solid, worthwhile regional town with genuine character, good food, and real local charm.
