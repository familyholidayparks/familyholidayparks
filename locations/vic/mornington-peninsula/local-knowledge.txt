# Mornington Peninsula Family Guide

The best time to visit is December through February when the weather's warm and the beaches are proper swimming territory – though it'll be packed and pricey. If you've got flexibility, October or April are gold: the weather's still beautiful, crowds thin out, and you won't need to remortgage for accommodation. School holidays are a nightmare for traffic, especially getting down the Nepean Highway on Friday arvo – leave early or stay overnight somewhere on the way. Winter's quieter but you'll need to pick your days weather-wise, though the penguin parade at Phillip Island is still worth the trip if it's clear.

Grab a cafe culture breakfast at Mornington township rather than the beachside spots – you'll actually get a table and decent coffee without waiting an hour. The wineries aren't really kid-friendly despite what the brochures suggest, so if you want a wine stop, book ahead and plan proper childcare because the little ones get bored fast. Pack layers even in summer because the wind whips up quick, and the southern beaches are cold and rough – stick to Portsea, Sorrento, or Flinders for safer swimming. The one thing locals wish visitors knew: the roads are narrow and winding, so don't speed, and watch for cyclists who seem to think they own the joint.

What families actually love doing is the short walks to viewpoints, the penguin colony at sunset (book tickets weeks ahead), rock pooling at Blairgowrie, and grabbing fish and chips to eat on the grass. Pro tip: visit the beaches on the bay side (like Sorrento Beach) if you've got young kids – they're calmer and warmer than the ocean-facing stretches. The secret weapon is visiting in shoulder season when you get the best of everything without the tourist madness and inflated prices.
