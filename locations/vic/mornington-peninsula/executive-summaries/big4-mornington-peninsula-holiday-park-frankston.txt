PARK: BIG4 Mornington Peninsula Holiday Park - Frankston
SCORE: 82/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-04-22
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 63

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (18/20) — Outstanding variety of kids activities including jumping pillow, pedal carts, flying fox, pump track, bike course, tennis and basketball courts, playground, skate ramp, and pool. Multiple reviews mention movie nights via projector, ice cream truck visits, and activities that kept children "loving it" and "entertained all day." Families specifically praise the "so much for kids to do" and return for repeat visits.

Nature (14/20) — Park features "lovely trees," bird life including tawny frogmouths, and shaded caravan sites with "beautiful big trees." However, it's located in suburban Frankston adjacent to highway (though noise "fades out") rather than in natural bushland or beachfront setting. Beach access is 5-10 minute drive. Nature score reflects pleasant park environment but limited immediate natural surroundings.

Cleanliness (15/15) — Exceptional performance with consistent praise as "probably the cleanest facilities of any holiday/caravan park," "beautifully cleaned every day," "sparkling," "immaculate," and "spotlessly clean." Cabins described as "absolutely spotlessly clean," bathrooms "clean, accessible, not overflowing with water," and grounds "very well maintained." This is a standout strength across all recent reviews.

Value (13/15) — Strong value proposition with "great value for money" mentioned, complimentary extras like milk, Tim Tams, and chocolates, free pedal carts and bikes with helmets, movie nights, and well-equipped cabins. No major pricing complaints though not explicitly described as budget-friendly. Small pool size noted but overall facilities justify pricing.

Site Size (7/10) — Mixed feedback with "caravan sites quite generous" and "lots plenty big enough for all kinds of caravans," but also "sites too close to each other" and "very tight hard to reverse into." Park described as "on the smaller side" though this creates comfortable atmosphere for families. Some sites better than others for larger rigs.

Sentiment (9/10) — Overwhelmingly positive family sentiment with numerous "will definitely be back," "second time here," "can't wait to return" comments. High recommendation rate and repeat visitation. Minor deductions for occasional operational issues (pool cleaning timing) and one lost item incident, but overall 5-star ratings dominate recent reviews.

Location (6/10) — Convenient for Melbourne access (5 minutes to motorway) and "good central location for exploring Mornington Peninsula," close to shops and Frankston amenities. However, park is in Frankston "which isn't very picturesque" rather than on the Peninsula itself—20 minute drive to Mornington. Highway noise noted. Location works well as base but lacks immediate scenic appeal.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Location in suburban Frankston rather than directly on Mornington Peninsula or beachfront—reviewers note it's "not actually on the Mornington Peninsula" with 20 minute drive to scenic areas, adjacent to highway, and "isn't very picturesque."
2. Site size constraints with some powered sites "too close to each other" and "very tight hard to reverse into" for larger rigs, though the smaller park creates family-friendly atmosphere.
3. Limited natural environment—park is pleasant with trees and birdlife but lacks immediate beach access, bushland, or creek features that would elevate the nature experience beyond a suburban setting.
