# Visiting Shepparton with Kids: Local Tips

**Best times to visit are spring (September-October) and autumn (March-May)** when the weather's bloody perfect—warm but not scorching—and you'll avoid the summer crowds at the lake and orchards. Winter can be grey and chilly, so unless you're keen on indoor stuff, skip it. School holidays get rammed, especially around Christmas and January, so plan accordingly or embrace the chaos. The Shepparton Show in February is a ripper if you're into that kind of thing, but book accommodation early.

**Hit up Lake Shepparton for a swim and picnic, grab fresh fruit straight from the orchards, and don't miss the SPC factory outlet** for cheap jams and sauces—the kids find it oddly entertaining. The art gallery and Shepparton Fine Food Store are great for a quieter arvo. Fair warning: the summer heat can be brutal (hitting 40°C+), so always carry water and sunscreen, and watch out for blue-bottle season at the lake from November onwards. The local drivers can be a bit quick on the Goulburn Valley Highway, so take it easy and give yourself time.

**Insider advice: stay near the lake foreshore if you can, skip the chain cafes and find the local spots run by actual locals**, and grab a Shepparton strawberry or stone fruit when they're in season—you can't get them fresher anywhere else. Stock up on petrol and supplies before you leave, especially on a Sunday, because everything shuts down early. The locals are genuinely helpful, so ask around—we'll point you in the right direction.
