G'day! Phillip Island sits just 90 minutes from Melbourne, and honestly, it's one of those rare spots where you can watch wild penguins waddle up the beach at sunset, then have the kids asleep in your cabin within minutes. We reckon April to October delivers the magic – cooler months mean the penguin parade is earlier (around 8pm in winter versus 9pm in summer), so little ones aren't melting down from exhaustion. Here's a local trick most visitors miss: grab your groceries at the Woolies in Cowes before you head to your park, because the island's shops close surprisingly early and you'll pay tourist prices at the smaller stores near the penguin parade.
