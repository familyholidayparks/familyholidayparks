PARK: Amaroo Park Phillip Island
SCORE: 42/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-24
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 153

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (4/20) — Reviews mention a pool and games room but no playground or structured kids activities. Multiple recent reviews report the pool was "green" with algae and poorly maintained. No evidence of kids clubs, waterpark features, or organized entertainment for children.

Nature (12/20) — Located walking distance (10-12 mins) to Cowes beach and close to Phillip Island nature attractions including Penguin Parade (10 min drive). Reviews mention "rabbits running around" the park and trees on site. Natural environment access is good but the park grounds themselves are described as "overgrown" and "unkept."

Cleanliness (4/15) — This is the park's most critical failure area. Recent reviews (2025-2026) consistently report: "amenities block was consistently dirty," "pool was green," "hairy shower," "dirty undies under bed," "floors have not been cleaned very well," "stains on the Doona (looked like blood)," "rubbish under pillows," "black mould covering window seals," and "toilet blocks are old, rundown and only cleaned once a day." Multiple reviews note dramatic decline since ownership change.

Value (5/15) — Reviews describe pricing as "ludicrous" ($220/night for powered site), "rip off" ($330/night for basic room), "not worth the $1600 for three days," and "overpriced." One guest noted they "could have stayed two nights at Crown for the same money." Only older reviews (2023-2024) mention good value; recent reviews universally cite poor value for money given the condition.

Site Size (6/10) — Powered sites described as "good size," "spacious," and "decent size sites." However, some cabins noted as "very small," "poky," "claustrophobic," and "half the size of a normal unit." Mixed feedback with powered sites scoring better than cabin accommodation space.

Sentiment (3/10) — Sharp divide between older reviews (2023-2024) praising the park as "wonderful," "cleanest cabins we've ever stayed in," and newer reviews (2025-2026) stating "would not recommend," "absolute worst caravan park in Australia," "would rather sleep in my car," "dreadful," and "do not book this park." Multiple repeat visitors explicitly state they will not return. Recent sentiment is overwhelmingly negative with few defenders.

Location (8/10) — The park's strongest asset. Consistently praised as "perfect location," "walking distance to shops, beach, restaurants," "right behind Coles," "close to everything," and "10 min walk to the main town." Easy access to Phillip Island attractions and Cowes amenities. Even highly critical reviews acknowledge the location advantage.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Severe cleanliness and maintenance decline since ownership change (late 2024/early 2025) with consistent reports of dirty amenities, green pool, uncleaned cabins, black mould, overgrown grounds, and poor housekeeping standards that represent health and safety concerns for families with young children.

2. Complete breakdown in customer service and management responsiveness with multiple reports of rude staff, communication barriers, lack of management presence after hours, no response to complaints, and guests being "kicked out" or having bookings cancelled without communication.

3. Poor value for money with premium peak-season pricing ($220-$330/night) that is completely misaligned with the current condition and service standards, making it one of the most expensive options on Phillip Island while delivering below-budget accommodation quality.
