G'day! Port Fairy and its surrounding coastal region is an absolute cracker for families, with those iconic limestone cliffs, protected beaches perfect for little swimmers, and the mainland's largest colony of short-tailed shearwaters that return every September – the kids will be mesmerized watching thousands of mutton birds arrive at dusk near Griffiths Island. We reckon the sweet spot for visiting is January through March when the water's warmest and the town's famous folk festival crowds have cleared out, giving you plenty of elbow room on East Beach. Here's an insider tip: grab your fish and chips from Rebecca's and head to the grassy area near the old wharf at sunset – the resident seals often pop up to say hello, and it's become our family's can't-miss tradition every time we roll through.
