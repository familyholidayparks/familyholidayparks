# Port Fairy Local Tips for Families

Port Fairy's best between September and November when it's warm enough for the beach but not scorching, and again in March-April for autumn. December-January gets rammed with holiday crowds and everything books out fast, so if you're keen on that time, lock in accommodation early. Winter (June-Aug) can be grim – it's cold, windy, and half the attractions shut down. The Port Fairy Folk Festival in March is brilliant but the whole town fills up, so book ahead or stay in nearby towns and day-trip in. Bring layers no matter the season – the wind off the ocean changes things quick smart.

The real gold is the coastal walks: Griffin's Head and Tower Hill are genuinely spectacular and kids can actually manage them without complaining too much. Hit the main beach early morning before the afternoon wind picks up, and if your kids are into rock pools, time it with low tide. The pier's great for a casual walk and you'll spot little penguins at dusk if you're patient. Skip the tourist trap fish and chip shops – honestly, head to the local IGA or bakery instead; better quality and your dollar goes further. Swimming's fine in summer but the water's bloody freezing year-round, so pack wetsuits if your kids actually want to get in.

Watch out for the traffic on the Princes Highway – locals complain about it constantly because it runs right through town and it's genuinely chaotic during peak times. Keep your kids close on the main street and don't assume drivers see them. Also, accommodation fills weeks in advance during school holidays, so plan ahead or you'll end up staying 45 minutes away. One last thing – the town shuts down hard on Mondays and Tuesdays, with heaps of cafes and shops closed, so plan your groceries and fuel accordingly.
