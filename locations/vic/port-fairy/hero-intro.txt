Port Fairy is a charming coastal town on Victoria's southwest coast that ticks all the boxes for a family getaway. The town's pristine beaches are perfect for kids – the main beach has gentle waters ideal for swimming, while nearby Griffiths Island offers rock pools to explore and a scenic walking track that's manageable for little legs. Beyond the sand, you've got quality nature experiences that don't require a long trek: the Cape Bridgewater sea caves are spectacular, shipwreck walking trails are genuinely interesting (not just for history buffs), and the local wildlife spotting is consistently good without being overhyped.

What makes Port Fairy particularly practical for families is the solid range of accommodation options, with caravan parks like BIG4 Port Fairy Holiday Park, Port Fairy Holiday Park, and Pelican Waters Holiday Park offering everything from powered sites to cabins – great if you're towing the van or want flexibility. The town itself is walkable and compact, with a good main street for grabbing fish and chips or ice cream, plus plenty of low-key attractions like the historical railway station and fishing village character that keep kids engaged without being theme-park intense. It's the kind of place where you can have a genuinely relaxed week without a detailed itinerary.
