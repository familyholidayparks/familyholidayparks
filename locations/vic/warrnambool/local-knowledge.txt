# Visiting Warrnambool with Kids

Best time to visit is October through April – the weather's bloody lovely, and you'll catch the southern right whales if you're here June to September, though it gets cold and can be grey. Summer holidays (December-January) are peak season, so expect crowds at the main spots like Logans Beach and Tower Hill. Spring is actually your sweet spot: fewer people, good weather, and the whales are still around. Avoid July unless you're keen on the cold – it's not ideal with little ones. Book accommodation early if you're coming during school holidays, or you'll be stuck with dodgy options further out.

Here's what families actually do and love: Logans Beach is the go-to for whale watching – grab a coffee from the beachside café and just wait, they literally come close to shore. Tower Hill Wildlife Reserve is brilliant for a half-day, the short walks are manageable with kids, and you'll see emus and native birds without needing to trek for hours. The Warrnambool Library has a solid kids' section and it's free, perfect if the weather turns. Grab fresh fish and chips from the pier area – honestly better and cheaper than the touristy spots. The beaches themselves are safe and patrolled in summer, but watch for strong currents at certain spots; the locals at any café will tell you which ones to avoid.

Watch out for: the Southern Ocean is bloody cold even in summer – wetsuits aren't just for show – and rips can be nasty, so check conditions before you let kids loose. Accommodation fills fast, and some places get pretty dodgy the further you go from the main drag. Midges and sandflies can be brutal in certain conditions, so bring insect repellent or you'll regret it. Finally, don't assume everything's open year-round; some attractions have limited hours outside school holidays, so ring ahead if you're visiting in shoulder season.
