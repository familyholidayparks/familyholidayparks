# Visiting Warrnambool with Kids

Warrnambool's best between September and April—spring through autumn—when the weather's actually cooperating and you can spend proper time outdoors without freezing. June to August is dead cold and the wind whips off the ocean something fierce, so unless you're chasing whales (which is genuinely worth it July-November), save your visit for the warmer months. School holidays get absolutely packed, so if you can avoid them, do. The shoulder seasons are gold—fewer crowds, decent weather, and you'll actually find a carpark at Logan's Beach without circling for twenty minutes.

Here's what locals do that tourists miss: get to Tower Hill early (like 7am) for wildlife spotting before it gets hot—you'll see emus, koalas, and birds properly instead of just glimpses. The Lady Bay Beach is better for families than the main Warrnambool Beach if your kids are younger because it's more sheltered. Grab coffee at one of the spots on Liebig Street rather than the tourist traps, and definitely hit up the farmers markets on Saturday mornings at the showgrounds. One thing to watch: the rips at Warrnambool Beach are serious, so stick to patrolled areas and listen to the lifeguards—they're not being paranoid.

Skip the school holidays if you can manage it, but if you must go then, book accommodation early and get to attractions by opening time. The Mahogany Ship's a bit of a letdown unless your kids are into maritime mystery (it's buried in sand most days). Bring layers because the weather changes on a dime, and definitely spend a full day at Flagstaff Hill—it's genuinely one of the best historical experiences in regional Vic, not just tourist busy-work.
