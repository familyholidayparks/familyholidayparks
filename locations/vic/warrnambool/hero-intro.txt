# Warrnambool: A Great Family Break

Warrnambool sits on Victoria's stunning southwest coast and delivers everything families need for a proper holiday. The town boasts beautiful beaches perfect for kids—Hopkins Beach and Levy Beach are safe, patrolled spots ideal for swimming and building sandcastles, while Warrnambool Beach itself offers rock pools to explore at low tide. The rugged coastline is home to the iconic Twelve Apostles nearby, and you'll spot southern right whales migrating through the bay from June to September. There's plenty of green space too, with parks scattered throughout town where kids can run around between beach sessions. The local holiday parks—NRMA Warrnambool Riverside, Surfside, Discovery Parks, BIG4 Tasman, and Shipwreck Bay—cater well to families with facilities ranging from powered sites to cabins, so you can pick what suits your setup.

Beyond the beach, there's enough to keep families genuinely occupied. The Warrnambool Botanic Gardens are peaceful for a wander, and the Tower Hill Wildlife Reserve offers a chance to spot native animals in their natural habitat. The Flagstaff Hill Maritime Museum is surprisingly engaging for kids, with shipwreck stories and maritime history brought to life. Fishing and rock pooling are solid free activities, and the town has good cafes and fish and chips shops where you won't feel rushed with restless children. It's close enough to other southwest attractions if you want day trips, but honestly, most families find plenty to do without leaving town.
