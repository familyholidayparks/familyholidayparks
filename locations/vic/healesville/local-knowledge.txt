G'day! Healesville sits right on Melbourne's doorstep where the Yarra Valley's vineyards meet the towering mountain ash forests of the Great Dividing Range, making it absolutely brilliant for families who want both wildlife encounters and proper bushwalking without the endless drive. The cooler months from April to October are magic here – you'll dodge the summer crowds at the Sanctuary and the kids can actually spot platypus in the Watts River during their active winter feeding times. Here's a local secret: grab your free Parks Victoria pass from the Healesville visitor centre before heading to any of the nearby parks, and while you're there, ask them which day the wombats are being fed at the Sanctuary – it's not advertised online but the rangers have a regular schedule.
