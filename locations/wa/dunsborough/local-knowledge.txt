# Visiting Dunsborough with the Kids

The best time to visit is October through April – spring and summer are when the weather's actually pleasant and the water's swimmable. December-January gets rammed with families and prices spike, so if you can dodge those weeks, do it. October and March are gold: warm enough, fewer crowds, and the schools aren't on holidays so you'll actually get a park at the beach. Winter's mild compared to the eastern states but it's still grey and damp, and half the tourist spots close down. Bring sunscreen year-round though – the UV here is brutal, even when it doesn't feel hot.

The locals all know Meelup Beach is where it's at for families – shallow, safe, and the playground's solid. Yallingup Caves is worth the drive and kids genuinely love it, just book ahead because groups fill up fast. Pro tip: hit the Dunsborough Farmer's Market on Saturday mornings if you're here then – better than the shops and locals actually chat to you. For a rainy day, the Dunsborough Chocolate Company is touristy but the kids won't complain. Watch out for the rip at Bunker Bay if anyone's swimming there – it's beautiful but proper dangerous, so stick to Meelup or Smiths Beach if your crew aren't strong swimmers. Also, parking at the main beaches fills up by 10am in summer, so arrive early or use the less obvious spots around the back.

Stock up at the Dunsborough IGA before heading to more remote beaches – cafes are pricey and sometimes closed outside peak season. Bring your own snacks and a decent esky because marking time between activities is real here. The Margaret River wine region's literally next door if you want to escape the kids for a few hours – grandparents or a babysitter makes that work. One more thing: download offline maps before you go; mobile reception's dodgy in spots, especially near the caves and some beach areas.
