PARK: Tasman Holiday Parks - Yallingup Caves
SCORE: 58/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-24
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 216

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (4/20) — Reviews consistently note "nothing for the kids" on-site except grass areas to run and play. Multiple families mentioned a TV room and games room, but parents expressed frustration that children disappeared to screens rather than outdoor play. A playground exists but is located across the main road, not within the park itself. No jumping pillow, pool, or organized activities mentioned.

Nature (18/20) — The park's strongest asset with near-universal praise for its "absolutely stunning" beachfront location. Reviewers highlight direct beach access "just across the road," spectacular coastal views, clifftop walks, proximity to caves, and "breathtaking" natural surroundings. Multiple mentions of whale watching from sites, beautiful sunsets over the ocean, and being "surrounded by nature."

Cleanliness (6/15) — This is a major weakness with extensive complaints across both recent and older reviews. Common phrases include "absolutely filthy," "never cleaned," "stains on toilets still there when I left," "disgusting floors," "dirty marks on walls," and "desperately in need of a deep clean." Multiple guests reported bathrooms not cleaned during 4-7 day stays. Cabins received complaints about mould, food stains, dirty floors, and poor maintenance.

Value (7/15) — Strong negative sentiment about pricing with frequent mentions of being "overpriced," "rip-off," and not worth $100-$201 per night for powered sites or $250-$423 for cabins given the facility condition. Many reviewers felt the park was "taking your money because it can" due to location alone. Dynamic pricing complaints emerged with rates doubling. Some acknowledged fair pricing in off-season but overwhelmingly guests felt value didn't match cost.

Site Size (4/10) — Widespread complaints that sites are "very small," "super tight," "cramped," and "difficult to navigate." Multiple reviewers with 17-21ft caravans reported extreme difficulty maneuvering into sites with narrow one-way roads. Phrases like "may as well have shared our awning" and "everyone is on top of each other" appear frequently. Some sites described as "the size of a shopping centre car park."

Sentiment (7/10) — Mixed overall sentiment with a clear divide. Long-term returners (10-40+ years) expressed disappointment that standards have declined, particularly since Tasman takeover. However, many first-time visitors loved the location enough to plan return trips. Positive mentions of friendly staff (especially Mary) balanced by complaints of rude or dismissive service. Repeat visitation appears driven entirely by location rather than facilities.

Location (10/10) — Universally praised as the park's standout feature. "Perfect location," "you simply can't beat the location," "hands down the nicest place." Walking distance to beach, Caves House pub, excellent bakery, general store, and surf breaks. Central to Margaret River region wineries, Busselton, and Dunsborough. Direct access to Cape to Cape walking track. Location alone keeps this park fully booked despite facility issues.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Cleanliness is severely substandard with bathrooms, toilets, and cabins consistently described as dirty, unhygienic, and inadequately maintained, with multiple reports of facilities not being cleaned for entire 4-7 day stays despite premium pricing.

2. Sites are far too small and tightly packed for modern caravans and RVs, with narrow access roads causing significant maneuverability problems and guests feeling "cramped" with no privacy from neighbors literally meters away.

3. Facilities are dated and inadequate for the number of guests, with tiny camp kitchens described as "pokey sheds," insufficient toilet/shower blocks for 90+ sites, rusty equipment, and amenities that "need desperate renovation" yet don't reflect the $100-$200+ nightly pricing.
