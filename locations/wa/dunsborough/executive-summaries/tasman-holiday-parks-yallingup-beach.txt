PARK: Tasman Holiday Parks - Yallingup Beach
SCORE: 62/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-06-16
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 216

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (6/20) — Reviews consistently note the park has "nothing for the kids except grass area to run and play" with no pool, waterpark, or organized activities. A playground exists "across the road" from the park. The games room had vandalism issues and some families were disappointed kids disappeared to a TV room. No bounce pillows or structured entertainment mentioned except occasional food trucks.

Nature (18/20) — The park's standout feature with "spectacular" beachfront location just "100 metres to an amazing beach" with "breathtaking ocean views." Guests praised "stunning coastal breeze," cliff-top walks, caves nearby, and "one of the most beautiful spots in Yallingup." Direct beach access, whale watching opportunities, and natural bushland surroundings earned high marks.

Cleanliness (8/15) — Major inconsistency emerged across 216 reviews. Multiple guests reported bathrooms "absolutely filthy," "never cleaned," with "stains on toilets," "black mould," "soap scum everywhere," and facilities "dirty regardless what time I went." Camp kitchen described as "rusty," "tiny and grotty," "health hazard." Some noted facilities were clean during their stay, but negative cleanliness mentions far outweighed positive.

Value (7/15) — Overwhelming criticism of pricing with guests calling it "overpriced," "rip-off," and "extreme." Powered sites ranged $53-$175/night, cabins $250-$423/night. Many noted "doesn't justify" the cost given small sites and poor facilities. "Dynamic pricing" criticized. Guests felt location was "taking your money because it can" without matching facility quality.

Site Size (4/10) — Consistent complaints about "very small and super tight" sites, "cramped," "tiny camp spaces." Caravans had "most difficult time getting into spot," roads "not wide enough for cars to pass," sites "so close" neighbors felt like they were sharing space. Multiple mentions of difficulty maneuvering rigs, "couldn't put awning out," and "shopping centre car park" sized sites.

Sentiment (7/10) — Mixed sentiment overall. Many long-term guests noted decline: "been coming 40 years, so disappointed," "gone downhill since Tasman took over." Some would "never back again," others "100% stay here again." Positive mentions of helpful staff (especially Mary) balanced against complaints of "rude" service. Location loyalty conflicted with facility disappointment.

Location (12/10) — Near-perfect location score with park "perfectly located opposite the beach," "walk straight over road to amazing beach," close to Caves House pub, general store, bakery, and cafe all "walking distance." Central to Margaret River region (30 min to Busselton, close to wineries and caves). "Can't beat the location" appeared repeatedly despite other criticisms.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Sites are extremely small and cramped with very narrow access roads making caravan entry/exit difficult, with guests literally feeling like they're "sharing awnings" with neighbors and unable to open awnings or place chairs comfortably.
2. Cleanliness standards are inconsistent and often poor with multiple reports of dirty bathrooms never properly cleaned, rusty camp kitchen facilities, black mould, overflowing bins, and "filthy" amenities that don't reflect the premium pricing charged.
3. Value for money is extremely poor with dynamic pricing reaching $175/night for grass sites and $423 for tiny cabins, while facilities remain outdated, cramped, and poorly maintained - the park is "taking your money because it can" based on location alone without investing in proper facilities.

NOTABLE REVIEW PHRASES:
"location is absolutely stunning right by the beach," "sites are so small and super tight squeezing van in," "bathrooms absolutely filthy never cleaned," "overpriced at $201 per night for caravan site," "camp kitchen like a tin shed with rust," "100 metres to an amazing beach," "cramped, smelly and cold showers," "beautiful location but facilities let it down"
