PARK: BIG4 Dunsborough Southern Stars Holiday Park
SCORE: 88/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-25
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 321

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (17/20) — Reviews consistently praise the adventure playground with flying fox, BMX pump track, games room with air hockey and table tennis, bike track, food trucks on weekends, coffee vans in mornings, wine tasting events, live music, and line dancing. Families note "so much for the kids to do," "kids loved the pump track," and "barely saw the kids due to playground and bike track." Chase the Ace on Friday nights is popular. Only limitation is no pool/waterpark.

Nature (16/20) — Located in peaceful rural setting "surrounded by paddocks" with "beautiful lush rural surrounds," walking distance to Lentedal Winery through vineyard, and chickens roaming the grounds. Park features "lush green grass everywhere," "beautiful green lawns," and native wildlife including kangaroos visible from sites. Beaches are 10-15 minutes drive in Dunsborough and Busselton. Not directly on beach but excellent nature-based setting.

Cleanliness (15/15) — Exceptional scores with reviews stating "spotlessly clean," "immaculate," "pristine condition," "amenities always spotless," and "cleanest campground ever." Staff clean bathrooms multiple times daily, even "wipe the clothes line." Ensuite-style bathrooms are "spacious and huge," facilities "always clean," and "everything is so well maintained." Groundsmen constantly maintain grass sites between guests.

Value (12/15) — Reviews show mixed feedback on pricing. Multiple guests note "a bit pricey," "rather pricey compared with Perth (at least by $155 additional per week)," "on the more expensive side," and "70 dollars is impertinence for 30 square meters" (international visitor). However, many counter that "you're certainly paying for quality" and facilities justify the cost. Value score reflects premium pricing but exceptional quality.

Site Size (10/10) — Perfect score with reviews consistently praising "huge sites," "massive sites," "large well spaced caravan sites," "spacious sites," "great big sites," and "so much room on each site." Families note "could fit 3 vans in the spot," "didn't feel like we were on top of anyone," and "plenty of space between vans." Big rig sites available for large caravans.

Sentiment (10/10) — Outstanding sentiment with families stating "will definitely be back," "already booked again before we left," "booked in for another few days," and "our new favourite caravan park." Multiple reviews call it "the best caravan park in WA," "best we've stayed at in 3.5 years around Australia," and "one of the best parks in Australia." Repeat visitation extremely high.

Location (8/10) — Central location between Dunsborough (10km) and Busselton (14km) with "easy access to the Dunsborough/Margaret River region." Close to wineries, beaches, attractions, and Vasse shopping centre. Reviews note "perfect location," "central to everything," and walking distance to winery. Not directly in town or on beach, hence slight point reduction, but strategic base location praised.

WHY NOT 100:
1. No swimming pool is the most frequently mentioned limitation, with multiple families noting "NO POOL!!!!" and "could do with a pool," which is a significant amenity gap for families, especially in summer heat.
2. Strict policies including no early check-in before 1pm, no mats on grass areas, rigid cancellation policy (less than 2 weeks notice means no refunds/rescheduling), and check-in cutoff at 8pm frustrate some guests who need flexibility.
3. Limited WiFi/internet availability with reviews noting "No WiFi" and "park was too big" for coverage, which matters to families needing connectivity, though many appreciate the digital detox opportunity.
