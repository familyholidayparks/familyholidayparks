PARK: Perth Caravan Park & Camp Site
SCORE: 52/100
DATE SCORED: 2024-01-15
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 42

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (12/20) — Reviews highlight a small but clean playground on-site that families appreciated, with one describing it as "one of the best and cleanest we've seen in scottish sites." The major entertainment value comes from adjacent facilities including Wonder World soft play centre, Air Thrill, mini golf, and a golf driving range. However, multiple reviews noted the park is "not good for dogs or kids" and entertainment is primarily off-site rather than integrated park amenities.

Nature (8/20) — The natural environment scored poorly with reviewers noting the park is "nestled under electrical pylons," located on "an industrial estate," and "not particularly close to anything." Positive nature mentions were limited to "lots of wild rabbits to watch" and "nice walks round the park." The dual carriageway proximity and industrial setting significantly detract from any natural ambiance.

Cleanliness (11/15) — Reviews showed highly inconsistent cleanliness standards across the review period. Recent positive reviews praised "spotlessly clean and tidy" facilities and "very clean toilets and showers." However, multiple 2024 reviews described deteriorating conditions: "grass not cut," "showers close to disgusting," "smell coming up from sink disgusting," and "toilet and shower unit out of date." The wide variance suggests maintenance depends heavily on timing and management attention.

Value (10/15) — Families found the pricing reasonable with reviews noting "amazing value," "bra pris" (good price), and affordable rates. However, complaints about "expensive" laundry facilities and "high surcharge for electricity connection" balanced the value perception. The park functions well as an economical stopover but doesn't deliver premium value for extended stays.

Site Size (7/10) — Pitch sizing received mixed feedback. Positive reviews mentioned "pitches are well spaced and well kept" and "nice open space," while negative experiences included "tiny untidy pitch" and being "stuck in between 2 other vans." One family with an 8m unit found it "easy to get in and out," suggesting adequate space for larger rigs when properly allocated.

Sentiment (2/10) — Overall family sentiment was concerning with significant negative reviews outweighing positives. Multiple reviewers stated "WONT BE BACK," "first and last visit," and "worst campsite we found in a two week trip across Scotland." Safety concerns about "travellers on site" appeared in multiple reviews causing families to feel uncomfortable. While some families did return after 8 years, the predominance of one-time negative experiences and low recommendation rates pulled this score down dramatically.

Location (2/10) — Location scored poorly for family holidays despite being "local to Perth." The park sits next to the A9 motorway causing "difficult to sleep" noise levels, though some families reported sleeping fine. Described as having "slightly random" location and "not particularly close to anything," it serves primarily as a transit point. Positive accessibility notes included bus stops to Perth city centre and nearby Costa, but the industrial estate setting and highway noise make it unsuitable as a destination park.

WHY NOT 100:
The park's location adjacent to the A9 dual carriageway creates persistent road noise that multiple families found disruptive to sleep, while the industrial estate setting with electrical pylons provides minimal natural environment for families seeking an outdoor holiday experience. Maintenance and cleanliness standards showed significant deterioration between 2023-2024 with multiple families reporting uncut grass, disgusting dish washing facilities, inadequate waste management, and declining shower conditions that contradict earlier positive reviews. Safety and comfort concerns emerged repeatedly with families feeling uncomfortable due to council-placed travellers on site, lack of site management presence outside reception hours, and poor enforcement of spacing regulations leading to fire hazards and privacy issues that drove away repeat visitors.
