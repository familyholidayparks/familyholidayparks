PARK: Discovery Parks - Coogee Beach
SCORE: 58/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-21
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 129

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (6/20) — Multiple families specifically noted "lacking amenities and activities for the kids", "no activities or swimming pools at the park for children", and "no park for kids". The park has communal areas with couches, TV and a pool table for adults, but virtually no organized kids' entertainment or facilities. One review mentioned a "facility block between the whole campsite is a little bit of a Letdown". The nearby public playground was mentioned as an asset but is outside the park itself.

Nature (18/20) — Exceptional beachfront location with consistent praise: "short walk to the beach", "close walk to the beach", "fantastic beach for children in the morning", and "short stroll to the beach". Families loved the "Omeo Wreck just a tiny walk north for snorkelling", beach tracks, natural bird life, shady trees throughout the park, and the "heart lifting vista of white shore and sparkling, aqua water". The natural coastal setting is the park's strongest feature.

Cleanliness (9/15) — Mixed feedback with significant concerns. Positive reviews noted "spotless amenities", "very clean facilities", "amenities well maintained & cleaned regularly", and "scrupulously clean" ablution blocks. However, multiple families reported serious issues: "cabin was dirty and broken", "extremely poor room cleanliness", "cobwebs in the toilet, and dirt on the floor", "disgusting smell", "portacot we were given was dirty and had holes", "dirty socks" under beds, and "amenity block was left in a disgusting state". Cleanliness appears inconsistent between caravan sites (better) and cabins (problematic).

Value (8/15) — Significant concerns about value for money. Families reported "cabins are over priced and dated", "$315 per night" that "did not reflect the quality", "extra $20 per child per night, which I thought was over priced considering there are no activities", "over priced at $140/night but in a normal world only worth about $80/night", and "prices have skyrocketed over the last 3 years". Several reviews noted the location was cheaper than alternatives "but there is a good reason for that". Caravan/powered sites received better value ratings than cabins.

Site Size (6/10) — Concerning feedback about site accessibility and size. Reviews mentioned "very tight checkerboard caravan sight", "tight sites with large overhanging trees", "some sites are very difficult to get into especially with a larger van", "required reversing the length of the cul de sac to get in", and "worst caravan site". Some travelers on concrete slabs reported "spacious" and "good size" sites with shade, but the overall layout received criticism for being poorly planned for larger rigs.

Sentiment (6/10) — Polarized sentiment with location driving positive reviews but serious concerns dampening enthusiasm. Multiple families stated they "wont go back", "wouldn't stay again", "surely we won't come back", and "DO NOT STAY HERE". However, loyal guests also said "we keep coming back all the way from Victoria", "second time here absolutely love it", and "will definitely be back". Major issues included prevalent theft ("bikes stolen daily", "E-bikes stolen on a daily basis", "GANGSTERS PARADISE"), safety concerns, aggressive management behavior, and dated facilities. Staff received mixed reviews—many praised individuals like James, Carl, and Wayne, while others found management unhelpful.

Location (10/10) — Outstanding location scores with universal praise. Families loved being "right on the beach", "close to Perth and Fremantle", "10 minutes drive to Freo", "convenient for Rottnest Island day trips", "20 minutes walk to the supermarket (woolworths)", "bus stop to Fremantle was right out front", and "15min drive to Fremantle". The beach access, proximity to cafes, shopping, and Perth attractions while being "out of the hustle and bustle" was consistently highlighted as the park's premier asset.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Complete absence of family entertainment facilities—no pool, no playground, no kids' activities, no waterpark—which multiple families specifically called out as deal-breakers for a holiday park experience.
2. Serious and ongoing security issues with "bikes stolen daily", "prevalent night time theft", inadequate fencing, and multiple families reporting stolen belongings, creating an unsafe environment particularly concerning for families with children.
3. Significant cleanliness and maintenance problems in cabin accommodation with reports of dirty, dated, broken facilities, spider infestations, and poor upkeep that doesn't justify premium pricing, though caravan site amenities fared better.
