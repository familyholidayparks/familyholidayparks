PARK: Banksia Tourist Park
SCORE: 68/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-24
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 243

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (11/20) — Reviews consistently mention a "nice playground" and "great activity park for the young ones" but no waterpark, jumping pillow, games room access after 5:30pm, or structured kids programs. Families noted the pool as "a big bonus" and "fantastic" but it closes at 7pm and the camp kitchen locks at 9pm, limiting evening family activities.

Nature (14/20) — The park features "beautiful gardens," "lots of trees," abundant birdlife, and native Quenda (bandicoots) that guests loved spotting. However, it's suburban rather than bushland, located near busy roads and airport, with no beach, creek, or natural swimming holes. Reviews describe "bushy" surrounds but emphasize it's a manicured caravan park rather than a natural setting.

Cleanliness (13/15) — Strong praise for "spotlessly clean" amenities, "very clean and well maintained" facilities, and "cleanest ablution blocks we have seen in our travels." Some complaints about occasional ants, mould in older cabins, and toilets needing more frequent cleaning. Overall maintenance scored well with "beautifully maintained" grounds.

Value (10/15) — Mixed feedback with some noting "great value for money" and "super affordable price" while others complained about "$175 for one night," "overpriced charge," "very pricey for what it was," and extra charges for toilet paper, dogs, and limited wifi. Budget families appreciated it; those expecting resort amenities felt disappointed.

Site Size (7/10) — Reviews mention "nice big sites," "large concrete sites," and "spacious" caravan sites, but many noted "internal sites can be tight for vans," "tiny parking space," "tight and poorly designed," and difficulty reversing into sites. Large rigs struggled with access and permanent residents blocking lanes.

Sentiment (7/10) — Positive repeat visitors and recommendations balanced by significant complaints about rude reception staff, inflexible refund policies, discriminatory pet policies, and feeling "scammed." Many said "will definitely be back" while others stated "never again" and "won't be recommending."

Location (6/10) — Praised for being "close to Midland," "30-40 minutes north or south of city," "10 mins from airport," with bus stop at gate and train access nearby. However, "far from shops," "not very convenient without a car," constant road noise from Roe Freeway, and low-flying aircraft. Good for transit, not for walking to attractions.

WHY NOT 100:
1. No dedicated kids entertainment facilities - no waterpark, no kids club, no jumping pillow, limited playground equipment, and facilities close early (pool at 7pm, camp kitchen at 9pm) preventing evening family activities.
2. Suburban location with significant traffic and aircraft noise, no beach/creek access, and too far from attractions to walk - families need a car and use it primarily as a transit base rather than a destination.
3. Inconsistent service standards with multiple reviews citing "rude reception staff," "aggressive confrontation," inflexible policies even for family emergencies, extra charges for basic amenities, and discriminatory treatment regarding pets and vehicle types.
