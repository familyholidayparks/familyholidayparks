# Visiting Perth with Kids: Local Parent's Guide

The best time to visit is September through November (spring) or March through May (autumn) – the weather's gorgeous, not scorching, and the kids won't melt. Avoid December to February unless you love 35°C+ days and crazy crowds at the beaches; school holidays (especially July) get rammed everywhere. Book accommodation near the river or northern beaches – Scarborough, City Beach, or South Perth – and you've cut travel time in half. Trust me, nothing kills a family day out like sitting in traffic with restless kids. The Swan River foreshore is your mate: it's free, safe, and has playgrounds, riverside walks, and plenty of spots to grab fish and chips.

What'll blow the kids' minds is Rottnest Island – the quokkas are real and ridiculous, the beaches are pristine, and the ferry ride's half the adventure. Book a family pass for the Aquarium or Kings Park; locals often forget Kings Park is free to walk around, and it's brilliant for letting the little ones burn energy. Watch out for the heat – seriously, sunburn here is brutal. Slip, slop, slap is law, not a suggestion. Also, the Indian Ocean's colder than you'd think and has proper rips, so swim between the flags and keep an eye on toddlers. One insider tip: head to Fremantle on a weekday morning before the tourists arrive, or visit South Perth Peninsula at sunset when it's peaceful and the light's perfect for photos.
