PARK: Pemberton Caravan Park
SCORE: 58/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-04-18
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 131

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (8/20) — No organized kids entertainment or activities on-site; families mentioned feeding wildlife (ducks, parrots) for $2, nearby Pemberton tramway and Aerial Adventure as external options, but no waterpark, playground or structured programs at the park itself.

Nature (18/20) — Outstanding natural setting praised consistently: towering karri forest trees, creek running through park, abundant birdlife (kookaburras, cockatoos, parrots, wrens), even emu sightings; direct access to extensive walking and mountain bike trails; adjacent to natural Pemberton Pool swimming hole; immersed in beautiful bushland while still close to town.

Cleanliness (7/15) — Major inconsistency evident, particularly in 2026 Easter reviews: multiple guests reported "disgustingly dirty" toilets and showers that appeared uncleaned for days during peak periods, filthy floors, stinking urinals; off-peak reviews generally positive about cleanliness; management's defensive responses to complaints concerning; lack of bins throughout park forcing long walks to dispose rubbish.

Value (10/15) — Mixed feedback: $50 for unpowered tent site during Easter Monday considered poor value given cleanliness issues; $41-50 for powered sites seen as reasonable by some but overpriced relative to dated facilities by others; community-owned park positioning appreciated; some felt excellent value for location and space, others disappointed given basic amenities.

Site Size (8/10) — Generally praised as spacious with "huge sites" and "lots of space between sites"; variety of shady and sunny options; some sites described as small and campers "packed together" during busy periods; sites vary significantly—some very spacious by creek, others tighter; level sites with good shade from trees; some sandy sites, others grassy.

Sentiment (6/10) — Polarized experiences: many repeat visitors love the natural setting and return regularly; recent 2026 Easter period generated multiple 1-star reviews citing filthy amenities and poor management attitude; strict cancellation policy ($50 fee, no flexibility even for emergencies) created significant ill-will; positive sentiment focused on nature/location, negative on cleanliness and management responsiveness.

Location (9/10) — Excellent: 15-minute walk to Pemberton town center, adjacent to Pemberton Pool (150m), direct access to bike/walking trails from park, close to bakery and pub, central base for exploring southwest WA attractions (Gloucester Tree, Warren National Park, wineries); somewhat hilly walk to town but convenient overall.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Amenities are significantly dated (1970s era) and critically, cleanliness standards collapse during peak periods with multiple Easter 2026 guests reporting toilets/showers not cleaned for entire 4-6 night stays, creating unacceptable hygiene conditions.
2. Management demonstrates poor customer service with inflexible cancellation policies (even for medical emergencies/war-related travel disruptions), defensive responses to legitimate feedback, and failure to acknowledge or address repeated cleanliness complaints.
3. Complete absence of on-site kids entertainment, playground equipment, or organized activities means families are entirely reliant on external attractions and wildlife interaction for child entertainment.
