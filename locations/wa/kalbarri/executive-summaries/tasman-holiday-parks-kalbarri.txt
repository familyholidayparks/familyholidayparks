PARK: Tasman Holiday Parks - Kalbarri
SCORE: 72/100
DATE SCORED: 2025-05-20
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 176

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (8/20) — Very limited on-site kids entertainment with only a small playground mentioned as needing renovation; families noted lack of activities compared to other parks, no water park or organized programs, though natural wildlife (kangaroos, emus, pelicans) provides some interest for children.

Nature (18/20) — Exceptional natural setting directly opposite the beach and pelican feeding station, kangaroos and emus regularly visit campsites, walking distance to Kalbarri National Park (30 mins drive), river mouth access, beautiful sunsets over Indian Ocean, abundant native wildlife sightings throughout the park.

Cleanliness (13/15) — Consistently praised for "spotless" and "super clean" amenities with individual ensuite-style bathroom cubicles well-maintained, though some facilities described as "outdated and rusty" or "old but clean"; grounds kept tidy with regular site cleaning between guests, occasional mentions of kangaroo droppings needing attention.

Value (10/15) — Mixed feedback with some calling it "great value" ($55-$61 per night for powered sites) while others complained of steep pricing ($90+ for unpowered sites, cabin prices "jacked up" nearly double since 2022); value perception varies significantly by accommodation type and season.

Site Size (7/10) — Drive-through sites available and appreciated, but many reviews mentioned "cramped" or "tight" sites especially when park busy, some sites described as "huge" while others "very small," inconsistent spacing with complaints about being "crammed in" when park only quarter full, newer sites at back reportedly tighter than older sections.

Sentiment (8/10) — Strong positive sentiment with many "will definitely return" comments and repeat visitors, friendly helpful staff (especially Lisa and Trina mentioned by name), but negative experiences with strict checkout enforcement, booking cancellations, and occasional hostile customer service incidents dragged sentiment down from excellent to good.

Location (10/10) — Perfect central location across from beach and pelican feeding, walking distance (5 mins) to IGA supermarket, pubs, cafes, restaurants, and shops, close to all Kalbarri attractions including National Park, river mouth, and foreshore playground, convenient access to everything families need.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Minimal on-site kids entertainment (no waterpark, activities, or organized programs) with only a dated small playground that reviewers say needs renovation, forcing families to rely entirely on beach and town attractions for children's entertainment.

2. Significant inconsistency in site allocation and spacing, with multiple complaints about being "crammed" into tight sites when park was only partially full, and some severe customer service incidents involving aggressive staff responses to reasonable guest questions.

3. Aging infrastructure with bathrooms described as "very outdated and rusty," cabins needing renovation with cleanliness issues (dirty table covers, gunk on floors, rusty bed frames), and inadequate facilities for park size (only 10 toilets/showers per block for 156 sites, insufficient camp kitchen equipment).
