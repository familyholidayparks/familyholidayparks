PARK: Kalbarri Tudor Holiday Park
SCORE: 82/100
DATE SCORED: 2025-05-16
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 211

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (17/20) — Families consistently praise the heated pool as a highlight, with the jumping pillow described as constantly covered in children. The park offers regular activities during school holidays including face painting, colouring competitions, lawn games, and even free breakfast events. Playground equipment receives positive mentions, and the park's proximity to a skate park and oval across the road adds extra options for active kids.

Nature (16/20) — The park features mature shady trees throughout and guests frequently encounter kangaroos feeding morning and evening, which delights children. It's walking distance to Kalbarri's stunning beachfront and foreshore areas, and serves as an ideal base for exploring Kalbarri National Park's Nature's Window, Skywalk, Red Bluff, and Eagle Gorge. The natural coastal environment and wildlife sightings significantly enhance the family experience.

Cleanliness (13/15) — Reviews are mixed with many praising spotless facilities and "among the best we have seen anywhere," particularly the modern ensuite bathrooms. However, multiple guests noted cleanliness issues during peak periods including dirty toilet blocks, lack of regular cleaning, mouldy extraction fans, and inconsistent maintenance. The newer facilities receive better ratings than older amenities blocks.

Value (11/15) — Opinions vary significantly on value, with some finding it reasonable and appreciating offers like "pay 3 stay 4" deals and G'day Parks discounts. However, several families felt pricing was high, particularly for unpowered sites at $42-50 per night given tight site allocation and occasional facility issues. Cabin pricing around $200 per night was considered expensive by some guests.

Site Size (7/10) — Reviews consistently mention that sites are "wide but shallow" and can be tight, particularly for larger rigs and caravans. Some guests struggled with site allocation, trees blocking awnings, uneven ground requiring significant levelling, and power/water connections being awkwardly positioned. However, many also noted sites were "a great size" and "plenty of room to set up" when allocated appropriately.

Sentiment (9/10) — Overall sentiment is strongly positive with many families saying they "loved" their stay, "will definitely be back," and describing it as "the best Holiday Park in Kalbarri." Guests frequently extended their stays ("came for 3 nights stayed 7") and many are repeat visitors over decades. The friendly, accommodating staff receive consistent praise, though a small number of reviews mention poor management communication and strict policies.

Location (9/10) — The location receives excellent ratings for being within easy walking distance (5-10 minutes) to Kalbarri's town centre, shops, cafes, pubs, brewery, and beachfront/foreshore areas. It's described as "close to everything" while still being "sheltered" and "quiet." The park serves as a convenient base for day trips to Kalbarri National Park attractions, though a few reviews noted it's "a fair way from the shopping areas" depending on mobility.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Cleanliness inconsistency during peak periods with multiple reports of dirty toilet blocks, lack of regular cleaning ("In 8 days, we never once saw a proper cleaning"), and maintenance issues in older amenities blocks that detract from the otherwise excellent facilities.
2. Site allocation challenges with tight spaces for larger rigs, awkward power/water connections, uneven ground, and occasional double-booking issues that caused frustration for guests with caravans and motorhomes.
3. Value perception concerns with pricing considered high by multiple families (particularly $42-50 for unpowered sites and $200 for cabins) relative to site size constraints and facility condition, especially during peak season when the park operates at full capacity.
