# Visiting Mandurah with Family

Best time to visit is September to November (spring) or March to May (autumn) – you'll dodge the scorching summer heat and the winter rains. December to February is doable if you don't mind 35+ degree days, but the kids will melt and the beaches get packed. Book accommodation early during school holidays; the town fills up quick with Perth families escaping the city. The estuary's great year-round for birdwatching, but water temps are coldest June-August, so factor that in if you're keen on swimming.

Get the kids down to the foreshore early – I'm talking 8am – to grab a park and hit the beach or playgrounds before the crowds arrive. The dolphin cruises are genuinely worth it; we've seen them nearly every time. Skip the pricey restaurants near the water and grab fish and chips from the local shops instead – honestly better quality and half the cost. Watch out for the strong currents at the main beach; stick to the patrolled areas and heed the flags. The estuary gets pretty warm and still in summer, which means mosquitoes can be brutal – bring decent insect repellent or you'll regret it.

Insider tip: take the kids to Secret Cove at Falcon Bay instead of battling the main beach crowds. The mudcrabs and sea life in the shallows keep children entertained for hours, and it's sheltered and safe. The Mandurah Performing Arts Centre often has family shows during school holidays – worth checking their schedule. And don't skip the local markets; they've got good produce and the kids usually enjoy the vibe. Most importantly, respect the local wildlife – we live alongside dolphins and seabirds here, so teach the kids to observe from a distance rather than chase.
