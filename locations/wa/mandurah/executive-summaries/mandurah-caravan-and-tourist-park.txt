PARK: Mandurah Caravan and Tourist Park
SCORE: 87/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-24
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 442

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (18/20) — Exceptional family facilities with heated pool featuring waterslide consistently praised as highlight, gated playground with jumping pillow, pedal kart hire, weekend food trucks and coffee vans, school holiday activities including crafts and Easter egg hunts, live entertainment on weekends, with parents particularly appreciating organised kids' activities that keep children engaged throughout their stay.

Nature (14/20) — Tree-filled sites providing excellent shade and birdlife that families enjoy feeding by hand, located near Serpentine River with cycle paths mentioned, approximately 15 minutes from Mandurah Foreshore and beaches, pleasant tropical retreat feel from established gardens and lawns, though not directly beachfront with some reviews noting need to drive to coastal areas.

Cleanliness (15/15) — Outstanding performance with overwhelming consensus on spotless amenities, "immaculate" bathrooms and camp kitchens cleaned multiple times daily, grounds crew constantly visible maintaining lawns and gardens, facilities described as "cleanest caravan park we've stayed in" and "pristine", laundry noted as cleanest encountered by grey nomads, bins provided at every site and emptied regularly.

Value (12/15) — Generally good value though some reviews note pricing "a bit expensive" for off-peak periods and tent sites at $57/night seen as overpriced for basic grass pitch, G'day membership discounts appreciated, families report good value compared to hotels given kitchenette facilities in cabins, special deals and vouchers available through website offering better rates than Facebook promotions, ice at $5 per large bag considered reasonable.

Site Size (9/10) — Large concrete slab sites with room for caravan, annex and vehicle side by side, "huge pitches" with generous space between neighbours, each site has double concrete slab plus grassed area for car, though access roads are narrow requiring staff assistance to navigate, some sites tight between trees but staff guidance makes parking manageable, individual grey water dump points per four sites.

Sentiment (10/10) — Exceptional repeat visitation with families returning annually and grey nomads rating it "top 10 parks" in Australia, glowing recommendations to friends and family, staff named individually in reviews (Jim, Helen, Noah, Shahar, Mick) for outstanding service, numerous "will definitely be back" statements, families booking 12 months ahead for return visits, overwhelmingly positive 5-star reviews dominate.

Location (9/10) — Strategic position on Pinjarra Road with Mandurah Forum shopping centre 2km away, Spud Shed walkable for essentials, 5-10 minutes drive to Mandurah Foreshore and town centre, bus stop at park entrance with park-and-ride nearby, one hour from Perth, convenient access to Serpentine River, Dolphin Discovery Centre, and Pinjarra attractions, though not beach-adjacent requiring short drive to coast.

WHY NOT 100:
The park's narrow internal roads create manoeuvring challenges despite excellent staff assistance, with several reviews noting tight access between established trees requiring careful navigation even with guidance. Pool and playground operating hours (9am-6pm) don't align with summer daylight or families' early-rising children, causing frustration when facilities close at 6pm with sun still high and kids wanting to swim. Mosquitoes are severe after dark making outdoor activity difficult, with multiple reviews describing them as "horrendous" and warning they swarm when doors open, significantly impacting evening enjoyment especially in warmer months.
