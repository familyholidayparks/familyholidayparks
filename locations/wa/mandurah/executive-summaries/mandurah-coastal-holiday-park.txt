PARK: Mandurah Coastal Holiday Park
SCORE: 52/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-26
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 119

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (6/20) — Reviews consistently note minimal entertainment with one small fenced playground that lacks shade, no pool, no jumping pillow (despite website claims), and zero organized kids activities. Multiple families explicitly stated "wouldn't recommend it for families" and "There's no way I would bring kids to this caravan park."

Nature (12/20) — Park offers reasonable beach access with 10-15 minute walk to waterfront and dog beach near Silver Sands, close to Dolphin Quay attractions, but the natural environment within the park itself is compromised by potholes, poor grass maintenance, and urban surroundings with homeless presence and police activity.

Cleanliness (9/15) — Mixed evidence with some praising "spotless" amenities and "super clean" toilets/showers, while others reported mould, dirty cabins requiring multiple cleaner visits, "floors dirty, drains dirty, kids hand prints all over," maintenance issues including leaking showers, and overall facility deterioration needing substantial work.

Value (10/15) — Reviews split between "excellent value for money $59 per night" and "ridiculous prices" for what's offered. Multiple guests felt other parks provided better value for similar or lower cost, with concerns about high deposits ($250 AUD) and fees for cleaning ($100).

Site Size (7/10) — Several reviews mention "great size" and "spacious flat site" with concrete slabs and grass, but multiple warnings that larger caravans 20ft+ find it "very tight" and difficult to maneuver, with narrow laneways and uneven ground on some sites.

Sentiment (4/10) — Heavily negative family sentiment with recurring safety warnings including "not safe for kids or family," multiple theft reports (bikes stolen, belongings taken from cars), "dodgey characters around," police presence, and families explicitly leaving early or refusing to return. Positive reviews mainly from solo/couple stays in clean rooms.

Location (4/10) — While technically close to Mandurah town centre, beach, and shops within walking distance, the surrounding area concerns significantly impact family suitability with "police sirens day and night," homeless people around local IGA, questionable neighbourhood atmosphere, and families feeling unsafe throughout their stay.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Critical safety failures for families with multiple documented thefts (bikes stolen on multiple occasions, belongings taken from cars and sites, items scavenged nightly), residents "terrorising" visitors, police regularly called, and explicit warnings from numerous families to "stay away for your safety."

2. Near-complete absence of family entertainment infrastructure with no pool, minimal playground lacking shade, no jumping pillow despite marketing claims, zero organized activities, and multiple families specifically stating they wouldn't recommend for children or felt it was boring.

3. Significant maintenance and management issues including potholes, broken cement slabs, varying standards of cleanliness (mould, dirty cabins), permanent resident conflicts creating uncomfortable atmosphere, inconsistent service quality, and ongoing facility deterioration that undermines the holiday experience despite new management attempting improvements.
