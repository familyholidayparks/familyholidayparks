PARK: Coral Coast Tourist Park
SCORE: 72/100
DATE SCORED: 2024-01-10
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 124

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (10/20) — Limited on-site entertainment with one German reviewer noting "the playground should be renewed as soon as possible" in April 2026. Food trucks visit regularly (fish & chips, pizza) and communal fires are lit in season, but no dedicated kids club or waterpark. Pool mentioned as "lovely" and "excellent" but mainly for cooling off rather than entertainment.

Nature (12/20) — Park is in Carnarvon town centre, not Coral Bay as name suggests, with "walking distance to town and waterfront." Shell grit base instead of grass, limited shade mentioned. Close to One Mile Jetty and river, but not a bush or beachfront setting. "Not a lot going on in Carnarvon" noted by visitors.

Cleanliness (14/15) — Exceptional cleanliness consistently praised: "spotless amenities," "super clean," "ensuite bathrooms spotlessly clean." Individual ensuite-style ablutions with toilet, shower, basin described as "best we've seen on our travels." Pool "cleaned every day." Minor issues: one blood stain complaint Jan 2026, basic cabins had "dust and dead flies" in Aug 2024.

Value (10/15) — Mixed feedback on pricing. $72/night mentioned as expensive April 2026, "above city centre hotel prices" noted July 2025. One review stated price "took a big leap to $65" making "better options elsewhere." However, many found it "reasonable" and "value for money" for the quality of facilities provided.

Site Size (8/10) — Sites described as "generous," "large," "spacious" with concrete awning pads. Shell grit base throughout. Some complaints: "some sites are small no where to park car," and the grit "gets everywhere and can be quite sharp." Generally adequate for caravans and families.

Sentiment (9/10) — Overwhelmingly positive with many repeat visitors. "Liked it so much we re-booked on the return trip," "stayed twice on our way up and down." Staff consistently praised as "exceptional," "super friendly," "incredibly kind." Two serious 1-star reviews: car break-in March 2025 with poor management response, and cabin with ants/roaches Dec 2024.

Location (9/10) — Excellent town location: "500m walk from shops and cafes," "Woolworths across the road," "walking distance to everything." Close to Carnarvon CBD, waterfront, and airport. Not beachfront despite coastal park name. One security concern noted about locking up after dark.

WHY NOT 100:
1. The playground is severely outdated and in urgent need of renewal according to multiple reviews, with no evidence of dedicated kids entertainment facilities or programs for families with young children.
2. Security concerns were raised with two serious incidents including a car break-in in March 2025 where management showed "zero empathy," and warnings to "lock everything up when the sun goes down."
3. Pricing has increased significantly (to $65-72/night) making it "too expensive" compared to alternatives according to several recent reviews, particularly given the basic nature of some accommodations.

NOTABLE REVIEW PHRASES:
"spotless amenities," "ensuite bathrooms spotlessly clean," "best facilities we've experienced," "staff super friendly and helpful," "playground should be renewed urgently," "walking distance to town centre," "food trucks most nights," "zero empathy" (regarding security incident), "too expensive," "best Caravan Park since we left Broome," "clean, well maintained," "pool lovely"
