# Coral Bay, Western Australia

Coral Bay is a small beachside town on WA's Coral Coast that punches well above its weight for families wanting a proper beach holiday without the crowds. The main beach is stunning – calm, turquoise waters perfect for kids to swim safely, with the Ningaloo Reef starting just offshore. You can snorkel right from the beach and spot fish, turtles and sometimes whale sharks without needing a boat, which beats driving hours to get the same experience. The town's got a relaxed vibe, local shops for supplies, and plenty of spots to grab fish and chips or a decent feed without fussing about.

For families, the real drawcard is how much there is to do without spending a fortune. Peoples Park and Coral Coast Tourist Park both have playgrounds and green space where kids can run around, while Ningaloo Coral Bay – Bayview campground puts you right near the action if you're into camping. Beyond the beach itself, you've got snorkeling, short walks through coastal scrub to spot wildlife, and rock pools to explore. The water stays warm enough for swimming most of the year, accommodation ranges from camping to holiday homes, and it's genuinely set up for families rather than trying too hard to be something it's not.
