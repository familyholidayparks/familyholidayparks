# Margaret River Family Guide

The sweet spot for visiting Margaret River is September to November (spring) or March to May (autumn) – you'll avoid the brutal summer heat and school holiday crowds that clog up the wineries and make accommodation pricey. Winter's mild but can get rainy, so pack layers either way. December to February is absolute chaos with families and tourists, and it's scorching hot if you've got little kids in tow. Book accommodation well ahead if you're coming during school holidays; everything gets snapped up fast. The beaches are stunning year-round, but spring is when you'll actually want to spend time there without melting.

Here's what families actually do here: hit the beaches at Prevelly and Surfers Point (great for teaching kids to swim in the calmer spots), explore the caves at Lake Cave or Jewel Cave (kids find them magic, though toddlers might struggle with stairs), and do the wineries – grab a picnic, plonk yourself on the lawns while you sip wine and the kids run around. The Margaret River Chocolate Company and local farm gates are brilliant for keeping everyone happy. Watch out for rips at the beaches and always check conditions; the Indian Ocean doesn't mess around, and tourists get into strife every summer ignoring the flags. Also, petrol and groceries are dearer out here, so stock up in town before heading out, and don't expect the same level of services you'd find in Perth – this is rural WA.

The real local tip: skip the touristy restaurant strips in peak times and grab takeaway from the good independent spots, or better yet, do a proper grocery shop and cook something yourself if you've got accommodation with a kitchen. Bring bikes if you can – there's a brilliant rail trail that's flat and safe for kids. And honestly, don't feel like you need to do the full wine-tasting circuit; half the magic is just being outdoors with the family, hitting the beaches and the farmers markets on weekends. The region's chilled – embrace that instead of trying to tick boxes.
