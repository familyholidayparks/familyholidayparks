Margaret River's holiday parks tap into what makes this corner of WA genuinely special – you've got the Southern Ocean minutes away, the Margaret River itself winding through native forest, and some of the best wine country in Australia all within a short drive. What sets these parks apart from your standard caravan stop is that they're positioned as bases for a proper region rather than just overnight spots. Discovery Parks – Margaret River sits closest to town and the river, giving you easy access to cellar doors and local cafes, while RAC Margaret River Nature Park leans into the bush experience with more space between sites and walking trails through jarrah and marri forest. Riverview Tourist Park and Margaret River Tourist Park fill different niches – one's more compact and family-focused, the other's got that mix of caravanners, motorhomes and a handful of cabins. You'll see serious caravan people who've been coming for decades, families who've worked Margaret River school holidays into their annual rhythm, and increasingly motorhomers doing longer loops through WA who've heard the reputation and stick around longer than planned.

Booking patterns here are pretty predictable – Easter and winter school holidays see the parks absolutely rammed, and you need to be in early. Summer's quieter than you'd expect given the proximity to beach and river, mainly because most families hit the coast further north. The real returning visitors are the ones who've figured out that Margaret River works year-round; September through November is honestly the sweet spot with fewer crowds and the forest at its best. Families come back because there's genuinely something for everyone – you've got river swimming and fishing spots, forest walks that don't require epic fitness, and the older kids can explore the cave systems or go mountain biking. The parks themselves aren't fancy, but that's not the point; people are here for what's around them, not resort facilities.

What keeps people coming back year after year is that Margaret River hasn't turned into a theme park version of itself. You can stay in a caravan park and spend your days doing anything from serious wine tasting to mucking about with fishing rods in the river to walking through old-growth forest where you'll actually see wildlife. The combination of river access, proximity to the ocean at Prevelly and Gnarabup, and the wine country creates something you don't get in other holiday park destinations – there's enough variety that different family members with different interests actually get what they want. And once people settle into a favourite park and discover their spots around the region, they tend to book the same weeks, same sites, year after year. It becomes less about the holiday and more about the ritual of returning somewhere that works.
