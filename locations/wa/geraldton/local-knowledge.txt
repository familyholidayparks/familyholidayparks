# Visiting Geraldton with the Family

**Best times to visit are April to May or September to October** – you'll dodge the scorching summer heat (40°C+ is common December to February) and the occasional cyclone season chatter. Winter's mild enough for a beach trip, but the water's bloody cold. School holidays are mental here, so book accommodation early if you're coming then. The wildflowers in spring are genuinely worth the trip if you've got kids who can handle a few nature walks.

**Get to Batavia Coast Maritime Museum early** – it opens at 10am and families pile in once school groups rock up. The wreck of the *Batavia* is legitimately fascinating, even for younger kids. Hit Fisherman's Wharf for fish and chips at lunch (takeaway's faster), then spend the afternoon at either Separation Pool or Coronation Beach depending on your energy levels. Stick to patrolled beaches during summer – we've got stingers and the occasional shark sighting, nothing that'll ruin your day but respect the flags. The pools at the leisure centre are a solid backup option if you want guaranteed calm water.

**Pro tip from locals: bring your own groceries if you're staying a few days** – the supermarkets are fine but pricier than Perth, and restaurant options are limited after 8pm. Download offline maps because phone service can be dodgy between towns. Most importantly, chat to the locals at the visitor centre – we're friendly and actually know where the good free stuff is. The kids will remember the beaches and the wildflowers, not overpriced tourist traps.
