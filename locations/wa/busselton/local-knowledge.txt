# Busselton Local Tips for Families

**Timing and Weather**
Visit during September to November (spring) or March to May (autumn) when the weather's perfect—warm enough for the beach but not scorching like December-February. School holidays get absolutely rammed with families, so if you can avoid them, do. The water's coldest June to August, so unless your kids are keen swimmers, bring wetsuits. That said, summer school hols mean heaps of activities and events running, so it's a trade-off between crowds and entertainment.

**What Makes Busselton Special for Families**
The Busselton Jetty is the real drawcard—kids reckon the underwater observatory is magic, and the walk out there's genuinely lovely. The beaches are patrolled and safe, particularly Busselton Beach itself. Queen's Gardens is underrated for families—proper green space with playgrounds, and you can grab fish and chips nearby without the tourist markup. The Underwater World aquarium is worth a morning, though it's not huge. Locals know to check the tides before beach days; low tide reveals heaps of rock pools around the Jetty that kids love exploring.

**Real Talk: Hazards and Insider Moves**
Watch for bluebottles in summer—check the beach reports and don't ignore the warning signs. The Indian Ocean can surprise you with strong currents, especially near the Jetty, so keep an eye on little ones. Book accommodation early if you're coming December to February; it books out and prices spike. Pro tip: grab supplies from Woolies or Coles before you hit the tourist shops on Geographe Bay Road—you'll save a fortune. And honestly, spend an afternoon in Dunsborough (20 mins away) rather than doing everything in Busselton proper; better restaurants, fewer crowds, equally good beaches.
