PARK: King River Palms Caravan Park
SCORE: 82/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-10
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 122

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (14/20) — Families consistently praised free canoes and kayaks for river use, fishing opportunities right from campsites, small playground, rope swings into river, and safe bike riding areas. Multiple reviews mentioned kids extending stays because they "loved kayaking and fishing" and "played in the playground and in/next to the river." One family noted "dancing birds" as bonus entertainment, though no jumping pillow or waterpark facilities.

Nature (19/20) — Outstanding natural setting directly on King River with "beautiful water frontage," established shade trees, green grassy sites, abundant birdlife including ducks and resident roosters. Reviews repeatedly highlight "right on the river," "beautiful trees and birds," and peaceful natural surrounds. Close proximity to national parks, beaches at Lower King/Nanarup, and 10km to Albany attractions.

Cleanliness (14/15) — Overwhelmingly positive feedback on "spotlessly clean," "immaculate shower areas," "squeaky clean amenities," and well-maintained facilities. Reviews note "possibly the cleanest we've ever found" and owners "kept the park spotless." Minor issues included one uncleaned cabin and occasional lack of soap/toilet paper in cubicles during peak periods.

Value (14/15) — Strong value proposition with "affordable accommodation," pricing at $35-45/night for powered sites, no markup for long weekends/school holidays, and free canoe use. Reviews describe "reasonable price," "fair price," and "good price compared to other parks." One review questioned $45/night value given permanent residents, but majority found excellent value.

Site Size (7/10) — Mixed feedback on site sizing. Positive reviews mention "large grassy sites," "roomy sites," and "big sites" especially for larger rigs. However, multiple reviews note sites can be "very tightly packed in" during busy periods with "everyone parked where they can fit" and even parking spaces used as campsites. River frontage sites are spacious but limited in number.

Sentiment (9/10) — Exceptional guest sentiment with numerous families extending stays, repeat visits ("third time back in last two months"), and strong recommendations. Phrases like "will definitely be back," "home away from home," and "best park we have stayed" dominate. Family-run warmth creates personal connections. Minor detractors from 2-3 negative service experiences.

Location (5/10) — Location 10-15 minutes from Albany town center is convenient but not beachfront. Highway proximity causes road noise for some sites. Tavern across river provides entertainment but also noise on weekends. Good access to attractions (Porongorups, Stirling Range 45min) but requires driving to beaches and amenities.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Limited dedicated kids entertainment infrastructure (small basic playground mentioned needing revamp, no jumping pillow, waterpark, or organized activities that modern family parks offer)
2. Site crowding and inconsistent sizing during peak periods, with some guests squeezed into parking spaces between permanent resident cabins, creating a "trailer park" feel according to some reviews
3. Highway and tavern noise affects some sites, with road trains audible at night and weekend bands playing until midnight, plus limited riverside sites means not all families get the premium river experience

NOTABLE REVIEW PHRASES:
