# Albany, Western Australia: A Family Holiday Destination

Albany sits on Western Australia's southern coast and offers everything families need for a proper break. The town boasts stunning beaches that suit all ages – Middleton Beach is patrolled and gentle, while Emu Beach gives you rockpools and sheltered swimming. Beyond the sand, there's the Albany Heritage Park for working farm experiences, the National Anzac Centre if the kids are old enough for history, and the Torndirrup National Park where you can walk to dramatic clifftop views without needing serious hiking skills. The town itself has good cafes, fish and chips shops, and a relaxed vibe that takes the pressure off.

For accommodation, you've got solid caravan parks scattered through the area – BIG4 Emu Beach and BIG4 Middleton Beach both have the facilities families expect, like playgrounds and camp kitchens, while King River Palms, Tasman Holiday Parks, and Panorama offer quieter alternatives depending on what suits you. The real drawcard is nature that's actually accessible with kids in tow. You get native wildlife, sheltered coastal walks, and beaches where the water's genuinely swimmable. It's the kind of place where children run around, parents relax, and everyone goes home actually tired in the good way.
