# Visiting Broome with Kids: Local Parent's Guide

The sweet spot for visiting Broome is April to September – it's hot but manageable, and you'll dodge the wet season cyclones that can shut things down. May through August is pure gold if you can swing it: brilliant sunny days, comfortable temps around 25-28°C, and the famous staircase to the moon phenomenon happens at Cable Beach (time it right during full moon and it's genuinely magical). Whatever you do, avoid November to March unless you're heat-proof – it's absolutely scorching and humidity will do your head in. Pro tip: book accommodation six months out if you're coming during school holidays; this place fills up fast and prices go silly.

Families absolutely lose it over Cable Beach – it's the main drawcard and for good reason, though keep an eye on tide times and swim between the flags during stinger season (October to May, use the netted areas). The pearl farms are worth a visit, the Japanese cemetery is quietly special, and Gantheaume Point has dinosaur footprints the kids can actually see. Hit the local cafes around Roebuck Street rather than tourist traps, grab fresh barramundi from the fish markets, and hire a 4WD if you want to explore properly – some beaches need it. Watch out for saltwater crocs in mangrove areas – locals aren't being dramatic about this one. Also, mobile reception is dodgy outside town, and fuel prices are brutal, so plan your drive routes carefully.

The real secret Broome experience is getting out to the small beaches and pindan cliffs at sunset, hiring bikes to ride around town, and chatting to locals – they're genuinely friendly and full of good stories. Book a sunset camel ride if your kids are into it (they're well-treated and it's iconic), but honestly, the free stuff beats the touristy gear. Most importantly, build in lazy days – Broome's vibe is deliberately slow, and if you're constantly rushing, you'll miss what makes it special. Bring good sunscreen and a hat you don't mind losing to the wind.
