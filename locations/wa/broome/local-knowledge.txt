# Visiting Broome with the Family

Dry season (May to October) is your sweet spot – it's hot enough to enjoy the beach without melting, and the cyclone risk is basically zero. April and November are transitional months that can work, but come December through March you're looking at stinking humidity, frequent storms, and the real possibility of a cyclone shutting everything down. School holidays are manic here; book accommodation months ahead or expect to pay through the nose. The locals will tell you the Wet season sunsets are incredible, but they're not wrong – if you time it right between storms, the light is unreal. Just don't bank on it.

Stick to Cable Beach and Town Beach during the day – they're patrolled and safe. The mudflats around Roebuck Bay look tempting but are genuinely dangerous; people get bogged and the tide comes in fast. Stingers are a real thing from November onwards, so check before you let the kids loose in the water. Bring serious sunscreen – the sun here is no joke, even for us tanned Aussies. The town itself is pretty compact, so you don't need a massive car, but do fill up on fuel before heading out to Cape Leveque or the inland spots; there's bugger all out there. Book restaurants ahead, especially dinner – places fill up quick and some close randomly.

Here's the insider move: skip the main tourist traps and hit the actual local beaches early morning. The pearl farms offer decent tours if your kids are into that sort of thing, but honestly, the Broome Crocodile Park and the dinosaur footprints near Gantheaume Point keep most families happiest. Grab coffee at a local spot rather than the chains, and don't miss the markets if you're here on a weekend – proper local vibe. The cable beach camel rides are touristy as they come, but if your family's into it, do it at sunset when it's cooler and actually pleasant.
