PARK: Broome's Gateway Pet Friendly Caravan Park
SCORE: 52/100
DATE SCORED: 2025
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 73

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (2/20) — Reviews consistently note this park has no entertainment facilities, playground, or kids' activities whatsoever; one review explicitly states "not family friendly" with additional charges for children over 12.
Nature (14/20) — Multiple reviews praise the "bush camping with amenities" feel, "country feel", "rural/station atmosphere", "beautiful waking up to the sound of birds", shady trees, and peaceful natural surroundings; however, some mention dusty red dirt sites with ants.
Cleanliness (11/15) — Most recent reviews praise "spotless" toilets and "clean amenities", though several older reviews mention ant infestations in camp kitchen, broken washing machines, and areas needing cleanup; facilities described as "dated but acceptable".
Value (8/15) — Highly polarized feedback: some praise "great price" at $36-40/night compared to Broome parks, while multiple families with children call it "expensive" and "absolute robbery" for unpowered sites with no dump point, especially with $20/night extra charges per child over 12.
Site Size (9/10) — Overwhelming consensus on "huge sites", "very spacious", "heaps of room between sites", "generous size", and "way bigger than any caravan park" with easy drive-through access.
Sentiment (5/10) — Mixed sentiment: self-contained couples and pet owners express high satisfaction and return intentions, while families with young children express disappointment; many note it was chosen only because Broome parks were full.
Location (3/10) — Consistently noted as 20-30km/20-30 minutes from Broome on bumpy dirt access road, requiring daily drives for tours and attractions; described as "inconvenient distance" for multi-night family stays.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Complete absence of family entertainment, playground, pool, or children's activities makes this unsuitable as a destination park for families with young children seeking holiday experiences.
2. Location 20-30km from Broome requires daily driving for all attractions, tours, and town visits, with "terrible road in, so bumpy" access limiting convenience for families.
3. No powered sites (generators only 8am-8pm), no dump point, insufficient toilet facilities for park size during peak periods ("only six unisex shower cubicles" causing "unbearable" wait times), and additional charges for children over 12 significantly reduce value and convenience for families.
