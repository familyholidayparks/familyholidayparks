# Broome, Western Australia: A Family Holiday Destination

Broome is a relaxed coastal town in Western Australia's Kimberley region that offers families a genuine outback experience without the stress. The town's main drawcard is Cable Beach, a stunning 22-kilometre stretch of red sand and turquoise water perfect for swimming, paddling and building sandcastles. Beyond the beach, families can explore natural attractions like Roebuck Bay's tidal flats, where the dramatic 11-metre tidal range creates unique landscapes, and nearby nature reserves that showcase the region's diverse wildlife. The town also has kid-friendly attractions including the Broome Crocodile Park, pearl industry heritage sites, and regular markets that give families a genuine taste of outback life.

For accommodation, several caravan parks cater well to families seeking a relaxed stay. Options like Cable Beach Caravan Park, RAC Cable Beach Holiday Park, Discovery Parks – Broome, Tarangau Caravan Park and Broome Caravan Park offer powered sites, swimming pools and easy access to the beach. Broome suits families who prefer a slower pace and don't need theme parks or elaborate entertainment—the real appeal is outdoor adventures, beach days and learning about the natural environment. With warm weather most of the year, particularly April to September, it's an ideal destination for families wanting to escape the southern winter.
