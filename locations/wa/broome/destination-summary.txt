Broome's caravan parks sit in a unique pocket of Western Australia where the Indian Ocean meets red pindan soil, and this geography shapes everything about staying here. Cable Beach Caravan Park and RAC Cable Beach Holiday Park put you right on the sand—literally metres from the water where you can swim, fish, or just watch the famous Broome sunsets that turn the beach orange. Tarangau and Broome Caravan Park offer a different experience, set back slightly with bush surrounds that feel more sheltered and quieter, though you're still close enough to reach the beach and river access within minutes. Discovery Parks - Broome fills the middle ground, giving families resort-style facilities without the beachfront intensity. What makes Broome different to other coastal parks down south is the raw feel of it—this is the Kimberley, not some sanitised holiday zone. The tides here are massive, the light is harsh, and the landscape doesn't apologise for itself.

You'll find everything from retirees in permanent vans who've essentially moved to Broome, to families packing a caravan for the winter school holidays when the weather's perfect, to motorhomers doing lap of Australia routes. The parks have evolved to handle this mix—Cable Beach caters heavily to tourists wanting beachfront convenience, while Tarangau gets repeat visitors who book the same site year after year. School holidays, particularly June through August, are when these parks fill to capacity; families know that Broome's dry season is the only realistic window to visit comfortably. You'll see the same caravans return regularly, their owners having worked out which park suits their style and staking their claim for the cooler months.

What keeps people coming back isn't just the beach access, though wading through shallow turquoise water at Cable Beach or spotting crocodiles from a distance at Roebuck Bay matters. It's the combination—mornings at the markets, afternoon swims, evening walks along the shore, and nights where the Milky Way is so bright it casts a shadow. Families appreciate that kids can safely explore rock pools, ride bikes through the park, and wind down in the evening without needing constant entertainment. The parks themselves are low-key; they're bases for exploring Broome rather than destinations in their own right, which is exactly what appeals to people who come back. Once you've spent a winter season here, you understand why caravanners queue up for bookings months in advance.
