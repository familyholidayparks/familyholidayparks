PARK: Quorn Caravan Park
SCORE: 59/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-18
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 140

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (4/20) — Reviews consistently note this park has no playground, kids' club, or on-site entertainment facilities. Families mention nearby attractions like the silo light show, Pichi Richi steam train, and heritage walks in town, but nothing specifically for children at the park itself.

Nature (16/20) — Multiple reviews praise the "lovely quiet park set amongst mature eucalyptus," "bush atmosphere," "beautiful and peaceful" setting, and "great surroundings." Guests appreciate the natural environment, bird life, walking tracks, and proximity to Flinders Ranges gorges and 4x4 tracks, though it's a town park rather than beachfront or creek-side.

Cleanliness (14/15) — Overwhelmingly positive feedback with phrases like "spotlessly clean," "super clean amenities," "cleanest caravan park in Australia," and "meticulously clean." A few reviews note facilities are dated but impeccably maintained. One review deducted a point mentioning grey water drainage issues on specific sites.

Value (10/15) — Reviews describe pricing as "reasonable," "affordable" ($25 tent sites mentioned), and "great price for power sites." However, one family complained about a $240/night cabin price being expensive and a $25 surcharge dispute, suggesting cabin pricing may be high for families. Overall feedback indicates fair value for standard sites.

Site Size (8/10) — Most reviews mention "spacious sites," "large sites," "big bays," and "plenty of space." Some feedback notes "no sardines feeling" and room for 40ft buses. However, a few reviews mention sites were "tricky to get into," some were "poorly allocated," and one guest felt their site wasn't quite big enough for camper and car.

Sentiment (8/10) — Strong repeat visitor mentions with "second time we have stayed," "been 2x and will be back," and guests extending stays. Friendly staff praised consistently. However, several one-star reviews cite "extremely rude" staff, pricing disputes, and being "sent away," suggesting inconsistent customer service experiences that lower overall sentiment.

Location (9/10) — Excellent feedback on "walking distance to town," "short walk across railway tracks," "3-4 minute walk to streets," and proximity to silo show, pubs, railway, and Flinders Ranges attractions. Very accessible for exploring Quorn and surrounding region.

WHY NOT 100:
1. No children's entertainment facilities whatsoever—no playground, pool, waterpark, kids' club, or organized activities that families with young children expect from holiday parks.
2. Several concerning reviews about rude staff and poor customer service experiences, including families with young children being turned away and pricing disputes, suggesting inconsistent management approach.
3. Limited on-site attractions beyond clean amenities and camp kitchen—this is purely a stopover park relying entirely on external town attractions rather than providing family entertainment within the park itself.
