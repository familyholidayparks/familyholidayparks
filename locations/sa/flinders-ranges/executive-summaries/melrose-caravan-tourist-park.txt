PARK: Melrose Caravan & Tourist Park
SCORE: 61/100
DATE SCORED: 2024-01-10
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 100

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (11/20) — Reviews highlight a playground, basketball court, epic dirt bike jumps, and extensive bike tracks that kids love, with families mentioning children were "constantly over at the epic dirt bike jumps." However, on-site organized entertainment is minimal, with activities primarily self-directed outdoor recreation focused on the mountain biking trail network.

Nature (18/20) — Exceptional natural setting "at the foot of Mount Remarkable" with "beautiful bush setting," creek access, "large shady gum trees," and "wooded creek" running through the park. Direct access to hiking trails, Heysen Trail, and "huge trail network on the slopes of Mount Remarkable" with multiple reviews praising the "stunning" natural environment and proximity to Alligator Gorge.

Cleanliness (8/15) — Highly inconsistent cleanliness with polarized reviews. Recent reviews mention "brand new shower and toilet block" and "very clean" facilities, but multiple 2024-2025 reviews report "filthy" toilets, "dirty" camp kitchens with "permanent sewerage smell," amenities "NOT been cleaned for a long time," mould issues in cabins, and "appalling conditions of the male amenities block" with broken fixtures throughout.

Value (10/15) — Mixed value perception with some praising cabin rates at "$135 a night" as a "bargain," but others finding "$21 per person for an unpowered campsite felt steep." Family travelers note good value for cabin accommodation but concerns about cramped sites and poor amenities quality diminishing overall value proposition.

Site Size (5/10) — Significant criticism of site sizes with reviews repeatedly mentioning "sites are very close together," "they cram you in like sardines," "unlevel cramped and disjointed sites," and "very hard to get a good spot." Multiple families noted feeling overcrowded especially during peak periods with "barely any level ground" for tent campers.

Sentiment (6/10) — Mixed family sentiment with loyal returners stating "we come every year" and "love it here," but offset by families reporting they "won't be coming back again" due to declining standards. Several three-generation family stays mentioned positively, but repeat visitors note "every stay has got progressively worse" with management issues impacting family enjoyment.

Location (3/10) — While located in the Flinders Ranges and close to Mount Remarkable, the park is in a remote regional area requiring significant travel for most Australian families. "Walking distance to the 2 local pubs" and town amenities, but limited broader accessibility and remote location reduces convenience for families with young children seeking diverse attractions.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Severe and persistent cleanliness issues across multiple amenity blocks, with families reporting "filthy" toilets not cleaned for days, mould in cabins causing health issues, broken fixtures throughout, and a "permanent sewerage smell" that has existed for years according to repeat visitors.
2. Extremely cramped site sizes that "cram you in like sardines" making it unsuitable for families wanting space, with particular concerns during school holidays when the park becomes "incredibly noisy" with uncontrolled teenage cyclists endangering young children and noise continuing until 4am.
3. Budget cabin accommodation described as "pretty grim" and "not really fit for habitation" with non-functioning hot water, broken power points, terrible mattresses, and spaces "way too small for 2 adults and a child" despite being marketed for 5 people, creating unsafe and uncomfortable conditions for families.
