# Visiting Flinders Ranges with Kids

Best time to visit is April to May or September to October – you'll dodge the brutal summer heat (regularly hits 40°C) and winter cold snaps that can make hiking miserable. School holidays are packed, so if you can swing weekdays you'll have carparks and walking tracks mostly to yourself. Spring wildflowers are magic if you time it right. Bring way more water than you think you'll need; the ranges are stunning but unforgiving, and dehydration sneaks up fast on kids. Stock up in Wilpena or Brachina before heading into remote areas – there's bugger all out there once you commit to a drive.

What families genuinely love: Wilpena Pound is the drawcard, and yeah, it's worth the hype – the Pound Walk is doable with primary school kids and the views are properly impressive. The old ruins and rock formations keep children entertained way longer than you'd expect. Brachina Gorge has an excellent self-guided geology walk that's short enough for little legs. Pro tip: book accommodation early and grab a park map – some of the best stuff (like the Parachilna Gorge loop) isn't plastered everywhere. Stargazing here is genuinely world-class, so bring a blanket and stay up one night.

Watch out for snakes and goannas (they're not aggressive but give them space), and don't underestimate how exposed you are on the walking tracks – there's minimal shade and the sun reflects off the rocks. Your phone reception is patchy at best, so don't rely on GPS alone. Fuel up in town before heading south; the petrol prices inland are eye-watering. If you've got toddlers, stick to the easier walks around Wilpena – some of the tracks are rocky and steep, and you won't enjoy carrying a tired three-year-old back uphill.
