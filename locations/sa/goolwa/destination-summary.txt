# Goolwa Holiday Parks

Discovery Parks Goolwa sits at the mouth of the Murray River where it opens into the Coorong, and that location is precisely what draws people back. The park itself is right on the riverbank with direct access to the water—you can launch a tinnie, kayak, or just wade in from your site. What makes Goolwa different from other South Australian park destinations is this river-to-ocean transition zone. You've got the calm, sheltered Murray waters for fishing and boating, but also the Coorong's shallow lagoons if you want something wilder. The town has a working port atmosphere rather than a holiday resort feel, which suits the mix of visitors: serious anglers in their caravans, motorhome couples doing the big lap, families after school holidays, and grey nomads who've booked the same week for five years running. The Discovery Parks site is the main game in town for powered and unpowered camping, and it fills hard during July and December when school holidays hit.

The park caters to different camping styles without pretending to be something it's not. You've got straight caravan sites with power and water if that's your setup, but also grassed areas for motorhomes and enough unpowered spots for families who want to reduce costs. There's a boat ramp right there, which is the real drawcard—bloke pull up with a 5m boat, park the van thirty metres away, and they're on the water within minutes. It's not a resort with pools and activities committees; it's a working holiday park where people come to fish, kayak the Coorong, or just sit on their deck watching the river. Families come back because kids can safely explore the foreshore, ride bikes around the park, and there's actual stuff to do outdoors rather than manufactured entertainment.

School holidays and long weekends drive the bookings hard, particularly when the barramundi are biting or when families want a proper water-based break without driving to Victoria. The repeat visitors—and there's plenty of them—come for the combination of river access, coastal proximity, and the fact that Goolwa itself is low-key enough that you're not fighting crowds. They know which sites have the best views, which spots are sheltered from the southerly, and where to launch if conditions are rough. That's the real character of holiday parks in Goolwa: they're functional, riverside-focused places where people come to do something, not just be somewhere. Discovery Parks is reliable infrastructure in a location that genuinely delivers on water access and small-town pace.
