# Visiting Goolwa with Kids: Local Know-How

**Best times to visit are September to November (spring) and March to May (autumn)** – the weather's perfect for mucking about outdoors without melting or freezing. Summer's busy and hot, winter can be grey and windy. School holidays absolutely pack the place out, so if you can avoid them, do. The town's quiet enough that you won't struggle for accommodation or restaurant spots if you come shoulder season. Water temperature's decent from November onwards if your kids want to splash in the Murray, though it's cold as hell before that, so don't be surprised when they scream.

Here's the real stuff: **book the Cockle Train in advance** – it genuinely sells out and the kids absolutely love it. Tauwitchere Barrage is free and fantastic for spotting birdlife and letting them run around safely. Bring your own picnic supplies because the café options are limited and overpriced for what you get. The beaches are sheltered and gentle, perfect for littlies, but check conditions before you go – the Murray mouth can get rough and the current's no joke. Don't let them wander off alone near the water; stick to designated swim areas. The local IGA and bakery are your mates for supplies; grab your coffee and pies there, not the tourist spots.

**Pack layers and sunscreen without fail** – the wind can flip from warm to freezing in an afternoon, and the sun's deceptive on the water. If your kids get bored, the nearby Coorong is worth a day trip, and Adelaide's only 90 minutes away if you need a backup plan. Chat to the locals at your accommodation; they'll point you toward hidden spots and warn you about what's closed. Most importantly, come for the relaxed vibe – Goolwa's not about thrills, it's about slowing down and letting the kids actually get tired instead of overstimulated.
