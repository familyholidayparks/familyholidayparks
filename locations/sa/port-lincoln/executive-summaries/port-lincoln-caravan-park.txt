PARK: Port Lincoln Caravan Park
SCORE: 72/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-15
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 368

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (12/20) — Reviews mention a small playground, swimming pool, games room with TV, and excellent fishing from the park's own jetty. The camp kitchen is well-equipped with indoor/outdoor seating and BBQ facilities. However, multiple families noted the playground "needs updating" and is "very poor," with limited on-site entertainment beyond fishing and the pool.

Nature (18/20) — Exceptional waterfront setting with "stunning ocean views" across Boston Bay. Direct access to jetty, swimming beach, and the Parnkalla walking trail that connects to town. Reviews consistently praise "beautiful water views everywhere," terraced sites ensuring "every site has an ocean view," and frequent wildlife sightings including dolphins, seals, and pelicans.

Cleanliness (12/15) — Mixed feedback with a clear divide. Newer amenities and camp kitchen described as "spotlessly clean" and "excellent," but older toilet blocks (Block 3) repeatedly criticized as "old and decrepit," "rusty," "disgusting," with "broken tiles," "peeling paint," and "grime." Daily cleaning maintained but facilities themselves need renovation. Most reviews acknowledge "dated but very clean" or "old but spotlessly clean."

Value (12/15) — Pricing ranges $27-$55 per night with mixed value perceptions. Many found it "reasonable" and "good value" compared to other Port Lincoln options, but some felt "overpriced" for the dated facilities, particularly at $45-$55 rates. Unpowered sites at $40-$45 considered poor value given slope issues. Most agreed powered waterfront sites offered best value.

Site Size (9/10) — Consistently praised as "huge," "massive," "generous sized sites" with "plenty of room" for large rigs. Drive-through sites particularly well-received. Main criticism: unpowered areas are unallocated steep slopes where "finding a decently level site is almost impossible," requiring leveling blocks or rocks under wheels.

Sentiment (7/10) — Generally positive with many stating "will definitely be back" and "highly recommend," but sentiment dampened by facility age concerns, unpowered site issues, noise from tradies leaving at 3am, and some security incidents (bike thefts, break-ins mentioned). Recent reviews show declining satisfaction with cleanliness standards and management responsiveness.

Location (9/10) — "10 minutes from Port Lincoln" on the waterfront highway. Easy access to town, national parks, and attractions. Walking/cycling track to town available but "hilly" and "2km." Next to servo with takeaway food. Some highway noise noted but "not intrusive." Perfect base for exploring Eyre Peninsula.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Older amenities blocks desperately need complete renovation - multiple reviews describe Block 3 as "disgusting," "rusty," "decrepit" with broken tiles, peeling paint, missing hooks, and inadequate cleaning despite daily servicing.
2. Unpowered camping areas are poorly designed steep slopes with no marked or level sites, making them nearly unusable for tents and challenging even with leveling equipment, yet charged premium rates.
3. Limited kids' entertainment and dated playground facilities don't meet modern family holiday park standards, with minimal organized activities or programs for children beyond basic pool and small playground.
