# Visiting Yorke Peninsula with the Family

The best time to visit is October through April – spring through autumn – when the weather's warm and the beaches are actually swimmable. December school holidays are hectic and prices spike, so if you can swing it, aim for late October or March when it's still beautiful but less packed. Winter's dead quiet, which some families love, but the water's freezing and you'll cop some rainy days. Pro tip: avoid long weekends and South Australian school holidays if you want a peaceful time – the carparks at Rapid Bay and Stenhouse Bay fill up quick. Book accommodation early if you're coming during peak season; the good spots get snapped up months out.

What families actually do here: the kids lose their minds at the beaches – Moana is perfect for little ones with its gentle shelving, while Prices Beach and Stansbury are fantastic for older kids who want to explore rockpools and hunt for shells. Encounter Bay's the big drawcard for swimming and has decent facilities. Bring a packed lunch because eating out gets expensive and options are limited once you're away from Kadina or Moree. The Cockle Train is a classic experience, though pricey for a family of four. Most locals just spend their time beach-hopping, fishing off the rocks, and visiting the small heritage spots like Solomontown.

Watch out for: the roads are narrow and winding – take it easy, especially heading down to Innes National Park. Summer brings bluebottles to the beaches, so check conditions and swim between the flags. Mobile coverage is patchy inland, so don't rely on GPS in all spots. If you're fishing or going out on the water, the currents can be sneaky – respect the ocean. And honestly? Don't expect gourmet dining or fancy attractions. Yorke Peninsula's about simple, outdoor family time; if you want theme parks and shopping, head to Adelaide. Come here to relax, swim, and let the kids run around – that's the whole point.
