PARK: Point Turton Caravan Park
SCORE: 76/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-10
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 134

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (12/20) — Reviews consistently mention no playground or pool facilities, with one family noting "nothing for children to do there" and another stating "lack of playgrounds and pools would have been a challenge." However, families praised natural entertainment including jetty fishing, jetty jumping, stingray and eagle ray watching, and one Easter egg hunt event. Kids "love the fishing, jetty jumping and exploring the area."

Nature (19/20) — Outstanding natural environment with spectacular ocean views, direct jetty access, and stingray/eagle ray watching described as a highlight by numerous families. "Crystal clear water," proximity to Flaherty's Beach, Innes National Park, and Hillock's Drive consistently praised. "Beautiful coastal location" and "stunning coastline on your doorstep" mentioned repeatedly. Wildlife including dolphins at boat ramp adds to appeal.

Cleanliness (14/15) — Exceptional cleanliness standards with reviewers using phrases like "immaculate amenities," "hotel grade clean," "spotlessly clean," and "constantly clean." Staff continuously maintain facilities with one guest noting "owners never stopped getting things fixed or cleaned." Camp kitchen, showers, and amenities consistently described as very clean, though some noted facilities are "dated but clean."

Value (12/15) — Mixed feedback on value with most finding it "reasonable price" and appreciating well-equipped cabins with coffee machines and provisions. However, complaints about dark luxpod accommodations, extra toilet paper requests being denied, and two-night minimum policies suggest value concerns. Limited local services (one general store, one pub) noted as restricting dining options.

Site Size (9/10) — Generally positive with "good sized sites," "huge grassed sites," and "lots of room for vans and tents" mentioned frequently. Waterfront sites particularly spacious. Some concerns about sites being "tight" and "hard to get into some" for big rigs, with one review noting roof tent sites "a bit on top of each other."

Sentiment (9/10) — Very strong positive sentiment with numerous "will be back" statements and repeat visitors. Families described it as "like a second home" and "one of our favorites." Staff (Dani and Malcolm) frequently praised by name. A few negative reviews about dark accommodations and lack of flexibility prevent full marks.

Location (10/10) — Perfect location score with jetty "right there," tavern 200m walk, boat ramp at park entrance, and described as "best position on the Yorke Peninsula." Excellent base for exploring lower Yorke Peninsula attractions. "Location location location" emphasized in multiple reviews.

WHY NOT 100:
1. No traditional kids' entertainment infrastructure (playground, pool, waterpark, jumping pillow) limits appeal for families with younger children, with multiple reviews specifically noting this gap.
2. Some accommodation units (luxpods) received criticism for being "dark horrible accommodation" with no windows in bedrooms, and dated amenities throughout the park need upgrading despite being clean.
3. Limited flexibility on policies (two-night minimums even in quiet season), restricted local services (one general store, tavern closed Tuesdays), and some value concerns regarding pricing versus amenities provided.

NOTABLE REVIEW PHRASES:
