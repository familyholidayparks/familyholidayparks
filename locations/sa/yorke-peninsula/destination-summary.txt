# Yorke Peninsula Holiday Parks

Yorke Peninsula's caravan park scene operates around a simple formula that keeps people coming back: easy coastal access without the crowds of more famous destinations. The standout parks here—Stansbury, Moonta Bay, and Port Broughton—sit at the sweet spot between serious fishing and family holiday country. What sets Yorke Peninsula apart is the geography itself. You've got the Gulf St Vincent side offering calmer waters and better sheltered anchorages, while the western reaches give you proper ocean conditions. The parks aren't sprawling resort-style operations; they're purposefully modest, which means they fill predictably during school holidays but stay manageable year-round. Repeat visitors know exactly which park suits their trip—anglers gravitate toward Port Broughton for the jetty and boat ramp access, while families with young kids prefer the gentler beaches around Stansbury and Edithburgh.

The variety of park types across the peninsula caters to different caravan styles and trip intentions. Beachfront parks like Moonta Bay and Stansbury let you wake to water views and have kids on the beach within minutes, while Point Turton and Edithburgh offer similar coastal proximity with slightly quieter carpark layouts. Port Broughton Tourist Park works differently—it's more functional than scenic, designed for serious boaties and fishermen who need reliable facilities and quick access to the gulf. Motorhomers and self-contained caravanners dominate the winter months (May to August), taking advantage of mild weather and empty sites. School holiday demand hits hard, especially Christmas-January and autumn half-term, when these parks operate near capacity. The parks themselves aren't pretentious—you won't find resort-style extras—but that's precisely why caravan park regulars prefer them to fancier alternatives up the coast.

Families return year after year because Yorke Peninsula delivers on basics without complication. Kids enjoy safe swimming at designated beaches, the rock pools around Edithburgh, and the straightforward predictability of the place—no surprises, same friendly staff, same reliable facilities. There's genuine fishing for those who want it, walking trails through native scrub at several sites, and enough small towns (Stansbury, Moonta, Port Broughton) to break up a stay without requiring major drives. Parents appreciate that the parks are affordable, the beaches are accessible, and there's genuinely nothing flash to worry about—you're paying for location and reliable service, not fancy amenities. Many families have booked the same park for ten, fifteen, or twenty years running. It's not nostalgia; it's efficiency. They know what they're getting, the kids know the beach, and the parks know their families.
