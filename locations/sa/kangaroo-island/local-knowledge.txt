# Kangaroo Island Visitor Tips

The best time to bring the kids is September through November (spring) or March to May (autumn) – you'll dodge the scorching summer heat and massive tourist crowds that hit December to February. Winter's mild enough, but some attractions close or have reduced hours, and the weather can be dodgy. Pro tip: avoid school holidays if you can unless you're prepared for packed beaches and booked-out accommodation. Book accommodation and the ferry well ahead, especially for peak times – don't rock up expecting to find somewhere decent last minute, because you won't.

What makes families lose their minds here is the wildlife – you'll see kangaroos, koalas, and echidnas without needing to go to a zoo, plus the beaches are genuinely stunning and way less crowded than the mainland. The Seal Bay colony is a big drawcard and worth doing, Remarkable Rocks is spectacular for photos, and Hanson Bay is perfect if your kids actually want to get tired out. But here's what locals bang on about: bring plenty of snacks and water because once you're out exploring, there aren't many spots to grab supplies, and the distances between attractions are bigger than they look on the map. Roads are decent but narrow and winding – take your time driving, especially with kids in the car.

Watch out for steep cliffs at lookout spots (seriously, keep toddlers close), book accommodation near Kingoodie or Penneshaw rather than outer spots unless you like 45-minute drives back to civilization, and check the ferry timetable obsessively because missing it means a rough overnight stay or a long detour. The island's brilliant for families, but it requires a bit of planning – wing it and you'll be frustrated. Do it properly and your kids will talk about it for years.
