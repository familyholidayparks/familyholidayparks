Kangaroo Island's holiday park scene punches well above its weight because you're essentially camping in a wildlife sanctuary. The three standout parks—Discovery Parks, KI Caravan Park in Kingscote, and Seafront Holiday Park—serve different needs, but they all share proximity to something you won't find on the mainland: genuine wilderness that's accessible without a four-wheel drive or serious hiking boots. Discovery Parks sits nicely positioned for accessing the island's interior attractions, while Seafront gives you that beachfront position families crave, and KI Caravan Park offers the traditional caravan park setup right in the main town. The parks themselves range from bush settings to waterfront locations, but what matters is that within 20 minutes' drive, you're looking at seal colonies, Flinders Chase National Park, or quiet beaches where you might actually see a kangaroo.

The customer base here tells you something important: these aren't flash resort parks with fancy amenities pulling in one-off tourists. You get serious caravanners who've been coming for decades, families who return the same week every school holidays because their kids won't let them skip it, and motorhome couples doing the slow lap of Australia. School holidays—particularly the winter break and summer stretch—absolutely fill these parks solid; booking 12 months ahead isn't unusual for families with school-age kids. The parks fill with repeat visitors who know exactly which cabin they want, where to launch the boat from, and which beaches their dogs are allowed on. That loyalty says something about what keeps people coming back rather than trying somewhere new.

What actually brings people back year after year is the combination of accessibility and authenticity. You get proper beach and river access without the pretence of a resort; you can take the caravan to within walking distance of penguin colonies or sea lions, chase good fishing in the right season, and have your kids genuinely tired out from exploring rock pools and spotting native wildlife. The parks themselves are comfortable enough—solid facilities, reliable power and water—but they're not competing on swimming pools or fancy restaurants. They're competing on location. A family can wake up in their caravan, walk to a beach, come back for lunch, drive 20 minutes to Seal Bay, and be back for tea. That's the Kangaroo Island formula, and it works because it's real.
