PARK: Brighton Beachfront Holiday Park
SCORE: 73/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-17
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 200

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (14/20) — Reviews consistently mention mini golf (9-hole course), playground, games room with table tennis, and bike hire as on-site activities. Many families noted "plenty for kids to do" and "thoughtful activities for the kids," but entertainment is primarily beach-focused rather than structured programs or waterpark facilities. The surf club next door offers meals and the on-site cafe is popular, but no waterslides, jumping pillow, or dedicated kids club mentioned.

Nature (19/20) — Outstanding natural setting with direct beachfront access repeatedly praised as "right on the beach," "literally 10 seconds away from the beach," and "stunning beach on the west." The safe swimming beach, dog-friendly sand, beautiful sunsets, coastal cliffs, and 2km esplanade boardwalk to Glenelg scored exceptionally well. Families loved morning beach walks, dolphin sightings, and snorkelling. Only minor point lost as it's suburban coastal rather than bushland.

Cleanliness (13/15) — Highly mixed results. Powered sites and amenities blocks received consistent praise: "spotless amenities," "extremely clean," "immaculate amenities," "always very clean." However, multiple recent reviews of budget cabins reported serious issues: "cabin was absolutely filthy," "mould above the bathroom shower," "floor making our feet black," "sand in the sheets," and "bedsheets were dirty and had a foul smell." Camp kitchen described as "superb" and "well equipped."

Value (11/15) — Mixed feedback on value. Several reviews noted "a little pricey" and "a bit expensive," though many felt the beachfront location justified costs. Positive value mentions included "special saving deals," "good price for cabin with three kids," and "prices very competitive." The $76/night ensuite sites were considered expensive by some but "well worth it" by others. Budget cabin quality issues undermined value perception.

Site Size (6/10) — Major recurring complaint across 3 years: "sites are very tight," "packed in like Sardines," "pitches are squashed in and caravans are on top of each other," "very cramped and difficult to reverse in," "narrow roads makes it difficult to back a caravan onto site." Multiple reviews warned that caravans over 22ft struggle, with one 8.3m van unable to open awning and park car. Positive notes: sites are level, grassy, some have mesh floors, and beachfront sites exist.

Sentiment (8/10) — Strong loyalty from repeat visitors ("fourth visit," "been coming here for 70+ years," "5 years in a row") and many "will definitely be back" statements. However, recent reviews show declining sentiment due to management changes: "won't be back," "this place used to be amazing but our recent visit was a disaster," complaints about "horrible managers" and poor handling of noise complaints. Staff praised as "amazing," "friendly," "helpful" but management criticized for being "unprofessional" and "demeaning."

Location (10/10) — Perfect score for location. Direct beachfront position with walking distance to cafes, surf club, yacht club, Seacliff pub, and train station (15-20 min walk, 25 min to Adelaide CBD). Families loved the accessibility: "close to everything," "perfect for city visit, south coast and McLaren Vale wines," "bike ridable to Glenelg." Safe, family-friendly suburb setting with esplanade for walking/cycling.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Site spacing is critically tight for family caravans and motorhomes, with multiple reviews warning larger rigs (over 22ft) genuinely struggle to maneuver and cannot fit awnings plus vehicles, creating a cramped, stressful experience that detracts from the holiday atmosphere.

2. Budget cabin cleanliness has deteriorated significantly in recent reviews with repeated reports of mould, filthy floors, dirty bedsheets, cockroaches, and spiders—unacceptable standards that suggest inconsistent housekeeping and maintenance oversight.

3. Management professionalism has declined noticeably, with multiple 2024-2026 reviews citing dismissive handling of legitimate noise complaints (early morning barking dogs), unhelpful attitudes, and guests feeling unwelcome or "interrogated," marking a clear departure from earlier positive experiences.
