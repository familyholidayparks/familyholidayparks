PARK: Mount Barker Caravan & Tourist Park - South Aust
SCORE: 58/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-18
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 171

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (6/20) — Reviews consistently note "not a heap for kids to do" with only a "small playground" and "very small toddler playground." Multiple reviews state "park possibly more suited to couples as not lot of kids facilities" and families complained about "extra charge per kid per day but they provide no kids amenities to justify the cost." No jumping pillows, waterpark, or organized activities mentioned. One family noted their son loves the playground but suggested adding shade, while another review emphasized it "suits more middle age to older people."

Nature (14/20) — The park sits in a "beautiful treed setting" with "lovely trees" and "green and lush" grounds in the Adelaide Hills. Reviews mention "beautiful large shady cedars," gum trees, walking trails along a creek pathway, and "lots of different birds singing." The adjacent "sports oval" provides open space, and guests enjoyed watching "rabbits run through the park at all hours." Close proximity to wineries, Hahndorf (10 minutes), and Monarto Safari Park adds nature access, though the park itself is more manicured gardens than bushland.

Cleanliness (13/15) — Overwhelmingly positive feedback on cleanliness with "spotless amenities," "exceptionally clean," and "amenities kept spotless" repeated frequently. However, two significant negative reviews cited "dirt everywhere, pubes in the shower" and "rubbish in the cupboard, main room has alot of dust." One reviewer warned "don't check under the beds" due to neglect underneath. Amenities described as "a bit dated" and "old" but "cleaned daily," with one noting showers "run hot and cold."

Value (12/15) — Reviews praise "very reasonably priced," "over $300 cheaper than caravan parks around," and "best value in town." However, a family complained about unexpected "$25 per linen set" charges and "$30 per head for guests" in executive cabin, calling it a "very negative situation." One guest felt $245/night for an uncleaned cabin was poor value. Generally seen as affordable for the region, particularly for powered sites, with "reasonable prices for laundry facilities."

Site Size (9/10) — Consistently positive with "spacious" sites, "good sized sites," "large sites," "fantastic large drive through sites," and "huge and perfectly flat" drive-through options. Sites described as "very generous," with "heaps of room" and "easy to get into." One review noted the park is "a little tight" but most found ample space for large vans and caravans with "plenty of room."

Sentiment (8/10) — Strong positive sentiment toward management (Mike and Trent praised extensively as "legends," "fantastic hosts," "best caravan park managers we have had in 20 years"), but divided family feedback. Many repeat visitors and "will definitely be back" statements, but critical reviews from families feeling unwelcome: "children are not welcome here," "owners are horrible people who judge and discriminate," and complaints about rude reception staff. Overall sentiment is positive for couples/retirees but negative for some families with young children.

Location (9/10) — Excellent location "walking distance to shops," "5 minutes walk to downtown Mt Barker," with Coles, restaurants, and cinema accessible. "30 minutes to Adelaide," "10 min drive to Hahndorf," and "close to all tourist spots in the hills." Bus terminal nearby for Adelaide access. Reviewers appreciated being "close to wineries" and attractions while maintaining a "quiet end of town" setting. Very well positioned as a base for exploring the Adelaide Hills region.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Minimal kids' entertainment facilities - Reviews consistently note lack of children's amenities despite charging per child, with only a basic small playground and no water features, jumping pillows, or organized activities that family holiday parks typically offer.

2. Significant cleanliness complaints in cabins - Despite excellent amenities reviews, multiple guests reported uncleaned cabins with "dirt everywhere," dust, rubbish in cupboards, and unhygienic conditions, with one experiencing complete refund refusal for a $245/night dirty cabin.

3. Reported discrimination against families with children - Several reviews explicitly stated children felt unwelcome, with complaints about staff attitude, extra charges without justification, and one stark warning: "Do not think of staying here if you have children... it has been made very clear our children are not welcome here."
