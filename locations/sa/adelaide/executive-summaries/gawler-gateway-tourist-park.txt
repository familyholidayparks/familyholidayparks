PARK: Gawler Gateway Tourist Park
SCORE: 58/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-04-08
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 58

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (10/20) — Reviews mention a playground and pool as the primary on-site entertainment. Multiple families noted "fantastic playground for the kids" and a "lovely clean pool" that was recently renovated. However, there's no evidence of organized activities, kids clubs, or structured entertainment programs that families expect from holiday parks.

Nature (12/20) — The park features "lots of native trees" and "lovely gardens" dividing sites, with "beautiful scenery" noted by long-term guests. A reserve at the back offers BBQ facilities and walking opportunities. However, it's described as being "in the middle of nowhere" on Main North Road with limited natural attractions nearby, serving more as a transit point than a nature-based destination.

Cleanliness (11/15) — Reviews are divided. Many praise "immaculate" amenities "cleaned twice a day" and "spotless" facilities. However, recent reviews cite "black mould on amenities ceilings," "outdated" facilities, and inconsistent cleaning standards in cabins. Multiple guests noted amenities are "dated but extremely clean," suggesting maintenance issues despite cleaning efforts.

Value (13/15) — Strong performer with consistent praise for "$38 a night for a powered sight" and "extremely well priced." Guests appreciated staying at "normal park prices" compared to Adelaide's higher costs. However, some felt even budget pricing wasn't justified given facility quality and safety concerns, with one noting they "should have paid the $20 extra elsewhere."

Site Size (8/10) — Positive feedback on "spacious" sites "ideal for your annexe" and "great park for big rigs." A 9m van was accommodated easily. Sites are "divided by lovely gardens" providing some separation. However, some complaints about "tiny sites" and being placed near dump points and bins detracted from the experience.

Sentiment (6/10) — Highly polarized reviews with passionate advocates and deeply dissatisfied guests. Safety concerns appear repeatedly: "didn't feel safe," "not a nice or safe place to stay," and disturbing incidents with room security. While many praised friendly staff and would return, the mix of long-term residents, controversial CCTV in bathrooms, and management issues significantly impacted family comfort levels.

Location (8/10) — Good access to Adelaide with "perfect location to explore Adelaide" and proximity to shopping at Munno Para and Gawler within 5 minutes drive. Close to train connections. However, "nothing in walking distance," located on a busy main road with noise from "drag races," and described as somewhat isolated for a tourist park.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Safety and security concerns dominate recent reviews, with families reporting feeling unsafe, incidents of inappropriate late-night check-ins (2:30-3am), disruptive long-term residents creating disturbances, and controversial CCTV placement in bathrooms that made guests uncomfortable.
2. Minimal on-site entertainment and activities for children beyond basic playground and pool facilities - no kids clubs, organized activities, jumping pillow, games room, or the structured entertainment programs that families expect from holiday parks.
3. Inconsistent cleanliness standards and maintenance issues including black mould, outdated facilities, inadequate cleaning of cabins, no air-conditioning turned on during extreme heat, and poor TV reception, plus concerning pet-unfriendly ground conditions (sharp rocks) despite pet-friendly advertising.
