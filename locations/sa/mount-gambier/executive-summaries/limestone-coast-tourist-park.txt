PARK: Limestone Coast Tourist Park
SCORE: 56/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-15
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 76

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (8/20) — One positive family review mentioned "a fantastic kids' play area — my kids absolutely loved it," but a critical review raised serious safety concerns stating "the playground has a very nasty fall hazard not many meters away from it. Really needs a fence around it." The games room has table tennis, air hockey and billiards, though one family reported negative interaction with management over pool balls. No waterpark, jumping pillow, or structured kids' activities mentioned. One review noted "Receptionist was friendly with kids providing the soccer balls."

Nature (12/20) — Park offers elevated views with "great sunsets looking over Mt Gambier" and "beautiful gums" on the property. Location provides access to natural attractions with reviews noting it's "close to all sinkholes n markets" and "Only 2ks from Umpherston sink hole." However, one review warned "if the wind is south west the pong from the Jubilee sawmill is really off" and another mentioned "the wail from the Jubilee Christian Centre next door will wake you." Limited natural environment on-site beyond mature trees.

Cleanliness (10/15) — Reviews show highly inconsistent cleanliness standards. Recent positive reviews praised "very clean solid rooms," "spotless" cabins, and "exceptionally clean rooms." However, multiple concerning reviews reported "dog hair on floor, couch and tiles" with a 9-month-old unable to play on the floor, "ensuite but it looked like it hadn't been cleaned in years," "mould coming up in places in the room," "bbq discusting very very dirty," and "dirty bed linen." Quality appears to depend heavily on which cabin is allocated.

Value (12/15) — Strong value perception in recent reviews with "Excellent value for money," "very well priced," and "Great prices for a powered site with a private ensuite!" Pricing noted at "$40/night for power, water, ensuite" for caravan sites. However, one family paid "$200 a night" during peak period and felt it poor value given condition. Multiple reviews appreciated the affordability, though one noted "Very cheap accomodation and I can see why." Renovated units offer better value than older cabins.

Site Size (7/10) — Caravan sites received mixed feedback with "Our van site was not flat but on a slight decline" and "uneven site." Cabins described as "quite large" with "separate kitchen and entry area" and "spacious" rooms. Limited specific detail on site dimensions for families with large rigs. Only "10 powered sites" available according to one review, suggesting limited space for caravans.

Sentiment (4/10) — Extremely polarised reviews create concerning sentiment picture for families. While many recent reviews show positive experiences with repeat visitation intent, several highly negative reviews from 2023-2025 describe serious incidents including alleged racist comments, safety hazards, aggressive management behavior toward children, and families leaving early. One review stated "This place is certainly not suited for children!" The inconsistency in experience creates significant risk for family travellers.

Location (3/10) — Location is "just on the outskirts of the city," "A little out of city centre but quiet," and "maybe 7-8 minutes to town." Access to Mt Gambier attractions within reasonable distance. However, positioning on main road with industrial neighbours (sawmill) and church noise issues detract from appeal. Not a destination location itself, primarily a stopover base for exploring the region.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Critical safety concerns for children including playground fall hazards, steep unsteady roads, and one extreme review stating a child was injured on the embankment with owners responding inappropriately — fundamentally unsuitable for young families in current condition.
2. Wildly inconsistent cabin quality and cleanliness creates unacceptable risk, with families reporting everything from "spotless" to dog hair-covered floors unsuitable for crawling babies, mould, dirty linen, and poorly maintained facilities depending on which cabin allocated.
3. Multiple serious complaints about management behaviour including alleged racist comments, aggressive interactions with families over children playing in games room, and dismissive responses to legitimate concerns — creating an unwelcoming and potentially hostile environment for some family guests.
