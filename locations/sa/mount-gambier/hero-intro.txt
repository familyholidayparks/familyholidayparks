# Mount Gambier: A Family-Friendly Escape

Mount Gambier is a relaxed regional town on South Australia's Limestone Coast that hits the mark for families wanting a proper getaway without the fuss. The main drawcard is the stunning Blue Lake, a volcanic crater lake that dominates the landscape with brilliant blue water perfect for swimming, picnicking, and walking around the rim. Beyond the lake, you've got caves to explore (the Engelbrecht Cave and Lady Nelson Caves are absolute crackers for kids), limestone sinkholes to investigate, and easy access to nearby beaches at Port MacDonnell and Port Campbell way. There's plenty of room to spread out here, and the pace is refreshingly slow.

For families, the caravan parks make it dead easy to settle in and enjoy your time. Blue Lake Holiday Park puts you right on the lake itself, while options like Pine Country Caravan Park, Limestone Coast Tourist Park, Mount Gambier Central Caravan Park, and Kalganyi Holiday Park offer good facilities and reasonable rates depending on what suits your lot. The town has solid family attractions without being overdone—there's the Mount Gambier Adventure Park, local pubs with decent feeds, and plenty of natural attractions that won't cost you a fortune. Kids get to burn energy exploring caves and swimming, while parents actually get a chance to relax.
