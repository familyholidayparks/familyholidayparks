Mount Gambier and the Limestone Coast are an absolute ripper for families who love exploring caves, swimming in impossibly blue sinkholes, and climbing dormant volcanoes – all without the usual tourist crowds you'll find up the coast. We reckon late spring through autumn (October to April) is your sweet spot for visiting, when the weather's perfect for the Blue Lake at its most vibrant and the sinkholes are warm enough for a proper swim. Here's a local tip that'll save you a few dollars: grab your free Parks Pass from the Lady Nelson Visitor Centre before you head out, as it covers entry to Umpherston Sinkhole and several other attractions that some tourists end up paying for separately.
