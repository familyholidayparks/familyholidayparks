# Victor Harbor, South Australia

Victor Harbor is a genuine coastal gem that ticks all the boxes for a family getaway. Located on the Fleurieu Peninsula about ninety minutes south of Adelaide, this working fishing town offers brilliant beaches, natural attractions and a relaxed pace that suits families perfectly. The main beach is patrolled by lifeguards during summer, and the sheltered waters make it safe for younger kids to splash about. The town's foreshore is easy to navigate with prams, and there's plenty of free parking, so you're not wrestling with logistics before you've even had a coffee.

The real drawcard for families is how much there is to do without breaking the bank. The penguin colony at Granite Island is a highlight—accessible via a causeway or scenic tram ride—and spotting the little penguins return to shore at dusk is genuinely magical for kids. You've got the South Australian Whale Centre, rock pools to explore, and beautiful walking trails through natural bushland where you might spot koalas or kookaburras. The NRMA Victor Harbor Beachfront Holiday Park puts you right on the sand with full facilities and a playground, making it ideal for families who want convenience without fuss. It's the kind of place where kids burn energy outdoors and parents actually get to relax.
