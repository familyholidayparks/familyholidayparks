PARK: Camp Nyroca
SCORE: 62/100
DATE SCORED: 2024-01-15
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 21

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (12/20) — Reviews mention games room, tree swings throughout the camp, walking trails, and resident chickens that kids enjoy feeding. One recent review specifically noted "tree swings everywhere" and "great place for kids with bikes," but the games room was described as "very old" by one visitor. No structured activities or programs mentioned.

Nature (18/20) — Consistently praised for beautiful natural setting "amongst beautiful gums," alongside a creek, with walking tracks throughout the property. Multiple reviews highlight the peaceful bush environment, one noting "serenity of the bush and birds." Easy access to Greenly Beach and Farm Beach mentioned. Fire pits available outside summer months.

Cleanliness (10/15) — Highly inconsistent between reviews. Older reviews (2023-2024) consistently praise cleanliness with phrases like "super clean," "very clean," and "clean tidy well kept grounds." However, most recent 2026 reviews reveal serious concerns: "no cleaning of toilets for our remaining 2 days," "all rubbish bins overflowing, bbq filthy," and "bit grubby." Hot showers consistently praised when facilities are maintained.

Value (13/15) — Excellent value noted across reviews: "seriously cheap," "around 20 bucks for a night," "same price as a NP" (National Park). Powered sites approximately $35/night. Multiple reviewers emphasize the affordability combined with decent facilities represents strong value for budget-conscious families.

Site Size (9/10) — Sites described as "generously spaced out," "plenty space," "loads of space," and "large sites." Multiple reviews note spaciousness and lack of crowding. Good for tents with grass sites mentioned as alternative to gravel.

Sentiment (7/10) — Mixed recent sentiment. While many older reviews show strong loyalty ("we will keep coming back," "will be back for certain"), recent 2026 reviews include disappointing experiences with two groups leaving early. Overall positive for nature lovers and value seekers, but recent service issues impact recommendation rate for families expecting consistent standards.

Location (7/10) — Remote Eyre Peninsula location described as "middle of nowhere" and "out of town." Approximately 30 minutes drive from Coffin Bay, short drive to Farm Beach and Greenly Beach. One review notes it's "beside the B100" road. "Away from shops and beach" noted as downside. Peaceful and quiet but requires self-sufficiency. Telstra mobile coverage confirmed, Optus doesn't work.

WHY NOT 100:
[1] Recent 2026 reviews reveal significant decline in caretaker engagement and facility maintenance, with specific complaints about no toilet cleaning for multiple days, overflowing bins, filthy BBQs, and absent management communication during 5-day stays.
[2] Minimal structured kids entertainment beyond basic amenities like old games room and natural features - no waterplay, organized activities, or dedicated playground equipment that modern family parks typically offer.
[3] Remote location away from shops and beaches requires significant self-sufficiency and travel time to attractions, limiting convenience for families with young children who need regular access to amenities.
