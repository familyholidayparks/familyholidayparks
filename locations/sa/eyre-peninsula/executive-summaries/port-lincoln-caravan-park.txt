PARK: Port Lincoln Caravan Park
SCORE: 72/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-15
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 368

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (12/20) — Limited structured kids entertainment with small playground and games room mentioned; main activities are self-directed including jetty fishing, swimming pool, beach access, and coastal walking trails (Parnkalla Track). No waterpark or organized kids programs noted.

Nature (18/20) — Exceptional natural setting on Boston Bay waterfront with direct beach and jetty access, stunning ocean views from terraced sites, coastal walking trails, opportunities for squid and King George whiting fishing, and regular wildlife sightings including dolphins, seals, and pelicans.

Cleanliness (12/15) — Mixed feedback: amenities consistently described as "very clean" and "spotlessly clean" with daily servicing and "amazing water pressure," but facilities are "dated," "old," "tired," and "in need of upgrade" with some reporting broken tiles, peeling paint, and worn fixtures in older blocks.

Value (12/15) — Generally considered reasonable with prices ranging $27-$55 per night depending on season and site type, though some reviewers felt pricing didn't match dated facilities; positive comments about "great prices" and "more than reasonable rates" balanced against concerns about value during peak season.

Site Size (9/10) — Consistently praised for "huge sites," "massive caravan sites," "generous sized sites," and "well spaced" with drive-through options and privacy from trees; however unpowered sites are steep and unlevel making them problematic for tents and caravans without leveling equipment.

Sentiment (8/10) — Generally positive with many repeat visitors and recommendations, particularly for the location and views; however concerns about noise from tradies (3am starts), security issues (bike thefts, break-ins), antisocial behavior in car park, and poor reception service detract from overall satisfaction.

Location (9/10) — Excellent waterfront position with ocean views, 10km (10 minutes) from Port Lincoln town center, adjacent to service station for convenience, access to Parnkalla walking trail, own jetty and beach, though some note it's too far to walk into town comfortably and highway noise is present.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Amenities infrastructure is significantly aged and dated (described as "1980s," "old," "tired") with urgent need for renovation in multiple toilet blocks, despite being kept clean by hardworking staff.
2. Serious safety and security concerns including caravan break-ins, stolen bikes ($15,000 worth), antisocial behavior with burnouts, inadequate gate security, and early morning noise from permanent resident tradies leaving for work from 3am.
3. Unpowered sites are poorly designed on steep slopes without proper terracing or marked bays, making them unusable for many guests with tents or caravans requiring level ground, and creating a "free for all" parking situation.
