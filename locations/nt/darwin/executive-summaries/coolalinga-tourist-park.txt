PARK: Coolalinga Tourist Park
SCORE: 52/100
DATE SCORED: 2025
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 61

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (5/20) — No dedicated kids club, playground, or organized activities mentioned. One family noted "our kids had a great time exploring the park" but no structured entertainment facilities. Reviews consistently note this is not a "Big 4 sort of place" and lacks entertainment compared to resort-style parks.

Nature (10/20) — Located on Stuart Highway with "fair amount of road noise." Positive mentions of "shady trees," "great canopy of trees over" pool area, and "lots of good shady spots." However, some areas described as "not very shady" and "exposed." No beach, creek, or significant natural features. Described as "nothing fancy" grounds that are "neat and tidy."

Cleanliness (10/15) — Strong consistent praise: "really clean," "amenities always clean," "clean and well cared for," "amenities are cleaned daily." Multiple reviews emphasize old facilities but very clean maintenance. Some concerns about pool leaves and green ant infestation addressed quickly by management.

Value (11/15) — Mixed feedback. Described as "cheapest campsite we found," "best value in Darwin," "great spot at a great price." However, recent reviews note "a bit pricey," "most expensive place we have stayed yet," and "not dirt cheap." Generally considered good value for Darwin standards despite dated facilities.

Site Size (7/10) — "Plenty of room," "larger sites," "good sized plots," and "spacious" mentioned positively. However, concerns about "very small sites for RVs," sites "quite small and not really well defined," and neighbors placed close together. Variable depending on which section of park.

Sentiment (6/10) — Polarized reviews with passionate positive and negative extremes. Multiple guests extended stays unexpectedly. However, serious management concerns cited including multiple evictions, lack of compassion, and communication issues. Recent 2025-2026 reviews show more negative management sentiment than older reviews.

Location (8/10) — Excellent location for practical access: "walking distance to shops," adjacent to Coles, Woolworths, Kmart. "25 mins south of Darwin CBD," "perfect mid way point to everything." Cinema 5 minutes away. However, requires crossing highway and noted as "a bit far out" for some visitors.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Complete absence of on-site children's entertainment, playground equipment, or organized family activities that are essential for young families
2. Facilities desperately dated from 1970s era with "very old facilities out of a mine camp," shower cubicles too small, short timer lights, and broken pool furniture requiring urgent upgrades
3. Significant management concerns in recent 2025 reviews including multiple guest evictions, poor communication, lack of compassion, and confrontational caretaker behavior that creates an unwelcoming atmosphere for families
