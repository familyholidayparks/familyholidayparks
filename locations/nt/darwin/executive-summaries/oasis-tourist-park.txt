PARK: Oasis Tourist Park
SCORE: 62/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-13
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 63

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (8/20) — Reviews consistently note "not a lot to do for kids" and "not many children," with one 2023 review explicitly stating the park caters to "mostly backpackers & grey nomads in the park, not a lot to do for kids." No mention of playground equipment, kids club, or organized family activities. One review mentions "entertainment nights" under previous management but no recent evidence of ongoing family entertainment.

Nature (16/20) — Strong tropical setting with "lots of trees," "beautifully maintained" grounds, "lovely shady" sites, and "lush with well-maintained plants and grass." Multiple reviews praise the "tropical look" and "leafy park" atmosphere. Close to natural attractions including Litchfield National Park, wetland sites like McMinns Lagoon and Corroboree Billabong, and Territory Wildlife Park within 20 minutes.

Cleanliness (10/15) — Mixed and declining evidence. Older reviews (2023-2024) praise "spotless amenities" and "extremely clean and well-maintained" facilities. However, multiple recent 2025 reviews cite serious concerns: "amenities are abysmal," "after 8 days the same crap was still in the plug holes," "toilets and kitchens could use a more thorough cleaning," and "cabins full of cockroaches and ants." Clear deterioration under new management.

Value (10/15) — Limited direct value commentary. One 2025 review states "this park is not worth the money" and another mentions "great prices" in 2023. One reviewer notes when "you pay so much for a cabin" expectations aren't met. No specific pricing mentioned but value perception appears average to below-average for families given limited entertainment options and variable cleanliness.

Site Size (5/10) — Significant concerns across multiple reviews: "sites a little small," "parking is a bit tight," "sites are small and everyone is crowded on top of each other," "our site was 5 metres by 4 metres," "not enough space for a camper trailer, awning and vehicle - not even close," and "very narrow little roadway and bays can be a tight squeeze." Some positive mentions of "big enough" sites and accommodating larger vans, but overall evidence shows constrained spacing.

Sentiment (7/10) — Highly polarized with temporal decline. Strong loyalty under previous management (Ben/Di) with multiple long-term stays and repeat visitors. However, recent 2025-2026 reviews show dramatic sentiment shift: multiple 1-star reviews citing "rude" staff, "standard has slipped considerably," "not as good as it once was," with guests stating they "will no longer be recommending this park" and leaving early. Management transition has clearly impacted family satisfaction.

Location (6/10) — Located 20km from Darwin city centre, described as "out of Darwin's city centre" but with "shopping centres close by" (6 centres within 5 minutes). Good access to natural attractions (Litchfield, wetlands) without city driving, but "you can't really walk anywhere from here" and "very noisy from the highway being so close." Functional but not ideal for families wanting walkable amenities or beach proximity.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Complete absence of children's entertainment, playground facilities, or organized kids activities makes this unsuitable for families seeking engagement for young children, as explicitly noted by multiple reviewers.
2. Significant decline in cleanliness standards and customer service quality under new 2025-2026 management, with multiple recent reports of dirty amenities, rude staff, and pest problems in cabins that weren't issues under previous ownership.
3. Cramped site sizes (as small as 5m x 4m) with tight parking and crowding create stress for families with caravans, particularly when combined with poor lighting placement and limited space for awnings and family setup.
