Darwin's holiday parks are an absolute ripper for families who want to experience the Top End's unique wet-dry season rhythms – think croc-spotting at jumping tours, swimming in waterfalls, and teaching the kids about ancient Aboriginal rock art all within easy day trips. The sweet spot for visiting is definitely the Dry season between May and September when humidity drops off and you can actually sleep at night without the aircon cranking (trust me on this one!). Here's a local tip that'll save you grief: always book your park accommodation well before June because Grey Nomads flood up the Stuart Highway after Easter and snap up the best sites faster than a barra takes a lure. Darwin's holiday parks give you that perfect base to explore Litchfield and Kakadu without the eye-watering resort prices, and the kids will love cooling off in the park pools after hot days of adventure.
