# Visiting Alice Springs with the Family

Best time to visit is April to September when it's actually bearable – temperatures sit around 20-25°C instead of the ridiculous 40+ degrees you'll cop in summer. School holidays work fine, but the town gets busy mid-July, so book accommodation early. Avoid October to March unless you're dead set on it; the heat is brutal and makes doing anything with kids a proper mission. Water and sunscreen aren't optional here – they're survival gear. The sun is no joke in the centre, so slap it on thick and reapply constantly, especially after swimming.

The MacDonnell Ranges hikes are brilliant for families, but start early and bring heaps of water. Emily Gap and Jesse Gap are perfect because they're short enough that even little legs won't complain, and there's usually a waterhole for a quick cool-off. The Reptile Centre is worth an hour, kids go mental for it, and the Alice Springs Desert Park gives you a real sense of what lives out here. Skip the crowded town centre and grab lunch from one of the takeaway spots – honestly, the locals' recommendations will serve you better than any tourist trap. Watch out for drop bears (nah, just kidding), but do keep an eye out for snakes and spiders in warmer months; they're more scared of you, but still check your shoes.

Here's the insider tip: hire a 4WD if you can swing it and head to the West MacDonnell Ranges early morning – fewer tourists, cooler temps, and you'll actually enjoy it. The locals know the best swimming holes, so ask around at your accommodation. Don't underestimate how far things are out here; distances are deceiving. Bring entertainment for long drives, respect Aboriginal sacred sites, and seriously, fill up your fuel tank before heading anywhere remote. Alice Springs isn't fancy, but it's genuine, and families who come prepared absolutely love it.
