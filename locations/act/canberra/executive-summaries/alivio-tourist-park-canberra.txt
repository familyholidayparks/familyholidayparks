PARK: Alivio Tourist Park Canberra
SCORE: 62/100
DATE SCORED: 2026-05-25
REVIEWS ANALYSED: 376

WHY THIS SCORE:
Entertainment (15/20) — Reviews consistently praise the "resort-style pool", "water play area", "splash park", basketball courts, tennis courts, volleyball courts, foosball, ping pong tables and multiple playgrounds. Many families note "so much for the kids to do", "outstanding facilities for the kids", and "kids absolutely loved the face painting each afternoon". The recreational offerings are described as "next level" and a major drawcard, though some note the splash park was occasionally out of order and the kids' games room needs updating.

Nature (16/20) — The park is "nestled in the bush", "surrounded by lovely bush land", with "walking/biking trails", "mountain bike tracks everywhere", and direct access to Bruce Reserve and Black Mountain walking tracks. Guests regularly report seeing "kangaroos", "Rosellas", "cockatoos" and native wildlife around the cabins. Reviews describe it as "middle of a jungle, surrounded by trees, with birds sounds" yet still close to the city, providing a "peaceful atmosphere and the feeling of being close to nature".

Cleanliness (8/15) — This is the park's most significant weakness with widespread reports of poor cleaning standards. Multiple reviews describe cabins as "absolutely filthy", "dirty and I mean, unclean dirty", with "hair all over", "mould in the shower", "cockroaches", "dust everywhere", "food scraps left", "blood stains on sheets", "sticky floors", and "dirty footprints in the shower". Even positive reviews often note cleanliness issues, with many stating "the cleanliness has gone down the drain" and housekeeping is "a bit of a joke". Amenities blocks for caravans also received complaints about infrequent cleaning.

Value (9/15) — Reviews are mixed on value, with many describing it as "overpriced for what you get", particularly for older cabins at "$230 a night" or caravans at "$75-$105 per night". The buffet breakfast at $21 is described as "massively overpriced" and "basic", while restaurant meals are noted as "overpriced, low quality food". However, guests who stayed in newer villas or appreciated the extensive facilities felt it offered "good value" and was "cheaper than hotels". The washing/dryer at $6 each was frequently mentioned as expensive.

Site Size (6/10) — Reviews indicate "sites are tightly packed", "very close to our neighbours", and "getting to sites was a bit difficult as they are tightly packed". Drive-through sites are praised as convenient, but multiple guests noted "roads are narrow, making it difficult to maneuver a large rig", parking spots are "inadequate for even a medium sized car", and the "top corner needs a major overall as its so tight of corner that you scrub out your tyres". Cabin parking is described as "really small" and "tricky".

Sentiment (4/10) — Sentiment is highly polarized. While many guests say "we come back every year", "will definitely be back", and "this is now our canberra stop", there are equally strong negative reviews stating "WE WONT BE BACK", "will never stay here again", "worst family holiday ever", and "this will be our last time staying here". Recent reviews (2024-2026) show declining satisfaction with many long-term guests noting the park has "gone down hill fast compared to 1st visit" and "the cleanliness has gone down the drain".

Location (4/10) — The location receives mixed feedback. Positively, it's "10-15 mins from mint, zoo and Questacon", "close to all Canberras attractions", and "super close to GIO stadium". However, it's described as "a little in no mans land", "very out of the way", "not accessible by public transport", and "location was abit far from everything by foot". Some note it takes "around 40 minutes to get back onto Monaro highway" for travelers heading south.

WHY NOT 100:
1. Severe and widespread cleanliness issues across cabins and amenities, with multiple detailed reports of filthy accommodation, inadequate housekeeping, and declining standards that consistently disappoint guests despite the park's premium pricing.

2. Inconsistent service quality and responsiveness, with numerous reports of staff being unwelcoming, maintenance requests being ignored, checkout issues, rooms not ready at check-in time, missing basic supplies (soap, coffee pods, toilet paper), and management failing to respond to complaints.

3. Poor value proposition for older accommodation types, with guests feeling the pricing ($230+ per night for older cabins, $75+ for caravan sites) doesn't match the quality delivered, particularly given the dated facilities, small cabin sizes, and additional charges for basics like breakfast, laundry ($12 per load), and even cot setup.
