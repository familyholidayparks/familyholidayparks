# Family Holiday Parks Scoring Rubric

All review and park scoring uses seven categories worth 100 points total.

The AI or reviewer evidence may assess category quality, but code must validate
category scores and calculate the final total.

## Entertainment / 20

Measures whether children and families had enough to do at the park.

Relevant evidence includes pools, splash parks, playgrounds, jumping pillows,
kids clubs, games rooms, bike/scooter space, organised activities and whether
those facilities suited the ages travelling.

Do not score nearby town attractions here unless the park experience directly
depends on them.

## Nature / 20

Measures the setting and natural appeal of the stay.

Relevant evidence includes beach, river, bush, lake, creek, shade, wildlife,
views, green space, walking paths and whether the setting improved the holiday.

Bad weather should not lower this score unless the park's controllable setting
or infrastructure made conditions worse.

## Value / 15

Measures whether the stay felt worth what the family paid.

Relevant evidence includes approximate price, inclusions, hidden costs, facility
quality for the price, and comparison with similar parks.

Do not punish a park only because a family had a generally expensive trip.

## Cleanliness / 15

Measures cleanliness and maintenance of amenities, accommodation, grounds and
shared facilities.

Relevant evidence includes bathrooms, camp kitchens, pools, cabins, bins,
laundry, paths, equipment condition and maintenance response.

## Site Size / 10

Measures the family's site, cabin or accommodation comfort.

Relevant evidence includes space, privacy, level ground, access, parking,
neighbour proximity, manoeuvring and cabin layout.

If the question is not applicable, use confidence warnings and score only from
available accommodation evidence.

## Sentiment / 10

Measures the family's overall feeling about the park-controlled experience.

Relevant evidence includes whether they would stay again, recommend it to a
similar family, emotional tone, strongest praise and biggest disappointment.

Separate the whole holiday mood from the park score when weather, travel fatigue
or unrelated family circumstances had the biggest impact.

## Location / 10

Measures whether the park location suited the holiday.

Relevant evidence includes convenience to beaches, attractions, shops, town,
walkability, road noise, access difficulty and whether the location matched the
trip purpose.

Do not score the park down because the family chose a destination that was wrong
for their plans unless the park listing or positioning caused that mismatch.
