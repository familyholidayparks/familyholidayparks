# Family Holiday Report

## Purpose

The Family Holiday Report turns a submitted review into something useful, personal and shareable.

It should feel like a family keepsake while still carrying trusted scoring information.

## Core hierarchy

1. Family Holiday Report
2. Family name
3. Park name and location
4. Family Score /100
5. Family composition
6. Short stay summary
7. Pull quote
8. Stay date
9. Family Holiday Parks branding

## Card variants

### Photo card

Best for families who upload a strong image.

### Stick-figure card

Best for personality and shareability.

Inputs may include:

- number of travellers
- ages
- relationships
- genders
- key review moment
- park setting
- one or two recognisable props

### Editorial card

Best for a more polished, magazine-like summary.

## Content rules

- Do not repeat the family name unnecessarily.
- Do not repeat the location in multiple chips and headings.
- Keep the score visually dominant.
- Use one strong quote rather than several weak excerpts.
- Keep summaries short.
- Do not expose private details.
- Make clear that the score belongs to this family's stay.

Suggested label:

> The McCarthy Family scored their stay 84/100

This avoids confusing it with the park's overall platform score.

## Manual-first image workflow

1. Create a controlled master prompt.
2. Generate a small test set.
3. Review style consistency.
4. Refine family representation.
5. Test common compositions.
6. Approve a house style.
7. Only then investigate automation.

## Accessibility

- Maintain strong text contrast.
- Do not rely only on colour.
- Provide alt text for published cards.
- Ensure the score and key text remain readable on mobile.
