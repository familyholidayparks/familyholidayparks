Frequently Asked Questions

What's the best time to visit Gold Coast Queensland with kids?

G'day! The sweet spot is April-May or September-October - you'll dodge the summer crowds and school holiday prices while enjoying beautiful weather. The water's still warm enough for swimming, and theme parks are less packed. Winter (June-August) is lovely too, with mild days perfect for exploring, though you might need a wetsuit for the ocean. Just avoid January if you can - it's stinger season and absolutely heaving with tourists!

Are there pet-friendly holiday parks in Gold Coast Queensland?

Too right there are! Many Gold Coast holiday parks welcome your furry mates, though policies vary. Most pet-friendly parks require dogs to be leashed, up-to-date with vaccinations, and some charge a small fee (usually $10-20 per night). Popular spots include BIG4 parks and some NRMA locations. Always book ahead and let them know you're bringing pets - some have designated pet-friendly cabins. Your pooch will love the beach walks nearby!

How much do holiday parks cost in Gold Coast Queensland during school holidays?

School holidays will hit your wallet harder, no worries about that! Expect to pay $150-400 per night for a family cabin depending on location and amenities. Powered sites for caravans run $60-120. Easter and Christmas holidays are priciest. Book 6-12 months ahead for better rates and availability. Off-peak (term time) can be 30-50% cheaper. Many parks offer longer-stay discounts, so a week-long booking often works out better value!

What should I pack for a holiday park stay with young kids?

Pack swimmers, sun protection (hat, rashies, SPF50+), and beach toys - essentials for Goldie life! Bring your own linen if required, basic toiletries, and insect repellent. Don't forget kids' comfort items like favourite teddies. Most cabins have kitchen basics, but pack any special utensils. Bikes or scooters are brilliant for getting around the park. A first-aid kit, nightlight, and portable high chair are lifesavers. Oh, and baby monitors work a treat!

What is there to do near Gold Coast Queensland holiday parks for families?

You're spoiled for choice, mate! Theme parks (Dreamworld, Sea World, Movie World) are the big drawcards - about 20-40 minutes from most holiday parks. Stunning beaches are everywhere for swimming and sandcastles. Try Currumbin Wildlife Sanctuary for koala cuddles, or explore the hinterland's rainforest walks and waterfalls. Many parks have pools, playgrounds, and jumping pillows on-site. The Broadwater's perfect for calm-water paddling with littlies too!
