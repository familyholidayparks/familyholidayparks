Local Knowledge

G'day! The Gold Coast hinterland and coastline offer families an incredible mix of theme parks, pristine beaches, and rainforest adventures all within a 30-minute drive of each other. We've found April to October is the sweet spot for visiting – you'll dodge the summer humidity and school holiday price hikes while still enjoying perfect beach weather. Here's a local tip that's saved us countless times: if you're planning theme park days, book your holiday park accommodation in Oxenford or Coomera rather than right on the coast – you'll pay less, avoid the tourist traffic, and you're actually closer to Dreamworld, Movie World, and Wet'n'Wild.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the best time to visit Gold Coast Queensland with kids?

G'day! The best time to visit the Gold Coast with little ones is during autumn (March-May) or spring (September-November). You'll get beautiful weather without the summer crowds and humidity. School holidays can be hectic, so if you can swing it, visit outside those times for better rates and quieter parks. Winter's still lovely too – mild days perfect for theme parks and beach walks!

Are there pet friendly holiday parks in Gold Coast Queensland?

Absolutely! Many Gold Coast holiday parks welcome your furry family members. Popular pet-friendly options include NRMA Treasure Island Holiday Park and BIG4 parks in the region. Just remember to book pet-friendly cabins in advance as they're super popular, and there's usually a small additional fee. Most parks require pets to be leashed and have designated pet areas. Always check specific pet policies when booking!

How much do holiday parks cost in Gold Coast Queensland during school holidays?

During school holidays, expect to pay between $150-$400 per night depending on accommodation type. Powered sites start around $60-$100, while family cabins range from $150-$400+. Easter and summer holidays are priciest. Pro tip: book 6-12 months ahead for better rates and availability. Off-peak visits can save you 30-50%! Many parks offer early bird discounts too, so definitely shop around.

What should I pack for a holiday park stay with young kids?

Pack the essentials, mate! Bring swimmers, sunscreen (SPF50+), hats, insect repellent, and beach towels. Most cabins have kitchens, so pack basic pantry items. Don't forget kids' favourite toys, bikes or scooters, and outdoor games. A portable high chair, night light, and first aid kit are lifesavers. Check if linen's included – some parks charge extra for bedding and towels.

What is there to do near Gold Coast Queensland holiday parks for families?

You're spoilt for choice! Theme parks like Dreamworld, Sea World, and Movie World are must-dos. Currumbin Wildlife Sanctuary is brilliant for animal encounters. The beaches are stunning – try Burleigh or Coolangatta for families. Most holiday parks have pools, playgrounds, and jumping pillows on-site. Don't miss the Broadwater for calm swimming, and explore nearby national parks for bushwalking adventures!
