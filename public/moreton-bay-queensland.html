The Moreton Bay Holiday Park Scene

Moreton Bay's caravan parks sit in that sweet spot where you're close enough to Brisbane for a quick getaway but far enough removed that you actually feel like you've left the city behind. The bay itself is the real drawcard—it's shallow, protected, and dotted with islands, which means the water's calm enough for families who don't want their kids getting tossed around by ocean swells. The three standout parks—Beachmere, Bells, and Toorbul—each have their own character. Beachmere's the most developed with proper facilities and grassy sites that appeal to families with caravans and motorhomes. Bells sits right on the water with riverfront appeal and a quieter vibe. Toorbul's the working-class option, less polished but solid for people who just want a cheap, functional base. Donnybrook rounds out the scene for those wanting something even more low-key. None of these are fancy resort-style parks with pools and entertainment programs—they're genuine caravan parks where the location does the heavy lifting.

What keeps people coming back isn't manicured gardens or themed activities; it's the access to the bay itself and the slow pace of the place. You can launch a tinny or kayak straight from most of these parks, crab off the jetties, or just wade in at high tide. The mangroves and saltmarsh give you that feeling of being somewhere wild without actually being remote. Families with young kids love it because there's no dangerous surf, the beaches are patchy but swimmable, and there's enough natural stuff to explore—mudflats, birdlife, the occasional stingray if you're paying attention. School holidays absolutely pack these parks out, especially around Christmas and winter break when Brisbane families want something achievable in a couple of hours' drive. The repeat visitors are mostly caravanners and motorhome owners who've worked out that Moreton Bay offers genuine waterfront living at reasonable rates, and they book the same site year after year.

The loyalty people have to these parks comes down to knowing what you're getting. You're not paying for luxury; you're paying for a reliable spot where you can pull the van in, walk fifty meters to the water, and spend a week doing very little. The bay's protected nature means older caravanners feel comfortable here, families aren't stressed about their kids getting into trouble, and motorhome travellers appreciate the straightforward facilities without the resort markup. The biggest booking crunch is definitely school holidays and public holiday weekends, but outside those periods you can usually find a site. It's the kind of place where people end up staying longer than they planned because the pace suits them, the fishing's decent enough to keep you occupied, and there's just no reason to rush back to the city. That's what the Moreton Bay parks offer—predictability, water access, and enough quiet to actually feel like a break.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the best family holiday parks in Moreton Bay, QLD?
Beachmere, Bells, Toorbul, and Donnybrook caravan parks all offer family-friendly facilities in Moreton Bay. Each provides different advantages—Beachmere and Bells are beachside locations, while Toorbul and Donnybrook offer quieter settings with good amenities. Compare powered sites, cabin options, and on-site facilities to find which suits your family's needs and budget best.
Which caravan park in Moreton Bay is best for kids?
Beachmere Caravan Park stands out for families with direct beach access and shallow swimming areas. Bells Caravan Park also offers excellent kid-friendly facilities. Both parks provide safe environments for children to play while parents enjoy nearby attractions like the Moreton Bay foreshore and local playgrounds.
Are there holiday parks with pools near Moreton Bay?
Several Moreton Bay caravan parks feature swimming pools as part of their amenities. Check individual park websites for current pool facilities, as offerings vary seasonally. Most parks also provide beach access within close proximity, offering multiple water activities for families during your stay.
Which holiday parks are nearest to the beach in Moreton Bay?
Beachmere Caravan Park and Bells Caravan Park are beachfront locations with direct beach access. Both parks position you steps away from Moreton Bay's sandy shores, ideal for families wanting easy beach days without driving. Perfect for morning swims and sunset walks along the waterfront.
Do Moreton Bay caravan parks have powered sites available?
All four parks—Beachmere, Bells, Toorbul, and Donnybrook—offer powered caravan sites with full hookups. Availability varies seasonally, especially during school holidays. Book in advance for peak periods to secure your preferred powered site length and location within each park.
What cabin accommodation options are available in Moreton Bay?
Beachmere, Bells, Toorbul, and Donnybrook caravan parks all offer cabin rentals ranging from basic to fully self-contained options. Cabins suit families without caravans or those wanting simpler setup. Compare amenities, bedroom configurations, and proximity to facilities when choosing between parks.
Are any Moreton Bay holiday parks pet friendly?
Most Moreton Bay caravan parks welcome pets, though policies and designated areas vary. Confirm pet policies directly with Beachmere, Bells, Toorbul, and Donnybrook before booking. Some parks charge additional fees or restrict pets to specific sites, so clarify all details when planning your family trip with animals.
What's the best value holiday park in Moreton Bay?
Toorbul and Donnybrook caravan parks typically offer competitive pricing compared to beachfront options. Compare nightly rates for cabins and powered sites across all four parks. Off-peak seasons provide better value, and longer stays often include discounts. Contact parks directly for current pricing.
What's the best area to stay in Moreton Bay for families?
Beachmere offers the most beach-focused experience for families, while Bells provides similar beachside convenience. For quieter settings with good facilities, Toorbul and Donnybrook are excellent alternatives. Consider your priorities: beach access, proximity to shops, or peaceful surroundings when selecting your base.
What are the best rainy day activities near Moreton Bay caravan parks?
Visit the Moreton Bay Discovery Centre for interactive marine exhibits, explore local shopping precincts in nearby towns, or check out indoor play centers in the region. Many parks have communal facilities and covered areas. The Bribie Island Lighthouse and local cafés provide sheltered entertainment options during wet weather.
