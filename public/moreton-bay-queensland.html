Frequently Asked Questions

Are there any kids festivals in Moreton Bay?
Moreton Bay hosts brilliant family festivals throughout the year, including the Redcliffe KiteFest and various community events at Scarborough and Clontarf. The parks like Beachmere Caravan Park and Bells Caravan Park make perfect bases for festival-goers. Check council websites closer to your dates, as many suburbs run school holiday programs and beachside markets with kids' entertainment that locals absolutely love.
What are the best kids activities in Moreton Bay?
The calm bay waters are perfect for teaching littlies to kayak, paddleboard or fish from the jetties near Toorbul Caravan Park. Families love crabbing at Donnybrook, exploring the rock pools at low tide, and cycling the foreshore paths. September to November offers ideal conditions before the stingers arrive. Many caravan parks organise kids' activities during school holidays too.
What kids events are on in Moreton Bay?
Local kids events vary seasonally but include beach carnivals, twilight markets with entertainment, and junior fishing competitions along the foreshore. Staying at spots like Beachmere Caravan Park or Bells Caravan Park puts families close to Scarborough and Redcliffe where most events happen. The Moreton Bay Regional Council's event calendar is worth checking before booking, especially during school holidays.
What are the best things to do in Redland Bay with kids?
Redland Bay's the gateway to stunning islands and offers safe swimming at Weinam Creek. Kids love feeding the pelicans, catching the ferry to North Stradbroke Island for a day trip, and fishing off the jetties. The Redland Showgrounds host regular family events, and nearby parks offer great playgrounds. It's quieter than the northern bay suburbs but equally gorgeous.
What should we do in Moreton Bay with kids?
The protected bay waters near Toorbul Caravan Park and Beachmere Caravan Park are brilliant for safe swimming and teaching kids to fish or crab. Visit between September and November for perfect conditions without stingers. Grab fresh seafood straight from the trawlers, explore the jetties, hire bikes to cruise the foreshores, or take a day trip to Bribie Island.
