Frequently Asked Questions

What's the best time to visit Whitsundays QLD with kids?
May to September is absolutely brilliant for families! You'll get gorgeous sunny days, calm seas perfect for little ones, and way fewer stingers in the water. School holidays (especially June-July) get pretty busy, so book ahead. April and October are ripper too - slightly warmer but less crowded. Just avoid January-March when it's stinking hot and jellyfish season's in full swing, mate!
Are there pet-friendly holiday parks in Whitsundays QLD?
Good news - several holiday parks around Airlie Beach and Cannonvale welcome your furry mates! Most have designated pet-friendly cabins or powered sites. You'll typically need to keep dogs on-lead and bring vaccination records. Always ring ahead to confirm pet policies and any extra fees. Some beaches nearby are dog-friendly too, so your four-legged family member won't miss out on the fun!
How much do holiday parks cost in Whitsundays QLD during school holidays?
Expect to pay around $150-$300 per night for cabins during school hols, while powered sites run $50-$80. Easter and Christmas holidays are priciest. Book at least 3-6 months ahead for school holiday periods! You'll save heaps visiting during term time - sometimes 30-40% less. Many parks offer stay-pay deals like 'stay 7, pay 5' outside peak times too.
What should I pack for a holiday park stay with young kids?
Essentials: swimmers, rashies, sunscreen (reef-safe), hats, and stinger suits if visiting October-May. Pack basics like sheets, towels, and toiletries - some parks provide them but not all. Bring outdoor toys, bikes, and beach gear. Don't forget insect repellent! Most cabins have full kitchens, so BYO food saves dollars. A portable fan's handy too for warm nights, mate!
What is there to do near Whitsundays QLD holiday parks for families?
Heaps! The lagoon at Airlie Beach is perfect for little tackers - free, patrolled, and stinger-free year-round. Take family-friendly cruises to Whitehaven Beach, visit the markets on weekends, explore Cedar Creek Falls, or check out the wildlife parks. Many holiday parks have pools, playgrounds, and jumping pillows to keep kids entertained without leaving the park. You'll never be bored!
