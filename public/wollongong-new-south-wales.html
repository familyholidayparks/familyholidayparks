Local Knowledge

G'day! The Wollongong region is an absolute cracker for families who want beaches, rainforest walks, and proper coastal adventures all within a stone's throw of each other. We love visiting between March and May when the summer crowds have thinned out but the ocean's still warm enough for the kids to splash about – plus you'll often score better rates at the holiday parks. Here's a local tip: grab fish and chips from Corrimal Beach on a Friday arvo and let the kids play at the adjacent playground while you watch the hang-gliders land on the headland – it's become our family tradition every time we're up this way.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the best accommodation in Wollongong for families with kids?

NRMA Shellharbour Beachside Holiday Park is a top pick with direct beach access and excellent facilities. Bulli Beach Tourist Park offers beautiful coastal views and easy access to rock pools. For something different, Lake Windemere Caravan Park provides calm lake swimming perfect for younger children. Book between March and May for better rates and smaller crowds.

What's the best caravan park in Wollongong?

NRMA Shellharbour Beachside Holiday Park consistently rates highest thanks to its beachfront location and family-friendly amenities. Corrimal Beach Tourist Park is another cracker choice, especially on Fridays when you can grab fish and chips from the local shop and enjoy the playground. Windang Beach Tourist Park offers fantastic fishing opportunities alongside beach access.

What's the best holiday park in NSW?

The Wollongong region punches well above its weight with NRMA Shellharbour Beachside Holiday Park leading the pack. Its combination of pristine beaches, proximity to rainforest walks, and quality facilities makes it stand out. The area offers that rare mix of coastal adventures and hinterland exploring, all without the tourist crowds of more northern destinations.

What's the best holiday park in NSW for kids?

NRMA Shellharbour Beachside Holiday Park is brilliant for families, offering safe swimming beaches and heaps of space for kids to roam. Bulli Beach Tourist Park puts you right near rock pools that little ones love exploring. Corrimal Beach Tourist Park has an excellent playground adjacent to the beach, making it easy to keep different age groups happy.

What are the best activities for kids in Wollongong?

The beaches are the main attraction – try rock pool exploring at Bulli or Corrimal. The Illawarra Fly Treetop Adventures offers zipline fun through rainforest canopy. Science Space museum keeps kids engaged with hands-on exhibits. Nan Tien Temple has beautiful gardens for wandering, and the Grand Pacific Drive offers stunning coastal views with plenty of stopping points.

Is there a kids museum in Wollongong?

Science Space is Wollongong's interactive science centre located at the university, offering hands-on exhibits that kids absolutely love. While not a traditional museum, it focuses on making science fun through experiments and displays. The Wollongong City Gallery also runs family-friendly programs. For broader museum experiences, the Illawarra Museum at the old harbour precinct explores local history.

Does Wollongong have a kids science museum?

Science Space at the University of Wollongong is the region's dedicated interactive science centre, perfect for curious minds. Kids can engage with hands-on experiments, planetarium shows, and rotating exhibits covering everything from physics to biology. It's particularly great for primary school-aged children who love getting stuck into practical activities rather than just looking at displays.
