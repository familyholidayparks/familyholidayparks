Local Knowledge

G'day! The Sunshine Coast is where we head when we want that perfect mix of patrolled beaches the kids can actually swim at, proper hinterland adventures up in the Glass House Mountains, and holiday parks where you're never more than 10 minutes from a decent coffee. We reckon April through October is your sweet spot – the stingers stay away, the humidity drops off, and you'll dodge those January school holiday crowds that pack out places like Mooloolaba and Noosa. Here's a local tip: if you're staying anywhere between Caloundra and Coolum, grab your fresh prawns and fish straight off the trawlers at Mooloolaba Wharf around 8am on weekday mornings – the kids love watching the boats come in, and you'll have the freshest seafood barbecue back at your park that night.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the best time to visit Sunshine Coast QLD with kids?

You can't go wrong visiting the Sunny Coast year-round, but April to May and September to November are absolute crackers! The weather's gorgeous, crowds are lighter, and accommodation's more affordable. School holidays (especially December-January) are buzzing but book early. Winter (June-August) is still lovely with mild temps around 20°C – perfect for little ones who overheat easily. Just pack a light jumper for evenings!

Are there pet-friendly holiday parks in Sunshine Coast QLD?

Good news – heaps of Sunshine Coast holiday parks welcome your furry family members! Many parks have designated pet-friendly cabins or powered sites where dogs are allowed. You'll usually need to keep them on-leash and bring their own bedding. Popular pet-friendly options include NRMA Holiday Parks and BIG4 parks. Always check specific pet policies when booking, as some have size or breed restrictions.

How much do holiday parks cost in Sunshine Coast QLD during school holidays?

School holiday rates vary heaps depending on accommodation type. Powered sites run $50-$90 per night, unpowered $40-$70. Cabins range from $150-$400+ per night during peak times. Easter and Christmas holidays are priciest. Book 6-12 months ahead for school holidays to snag better rates. Off-peak and shoulder seasons can be 30-50% cheaper – brilliant value for young families!

What should I pack for a holiday park stay with young kids?

Pack like you're heading to the beach and staying home! Essentials include swimmers, reef-safe sunscreen, hats, and rash vests. Bring basics like pillows, towels, and toiletries (most cabins have linen). Don't forget bikes, scooters, or outdoor toys. Include insect repellent, a first-aid kit, and favourite comfort items. Most parks have BBQs and playgrounds, so pack outdoor dining gear and let the kids run wild!

What is there to do near Sunshine Coast QLD holiday parks for families?

You'll be spoilt for choice! Hit stunning beaches like Mooloolaba and Noosa, explore Australia Zoo, or visit the Ginger Factory. Little ones love Aussie World theme park and Underwater World SEA LIFE Aquarium. Take scenic walks at Noosa National Park, hire bikes along the coastal paths, or paddle at Currumbin Creek. Most holiday parks have pools, playgrounds, and jumping pillows to keep kids entertained on-site too!
