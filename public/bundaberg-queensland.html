Frequently Asked Questions

What's the best caravan park in Bundaberg?
Burnett Heads Holiday Park takes top honours for families, with direct beach access and brilliant facilities. Moore Park Beach Holiday Park is another cracker choice if you're after a quieter vibe. For riverfront camping closer to town, AAOK Riverdale Caravan Park hits the sweet spot. All three offer powered sites, cabins, and those essential family-friendly amenities that make holiday life easy.
What's the best caravan park in Bundaberg QLD?
You can't go wrong with Burnett Heads Holiday Park for beachfront family fun, or Moore Park Beach Holiday Park if you prefer less crowds and pristine coastline. AAOK Riverdale Caravan Park offers lovely river views right near town, while Bundaberg East Cabin & Tourist Park keeps you close to shops and attractions. Each park's got its own charm depending on what matters most to your crew.
What's the best caravan park in Bundaberg right on the beach?
Burnett Heads Holiday Park puts you right on the sand with safe swimming and gorgeous coastal views – absolute paradise for families. Moore Park Beach Holiday Park is another beachfront beauty, offering more seclusion and those long stretches of uncrowded beach perfect for sandcastle marathons. Both parks deliver that genuine Aussie coastal experience where the kids can fall asleep to ocean sounds.
What are the best things to do in Bundaberg with kids?
The Mon Repos turtle experience tops every family's list – watching hatchlings scramble to the sea is pure magic. Bundle Beach offers safe swimming, while Mystery Craters provides quirky exploring. Don't miss the Bundaberg Rum Distillery tour (parents will thank you) and the Hinkler Hall of Aviation. The Botanic Gardens have brilliant playgrounds, and Alexandra Park Beach delivers calm waters perfect for littlies.
What kids activities are there in Bundaberg?
Beyond the famous Mon Repos turtles, kids love splashing at Riverside Aquatic Centre, exploring the interactive displays at Hinkler Hall of Aviation, and discovering the Mystery Craters. The Botanic Gardens offer free entry with lovely playgrounds and picnic spots. Snorkel the Great Barrier Reef's southern tip at Bargara, or let them burn energy at Alexandra Park Beach's playground right beside calm swimming waters.
What kids sports are available in Bundaberg?
Bundaberg offers plenty of active options for energetic kids. The Riverside Aquatic Centre has pools and water play areas perfect for burning energy. Most holiday parks feature tennis courts, beach volleyball, and open spaces for cricket or footy. Local beaches like Bargara and Moore Park are brilliant for boogie boarding, while calmer spots suit stand-up paddleboarding and kayaking for beginners.
Is there a kids train in Bundaberg?
The Bundaberg Botanic Gardens features a charming miniature railway that operates on Sundays, delighting younger kids with scenic rides through the gardens. It's a lovely affordable activity that pairs perfectly with a picnic. For proper train enthusiasts, check out the vintage rail displays around town, though the Botanic Gardens railway is your best bet for an actual ride the kids will remember.
What are the best things to do around Bundaberg with kids?
Venture beyond town to discover Bargara's rock pools and safe beaches, perfect for snorkelling beginners. Mon Repos Conservation Park delivers unforgettable turtle encounters from November to March. Moore Park Beach offers kilometres of uncrowded sand, while Gin Gin's Mystery Craters make a quirky day trip. The nearby Town of 1770 provides excellent fishing and calm waters for young swimmers exploring further north.
What can we do in Bundaberg with kids this weekend?
Hit Alexandra Park Beach for safe swimming and playground fun, then explore Bundaberg Botanic Gardens where entry's free and the kids can ride the miniature railway on Sundays. Check if Mon Repos has turtle sessions happening (book ahead). The Riverside Aquatic Centre keeps everyone cool, while Hinkler Hall of Aviation fascinates budding pilots. Finish with fish and chips at Bargara watching the sunset.
