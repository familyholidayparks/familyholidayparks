The Bundaberg Holiday Park Scene

Bundaberg's holiday park scene thrives on its unique position straddling coastal and riverside experiences, something you won't find in other Queensland destinations quite the same way. The town sits where the Burnett River meets the ocean, and the parks here are deliberately positioned to take advantage of both environments. Burnett Heads Holiday Park sits right at the river mouth with direct beach access, while AAOK Riverdale Caravan Park capitalises on the slower, more sheltered river life upstream. Moore Park Beach Holiday Park offers something different again—beachfront positioning with a quieter vibe than the main tourist beaches further south. This variety means different types of travellers get what they're after without compromise, whether that's anglers after the tidal flows, families wanting safe swimming, or grey nomads simply looking for a peaceful base with good facilities.

The parks draw a predictable but dedicated crowd: caravanners and motorhome owners make up the backbone of visitors, particularly during autumn and winter when they're escaping southern weather. School holidays pack these parks to capacity—you'll struggle to find a site during July and December if you haven't booked months ahead. But what's notable about Bundaberg is the volume of repeat visitors, particularly families who book the same park at the same time every year. They return because the parks offer genuine river and beach access without the chaos of the Gold Coast or Sunshine Coast, and the local area delivers activities beyond just caravan park lounging—the Mon Repos turtle rookery, the Bundaberg Rum Distillery, reasonable fishing opportunities, and low-key beach towns like Moore Park and Burnett Heads proper.

The physical infrastructure across these five parks ranges from basic to mid-range resort-style facilities, which suits the market. You've got powered sites with full hookups, self-contained cabins for families who don't own caravans, and bush-style unpowered sites for traditionalists. The parks themselves aren't flashy—they're well-maintained working parks rather than playground destinations—and that's exactly why people keep coming back. Families appreciate that kids can ride bikes between sites safely, that there's direct river or beach access for morning swims, and that you're not paying resort prices for basic amenities. For caravanners, the accessibility to water for fishing or simply launching a tinny makes these parks practical bases rather than just overnight stops. Bundaberg has carved out a niche as the thoughtful alternative to the more commercialised caravan destinations further up the coast.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the best family holiday parks in Bundaberg, QLD?
Bundaberg's top family parks include Burnett Heads Holiday Park with beachfront access, Moore Park Beach Holiday Park offering coastal views, AAOK Riverdale Caravan Park with river frontage, and Bundaberg East Cabin & Tourist Park in town. Each offers different advantages—choose based on whether you prefer beach proximity, river activities, or central location for exploring Bundaberg attractions.
Which caravan park in Bundaberg is best for kids?
Moore Park Beach Holiday Park stands out for families with a pool and beach access, ideal for children's activities. Burnett Heads Holiday Park also offers family-friendly facilities near the water. Both parks provide safe environments where kids can enjoy beach play, swimming, and outdoor recreation without extensive travel from your accommodation.
Are there holiday parks with pools near Bundaberg?
Yes, Moore Park Beach Holiday Park features a pool facility, perfect for families. Several parks also offer beach access as an alternative water activity. Check directly with parks about seasonal pool operations and whether heated pools are available during cooler months if you're visiting outside peak summer.
What holiday parks near the beach in Bundaberg would you recommend?
Burnett Heads Holiday Park and Moore Park Beach Holiday Park are your best beachfront options, both offering direct or very close beach access. These parks let families enjoy swimming, fishing, and beach walks without traveling distances. Ideal if beach time is central to your holiday plans with children.
Which Bundaberg parks have powered sites available?
All four parks—Burnett Heads, Moore Park Beach, AAOK Riverdale, and Bundaberg East—offer powered caravan sites. Powered sites are essential for families with caravans needing electricity. Book in advance during school holidays and peak seasons, as powered sites fill quickly in Bundaberg during popular travel periods.
Where can we find cabins in Bundaberg for our family holiday?
Bundaberg East Cabin & Tourist Park specializes in cabin accommodation, ideal if you prefer sleeping indoors without towing. Other parks also offer cabin options alongside caravan sites. Cabins suit families wanting hotel-style comfort with holiday park facilities, typically including ensuite bathrooms and kitchenettes.
Are any Bundaberg holiday parks pet friendly?
Most Bundaberg parks accommodate pets, though policies vary—some restrict dogs to certain areas or require leads. Contact parks directly: Burnett Heads, Moore Park Beach, AAOK Riverdale, and Bundaberg East all accept pets with specific conditions. Confirm pet policies before booking if traveling with family pets.
What's the best value holiday park in Bundaberg for families?
AAOK Riverdale Caravan Park and Bundaberg East Cabin & Tourist Park typically offer competitive pricing while maintaining good facilities. Compare nightly rates across all four parks—prices vary seasonally. Mid-week stays and longer bookings often secure better value than peak-season weekends.
What's the best area to stay in Bundaberg for families?
For beach lovers, Burnett Heads and Moore Park Beach locations are ideal. For central access to shopping, restaurants, and attractions, Bundaberg East suits families exploring town. Consider your priorities: beach activities favor coastal parks; sightseeing favors central locations. Most parks are within 20 minutes of main attractions.
What can families do with kids on rainy days in Bundaberg?
Visit the Bundaberg Rum Distillery (children's tours available), Bundaberg Regional Art Gallery, or indoor play facilities in town. The Woongarra Reptile Park suits all weather. Many families also enjoy shopping at local markets or trying local cafes. Ask park staff for current indoor attraction recommendations.
