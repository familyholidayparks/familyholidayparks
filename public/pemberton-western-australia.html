If We Were Booking...

  • The one option: We'd choose Pemberton Caravan Park, the main family base in town, for wildlife feeding, bike trails and the adjacent natural swimming hole.
  • Our tip: Only attempt the Gloucester Tree climb with kids who are genuinely comfortable with heights, it's a serious, hair-raising ascent with no cage or barrier beyond the steel pegs, and it's not for everyone. Big Brook Dam is the gentler swimming alternative if the Pemberton Pool feels too cold.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Pemberton good for families?
Yes, for families wanting a forest-based holiday with natural swimming and unique attractions like the climbable Gloucester Tree.
What is the Gloucester Tree?
A former fire lookout karri tree with a steel-pegged spiral climb to a platform near the top, a genuinely challenging and slightly scary activity suited to brave older kids.
Where can families swim in Pemberton?
The Pemberton Pool, a natural swimming hole beside the caravan park, or Big Brook Dam, which has a gentler, sandier foreshore.
Is Pemberton Caravan Park the only option?
It's the primary family-oriented option in the immediate town area.
What is the Karri Forest Explorer?
A scenic drive linking several of the region's tallest karri trees and lookout points.
How cold is the swimming in Pemberton?
The natural pools can run cool, particularly outside the warmest summer months, given the forest, inland setting.
What are the best free family activities?
The Karri Forest Explorer drive, and swimming at Big Brook Dam.
When is the best time to visit?
Summer for swimming, or autumn for cooler forest walking weather.
What should families do when it rains?
The forest itself is atmospheric in light rain; heavier rain calls for the town's cafes and small shops.
How far is Pemberton from Perth?
About 3.5 hours drive south, often combined with a broader south west WA forest and coast trip.
