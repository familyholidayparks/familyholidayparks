Local Knowledge

G'day! If you're chasing a family holiday that mixes proper Tassie wilderness with surprisingly manageable drives, the parks around Hobart absolutely deliver. We've found autumn (March-May) is magic here – the kids can actually enjoy bushwalking without melting, the beaches aren't freezing, and you'll dodge the summer crowds at Port Arthur. One tip from our many visits: grab fresh fish and chips from Barilla Bay just north of the airport on your way to whichever park you're staying at – the oysters are stunning if your little ones are adventurous eaters, and there's a jetty where kids can spot rays and pufferfish while you devour lunch.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the best accommodation in Hobart for families with kids?

BIG4 Hobart Airport Tourist Park is brilliant for families – jumping pillow, playground, and you're sorted for early flights. If you want beachside vibes, White Beach Tourist Park lets the kids burn energy on the sand between Hobart adventures. Snug Beach Cabin & Caravan Tourist Park down south offers proper coastal exploring with rock pools and wildlife spotting that'll keep little ones entertained for hours.

What's the best caravan park in Hobart?

BIG4 Hobart Airport Tourist Park takes the cake for convenience and facilities – kids' amenities are top-notch and you're only fifteen minutes from the city. For something more scenic, White Beach Tourist Park offers stunning water views and calm swimming. Barilla Holiday Park up at Seven Mile Beach is another cracker, especially if you love long beach walks and want to grab fresh seafood nearby at Barilla Bay.

What's the best caravan park in Hobart, Tasmania?

Can't go past BIG4 Hobart Airport Tourist Park for families wanting easy access to everything – great facilities and the kids will love the playground. White Beach Tourist Park is gorgeous if you prefer beachfront stays with calmer waters for swimming. Down in the Huon Valley, both Huon Valley Caravan Park and Snug Beach offer that quintessential Tassie coastal experience with apple orchards and wildlife as bonuses.

Where should families stay in Hobart?

Staying at one of the holiday parks gives you flexibility and space families need. BIG4 Hobart Airport Tourist Park works brilliantly as a base for day trips to Port Arthur, MONA, and Mount Wellington. If you'd rather beach-based holidays, White Beach or Snug Beach let kids explore rock pools and beaches while you're still close enough for Salamanca Market runs and fish and chip dinners.

What are the best kids activities in Hobart?

Bonorong Wildlife Sanctuary is absolutely magic for meeting Tassie devils and wombats up close. Salamanca Market on Saturdays has buskers and treats to keep kids engaged. The Museum of Old and New Art (MONA) has a dedicated kids' area that's surprisingly engaging. Mount Wellington offers easy walks with epic views, and in summer, hitting the beaches at Seven Mile Beach or White Beach is perfection for family days out.

What are the best parks for kids in Hobart?

The holiday parks like BIG4 Hobart Airport Tourist Park have brilliant playgrounds and jumping pillows that'll tire the kids out nicely. For regular parks, the Royal Tasmanian Botanical Gardens has huge lawns perfect for picnics and running around. Cornelian Bay has a great beachside playground and BBQ facilities. Down at Sandy Bay, there's Long Beach with its protected swimming area and playground combo.

What are the best things to do in Hobart with kids?

Bonorong Wildlife Sanctuary is non-negotiable for meeting Tasmanian devils and hand-feeding kangaroos. Salamanca Market on Saturdays combines food, crafts, and street performers the kids will love. Take the drive up Mount Wellington for short walks and views that'll blow their minds. Port Arthur Historic Site has a dedicated kids' trail making history actually interesting, and the beaches at Seven Mile or White Beach are perfect for summer splashing.
