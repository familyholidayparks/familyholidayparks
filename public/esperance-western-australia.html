If We Were Booking...

  • Modern facilities: We'd choose RAC Esperance Holiday Park, scoring 87/100, for the playground, games room, daily activities and heated saltwater pool across from the beach.
  • Cape Le Grand base: We'd choose Esperance Pink Lake Tourist Park for spacious sites well positioned for day trips to Cape Le Grand National Park.
  • Our tip: Drive to Lucky Bay in Cape Le Grand National Park and watch for kangaroos genuinely lounging on the beach, it's one of Australia's most famous wildlife photo opportunities and the beach itself is extraordinary.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Esperance good for families?
Yes, RAC Esperance Holiday Park offers strong modern facilities, and the surrounding coastline, including Lucky Bay, is among the most spectacular in Australia.
Which Esperance park is best for kids?
RAC Esperance Holiday Park, scoring 87/100, with a playground, games room, daily holiday activities and a heated saltwater pool.
What is Lucky Bay?
A beach in Cape Le Grand National Park famous for kangaroos that regularly lounge right on the sand, a genuine wildlife highlight.
How remote is Esperance?
Fairly remote, several hours from Perth or the nearest major regional centre, so plan supplies and fuel accordingly.
Is the swimming good for families?
Yes, the water is exceptionally clear and many beaches are sheltered, though always check current conditions given the remote coastline.
What are the best free family activities?
Beach visits throughout Cape Le Grand National Park, and the town's foreshore.
When is the best time to visit?
Spring through autumn for the mildest weather.
What should families do when it rains?
RAC Esperance's covered facilities and games room offer a wet-weather option.
How far is Esperance from Perth?
About 7 to 8 hours drive south east, or a short flight, given its remote position.
How far ahead should you book?
RAC Esperance books out for school holidays given its strong reputation, so plan ahead.
