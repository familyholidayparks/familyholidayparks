Local Knowledge

G'day fellow family adventurers! Ulladulla and its stunning Shoalhaven coastline have stolen my heart over countless visits – the combination of patrolled beaches with rock pools for littlies, brilliant whale watching from May to November, and those famous Blessing of the Fleet prawns makes it pure gold for family holidays. We always time our trips for the quieter shoulder seasons of April-May or September-October when the weather's still lovely but the parks aren't bursting at the seams. Here's an insider tip: grab fish and chips from any local shop, but head to Warden Head Reserve for sunset rather than the main Harbour precinct – the kids can run free on the grass while you spot dolphins, and there's rarely another soul there midweek.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the best accommodation in Ulladulla for families with kids?

BIG4 Ulladulla Beachside Holiday Park and Holiday Haven Ulladulla are both brilliant choices, offering jumping pillows, playgrounds, and pools to keep the little ones entertained. Discovery Parks Burrill Lake is another cracker option with direct water access. All three parks provide family-friendly cabins and powered sites, with amenities designed specifically for families. Book ahead during school holidays as they fill up quickly!

Where's the best family accommodation in Ulladulla?

Holiday Haven Burrill Lake and Ingenia Holidays Ulladulla are top picks for families, both offering excellent facilities including pools, playgrounds, and various cabin options. BIG4 Ulladulla Beachside is hard to beat for beach access, while Discovery Parks Burrill Lake suits families who love water activities. The shoulder seasons of April-May and September-October offer better availability and beautiful weather without the peak crowds.

What's the best caravan park in Ulladulla?

BIG4 Ulladulla Beachside Holiday Park takes the cake for many families with its prime beachfront location and excellent facilities. Holiday Haven Burrill Lake is another standout, particularly for families who enjoy calm lake swimming. Discovery Parks Burrill Lake offers great value with modern amenities, while Holiday Haven Ulladulla provides a good middle ground between beach and town access.

What's the best caravan park in Ulladulla NSW?

Holiday Haven Burrill Lake and BIG4 Ulladulla Beachside are consistently rated as the region's finest caravan parks. Both offer top-notch facilities including jumping pillows, pools, and camp kitchens. Discovery Parks Burrill Lake is perfect for water-loving families, while Ingenia Holidays Ulladulla provides excellent value. Each park has its own charm, so choose based on whether you prefer ocean beaches or calm lake swimming.

What are the best kids activities in Ulladulla?

The rock pools at Rennies Beach are perfect for little explorers, while the patrolled beaches offer safe swimming. Whale watching from May to November is absolutely magical for kids. The Ulladulla Harbour precinct has fishing opportunities and playgrounds, plus you can watch the fishing fleet come in. Don't miss the Funland amusement centre on rainy days, and grab some famous local prawns for a beachside picnic!

Where are the best kids parks in Ulladulla?

The playground at Ulladulla Harbour is a local favourite with ocean views and plenty of space to run around. Most family holiday parks like BIG4 Ulladulla Beachside and Holiday Haven locations have excellent playgrounds and jumping pillows on-site. Rennies Beach reserve has fantastic play equipment plus those brilliant rock pools nearby. Mollymook Beach also features a great playground right near the patrolled swimming area.

Where can kids play in Ulladulla?

The harbour playground offers fantastic play equipment with beautiful water views, while Rennies Beach rock pools provide hours of natural exploration. All major holiday parks including BIG4 Beachside, Holiday Haven, and Discovery Parks feature jumping pillows, playgrounds, and pools. The beaches between Ulladulla and Mollymook have safe swimming zones, and Funland amusement centre is brilliant for rainy days when outdoor play isn't possible.
