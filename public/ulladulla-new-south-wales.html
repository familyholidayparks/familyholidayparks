If We Were Booking...

  • Beachfront and facilities: We'd choose BIG4 Ulladulla Beachside for the new pool, playground and direct access to Racecourse Beach.
  • Calm lake swimming: We'd choose Discovery Parks Burrill Lake for three playgrounds, a jumping pillow and lake access alongside two heated pools.
  • Our tip: Swim the lake at Burrill in the mornings when it's calm, then head to Racecourse or Mollymook Beach in the afternoon for the ocean swim. Both are a short drive apart and give very different water experiences.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Ulladulla good for families?
Yes, it combines a strong resort-style park, calm lake swimming and excellent beaches in a compact, easy-to-navigate area.
Which Ulladulla park is best for kids?
BIG4 Ulladulla Beachside, with a new pool, playground and grassed play areas right beside Racecourse Beach.
Is Burrill Lake good for young children?
Yes, it's calm and suits paddling and swimming well, an easier option than the ocean for younger kids.
What is Pigeon House Mountain?
A well-known hike inland from Ulladulla with a steep but rewarding summit, suited to fit older kids and families who enjoy hiking.
Can you see whales from Ulladulla?
Yes, whale watching cruises run from the harbour during the migration season, roughly May to November.
What are the best free family activities?
Racecourse Beach, Mollymook Beach, the Ulladulla harbour walk, and the lighthouse at Warden Head.
When is the best time to visit?
Whale season for an extra activity, or summer for the best swimming conditions at the beaches.
What should families do when it rains?
The park games rooms cover most wet days, and Nowra's indoor options are about 45 minutes north if needed.
How far is Ulladulla from Sydney?
About 2.5 to 3 hours drive south, a popular South Coast weekend destination.
How far ahead should you book?
BIG4 Ulladulla Beachside books out well ahead for school holidays given its beachfront position.
