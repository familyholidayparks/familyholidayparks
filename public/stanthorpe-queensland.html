Frequently Asked Questions

What's the best accommodation in Stanthorpe for families with kids?
Country Style Holiday Park and Top of the Town Tourist Park are brilliant choices for families, both offering easy access to Stanthorpe's apple orchards and strawberry farms. For something a bit different, Tiny Away Escape @ Granite Belt delivers a unique glamping experience. The cool mountain climate means the kids won't be melting in summer, and you'll actually need jumpers in winter – quite the novelty for Queensland!
Where can families stay in Stanthorpe with children?
Stanthorpe's family-friendly spots include Country Style Holiday Park and Top of the Town Tourist Park, both well-equipped for families exploring the Granite Belt region. Tiny Away Escape @ Granite Belt offers a memorable tiny house experience if you're after something special. Being 900 metres above sea level, the area stays beautifully cool, making it perfect for active kids who want to explore without the typical Queensland heat.
Are there family cabins available in Stanthorpe?
Absolutely! Country Style Holiday Park and Top of the Town Tourist Park both offer comfortable family cabins that are perfect basecamp for exploring the apple orchards and strawberry farms. Visit between September and May when the fruit-picking season is in full swing. The elevated location means pleasant temperatures year-round, so pack layers – winter evenings get genuinely chilly up here in the Granite Belt.
What's the best caravan park in Stanthorpe?
Country Style Holiday Park and Top of the Town Tourist Park consistently rate as Stanthorpe's top caravan parks, offering excellent facilities for families touring the Granite Belt. Both provide great access to local attractions like orchards and wineries. Remember to fill up with fuel before heading into the region, and pack warm clothes – even in summer, evenings can be surprisingly cool at this altitude.
What activities are there for kids in Stanthorpe?
Fruit picking is the star attraction – apples, strawberries, and stone fruits depending on the season (September to May is prime time). The cool mountain air makes outdoor activities comfortable year-round. Many orchards welcome families for pick-your-own experiences, and there are lovely nature walks through the Granite Belt. It's proper hands-on fun that gets kids outdoors and learning where their food comes from.
Is there a kids park in Stanthorpe?
Stanthorpe township has playgrounds for the littlies, but the real drawcard is the natural playground of orchards and farms scattered throughout the Granite Belt. Many holiday parks like Country Style and Top of the Town have their own play areas too. The region's elevation means comfortable temperatures for outdoor play, even during Queensland's sweltering summer months when coastal areas become unbearable.
What is Bush Kids Stanthorpe?
Bush Kids is a fantastic outdoor playgroup and nature-based program in Stanthorpe that gets children exploring the beautiful Granite Belt environment. It's perfect for families visiting during the week who want their kids to experience proper bush play – climbing, building, discovering native plants and wildlife. The cool mountain climate makes it comfortable for active outdoor play that'd be too hot elsewhere in Queensland.
What things can you do with kids in Stanthorpe?
Fruit picking tops the list – strawberries, apples, and stone fruits are all pick-your-own depending on season. September through May is your best window. The Granite Belt's wineries often have lawn areas where kids can play while parents sample. Nature walks, wildlife spotting, and visiting local farms make for brilliant family days out. Base yourself at Country Style or Top of the Town for easy access to everything.
What things can we do in Stanthorpe with kids this weekend?
Hit the fruit farms first – most operate weekends and kids love filling their buckets with strawberries or apples. Pack a picnic and explore Girraween National Park for stunning granite formations and easy walking tracks. Visit a local farm for fresh produce, then let the kids burn off energy at your holiday park. Country Style and Top of the Town both make excellent weekend bases with facilities to keep everyone happy.
