Local Knowledge

G'day! Gippsland's stunning mix of pristine beaches, ancient rainforests, and the incredible Ninety Mile Beach makes it one of Victoria's most diverse regions for a family holiday park stay. We love visiting during autumn (March-May) when the crowds thin out, the weather stays beautifully mild, and you can actually spot wombats wandering near your cabin at dusk in places like Wilsons Promontory. Here's a local tip that'll save you money: grab your fresh fish and chips from the co-op in Lakes Entrance rather than the tourist strip – the locals queue there for a reason, and you'll pay half the price for twice the serving.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the best time to visit Gippsland VIC with kids?

Spring (September-November) and autumn (March-May) are brilliant for families! You'll get mild weather perfect for beach days and bushwalks without the summer crowds. That said, summer holidays are magic here too – just book early as holiday parks fill up fast. Winter can be lovely if you don't mind cooler temps, plus you'll snag better rates and have attractions practically to yourselves!

Are there pet-friendly holiday parks in Gippsland VIC?

Absolutely! Heaps of Gippsland holiday parks welcome your furry family members. BIG4 parks and NRMA Holiday Parks often have pet-friendly cabins and sites. Just check each park's specific pet policy beforehand – some restrict pets to certain areas or cabin types. You'll usually need to keep dogs on leads and bring their own bedding. Many parks are near dog-friendly beaches too, which is a win-win!

How much do holiday parks cost in Gippsland VIC during school holidays?

During school holidays, expect to pay $150-$350 per night for a cabin sleeping 4-6 people, depending on location and amenities. Powered sites for caravans or campers run around $60-$120 per night. Spots near beaches like Lakes Entrance or Inverloch cost more. Book at least 3-6 months ahead for school holidays to get the best rates and availability. Off-peak visits can save you 30-50%!

What should I pack for a holiday park stay with young kids?

Besides the usual clothes and toiletries, bring outdoor toys (bikes, balls, buckets and spades), insect repellent, sunscreen, and hats. Pack a basic first aid kit and any comfort items for bedtime. Most cabins have kitchens, so bring breakfast essentials and snacks. Don't forget beach/swimming gear, torches for evening walks, and wet weather gear – Gippsland weather can be unpredictable!

What is there to do near Gippsland VIC holiday parks for families?

You're spoilt for choice! Wilson's Prom has incredible walks and beaches, Lakes Entrance offers boat tours and beaches, and Phillip Island has penguins and wildlife parks. There's also Tarra-Bulga National Park for rainforest walks, countless patrolled beaches, playgrounds, mini golf courses, and local wildlife sanctuaries. Most holiday parks have pools, jumping pillows, and games rooms to keep little ones entertained on-site too!
