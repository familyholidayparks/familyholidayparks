Local Knowledge

G'day! Brunswick Heads sits right where the Brunswick River meets the Pacific, and it's this gorgeous river-meets-ocean setup that makes it absolutely brilliant for families – the kids can paddle in calm river waters one day and surf gentle beach breaks the next. We reckon April through October is your sweet spot for visiting, when the humpback whales cruise past during their migration and the summer holiday crowds have thinned right out. Here's a local tip that'll save you a packet: grab your fish and chips from the Brunswick Heads Hotel (the pink pub on the river), then stake out a spot on the grassy banks for sunset – the kids can run around while you actually enjoy a warm meal, and the local pelicans put on quite a show at feeding time around 3:30pm daily.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the best family accommodation in Brunswick Heads?

Reflections Brunswick Heads Holiday Park is top-notch for families, set right where the river meets the ocean. The park offers easy access to both calm river paddling and beach adventures. Reflections Ferry Reserve and Reflections Massy Greene are also cracker options nearby. Book between April and October to avoid the summer crowds and catch the humpback whale migration – the kids will love it.

Where should families stay in Tweed Heads?

While Tweed Heads has plenty of options, many families actually base themselves at Brunswick Heads (just 20 minutes south) for a quieter vibe with better beach and river access. The Reflections Holiday Parks around Brunswick offer excellent facilities and you're still close enough for day trips to Tweed's attractions like the Minjungbal Aboriginal Cultural Centre.

What's the best family accommodation in Nambucca Heads?

Nambucca Heads is about three hours south of Brunswick Heads, so quite a different region altogether. If you're looking at the mid-north coast, Nambucca has some lovely caravan parks right on the river. However, for the Brunswick Heads area specifically, the Reflections Holiday Parks offer unbeatable river and ocean access that kids absolutely love.

What's the best caravan park in Brunswick Heads?

Reflections Brunswick Heads Holiday Park takes the cake, positioned perfectly between the river and ocean. Reflections Ferry Reserve is another beauty, offering direct river access for kayaking and fishing. Both parks have great facilities for families and you can walk to the village for fish and chips. Book ahead for school holidays as they fill up quickly.

What activities are there for kids in Brunswick Heads?

Kids can paddle in the calm Brunswick River, surf at Main Beach, or explore rock pools at low tide. The river foreshore is perfect for picnics and fishing, while the nature reserve offers easy walking tracks. Grab fish and chips from one of the local spots and watch dolphins playing in the river mouth – it's magic and costs nothing.

Which beach is best for kids in Brunswick Heads?

Brunswick Heads Main Beach is brilliant for families – patrolled during summer with gentle waves perfect for beginner surfers. The real winner though is the river beach at Torakina, where littlies can safely splash in shallow, calm water while parents relax on the sand. The river's also warmer than the ocean, which the kids definitely appreciate!

What can kids do in Tweed Heads?

Tweed Heads offers the Minjungbal Aboriginal Cultural Centre for learning about local Indigenous culture, plus great beaches at Coolangatta just across the border. The Tweed Regional Gallery often runs kids' workshops, and Point Danger has brilliant views and the Captain Cook Memorial. It's an easy day trip from Brunswick Heads for families wanting variety.
