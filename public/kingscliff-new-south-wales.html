Local Knowledge

G'day! If you're after a family holiday that combines stunning beaches with that relaxed Northern Rivers vibe, Kingscliff and its surrounds absolutely deliver. The best time to visit is May through October when the humpback whales cruise past the headland and the weather's still gloriously mild – you'll dodge the summer crowds and those intense January heatwaves. Here's a local tip that'll save you a bundle: grab your fresh fish and chips from Kingscliff Beach Bowl (the servings are massive) and head to Dreamtime Beach just five minutes south, where the rock pools keep little ones entertained for hours while you actually get to eat your lunch in peace.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the best family accommodation in Kingscliff?

Tweed Holiday Parks Kingscliff Beach and Kingscliff North are absolute winners for families, both offering beachside locations with excellent facilities. Ingenia Holidays Kingscliff is another ripper option with plenty of space for the kids to run around. All three parks put you within walking distance of the patrolled beach and that gorgeous beachfront village vibe Kingscliff's famous for.

What's the best caravan park in Kingscliff?

Tweed Holiday Parks Kingscliff Beach takes some beating with its absolute beachfront position – you can literally roll out of your van onto the sand. Kingscliff North is equally fantastic, tucked behind the dunes with direct beach access. Both are council-run parks that are beautifully maintained. Ingenia Holidays Kingscliff offers more resort-style facilities if that's what you're after.

What are the best kids activities in Kingscliff?

The patrolled beach is your go-to for swimming and body boarding, while the rock pools at the headland are brilliant for little explorers. The Kingscliff Beach foreshore has a great playground, and the calm Cudgen Creek is perfect for kayaking with youngsters. Don't miss whale watching from May to October – the humpbacks put on an incredible show right from the headland!

Where's the best kids playground in Kingscliff?

The beachfront playground on Marine Parade is an absolute cracker, right next to the sand with ocean views to keep parents happy while the kids burn off energy. There's also a lovely fenced playground at Cudgen Headland Reserve, which combines equipment with wide open spaces perfect for ball games and picnics after a morning at the beach.

Where can kids play soccer in Kingscliff?

Kingscliff has several great grassy areas perfect for a kickabout, including the expansive reserves at Cudgen Headland and Salt Village. For organised soccer, check out Kingscliff Football Club at Kingscliff Park on Turnock Street – they run junior programs and might have holiday clinics. The beach at low tide also makes for a brilliant impromptu pitch!
