If We Were Booking...

  • Walk to everything: We'd choose NRMA Port Macquarie Breakwall Holiday Park for the position, walking distance to Town Beach, the breakwall and town, plus a genuinely big heated pool.
  • Quiet beachside: We'd choose Flynns Beach Caravan Park for a more natural, laid-back setting a short walk from its own beach.
  • Our tip: Time a visit to the Koala Hospital for a feeding tour, then walk the breakwall at sunset with fish and chips from town. Both are free or cheap and both are the things families remember most about Port Macquarie.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Port Macquarie good for families?
Yes, it's a well-rounded family town with good beaches, the Koala Hospital and an easy, walkable centre.
Which Port Macquarie park is best for families?
NRMA Breakwall Holiday Park for position and its large heated pool, walking distance to Town Beach and the CBD.
What is the Koala Hospital?
A genuine wildlife hospital that rehabilitates injured koalas, with daily public feeding tours that let visitors watch from a respectful distance.
Are the beaches safe for kids?
Town Beach and Flynns Beach are both patrolled in season and suit family swimming.
What is Sea Acres?
An elevated boardwalk through coastal rainforest just south of town, an easy, shaded walk suited to all ages.
What are the best free family activities?
The breakwall walk, Town Beach, and the Koala Hospital's feeding tours, which are free to attend.
When is the best time to visit?
Spring through autumn for the mildest weather. Summer is busiest given the strong beach appeal.
What should families do when it rains?
The Glasshouse regional gallery and library precinct in town, and the park games rooms cover wet days.
How far is Port Macquarie from Sydney?
About 4.5 hours drive north, a popular first or last stop on a NSW north coast trip.
How far ahead should you book?
The Breakwall park's position makes it popular for school holidays, so book ahead for peak periods.
