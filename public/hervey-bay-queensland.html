Local Knowledge

G'day! Hervey Bay's sheltered waters make it one of the few spots in Queensland where kids can actually swim at the beach year-round without battling massive waves or worrying about stingers during summer. The absolute sweet spot for visiting is July to November when humpback whales cruise through on their migration – you'll often spot them from the shore while the little ones are building sandcastles. My top tip: grab fish and chips from the Urangan Pier kiosk at sunset and let the kids feed the pelicans that rock up like clockwork at 4pm for the daily fish cleaning session. The holiday parks here cater brilliantly to families who want that perfect mix of beach days, wildlife encounters, and easy day trips to Fraser Island.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the best accommodation in Hervey Bay for kids?

Discovery Parks Fraser Street and Ingenia Holidays Hervey Bay are brilliant choices for families. Both have swimming pools, playgrounds, and jumping pillows to keep the kids entertained between beach visits. Fraser Coast Beachfront Tourist Parks at Scarness puts you right on the sand, perfect for those calm morning swims Hervey Bay's famous for.

Where should we stay in Hervey Bay with kids?

You can't go wrong with Hervey Bay Caravan Park or Pier Caravan Park Hervey Bay – both offer family-friendly facilities close to the Esplanade. The sheltered beaches nearby mean the little ones can actually swim safely year-round. Discovery Parks Fraser Street is another top pick with excellent amenities and jumping pillows the kids will love.

What's the best family accommodation in Hervey Bay?

Ingenia Holidays Hervey Bay and Discovery Parks Fraser Street lead the pack for families, with pools, playgrounds, and spacious powered sites. Fraser Coast Beachfront Tourist Parks at Scarness is ideal if you want the beach at your doorstep. All these parks are well-maintained and offer the cabin or caravan site options families need.

What's the best caravan park in Hervey Bay?

Discovery Parks Fraser Street consistently rates highest for facilities and family-friendliness. Hervey Bay Caravan Park and Pier Caravan Park Hervey Bay both offer excellent locations near the town centre and beaches. For beachfront stays, Fraser Coast Beachfront Tourist Parks at Scarness is hard to beat – you'll wake up to those famous calm waters perfect for the kids.

What activities are there for kids in Hervey Bay?

The calm beaches are perfect for swimming year-round, and between July and November you might spot humpback whales from the shore. Head to Urangan Pier for fish feeding, visit WetSide Water Park for free water play, or explore the Esplanade's playgrounds and bike paths. Reefworld Aquarium is great for rainy days too.

Are there occupational therapists for kids in Hervey Bay?

Hervey Bay has several paediatric occupational therapy services available for families. You'll find practitioners at local medical centres and private clinics throughout the area. For specific recommendations and availability, it's best to contact the Hervey Bay Hospital or ask your GP for referrals to local OT services that specialise in children's needs.

Is there a kids water park in Hervey Bay?

WetSide Water Park at Pialba is absolutely free and perfect for littlies on hot days. It's got water jets, tipping buckets, and splash zones right near the Esplanade. The beach itself is also like nature's water park – those sheltered, calm waters mean kids can actually swim safely without the big waves you get elsewhere on the Queensland coast.
