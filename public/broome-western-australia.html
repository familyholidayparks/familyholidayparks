The Broome Holiday Park Scene

Broome's holiday parks sit in one of WA's most distinctive coastal settings, where the Indian Ocean meets red pindan soil and tidal ranges that hit some of the biggest in the world. The five parks in the area each have their own character—Cable Beach Caravan Park and RAC Cable Beach Holiday Park sit right on the famous sweep of sand that defines Broome, while Tarangau and Broome Caravan Park are closer to town but still within easy reach of water. Discovery Parks - Broome rounds out the options. What makes this pocket different from southern caravan parks is the sheer number of repeat visitors who've been coming for decades—grey nomads who time their migration north to hit Broome in the cooler months, families who've booked the same dates every school holiday for years, and couples who've made it their annual reset button. The parks fill predictably hard during WA school holidays and the May-September shoulder season when southern Australia's winter drives people north.

The beachfront parks give you direct access to Cable Beach's 22 kilometres of sand, and timing matters here—when the tide goes out, you can walk for hours on hard-packed beach. Tarangau and Broome Caravan Park offer a quieter setup slightly inland but with Roebuck Bay nearby, plus you're closer to the town's proper infrastructure, the markets, and the cafes. Beyond the water, there's the nature side: Broome's surrounded by spinifex country, bird-watching spots around Cygnet Bay, and the Dampier Peninsula's remote beaches if you're willing to drive further. Most parks have reasonable facilities—powered sites are standard at the top-ranked parks, and the beachfront ones have that particular appeal of waking to ocean views and the smell of salt. Families with young kids tend to stay longer here than they would down south, partly because the weather works for them during winter and partly because there's enough to do across a week or two without getting bored.

What keeps families coming back isn't one thing—it's the combination. Cable Beach Caravan Park and RAC Cable Beach Holiday Park offer that direct beach access, while the town-closer options like Broome and Tarangau give families more flexibility for daily activities. School holidays see occupancy spike hard; booking six to twelve months ahead isn't uncommon for families wanting to lock in their preferred park and dates. The parks themselves have regular visitors who've watched their kids grow up coming here, and that creates a genuine community feel that seasonal parks often lack. Broome's remoteness—it's still five hours from Kununurra, hours from anywhere else—actually works in its favour; once you're here, you stay put and settle in, which is exactly what families want. The combination of reliable winter weather, accessible beaches with safe swimming conditions, and a town that's relaxed enough to absorb families without feeling overwhelmed keeps the parks full and keeps people returning.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the best family holiday parks in Broome, WA?
Cable Beach Caravan Park, RAC Cable Beach Holiday Park, Tarangau Caravan Park, and Broome Caravan Park are Broome's top family options. Each offers different amenities suited to families—some feature pools, others prioritize beach access. Consider your priorities: Cable Beach parks offer direct ocean access, while Tarangau and Broome parks provide central locations near town attractions and shops.
Which caravan park in Broome is best for kids?
RAC Cable Beach Holiday Park stands out for families with its dedicated pool, playground facilities, and direct Cable Beach access. Cable Beach Caravan Park also excels with similar amenities plus water activities. Both parks keep children entertained with onsite facilities while parents relax, making them ideal for younger families.
Are there holiday parks with pools near Broome?
Yes, RAC Cable Beach Holiday Park and Cable Beach Caravan Park both feature swimming pools—essential during Broome's hot summers. These pools provide safe swimming alternatives when the ocean's stinger season (October-April) restricts beach swimming. Pools are typically open year-round, making them valuable amenities for families.
Which holiday parks in Broome are near the beach?
Cable Beach Caravan Park and RAC Cable Beach Holiday Park offer direct or immediate beach access, perfect for families wanting easy ocean access. Both parks sit steps from Cable Beach's famous red cliffs and stunning sunsets. Broome Caravan Park and Tarangau are closer to town but require short drives to beaches.
Does Broome have caravan parks with powered sites?
All four major parks—Cable Beach Caravan Park, RAC Cable Beach Holiday Park, Tarangau Caravan Park, and Broome Caravan Park—offer powered sites. Powered sites include electricity, water, and sewerage connections, essential for families with caravans or motorhomes needing appliance access during stays.
What cabins are available at Broome holiday parks?
Cable Beach Caravan Park, RAC Cable Beach Holiday Park, and Broome Caravan Park offer cabin accommodations ranging from basic to self-contained options. Cabins suit families avoiding caravan setup hassles. Options vary from budget-friendly to premium, with most including air-conditioning and ensuite bathrooms—check individual parks for specific cabin types and pricing.
Are any Broome caravan parks pet-friendly?
Several Broome parks welcome pets, but policies vary significantly. Cable Beach Caravan Park and Broome Caravan Park typically allow well-behaved pets in certain areas. Contact parks directly before booking, as restrictions apply to breed types, size, and designated zones. Keeping pets contained prevents beach wildlife disturbance.
What's the best value holiday park in Broome?
Broome Caravan Park and Tarangau Caravan Park typically offer competitive rates compared to beachfront options. Both provide good facilities without premium beach positioning, making them budget-friendly choices. Check seasonal rates—dry season (April-September) offers better value than peak summer months when prices increase significantly.
What's the best area to stay in Broome for families?
Cable Beach is ideal for families wanting beach activities and sunset views, though accommodation costs more. Town center areas near Tarangau and Broome parks offer convenient shopping, restaurants, and attractions. Choose beachfront parks for water-focused holidays; central parks for exploring town culture and museums during cooler months.
What can families do with kids in Broome during rainy season?
Broome has excellent indoor options: the Broome Museum, Broome Cinemas, and indoor pools at caravan parks. Shopping centers provide air-conditioned relief. Pearl farms offer guided tours indoors. Sunset Poolside cinema operates seasonally. The Broome library hosts activities. Plan flexible schedules during December-March when tropical showers interrupt outdoor activities but don't deter family fun.
