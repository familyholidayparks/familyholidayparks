The Broome Holiday Park Scene

Broome's caravan parks sit in a unique pocket of Western Australia where the Indian Ocean meets red pindan soil, and this geography shapes everything about staying here. Cable Beach Caravan Park and RAC Cable Beach Holiday Park put you right on the sand—literally metres from the water where you can swim, fish, or just watch the famous Broome sunsets that turn the beach orange. Tarangau and Broome Caravan Park offer a different experience, set back slightly with bush surrounds that feel more sheltered and quieter, though you're still close enough to reach the beach and river access within minutes. Discovery Parks - Broome fills the middle ground, giving families resort-style facilities without the beachfront intensity. What makes Broome different to other coastal parks down south is the raw feel of it—this is the Kimberley, not some sanitised holiday zone. The tides here are massive, the light is harsh, and the landscape doesn't apologise for itself.

You'll find everything from retirees in permanent vans who've essentially moved to Broome, to families packing a caravan for the winter school holidays when the weather's perfect, to motorhomers doing lap of Australia routes. The parks have evolved to handle this mix—Cable Beach caters heavily to tourists wanting beachfront convenience, while Tarangau gets repeat visitors who book the same site year after year. School holidays, particularly June through August, are when these parks fill to capacity; families know that Broome's dry season is the only realistic window to visit comfortably. You'll see the same caravans return regularly, their owners having worked out which park suits their style and staking their claim for the cooler months.

What keeps people coming back isn't just the beach access, though wading through shallow turquoise water at Cable Beach or spotting crocodiles from a distance at Roebuck Bay matters. It's the combination—mornings at the markets, afternoon swims, evening walks along the shore, and nights where the Milky Way is so bright it casts a shadow. Families appreciate that kids can safely explore rock pools, ride bikes through the park, and wind down in the evening without needing constant entertainment. The parks themselves are low-key; they're bases for exploring Broome rather than destinations in their own right, which is exactly what appeals to people who come back. Once you've spent a winter season here, you understand why caravanners queue up for bookings months in advance.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the best family holiday parks in Broome?
Cable Beach Caravan Park, RAC Cable Beach Holiday Park, Tarangau Caravan Park, and Broome Caravan Park all offer family-friendly facilities. Cable Beach parks are closest to swimming and water activities. Tarangau and Broome Caravan Park offer quieter settings. Compare amenities like pools, playgrounds, and proximity to beaches before booking based on your family's priorities.
Which caravan park in Broome is best for kids?
RAC Cable Beach Holiday Park and Cable Beach Caravan Park excel for families, offering pools, playgrounds, and direct beach access. Kids can safely explore Cable Beach's shallow waters and enjoy water sports. These parks' proximity to family attractions and modern facilities make them top choices for keeping children entertained throughout your stay.
Are there holiday parks with pools near Broome?
Yes, RAC Cable Beach Holiday Park and Cable Beach Caravan Park both feature swimming pools—essential during Broome's hot summers. Pools provide safe cooling-off spots when beach conditions aren't ideal. Having onsite pools means children can swim while parents relax, making these parks particularly convenient for families wanting water access without leaving the park.
Which holiday parks in Broome are closest to the beach?
Cable Beach Caravan Park and RAC Cable Beach Holiday Park sit directly on or steps away from Cable Beach, Broome's most famous swimming spot. Beach proximity means quick access to sunset viewing, water activities, and safe family swimming. Tarangau Caravan Park and Broome Caravan Park are slightly further inland but still central to Broome's attractions.
Do Broome caravan parks offer powered sites?
All four parks—Cable Beach Caravan Park, RAC Cable Beach Holiday Park, Tarangau Caravan Park, and Broome Caravan Park—provide powered caravan and motorhome sites. Powered sites include water and electricity connections, ideal for families needing modern conveniences. Book early during peak seasons as powered sites fill quickly in this popular destination.
What cabin options are available at Broome holiday parks?
Cable Beach Caravan Park, RAC Cable Beach Holiday Park, and Tarangau Caravan Park offer cabin accommodations ranging from basic to deluxe. Cabins suit families preferring tent-free holidays with beds and bathrooms. Options include self-contained units with kitchens. Check specific parks for cabin types, as availability and quality vary—book ahead during school holidays.
Are there pet-friendly caravan parks in Broome?
Most Broome parks accept pets, including Cable Beach Caravan Park and RAC Cable Beach Holiday Park, though restrictions apply. Rules typically require leashed pets and prohibit them from certain areas. Always confirm pet policies when booking, as some parks have peak-season restrictions or additional fees. Broome's outdoor lifestyle suits holiday traveling with family pets.
Which Broome holiday park offers the best value?
Broome Caravan Park and Tarangau Caravan Park typically offer competitive rates compared to beachfront properties. Budget-conscious families should compare nightly rates, what's included (pools, amenities), and off-season discounts. Cable Beach parks command premium prices due to location. Off-peak visits (April-September) provide better value across all parks while maintaining excellent weather.
What's the best area to stay in Broome for families?
Cable Beach is ideal for families—offering safe swimming, sunset views, and proximity to shops and restaurants. Cable Beach Caravan Park and RAC Cable Beach Holiday Park provide direct access. Town center accommodations suit families wanting walkable restaurant and shopping access. Consider whether you prioritize beach convenience or town amenities when choosing between parks.
What are fun things to do with kids in Broome?
Cable Beach swimming, Broome Crocodile Park, Broome Wildlife Park, and camel rides are major kid favorites. The Broome Museum and Japanese Cemetery offer cultural education. Staircase to the Moon at Town Beach (seasonal) amazes children. Snorkeling, pearl farm tours, and fishing trips appeal to older kids. Most parks are centrally located for accessing these attractions easily.
