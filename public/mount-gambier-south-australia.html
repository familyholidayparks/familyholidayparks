If We Were Booking...

  • Resort facilities by the lake: We'd choose Blue Lake Holiday Park for the jumping pillow, pump track and indoor heated pool with two waterslides, right by the Blue Lake itself.
  • Budget option: We'd choose Pine Country Caravan Park for a simpler, clean base with a playground and jumping pillow.
  • Our tip: Visit the Blue Lake during its vivid blue season, November to March, and pair it with a stop at the free Umpherston Sinkhole, a sunken garden inside an old cave collapse that genuinely surprises kids.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Mount Gambier good for families?
Yes, the Blue Lake is a genuine natural wonder and Blue Lake Holiday Park offers strong facilities right beside it.
Which Mount Gambier park is best for kids?
Blue Lake Holiday Park, scoring 86/100, with a jumping pillow, pump track and an indoor heated pool with two waterslides.
When does the Blue Lake turn blue?
Roughly November through to March, when it transforms into a vivid blue colour, a striking natural phenomenon.
What is the Umpherston Sinkhole?
A free sunken garden built inside a collapsed limestone cave, an easy and genuinely surprising family stop in town.
Are there other parks worth avoiding in Mount Gambier?
One park in the area has been flagged with serious safety and cleanliness concerns in reviews; check current conditions carefully before booking any option outside the two recommended here.
Can you swim in the Blue Lake?
No, swimming is not permitted in the Blue Lake as it's a drinking water source for the town; viewing is from surrounding walking tracks.
What are the best free family activities?
Walking around the Blue Lake, the Umpherston Sinkhole, and Cave Gardens in the town centre.
When is the best time to visit?
November to March for the Blue Lake's vivid colour, though the town is pleasant to visit year-round.
What should families do when it rains?
Blue Lake Holiday Park's indoor heated pool with waterslides operates all season, a reliable wet-weather option.
How far is Mount Gambier from Adelaide?
About 4.5 hours drive south east, often visited as part of a broader Limestone Coast trip.
