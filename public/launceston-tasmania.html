The Launceston Holiday Park Scene

# Launceston Holiday Parks

Launceston's holiday park scene is built around two established operators who've carved out solid niches in Tasmania's northern hub. The Launceston Holiday Park and BIG4 Launceston Holiday Park both leverage the city's riverside setting, giving caravanners and motorhome travellers direct access to the South Esk River precinct without the long haul to coastal alternatives. Neither park is beachfront—that's the trade-off of being inland—but what you get instead is proximity to the city's walking trails, the Cataract Gorge Reserve just minutes away, and easy access to Tasmania's northern attractions like Deloraine and the Tasmanian Wildlife Park. Repeat visitors know these parks cater to the serious traveller who wants a base rather than a destination, which suits families using Launceston as a jumping-off point for longer road trips.

The parks themselves skew toward practical caravan and motorhome facilities rather than resort-style amenities. You'll find the usual on-site van and cabin accommodation, powered sites for extended stays, and well-maintained facilities designed for the grey-nomad set and families towing conventional caravans. The riverside location means reasonable walking access to local cafes and the broader Launceston CBD, though neither park has that isolated bush feel you'd find further south. BIG4 in particular caters to families with kids' facilities and family-friendly activities during school holidays, while the standard Holiday Park tends to attract older caravanners and couples after quieter stays. Both fill fast during school holiday periods—Easter, winter break, and Christmas are booked solid months ahead—but shoulder seasons remain accessible for flexible travellers.

Families return to these parks year after year because Launceston itself functions well as a base for northern Tasmania exploration without being remote or isolating. The combination of river access, easy gorge walks, and proximity to farmland attractions keeps kids engaged while parents appreciate the straightforward facilities and reliable infrastructure. Locals know the parks do their job without pretence: clean ablutions, reliable power and water, and enough to do within an hour's drive to justify bringing the caravan back. For caravanners chasing a rhythm of park-hopping down the east coast, Launceston's parks remain a logical midpoint stop rather than a destination in themselves, which is precisely why they maintain steady, loyal patronage.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the best family holiday parks in Launceston, Tasmania?
Launceston Holiday Park and BIG4 Launceston Holiday Park are the top choices for families. Both offer kid-friendly facilities, convenient locations near attractions, and accommodation options ranging from powered sites to cabins. BIG4 is known for its amenities, while Launceston Holiday Park provides excellent value with direct access to local activities.
Which caravan park in Launceston is best for kids?
BIG4 Launceston Holiday Park stands out for families with children, featuring swimming pools, playgrounds, and entertainment programs during school holidays. The park's layout is spacious and safe for kids to explore, with proximity to family-friendly attractions like Cataract Gorge and the Launceston Zoo.
Are there holiday parks with pools near Launceston?
BIG4 Launceston Holiday Park features heated swimming pools suitable for families. Launceston Holiday Park also offers pool facilities. Both parks maintain their pools year-round, making them ideal for families visiting during cooler months when the pools are heated.
What holiday parks near Launceston are close to beaches?
While Launceston is inland, both major parks are within 30 minutes of coastal areas. The Tamar Valley provides river beaches, and George Town's beaches are accessible for day trips. Both parks offer easy access to water activities without requiring long drives from accommodation.
Does Launceston have holiday parks with powered sites?
Both Launceston Holiday Park and BIG4 Launceston Holiday Park offer extensive powered caravan sites with full hookups including water, electricity, and sewerage. These sites accommodate caravans of various sizes, with BIG4 offering premium powered options with extra amenities.
What cabin options are available in Launceston holiday parks?
BIG4 Launceston Holiday Park offers luxury cabins and budget-friendly options, from ensuite cabins to family units with kitchens. Launceston Holiday Park provides cabin accommodation ranging from basic to self-contained, catering to different budgets and group sizes for families.
Are there pet-friendly holiday parks in Launceston?
Both Launceston Holiday Park and BIG4 Launceston Holiday Park welcome pets, though restrictions apply to certain areas. Most sites permit dogs and cats in caravan areas, making them suitable for families traveling with animals. Contact parks directly about specific pet policies and any additional fees.
Which holiday park offers best value in Launceston?
Launceston Holiday Park typically offers competitive pricing across all accommodation types, making it excellent for budget-conscious families. BIG4 provides good value through package deals and off-season discounts. Both parks offer free or low-cost activities, reducing overall holiday expenses for families.
What's the best area to stay in Launceston for families?
Both parks are centrally located, providing easy access to Cataract Gorge, Launceston Zoo, and the CBD. The parks' proximity to the South Esk River and walking trails makes them ideal. Launceston's downtown is walkable, with restaurants, shops, and attractions within reach from both locations.
What are rainy day activities near Launceston holiday parks?
Launceston offers indoor attractions including the Queen Victoria Museum, Launceston Zoo's indoor exhibits, and the Lilydale Falls scenic area. Local shopping centers provide entertainment, while parks like BIG4 often organize indoor activities. The region's covered markets and galleries offer all-weather options for families.
