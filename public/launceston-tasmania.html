Local Knowledge

G'day! Launceston and the Tamar Valley are absolutely brilliant for families who want that perfect mix of nature and easy-going fun – the kids can feed wallabies in the morning and splash in Cataract Gorge's swimming basin by lunch. We reckon April through May is magic here: the autumn colours along the Tamar are stunning, the crowds have thinned out, and you'll still get those crisp, sunny days perfect for exploring. Here's a local secret – grab fresh berries and cherries from the roadside stalls along the West Tamar Highway (the kids love picking their own), then have a picnic at one of the river beaches where the water's actually swimmable unlike most Tassie spots.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the best accommodation in Launceston for families with kids?

Launceston Holiday Park and BIG4 Launceston Holiday Park are both cracker choices for families. They're well set up with playgrounds, jumping pillows, and family-friendly facilities. Both parks put you within easy reach of Cataract Gorge and City Park, so you won't be spending half your holiday in the car getting to attractions the kids will love.

What's the best caravan park in Launceston?

BIG4 Launceston Holiday Park and Launceston Holiday Park consistently top the charts with families. Both offer excellent facilities including playgrounds, camp kitchens, and clean amenities blocks. They're conveniently located for exploring the Tamar Valley's wineries and farm gates, plus you're only a short drive from Cataract Gorge – perfect for a refreshing swim after a day of adventuring.

What's the best caravan park in Launceston, Tasmania?

You can't go wrong with Launceston Holiday Park or BIG4 Launceston Holiday Park – both are brilliant family bases. They've got all the essentials sorted: powered sites, cabin options, jumping pillows for the kids, and spotless facilities. The autumn months (April-May) are particularly lovely, with gorgeous weather and fewer crowds at the parks and local attractions.

What are the best things to do in Launceston with kids?

Cataract Gorge is absolutely unmissable – the kids can swim in the basin, cross the swinging bridge, and ride the chairlift. City Park's Japanese macaques and free-roaming peacocks are always a hit. Don't miss the berry farms along the Tamar Valley where little ones can pick their own strawberries and cherries from roadside stalls, especially during autumn.

What are the best kids' activities in Launceston?

Feeding wallabies at wildlife parks around the Tamar Valley is always magical for little ones. Cataract Gorge offers swimming, bushwalking, and that thrilling chairlift ride. The Queen Victoria Museum has interactive displays that keep kids engaged, and City Park's playground and resident monkeys provide hours of entertainment. Autumn brings perfect weather for outdoor exploring without the summer crowds.

Where are the best kids' parks in Launceston?

City Park is the standout – it's got brilliant playgrounds, Japanese macaques the kids will be fascinated by, and wandering peacocks. The grounds at Cataract Gorge also have lovely picnic areas and walking tracks perfect for little legs. Both Launceston Holiday Park and BIG4 Launceston Holiday Park feature excellent on-site playgrounds and jumping pillows if you're staying put for the day.

Is Launceston a kids' paradise?

Launceston's definitely up there for family-friendly Tassie destinations! You've got the stunning Cataract Gorge right in town, wildlife encounters throughout the Tamar Valley, and brilliant parks like City Park where peacocks strut about freely. The region's compact size means less driving and more doing, plus both major holiday parks are well-equipped for families seeking that perfect nature-meets-comfort balance.
