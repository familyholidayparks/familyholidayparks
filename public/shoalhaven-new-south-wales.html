The Shoalhaven Holiday Park Scene

# Shoalhaven Holiday Parks

The Shoalhaven region has carved out a reputation as one of the South Coast's most reliable holiday park destinations, largely because the park operators here actually understand what regulars want. Holiday Haven dominates the scene with three strategically positioned properties—Culburra Beach sits right on the sand with direct beach access, Shoalhaven Heads occupies prime real estate where the Shoalhaven River meets the ocean, and Currarong offers a quieter alternative across the water. What sets these parks apart isn't flashy resort amenities but rather their grip on the fundamentals: solid powered sites, genuine waterfront positioning, and management that's been fine-tuning operations for decades. The supporting parks—Nowra Riverside Tourist Park and Treehaven Tourist Park—fill gaps for those wanting riverside or bush-surrounded camping, giving visitors real choice depending on whether they're chasing beach time or something more sheltered.

You'll find every variety of visitor here, from grey nomads who've been parking in the same spot for 20 years to young families discovering the coast for the first time. Caravanners and motorhomers make up the bulk of the crowd, particularly the dedicated repeat visitors who book school holiday periods a year in advance. The Culburra and Shoalhaven Heads parks fill to capacity during July and December-January, with Easter and September school holidays running a close second. Many families return annually because the parks sit within striking distance of proper beaches—Culburra's patrolled and suitable for kids, while Shoalhaven Heads offers river swimming and rock pools that younger children can explore safely. It's the accessibility that keeps them coming back: you're not fighting crowds, the parks maintain their grounds properly, and you can actually get your caravan in and out without a logistics operation.

The real advantage the Shoalhaven parks offer is their position between serious beach access and the quieter Shoalhaven River system. Culburra Beach gives you proper coastal experiences—patrolled swimming, fishing, and that open water feel—while the riverside parks cater to a different tempo entirely, with kayaking, birdwatching, and calmer water activities. From Shoalhaven Heads, you're in a transitional zone where families can split time between river and ocean without relocating. Nowra sits far enough upriver to be genuinely peaceful but close enough to town for supplies and services. What brings families back year after year isn't marketing; it's the simple fact that these parks work logistically, the facilities don't disappoint, and there's enough water-based activity within arm's reach to keep everyone occupied. The South Coast genuinely gets quieter than the Central Coast once you're south of Wollongong, and Shoalhaven captures that sweet spot between accessibility and actual coastal peace.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the best family holiday parks in Shoalhaven, NSW?
Holiday Haven Culburra Beach, Holiday Haven Shoalhaven Heads, Holiday Haven Currarong, and Nowra Riverside Tourist Park are top-rated family options. Each offers different advantages—beachfront locations, river access, or quieter settings. All provide family-friendly facilities including playgrounds, pools, and on-site activities. Choose based on your preference for beach versus river experiences and desired amenities.
Which caravan park in Shoalhaven is best for kids?
Holiday Haven Culburra Beach and Holiday Haven Shoalhaven Heads excel for families with children. Both feature swimming pools, kids' clubs during peak seasons, playground equipment, and shallow beach access. Nowra Riverside Tourist Park offers river activities and spacious grounds for exploration. All three provide safe, supervised environments specifically designed for family holidays.
Are there holiday parks with pools near Shoalhaven?
Yes, Holiday Haven Culburra Beach and Holiday Haven Shoalhaven Heads both have heated pools ideal for families. These facilities operate year-round, with extended hours during school holidays. Pools are monitored and complemented by other water activities like beach swimming and river access, ensuring multiple cooling-off options for children.
What holiday parks are near the beach in Shoalhaven?
Holiday Haven Culburra Beach and Holiday Haven Shoalhaven Heads offer direct or immediate beach access. Culburra provides patrolled swimming beaches perfect for families, while Shoalhaven Heads has scenic coastal walks and sheltered swimming spots. Both locations combine beach convenience with park facilities, eliminating the need for separate accommodation and beach trips.
Which Shoalhaven parks offer powered sites?
All four parks—Holiday Haven Culburra Beach, Holiday Haven Shoalhaven Heads, Holiday Haven Currarong, and Nowra Riverside Tourist Park—provide powered caravan sites. Powered options typically include 15-amp or 10-amp connections suitable for caravans and motorhomes. Book ahead during school holidays and peak seasons as powered sites fill quickly.
What cabin options are available in Shoalhaven holiday parks?
Holiday Haven locations offer various cabin styles from basic self-catering to fully equipped units with ensuites. Options range from budget-friendly cabins to premium family units sleeping 4-8 people. Nowra Riverside Tourist Park also provides cabin accommodation near the river. Most include heating, kitchenettes, and bedding—ideal for families preferring established accommodation over caravans.
Are there pet-friendly holiday parks in Shoalhaven?
Holiday Haven parks and Nowra Riverside Tourist Park welcome pets in designated areas. Most allow dogs on-site with restrictions—they must be contained, non-aggressive, and cleaned up after. Some cabins are pet-friendly while others prohibit animals. Confirm specific pet policies before booking, as regulations vary between locations and seasonal periods.
Which holiday park offers the best value in Shoalhaven?
Nowra Riverside Tourist Park typically provides competitive rates for families, especially during off-peak periods. Holiday Haven parks offer various pricing tiers depending on season and accommodation type. Budget-friendly options include powered sites and basic cabins. Mid-week and school-term visits offer better value than peak season holidays. Compare nightly rates and included facilities before deciding.
What's the best area to stay in Shoalhaven for families?
Culburra Beach suits families seeking beach activities and patrolled swimming. Shoalhaven Heads offers scenic beauty with coastal walks suitable for older kids. Currarong provides quieter, more isolated experiences for adventurous families. Nowra suits those preferring river activities and inland exploration. Choose based on your family's activity preferences and children's ages and interests.
What can families do with kids in Shoalhaven?
Shoalhaven offers beach swimming, rock pool exploring, bush walking, and river kayaking. Nowra has playgrounds, gardens, and the Nowra Bridge for photos. Culburra and Shoalhaven Heads feature coastal villages with fish and chips shops, ice cream parlors, and local markets. Jervis Bay day trips showcase dolphins and seals. Parks often organize kids' activities during school holidays too.
