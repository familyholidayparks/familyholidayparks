Local Knowledge

G'day! The Shoalhaven region is an absolute cracker for family holidays, stretching from the white sands of Jervis Bay down to the relaxed riverside towns near Nowra, with holiday parks that put you within minutes of both pristine beaches and the stunning Shoalhaven River. We've found late spring (November) hits the sweet spot – the water's warming up, school crowds haven't arrived yet, and you'll snag better rates before the Christmas rush. Here's a local tip that's saved us countless times: if you're planning beach days at Huskisson or Vincentia, grab your morning coffee and provisions at the Huskisson bakery before 9am, because once the day-trippers arrive from Canberra (it's only a 2.5-hour drive), you'll be queuing out the door. The parks here range from bushland hideaways perfect for exploring to beachfront spots where the kids can be in the water before you've finished your first cuppa.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the best time to visit Shoalhaven NSW with kids?

G'day! Spring (September-November) and autumn (March-May) are absolutely ripper times to visit with little ones. You'll enjoy mild weather, fewer crowds, and cheaper accommodation than peak summer. That said, summer's magic for beach days! Just book early for school holidays. Winter's lovely too – perfect for whale watching at Jervis Bay and cosy campfires without the humidity.

Are there pet-friendly holiday parks in Shoalhaven NSW?

You bet! Many Shoalhaven holiday parks welcome your furry mates. Places like BIG4 Easts Jindabyne and several parks around Huskisson offer pet-friendly cabins and sites. Always check specific pet policies when booking – some have size restrictions or designated areas. Remember to bring vaccination records, and keep dogs on leads. Your four-legged family member will love exploring the region too!

How much do holiday parks cost in Shoalhaven NSW during school holidays?

School holiday prices typically range from $150-400+ per night depending on accommodation type. Powered sites start around $60-100, while family cabins run $150-300+. Peak summer holidays (Christmas-January) are priciest. Pro tip: book 6-12 months ahead for better rates and availability. Off-peak visits can save you 30-50%! Midweek stays often cheaper than weekends too.

What should I pack for a holiday park stay with young kids?

Essential packing list: swimmers, sunscreen (SPF50+), hats, insect repellent, and a first aid kit. Bring beach toys, bikes or scooters for getting around. Pack your own sheets/towels to save rental fees. Don't forget favourite snacks, outdoor games, and torches for night adventures. Most parks have BBQs, but BYO cooking basics. Rain jacket just in case!

What is there to do near Shoalhaven NSW holiday parks for families?

You're spoilt for choice! Jervis Bay's pristine beaches are perfect for little swimmers and snorkelling. Visit Kiama's blowhole, pat kangaroos at Pebbly Beach, or explore rock pools at Hyams Beach. Try the Minnamurra Rainforest boardwalk, Berry's lolly shops, or dolphin watching cruises. Many parks have pools, jumping pillows, and playgrounds onsite too. Never a dull moment!
