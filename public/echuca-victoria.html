Local Knowledge

G'day! Echuca sits right on the Murray River where Victoria meets New South Wales, and it's absolutely brilliant for families who love messing about on the water and soaking up a bit of Aussie history. The paddle steamers chugging past your campsite, the river red gums perfect for climbing, and those gloriously warm summer evenings make this one of my favourite spots for a proper family getaway – though I'd actually recommend visiting in autumn (March to May) when the crowds thin out, the temps are spot-on for exploring, and the kids can still swim without the January madness. Here's a local tip: grab your fishing licenses at the Bridge General Store before you head to the parks, because the redfin and Murray cod are biting year-round, and there's nothing quite like cooking up your own catch at the campsite BBQ.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the best accommodation in Echuca for families with kids?

NRMA Echuca Holiday Park and Discovery Parks - Echuca are both fantastic for families, with pools, playgrounds and easy river access. Rich River Tourist Park offers brilliant waterfront sites where kids can spot paddle steamers cruising past. For something quieter, Rotary Park suits self-contained families perfectly. All are close to the historic port precinct, making evening strolls to watch the boats incredibly easy.

Where should families stay in Echuca?

Discovery Parks and NRMA Echuca Holiday Park are top choices with excellent facilities including jumping pillows and heated pools. Rich River Tourist Park positions you right on the Murray River for stunning water views. Riverlander Caravan Park offers great value with spacious sites, while Rotary Park provides a peaceful base for families with caravans or RVs exploring the region.

What's the best holiday park in Echuca Moama?

NRMA Echuca Holiday Park consistently rates highest with families thanks to its jumping pillow, heated pool and proximity to town. Discovery Parks offers similar facilities with fantastic kids' entertainment during school holidays. Rich River Tourist Park can't be beaten for location—right on the Murray with paddle steamers drifting past your cabin. Visit during autumn for fewer crowds and gorgeous weather.

What's the best caravan park in Echuca?

Rich River Tourist Park and Riverlander Caravan Park both offer excellent powered sites with river views. NRMA Echuca Holiday Park provides top-notch amenities including a camp kitchen and modern facilities. For a quieter stay with easy Murray River access, Rotary Park welcomes self-contained caravans and RVs. All parks position you brilliantly for exploring the historic port and paddle steamer attractions.

What activities are there for kids in Echuca?

Paddle steamer cruises are magical for children, especially aboard the PS Emmylou. The Port of Echuca Discovery Centre brings history alive with interactive displays. Kids love exploring the old wharf precinct, cycling along the Murray riverbank, and swimming at Victoria Park Lake. Many holiday parks offer bike hire, making it easy to explore the riverside trails and spot river red gum wildlife.

What entertainment is there for kids in Echuca?

During school holidays, Discovery Parks and NRMA Echuca Holiday Park run kids' clubs with activities and movie nights. The historic port offers street entertainment and paddle steamer demonstrations. Victoria Park Lake has an excellent splash park for hot days. Several parks feature jumping pillows and playgrounds. Echuca's old-fashioned lolly shops and ice-creameries provide sweet entertainment after exploring the riverfront.

Are there good parks for kids in Echuca?

Victoria Park features brilliant playgrounds, BBQ areas and the fantastic splash park—perfect for cooling off. Rotary Park on the riverbank offers shaded picnic spots under river red gums. Most holiday parks including NRMA and Discovery Parks have their own playgrounds and recreation areas. The historic port precinct itself becomes an adventure playground where kids explore old wharves and imagine steamboat days.
