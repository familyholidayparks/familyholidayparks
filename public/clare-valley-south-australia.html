If We Were Booking...

  • The family choice: We'd choose Discovery Parks Clare without hesitation, the only park in the valley with genuinely comprehensive kids' facilities, including a splash park, pump track and organised activities.
  • Our tip: Hire bikes and ride a section of the Riesling Trail as a family. It's flat, safe and links directly to several cellar doors with lawns, so you can combine a family bike ride with a stop for the adults to taste.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the Clare Valley good for families?
With the right park choice, yes. Discovery Parks Clare has genuinely strong kids facilities, and the Riesling Trail offers an excellent family cycling activity.
Which Clare Valley park is best for kids?
Discovery Parks Clare, with a jumping pillow, pump track, playground, organised activities and two pools including a splash park with slides.
What is the Riesling Trail?
A flat, sealed walking and cycling path running the length of the Clare Valley, connecting several towns and wineries, an excellent family activity.
Are the other Clare Valley parks suitable for families?
Most lean toward adult wine tourists with minimal kids facilities, so Discovery Parks Clare is the clear choice for families with children.
Are the wineries family friendly?
Many have lawns and grounds where kids can play while parents enjoy a tasting.
What are the best free family activities?
Riding sections of the Riesling Trail, and exploring the valley's small towns.
When is the best time to visit?
Spring and autumn for the mildest weather and best vineyard scenery.
What should families do when it rains?
Discovery Parks Clare's indoor facilities cover a wet day.
How far is the Clare Valley from Adelaide?
About 1.5 hours drive north, an easy weekend wine region trip.
How far ahead should you book?
Generally manageable outside major regional wine festival weekends.
