Frequently Asked Questions

What's the best time to visit Eyre Peninsula SA with kids?
G'day! The best time to visit Eyre Peninsula with young ones is during autumn (March-May) or spring (September-November). You'll get mild weather perfect for beach days and exploring, plus you'll dodge the summer crowds and scorching heat. Winter's still lovely too, especially for whale watching at Head of Bight! School holidays are popular but book early, mate.
Are there pet friendly holiday parks in Eyre Peninsula SA?
Yeah, there are a few ripper pet-friendly holiday parks around Eyre Peninsula! Many parks welcome your furry family members, but you'll need to book pet-friendly cabins specifically and keep dogs on leads. Always ring ahead to confirm their pet policy and any extra fees. Some beaches allow dogs too, so your pooch can enjoy the holiday!
How much do holiday parks cost in Eyre Peninsula SA during school holidays?
During school holidays, expect to pay around $150-$300 per night for a family cabin, depending on location and facilities. Powered sites for caravans run about $50-$80. Port Lincoln and Coffin Bay are pricier, while smaller towns offer better value. Booking a few months ahead saves you money and stress, especially for peak times like Christmas!
What should I pack for a holiday park stay with young kids?
Pack the essentials, mate! Bring swimmers, sun hats, reef shoes for rocky beaches, and loads of sunscreen. Don't forget beach toys, a portable high chair if needed, and bedding (check if provided). Kids' bikes or scooters are great for exploring. Pack a first aid kit, insect repellent, and some easy meals for tired nights in.
What is there to do near Eyre Peninsula SA holiday parks for families?
Heaps of family fun awaits! Swim with sea lions at Baird Bay, explore rock pools, visit the awesome playgrounds, or try fishing off the jetties. Kids love feeding the stingrays at Port Lincoln and spotting dolphins. Many parks have playgrounds and jumping pillows. Beach walks, national parks, and fish and chip dinners by the water are bonza too!
