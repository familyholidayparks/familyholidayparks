If We Were Booking...

  • Facilities and fishing: We'd choose Port Lincoln Caravan Park or Port Lincoln Tourist Park for pools alongside jetty fishing and safe beach swimming.
  • Natural setting: We'd choose Discovery Parks Coffin Bay for a genuinely beautiful coastal setting suited to families comfortable with more basic facilities.
  • Our tip: Do an oyster farm tour at Coffin Bay, it's a genuinely interesting, hands-on activity, and even fussy eaters usually enjoy seeing where the local seafood industry actually operates.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the Eyre Peninsula good for families?
Yes, particularly for families interested in fishing, seafood and quiet, uncrowded beaches, though the region requires significant driving given its size.
Which Eyre Peninsula park is best for families?
Port Lincoln Caravan Park or Port Lincoln Tourist Park, both with pools, jetty fishing and safe swimming beaches.
What is Coffin Bay known for?
Its oyster industry, with farm tours available, and a genuinely beautiful, less crowded coastal setting.
How much driving is involved on the Eyre Peninsula?
Considerable; the region is large and spread out, so plan for substantial travel time between towns.
Are the beaches safe for young children?
Many of the sheltered bay beaches are calm and suitable for family swimming; check local conditions for specific beaches.
What are the best free family activities?
Jetty fishing, beach walks, and wildlife spotting along the coastline.
When is the best time to visit?
Spring through autumn for the mildest weather.
What should families do when it rains?
Options are limited in this outdoor-focused region; the Port Lincoln parks' indoor facilities offer some cover.
How far is the Eyre Peninsula from Adelaide?
Port Lincoln, the main hub, is about 7 hours drive around the gulf, or a short flight.
How far ahead should you book?
Generally manageable outside peak summer school holidays given the region's size and relatively lower crowds.
