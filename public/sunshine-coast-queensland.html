The Sunshine Coast Holiday Park Scene

# Sunshine Coast Holiday Parks Destination Summary

The Sunshine Coast has carved out a unique niche in Queensland's holiday park scene, sitting right in that sweet spot between genuine beachside living and laid-back regional charm. What sets this stretch apart is the access you get—within an hour's drive you're hitting proper sandy beaches at Caloundra and Mooloolaba, cruising up the Maroochy and Noosa rivers, or slipping into the hinterland rainforest. The parks here aren't trying to be theme parks; they're built for people who actually know what they want from a holiday. BIG4 Caloundra sits on beachfront land that's genuinely hard to come by these days, while Ingenia Holidays Rivershore gives you that riverside living without the Melbourne price tag. Alex Beach and the smaller parks scattered through Maroochy River cater to different crowds—caravan enthusiasts after basic no-fuss sites, families wanting cabin comfort, motorhome travellers who've learned to avoid the Nerang Valley gridlock by heading north.

The demographic staying here tells you everything about why these parks work. You've got serious caravan and motorhome owners who've been coming for 15-20 years, folk who've mapped out every decent spot between here and Hervey Bay and keep coming back because the sites are proper sized and the locals aren't resentful. Family groups book the school holidays hard—Easter and winter breaks fill up months in advance because the weather's decent and kids can actually run around without supervision. But here's the thing that catches people: the repeat visitor rate is genuinely high. Parents bring kids who are now bringing their own kids back. It's not Instagram-famous, which is exactly why people keep coming.

The activity and access point is what really locks people in. From Caloundra you can walk to the beach or launch a tinny into the estuary before breakfast. Rivershore puts you on the water with actual fishing happening yards from the vans. The hinterland's 20 minutes inland if you want to lose the sand and crowds, and Noosa's close enough for a day trip without the peak-season madness. School holiday periods see bookings locked in 12-18 months ahead, but shoulder seasons—April, September, October—are when the place actually shines. It's warmer than Victoria, less touristy than the Gold Coast, and the parks here have figured out how to run sites properly without treating guests like they're at a theme park. That's why families return: it works, the kids are safe, and honestly, nobody's trying too hard.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the best family holiday parks on the Sunshine Coast?
BIG4 Caloundra, Ingenia Holidays Rivershore, Alex Beach Cabins and Tourist Park, and BIG4 Maroochy River are top choices. Each offers different strengths—BIG4 parks provide extensive facilities and activities, Ingenia Holidays focuses on riverside relaxation, while Alex Beach combines beach access with affordable options. Compare amenities like pools, playgrounds, and entertainment programs to match your family's needs.
Which caravan park on the Sunshine Coast is best for kids?
BIG4 Caloundra and BIG4 Maroochy River lead with dedicated kids' clubs, heated pools, and daily activities during school holidays. Both parks offer playground facilities and family-friendly entertainment. Ingenia Holidays Rivershore provides a quieter alternative with nature-based activities. Choose BIG4 if your kids enjoy structured programs, or Ingenia for a more relaxed pace.
Which holiday parks near Sunshine Coast have pools?
All four parks feature pools—BIG4 Caloundra and BIG4 Maroochy River have heated resort-style pools with water features. Ingenia Holidays Rivershore offers a saltwater pool. Alex Beach Cabins has a standard pool. BIG4 parks are ideal if pool activities are central to your holiday, offering lap swimming and splash zones for younger children.
What holiday parks are closest to Sunshine Coast beaches?
Alex Beach Cabins and Tourist Park offers direct beach access—you can walk to the patrolled beach in minutes. BIG4 Caloundra is within walking distance of Caloundra Beach. BIG4 Maroochy River and Ingenia Holidays Rivershore are slightly inland but close to river and beach access points. Alex Beach is unbeatable for beach lovers wanting minimal travel.
Where can I find powered sites in Sunshine Coast holiday parks?
All four parks offer powered caravan sites with varying amenities. BIG4 parks provide premium powered sites with full hookups. Ingenia Holidays Rivershore offers riverside powered sites. Alex Beach Cabins has powered options alongside cabins. Book early during school holidays, as powered sites fill quickly. Check specific amp ratings before booking if you need high-power appliances.
What cabins are available at Sunshine Coast holiday parks?
Alex Beach Cabins offers the widest cabin range, from budget to family options. BIG4 parks feature luxury cabins and self-contained villas with ensuite bathrooms. Ingenia Holidays Rivershore provides modern cabins with river views. Cabin quality and pricing vary significantly—Alex Beach suits budget-conscious families, while BIG4 offers premium experiences. Book 2-3 months ahead for school holidays.
Are there pet-friendly holiday parks on the Sunshine Coast?
BIG4 Caloundra and BIG4 Maroochy River accept pets in designated areas with extra fees. Alex Beach Cabins allows pets in select cabins. Ingenia Holidays Rivershore has limited pet-friendly accommodation. Check specific pet policies, size restrictions, and additional costs when booking. Always confirm your pet type is accepted, as some parks restrict certain breeds or sizes.
What offers the best value holiday park on the Sunshine Coast?
Alex Beach Cabins and Tourist Park provides competitive pricing for families, especially off-peak. BIG4 parks offer better value during shoulder seasons than school holidays. Ingenia Holidays Rivershore suits couples or small families seeking peaceful riverside stays at moderate rates. Compare nightly rates across seasons—mid-week stays offer significant savings across all parks year-round.
What's the best area to stay on the Sunshine Coast for families?
Caloundra suits families wanting beach proximity and patrolled swimming. Maroochy River appeals to those preferring quieter river settings with nature walks. Alex Beach offers affordable beachside family holidays with straightforward amenities. Rivershore provides peaceful riverside relaxation away from crowds. Your choice depends on whether you prioritize beaches, activities, quietness, or budget—each location has distinct advantages.
What rainy day activities are available at Sunshine Coast holiday parks?
BIG4 parks offer indoor entertainment centers, kids' clubs, and movie nights during wet weather. Most parks have covered communal areas and board games. Nearby, visit Sundale Markets, indoor play centers, or the Sunshine Coast Hinterland. Museums and shopping centers in Caloundra and Maroochydore provide alternatives. Pack indoor activities and check what rainy-day programs each park offers during your planned visit.
