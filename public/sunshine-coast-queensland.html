Local Knowledge

G'day! The Sunshine Coast is where we head when we want that perfect mix of patrolled beaches the kids can actually swim at, proper hinterland adventures up in the Glass House Mountains, and holiday parks where you're never more than 10 minutes from a decent coffee. We reckon April through October is your sweet spot – the stingers stay away, the humidity drops off, and you'll dodge those January school holiday crowds that pack out places like Mooloolaba and Noosa. Here's a local tip: if you're staying anywhere between Caloundra and Coolum, grab your fresh prawns and fish straight off the trawlers at Mooloolaba Wharf around 8am on weekday mornings – the kids love watching the boats come in, and you'll have the freshest seafood barbecue back at your park that night.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the best accommodation on the Sunshine Coast for families with kids?

The Sunshine Coast offers brilliant family-friendly holiday parks close to patrolled beaches and attractions. BIG4 Caloundra and Dicky Beach Family Holiday Park are favourites for their beachside locations, while Ingenia Holidays Rivershore suits families wanting waterfront calm. Alex Beach Cabins and BIG4 Maroochy River offer excellent facilities with jumping pillows and pools. Most parks put you within 10 minutes of cafes and beaches – the perfect family holiday setup.

Where can you go camping on the Sunshine Coast with kids?

Family camping on the Sunshine Coast is best at established holiday parks with facilities kids need. BIG4 Maroochy River and Alex Beach Cabins Tourist Park offer powered sites with clean amenities blocks and playgrounds nearby. Dicky Beach Family Holiday Park puts you steps from a patrolled beach. These parks beat bush camping with young ones – you'll have hot showers, camp kitchens, and other families around. Book ahead for school holidays.

What family accommodation is available on the Sunshine Coast?

Holiday parks dominate the Sunshine Coast family accommodation scene, offering everything from ensuite cabins to camping sites. Top picks include BIG4 Caloundra with its water park, Ingenia Holidays Rivershore for riverside relaxation, and Dicky Beach Family Holiday Park for beachfront convenience. Most feature jumping pillows, pools, and games rooms. The beauty is being close to beaches, hinterland walks, and towns like Mooloolaba – without resort prices.

What's the best caravan park on the Sunshine Coast?

BIG4 Caloundra consistently tops the list with its water slides and prime location near beaches and Bulcock Street cafes. Ingenia Holidays Rivershore offers peaceful river frontage, while BIG4 Maroochy River suits families wanting space and facilities. For beachside simplicity, Dicky Beach Family Holiday Park can't be beaten – you're literally across the road from patrolled surf. Each park has its charm depending on what your family needs.

What are the best kids activities on the Sunshine Coast?

The patrolled beaches at Mooloolaba, Caloundra, and Coolum are perfect for safe swimming and sandcastles. Australia Zoo is a must-do, while the Glass House Mountains offer bushwalks suited to little legs. SEA LIFE Sunshine Coast and Aussie World theme park keep kids entertained. Don't miss the Eumundi Markets on weekends, rock pool exploring at Kings Beach, or paddling at Currimundi Lake – calmer water than the ocean for younger swimmers.

What are the top kids attractions on the Sunshine Coast?

Australia Zoo leads the pack for wildlife encounters the kids will remember forever. SEA LIFE Sunshine Coast in Mooloolaba offers close-up marine experiences, while Aussie World at Palmview delivers old-school theme park fun without the Gold Coast chaos. The beaches themselves are attractions – Kings Beach has rock pools and a salt water pool. Underwater World and the Glass House Mountains lookouts round out the family-friendly highlights within easy driving distance.

Where can I find kids therapy services on the Sunshine Coast?

The Sunshine Coast has excellent paediatric therapy services across Caloundra, Maroochydore, and Noosa. Speech pathology, occupational therapy, and physiotherapy services are available through private clinics and community health centres. Sunshine Coast Hospital and Health Service coordinates many programs. For visiting families needing appointments, booking ahead is essential, particularly during school holidays when local practitioners fill quickly. Your GP can provide referrals to qualified local practitioners.
