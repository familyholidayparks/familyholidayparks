Local Knowledge

G'day! Perth's holiday parks are an absolute ripper for families, with that unique combo of pristine Indian Ocean beaches, the stunning Swan Valley on your doorstep, and enough space that the kids can actually run around without bumping into crowds. We've found September through November is ideal – the wildflowers are putting on a show, the weather's warm but not scorching, and you'll dodge both the winter rain and the January heat that sends everyone scrambling for aircon. Here's a local tip that'll save you a packet: grab your free transit pass on Sundays and Mondays (yep, it's called the SmartRider Family Funday) and take the kids from your park into the city or down to Fremantle without spending a cent on transport.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the best time to visit Perth WA with kids?

Spring (September-November) is absolutely brilliant for families! You'll get gorgeous weather without the scorching summer heat, perfect for little ones. Autumn (March-May) is also lovely. Summer's great for beach days but can get pretty hot. Winter's mild compared to eastern states, though you might cop some rain. School holidays mean more crowds and higher prices, so consider those shoulder seasons if you can swing it!

Are there pet-friendly holiday parks in Perth WA?

Yeah, there are! Several holiday parks around Perth welcome your furry mates, though it varies by park. Most pet-friendly spots require dogs to be leashed and up-to-date with vaccinations. Some have designated pet-friendly cabins or sites. Always ring ahead to confirm their pet policy and any extra fees. Popular spots include parks in Mandurah and some coastal areas. Your pooch will love exploring WA too!

How much do holiday parks cost in Perth WA during school holidays?

School holiday prices vary heaps depending on location and accommodation type. Expect to pay $150-$350 per night for a cabin sleeping 4-6 people. Powered caravan sites run $50-$80 nightly. Prices jump significantly during peak times like Christmas and Easter. Book early for better deals! Midweek stays and shoulder season visits can save you serious cash. Many parks offer discounts for longer stays too.

What should I pack for a holiday park stay with young kids?

Besides the usual clothes and toiletries, bring outdoor toys, bikes or scooters, swimmers and sun protection (hats, rashies, zinc). Pack a first aid kit, insect repellent, and any comfort items for bedtime. Most cabins have kitchens, so BYO basics if you're cooking. Don't forget chargers, torches, and cards or board games. Beach gear if you're coastal. Oh, and baby wipes—they fix everything!

What is there to do near Perth WA holiday parks for families?

Heaps! Most parks have pools, playgrounds and jumping pillows on-site. Nearby you've got stunning beaches, Rottnest Island (the kids will love the quokkas!), Perth Zoo, Adventure World, and Scitech. The Swan Valley's great for family-friendly farms and chocolate factories. Plenty of national parks for bushwalking, and Fremantle's fantastic for fish and chips by the water. You won't run out of adventures!
