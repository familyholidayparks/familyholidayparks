Local Knowledge

G'day families! The Great Ocean Road stretch between Torquay and Port Campbell is bloody brilliant for a family holiday – you've got those iconic limestone stacks the kids will actually remember, plus rock pools, safe swimming beaches, and koala spotting that doesn't feel forced. We reckon April to May or September to November are your sweet spots: the crowds thin out after school holidays, the weather's still decent for beach days, and accommodation prices drop considerably. Here's an insider tip most visitors miss – stop at the Kennett River General Store early morning (around 8am) to grab your koala-spotting map, then head straight up Grey River Road where the koalas are so close to the walking tracks that even toddlers can spot them without binoculars.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the best time to visit Great Ocean Road VIC with kids?

G'day! The sweet spot is spring (September-November) and autumn (March-May) when the weather's mild and the crowds thin out. Summer's gorgeous but gets packed, especially during school hols. Winter can be magic for whale watching, but pack the warm gear! If you're after quieter beaches and better holiday park availability, shoulder seasons are your best bet, mate.

Are there pet friendly holiday parks in Great Ocean Road VIC?

You're in luck! Plenty of holiday parks along the Great Ocean Road welcome your furry mates. BIG4 Beacon Resort in Queenscliff, Anglesea Family Caravan Park, and Discovery Parks - Torquay all have pet-friendly cabins or sites. Just book ahead and check their specific pet policies – some have breed restrictions or require pets to stay inside. Your pooch will love those beach walks!

How much do holiday parks cost in Great Ocean Road VIC during school holidays?

School holiday rates vary heaps depending on location and accommodation type. Expect to pay $150-$300 per night for a basic cabin, and $250-$500+ for fancier villas near the surf. Powered sites run $50-$100 nightly. Book at least 3-6 months ahead for school hols to snag better rates. Off-peak can be 30-40% cheaper – worth considering if your schedule's flexible!

What should I pack for a holiday park stay with young kids?

Don't stress! Most cabins have kitchens and linen, but double-check when booking. Essentials: swimmers, warm layers (weather's unpredictable!), sun protection, beach toys, and a first-aid kit. Pack your own pillows if kids are fussy. Bring basics like coffee, snacks, and dishwashing liquid – camp shops can be pricey. Oh, and chuck in some games for rainy days!

What is there to do near Great Ocean Road VIC holiday parks for families?

Heaps of ripper activities! The Twelve Apostles are a must-see, plus kid-friendly beaches at Lorne and Apollo Bay. Try the Otway Fly Treetop Walk, spot koalas in Great Otway National Park, or visit Split Point Lighthouse. Many parks have jumping pillows, mini golf, and pools. Torquay's got Surf World Museum, and there are awesome playgrounds scattered throughout coastal towns.
