Local Knowledge

G'day! The Gold Coast hinterland and coastal strips are packed with family holiday parks that put you minutes from theme parks, surf beaches, and rainforest walks – which means the kids can burn energy at Dreamworld in the morning and still have time for an afternoon swim at your park. We reckon September to November is your sweet spot for visiting, when the weather's stunning, the school holiday crowds have thinned out, and accommodation prices drop by nearly half compared to peak season. Here's a local trick: if you're staying anywhere between Oxenford and Coomera, grab your free parking pass from your holiday park – most offer them for Movie World and Wet'n'Wild, which saves you $30-35 per day that's better spent on ice creams and hot chips.

Frequently Asked Questions

Where should families with kids stay on the Gold Coast?

Families love staying at BIG4 Gold Coast Holiday Park or NRMA Treasure Island Holiday Resort, both offering pools, playgrounds, and easy access to theme parks. Tallebudgera Creek Tourist Park is brilliant for little ones who prefer calm creek swimming over ocean waves. Broadwater Tourist Park puts families right near parklands and patrolled beaches, while Ocean Beach Tourist Park suits families wanting direct beach access without the tourist strip crowds.

What's the best accommodation on the Gold Coast for families with kids?

Holiday parks offer the best value and space for families, with options from powered sites to fully-equipped cabins. BIG4 Gold Coast Holiday Park and NRMA Treasure Island Holiday Resort feature jumping pillows, water playgrounds, and games rooms that keep kids entertained at the park. Many parks have camp kitchens and BBQ areas, perfect for budget-conscious families who want to cook their own meals between theme park adventures.

What's the best caravan park on the Gold Coast?

BIG4 Gold Coast Holiday Park consistently tops the list with excellent facilities, spacious sites, and a central location near major attractions. Tallebudgera Creek Tourist Park offers something special with direct creek access and a more relaxed vibe. Broadwater Tourist Park shines for its waterfront position and proximity to Southport's amenities, while Ocean Beach Tourist Park delivers unbeatable ocean views and direct beach access for families who prioritize coastal living.

What's the best caravan park on the Gold Coast for families with kids?

NRMA Treasure Island Holiday Resort takes top honours with its resort-style pools, jumping pillow, and organized kids' activities during school holidays. Tallebudgera Creek Tourist Park is magic for younger children thanks to the calm, shallow creek that's perfect for safe swimming and paddling. BIG4 Gold Coast Holiday Park offers excellent playgrounds and splash parks, plus it's super close to theme parks when the kids inevitably start begging to visit Dreamworld.

Where can I get great photos of kids on the Gold Coast?

The beaches at Tallebudgera Creek create stunning natural shots with kids playing in shallow water backed by lush headlands. Burleigh Headland offers dramatic coastal backdrops, while Currumbin Wildlife Sanctuary provides iconic Aussie animal encounters worth capturing. Many holiday parks have colourful playgrounds and pool areas that photograph beautifully. Early morning at your park's beachfront or golden hour at local lookouts will give the best natural lighting for family snaps.

Several bowling centres on the Gold Coast run kids bowl free promotions, typically during school holidays or specific weekdays when registered online beforehand.
