Frequently Asked Questions

Is the Gold Coast good for families?
The Gold Coast is one of Australia's most popular family holiday destinations thanks to its combination of beaches, theme parks, playgrounds, wildlife attractions and family-friendly holiday parks. Families can choose between beachfront locations, calm creek swimming, nature experiences and resort-style parks with pools and waterparks.
Which Gold Coast holiday parks are closest to Dreamworld?
BIG4 Gold Coast Holiday Park and NRMA Treasure Island Holiday Resort are among the closest family holiday parks to Dreamworld, making them popular choices for families planning to visit the theme parks during their stay.
What is the best area of the Gold Coast for families to stay?
The best area depends on your family's holiday style. Broadwater and Main Beach are close to attractions and playgrounds, Burleigh offers beaches and a relaxed atmosphere, while Tallebudgera Creek is ideal for younger children who love calm water swimming.
Are there family holiday parks near Tallebudgera Creek?
Yes. Tallebudgera Creek Tourist Park is located directly beside one of the Gold Coast's most popular family swimming spots, offering easy access to calm water, paddleboarding and family-friendly beaches.
What are the best free family activities on the Gold Coast?
Popular free family activities include Broadwater Parklands, Burleigh Head National Park, Tallebudgera Creek, Surfers Paradise Beach and the Gold Coast's extensive network of playgrounds, walking tracks and foreshore areas.
Which Gold Coast holiday parks have the best pools and waterparks?
BIG4 Gold Coast Holiday Park is the top-rated option for families wanting waterparks, pools and resort-style facilities. Other highly rated parks also offer heated pools, splash areas and water-based activities.
How far in advance should you book a Gold Coast holiday park?
School holidays, long weekends and summer periods often book out months in advance. Families travelling during peak periods should book as early as possible to secure the best sites, cabins and family-friendly parks.
What is the best beach on the Gold Coast for young children?
Many families prefer Tallebudgera Creek and Currumbin Creek for younger children because of the calmer water conditions. Patrolled beaches such as Burleigh, Kirra and Surfers Paradise are also popular family choices.
Are Gold Coast holiday parks worth it for families?
Holiday parks are often one of the best-value accommodation options for families. Many offer pools, playgrounds, jumping pillows, daily activities and larger spaces for children to play compared with traditional hotels.
What should families do on the Gold Coast when it rains?
Popular wet-weather options include Currumbin Wildlife Sanctuary, Aquaduck Gold Coast, indoor play centres, shopping precincts and selected theme park experiences that run in all weather.
