Frequently Asked Questions

What's the best accommodation in Forster for families with kids?
Forster's family accommodation shines at parks like NRMA Forster Tuncurry and Discovery Parks - Forster, both offering jumping pillows, playgrounds, and easy beach access. Lakeside Forster Holiday Park gives direct lake frontage for safe paddling with littlies. Most parks sit between the lake and ocean, so you're never far from either. Book waterfront cabins during school holidays as they fill fast.
What's the best caravan park in Forster-Tuncurry?
NRMA Forster Tuncurry Holiday Park consistently tops family reviews thanks to its premium facilities and location near One Mile Beach. Discovery Parks - Forster offers brilliant value with great pools and activities. For lakeside stays, Lakeside Forster Holiday Park can't be beaten, while BIG4 Twin Dolphins delivers those classic holiday park vibes with waterslides and entertainment.
What's the best caravan park in Forster?
NRMA Forster Tuncurry leads the pack with top-notch facilities, jumping pillows, and proximity to patrolled beaches. Lakeside Forster Holiday Park suits families wanting calm lake swimming, while Discovery Parks - Forster offers excellent pools and kids' clubs during holidays. Each park has its strengths, so choose based on whether you prefer ocean or lake access.
Where can I find a map of the best caravan parks in Forster-Tuncurry?
The main family parks cluster around three areas: ocean side near One Mile Beach (NRMA, Discovery Parks), lakefront along The Lakes Way (Lakeside Forster), and Tuncurry side (Reflections Tuncurry, BIG4 Twin Dolphins). Check each park's website for exact locations and interactive maps. They're all within ten minutes' drive of each other, making park-hopping for facility use easy.
What activities are available for kids in Forster?
Forster's packed with family fun: calm lake beaches for toddlers, patrolled ocean beaches for bodyboarding, dolphin-watching cruises on Wallis Lake, and the coastal walk to Bennetts Head with rock pools. Grab fish and chips from Wharf Street for lakeside picnics. The rockpool at Pebbly Beach is brilliant for little explorers, and most holiday parks offer kids' clubs during school holidays.

What are the best things to do around Forster with kids?
Beyond the beaches, explore the Wallis Lake foreshore playground, take the coastal Bicentennial Walk with its lighthouse views, and visit Tobwabba Art Gallery. Head south to Booti Booti National Park for bushwalking and pristine Elizabeth Beach. The Tuncurry Rockpool is perfect for safe swimming, and hiring kayaks or stand-up paddleboards on the lake keeps older kids entertained for hours.
What things can we do in Forster with kids this weekend?
Check if Saturday's Forster Farmers Markets are on for fresh treats, then hit the patrolled beaches at One Mile or Main Beach. Book a dolphin cruise on Wallis Lake (kids love spotting them), explore rock pools at low tide, or hire bikes to ride the lakefront paths. Many holiday parks run weekend activities during school holidays, from mini golf tournaments to movie nights.
