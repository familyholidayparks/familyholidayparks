Local Knowledge

G'day! Tasmania's East Coast is an absolute ripper for families, with those impossibly white beaches at Freycinet and the calm, turquoise waters that are actually swimmable (unlike much of mainland Australia). We reckon late December through February gives you the best shot at beach weather, though you'll want to book your park well ahead since half of Melbourne seems to have the same idea. Here's a local tip that'll save you a packet: grab your fresh crayfish directly from the boats at Triabunna or Bicheno in the early arvo – the kids will love watching the catch come in, and you'll pay half what the restaurants charge for the same thing on your barbie that night.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the best family accommodation on Tasmania's East Coast?

Scamander Sanctuary Holiday Park and BIG4 Tassie Getaway Parks St Helens are top picks for families, offering excellent facilities and beach access. Swansea Holiday Park puts you near Freycinet's stunning coastline, while Lakeside Tourist Caravan Park offers a peaceful waterfront setting. Port Sorell Caravan Park up north is brilliant for younger kids with calm swimming conditions. Book well ahead for December-February when availability gets tight.

Is Tasmania's East Coast good for kids?

Absolutely! Tasmania's East Coast is brilliant for families, with swimmable turquoise waters and white sandy beaches that rival anywhere in Australia. The calm bays are perfect for little ones, and there's plenty of wildlife spotting opportunities. Late December through February offers the best beach weather. The region is safe, relaxed, and less crowded than mainland hotspots, making it ideal for family adventures.

What are the best East Coast destinations for families?

Freycinet National Park is the showstopper with Wineglass Bay, though families love the actual swimming beaches like Honeymoon Bay. St Helens offers fantastic fishing and the Bay of Fires' orange-lichen rocks. Swansea provides easy access to beaches and the historic town centre. Scamander's beaches are excellent for kids, while Port Sorell's calm waters suit families with younger children perfectly.

What family activities are there on Tasmania's East Coast?

Beach days are the main attraction, with safe swimming at most bays. Families can explore rock pools, try fishing off jetties, or kayak in calm waters. Freycinet offers shorter walks suitable for kids, while wildlife spotting (wallabies, penguins at dusk) keeps everyone entertained. Many families grab fresh crayfish directly from fishing boats at Triabunna for a memorable seafood feast the kids will love.

What things can you do with kids on Tasmania's East Coast?

Swimming and beach activities dominate most itineraries, with the calm, clear waters perfect for snorkelling and paddling. Visit coastal reserves to spot wildlife, explore tide pools, or take easy coastal walks with stunning views. Fishing from jetties or beaches is popular with older kids. Don't miss picking up fresh seafood from local boats—it's educational and delicious!

What are the best things to do with kids on the East Coast of Tasmania?

The impossibly white beaches at Freycinet and surrounding areas are perfect for swimming, sandcastles, and snorkelling. Wildlife watching is fantastic—spot wallabies, wombats, and Little Penguins returning at dusk. Take family-friendly walks in Freycinet National Park, fish from local jetties, or kayak in sheltered bays. Buying fresh crayfish straight from fishing boats at Triabunna makes for an unforgettable family experience.
