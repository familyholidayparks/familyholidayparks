The Exmouth Holiday Park Scene

Exmouth's holiday parks sit at the gateway to one of Australia's most dramatic coastal landscapes, and that geography shapes everything about staying here. You've got the Ningaloo Reef literally a few minutes' drive away, the North West Cape pushing into the Indian Ocean, and inland you hit proper outback country fast. The three main parks here—Bullara Station Stay, RAC Exmouth Cape Holiday Park, and Yardie Homestead—each tap into different reasons people come, but they all benefit from that same rare combination of accessible wilderness. Bullara is the genuine bush experience, sitting on a working station inland where you wake up to red earth and quiet. RAC Exmouth Cape is your closest option to town and the beach, purpose-built for the caravan and motorhome crowd who want creature comforts within arm's reach of the water. Yardie Homestead slots somewhere between—homestead accommodation with that station feel but closer to the coast and Yardie Creek where the gorge country starts.

The parks here pull different mobs. You get serious grey nomads in their 40-foot rigs at RAC Exmouth Cape from May through September when the weather's perfect and the crowds from down south haven't hit yet. Families pack the places during WA school holidays, particularly the September and December breaks, when every park fills to capacity and you need to book months ahead. But what's distinctive about Exmouth is the repeat visitor rate—people come back year after year, often at the same time, which tells you something about the pull of the place. Bullara and Yardie especially attract people after something slower; blokes who want to fish Exmouth Gulf, families who come for the Ningaloo snorkelling and the space to breathe, and couples doing the long lap who've learned Exmouth rewards a proper stay rather than a night-pass-through.

The real reason families return is straightforward: nowhere else in WA gives you this mix within one trip. You can be snorkelling whale sharks or manta rays at Ningaloo in the morning, walk Yardie Creek's gorge in the afternoon, and sit watching the sunset over the gulf from your caravan. The parks themselves are built around that reality—RAC Exmouth Cape has direct beach access and is geared for families who want the facilities sorted; Bullara and Yardie cater to people after something quieter who'll self-cater and spend their days in the national park or on the water. School holidays see sustained pressure from June through August and again in September, with December chaotic and January-February quieter as families head elsewhere. What keeps people coming back isn't fancy amenities—it's that you can do genuine things here without buggering about. Your kids will remember the rays, the walks, the space. That sticks.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the best family holiday parks in Exmouth, WA?
Exmouth's top family parks include RAC Exmouth Cape Holiday Park with modern facilities and beach proximity, Bullara Station Stay offering unique outback experiences, and Yardie Homestead providing homestead-style accommodation. Each caters to families with different preferences—RAC for convenience, Bullara for adventure, and Yardie for a relaxed homestead atmosphere near scenic Yardie Creek.
Which caravan park in Exmouth is best for kids?
RAC Exmouth Cape Holiday Park is ideal for kids with its swimming pool, playground facilities, and proximity to Exmouth's main beach. The park offers powered sites suitable for family caravans and provides easy access to child-friendly attractions like the Exmouth Aquarium and nearby snorkeling spots.
Are there holiday parks with pools near Exmouth?
RAC Exmouth Cape Holiday Park features an on-site swimming pool, making it the primary choice for families prioritizing water recreation. The pool provides a safe option for younger children and is available year-round, complementing beach activities nearby.
Which holiday parks in Exmouth are near the beach?
RAC Exmouth Cape Holiday Park is positioned closest to Exmouth's main beach, offering direct access for swimming and water sports. Yardie Homestead provides scenic location access to Yardie Creek's picturesque waterfront, though slightly further from town. Both parks position families near ocean activities.
What Exmouth parks have powered sites for caravans?
RAC Exmouth Cape Holiday Park and Bullara Station Stay both offer powered caravan sites with full hook-ups. RAC provides modern amenities in a beachside location, while Bullara Station Stay offers powered sites within an authentic outback station setting suitable for self-contained caravans.
Where can families find cabin accommodation in Exmouth?
All three parks—RAC Exmouth Cape, Bullara Station Stay, and Yardie Homestead—offer cabin options. RAC provides modern cabins near town, Bullara offers station-style accommodation, and Yardie Homestead features homestead cabins. Choose based on preferred setting: beachside convenience, outback adventure, or creek-side relaxation.
Are there pet-friendly holiday parks in Exmouth?
Several Exmouth parks accommodate pets with restrictions. Contact RAC Exmouth Cape Holiday Park, Bullara Station Stay, and Yardie Homestead directly for current pet policies. Most require advance notice and may restrict pets to specific areas or impose additional fees for furry family members.
What's the best value holiday park in Exmouth?
Bullara Station Stay and Yardie Homestead typically offer competitive pricing compared to RAC Exmouth Cape. Bullara provides good value for outback experiences with powered sites and station activities, while Yardie Homestead offers affordable homestead-style stays. Compare seasonal rates and package deals for best savings.
What's the best area to stay in Exmouth for families?
RAC Exmouth Cape Holiday Park's location suits most families—close to town facilities, the beach, and attractions. For families seeking quieter settings, Yardie Homestead offers peaceful creek-side surroundings. Bullara Station Stay appeals to families wanting authentic station experiences slightly outside town with wider open spaces.
What rainy day activities are available in Exmouth for kids?
The Exmouth Aquarium provides engaging indoor exhibits on local marine life. RAC Exmouth Cape's pool and indoor recreation facilities offer alternatives. Indoor shopping and cafes in town provide shelter. Many parks offer board games and indoor facilities, while nearby Ningaloo Reef snorkeling works in most weather conditions.
