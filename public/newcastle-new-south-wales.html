If We Were Booking...

  • Maximum on-site play: We'd choose Swansea Lakeside Holiday Park for the ninja park, splash park and lakefront position, the strongest all-round facilities in the region.
  • Beach and bay choice: We'd choose Reflections Jimmys Beach for the walkable choice between calm bay swimming and the surf beach.
  • Our tip: Base at one of the lake parks and day-trip into Newcastle city for the ocean baths and Fort Scratchley. You get lakeside calm most days with an easy dose of city beach culture whenever you want it.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Newcastle good for families?
Yes, the mix of Lake Macquarie's calm water, patrolled ocean beaches and an easy city day trip makes it a versatile family base.
Which park has the most for kids to do?
Swansea Lakeside Holiday Park, with a ninja park, splash park, pool, putt-putt and a sensory room alongside lake access.
Is Lake Macquarie safe for young children?
Generally yes in the shoreline shallows, and it's a popular spot for kayaking and gentle swimming away from ocean surf and rips.
Which park is best for beach access?
Blacksmiths Beachside or Reflections Jimmys Beach, both walking distance to patrolled beaches.
What is there to do in Newcastle city with kids?
The ocean baths, Fort Scratchley's tunnels and history, and Nobbys Beach lighthouse walk are all popular family stops.
How far are the lake parks from Newcastle city?
About 20 to 30 minutes drive, an easy day trip in either direction.
What are the best free family activities?
Lake Macquarie's foreshore parks, Nobbys Beach breakwall walk, and Fort Scratchley's grounds.
When is the best time to visit?
Year-round given the lake option, though summer brings the best ocean swimming conditions.
What should families do when it rains?
Newcastle Museum and the Civic Theatre precinct offer indoor options within the city.
How far ahead should you book?
Swansea Lakeside books out for school holidays given its facilities reputation, so plan ahead.
