Local Knowledge

G'day! Newcastle and its surrounds have become one of our favourite spots for a family holiday park getaway, and it's easy to see why – you've got those gorgeous patrolled beaches like Merewether and Redhead where the kids can safely splash about, plus you're only a stone's throw from the wineries and wildlife of the Hunter Valley. We reckon the sweet spot for visiting is April through May or September through October, when the weather's still lovely but the school holiday crowds have thinned out and park rates drop considerably. Here's a local tip that's saved us more than once: if you're planning beach days, always check the Surf Life Saving NSW app in the morning, as the flags move around quite a bit depending on the sandbanks, and what's safe at Newcastle Beach mightn't be ideal at Stockton on the same day.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the best caravan park in Newcastle?

Belmont Lakeside Holiday Park consistently tops the list for families, offering easy access to both the lake and ocean beaches. Swansea Lakeside Holiday Park is another cracker, especially if you're after calm water for little ones. Both have excellent facilities and put you within easy reach of Newcastle's beaches and the Hunter Valley attractions.

What's the best caravan park in Newcastle right on the beach?

Blacksmiths Beachside Holiday Park is your go-to for beachfront camping – you're literally steps from the sand at a beautiful patrolled beach. The kids can be surfing or swimming within minutes of leaving your van, and it's got that classic Aussie beach holiday vibe families love.

What's the best caravan park in Newcastle NSW?

For a top family experience, Belmont Lakeside Holiday Park offers the best of both worlds with lake and ocean access nearby. If you're after something quieter, Swansea Lakeside Holiday Park provides excellent facilities with beautiful water views. Both parks have fantastic playgrounds and are close to Newcastle's attractions and Hunter Valley wineries.

What's the best beachfront caravan park in Newcastle NSW?

Blacksmiths Beachside Holiday Park takes the cake for direct beach access in the Newcastle area. The kids can safely swim at the patrolled beach, build sandcastles, and try their hand at surfing. It's also perfectly positioned between Newcastle and the Central Coast for day trips either direction.

Where can I get Newcastle kids' kits?

Newcastle Jets kids' merchandise, including training kits, can be found at the official Newcastle Jets shop at McDonald Jones Stadium or through their online store. Rebel Sport and SportsPower stores around Newcastle also stock junior Jets gear. Check availability during football season for the best range of sizes.

What are the best kids' activities in Newcastle?

The beaches are brilliant – Merewether and Redhead have excellent swimming and surf lessons. Fort Scratchley offers history and harbour views, while Blackbutt Reserve lets the kids meet native wildlife for free. The Newcastle Museum is engaging for all ages, and don't miss the Bathers Way coastal walk with rock pools along the route.

Where can I buy Newcastle Jets kids' training kits?

Official Newcastle Jets junior training kits are available at the Jets' shop at McDonald Jones Stadium in Broadmeadow or online through their website. Local sports stores like Rebel and SportsPower typically stock Jets junior gear during the A-League season. Sizing ranges from toddlers through to teens.
