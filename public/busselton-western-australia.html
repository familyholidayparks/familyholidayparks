Local Knowledge

G'day! Busselton and the greater Geographe Bay region punch well above their weight for family holidays, with that famous jetty stretching nearly 2km into impossibly turquoise water – and yes, the kids will want to ride the little red train to the underwater observatory at the end. We reckon late spring through autumn (October to April) gives you the best crack at the weather, though locals will tell you the secret is visiting in March when the water's warmest and the summer crowds have cleared out. Here's a pro tip: grab fish and chips from Goose on the loose near the foreshore rather than the pricier jetty spots, then let the kids burn off energy at the massive playground right there while you watch the sunset over the bay.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the best kids accommodation in Busselton?

Busselton's sorted for family stays! RAC Busselton Holiday Park and BIG4 Breeze are standouts with playgrounds, pools and cabin options. Sandy Bay Holiday Park puts you right near the beach, while Discovery Parks offers solid facilities for families. All are close enough to the jetty and foreshore that you won't be doing marathon car trips with tired kids.

Where's the best accommodation in Busselton for families with kids?

For families, RAC Busselton Holiday Park and BIG4 Breeze Holiday Parks deliver the goods with jumping pillows, swimming pools and entertainment options. Discovery Parks - Busselton is another cracker choice with family-friendly facilities. Most parks offer everything from powered sites to cabins, so whether you're towing or just want a key-turn option, there's something to suit.

What family accommodation is available in Busselton?

Busselton's loaded with family-friendly parks. RAC Busselton Holiday Park and BIG4 Breeze are top picks with great amenities, while Sandy Bay Holiday Park gives you beachside access. Busselton Lazy Days Caravan Park offers a quieter vibe if that's more your speed. Most have cabin options if you're not into camping, plus pools and playgrounds to keep the tribe entertained.

What's the best caravan park in Busselton?

RAC Busselton Holiday Park consistently tops the list with families, thanks to brilliant facilities and a central location. BIG4 Breeze Holiday Parks runs a close second with quality amenities. Sandy Bay Holiday Park wins if beach access is your priority, while Discovery Parks offers reliable family facilities. Each has its own vibe, so pick based on whether you want beachfront or closer to town.

What are the best kids activities in Busselton?

The Busselton Jetty train ride to the underwater observatory is non-negotiable – kids absolutely love it. The foreshore playgrounds are brilliant, there's safe swimming at the beaches, and the new playground near the jetty is a ripper. Grab fish and chips, hire bikes to cruise the coastal path, or hit up the Busselton Aqua Park for inflatable fun during warmer months.

Is there a kids Ironman in Busselton?

Yep! The Busselton IronKids event runs alongside the famous Ironman in December, giving little legends aged seven to fourteen their own race experience. It's a fantastic atmosphere with different distance options based on age. Even if your kids aren't competing, watching the main Ironman event is pretty inspiring, and the festival vibe around town is brilliant for families.

Where's the best kids playground in Busselton?

The foreshore playground near the jetty is the headline act – modern equipment, shade sails, and ocean views while the kids burn energy. There's also Barnard Park with nature-based play equipment that's worth a look. Most of the holiday parks have their own playgrounds too, but for a proper outing, the jetty foreshore area ticks all boxes with toilets, cafes and picnic spots nearby.
