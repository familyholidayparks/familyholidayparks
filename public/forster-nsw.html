Local Knowledge

G'day! The twin towns of Forster-Tuncurry sit right where Wallis Lake meets the Pacific, giving families that magic combo of calm lake paddling for little ones and proper ocean waves for older kids – all within a five-minute drive of each other. We've found late autumn (April-May) absolutely brilliant for visiting, when the water's still swimmable, school crowds have thinned, and accommodation prices drop nicely. Here's an insider tip: grab fish and chips from Wharf Street and head to the grassy area at the southern end of One Mile Beach for sunset – the kids can run around while you watch dolphins cruising past, and you'll dodge the sandflies that plague the northern picnic spots.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the best time to visit Forster NSW with kids?

G'day! Forster's pretty much brilliant year-round, but if you're after warm beach days, December to March is tops. That said, autumn (March-May) is an absolute ripper - warm enough for swimming, fewer crowds, and you'll dodge the school holiday rush. Winter's still lovely for dolphin watching and bush walks, though the water's a bit chilly. Spring brings perfect weather and whale watching season!

Are there pet-friendly holiday parks in Forster NSW?

You bet! Several holiday parks around Forster welcome your furry mates. Just keep in mind most have specific pet-friendly cabins or sites, so you'll need to book these specifically. There are usually some rules - keeping dogs on leads, cleaning up after them, that sort of thing. Best to ring ahead and check their pet policy, as some parks only allow pets outside peak seasons.

How much do holiday parks cost in Forster NSW during school holidays?

School holiday prices typically range from about $150-$400 per night depending on what you're after. Powered sites for caravans or tents are cheapest ($50-$100), while fully-equipped cabins run $200-$400+. Expect to pay premium rates during Christmas and Easter. Book early though - popular parks fill up months ahead! Off-peak rates are usually 30-40% cheaper if you can swing it.

What should I pack for a holiday park stay with young kids?

Don't stress too much! Definitely bring beach gear, sunscreen (loads of it!), hats, and insect repellent. Pack your own sheets and towels unless you've confirmed they're included. Kids' bikes or scooters are brilliant for getting around parks. Bring basic cooking supplies, but there's usually a Woolies nearby. A first aid kit, night lights, and favourite toys help littlies settle in.

What is there to do near Forster NSW holiday parks for families?

Heaps! The beaches are obviously ace - One Mile Beach is perfect for little ones. Take a dolphin-watching cruise, visit the rock pools at Seal Rocks, or try the easy Wallis Lake foreshore walk. Cape Hawke Lookout has stunning views (bit of a climb though!). Kids love feeding fish at the boat ramp. There's mini golf, the Toboggan Hill Park, and plenty of playgrounds scattered around town.
