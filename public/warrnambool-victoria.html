Frequently Asked Questions

What's the best time to visit Warrnambool VIC with kids?
G'day! Summer (December-February) is brilliant for beach days and whale watching, but it's peak season so book early. We reckon autumn (March-May) is a ripper choice - still warm enough for swimming, fewer crowds, and you might catch whales migrating. Spring's lovely too! Winter can be chilly but perfect for cosy cabin stays and storm watching. School holidays get busy year-round, so plan ahead!
Are there pet-friendly holiday parks in Warrnambool VIC?
Too right there are! Several holiday parks around Warrnambool welcome your furry mates. BIG4 Warrnambool and Discovery Parks are popular pet-friendly options. You'll usually need to book specific pet-friendly cabins or powered sites, and there's often a small extra fee. Always check their pet policy beforehand - most require dogs to be leashed and some have breed restrictions. Your four-legged family members will love it!
How much do holiday parks cost in Warrnambool VIC during school holidays?
School holiday rates definitely jump up! Expect to pay around $150-300+ per night for a family cabin during peak times, compared to $100-200 off-peak. Powered sites for caravans are more budget-friendly at $50-80 per night. Summer holidays (December-January) are priciest. Pro tip: book months ahead for better rates and availability. Mid-week stays are often cheaper than weekends too!
What should I pack for a holiday park stay with young kids?
Don't stress! Most cabins have kitchens and linen, but check first. Pack bathers, towels, sun protection (slip, slop, slap!), and layers for changeable weather. Bring beach toys, bikes, and favourite bedtime books. Essential extras: insect repellent, first aid kit, and snacks for the car. Kids' water shoes are handy for rocky beaches. Oh, and torches for night-time adventures around the park!
What is there to do near Warrnambool VIC holiday parks for families?
You'll be spoilt for choice! The Flagstaff Hill Maritime Village is fantastic for kids, plus there's whale watching (June-September), seal colonies at Logan's Beach, and gorgeous beaches for swimming. Tower Hill Wildlife Reserve offers amazing native animal spotting. The Warrnambool Breakwater is perfect for fishing, and there are brilliant playgrounds throughout town. Most holiday parks have pools and jumping pillows too!
