Frequently Asked Questions

What's the best accommodation in Lakes Entrance for families with kids?
NRMA Eastern Beach Holiday Park and Koonwarra Family Holiday Park are both brilliant choices for families. They're close to the main swimming beaches and footbridge, so kids can burn energy while parents enjoy those gorgeous lake views. The Lakes Entrance Recreation Reserve offers fantastic value if you're camping, with plenty of space for the little ones to roam safely.
Where should families stay in Lakes Entrance?
For families, you can't go past NRMA Eastern Beach Holiday Park or Echo Beach Tourist Park – both offer excellent facilities and easy beach access. North Arm Tourist Park is perfect if you want a quieter spot away from the main strip, while Koonwarra Family Holiday Park lives up to its name with kid-friendly amenities and safe swimming nearby.
What's the best caravan park in Lakes Entrance?
NRMA Eastern Beach Holiday Park consistently tops the list thanks to its prime location near the footbridge and calm lake beaches. Echo Beach Tourist Park and North Arm Tourist Park are also excellent options, offering great facilities and easy access to fishing spots. The Lakes Entrance Recreation Reserve is perfect for families wanting a more budget-friendly camping experience.
What's the best caravan park in Lakes Entrance, Victoria?
NRMA Eastern Beach Holiday Park leads the pack with its unbeatable location and family-friendly facilities. Koonwarra Family Holiday Park and Echo Beach Tourist Park are both cracker choices too, especially during school holidays when you want everything within easy reach. Each park offers something special, from direct beach access to those peaceful lake-front camping spots the kids absolutely love.
What are the best kids activities in Lakes Entrance?
Fishing off the iconic footbridge is a must-do – kids regularly catch flathead and get hooked on the experience! The calm lake beaches are perfect for swimming and kayaking, while feeding the pelicans at the wharf happens daily around 11am and 4pm. Don't miss the Griffiths Sea Shell Museum for a rainy day, and hire paddleboats to explore the waterways at your own pace.
Are there good parks for kids in Lakes Entrance?
The Eastern Beach foreshore has brilliant playgrounds with lake views, perfect for burning energy before dinner. Koonwarra Family Holiday Park and NRMA Eastern Beach Holiday Park both feature excellent play equipment on-site. The Recreation Reserve also has plenty of open space for ball games and bike riding, keeping active kids entertained for hours while parents relax nearby.
What is the Lakes Entrance Convoy for Kids?
The Lakes Entrance Convoy for Kids is a beloved annual charity event where hundreds of decorated trucks parade through town, raising funds for sick children. It's usually held in late October and transforms the town into an absolute spectacle – kids go wild watching the illuminated trucks roll past! Book accommodation early as the town fills up completely during convoy weekend.
What are the best things to do near Lakes Entrance with kids?
Beyond the lakes themselves, take a drive to Buchan Caves for an underground adventure the kids will remember forever. Wyanga Park Winery has beautiful gardens and animal feeding that families love. The Ninety Mile Beach is just minutes away for proper ocean swimming and beachcombing. Raymond Island's koala trail is also a short ferry ride away – spotting wild koalas never gets old!
What are some unique things to do in Lakes Entrance with kids?
Take the ferry to Raymond Island and walk the koala trail – spotting these sleepy locals in the wild beats any zoo visit! Try catching your own dinner from the footbridge, then get it cooked at a local takeaway. Visit Wyanga Park's animal farm and mini golf combo, or book a calm-water boat cruise through the Gippsland Lakes to see dolphins and birdlife up close.
