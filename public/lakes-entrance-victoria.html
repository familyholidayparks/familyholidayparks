The Lakes Entrance Holiday Park Scene

Lakes Entrance has carved out a genuine reputation as one of Victoria's best-kept caravan destinations, and it's not hard to see why once you've spent time here. The town's unique geography—sitting at the junction where the Gippsland Lakes meet the ocean through a narrow entrance—means the holiday parks genuinely offer something different from other coastal spots. You've got beachfront parks like NRMA Eastern Beach where you can literally walk onto the sand, riverside options where the Cunninghame Arm laps against your van, and bush-set alternatives further back that appeal to those wanting quieter surroundings without the sound of waves. The Recreation Reserve sits right in the middle of town action, while Koonwarra over on the western side offers a bit more seclusion. What makes it work is that all these parks are within spitting distance of each other, so you can choose your vibe without leaving the area entirely.

The demographic across these parks tells you something about Lakes Entrance's staying power. You see plenty of grey nomads who've been coming here for decades, many booking the same spot year after year—they know the rhythm of the tides and exactly which carpark gets sun in winter. Families with caravans and motorhomes dominate the school holidays, particularly Christmas and the autumn break, and the parks fill fast during these windows. There's good reason: kids can alternate between safe beach swimming at Eastern Beach and exploring the calm, shallow lakes for a completely different water experience. The river access means proper kayaking and fishing without massive swells, and the hinterland walks through the national park aren't far if you want to stretch legs without crowds.

What keeps people returning isn't just the scenery, though that helps. It's the practical stuff that works here. The fishing is genuinely good, the boat ramp infrastructure is solid, and the town has real services without feeling overdeveloped. Parents know their kids will be occupied between water activities, the adventure playground, and the jetty precinct without needing theme parks or entertainment complexes. The mix of park types also means repeat visitors can try different spots as their circumstances change—upgrading from a tent site to van accommodation, or downsizing from Eastern Beach's busier vibe to the quieter Recreation Reserve once they've settled into the rhythm of the place. It's this combination of accessible lake and ocean, workable park facilities, and genuine town character that explains why you'll see the same caravans and motorhomes rolling back in season after season.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the best family holiday parks in Lakes Entrance?
NRMA Eastern Beach Holiday Park, Koonwarra Family Holiday Park, and Echo Beach Tourist Park are top choices for families visiting Lakes Entrance. Each offers different amenities suited to family stays. NRMA Eastern Beach provides excellent facilities near the beach, Koonwarra specializes in family-friendly activities, and Echo Beach offers a quieter setting. Lakes Entrance Recreation Reserve and Camping Ground is budget-friendly with basic facilities ideal for camping families.
Which caravan park in Lakes Entrance is best for kids?
Koonwarra Family Holiday Park is specifically designed for families with children, offering playgrounds, entertainment programs, and family-oriented facilities. Echo Beach Tourist Park also welcomes families with activities and spacious grounds. Both parks provide safe environments where kids can play while parents relax nearby, making them ideal for family holidays in Lakes Entrance.
Are there holiday parks with pools near Lakes Entrance?
NRMA Eastern Beach Holiday Park features a swimming pool, making it excellent for families. Koonwarra Family Holiday Park also offers pool facilities. These parks provide supervised swimming options during warmer months, perfect for families wanting water activities without needing to visit the beach or lakes directly.
What holiday parks are closest to the beach in Lakes Entrance?
NRMA Eastern Beach Holiday Park is positioned right near Eastern Beach, offering direct beach access. Echo Beach Tourist Park is also beach-adjacent with lake views. Both parks let families walk to swimming and water activities within minutes, ideal for beach-loving families wanting convenient access to Lakes Entrance's main swimming areas.
Which Lakes Entrance parks have powered sites for caravans?
NRMA Eastern Beach Holiday Park offers powered caravan sites with full hookups. Koonwarra Family Holiday Park provides powered sites suitable for caravans and motorhomes. Echo Beach Tourist Park has powered options available. All three parks cater to families wanting electrical connections for comfortable caravan holidays in Lakes Entrance.
What cabin options are available in Lakes Entrance holiday parks?
NRMA Eastern Beach Holiday Park offers cabins ranging from basic to self-contained, accommodating various budgets. Koonwarra Family Holiday Park provides family cabins with modern amenities. Echo Beach Tourist Park has cabin accommodation options. These alternatives to camping appeal to families preferring more comfort while holidaying in Lakes Entrance.
Which holiday parks in Lakes Entrance are pet-friendly?
NRMA Eastern Beach Holiday Park welcomes pets in designated areas, allowing families to bring dogs. Echo Beach Tourist Park also permits pets, making it suitable for families with animals. Lakes Entrance Recreation Reserve and Camping Ground accommodates pets in certain sites. Check specific pet policies and designated pet-friendly areas before booking.
What's the best value holiday park in Lakes Entrance?
Lakes Entrance Recreation Reserve and Camping Ground offers the most affordable accommodation with basic unpowered sites perfect for budget-conscious families. NRMA Eastern Beach Holiday Park provides good mid-range value with facilities justifying costs. Compare nightly rates across all four parks to find deals matching your family's budget and required amenities.
What's the best area to stay in Lakes Entrance for families?
Eastern Beach precinct is ideal for families, hosting NRMA Eastern Beach Holiday Park near swimming areas, playgrounds, and the main town center. This location provides easy access to restaurants, shops, and attractions. Koonwarra and Echo Beach offer quieter alternatives if your family prefers peaceful, nature-focused environments away from busy areas.
What can families do with kids on rainy days in Lakes Entrance?
Visit the Lakes Entrance Visitor Centre, explore local museums, or enjoy indoor dining options. Many parks have communal facilities and entertainment programs. The nearby Ninety Mile Beach offers sheltered spots, and families can visit local shops and markets. Some parks arrange indoor activities during poor weather, so check with your chosen holiday park.
