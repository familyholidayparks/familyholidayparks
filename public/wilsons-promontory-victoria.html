If We Were Booking...

  • Facilities and comfort: We'd choose Toora Tourist Park for the multiple playgrounds, jumping pillow, mini golf and heated indoor pool, a comfortable base for exploring the Prom by day.
  • Inside the national park: We'd choose Tidal River Campground for the genuine wilderness experience, safe river swimming and wildlife, understanding facilities are basic and bookings need to be made well ahead.
  • Our tip: If you can secure a Tidal River booking, take it, waking up inside the national park with wombats wandering through camp is a genuine highlight. If not, Toora's facilities make an excellent alternative base just outside.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Wilsons Promontory good for families?
Yes, it's one of Victoria's best nature-based family destinations, with safe swimming, wildlife encounters and excellent walking tracks.
Should families stay inside or outside the national park?
Tidal River Campground inside the park offers the full wilderness experience but is basic and books out early. Toora Tourist Park outside the park offers stronger facilities as a day-trip base.
How far ahead do you need to book Tidal River?
Extremely early, often many months ahead for peak periods, given its popularity and limited capacity.
Is the swimming safe for kids at Wilsons Prom?
Tidal River and Norman Beach offer relatively safe swimming within the national park; always check current conditions and lifeguard presence.
What wildlife might families see?
Wombats are commonly seen around Tidal River campground, along with various native birds and, with luck, echidnas.
What are the best walks for kids?
Shorter tracks around Tidal River and Norman Beach suit younger children, while longer hikes to Squeaky Beach and beyond suit older, fitter kids.
Does Toora Tourist Park have good facilities?
Yes, it's a genuinely well-equipped park with multiple playgrounds, a jumping pillow, mini golf and a heated indoor pool.
What are the best free family activities?
Wildlife spotting around Tidal River, beach time at Norman Beach, and shorter national park walks.
When is the best time to visit?
Summer for swimming, though the Prom is scenic and walkable year-round with proper preparation.
What should families do when it rains?
Toora Tourist Park's indoor pool and games facilities cover a wet day; options inside the national park itself are more limited.
