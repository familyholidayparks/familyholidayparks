Local Knowledge

G'day! Sydney's holiday parks are absolute gold for families, giving you the rare combo of surf beaches, bushland adventures, and harbour swimming spots all within an hour's drive of the city. We've found April to October is your sweet spot – the parks are quieter after school holidays, the weather's still lovely for outdoor fun, and you'll actually snag a waterfront site without booking six months ahead. Here's an insider tip: if you're staying north of the Harbour Bridge, grab your morning coffee and pastries from the bakeries along Pittwater Road in Mona Vale before heading to the parks – the kids will thank you, and you'll save a fortune compared to kiosk prices.

Frequently Asked Questions

Where's the best place to stay in Sydney with kids?

BIG4 Sydney Lakeside Holiday Park and Discovery Parks - Lane Cove are top choices for families, offering pools, playgrounds, and easy access to both the city and beaches. Both parks provide cabin options if you're not into camping. For something unique, Cockatoo Island Waterfront Campground lets you camp right on the harbour with stunning city views – the kids will love catching the ferry there!

What's the best caravan park in Sydney?

Discovery Parks - Lane Cove takes the crown for most families, tucked into beautiful bushland yet only 10km from the CBD. The park has excellent facilities, a pool, and you can kayak right from the grounds. BIG4 Sydney Lakeside is another ripper choice with more resort-style amenities. Book well ahead for school holidays – these spots fill up fast!

What's the best caravan park close to Sydney CBD?

Cockatoo Island Waterfront Campground is your closest option, sitting right in Sydney Harbour with jaw-dropping city skyline views. You'll catch a ferry from Circular Quay to reach this unique camping spot. For drive-in convenience, Discovery Parks - Lane Cove is only 10km from the CBD, nestled in bushland along the Lane Cove River with proper caravan facilities.

What's the best caravan park between Sydney and Melbourne?

While this question focuses on the route rather than Sydney itself, families often stop at Sheralee Tourist Caravan Park in Camden (southwest Sydney) before heading south. It's a peaceful spot to break up the journey. Camden also has great cafes and The Argyle Pub for a hearty feed before tackling the Hume Highway southbound.
