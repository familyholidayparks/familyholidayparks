If We Were Booking...

  • Northern beaches base: We'd choose BIG4 Sydney Lakeside Holiday Park for proximity to Narrabeen Lagoon and the northern beaches' surf culture, a calmer alternative to staying in the city.
  • City access: We'd choose Sheralee Tourist Caravan Park at Brighton-Le-Sands for straightforward public transport into the CBD without inner-city prices.
  • Our tip: If your kids are old enough for an adventure, book a night at Cockatoo Island. Ferry over, camp under the stars on a harbour island with genuine history, and wake up to a harbour view no hotel room can match.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are there good caravan parks in Sydney?
Options are limited and mostly practical bases rather than resort-style destinations. Choose based on which part of Sydney you want to explore.
Which Sydney park is best for beach access?
BIG4 Sydney Lakeside Holiday Park, near Narrabeen Lagoon and the northern beaches.
Can you get into the Sydney CBD easily from these parks?
Sheralee at Brighton-Le-Sands has good public transport access to the city. Discovery Lane Cove also connects via train.
What is Cockatoo Island?
A World Heritage-listed island in Sydney Harbour with a waterfront campground, reached by ferry, with genuine convict and industrial history to explore.
Do any Sydney parks have pools?
Most don't have significant pool facilities; this is a region where families rely on Sydney's beaches and harbour pools rather than on-site water play.
Is Sydney expensive for family accommodation?
Caravan parks are one of the more affordable options compared to hotels, though sites still price at a premium compared to regional NSW.
What are the best free family activities?
Sydney's beaches, harbour foreshore walks, and many of the city's parks and playgrounds cost nothing.
How far ahead should you book?
Sydney's limited caravan park supply means booking well ahead for school holidays is essential.
What should families do when it rains?
Sydney has extensive indoor options, museums, aquariums and shopping centres, regardless of which park you're based at.
Is it better to stay in Sydney or a nearby region?
Many families base at the Central Coast or Royal National Park area and day-trip into Sydney, given the limited in-city caravan park options.
