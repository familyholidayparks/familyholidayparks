If We Were Booking...

  • Practical family base: We'd choose Sunset Beach Holiday Park for the good playground with age-appropriate equipment and easy beach access.
  • Quieter setting: We'd choose Drummond Cove Holiday Park for a more natural coastal setting and a crystal-clear pool, accepting a short drive to town.
  • Our tip: Visit the HMAS Sydney II Memorial, it's a moving tribute to one of Australia's greatest naval losses and genuinely engages older kids interested in history, even if younger children find it more sobering than exciting.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Geraldton good for families?
Yes, particularly for beach time and some engaging maritime history, though facilities are generally more modest than the bigger coastal resort towns.
Which Geraldton park is best for kids?
Sunset Beach Holiday Park, with a good playground suited to different ages and easy beach access.
Why is Geraldton known for wind?
It sits in a consistently windy corridor, popular with windsurfers and kitesurfers, which can also mean breezy beach days for families.
What is the HMAS Sydney II Memorial?
A memorial and museum commemorating the loss of HMAS Sydney II in WWII, a significant and moving piece of Australian naval history.
Are Geraldton's beaches safe for swimming?
Many are, though check conditions given the region's strong winds, which can affect swimming comfort on some days.
What are the best free family activities?
The HMAS Sydney II Memorial, and walks along the Geraldton foreshore.
When is the best time to visit?
Spring through autumn for milder conditions, keeping in mind the region's winds are a near-constant feature.
What should families do when it rains?
The Museum of Geraldton offers a good indoor option regardless of weather.
How far is Geraldton from Perth?
About 4 hours drive north, often a stop on a longer WA coastal road trip.
How far ahead should you book?
Generally straightforward outside major school holiday periods.
