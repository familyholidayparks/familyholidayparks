If We Were Booking...

  • The Coles Bay base: We'd choose BIG4 Iluka on Freycinet, the only holiday park at the national park's doorstep, walkable to the store and Muirs Beach, with a playground for downtime between walks.
  • Booked out? We'd stay at Bicheno, 30 minutes north, and day-trip in. Bicheno adds its own penguin tours and blowhole, which almost makes missing out on Coles Bay worth it.
  • Our tip: Do the Wineglass Bay lookout at 8am. You'll have the view largely to yourself, the car park still has spaces and the kids climb better before the heat. Honeymoon Bay afterwards is the reward swim.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can kids do the Wineglass Bay walk?
The lookout walk, yes. It's 60 to 90 minutes return with a steady uphill, and school-aged kids handle it well. The full descent to the beach doubles the effort, save it for older kids.
Where should families stay at Freycinet?
BIG4 Iluka at Coles Bay is the only holiday park at the national park's edge, with a playground and walkable access to the general store and Muirs Beach.
How far ahead should you book?
For summer, many months, it's one of Tasmania's tightest bookings. Shoulder seasons are easier and the park is arguably better in autumn light.
Where can young kids swim?
Honeymoon Bay's sheltered granite coves are calm, shallow and the best easy snorkelling. Muirs Beach at Coles Bay is the everyday option. The water is brisk year-round, wetsuits help.
Do you need a parks pass?
Yes, Tasmanian national parks require a vehicle pass. A holiday pass covers your whole Tasmanian trip and pays for itself quickly.
What wildlife will kids see?
Wallabies around the campground and car parks, often too friendly, plus sea eagles, dolphins offshore and, on night drives, wombats and possums. Don't feed the wallabies.
Is there food at Coles Bay?
A general store and a couple of eateries covering basics. Stock up properly in Bicheno or Swansea before you arrive.
What else is near Freycinet for families?
Bicheno's blowhole and penguin tours 30 minutes north, the Friendly Beaches for wild empty sand, and Kate's Berry Farm near Swansea for the drive-day treat.
What should families do when it rains?
Short showers pass quickly on the east coast. The lookout walk is fine in drizzle, Bicheno's aquarium covers a wet morning, and cabin board games handle the rest.
When is the best time to visit?
December to March for swimming, but October, November and April bring quieter tracks, easier bookings and softer light on the pink granite.
