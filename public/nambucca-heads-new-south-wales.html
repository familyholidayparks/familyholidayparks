If We Were Booking...

  • Lagoon and facilities: We'd choose Ingenia Holidays Nambucca Heads for the safe lagoon swimming, pool, water play area and kayaking, all in one spot.
  • Kids club and activities: We'd choose BIG4 Tasman Holiday Parks Nambucca Heads for the Goanna Cave kids zone and school holiday activity programs.
  • Our tip: Walk the V-Wall breakwater and let the kids read (and maybe add to) decades of colourful graffiti art left by visitors. It's a completely free, genuinely fun half-hour that most families stumble onto and love.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Nambucca Heads good for families?
Yes, the combination of a safe lagoon, strong entertainment-focused parks and the unique V-Wall art makes it a well-rounded family stop.
Which Nambucca Heads park is best for kids?
Ingenia Holidays Nambucca Heads for the safe lagoon and water play area, or BIG4 Tasman for its Goanna Cave kids zone and activity programs.
What is the V-Wall?
A breakwater at the river mouth covered in colourful graffiti art added by visitors over many years, a free and genuinely fun attraction.
Is the lagoon safe for young children?
Yes, it's specifically noted as a safe swimming lagoon, calmer than the open ocean.
Can you paddle or kayak in Nambucca Heads?
Yes, the river and lagoon offer good conditions for kayaking, and some parks provide access or hire.
What are the best free family activities?
The V-Wall breakwater walk, the main beach, and the river foreshore walking tracks.
When is the best time to visit?
School holidays for full activity programs at the entertainment-focused parks, or shoulder season for a quieter visit.
What should families do when it rains?
The Goanna Cave and other indoor play areas at BIG4 Tasman cover a wet day.
How far is Nambucca Heads from Coffs Harbour?
About 30 to 40 minutes south, an easy addition to a mid-north coast trip.
How far ahead should you book?
The entertainment-focused parks book out for school holidays, so plan ahead for peak periods.
