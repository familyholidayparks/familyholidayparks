Frequently Asked Questions

What's the best family accommodation in Cairns with kids?
BIG4 Ingenia Holidays Cairns Coconut is a cracker for families, with pools and jumping pillows to keep the littlies entertained. Lake Placid Rainforest Retreat offers a quieter rainforest vibe if you're after something more peaceful. Crystal Cascades Holiday Park puts you near gorgeous swimming holes, while Tasman Holiday Parks - Cairns Cool Waters has fantastic facilities. Book ahead during May to October – that's peak family season up north.
Which is the best caravan park in Cairns?
Fishery Falls Cairns Holiday Park is brilliant for families wanting that tropical feel without breaking the bank. BIG4 Ingenia Holidays Cairns Coconut offers top-notch facilities and jumping pillows the kids will love. For something special, Lake Placid Rainforest Retreat nestles you amongst the rainforest with walking tracks right on your doorstep. All three make excellent bases for exploring the reef and rainforest.
What's the best holiday park in Queensland for families?
Queensland's spoilt for choice, but Cairns parks like BIG4 Ingenia Holidays Cairns Coconut really deliver the tropical north experience – reef trips in the morning, rainforest adventures by arvo. The May to October window gives you stinger-free swimming and perfect weather. Combine world-class natural attractions with excellent park facilities, and Cairns holiday parks are tough to beat for families wanting that quintessential Queensland adventure.
Which is the best caravan park in Queensland?
For sheer natural beauty and adventure, Cairns caravan parks are hard to beat. Lake Placid Rainforest Retreat offers rainforest immersion, while Crystal Cascades Holiday Park puts you near stunning swimming holes. BIG4 Ingenia Holidays Cairns Coconut combines excellent facilities with easy access to the Great Barrier Reef. Visit May through October for ideal conditions – warm days, cool nights, and no stingers in the water.
What are the best kids activities in Cairns?
The Great Barrier Reef snorkelling trips accept kids from age four – absolutely unmissable! Hartley's Crocodile Adventures lets them meet cassowaries and crocs safely. The Cairns Esplanade Lagoon is free and perfect for littlies. Kuranda's Skyrail and Scenic Railway make a brilliant day out. Don't miss the Tjapukai Aboriginal Cultural Park for didgeridoo lessons and spear throwing. Rusty's Markets on Saturday mornings is surprisingly fun for kids too.
Are there any kids festivals in Cairns?
Cairns Festival in August/September features heaps of family-friendly events, including outdoor performances and workshops the kids love. The winter school holidays (June/July) see most holiday parks running fantastic kids' clubs with daily activities. Port Douglas Carnivale in May is worth the short drive north for colourful street parades. Check what's on during your dates – the tropical north really comes alive with events during the dry season.
Which is the best kids resort in Cairns?
BIG4 Ingenia Holidays Cairns Coconut functions like a resort with its pools, playgrounds, and jumping pillows keeping kids entertained for hours. Lake Placid Rainforest Retreat offers a more boutique rainforest resort experience with nature walks right from your cabin. Both provide excellent facilities while staying more affordable than traditional resorts. Many families find holiday parks offer better value and more space than hotels for longer tropical north stays.
What are the best things to do in Cairns with kids when it's raining?
Head to Cairns Aquarium to see reef creatures up close without getting wet – absolutely brilliant for all ages. The Tanks Arts Centre in an old WWII fuel tank hosts exhibitions and workshops. Muddy's Playground at the Esplanade is undercover and completely free. Cairns Museum is surprisingly engaging for kids. Rusty's Markets operates rain or shine. Many families also do their reef trips on rainy days since you're getting wet anyway!
What are the best things to do around Cairns with kids?
Port Douglas (an hour north) offers calm Four Mile Beach and Wildlife Habitat. The Atherton Tablelands deliver waterfalls, crater lakes, and platypus spotting at Granite Gorge. Palm Cove's beach is perfect for littlies. Crystal Cascades and Josephine Falls make brilliant swimming holes. Kuranda village combines rainforest, markets, and the Skyrail experience. Most holiday parks offer tour booking services to help plan your adventures.
