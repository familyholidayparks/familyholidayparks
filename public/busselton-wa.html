Local Knowledge

G'day! Busselton and the greater Geographe Bay region punch well above their weight for family holidays, with that famous jetty stretching nearly 2km into impossibly turquoise water – and yes, the kids will want to ride the little red train to the underwater observatory at the end. We reckon late spring through autumn (October to April) gives you the best crack at the weather, though locals will tell you the secret is visiting in March when the water's warmest and the summer crowds have cleared out. Here's a pro tip: grab fish and chips from Goose on the loose near the foreshore rather than the pricier jetty spots, then let the kids burn off energy at the massive playground right there while you watch the sunset over the bay.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the best time to visit Busselton WA with kids?

G'day! The sweet spot is definitely spring (September-November) and autumn (March-May) - you'll get gorgeous weather without the summer crowds. That said, summer (December-February) is magic for beach days, though it gets busy during school hols. Winter's mild here too, perfect if your little ones love whale watching! Just pack a jumper for cooler evenings year-round.

Are there pet-friendly holiday parks in Busselton WA?

You bet! Several holiday parks around Busselton welcome your furry family members. Most have designated pet-friendly cabins or caravan sites, though you'll need to keep dogs on leads in common areas. It's always worth ringing ahead to check their specific pet policies and any additional fees. The nearby beaches have dog-friendly sections too - your pooch will love it!

How much do holiday parks cost in Busselton WA during school holidays?

During school holidays, expect to pay around $150-$350 per night for family cabins, depending on size and facilities. Powered caravan sites are more budget-friendly at $50-$80 per night. Peak times like Christmas and Easter are priciest. Book early for better rates! Off-peak visits can save you 30-40% - bonus if you can sneak away term-time!

What should I pack for a holiday park stay with young kids?

Don't stress too much! Essentials: swimmers, sunscreen (SPF 50+), hats, beach toys, and insect repellent. Most parks have basic kitchen gear, but bring your own linen or check if it's included. Pack bikes, scooters, and outdoor games - kids love the freedom! A portable high chair and nightlight are lifesavers for littlies. Oh, and baby wipes - trust me!

What is there to do near Busselton WA holiday parks for families?

You're spoilt for choice, mate! Walk the iconic Busselton Jetty, visit the underwater observatory, and splash at the calm beaches. Kids love the playgrounds along the foreshore and mini golf. Take a day trip to Margaret River for cave tours, or head to Dunsborough's amazing beaches. The local wildlife parks are brilliant too - koalas and kangaroos galore!
