The Margaret River Holiday Park Scene

Margaret River's holiday parks tap into what makes this corner of WA genuinely special – you've got the Southern Ocean minutes away, the Margaret River itself winding through native forest, and some of the best wine country in Australia all within a short drive. What sets these parks apart from your standard caravan stop is that they're positioned as bases for a proper region rather than just overnight spots. Discovery Parks – Margaret River sits closest to town and the river, giving you easy access to cellar doors and local cafes, while RAC Margaret River Nature Park leans into the bush experience with more space between sites and walking trails through jarrah and marri forest. Riverview Tourist Park and Margaret River Tourist Park fill different niches – one's more compact and family-focused, the other's got that mix of caravanners, motorhomes and a handful of cabins. You'll see serious caravan people who've been coming for decades, families who've worked Margaret River school holidays into their annual rhythm, and increasingly motorhomers doing longer loops through WA who've heard the reputation and stick around longer than planned.

Booking patterns here are pretty predictable – Easter and winter school holidays see the parks absolutely rammed, and you need to be in early. Summer's quieter than you'd expect given the proximity to beach and river, mainly because most families hit the coast further north. The real returning visitors are the ones who've figured out that Margaret River works year-round; September through November is honestly the sweet spot with fewer crowds and the forest at its best. Families come back because there's genuinely something for everyone – you've got river swimming and fishing spots, forest walks that don't require epic fitness, and the older kids can explore the cave systems or go mountain biking. The parks themselves aren't fancy, but that's not the point; people are here for what's around them, not resort facilities.

What keeps people coming back year after year is that Margaret River hasn't turned into a theme park version of itself. You can stay in a caravan park and spend your days doing anything from serious wine tasting to mucking about with fishing rods in the river to walking through old-growth forest where you'll actually see wildlife. The combination of river access, proximity to the ocean at Prevelly and Gnarabup, and the wine country creates something you don't get in other holiday park destinations – there's enough variety that different family members with different interests actually get what they want. And once people settle into a favourite park and discover their spots around the region, they tend to book the same weeks, same sites, year after year. It becomes less about the holiday and more about the ritual of returning somewhere that works.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the best family holiday parks in Margaret River?
Discovery Parks - Margaret River, RAC Margaret River Nature Park, Margaret River Tourist Park, and Riverview Tourist Park all offer family-friendly facilities. Each provides different amenities suited to various needs. Discovery Parks features modern facilities and activities, while RAC offers nature-focused experiences. Compare options based on your priorities—some excel with pools, others with beach proximity or cabin quality.
Which caravan park in Margaret River is best for kids?
Discovery Parks - Margaret River stands out for families with its dedicated kids' activities, playground facilities, and family-oriented amenities. Margaret River Tourist Park also caters well to children with recreational spaces. Both parks understand family needs and provide entertainment options beyond typical caravan facilities, making them top choices for parent travelers.
Do Margaret River holiday parks have swimming pools?
Yes, Discovery Parks - Margaret River features a swimming pool, making it excellent for families wanting water play without traveling to the beach. This is particularly valuable during cooler months or when you need a break from ocean conditions. Check current facilities before booking, as amenities can vary seasonally.
Which holiday parks near Margaret River are closest to the beach?
Margaret River Tourist Park and Riverview Tourist Park offer proximity to local beaches and waterfront areas. Discovery Parks - Margaret River also provides reasonable beach access. Most Margaret River parks sit within 10-15 minutes of coastal spots, making beach visits convenient for families. Confirm exact distances when booking for your preferred beach activities.
Where can I find powered sites in Margaret River caravan parks?
All four parks—Discovery Parks, RAC Margaret River Nature Park, Margaret River Tourist Park, and Riverview Tourist Park—offer powered caravan sites. Powered sites typically accommodate caravans, motorhomes, and camper vans with electrical hookups. Availability varies by season, so book well in advance, especially during school holidays and peak summer periods.
What cabin options are available at Margaret River holiday parks?
Discovery Parks - Margaret River and Margaret River Tourist Park offer cabin accommodations ranging from basic to self-contained options. These suit families preferring fixed accommodation over caravanning. Cabins vary in size and amenities, so specify your group size and requirements when inquiring. Cabins book quickly during peak season.
Are Margaret River holiday parks pet-friendly?
Pet policies vary across parks. Some sites accept well-behaved pets in designated areas, while others have restrictions. Contact Discovery Parks, RAC Margaret River Nature Park, Margaret River Tourist Park, and Riverview Tourist Park directly about their current pet policies. Many Margaret River accommodations welcome pets, but advance notice is usually required.
What offers the best value holiday park in Margaret River?
Riverview Tourist Park and RAC Margaret River Nature Park typically offer competitive pricing without sacrificing essential family amenities. Off-season visits provide better rates across all parks. Compare nightly costs against included facilities—some parks bundle activities or discounts, improving overall value. Weekly stays often attract better pricing than nightly rates.
What's the best area to stay in Margaret River for families?
Stay centrally near Margaret River town for easy access to wineries, restaurants, and attractions. Discovery Parks - Margaret River and Margaret River Tourist Park's locations offer convenience to family activities. Proximity to beaches, playgrounds, and the Margaret River itself influences your choice. Consider whether you prioritize town access, nature immersion, or beach closeness.
What rainy day activities work near Margaret River holiday parks?
Margaret River offers excellent rainy-day options: visit local wineries (many kid-friendly), explore the Margaret River Discovery Centre, try indoor adventure parks, or visit the Mammoth Cave. Many parks have covered recreation facilities. Beach walks remain possible in light rain. Plan indoor activities for guaranteed bad weather days during winter visits (June-August).
