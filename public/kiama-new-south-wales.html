The Kiama Holiday Park Scene

Kiama's holiday park scene sits in that sweet spot on the South Coast where you've got the beach practically on your doorstep but you're close enough to the Shoalhaven River system that you can shift your focus inland if the ocean swells are pumping or the weather turns. What makes this stretch different from other NSW coastal clusters is the variety packed into a small radius—you can stay beachfront at places like BIG4 Easts and Surf Beach, where the sand's literally metres away, or grab a riverside spot at Werri Beach or Seven Mile Beach if you want calmer water and better shelter. The bush-backed parks like Kendalls give you that semi-rural feel while still being walkable to the beach, so you're not choosing between nature access and coastal views. Most of these aren't flashy resort setups with water features and activities programs; they're solid, unpretentious parks built by people who understand that caravanners and motorhome crews want decent facilities, good sites, and proximity to what matters—which here means the water.

The parks fill a real niche for repeat visitors and families who've worked out that Kiama delivers consistent conditions without the chaos of peak holiday destinations. You'll find plenty of grey nomads who've basically got standing bookings, families who return Easter and winter school holidays like clockwork, and a steady stream of caravaners doing the South Coast loop who know which parks have the best infrastructure and the fewest headaches. School holidays get tight—especially winter and summer breaks when the parks fill weeks in advance—but the demand is predictable enough that regulars have learned to book early. The mix means parks stay busy enough to maintain standards but not so overcrowded that you're packed in like the holiday parks up near Coffs Harbour.

What keeps families and regular visitors coming back is the combination of reliable beach access, river activities, and enough space to actually relax without feeling like you're on top of your neighbours. The kids can walk to Easts Beach or Surf Beach from the top-ranked parks, you've got rock pools and protected swimming spots along the coast, and if you want something quieter, the river options give you kayaking, fishing, and natural bushland without fighting crowds. There's no pretence to it—no themed attractions or forced entertainment—just decent caravan sites, the ocean or river within walking distance, and enough local knowledge floating around the parks that newcomers quickly figure out where to find the best fish and chips, where the swell's pumping, or which beach is best for families with young kids. That straightforward approach is exactly why people come back year after year.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the best family holiday parks in Kiama, NSW?
BIG4 Easts Beach, Surf Beach, Kendalls On The Beach, and Werri Beach Holiday Park (Kiama Coast) are top choices for families. Each offers beachfront or near-beach locations with facilities suited to children. BIG4 Easts Beach provides extensive amenities, while smaller parks like Surf Beach offer quieter, more intimate experiences. Compare what matters most—pools, entertainment, or beach access—when choosing.
Which caravan park in Kiama is best for kids?
BIG4 Easts Beach Holiday Park stands out with dedicated kids' facilities, swimming pool, playground, and organized activities during peak seasons. Werri Beach Holiday Park (Kiama Coast) also caters well to families with beach access and good on-site amenities. Both parks have shallow pools and safe environments for younger children.
Are there holiday parks with pools near Kiama?
Yes, BIG4 Easts Beach features a heated swimming pool ideal for families. Kendalls On The Beach and Werri Beach Holiday Park also have pool facilities. Pool temperatures vary seasonally, so confirm heating during winter months when booking if your family prefers warmer water.
What holiday parks near Kiama are directly beachfront?
Surf Beach Holiday Park, Kendalls On The Beach, and Werri Beach Holiday Park (Kiama Coast) offer direct beach access. BIG4 Easts Beach is near-beachfront with easy walking distance to the sand. Beachfront locations mean families can access swimming, rock pools, and beach activities immediately from accommodation.
Which Kiama parks have powered caravan sites?
All four parks—BIG4 Easts Beach, Surf Beach, Kendalls On The Beach, and Werri Beach Holiday Park—offer powered sites. BIG4 Easts Beach has the most extensive powered options. Availability varies seasonally, so book ahead during school holidays and summer months for guaranteed powered site access.
What cabin options are available in Kiama holiday parks?
All four parks offer cabin accommodation ranging from basic to self-contained. BIG4 Easts Beach has the widest cabin variety. Werri Beach Holiday Park, Kendalls On The Beach, and Surf Beach provide family-friendly cabins with ensuites. Cabin prices vary significantly—budget options to premium. Book early for school holidays.
Are there pet-friendly holiday parks in Kiama?
Most Kiama parks accommodate pets, including BIG4 Easts Beach and Werri Beach Holiday Park. However, pet policies vary—some restrict certain dog breeds or sizes. Always confirm pet accommodation directly when booking. Designated pet areas and exercise spaces differ between parks, so clarify beforehand if you're bringing furry family members.
Which is the best value holiday park in Kiama?
Surf Beach Holiday Park and Werri Beach Holiday Park (Kiama Coast) generally offer better value than larger operators. BIG4 Easts Beach justifies higher prices through extensive facilities. Compare nightly rates against what matters to your family—amenities, location, or activities—rather than price alone for actual value.
What's the best area to stay in Kiama for families?
Werri Beach and Kendalls On The Beach areas offer quieter, family-friendly environments with excellent beach access. BIG4 Easts Beach suits families wanting more organized activities and facilities. Consider your priorities: peaceful beach time, kids' entertainment, or proximity to nearby towns like Gerringong for additional activities.
What can families do with kids on rainy days in Kiama?
Kiama lacks major indoor attractions, so rainy-day options are limited. Nearby Gerringong has a small library and local shops. Most families rely on caravan park facilities—pools, communal areas, and organized activities at BIG4 Easts Beach. Pack indoor games and books. Consider day trips to larger towns within 30 minutes for wet-weather alternatives.
