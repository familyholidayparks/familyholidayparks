Frequently Asked Questions

What's the best accommodation in Yeppoon for families with kids?
Yeppoon's holiday parks are brilliant for families! Ingenia Holidays Kinka Beach offers fantastic facilities right near calm swimming beaches, while Discovery Parks Coolwaters has excellent pools and playgrounds. NRMA Capricorn Yeppoon Holiday Park sits close to the town centre, making it easy to grab fish and chips. All three parks have jumping pillows, games rooms, and family-friendly cabins that won't empty your wallet.
What's the best caravan park in Yeppoon?
Ingenia Holidays Kinka Beach consistently tops the list with its beachfront location and modern amenities. Discovery Parks Coolwaters is another cracker choice with its massive waterpark and spotless facilities. For budget-conscious families, Blue Dolphin Caravan Park offers great value, while Poinciana Tourist Park provides a quieter, more relaxed vibe. The best pick really depends on whether you're after entertainment or peace and quiet!
What's the best caravan park in Yeppoon QLD?
NRMA Capricorn Yeppoon Holiday Park wins many families over with its central location and well-maintained grounds. Ingenia Holidays Kinka Beach is hard to beat if beachfront access matters most to you. Discovery Parks Coolwaters stands out for kids who love waterslides and pools. Visit between May and October for the best weather – no stingers and beautifully mild temperatures perfect for exploring.
What are the best things to do in Yeppoon with kids?
The calm beaches are perfect for little swimmers, and the Capricorn Caves just 20 minutes north are absolutely magical – kids love spotting glowworms in the Cathedral Cave. The Yeppoon Lagoon is free and brilliant for toddlers, while Cooberrie Park Wildlife Sanctuary lets children hand-feed kangaroos and hold koalas. Don't miss fish and chips on the foreshore at sunset – it's a Yeppoon tradition!
What kids activities are there in Yeppoon?
Beyond the gorgeous beaches, Yeppoon offers the free lagoon with shallow areas perfect for toddlers, plus playgrounds dotted along the foreshore. Take a day trip to Cooberrie Park to meet native animals, or explore rock pools at low tide. Most holiday parks like Ingenia Kinka Beach and Discovery Parks Coolwaters have jumping pillows, mini golf, and organized kids' activities during school holidays.
Where can kids play soccer in Yeppoon?
Capricorn Coast Junior Soccer runs programs at various local ovals throughout the season. The main playing fields are at Yeppoon Showgrounds and Cooee Bay. If you're just after a casual kick-around during your holiday, the grassed areas at Yeppoon Foreshore are perfect, or many holiday parks like NRMA Capricorn Yeppoon have open spaces where kids can boot the ball around safely.
What coastal activities are there for kids in Yeppoon?
Yeppoon's beaches are absolutely perfect for families – the water stays calm and clear, ideal for snorkeling and swimming. Try rock pool exploring at low tide, building sandcastles on the white sand, or hiring stand-up paddleboards. The foreshore pathway is great for bike riding, and fishing off the rocks often yields decent catches. Visit between May and October when the water's stinger-free and stunning!
What things can you do around Yeppoon with kids?
Beyond Yeppoon itself, the Capricorn Caves are a must-see – about 20 minutes north and absolutely fascinating for children. Great Keppel Island makes a brilliant day trip with crystal-clear snorkeling. Head to Cooberrie Park Wildlife Sanctuary for hands-on animal encounters, or visit Emu Park's singing ship monument. The coastal drive between Yeppoon and Emu Park offers stunning views and several beach stops.
What can we do in Yeppoon with kids this weekend?
Hit the beach early before it gets too warm, then cool off at Yeppoon Lagoon which is free and perfect for all ages. Grab lunch at one of the foreshore cafes, then explore the rockpools or hire bikes to cruise the coastal path. If the weather's iffy, Capricorn Caves makes a fantastic rainy day adventure. Most holiday parks run weekend activities like outdoor movies or treasure hunts during peak seasons.
