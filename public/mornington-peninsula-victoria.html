If We Were Booking...

  • Maximum on-site entertainment: We'd choose BIG4 Mornington Peninsula at Frankston for the jumping pillow, flying fox and pedal karts, or Amberlee Family Holidays for its four playgrounds and splash park.
  • Beach walkable: We'd choose Peninsula Holiday Park at Safety Beach or Kanasta at Rye if being able to walk to calm bay swimming mattered more than facilities.
  • Our tip: Stick to the front beaches with young kids. Dromana, Rosebud and Rye are shallow, flat and patrolled in summer. Save the ocean back beaches for watching, not swimming.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the Mornington Peninsula good for families?
Yes. The bay beaches are shallow and calm, the parks are strong on entertainment, and attractions like the Arthurs Seat Eagle, Ashcombe Maze and the hot springs fill a week easily.
Which beaches are best for young children?
The front beaches on the bay side: Dromana, Rosebud, Rye and Safety Beach. They're shallow a long way out and patrolled in summer.
Are the ocean beaches safe for kids?
The back beaches like Gunnamatta and Portsea ocean beach carry dangerous rips. They're worth seeing but keep swimming to the patrolled bay beaches with children.
Which park has the most for kids to do on-site?
BIG4 Frankston and Amberlee are the standouts. Frankston runs a jumping pillow, flying fox and pedal karts; Amberlee has four playgrounds, mini golf and a splash park.
Can kids go to Peninsula Hot Springs?
Yes, the hot springs run family-friendly bathing areas and sessions. Book ahead, especially weekends and school holidays.
Is the Peninsula a good base for Melbourne day trips?
Frankston is about 50 minutes from central Melbourne by car or train, which makes BIG4 Frankston a realistic base for a city day without staying in the city.
What are the best free family activities?
The front beaches, the Dromana and Rosebud foreshore playgrounds, the Millionaires Walk at Sorrento and the Cape Schanck boardwalk are all free.
What should families do when it rains?
Boneo Maze's indoor play space, the Frankston cinema, the games rooms at the bigger parks and an indoor session at the hot springs cover wet days.
How far in advance should you book?
Summer school holidays book out months ahead, especially anything walkable to a front beach. Melbourne long weekends fill fast year-round.
Do the parks allow pets?
Several do, including Peninsula Holiday Park, Capel, Amberlee and Kanasta. Check each park's rules for peak season restrictions.
