The Mandurah Holiday Park Scene

Mandurah's holiday parks sit in a sweet spot between the Indian Ocean and the Peel-Harvey Estuary, which fundamentally shapes what you get here compared to parks further north or south. The two main parks—Mandurah Caravan and Tourist Park and Mandurah Coastal Holiday Park—both leverage this dual-water-body advantage, though differently. The Caravan and Tourist Park is closer to town and the estuary foreshore, making it popular with older caravanners and families who want walkable access to the main strip without constantly moving their rig. The Coastal park sits closer to the actual beach at Silver Sands, which pulls in the motorhome crowd and families keen on ocean swimming over calmer estuary paddling. You won't find sprawling bush parks here like you do inland—Mandurah's appeal is water access, not isolation.

The demographic at these parks shifts noticeably with the school calendar. During term time, you'll see mostly retirees in permanent or semi-permanent vans, couples doing lap work, and the occasional young family taking a week off-season. Come school holidays—particularly the two-week winter and summer breaks—both parks fill to capacity with families, often the same families year after year. Parents return because there's genuine variety without having to pack up and move: kids can switch between safe estuary paddling, proper swimming at the beach, and quieter foreshore walks depending on the day and tide. The river's calm waters mean younger kids can actually do water activities their parents feel comfortable with, which you don't get at Cottesloe or the Southern Ocean beaches. Repeat visitors book 12 months ahead, and some families have been coming for twenty-odd years.

What keeps people coming back isn't fancy resort amenities—these are solid working parks, not luxury destinations. It's that Mandurah as a town has matured into something liveable without being overdeveloped. The estuary's protected status means the water quality and wildlife remain consistent year to year. Families fish off the jetties, spot dolphins regularly during winter, and do the Mandurah Boardwalk without the chaos of Perth beaches. The parks themselves offer reliable facilities, reasonable rates, and proximity to decent takeaway, pubs, and produce. For caravanners, the locations tick boxes—power and water sites, dump points, good roads for towing in and out. There's a reason the same vans show up in the same parks at the same time each year: Mandurah delivers predictably on what it promises, which in caravan culture is worth more than novelty.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the best family holiday parks in Mandurah?
Mandurah Caravan and Tourist Park and Mandurah Coastal Holiday Park are the top family destinations. Both offer excellent facilities, proximity to beaches and attractions, and dedicated family areas. Mandurah Coastal Holiday Park sits closer to the water, while Mandurah Caravan and Tourist Park provides more spacious grounds. Compare amenities like pools, playgrounds, and accommodation types to match your family's needs and budget.
Which caravan park in Mandurah is best for kids?
Mandurah Coastal Holiday Park excels for families with children, featuring dedicated kids' facilities, safe swimming areas, and activities designed for younger guests. Mandurah Caravan and Tourist Park also caters well to families with playground equipment and family-friendly zones. Both parks create secure environments where children can play safely while parents relax nearby.
Are there holiday parks with pools near Mandurah?
Both Mandurah Caravan and Tourist Park and Mandurah Coastal Holiday Park feature swimming pools ideal for families. These heated pools provide year-round enjoyment, keeping children entertained during downtime. The pools are well-maintained and supervised, making them perfect for families with young swimmers. Pool facilities vary between parks, so confirm specifics when booking.
Which Mandurah holiday parks are closest to the beach?
Mandurah Coastal Holiday Park offers beachfront or near-beach positioning, providing direct access to sandy shores and water activities. Mandurah Caravan and Tourist Park is also conveniently located within walking or short driving distance to Mandurah's beaches. Both parks position you perfectly for swimming, paddleboarding, and beach picnics without lengthy commutes.
Do Mandurah holiday parks offer powered caravan sites?
Yes, both Mandurah Caravan and Tourist Park and Mandurah Coastal Holiday Park provide powered caravan sites with full hookups including water, electricity, and sewerage. Powered sites are ideal for families needing appliances and comfort. Availability varies seasonally, so book early, especially during school holidays and summer months when demand peaks.
What cabin accommodation is available in Mandurah?
Both parks offer cabins and self-contained units ranging from basic to luxury. Mandurah Caravan and Tourist Park and Mandurah Coastal Holiday Park provide family-sized cabins with kitchens, bathrooms, and comfortable sleeping areas. Cabin options suit families preferring not to caravan. Prices vary by season and cabin quality, so review options based on your budget and family size.
Are Mandurah holiday parks pet-friendly?
Both Mandurah Caravan and Tourist Park and Mandurah Coastal Holiday Park welcome pets in designated areas. Pet-friendly policies typically require dogs on leads in communal areas and restrictions in certain zones. Confirm pet policies, any additional fees, and available pet facilities when booking. Many families appreciate bringing pets on Mandurah holidays for complete family getaways.
What's the best value holiday park in Mandurah?
Mandurah Caravan and Tourist Park typically offers competitive pricing with excellent facilities, making it strong value for families. Mandurah Coastal Holiday Park provides premium positioning near beaches, justifying higher rates. Compare nightly rates, included amenities, and seasonal discounts. Off-season visits to either park provide better value, especially outside school holidays and summer months.
What's the best area to stay in Mandurah for families?
Mandurah's beachfront and central areas suit families best. Mandurah Coastal Holiday Park positions you near water activities and attractions, while Mandurah Caravan and Tourist Park offers convenient access to shops, restaurants, and entertainment. Both locations provide safe, family-friendly environments with plenty of activities within short distances, ideal for parents wanting minimal travel time.
What are the best rainy day activities in Mandurah for kids?
Indoor pools at both parks keep children entertained during rain. Mandurah also features the Mandurah Performing Arts Centre, aquariums, and shopping centres nearby. Many parks offer game rooms and entertainment programs. Beach-side covered areas and local cafes provide shelter. Pack indoor games and crafts. Holiday parks' facilities ensure rainy days don't derail family fun during Mandurah visits.
