Frequently Asked Questions

Are there any Port Douglas holiday parks where kids stay free?
Several Port Douglas parks offer kids stay free promotions during off-peak periods, particularly outside school holidays. BIG4 Port Douglas Glengarry Holiday Park and Tropic Breeze Caravan Park regularly run family deals where children under certain ages bunk in at no extra cost. These specials pop up most often during the quieter months from February to April, so it's worth ringing ahead to ask what's current.
What's the best accommodation in Port Douglas for families with kids?
The top picks are BIG4 Port Douglas Glengarry Holiday Park for its lagoon pool and kids' activities, Tropic Breeze Caravan Park if you're after a quieter vibe with tropical gardens, and Pandanus Caravan Park for excellent value close to town. All three offer cabin accommodation with multiple bedrooms, handy kitchens, and are within easy reach of Four Mile Beach and the Sunday markets on Anzac Park.
Where should families stay in Port Douglas?
Families do brilliantly at the three main holiday parks: BIG4 Port Douglas Glengarry Holiday Park is the most resort-style with its waterslides and jumping pillow, Tropic Breeze Caravan Park offers shady, spacious sites perfect for unwinding, and Pandanus Caravan Park delivers solid facilities at good prices. All are positioned within walking or quick driving distance of Four Mile Beach, which is ideal for little ones during the dry season.
What's the most kid-friendly accommodation in Port Douglas?
BIG4 Port Douglas Glengarry Holiday Park takes the crown for kid-friendly features – think resort pool with waterslides, jumping pillow, playground, and organised activities during peak times. Tropic Breeze Caravan Park is brilliant for families wanting space to spread out in tropical surrounds, while Pandanus Caravan Park offers excellent swimming facilities and a relaxed atmosphere where kids can safely ride bikes around the grounds.
What are the best kids' activities in Port Douglas?
The Wildlife Habitat is unbeatable for up-close encounters with cassowaries and tree kangaroos. Four Mile Beach is perfect for sandcastle marathons and safe swimming during stinger-free season. Hop aboard a family-friendly reef cruise to the Low Isles, tackle the Mossman Gorge rainforest circuit, or let the kids burn energy at Rex Smeal Park. Sunday markets at Anzac Park are fantastic for grabbing tropical fruit and wandering about.
Are there kids' clubs at Port Douglas holiday parks?
BIG4 Port Douglas Glengarry Holiday Park runs supervised kids' activities during school holidays and peak periods, including craft sessions, pool games, and outdoor activities. The program typically operates during Queensland school holidays when families are visiting in bigger numbers. Outside these times, the parks focus more on facilities like playgrounds, pools, and games rooms where kids can entertain themselves while parents relax nearby.
Which Port Douglas holiday parks have the best playgrounds?
BIG4 Port Douglas Glengarry Holiday Park comes out on top with a proper playground plus the massive jumping pillow that'll wear out even the most energetic youngsters. Tropic Breeze Caravan Park has a decent playground tucked among the tropical gardens, while Pandanus Caravan Park offers play equipment suited to younger children. Rex Smeal Park in town also has excellent public playground facilities if you fancy a change of scenery.
What are the best things to do in Port Douglas with young kids?
Four Mile Beach is perfect for toddlers and young kids – gentle waves, soft sand, and rock pools at the southern end during low tide. Wildlife Habitat lets little ones hand-feed kangaroos at breakfast time. The calm waters at Mossman Gorge are brilliant for paddling, and the boardwalk makes pram access easy. Sunday markets at Anzac Park offer hassle-free entertainment, and most reef operators welcome families with young children on Low Isles trips.
What free things can you do in Port Douglas with kids?
Four Mile Beach is the obvious winner – gorgeous, free, and patrolled during stinger season. The southern end at low tide reveals rock pools teeming with marine life that'll keep kids fascinated for ages. Rex Smeal Park has excellent playground equipment and toilets. Stroll along the Esplanade, spot fish off Crystalbrook Marina, or explore Anzac Park. The Sunday markets are free to wander, and kids love the tropical atmosphere.
