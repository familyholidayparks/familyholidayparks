If We Were Booking...

  • Waterfront camping: We'd choose 1770 Camping Ground for the absolute waterfront sites on calm, shallow water, accepting rustic facilities as the price of the position.
  • Nature and comfort: We'd choose Traveller's Rest 1770 for the boardwalk, birdlife and tidier amenities, driving five minutes to the water.
  • Our tip: Book a LARC tour. The big pink amphibious vehicle drives off the beach straight into the water on its way to the lighthouse, and no child has ever been unimpressed by that.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is 1770 good for young children?
Yes, the protected side of the headland offers calm, shallow paddling water, and the village pace suits families who want quiet.
What is the LARC tour?
An amphibious vehicle tour that drives along beaches and through the water to Bustard Head lighthouse. It's the region's signature family experience.
Which park should families choose?
1770 Camping Ground for waterfront sites and calm-water paddling, or Traveller's Rest for nature boardwalks and neater facilities a short drive from the water.
Why is the sunset famous here?
The headland faces west over the water, rare on the east coast, so the sun sets into the sea. The Round Hill headland gathers a small crowd nightly.
Can you get to the reef from 1770?
Yes, Lady Musgrave Island day trips leave from the 1770 marina with calm lagoon snorkelling suited to families.
Where do you buy supplies?
Agnes Water, ten minutes away, has the supermarket, fuel and shops. 1770 itself is a cafe, a marina and not much else, deliberately.
What are the best free family activities?
Paddling the calm foreshore, the headland walk at Round Hill, the paperbark boardwalk and crab-spotting in the mangroves at low tide.
When is the best time to visit?
April to October for mild weather. Winter sunsets over the water are the postcard version.
What should families do when it rains?
Very little is undercover. A shower means the cafe; settled rain means Agnes Water's shops or an early LARC booking when it clears.
How far ahead should you book?
The camping ground's waterfront sites are the region's hottest ticket and book out months ahead for holidays.
