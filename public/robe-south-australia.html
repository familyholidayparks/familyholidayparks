If We Were Booking...

  • Bushland and beach access: We'd choose Robe Holiday Park for the jumping pillow and bush tracks alongside nearby beach and lake access.
  • Direct beach position: We'd choose Sea Vu Caravan Park for swimming right at the doorstep and easy town access.
  • Our tip: Try fresh local crayfish for a family dinner, Robe's fishing fleet lands it daily, and even fussy eaters often enjoy watching the boats come in at the harbour beforehand.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Robe good for families?
Yes, particularly for families wanting a relaxed beach town with good fishing, swimming and a genuine working harbour to explore.
Which Robe park is best for families?
Robe Holiday Park for bushland setting with a jumping pillow, or Sea Vu Caravan Park for direct beach access.
Is Robe's beach safe for swimming?
Generally yes, though as with any ocean beach, check current conditions and swim in patrolled areas where available.
What is Lake Butler?
A calmer body of water in Robe, a good alternative to the ocean for younger or less confident swimmers.
Is Robe known for seafood?
Yes, it's a significant fishing port known particularly for crayfish, and fresh local seafood is readily available.
What are the best free family activities?
The Cape Dombey Obelisk walk, watching the fishing fleet at the harbour, and the little penguin colony viewing area.
When is the best time to visit?
Summer for swimming, though Robe's relaxed pace works well across most of the year.
What should families do when it rains?
The town's cafes and small shops offer a relaxed wet-weather wander.
How far is Robe from Adelaide?
About 3.5 hours drive south east, a popular Limestone Coast destination.
How far ahead should you book?
Summer school holidays can fill Robe's limited accommodation, so book ahead for peak periods.
