Local Knowledge

G'day! The limestone coast around Robe is an absolute cracker for families, with those incredible blue lakes, rock pools teeming with life at low tide, and beaches where the kids can actually swim without you having a heart attack. We reckon April to November is your sweet spot – the summer crowds have thinned out, accommodation prices drop, and you'll still get plenty of sunny days for beach walks and cray spotting. Here's a local tip that'll save you a packet: grab your fresh crayfish straight off the boats at the town jetty rather than paying restaurant prices, then cook them up at your holiday park – the kids will never forget watching those crays come in while the pelicans put on their daily show around 4pm.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the best time to visit Robe SA with kids?

G'day! The best time to visit Robe with little ones is during summer (December-February) for beach fun, though it can get busy during school holidays. Autumn (March-May) is brilliant too – still warm enough for swimming, fewer crowds, and more affordable accommodation. Winter's great for rock pooling and cosy cabin stays, while spring brings wildflowers and perfect walking weather. Really, Robe's a ripper year-round!

Are there pet-friendly holiday parks in Robe SA?

You betcha! Several holiday parks in Robe welcome your furry family members. Discovery Parks Robe and Lake Butler Holiday Park both offer pet-friendly cabins and sites, though you'll need to book specific pet-approved accommodation. Just keep your pooch on a leash, bring their bedding, and check each park's pet policy beforehand. The nearby beaches have designated dog-friendly areas too – perfect for morning walks!

How much do holiday parks cost in Robe SA during school holidays?

During school holidays, expect to pay $150-$300+ per night for cabins sleeping 4-6 people, depending on the park and cabin type. Powered sites for caravans and campervans run $50-$80 per night. Unpowered camping sites are the most budget-friendly at $40-$60. Book early for better rates – seriously, like 6-12 months ahead for peak summer holidays. Off-peak prices can be 30-40% cheaper!

What should I pack for a holiday park stay with young kids?

Pack the essentials: swimmers, towels, sunscreen (SPF50+), hats, and beach toys for the littlies. Most cabins have kitchens, so bring basic groceries and kids' favourites. Don't forget warm layers for cool evenings, a first-aid kit, insect repellent, and comfort items like favourite teddies. Bike helmets if you're bringing bikes, and gumboots for rock pooling. Oh, and plenty of patience for sandcastle building!

What is there to do near Robe SA holiday parks for families?

Heaps! Long Beach is perfect for swimming and building sandcastles. The rock pools at Little Dip are brilliant for little explorers. Walk to the Obelisk for stunning views, visit the lobster boats at the jetty, or spot wildlife at Bool Lagoon. The Robe Bakery's a must for treats! Many parks have playgrounds, jumping pillows, and pools on-site, so the kids are entertained rain or shine.
