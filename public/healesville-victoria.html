If We Were Booking...

  • The family base: We'd choose BIG4 Yarra Valley Park Lane Holiday Park for its solid on-site entertainment and easy access to both Healesville Sanctuary and the surrounding wine region.
  • Our tip: Give Healesville Sanctuary a full day, not a half day. The birds of prey flight display and platypus viewing are genuine highlights, and kids often want to circle back through favourite enclosures more than once.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Healesville good for families?
Yes, primarily thanks to Healesville Sanctuary, one of Australia's best wildlife parks, alongside a reasonable holiday park base.
Which Healesville park is best for kids?
BIG4 Yarra Valley Park Lane Holiday Park, with good on-site entertainment and a convenient location for exploring the region.
What is Healesville Sanctuary?
A leading Australian wildlife sanctuary with platypus, Tasmanian devils, birds of prey shows and hands-on animal encounters, best explored over a full day.
Are the Yarra Valley wineries kid-friendly?
Many have grounds and lawns children can explore while parents enjoy a tasting, though the tastings themselves are for adults.
What else is there to do near Healesville?
The scenic drive to Yarra Glen, and Maroondah Reservoir Park for an easy picnic and walk.
What are the best free family activities?
Maroondah Reservoir Park's walking tracks and picnic areas.
When is the best time to visit?
Spring and autumn for the mildest weather and prettiest Yarra Valley scenery.
What should families do when it rains?
Healesville Sanctuary has covered walkways and indoor exhibits that work well even in wet weather.
How far is Healesville from Melbourne?
About 1 hour drive, an easy day trip or weekend destination from the city.
How far ahead should you book?
Generally straightforward outside major school holiday periods.
