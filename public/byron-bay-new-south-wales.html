Frequently Asked Questions

Is Byron Bay good for families?
Yes. Byron Bay is one of Australia's most family-friendly beach destinations, offering safe beaches, wildlife encounters, playgrounds, markets and family accommodation. It works well for toddlers, primary-school children and teenagers alike.
What are the best things to do in Byron Bay with kids?
Popular family activities include visiting Cape Byron Lighthouse, exploring Wategos Beach rock pools, spotting dolphins, swimming at Main Beach, visiting nearby waterfalls and exploring the Byron Bay hinterland.
Which Byron Bay holiday park is best for families?
Suffolk Beachfront Holiday Park ranks highest overall for families seeking a relaxed beach holiday, while Discovery Parks Byron Bay is the best option for families wanting pools, water play and resort-style facilities.
Are there family caravan parks in Byron Bay with pools?
Yes. Discovery Parks Byron Bay offers one of the best pool and water play areas in the region. Ingenia Holidays Byron Bay also has a pool suitable for families.
Where should families stay in Byron Bay?
Families wanting a quieter coastal experience often choose Suffolk Beachfront Holiday Park or Broken Head Holiday Park. Families wanting more facilities generally prefer Discovery Parks Byron Bay. First Sun Holiday Park and Reflections Byron Bay are ideal for families wanting to walk into town.
Is Byron Bay worth visiting with kids?
Absolutely. Byron Bay combines beaches, wildlife, family-friendly attractions and outdoor adventures in one compact destination. Many families return year after year because there is plenty to do without spending hours in the car.
What are the best beaches in Byron Bay for families?
Clarkes Beach and Main Beach are popular for family swimming, while Wategos Beach is perfect for rock pooling and dolphin spotting. Tallow Beach and Broken Head Beach are better suited to families looking for quieter stretches of sand.
What can kids do in Byron Bay when it is raining?
Rainy days are perfect for exploring the Byron Bay hinterland, visiting local markets and cafes, discovering nearby waterfalls or taking short rainforest walks. Many families also enjoy indoor activities and local attractions around town.
