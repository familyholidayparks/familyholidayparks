If We Were Booking...

  • Waterpark afternoons: We'd choose Discovery Parks Barossa Valley for the heated pool, waterpark and splash pad, and plan cellar-door lunches around pool sessions.
  • Space to roam: We'd choose BIG4 Barossa Tourist Park for the three playgrounds, games room and roomier sites, 10 minutes from the main cellar doors.
  • Our tip: Take the kids to the Whispering Wall at Barossa Reservoir. Whisper into the wall at one end and they'll hear you clearly 140 metres away at the other. Free, quick and it genuinely blows their minds.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the Barossa Valley worth visiting with kids?
Yes, and it's easier than most wine regions. Two strong family parks with waterplay and playgrounds, wineries that genuinely welcome children and the Whispering Wall for free entertainment.
Which Barossa holiday park is best for kids?
Discovery Parks Barossa Valley for water play, with a heated pool, waterpark and splash pad. BIG4 Barossa is the pick for space, with three playgrounds and larger sites.
Do Barossa wineries allow children?
Most do, and several cater well with lawns, playgrounds and kid-friendly menus. Ask your park for the current family favourites, they field the question daily.
What is the Whispering Wall?
The curved dam wall at Barossa Reservoir carries a whisper clearly from one end to the other, 140 metres apart. It's free and reliably amazes kids.
How far is the Barossa from Adelaide?
About an hour's drive, which makes it an easy first or last stop on a South Australian loop, or a weekend trip from the city.
What free activities are there for families?
The Whispering Wall, Mengler Hill lookout and sculpture park, town playgrounds at Tanunda and Nuriootpa, and the shared-use bike path linking the towns.
Can you ride bikes around the Barossa with kids?
Yes, the Barossa Trail links Tanunda, Nuriootpa and Angaston on a sealed shared path away from traffic. Both parks are handy to it.
What should families do when it rains?
The National Motor Museum at Birdwood is 40 minutes away and a reliable hit, the park games rooms cover the gaps, and cellar door lunches don't mind rain.
When is the best time to visit?
Autumn, when the vines turn and the weather is mild. Summer works because of the water facilities, and winter is quiet with open fires at the cellar doors.
How far in advance should you book?
School holidays and festival weekends, especially around the Barossa Vintage Festival, book out ahead. Ordinary weekends are usually manageable.
